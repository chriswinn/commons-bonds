# Comp Titles

**Status.** Working list — refine to 5–6 for the final proposal.

**Purpose.** Anchor Commons Bonds in the agent's mental map. Each comp shows: there is a market for this register; how Commons Bonds is similar; how it advances the conversation.

## Comps ⊆ Bibliography (with limited market-signal exceptions)

The cleanest comps are books the manuscript already engages with substantively — i.e., entries in `research/literature/bibliography.md`. A comp that the framework cites and engages reads as intellectual honesty; a comp that's only positioning ("similar audience") reads as marketing-only and is weaker.

**Rule of thumb:** lead with bibliography-anchored comps. At most 1, occasionally 2, market-signal-only comps are defensible — and only when explicitly framed as "audience overlap" rather than "intellectual neighbor."

## Working list (with bibliography status)

### Tier 1 — bibliography-anchored, mechanism-level neighbors

1. **Mariana Mazzucato — *The Value of Everything* (2018)** — *bibliography §13, LOAD-BEARING (Ring-1 flagship term "Value Extraction").*
   *Similar:* renames a category in economic vocabulary; trade-press accessible; non-partisan-readable.
   *Different:* Mazzucato critiques value-extraction at the level of GDP and rent in financial capitalism; Commons Bonds extends the diagnosis into physical-resource extraction and operationalizes a pricing apparatus (RCV) for the costs that extraction severs.

2. **Katharina Pistor — *The Code of Capital* (2019)** — *bibliography §13, STRONG SUPPORT (candidate; engagement pending Ch 5 / Ch 9 paragraph integration).*
   *Similar:* institutional/legal frame; scholarly-trade crossover; introduces a formal mechanism (legal coding) to explain market outcomes that look natural.
   *Different:* Pistor traces how law produces capital; Commons Bonds traces how extraction produces severed cost — and prices it.

3. **Brett Christophers — *The Price is Wrong* (2024)** — *bibliography §13, STRONG SUPPORT (candidate; engagement pending Ch 9 paragraph integration).*
   *Similar:* mechanism-level engagement with how pricing fails (in his case, renewable-energy investment); recent; same intellectual neighborhood.
   *Different:* Christophers diagnoses the pricing failure in the energy-transition context; Commons Bonds attempts to construct a corrective pricing instrument (RCV) for the cost-severance pattern more generally.

### Tier 2 — bibliography-anchored, register-adjacent

4. **Mariana Mazzucato — *Mission Economy* (2021)** — *not yet in bibliography; same author as Tier 1 #1, solutions register; Mazzucato readership re-buys.* Most useful as evidence of audience appetite for Mazzucato-register sequels rather than as a direct intellectual comp.

5. **Daniel Susskind — *Growth: A Reckoning* (2024)** — *bibliography §13, SUPPORT (candidate; engagement pending Ch 9 paragraph integration).*
   *Similar:* big-idea econ register; recent; takes "the costs of growth" as explicit subject.
   *Different:* macro-level (the growth objective itself) vs. Commons Bonds' per-extraction-event mechanism level.

### Tier 3 — market-signal candidates only (not in bibliography)

## Selection rule for the final proposal

Cite 5–6, not 8. Lead with Mazzucato + Pistor (Tier 1). Add Christophers (Tier 1) for current-market recency + mechanism-level fit. Add Susskind (Tier 2) for register breadth if a fourth bibliography-anchored comp helps. Round out with at most *one* Tier 3 comp — Patel & Moore is the strongest single voice-range signal — explicitly framed as audience overlap rather than intellectual lineage.

If the final 5 lands as: Mazzucato + Pistor + Christophers + Susskind + (one Tier 3), every comp except possibly the Tier 3 round-out is bibliography-anchored. That is the target.
