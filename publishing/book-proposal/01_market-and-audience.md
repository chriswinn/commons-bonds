# Market & Audience

**Status.** Skeleton — not yet drafted. Target completion: Sept–Oct 2026.

**Purpose.** Demonstrate to the agent (and through them, to the publisher's marketing/sales) that there is a paying readership for this book. Hard numbers where possible.

## Section sketch

- **Primary audience.** Readers of big-idea econ + policy nonfiction. Sizing via comp-title sales (Mazzucato, Pistor, Christophers, Susskind, Tooze). Pull figures from Publishers Marketplace and BookScan where accessible.
- **Secondary audiences.** Policy practitioners; environmental/labor lawyers; finance professionals interested in externalities; academic adjacents (econ, sociology, philosophy programs).
- **Tertiary / long-tail.** Indigenous-rights readers; intergenerational-justice readers; speculative-futures readers (drawn by Ch 7 Mars frame).
- **Course adoption potential.** Heterodox econ syllabi; environmental-economics seminars; commons / governance studies.
- **International rights signal.** Framework's universality across extraction domains (oil/coal/fisheries/rare-earth/finance/pharma/off-Earth) supports translation rights pitch — note for the proposal but don't oversell.

## What to research before drafting

- Comp-title BookScan trajectories where available.
- Recent Publishers Marketplace deals tagged "economics," "big idea," "policy" 2023–2026.
- University-press course-adoption data for closest comps.

## What to avoid

- Padded TAM math. Agents see through it.
- Implying mass-market reach. The honest pitch is thoughtful-trade with course-adoption tail and translation potential.
