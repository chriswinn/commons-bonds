## Sample-chapter conventions (trade nonfiction proposals)

Trade-press agents almost universally expect two patterns:

1. **Ch 1 is non-negotiable.** ~90% of trade proposals lead with Ch 1. Agents read the opening to assess whether the book hooks. They don't trust mid-book samples without seeing the opening — the opening is the book's most important sales tool. If Ch 1 doesn't land, nothing else matters.
2. **A second chapter that demonstrates a different register.** Either an early chapter that establishes the first apparatus move (so agents see voice → mechanism progression), OR a mid-book chapter that demonstrates sustained capacity beyond the opener.

Convention ceiling is ~50 pp of sample material total. Two chapters fits; three pushes the limit; one-and-a-fragment also works.

## Suggested redraft workflow

### Pass type — the discipline already exists

Per `tools/memory/feedback_audience_aware_drafting_discipline.md` (v3.1), the redraft pass is the **Stage 3 five-pass rigor cascade** plus any **Stage 1c cross-artifact coherence** triggered by citation additions:

- **Automatic-on-edit cascade** (cheap; fires on every prose edit): Pass 3.1 fact-check + Pass 3.2 voice-polish + Stage 1c-light cross-artifact coherence. ~10K–30K tokens per prose-edit event.
- **Explicit-gate cascade** (heavy; fires only on author trigger at specific gates): Pass 3.3 acceptance + Pass 3.4 robustness + Pass 3.5 developmental-edit + Stage 4 render-audit + Stage 5 sign-off. 40K–160K tokens per pre-pub batch.

For the proposal §06 sample chapters, the **explicit-gate cascade** should fire on the chosen sample chapters before they land in the proposal — Pipeline A Query Wave 1 firing window (~Jun 24 – Jul 1) is a distribution-readiness gate that qualifies for the heavy passes.

### Per-chapter redraft cadence (recommended)

For each priority-1 chapter (Ch 1, then Ch 5):

1. **Citation-integration pass** — surface and integrate any pending citations (Pistor / Christophers / Susskind / Darity & Mullen / Mazzucato lineage updates per bibliography §13 engagement state).
2. **Pass 3.1 fact-check** — automatic-on-edit; verifies factual claims against canonical fact-ground.
3. **Pass 3.2 voice-polish** — automatic-on-edit; smooths prose; em-dash calibration per `tools/memory/feedback_em_dash_overuse.md`; LLM-tic excision.
4. **Pass 3.3 acceptance** — explicit-gate; full audience-acceptance matrix (~18-character set per current discipline).
5. **Pass 3.4 robustness** — explicit-gate; adversarial-character pressure test.
6. **Pass 3.5 developmental-edit** — explicit-gate; whole-chapter restoration-of-richness lens (different polarity from 3.2 cuts — 3.5 restores scene-anchor density, sensory detail, emotional-arc continuity).
7. **Stage 4 render-audit** — character-integrity check.
8. **Stage 5 sign-off** — academic-rigor + prose-quality bookend; ratifies the chapter is publisher-facing-clean.

Per-prompt serial cadence: each pass fires in its own prompt; author ratifies + spot-fixes apply before next pass fires. Combined ratify-and-apply pattern per Amendment C interactive-ratification format.

### Token-budget estimate

Per chapter, full explicit-gate cascade: ~80K–160K tokens. For two chapters (Ch 1 + Ch 5): ~160K–320K tokens total. Per `tools/memory/feedback_token_cost_not_a_constraint.md` (ratified 2026-05-28), tokens are not a binding constraint — author attention + ratification cycles + clock time are. Budget the cascade in **author-attention units**, not in tokens: each pass takes ~10–20 minutes of author ratification time.

Two-week sprint to Pipeline A Query Wave 1 firing (~Jun 24 – Jul 1) is sufficient if author can clear ~2–3 ratification cycles per chapter per week. If author attention is constrained, prioritize Ch 1 alone for primary sample + abbreviated Ch 5 excerpt rather than two full chapters at half-polish.

### Where the redrafts land when ready

When a redrafted chapter is Stage 5 RATIFIED:

1. **Copy the polished prose into `publishing/book-proposal/06_sample-chapters/Ch_N_<title>.md`** (or symlink from the manuscript if author prefers single-source).
2. **Update `publishing/book-proposal/05_chapter-summaries.md`** if the redraft changed any load-bearing claims that the summary references (e.g., case selection, framework-apparatus naming, lineage attributions).
3. **Update `publishing/book-proposal/proposal-master.md`** assembly order line 17 to reflect actual sample chapters chosen.
4. **Commit + push** under the merge-on-ratification rule (end-user-facing prose; auto-merges to main on Stage 5 RATIFIED per CLAUDE.md §"Merge-on-ratification rule" 2026-05-28).
