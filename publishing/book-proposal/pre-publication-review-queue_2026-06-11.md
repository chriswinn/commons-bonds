## §1. What has been internally verified, and how

1. **A single figure canon.** Every shared figure in the book runs from one ratified ledger; chapters, appendix, and proposal are recascaded against it whenever it amends. Key anchors and their source classes: 1960 McDowell mine-mouth price $4.71/ton (EIA Total Energy Table 7.9); social cost of carbon $190/t (EPA 2023 central estimate); CO₂ factor 2.61 t/ton (EPA AP-42 emission coefficient × measured Pocahontas-seam heat content); the $510/ton eight-component total, label-pinned against its $496 carbon term; Norway's fund at NOK 21,268B end-2025 (NBIM annual report — the krone figure is the stable anchor; dollar conversions are date-stamped and re-stamped at each send); cumulative federal black-lung payments ≈$44B *through 2009* (GAO/CRS — deliberately dated; no authoritative later cumulative exists) against the $5.1B trust-fund debt as of September 2024; Chesapeake reef restoration floor $20–37M (documented acreage loss, Schulte 2017, × realized NOAA per-acre costs) against ~$115M of itemized public restoration spending (NOAA per-tributary accounting).
2. **Computational reproduction.** The appendix's quantitative apparatus is backed by a calculation engine (45 checked values across two scripts as of this date; zero mismatches). Every constant in the engine is either externally sourced or explicitly labeled an assumption; no posited number is typeset to look sourced.
3. **Internal-consistency gates.** An automated registry of previously-fixed errors and retired figures runs against the corpus at commit time; the appendix currently passes with zero high-severity findings. Retired claims (including several earlier cross-model figures whose derivations did not survive scrutiny) are blocked from re-entry by the same gate.
4. **Methodological honesty checks.** The framework does not treat agreement among its estimates as validation: where estimating lenses share an input (the carbon price dominates two of them on fossil cases), the text says so and does not count the agreement twice; where lenses diverge, the divergence is reported as informative. Figures are conservative by construction — floors, low ends of ranges, and stated sensitivities (e.g., the Libby ratio is given at ≈26× on documented cash and ≈13× under a revenue-doubling assumption).
5. **Multi-pass editorial rigor.** Each publisher-facing artifact passes a five-pass audit (fact-check; voice; audience acceptance; adversarial robustness; developmental) with per-pass findings ratified by the author before application. The full rigor-pass repository — findings, ratification records, and commit history — is preserved and available on request.
6. **Quote and consent discipline.** All public-figure quotes are verbatim from on-record sources, multi-source-confirmed where flagged, with courtesy-notification packets pre-staged for pre-publication send. Living private subjects are anonymized pending signed consent, with load-bearing detail preserved. One recorded scholarly interview is held entirely out of the text pending the subject's written approval.

## §2. What has NOT been externally verified

1. **The formal apparatus has had no external peer review.** The Residual Commons Value integrand and its adopted calibration; the Method-3 premium form (whose 5%/yr Hotelling anchor is a posited calibration parameter, labeled as such); the Hotelling Identity derivation; and the appendix's theorems and empirical observations have been stress-tested internally and computationally, not refereed.
2. **Inputs the corpus itself flags as derived or unconfirmed:** Deepwater Horizon's ~$4B private-gain denominator (a reserve-times-price derivation, not a reported figure); the $8–12B ongoing-Gulf-externalities estimate; Libby's ~$100M lifetime revenue (an industry estimate no primary source has confirmed — the stated ratio survives a doubling); the U.S. Steel Gary wind-down, framed "across the 1980s" pending a primary-source year.
3. **Figures that move on their own schedules:** the fund's dollar gloss (exchange-rate-dependent; re-stamped at send), carbon-pricing jurisdiction counts, and annual fishery survey indices.
4. **Work in flight, disclosed:** the methodology chapter (Ch 6) is being rewritten to match the appendix's current cross-check architecture (the numbers are settled; the chapter prose follows); the comparative-titles section's review-coverage claims await a web-verification pass; the print-render audit is pending offline.
