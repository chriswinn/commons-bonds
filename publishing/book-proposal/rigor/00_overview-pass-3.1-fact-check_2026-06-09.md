## HIGH-priority findings

### F-3.1-H1 — McDowell County population collapse: "a third" overstates the substrate

**Issue.** §00 prose (¶2 of "A note worth a name"): *"a coal company drew metallurgical seam out of your county for sixty years, the company left, and the county is now buying food stamps for a population a third of what it was."*

Ch 2 substrate establishes: *"a county of nearly 100,000 in 1950, under 20,000 by 2020."* Under 20K against nearly 100K is roughly a **fifth**, not a third. The "buying food stamps" framing also softens Ch 2's more precise *"the first food stamps in American history were issued there; the latest data suggests the county may need them again."*

**Options.**
- **(a) Track the substrate verbatim:** *"…the county now under twenty thousand people, down from a hundred thousand in 1950, where the first food stamps in American history were issued and where the latest data suggests they may be needed again."* (~28 words; more precise)
- **(b) Tighten + keep the rhythm:** *"…and the county is now a fifth of what it was, where the first food stamps in American history were issued and where the latest data suggests they may be needed again."* (~28 words; preserves the original rhythm)
- **(c) Strip to gestural:** *"…and the county is now a fraction of what it was."* (~10 words; sacrifices the food-stamps anchor)

**Recommendation: (b).** Tracks Ch 2 substrate precisely + preserves the original sentence's rhythm + keeps the "first food stamps in American history" anchor (one of the chapter's most resonant single facts). The "fifth" is mathematically defensible against any year-pair in the substrate; "third" is not.

**Reasoning.** Trade-press agents will sometimes spot-check named figures, and a population claim that misstates the substrate is exactly the kind of slip that costs credibility cheaply. Per `feedback_no_invented_factual_claims_in_publisher_facing_prose.md`, the hard rule applies; a wrong number is a fabrication of magnitude even if drafted in good faith. The two-character change (third → fifth) plus the substrate anchor is a low-cost upgrade.

### F-3.1-H2 — McDowell coal "sixty years" is over-precise against gestural substrate

**Issue.** §00 prose (same paragraph): *"a coal company drew metallurgical seam out of your county for sixty years…"*

Ch 2 substrate does not name "sixty years." It describes a multi-decade extraction era: "peak production through the war years, mechanization in waves, the U.S. Steel Gary closure dropping personal income by two-thirds in a single year." The McDowell coal-extraction era runs roughly the 1900s through the present. "Sixty years" is a specific number the substrate does not anchor.

**Options.**
- **(a) Strip to gestural:** *"for generations"* — matches substrate register
- **(b) Decade-range:** *"for the better part of a century"* — accurate; resonant
- **(c) Keep but soften:** *"for roughly sixty years"* — still introduces a specific number the substrate doesn't ground

**Recommendation: (b).** "For the better part of a century" is true (McDowell coal extraction spans roughly 1900-present with peak war-years to late-20th-century cycles), preserves rhythm, and avoids the false precision of "sixty." (a) also works if author prefers the lighter touch.

**Reasoning.** Same discipline as F-3.1-H1 — invented or over-precise numbers in publisher-facing prose are exactly what the no-invented-facts rule guards against. The §00 overview is the first thing an agent reads; the false-precision tic is what a sharp agent will flag and dock points for.

### F-3.1-H3 — "Two decades of healthcare-IT consulting at the World Bank and IMF scale" — overclaim risk

**Issue.** §00 prose (¶2 of "Why this author"): *"From there to two decades of healthcare-IT consulting at the World Bank and IMF scale, to the CEO seat of a healthcare-IT firm."*

Ch 1 substrate names the IMF and World Bank: *"He called me Monday morning. That was how I left the NIH. That was how the next decade began."* The substrate establishes that the author's post-NIH contracts BEGAN via IMF and World Bank introductions through one ex-NIH connection on a golf course. The substrate does NOT establish that the author worked AT or FOR the World Bank and IMF, nor that the consulting career operated continuously "at World Bank and IMF scale" for two decades.

The standing 03_author-platform bios use the safer framing: *"federal research and consulting, and the CEO seat of a healthcare-IT firm"* (medium bio).

**Options.**
- **(a) Align with bio framing:** *"From there to two decades of healthcare-IT consulting, federal research, and the CEO seat of a healthcare-IT firm."* — strips the WB/IMF mention; matches the standing bio register
- **(b) Anchor the substrate precisely:** *"From there to a consulting career that opened through World Bank and IMF engagements and ran for two decades, ending in the CEO seat of a healthcare-IT firm."* — keeps the WB/IMF anchor; describes it accurately as how the career opened
- **(c) Cite specific named contracts:** would require additional substrate the §00 doesn't carry

**Recommendation: (b).** Preserves the credibility signal (WB/IMF-scale work IS in the author's history) while describing it accurately rather than overclaiming. The two-decade duration is substrate-anchored.

**Reasoning.** Trade-press editors do background diligence on author claims, especially platform claims. "Consulting at the World Bank and IMF scale" reads as a strong career assertion that, if questioned, has to be defended with named contracts and dates. The substrate supports "engagements opened through" WB/IMF intros, not "operated at WB/IMF scale for two decades." This is one of the few claims in §00 where understating slightly is safer than the current overstate.

### F-3.1-H4 — Missing boundary-honoring move on coercion / Tubman territory

**Issue.** §00 prose lists "a body of labor" as one of the extracted-from substrates without naming the Ch 1 boundary that "a commute and a stolen life do not belong on the same line." Ch 5 ratified the coercion-deferral discipline explicitly: *"the direct pricing of the coercion vector itself…remains methodologically unresolved in the reparations-economics field, and it lies outside the empirical scope of this book. The framework reaches coerced cases through their legacy effects."*

A politically-attentive trade-press agent reading §00 without that boundary-honoring framing may worry that the framework flattens coerced labor into the same scalar pricing as mineral extraction — which is exactly what Ch 1's *"Naming the rhyme is not the same as making them equal"* guards against.

**Options.**
- **(a) Add a single sentence to the "What this book uniquely does" section:** *"The framework reaches the legacy effects of historical coercion — wealth gaps, longevity gaps, disrupted knowledge transmission — through the same matched-comparison discipline. It defers the direct pricing of coercion itself as an open methodological question, leaving the moral weight of that question to those who hold the standing to carry it."*
- **(b) Add a brief boundary-honoring beat to the "note worth a name" hook:** *"The pattern rhymes across a stunning range of cases, and a discipline of the book is that naming the rhyme is not the same as making the cases equal — coerced labor and a long commute do not share a line."*
- **(c) Combine (a) + (b) — both the hook beat AND the methodological deferral**
- **(d) Defer to §01 about-the-book** — let §01 carry the full boundary discipline; §00 stays at higher altitude

**Recommendation: (c).** Both moves are load-bearing. The hook beat guards against the flattening-misread early; the methodological-deferral sentence in "What this book uniquely does" lands the technical move that Ch 5 already ratified. Together they signal intellectual honesty + methodological rigor to exactly the kind of agent who will look for these tells.

**Reasoning.** Trade nonfiction proposals on adjacent territory (Coates, Darity & Mullen, the broader reparations conversation) get scrutinized for whether the author understands what the framework can and cannot price. The §00 currently signals the framework's REACH (universality across extraction substrates) without signaling its LIMITS (the deferred coercion vector). A reader from the reparations-economics neighborhood — including Darity himself, who the author is in active relationship with — would want to see the limit named. Per `feedback_named_subject_consent.md` discipline + the Darity & Mullen Tier-3 round-out decision (2026-06-03), the boundary-honoring move is essentially a credentialing signal for the Pipeline A audience.

### F-3.1-M3 — "He earned" → "salary and commission together touched" to match Ch 1's exact framing

**Issue.** §00 prose: *"He earned, in his best years, around four hundred thousand dollars at the ceiling — and even that ceiling would not have covered the year and more of surgery and rehabilitation life eventually required."*

Ch 1 substrate: *"In my best years, the peak of them, salary and commission together touched around four hundred thousand dollars. That was not a steady annual figure. It was the ceiling, the highest the earning ever reached, in the best years only."*

The Ch 1 substrate is careful: salary + commission together TOUCHED around $400K; not "earned $400K." This is the kind of compensation precision an agent recognizes — peak-year ceiling, not annualized income.

**Recommendation.** *"In his best years, salary and commission together touched around four hundred thousand dollars at the ceiling — and even that ceiling would not have covered the year and more of surgery and rehabilitation life eventually required."*

**Reasoning.** Same substrate-alignment discipline. Also: the more precise framing reads more credibly to a financially-literate reader (agents at the Wylie / Chalfant tier ARE financially literate). "Salary and commission touched $400K at the peak" is a different and more honest claim than "earned $400K."

### F-3.1-M4 — CBAM "fully operational in 2026" → "definitive period from 2026"

**Issue.** §00 prose: *"the European Union's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism — staged from 2023 and fully operational in 2026 — demonstrates that pricing a severed cost at the border is administratively feasible at scale."*

Public-record reality: CBAM's transitional period ran Oct 2023 – Dec 2025. The definitive period begins 1 January 2026 with phased implementation through 2034 (free EU ETS allowances decline over the window). So "staged from 2023" = accurate (transitional period); "fully operational in 2026" = slightly soft (definitive period begins 2026 but full strength is reached over the 2026-2034 phase-in).

**Recommendation.** *"the European Union's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism — in its transitional period from 2023 and entering its definitive phase in 2026 — demonstrates that pricing a severed cost at the border is administratively feasible at scale."*

**Reasoning.** The looser "fully operational" framing is the kind of soft-claim a climate-finance-attentive editor will flag. The tightened framing is just as evocative and more precise.

### F-3.1-M5 — "Gulf states" → "several U.S. states"

**Issue.** §00 prose: *"Sovereign-wealth instruments now exist in Alaska, in the Gulf states, and in a growing set of jurisdictions."*

"Gulf states" is gestural. Real US sovereign-wealth-adjacent instruments include: Alaska Permanent Fund (canonical); Texas Permanent School Fund + Texas Permanent University Fund + Texas Rainy Day Fund (oil-funded); Louisiana Education Quality Trust Fund; Alabama Trust Fund (gas royalties); Mississippi Permanent Trust Funds; Wyoming Permanent Mineral Trust Fund; North Dakota Legacy Fund; New Mexico Severance Tax Permanent Fund. Several Gulf states have these, but so do non-Gulf states.

**Recommendation.** *"Sovereign-wealth instruments now exist in Alaska, in several U.S. states funded by resource severance, and in a growing set of jurisdictions."*

**Reasoning.** "Several U.S. states funded by resource severance" is more precise (and stronger — the framework's vocabulary literally uses "severance") without overcommitting on specifics.
