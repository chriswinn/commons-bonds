## Findings

### F-3.2-1 — Single em-dash → period (salary-and-commission sentence)

**Location:** L65, "...salary and commission together touched around four hundred thousand dollars at the ceiling — and even that ceiling would not have covered..."

**Issue.** The em-dash splits a sentence where a period would land cleaner. The two halves are independent claims (one factual, one logical) and reading benefits from the breath.

**Fix.** Replace " — " with ". " and adjust capitalization.

### F-3.2-2 — Paragraph break in non-partisan paragraph

**Location:** L57. The non-partisan paragraph runs ~167 words and chains two distinct moves: (a) the AEI/Cato/Mercatus/GMU invitation; (b) the "not-for-capitalism / not-against-capitalism / mechanism-not-ownership-structure / vocabulary-adoption-success" claim.

**Fix.** Break paragraph after "...the two halves are stronger joined." Second paragraph begins "The framework is not an argument for capitalism..."

### F-3.2-3 — "world-class research" → "their research"

**Location:** L63 ("…the systems that took world-class research, ran it through peer review, and translated it…")

**Issue.** "World-class" is stock-adjective LLM tic. Ch 1 substrate uses simpler: "the things that took their research, ran it through peer review, and translated it for the people who needed it." Match Ch 1 substrate.

**Fix.** "world-class research" → "their research"

### F-3.2-4 — "a stunning range of cases" → "a striking range of cases"

**Location:** L21 ("The pattern rhymes across a stunning range of cases...")

**Issue.** "Stunning" is a generic intensifier; "striking" preserves the move's energy while dropping the stock-adjective register.

**Fix.** "a stunning range of cases" → "a striking range of cases"

### F-3.2-5 — "reserved against a depleted commons" → precision fix

**Location:** L34 ("...Norway's sovereign wealth fund, now exceeding $1.9 trillion in assets reserved against a depleted commons.")

**Issue.** "Depleted commons" overloads the phrasing — the GPFG is capital reserved from petroleum rents, compounded in global equities/bonds. "Reserved against a depleted commons" is poetic but slightly inaccurate to how the fund actually operates.

**Fix.** "in assets reserved against a depleted commons" → "in assets reserved from the country's oil rents"

### F-3.2-6 — Article inconsistency in convergence table

**Location:** L39 ("McDowell at five to a hundred-and-thirty-three times the price, and at nineteen to forty-seven times by an independent method. Libby at fifty-five to eighty-two times, and at forty to eighty by the independent method.")

**Issue.** "an independent method" → "the independent method" — definite article in second instance breaks the parallelism (definite article implies "the one we just named," which is wrong because each is its own method).

**Fix.** "by the independent method" → "by an independent method"
