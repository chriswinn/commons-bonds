## Findings

### F-3.3-1 — "essays at editor-review or placed" overclaim

**Location:** L85, "the author bio and platform stack, including the essays at editor-review or placed across the *Boston Review*, *Phenomenal World*, Aeon, Noema, and the Atlantic Ideas pipeline (§03)"

**Issue.** "Or placed" invites the reader to look for evidence of placement and find none yet. No essays have placed as of 2026-06-09. The "or" phrasing is technically defensible (it allows for either state) but reads as overclaim-shaped to a sharp agent's eye.

**Fix.** Drop "or placed" until placements land. "the author bio and platform stack, including the essays at editor-review across the *Boston Review* and *Phenomenal World*, with additional submissions at Aeon, Noema, and Atlantic Ideas (§03)"

---

### F-3.3-2 — "largest intergenerational obligation in human history" Social Security overclaim

**Location:** L37, "Social Security (the largest intergenerational obligation in human history, severed in the decades the actuaries had told us it would be severed in)"

**Issue.** Ch 5 substrate does NOT support "largest in human history." Ch 5 specifically debunks the $108-trillion figure that was the basis for "largest in human history" claims and rebuilds the honest version as "a few trillion dollars, not a hundred trillion." The actuarial shortfall is on the order of $22.6 trillion — large, but not framed by Ch 5 as "largest in history."

This is exactly the kind of overclaim B4 (AEI/Cato-attuned reader) is trained to spot — and an agent recovers it cheaply by Googling "Social Security largest intergenerational obligation in human history" and finding no rigorous source.

**Fix.** Drop the overclaim. "Social Security (severed in the decades the actuaries had told us it would be severed in)" — accurate, tight, lets the timing-deferral move carry the weight.

---

### F-3.3-3 — "several orders of magnitude greater" overclaim

**Location:** L39, "the floor for the gap between what extraction has cost and what has been paid is several orders of magnitude greater than the figures normally cited"

**Issue.** "Several orders of magnitude" technical-speak = 3+ OOM = 1000x or more. Ch 6 + Ch 8 substrate supports McDowell at 5-133x (under 1 OOM to ~2 OOM) and Libby at 40-80x (~1.6-1.9 OOM). The actual range is "one to two orders of magnitude," not "several."

B4-type readers will spot this. The fix is rhetorical-and-accurate.

**Fix.** "an order of magnitude or more greater" — technically defensible against Ch 6 + Ch 8 substrate; preserves the move's force without overclaim.

---

### F-3.3-4 — Pistor FT Best Books designation unverified

**Location:** L75, "*The Code of Capital* won an FT Best Books designation."

**Issue.** Per `02_comp-titles.md` matrix, the FT Best Books 2019 designation for Pistor is flagged `[verify]` and has NOT been verified. Asserting an unverified award is exactly the credentialing-claim a sharp agent would Google. If false, costs credibility.

**Fix.** Replace with substrate-anchored framing. "*The Code of Capital* has shaped the legal-academic and economic-sociology conversation on how capital is constructed." (Substantive without depending on an unverified award.)
