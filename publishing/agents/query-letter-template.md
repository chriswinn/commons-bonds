# Query Letter Template

**Date drafted:** 2026-05-06  ·  **Date modified:** 2026-05-06

**Status.** Skeleton — finalize by Sept–Oct 2026 per cascade plan.

**Purpose.** A reusable query-letter spine that agent-specific personalization (in `personalization-snippets.md`) plugs into. Single-page email; opens cold; closes with an explicit next-step ask.

## Structure

### Subject line
```
Query: Commons Bonds — [hook fragment, ~5–8 words]
```

### Paragraph 1 — Personalized hook (2–3 sentences)
- Why this agent specifically (referencing a recent deal, a comp-title client, or an essay/interview they've given).
- Don't fake familiarity. If there's no genuine tie, say so plainly: "I'm querying you because [specific public reason]."

### Paragraph 2 — The book (4–6 sentences)
- Title. One-sentence thesis.
- The framework's two core concepts (Cost Severance + Residual Commons Value) named in plain language.
- The book's reach across extraction domains (one-clause list).
- The voice signal: lived-experience-from-inside, not ivory-tower critique.

### Paragraph 3 — Comps + market (2–3 sentences)
- Two comps from `02_comp-titles.md` (lead with Mazzucato + Pistor).
- One sentence on why now.

### Paragraph 4 — Author + platform (2–3 sentences)
- Honest version. Outsider-credibility framing.
- Placed essays at the time of query (named with venue + status).
- Interviews recorded with named subjects (where confirmed for use).

### Paragraph 5 — Close (2 sentences)
- Materials available: full proposal + two sample chapters.
- Explicit ask: "May I send the proposal?"

### Signature
Chris Winn  ·  [contact]  ·  [single relevant link]

## Length discipline

≤ 400 words. One screen. No attachments unless the agent's submission guidelines explicitly invite them.

## What not to do

- Don't open with a quote or rhetorical question.
- Don't list every chapter.
- Don't mention AI-collaboration in the query — reserve for the proposal's overview / author's note. (Disclosure goes in the proposal, not the cold query.)
- Don't query simultaneous unless the agent's guidelines explicitly allow it. Track per-agent in the tracker.
