# The American Prospect — submission norms

> **Firewall (§6.1):** drafter-safe block may go to a generation prompt; the editorial-only block (max + calibration band) is for Cut + Stage 5 only.

## Drafter-safe block

- **What it is:** long-running American **liberal/progressive political-economy** magazine. Publishes **features, investigations, op-eds, and news analyses** across domestic and international **economic, political, and cultural** issues, from emerging and seasoned journalists, academics, and newsmakers.
- **Editorial brain:** rewards **political-economy argument with a clear who-pays / who-benefits / who-is-accountable spine**, tied to **the current political moment**, engaging contemporary debates by name. Accountability-journalism sensibility. Left-of-center, but a non-naive, institutionally-grounded argument reads as a strength, not a liability.
- **"Current political moment" is an explicit ask:** AP's pitch guidance says to explain "how it fits into the current political moment." A timeless argument needs a live peg surfaced explicitly (for $100 Barrel: the energy-transition extraction frontier — critical minerals, deep-sea mining — financed on the same blank-column accounting).
- **Dispositive audience (carry-forward from the $100 Barrel Stage-0 + PW norms):** the **center-right policy reader remains a Tier-1 non-partisan-framing test** for THIS essay — the "architecture, not a political program" close must hold. For an AP audience the disarming has lower marginal value than at PW/Boston Review (the reader leans the essay's way), so lead with the accountability/severance angle and keep the non-partisan framing as a credibility signal rather than the headline move.
- **Format:** **pitch via Submittable** ("Editorial Submissions" form), NOT emailed proposal. A "dynamic, well-developed pitch that outlines your story; why it would be compelling for readers; and how it fits into the current political moment."
- **Exclusivity:** **NO simultaneous submissions** — must be unique to the Prospect and not previously published. (Clears only after any prior venue has declined.)
- **Time-sensitivity:** flag in the subject line ONLY if genuinely time-sensitive. Do not manufacture urgency.

## Editorial-only block (Cut + Stage 5 only)

- **No published hard word limit;** word count + fee are set in the contract AFTER a pitch is accepted ("they will send you a contract that outlines the specific parameters... including word count and fee"). The $100 Barrel project target (~4,200w as drafted) is within AP's long-form range.
- **Calibration band:** to establish once we have a published-length AP sample; for now the existing essay length is the working target.
