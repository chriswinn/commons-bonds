# Boston Review — submission norms

> **Contamination firewall (§6.1):** the **drafter-safe block** may be surfaced to a generation prompt; the **editorial-only block** (which holds the maximum + calibration band) is consulted by the Cut phase + Stage 5 only, and is NEVER injected into a generation prompt. A maximum must not reach the drafter.

## Drafter-safe block

- **Editorial brain:** Boston Review publishes **long-form argumentative essays**, substantial/ambitious **review essays**, and political/social/cultural analysis. It rewards a constructive argument that goes *beyond diagnosis* (this is why the brief authorized naming the framework's bond apparatus for this venue). Civic-republican / democratic-theory register lands well (Cohen/Chasman lineage).
- **Does NOT accept:** unsolicited **memoirs or personal essays**; op-eds or op-ed-style pieces; previously published work (including blogs/Substack). *(Corpus implication: a memoir-register piece — e.g., the Noema essay — does NOT fit BR; an argumentative essay does.)*
- **Format:** complete draft OR pitch. Complete draft: include a short summary/description in the cover letter. Pitch: ~one single-spaced page, a mini-prospectus showing how you will develop the argument (not just a list of questions).
- **Minimum floor:** nothing under **2,000 words** is considered. Aim for the published sweet spot (see note); the generation floor for our work is ~2,500w.

## Editorial-only block (Cut + Stage 5 only — do NOT put in a generation prompt)

- **Stated submission range:** **2,000–6,000 words.** **6,000 is the hard maximum.**
- **Calibration band:** *most published BR essays run **2,500–4,000 words**.* Our framework pieces tend to run longer (the iterated accountability-gap essay was ~4,853w — above the typical published range; the new-pipeline cut/focus base is ~4,458w — closer to the band, well under the 6,000 max). **Target ~3,500–4,500w**; treat anything ≥4,800w as a flag to cut toward the band.
