# Academic-Trade Press Editor Targets — Pipeline B

**Date drafted:** 2026-06-03  ·  **Date modified:** 2026-06-15  ·  **Status.** Exhaustive lane research sweep RATIFIED 2026-06-03 per Pipeline A + B exhaustive lane research chip; supersedes the prior tier-bound Pipeline B chip with count cap (~10-15). 73 verified editor rows across 16 academic-trade presses + 1 trade-publisher hybrid (Bloomsbury Academic). **Per-press submission routes (editor emails / proposal pages) verified + filled 2026-06-15 — see §Submission protocol per press.**

**Purpose.** Per cascade-plan v2 §11, Pipeline B = academic-trade direct-to-editor queries, parallel-Plan-A territory (NOT Plan-B fallback). Half of the closest comps (Pistor / Patel & Moore / Sassen / Rodrik) deliberately chose academic-trade routes. Pipeline B optimizes against FPD v1.0.0 success criterion 4 (vocabulary adoption at scale in legal, regulatory, advocacy, and academic contexts) — the financial-advance + general-retail metrics that conventionally favor full trade are not the load-bearing criteria for a framework book.

**First Priority B target:** Duke University Press (register-perfect fit per Robinson / Hall / Sassen / Wendy Brown / Fred Moten lineage). Joe Jackson (Princeton) is the dominant single-editor target across the entire list (acquired Rodrik + Pistor).

- Press editor pages (e.g., dukeupress.edu/About-Us, press.princeton.edu/our-team, hup.harvard.edu, mitpress.mit.edu, ucpress.edu, yalebooks.yale.edu/about-us/editors).

- LinkedIn current-employment verification.
- Industry coverage (Publishers Weekly, Publishing Trends, Chronicle of Higher Education, Inside Higher Ed, The Bookseller, Shelf Awareness, Manuscript Works newsletter).

**UNC submission discipline (lead REVISED 2026-06-11 → Kinney, author-ratified).** Standard single-editor query. **Recommended lead = Kinney (#75)** — the trade-imprint/form-match. Rationale: the book is a cross-domain big-idea trade title whose thesis is breadth ("a mechanism demonstrated across cases"), not membership in any single field; the reparations-economics lineage is one chapter's engagement (Ch 5), not the spine. The fit rule: a *subject* editor is right when a book sits squarely in one field; a *big-idea/trade* editor is right when the book's defining feature is that it crosses fields. This book is the second kind by design, and Kinney runs the imprint built for it ("books about America and the world" — US cases in a global frame). The earlier "lead = Durante" call (subject-thread match to the African American studies layer) is **walked back**: matching the editor to the reparations thread risks the press mis-positioning the book as a reparations title and underselling its breadth. **Durante (#74) holds two real secondary roles:** (a) the alternative lead *if* a given pitch deliberately leads with the racial-extraction / reparations-economics framing; (b) as Editorial Director, a legitimate "unsure of the right in-house home" routing address. No econ-subject editor exists at UNC to default to — which is the whole reason this is a form-match decision rather than a subject-match one (contrast HUP/Tovbis #10 + UC Press/Lipinski #17, where a real econ/comp-acquiring subject editor exists).

## Submission protocol per press

**Verified editor-direct routes added 2026-06-15** (3 parallel research agents; every
address checked against the press's own site where reachable; unverifiable local-parts
flagged, not fabricated). General rule across academic presses: **query ONE acquiring
editor; do not cross-submit** — editors route internally. None of these presses uses a
"submit-everything" online portal for monographs (the exceptions are Cambridge/Oxford
"find an editor" directories and Duke's form).

- **Duke UP:** Single-editor query via Book Proposal Submission Form. Do NOT cross-submit. If misfit, editor will internally route.
- **Princeton UP:** Email proposal directly to subject editor (econ: Joe Jackson `Joe_Jackson@press.princeton.edu`). Full proposal per [PUP Proposal Guidelines](https://assets.press.princeton.edu/authors/PUP-PROPOSAL-GUIDELINES.pdf).
- **Harvard UP:** Email to relevant acquisitions editor OR submissions_hup@harvard.edu. Full proposal required. Does NOT accept first-time queries via referral-only.
