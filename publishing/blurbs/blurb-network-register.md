## §0. Why this register exists

Trade nonfiction launch economics depend materially on:

1. **Cover blurbs (4–8 typical)** — short endorsement quotes printed on back cover + marketing copy. Influence acquisitions-editor confidence during proposal evaluation, bookstore-buyer ordering, library acquisitions, syllabi adoption, and assigned-reviewer engagement at launch. Trade-nonfiction blurbs cluster from established voices in adjacent fields whose endorsement signals the book is in serious dialog.
2. **Foreword (0 or 1)** — a 5–10-page introductory essay (~1500–5000w) written by a senior figure, published inside the front matter. Higher commitment + higher prestige than a blurb. Common for framework-extending or lineage-claiming trade nonfiction. Decided early in the contract-to-production cycle.
3. **Marketing-paragraph endorsements** — used in agent pitch letters, proposal §02, publisher pitch decks, pre-launch outreach. Sometimes pre-contract warm-asks ("would you read the proposal?") generate marketing-paragraph-worthy responses without committing the endorser to a printed blurb.

Without a register tracking who/why/status/path, blurb planning typically happens late + ad-hoc post-contract, and high-leverage warm paths (interviewed scholars; comp-title lineage authors; mutual-network connections) get under-utilized.

## §1. Trigger framework — when to fire which kind of ask

Different ask types have different firing moments. PM-session gated task (cross-thread-todos #20) fires the right sequence at the right moment.

| Ask type | Trigger moment | What's asked | Author commitment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Marketing-paragraph endorsement** (pre-contract; rare; only warmest paths) | After agent signs but before proposal goes to publishers — typically late Jul / Aug / Sep 2026 | "Would you be willing to look at the proposal and, if it resonates, contribute a paragraph for the agent's pitch?" | Low — agent's discretion to incorporate response |
| **Foreword commitment** (pre-contract OR post-contract early) | Pre-contract: if a high-prestige path is warm enough to ask before proposal goes out (~Aug 2026), agent inquires "is X willing to consider writing a foreword if the proposal lands?" Post-contract: at trade-publisher contract signing or shortly after; publisher's editor typically coordinates | "Would you write a foreword (~1500–5000w) for the finished book?" | Material — represents ~3–6 months of relationship management + draft review + back-and-forth + production schedule integration |
| **Pre-publication blurb** (most common) | After trade-publisher contract signs + advance galleys ready (typically 4–6 months before pub date) | "Would you read the advance copy and consider providing a short endorsement quote (~50–150 words) for the back cover + marketing copy?" | Moderate — galley + reading time + drafting + agreed wording |
| **Post-publication endorsement** (occasional) | Post-launch | Review-essays + podcast appearances + public conversations that operate as continuing endorsement | Variable; usually folds into post-publication marketing |

### Trigger discipline

- **Pre-Wave-1 (now → late Jul 2026):** NO blurb requests. Decision: any **forewords** to inquire about during proposal-pitch phase? Any **marketing-paragraph** warm-asks worth pursuing? PM surfaces these decisions at trigger time per §3 below.
- **At agent-signs (target Aug 2026):** PM fires marketing-paragraph warm-asks for 1–3 highest-prestige + warmest-path candidates if agent's strategy supports it. Foreword inquiries similarly if appropriate.
- **At trade-publisher-contract-signs (target 2026–2027 per cascade plan v2):** PM fires the **full blurb + foreword request process**. Galleys go out to blurbers ~4–6 months before pub date via publisher's marketing team (typically) + author's network (in parallel).
- **At ~6 months pre-pub:** blurb collection in flight. PM tracks status per candidate; collects + confirms quotes; coordinates with publisher.

## §3. Decisions PM surfaces at trigger time

When PM-session fires the gated task (cross-thread-todos #20), surface these decisions to author per trigger phase:

### Phase 3a — At agent signs (~Aug 2026)

1. **Foreword strategy:** is a foreword pursued at all? If yes, who is primary candidate (PM-recommended: Sandel OR Mazzucato OR Darity) + who is secondary fallback?
2. **Pre-proposal warm-ask:** does agent want any candidate-paragraph-endorsements for the proposal pitch deck? If yes, which 1–3 warmest-path candidates (PM-recommended: Darity is highest-probability ask; Mazzucato post-NYRB-essay-publication if timing aligns).

### Phase 3b — At trade-publisher contract signs

3. **Foreword commitment fired (if Phase 3a affirmative).** Letter goes to primary foreword candidate; secondary fallback prepared.
4. **Blurb-candidate prioritization.** From the §2 roster, rank-order the asks. PM-recommended priority ordering:
   - Tier 1 first asks: Darity (warmest path) + Pistor + Christophers + Susskind
   - Tier 2 second asks: Sandel + Tooze + Mazzucato (post-NYRB-essay-publication only)
   - Tier 3 third asks: Boyce + Patel & Moore + Mullen + Kendi
5. **Galley-distribution coordination** with publisher's marketing team — who sends to which candidate; per-candidate timing.

### Phase 3c — Throughout galley window (~4–6 months pre-pub)

6. **Per-candidate status tracking** — update §2 status column as responses land.
7. **Blurb-quote collection + agreed wording** — confirm phrasing with each `ENDORSED` candidate.
8. **Marketing-copy assembly** — feed confirmed quotes to publisher's marketing team + book cover designer + agent's launch materials.
