### §1.2 F-FC-VDp-M1 — Dunbar publication-year precision (MEDIUM)

**Severity:** MEDIUM.

**Location:** Line 207 (V-D-prime body) §Close paragraph — "*We wear the mask that grins and lies, / It hides our cheeks and shades our eyes* — a poem published in 1896, more than a century before the framework I am proposing existed in any form."

**Diagnosis.** "We Wear the Mask" was **first published October 1895** in Dunbar's self-published *Majors and Minors* (500 copies, limited circulation, primarily Ohio-region). The poem reached significant audience in 1896 via *Lyrics of Lowly Life* with William Dean Howells's influential introduction. Popular references frequently attribute "1896" because that's the publication that put Dunbar on the national map; strict scholarship attributes "1895" as the first-publication year.

This is the same class of fact-precision finding as F-FC-Noema-1 RATIFIED 2026-05-21 (Norway fiscal rule 2001 adoption at 4%, lowered to 3% in 2017; locked cut's earlier "approximately three percent" conflated dates). The cadence-load on V-D-prime's sentence is "more than a century before" — both years are >130 years before 2026, so the cadence-load doesn't depend on the year-precision. But Black-Studies-resonance reader (T3.9) + Berggruen-adjacent editorial brain (T1.1) reading at high attention may pull the year.

**Options:**

- **A (recommended).** Revise "1896" to "1895" (strict first-publication date). One-character swap; preserves cadence; preserves the "more than a century before" frame; closes the precision concern.

  Verbatim revision: "*— a poem published in 1895, more than a century before the framework I am proposing existed in any form.*"

- **B.** Revise to "1895–96" to acknowledge both publication contexts.

  Trade-off: more pedantic; cadence-mild-cost; possibly confusing to general-intellectual reader expecting clean year.

- **C.** Hold "1896" + add Stage 5 pre-pub-queue acknowledgment.

  Defensible (the year IS what most popular references say) but technically imprecise.

- **D.** Hold "1896" silently.

  Not recommended at no-cost-pressure framing; the precision is available.

**Recommendation:** **A.**

**Reasoning.** The Pass 3.1 fact-check discipline at locked-cut treated Norway-style year-precision findings as RATIFIED + APPLIED corrections (F-FC-Noema-1 4%→3% precision; F-FC-Noema-2 Mullen folklorist; F-FC-Noema-3 Hamilton lead-author). Symmetric discipline applied here would route to "1895" since that IS the first-publication year. Cost of correction is one character; benefit is fact-precision symmetric with locked-cut Pass 3.1 RATIFIED discipline.

**Cross-pass impact:** Pass 3.3 — Black-Studies-resonance T3.9 reader at high-attention literary register may register the precision (improvement). Pass 3.4 — A7 reactionary intellectual reader may not engage (no procedural surface). Pass 3.5 — no cadence-arc impact; one-character swap.
