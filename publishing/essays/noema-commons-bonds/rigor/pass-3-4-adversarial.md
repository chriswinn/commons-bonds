## §4. Per-character adversarial verdicts

### A1. Public-choice theorist (Buchanan/Tullock)

**Reads for:** rent-seeking analysis; whether architectures are treated as exogenous (essay-as-naive) or as endogenous outputs of rent-seeking actors (essay-as-rigorous); whether the framework engages bargaining-game-theoretic accounts of institutional formation.

**What this reader pulls.**

- **Architecture-as-exogenous flag.** The essay treats architectures as if they're outputs of design-choices ("It is an outcome of architectures, and architectures are made by people, and what people make they can also remake" — line 93). Public Choice reads this as eliding the prior question: who shapes the architecture, with what incentives, and under what institutional constraints on their rent-seeking? Mondragon's 3:1 vs Fortune 500's 300:1 ratio is documented; Public Choice asks how the 300:1 ratio is maintained — C-suite capture of compensation-committee architecture; cross-board interlocks; institutional rent-extraction enabled by specific corporate-governance design choices.
- **Norway treatment elides political-economy question.** The 2001 fiscal rule is named as discipline-held-by-every-government; Public Choice asks WHO benefits from the rule's discipline + what interests organized around its maintenance. The intergenerational-saving discipline of GPFG is itself a political-economy outcome of specific Norwegian interests (export-industry stabilization; oil-revenue insulation from electoral cycles; sovereign-financial-power projection). Treating it as architecture-redesign without naming the rent-seeking actors who shaped it reads as politically-naive.
- **Pacemaker case (line 119) implies architecture-naive framing.** Public Choice asks WHO shaped the legal-and-healthcare architecture that creates the $6K/$86 binary — medical device manufacturers + AMA + Medicare reimbursement-rate-setting + Congress have specific shaping interests + specific rent-extraction patterns. The essay's architecture-surrounds-the-clinical-decision framing implies a passive architecture; Public Choice would name active rent-extraction.
- **Cost-severance framework implicitly assumes good-faith architecture-redesign is possible.** Public Choice asks: even if the architecture is reducible, the redesign process is itself subject to rent-seeking capture. Honest accounting requires accounting for the rent-seeking incentives in the architecture-redesign process itself.

**Verdict: ⚠ EXCLUDE.** Public Choice critique is intellectually-serious; the chapter's no-villain / architecture-not-blame framing earns acceptance-audience good-faith but doesn't disarm the rent-seeking critique. The reader engages substantively rather than ideologically — this is the most intellectually-serious adversarial thread.

**Type of thread:** **load-bearing essay claim** (architecture-not-blame is doing major audience-management work for several Tier-1/2 acceptance audiences — anti-capital / left-policy / structural-critique / civic-republican / framework-skeptic). Cross-chapter workstream RESOLVED at book-level per Ch 1 REAUDIT §5.3 disposition (Ch 5 + Ch 9 + Tech Appendix + Ch 8 + Bibliography §22). For the Noema essay specifically — the essay is Ch 1-derivative-content per Stage 1 §6 Path B preemptive policy; the book-side rent-seeking-engagement covers the book-level adversarial-engagement; essay-scope cannot resolve at chapter-depth.
