## §0. Why this comparison matters now

Two parallel-draft chips spawned 2026-05-28 produced alt variants of the Noema submission cut: one with the em-dash treated as not-part-of-English (Alt-No-EM); one with em-dash treated as a flag requiring per-site justification (Alt-Norm-Punct). The first tests structural integrity under a forced punctuation constraint; the second tests whether disciplined calibration produces a meaningfully distinct draft.

This artifact scores all three variants against the Stage 1 brief §1 14-character acceptance set + 8-character adversarial set + reading-smoothness + book-success-criteria alignment + Noema publishability + standalone-strength axes, then recommends disposition. Per the Aeon precedent (V-C / Alt-No-EM / Alt-Norm-Punct / V-D hybrid comparison 2026-05-28), the comparison-artifact format is fresh-session-from-first-principles scoring with explicit Δ from the locked cut's RATIFIED baseline where applicable.

**Three structural deviations to flag at top so they're visible across §1–§7:**

1. **Alt-No-EM rewrites the Stage 1 §7.1 locked Section I closing paragraph.** Stage 1 brief §6 prescribes this paragraph as verbatim-required; Alt-No-EM breaks the em-dash pair around the routines-list into separate sentences. This is the experimental difference the variant deliberately imposed — the trade-off is the em-dash-discipline-cleanliness vs the brief's explicit verbatim-lock. (This is a Pass-3.3-Tier-1-relevant deviation, not just a punctuation choice.)
2. **Alt-Norm-Punct adds substantive citations** (Dunbar couplet PD; Du Bois "unasked question" PD; Ellison fair-use phrase; Ostrom *Governing the Commons* 1990 citation at Fox Hill). These uplift character #9 (Black-lineage reader) and character #7 (Ostrom-successor); they are NOT em-dash-discipline gains — they are independent content additions the drafter bundled into the alt. The alt's self-audit (frontmatter + Phase E note) is explicit that the em-dash calibration produced essentially no density change vs the locked cut.
3. **Alt-Norm-Punct renames §II "Pooh" → "Pou".** The locked cut's §II title is the grandfather's name (Pooh); the section opens with the grandfather and pivots to the grandson-mispronunciation passing the name forward as "Pou." Renaming the section "Pou" inverts the opening — the section is structurally about Pooh (the grandfather is the lineage anchor; the patents; the air-compressor scene); naming it "Pou" reads as front-loading the father's name and demoting the grandfather to setup. This is a structural-narrative deviation.

These three deviations are weighted in §1 (acceptance) + §3 (smoothness) + §5 (publishability) verdicts.
