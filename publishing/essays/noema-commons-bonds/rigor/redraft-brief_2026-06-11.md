## §4. LITERATURE POSITIONING (PRIMARY success criterion)

The redraft's first job: a Noema reader finishes knowing **where this book sits on the shelf** — what it stands with, what it pushes against, and what it adds that was not there before. Minimum **five named works/authors engaged in prose** with an explicit support / contradict / extend relation each. Engagement means an argumentative move the essay actually performs, not a name-drop; no academic apparatus (no parenthetical cite-stacking — titles + years woven into sentences, Noema register). Every positioning move obeys the §3 placement rule: the lived material earns the literature.

**Core five (required):**

1. **Mariana Mazzucato — *The Value of Everything* (2018).** SUPPORTS the book's diagnosis: the economy conflates value creation with value extraction (financier-fee and Daraprim cases live here). The book EXTENDS her: *Mazzucato names the extraction; cost severance adds the accounting* — the per-architecture ledger of who captured, who absorbed, and whether the absorber can bill back. (The locked cut's positioning sentence survives in substance; rephrase without self-narration per §6 — name the framework's relation directly, not "the framework I am proposing.")
2. **Elinor Ostrom — *Governing the Commons* (1990).** SUPPORTS: commons governance below and before formal institutions is real, durable, documented — the Fox Hill mutual-aid arrangement is an Ostrom case operating in the wild. EXTENDS: Ostrom documented how commons are governed; cost severance prices what happens when the governing arrangement is overridden or priced out — the ledger for the failure case her design principles guard against. Placement: after the title-earning sentence (the SF-VD-1 rule).
3. **Karl Polanyi (*The Great Transformation*, 1944) — with the lineage's living edge in Nancy Fraser (*Cannibal Capitalism*, 2022).** SUPPORTS: markets treat land, labor, and the social fabric as free inputs (Polanyi's fictitious commodities; Fraser's cannibalized background conditions) — cost severance is that insight wearing an accountant's eyeshade. CONTRADICTS on the remedy: the Polanyi lineage's instinct is to re-embed and decommodify, to pull the commons OUT of the price system; the book argues the unpriced commons has not been protected by its pricelessness — the cost lands on someone either way, and the honest move is to put both sides of the ledger on the same page.
4. **Michael Sandel — *What Money Can't Buy* (2012).** CONTRADICTS (the sharpest engagement; see §8 call #5 for pitch): Sandel argues pricing corrupts the goods it touches and some things must not be priced. The book answers: refusal-to-price has not kept the market out; it has only kept the cost invisible and the cost-bearer uncompensated. The watermen's catch was priced out whether or not anyone priced the water; the question is not whether the commons enters the ledger, it is whether it enters honestly. SUPPORTS his deeper diagnosis (market reasoning crowding out moral reasoning) even while inverting his conclusion.
5. **William Darity Jr. & A. Kirsten Mullen — *From Here to Equality* (2020; updated reckoning 2nd ed./JEP 2022 for the $14T).** SUPPORTS: theirs is the worked example of an honest cost-side ledger read across centuries. EXTENDS: the book generalizes the ledger discipline to any architecture — while the author, first-person, declines to fill the coercion column himself (the abstention discipline, §2.2, must be legible in the prose, not just absent-by-omission).
