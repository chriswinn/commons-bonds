## §2. Editorial-brain map for The Atlantic main magazine specifically

**What Atlantic main-magazine editors want (distinct from Atlantic Ideas, Harper's, Boston Review, Noema, Aeon):**

- **Scene-led opening that earns the essay's length within the first 700–1,000 words.** Atlantic features begin in scene + place. *"The harbor at Old Point Comfort wakes a long time before the city does..."* (Ch 3 line 10 — already this register at chapter scope, available verbatim-or-near-verbatim if a §I open uses the existing chapter opener as canonical scene-anchor, subject to Stage 2 audience-blind Path B discipline — see §6 below). Atlantic reader sits with a long essay if the opening scene + voice + early-paragraph density of detail signal that the reading time will be earned.

- **Cumulative-portrait architecture.** The argument emerges from the cumulative voices + cumulative cases + cumulative observation, not from declarative-thesis-and-defend architecture. This is the load-bearing register-differentiation from Atlantic Ideas (which DOES use declarative-thesis architecture per the Ch 9 brief). Coates *Case for Reparations* (2014) is the canonical Atlantic-features model: the historical-economic argument unfolds via Clyde Ross's biography + the Contract Buyers League + the redlining maps + the wealth-deficit accounting + the international precedents (German + Holocaust survivor compensation) — the argument is cumulative; the case-set IS the argument.

- **Named-source density at journalistic-record standard.** Every named voice attributed to a specific public-record source (cited interview; cited testimony; cited public statement; cited photographed-and-named appearance). The chapter already meets this standard (Hudgins / Colden / Goldsborough / Nesslage / Latour / O'Connor / Eskridge / Cannon / Whewell / Fisher / Schwartz / Tarnowski / Ruth / Evans / Harris — 15+ named voices, all public-record-attributed); the essay must preserve the standard at essay scope.

- **American-place authority earned in the prose.** The writer's claim to the place must be substantively earned, not asserted. Ch 3 already does this work: "I grew up on this bay" + "My father, who is from Fox Hill" + "I have watched the green of the water change" + "I have lived near this water long enough to have known three rough generations of the men who work it." The essay carries this lineage forward without inflation. Author bona fides: lived near the water + family connection to the working community + multi-generational observational record from inside the relationship.

- **Refusal of partisan-brief register.** The Atlantic is center-left but consistently publishes long-form reportage that refuses partisan-brief framing entirely. The chapter's Mechanism-Not-Motive register (already structurally present at Ch 3 line 224: "Each of them has been given an enemy who is, in fact, the other one. The system has been extracting from them both...") is the load-bearing non-partisan structural move. Preserve + intensify in the essay's closing register.

- **Apparatus drops at the ~10–13% effective reveal level per Stage 0 §3.1.** This is the lightest apparatus reveal across all four Wave 2 candidates; the essay is structurally well-positioned for clean register. Cost severance can surface in plain prose ("the cost that gets paid by someone else" / "the difference is absorbed somewhere") without naming the vocabulary as a term-of-art. No formal apparatus (no CS = RCV − B equation; no bond decomposition; no Three Ways of Counting; no CIT; no Four Gates; no Cᵢ vocabulary; no ARR; no named cost-component proper nouns). See §8 below for the full apparatus exclusion list.

- **First-person frame with reportage spine.** The Atlantic features tradition tolerates first-person grounding in reportage (the writer's presence orients the reader; the named voices do the analytical work). Ch 3 already does this hybrid: first-person observational frame ("I am writing from Hampton" / "I have watched..." / "I went back to McDowell County, in my mind, while I was finishing this chapter") + multi-voice reportage spine + structural-argumentative close. The essay inherits this voice register. **NOT memoir register** (which would shift the center of gravity to the writer's own biography rather than to the working community + the cumulative-portrait architecture).

- **Length discipline.** Target ~7,000–9,000w body in the middle of the Atlantic features band per Stage 0 §4.2 Q2 (6,000–10,000w preliminary target); substance drives length per [`tools/memory/feedback_substance_drives_length.md`](../memory/feedback_substance_drives_length.md). Atlantic features routinely run 8,000–15,000w on single-subject deep reportage (Coates 2014 = ~15,000w; recent Atlantic environmental long-form ranges 6,000–12,000w). The 6,000–10,000w preliminary target is conservative; substance may earn longer.

**What hurts Atlantic main-magazine submissions specifically:**

- **Argument/analysis/commentary register confused with reportage register.** This is the Atlantic Ideas register, not Atlantic features. Declarative thesis sentences in the opening paragraph + numbered-steps mechanism + comparative-historical anchoring is the Ideas play. Atlantic features open in scene + place + voice; let the argument surface cumulatively.

- **Outsider-documentary register on a working community.** Atlantic features reader is sensitive to the difference between insider witness + outsider documentary. The chapter's existing authorial-positioning (I grew up here + my father is from this community + my family is one generation removed from the working life + zero generations removed from the community) is the right register; do not let the essay drift toward generic-observer-on-a-fishing-community framing.

- **Apparatus jargon, Greek letters, named instruments.** Atlantic feature reader bails immediately.

- **Op-ed throat-clearing.** "In recent decades, the question of how we account for the costs of fishery decline has become..." Atlantic features do not open this way. Begin in scene.

- **Manifesto register.** "We must..." / "It is time to..." / "The future demands..." The Mechanism-Not-Motive register at Ch 3 lines 222–227 is the antidote — already structurally present in the chapter at essay-ready quality.

- **Defensive author register / preemptive-rebuttal sections.** Atlantic features trust the reader; objection-handling happens briefly inside the prose's natural argumentative motion.

### 7.1 Historical Chesapeake oyster fishery

- **Hampton oyster-packing operation:** J.S. Darling and Son took that name in **1884**, when Frank Darling joined his father's existing oyster business. At its peak, **160 shuckers** handled **200,000 bushels** of Chesapeake oysters every year. Oyster shell mountains rose **more than four stories high**. The Darlings also ran a menhaden factory at **Factory Point in Fox Hill** — thousands of tons per year into fish meal for fertilizer and oil for industrial uses.
- **Crabtown:** Hampton was known up and down the coast as Crabtown in the 19th century.
- **James McMenamin canning patent:** **1870s** Hampton; canning process for cooked Chesapeake crabmeat in airtight tins.

### 7.2 Oyster decline arithmetic

- **Late 1880s Chesapeake oyster production:** approximately **15 million bushels per year**.
- **1990s production:** under **200,000 bushels per year**. **Two orders of magnitude in a century. ~99 percent decline.**
- **Cause structure:** wave 1 = overharvesting (dredges scraping reefs flat); wave 2 = disease (MSX late 1950s; Dermo following years); wave 3 = sediment + agricultural runoff smothering oyster spat + nutrient loading driving algal blooms + dissolved oxygen depletion.

### 7.3 Striped bass / rockfish — 1985 moratorium + 1995 recovery + 2020s second-decline

- **1970s collapse → 1985 Maryland moratorium for 5 years; Virginia coordinated.** Federal coordination. Both commercial + recreational + charter shut down.
- **1995:** fishery declared restored.

### 7.7 Menhaden — the reduction-fishery case

- **Species names:** menhaden / bunker (NJ) / pogy (New England). Forage fish at base of Bay food web.
- **Reduction operation:** Reedville, Virginia (mouth of Great Wicomico River in Northumberland County); industrial reduction into fish meal / fish oil / omega-3 supplements / pet food / aquaculture feed. End customers: omega-3 capsule in Indianapolis; salmon farm in Chile; 40-pound bag of dog food in a Phoenix grocery.
- **Plant employment:** several hundred people, shifts following menhaden runs.
- **Bay-specific cap:** set by ASMFC in **2006**; reduced in **2017** to current level of **51,000 metric tons**. Conservation side regards as floor not ceiling; industry side regards as hard-fought concession.
- **Scientific committee** recommending coastwide reductions + Bay-specific precautions; **management board** adopting reductions smaller than committee recommended + slower than proposed.
- **Genny Nesslage verbatim (associate research professor, University of Maryland Center for Environmental Science; WHRO):**
  - "We don't know the abundance of menhaden in the bay at any given time relative to the coast."
- **Rob Latour verbatim (VIMS menhaden researcher; WHRO):**
  - "We have no data to really do this in a meaningful way, from a biological perspective."
- **VIMS proposal:** drafted **2023** for a **$3 million Bay-specific menhaden study**. Virginia General Assembly declined to fund it.
- **Allison Colden verbatim:**
  - "Just as we were confident in increasing the total allowable catch when the science says we should, we need to be as willing to take reductions when the science indicates that that's warranted."
- **Ecological-impact pattern:** osprey-fledging failure suspected attributable to insufficient menhaden in some Bay tributaries; rockfish summer-heat-stress + oxygen-stress + insufficient menhaden compounding.
