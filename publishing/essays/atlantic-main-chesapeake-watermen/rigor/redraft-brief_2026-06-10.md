### 2.1 Historical Chesapeake oyster fishery
- **J.S. Darling and Son:** took that name **1884** (Frank Darling joined his father's existing business). Peak: ~**160 shuckers**, ~**200,000 bushels** of Chesapeake oysters packed per year; ~**50,000 gallons** shucked annually by ~1938. Shell mountains rose **more than four stories** high. Menhaden reduction factory at **Factory Point, Fox Hill** (thousands of tons/yr into fish meal + oil). Source: Library of Virginia, *Dictionary of Virginia Biography*, "Frank Wilkinson Darling (1865–1941)"; J.S. Darling and Son Historical Marker (HMdb). NB: **200,000 bu is a peak/representative single-year figure, NOT a documented constant** — never multiply across the ~98-yr run (single biggest adversarial vulnerability per the reef-numbers research).
- **Crabtown:** Hampton known up and down the coast as Crabtown in the 19th century.
- **James McMenamin canning patent:** **1870s** Hampton; airtight-tin canning of cooked Chesapeake crabmeat.

### 2.2 Oyster decline arithmetic
- **Late 1880s Chesapeake oyster production:** ~**15 million bushels/yr**.
- **1990s:** under **200,000 bushels/yr**. **Two orders of magnitude in a century; ~99% decline.** (Headline; historical-record figure — keep as VOLUME/biomass framing; do not multiply by an acre price, see §2.13.)
- **Cause structure:** wave 1 overharvesting (dredges scraping reefs flat); wave 2 disease (**MSX late 1950s; Dermo** following years); wave 3 sediment + agricultural runoff smothering spat + nutrient loading + dissolved-oxygen depletion.

### 2.3 Striped bass / rockfish
- 1970s collapse → **1985 Maryland 5-year moratorium** (Virginia coordinated; federal coordinated; commercial + recreational + charter all shut). **1995** fishery declared restored.
- **2024 Maryland young-of-year index: 2.0 fish/sample vs long-term average 11.0; sixth consecutive below-average year.** Source: Maryland DNR juvenile striped bass seine survey.
- **Allison Colden** (Chesapeake Bay Foundation Maryland executive director), verbatim public statements: "Maryland's striped bass surveys have made it clear; we're facing a looming disaster. Without action now, the Chesapeake Bay won't have a striped bass fishery in the future." / "there are very few levers left to pull." / "[Many people in Maryland] rely on striped bass for their food, their businesses, and their futures. Without action, that will all be at risk." / "[Rising water temperatures and the low-oxygen periods of summer] are pummeling [the species] during the months it can least afford to be pummeled."
- **Bill Goldsborough** (CBF fisheries director through the moratorium years; *Smithsonian Magazine*): "Our objective is to try to restore the health of bay and aquatic life so that it can support viable fisheries. We see that task taking place over a longer term [...]. But the watermen don't have the luxury of the long term. They are concerned about making their next boat payment."
- **Chris Moore** (CBF Virginia executive director; thesis-anchor quote currently MISSING from all Ch 3 texts per the coverage audit — INCLUDE in the redraft), on-record in response to the 2024 stock assessment: "Despite recent management actions, striped bass continue to struggle. It is obvious that conditions have changed and striped bass will not have the same recovery they had in the 1980s. Warmer water from climate change, habitat loss, and invasive species are all making it very hard for striped bass to bounce back." Source: CBF public statement / 2024 stock-assessment response (public-record exception; **Moore citation-verification packet owed pre-submission** — mirror the Colden packet, route via David Sherfinski).

### 2.4 Shad
- **January 1994:** Virginia closed its commercial shad fishery (main stem). Shad anadromous (open Atlantic most of life; spring spawning runs up Bay tributaries to fresh water). Runs did not recover; science has not converged on a single attribution; what it has converged on is the absence of a recovery.

### 2.5 Blue crab

- **Colden** verbatim: "The red flags are flying for blue crabs." / "with more than five years of below average crab numbers, it is clear that changing conditions in the Bay are undermining the current management of this important species."

### 2.7 Menhaden — the reduction-fishery case
- Forage fish at base of Bay food web (bunker in NJ; pogy in New England). Reduction operation at **Reedville, Virginia** (mouth of Great Wicomico River, Northumberland County); ground into fish meal / fish oil / omega-3 supplements / pet food / aquaculture feed. End customers: an omega-3 capsule in Indianapolis; a salmon farm in Chile; a 40-lb bag of dog food in a Phoenix grocery. Plant employs several hundred, shifts following the runs.
- **CAP CORRECTION (apply in the redraft):** the **51,000 metric-ton** Bay-specific reduction cap was set by **ASMFC Amendment 3 (adopted 2017, implemented 2018)** — a cut of **~41.5% (≈87,000 → 51,000 mt)** on Colden's own original motion (she was then CBF MD Senior Fisheries Scientist). Source: ASMFC Amendment 3; Anstead et al., *Frontiers in Marine Science* 7:606417 (2020). **RETIRE the canonical essay's "set by ASMFC in 2006 and reduced in 2017."** "2006" is wrong; the cap is Amendment 3 (2017/2018). 2025: ASMFC Addendum II initiated to cut the Bay cap up to 50%.
- **Genny Nesslage** (associate research professor, University of Maryland Center for Environmental Science; WHRO): "We don't know the abundance of menhaden in the bay at any given time relative to the coast."
- **Rob Latour** (VIMS menhaden researcher; WHRO): "We have no data to really do this in a meaningful way, from a biological perspective."
- **VIMS 2023 proposal:** ~**$3 million** Bay-specific menhaden study; Virginia General Assembly declined to fund it.
- **Colden** verbatim: "Just as we were confident in increasing the total allowable catch when the science says we should, we need to be as willing to take reductions when the science indicates that that's warranted."
- Ecological-impact pattern: osprey-fledging failure suspected to track insufficient forage in some Bay tributaries; rockfish summer-heat + oxygen stress compounding. Frame as suspected/observed, NOT as settled single-cause attribution (climate-realist adversary F).
- **Adversarial-balance note (for Pass 3.4 awareness, not body prose):** Menhaden Fisheries Coalition rebuttal (Oct 2025) + Ocean Harvesters response (Aug 2025) dispute the catch-distribution characterization. Do not overclaim the foodweb causal model.
