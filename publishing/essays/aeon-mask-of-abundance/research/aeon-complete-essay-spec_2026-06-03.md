## The through-line (the spine that makes this ONE essay, not a grab-bag)

**One honest ledger, on any scale, on any world, run in either direction.** Every section is the *same move*: find the commons value that abundance, distance, or time has hidden, and put it on the ledger honestly. The mask of abundance is the way *in*; the framework is what the mask reveals. The essay should feel like a single argument widening — breath → barrel → county → other worlds → any economy, any world — not a tour of topics.

## The arc (beats in order; NO word allocations — let each run to its natural length)

1. **Open on the breath / the mask of abundance.** Air costs ~zero not because it has little value but because it has nothing but value, distributed so completely no market can find an edge to charge at. A price measures scarcity at the margin; abundance posts a zero we misread as a verdict on worth. The commons (air, water, the carrying-away of carbon, a stable climate) are not merely underpriced the way nurses or topsoil are underpriced — they have no price at all, and a thing with no price is invisible to the only instrument we trust.
2. **The sealed-habitat lens** (signaled thought-experiment) + the three REAL anchors where the bill already arrives: saturation divers buying heliox by the hour; Biosphere 2; the ISS. Seal the system and the priceless commons become the most expensive things there are.
3. **Name the mechanism.** *Cost severance*: someone creates a cost, someone else absorbs it, and the second party has no way to bill it back. *Value extraction* and the *residual commons value* — the worth left unpriced after the sale is done, owed to people not at the table (often not yet born). Honest pricing usually means re-attaching a severed cost; the commons case is stranger — a value so total it never acquired a price, revealed only when scarcity arrives.
4. **The $100 barrel.** A barrel priced at $100 (illustrative, signaled) is priced for the present; the thing priced extends to permanence. *Renewable vs non-renewable precision* (does double duty): renewables pose a question of rate (take less than it breeds); oil is liquidated, gone forever, no rate slow enough. The hundred dollars buys the present its tank of gas; it does not buy the barrel back for anyone after.
5. **The lineage of near-misses (COMPRESSED — name the pattern + a few names per cluster, not a full treatment).** Several traditions circled the empty plot and none built on it: Marx priced the surplus (class, not time); Ostrom governed the renewable commons (not the liquidated one); Daly warned about throughput (no invoice for one foreclosed thing); Hartwick saved the rent (not the barrel). Then the moral-philosophy convergence: Rawls's veil widened across generations; Sandel on what markets corrupt; Parfit on obligations to future people. Then the discount-rate battleground: Stern vs Nordhaus (and Weitzman's fat tail) all price the *damage flow* from combustion, not the resource's *absence*. The residual commons value survives even Stern's low rate, because it is not a discounted flow — it is the present absence of a thing that will not return.
6. **McDowell County.** Coal sold at the mine mouth for a few dollars a ton; the honest price, once severed costs are added back, is **more than $500 per ton, nearly all of it carbon** (the carbon term alone is ~$496 = 2.61 t × $190, the EPA's 2023 figure). The framework run *backward* on a realized extraction.
7. **Forwards and in reverse.** The framework runs both directions, and it has a named instrument for each: a **Foreclosure Bond** forward (price the future loss, set it aside) and a **Restitution Bond** backward (price extraction already realized). The reverse direction builds directly on **William Darity Jr. and A. Kirsten Mullen, *From Here to Equality* (2020)** — reparations economics as backward honest-accounting (the racial wealth gap since 1619; the Black-white longevity gap as a measurable legacy effect). **Coercion-line deferral discipline (include):** putting a price on the costs of coercion borne by a group the author does not belong to is not the author's to do; leave that work to those continuing it. The framework reaches coerced cases through their legacy effects and defers the direct pricing of the coercion vector.
8. **Norway (existence-proof) + Niger Delta (counter-case).** Norway is proof the future *can* be priced in practice: oil confirmed off the North Sea just before Christmas 1969 (Ekofisk); the Government Petroleum Fund (1990 → Government Pension Fund Global 2006); a 2001 fiscal rule spending only the ~3% expected real return, capital inviolate; now **well over $2 trillion, more than $390,000 per citizen**; durable across the entire political spectrum (no government of any color has raided the principal). That is Hartwick's rule at sovereign scale — and a *reserve fund*, which ties to the reserves theme. The Niger Delta is the counter-case: oil pumped since the 1950s, hundreds of billions of dollars of revenue, the costs (spills, flares, foreclosed fisheries) borne by people who did not sell it and could not bill anyone; Ken Saro-Wiwa and eight others executed 1995; a UN assessment of Ogoniland later put cleanup at an initial $1 billion over 25–30 years. Same physical act, two architectures, one question: when the cost outlives the sale, who carries it, and is anything set aside? **Not anti-extraction; not a villain story; architecture, not a political program.**
9. **Off-world: the framework in operation** (signaled thought-experiment — a MAJOR section, give it real room; two scenes of the framework running forward in real time on another world).
   (a) **The colony economy** — the pitch's airlock scene, developed. Imagine actually *living* inside a sealed habitat, not as a measurement device but as a home with a functioning economy. At the airlock you are asked how you intend to pay for your air. Inside, every commons the Earth gives away for free is metered and billed by usage: a breath, a litre of recycled water, the removal of the carbon dioxide you exhale. This is the framework as a colony's operating ledger — honest accounting as the day-to-day economy of a world too sealed to let abundance hide any value. It is the same ledger as McDowell's and Norway's, run forward, in real time, on a world that cannot afford the mask. (Reconnects to the opening: first we used the seal to *see* the value; now we live inside it and *pay* it.)
   (b) **The asteroid miner** — out where there is no community and no ecosystem to bear the cost, cost severance is near zero, so the framework says **extract freely** (asteroid iron, take it; if a comet is only briefly in reach, extract aggressively while you can). And yet, on the rock itself, you must **hold reserves** — fuel, air, life-support — enough to be sure you can get off it and get home, or on to the next world. The same ledger that says "take freely here" says "reserve there." Honest accounting is not anti-extraction; it is knowing the real cost, and keeping enough back to survive the future you are extracting into.
10. **Whatever economy you choose, you have to see the real costs.** The framework is not a rival economic system; it is the accounting any system needs. Kate Raworth's Doughnut Economics, Ostrom's commons governance, and far older reserve systems — ancient China's ever-normal granary, ancient Egypt's state granaries (HIGH-LEVEL historical reference ONLY; no invented specifics) — are all, among others, attempts by a civilization to know its real costs and hold reserves against scarcity. Capitalist, planned, ancient, or off-world: the ledger has to be honest or the economy is flying blind.
11. **Reserves on the longest horizon.** Finite resources demand reserves not only for the next generation but across civilizational time. The Sun's rising luminosity will make Earth uninhabitable on the order of **500 million to 1 billion years** from now (Sackmann et al. 1993), long before the red-giant Sun **engulfs the planet around 7.5 billion years** out (Schröder & Smith 2008). On those horizons, the off-world habitat is not science fiction but a coherent forward-looking concern — option-preservation across deep time.
12. **Close.** The framework is honest accounting an **individual, a community, or a government** can run — on this world or any other. One ledger. The mask comes off; the number was always there. (You may return to the breath, as the low-floor arms do, and close on agency — whether the freedom stays is a thing we still get to decide.)
