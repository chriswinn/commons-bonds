## 1. Calibration note

The plain-language overlay **adds substantial length** versus the literary arms at the same floors (literary leads: 8,569 / 9,256 / 10,553; plain arms: 10,898 / 11,632 / 12,139 — roughly +2,000–2,400w each). Cause is structural, not padding: walking each reasoning step explicitly and introducing every term of art in plain words before naming it costs words. All three overshot their floors by ~28% (vs ~3–13% for the denser literary arms), which is the expected signature of "explain, don't assert." Substance still drove length; no per-section budgets imposed.

## 2. Discipline scan — CLEAN across all three

- **0 em-dashes, 0 en-dashes** in all three (en-dash ranges like "500M–1B yr" rendered in words: "five hundred million to a billion years").
- **No fabrication-risk patterns**: no `$524`, no "ten billion years," no "30 billion barrels," no "three days before Christmas." Corrected McDowell figure present in all three (honest price more than $500/ton, ~$510 total, carbon term ~$496 = 2.61 t × $190, EPA's 2023 estimate).

- **Required content present**: airlock + asteroid + Norway (>$2T and >$390K per citizen, spelled out) + Ekofisk + Saro-Wiwa + Ogoniland + Ostrom + Raworth/Doughnut + Darity + Rawls + Sackmann sun figures + coercion-line deferral.
- Off-world signaled as lens-not-forecast in all three; granaries kept high-level (explicit "I will not invent particulars").

## 3. Quality (8,500 read in full)

The plain register **works**. The 8,500 arm reads as genuinely digestible for a layman: short declarative sentences, term-of-art introduced plainly first ("Someone creates a cost and someone else pays it, with no way to send the bill back. Call that cost severance."), reasoning walked step by step, vivid anchors (breath, barrel, coal seam, airlock) retained. The colony section is the strongest single beat — the airlock "how do you intend to pay for your air" scene, the metered breath/water/CO₂-removal, the reconnect to the opening ("first the seal let us see the value; now the seal makes us pay it"), and the fairness-falls-out-of-the-engineering close ("fair not because its people are better than us but because its walls will not let them be worse"). The asteroid miner lands the "one rule, two faces — take freely / hold reserves" point cleanly.
