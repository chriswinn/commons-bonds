# The Mask of Abundance — consolidated harvest scan

## OVERALL JUDGMENT (read this first)

**The corpus already contains everything needed for a strong final. There is
no gap that requires fresh drafting.** The base (airlock-seed-verbatim-9000w)
plus the four-way graft list cover the spine, the corrected figures, the
discipline, and the best images and argument-steps. This scan adds a curated
set of HIGH-VALUE enrichments — almost all of them *images, aphorisms, or
self-contained reasoning passages* that can be lifted into the base — plus one
genuinely new structural option (the **Devon Island / HI-SEAS real-sealed-world
anchor**) that is the single most worthwhile new find. None of it is
load-bearing in the sense of "the argument is incomplete without it"; all of it
is "the final reads richer / more Aeon with it."

Two things worth flagging at the top:

1. **The McDowell arithmetic is shown in full in the plain-language and
   global-economical arms** (2.61 t CO₂ × $190 = ~$496 carbon; ~$510 total;
   Pocahontas vs. 2.33 national average; EPA 2023). The base only *asserts*
   $496/$510. If the author wants the "show-the-multiplication" move (it is a
   strong rhetorical beat — "the only difficulty was ever the willingness to do
   it"), the corrected worked arithmetic is sitting ready in three arms. This is
   the most useful single harvest after the Devon Island anchor.

2. **Recurring load-bearing lines** (appear across many arms, a sign they are
   doing real work and should survive in the final in some form): "the planet
   does not send statements" (base); "free TO YOU, not free" distinction
   (global-economical arms); "it is missing at every point in the future
   equally" / "a present absence, not a discounted flow" (every arm); "take
   freely here, reserve there" (every colony arm); "honest accounting includes
   being honest about whose accounting it is not" (base + variants everywhere);
   "the books have to be honest or the economy is flying blind" (most arms).
   These are already in or adjacent to the base — noted so the author does not
   accidentally cut them.

Below, organized by beat. "CF-inv?" = whether `carry-forward-inventory.md`
already lists it (that file is thin — only Entry #1, the four-traditions /
permanent-foreclosure foundation — so the answer is **No** for essentially

## 1. OPENING

**Candidate O-1 — Devon Island / HI-SEAS real-sealed-world anchor (NEW, highest-value structural find).**
- Source: `global-economical-airlock-9000w` (lines 9–10) and `9500w` (lines 9–10).
- Quote (9000w): *"We have already built the smaller version of what I am
  describing. On Devon Island in the Canadian Arctic, in a crater that resembles
  parts of Mars closely enough to rehearse there, researchers spend their
  summers in a sealed station and practise the discipline of a place where
  nothing outside the wall will keep you alive. On the slope of a Hawaiian
  volcano, in a dome called HI-SEAS, crews have lived for months at a time under
  the rules of an off-world mission… These are not films. They are real places
  where real people learn what it costs to keep a human being breathing in a
  spot the Earth did not prepare for them."*
- Why include: This is the strongest piece of unique material in the entire
  corpus that is NOT in the base or the four-way. It pre-empts the "this is just
  sci-fi" objection at the *opening* by grounding the airlock in two real,
  named, currently-operating analog habitats. The base only gets this grounding
  later and more glancingly ("Devon Island… a dome on the side of a Hawaiian
  volcano" appears once, base line 15, unnamed as HI-SEAS). Naming HI-SEAS +
  Devon Island explicitly, early, is a real robustness upgrade against the
  skeptical-reader failure mode.
- Discipline caveat: **VERIFY** — Devon Island = the Haughton-Mars Project /
  Flashline Mars Arctic Research Station (real); HI-SEAS = Hawaii Space
  Exploration Analog and Simulation on Mauna Loa (real). Both are genuine and
  citable, but confirm spelling/capitalization of "HI-SEAS" and that the
  "months at a time" framing is accurate (HI-SEAS missions ran up to 12 months).
  No em-dash/idiom issue. Register is plain — drops cleanly into any base.
- CF-inv? No.

## 3. COST-SEVERANCE MECHANISM

**Candidate CS-1 — the three structural reasons a severed cost can't be billed back (NEW, genuine added reasoning).**
- Source: `lead-candidate-9000w` line 37.
- Quote: *"To send an invoice you need three things: you need to know that a cost
  has landed on you, you need to be able to trace it to the party who caused it,
  and you need a forum in which the claim can be heard… The town downstream may
  take years to learn that its rising rates of illness trace to the water. Even
  once it knows, the harm may come from a hundred upstream sources… And the
  people who will bear the worst of it… are not present to file any claim at all,
  because they do not yet exist… The market is built to hear from whoever is
  standing at the till with money in hand, and the future never stands at the
  till."*
- Why include: This is a real *argument-step* the base does not make — it
  explains WHY severance is structural rather than accidental, which strengthens
  the mechanism beat against an economist reader who would otherwise say "Coasean
  bargaining solves this." "The future never stands at the till" is a quotable
  closer. Complements (does not duplicate) the four-way's already-listed Arm-4
  "the years have no courtroom" / "it did not roll, it was cut" grafts.
- Discipline caveat: none. Note potential redundancy: if you graft Arm 4's
  "years have no courtroom" AND this, trim so the beat does not over-explain.
- CF-inv? No (and note: this directly serves CF-inv Entry #1's "why
  supply-and-demand structurally cannot price what's not present" — it is the
  best prose realization of that inventory item).

**Candidate CS-2 — "externality… lets you keep the cost at arm's length" critique (NEW, plain version of Arm 4's move).**
- Source: `lead-candidate-9000w` line 33.
- Quote: *"Economists have a tidy word for the river case, externality, and the
