# Aeon "Mask of Abundance" — gather

## Thesis

**Abundance is the mask.** The commons we never price — air, water, the cycling of waste, a stable climate — are priceless precisely *because* they are everywhere, so the price the market posts for them is approximately zero. Abundance hides value. Seal the system and the mask comes off: the things that were free become the most expensive things there are. Cost severance, run in reverse — instead of a cost severed from a price, here a value so total it never acquired a price at all, until scarcity reveals it.

## The thought-experiment (explicitly theoretical — signal it in the prose)

An off-world closed habitat (or any sealed life-support system) where a resident pays, by usage, for the commons that Earth supplies for free: a metered breath, a litre of recycled water, the carrying-away of carbon dioxide. The point is not prediction; it is a lens. The off-world habitat is the moral X-ray that shows what the Earth-commons would cost if they were ever billed.

## Real-world anchors (researched 2026-06-02 — the thought-experiment is ALREADY real for some people)

- **Saturation / deep-sea divers** pay for breathable gas by the hour. Commercial heliox (helium + oxygen); helium runs roughly **$1.70–$2.75 per cubic foot**; technical divers estimate **$80–$200 per hour just for the helium**; ship saturation systems run helium-**reclaim** rigs to recapture it. A diver's breath is metered and expensive. *(Sources: Divers Alert Network; Trimix/heliox refs.)*
- **Biosphere 2** (1991–93): a sealed 3.14-acre glass world. Oxygen fell from the ambient **21% to 14% in 16 months** (soil-microbe respiration + CO₂ reacting with the concrete), enough to disable the crew; managers pumped oxygen in. **19 of 25** vertebrate species went extinct; **all** pollinators died (crops hand-pollinated). Cost **~$150 million**. The lesson: how hard, and how expensive, it is to replicate the ecosystem services Earth provides at no charge. *(Source: Biosphere 2 record; "Oxygen Loss in Biosphere 2.")*
- **International Space Station / ECLSS**: water costs about **$21,000 per kilogram** to lift; a six-month supply (~30 L/day) runs on the order of **$135 million**; the life-support system **recycles ~90%** of crew sweat and urine; oxygen is made by **electrolysis** of water. In orbit, air and water are the costliest things aboard. *(Source: ISS ECLSS.)*

## Framework connection (the move)

On Earth the price of air is zero because the supply is effectively infinite; the value is total but unbilled. The diver, the astronaut, the Biosphere-2 crew are simply living in the version where the bill arrives. Honest pricing usually means adding back a cost the market severed; here it means *seeing* a value the market never had to name, because abundance did the hiding. The mask of abundance is why we under-protect the very commons we most depend on.
