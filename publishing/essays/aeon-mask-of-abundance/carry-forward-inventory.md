## Open inventory

### Entry #1 — Four-traditions / permanent-foreclosure analytical foundation

- **Surfaced by:** Author 2026-05-21 (Aeon Pass 3.3 + 3.4 closing session)
- **Status:** Fresh; treat as source material, not verbatim passage
- **Analytical work this entry does:**
  - **Lineage naming:** four canonical economic traditions (Marx labor; Ostrom commons; Daly biosphere; Hartwick intergenerational), well-chosen to span what economics has historically addressed about resource-cost-bearing
  - **What each addresses, glossed:** parallel structure — *who labors / who governs the commons / who lives inside the biosphere / who inherits what is left*
  - **Common gap claim:** none of the four traditions priced **permanent foreclosure** (the specific category nothing in the inherited apparatus prices)
  - **Why supply-and-demand also misses (structural argument):** parties-present-at-the-moment-of-sale logic literally cannot price what's not present; the generation that pays is not seated at the table; the price clears without them
  - **Emotional landing:** the bill comes due in their lifetimes rather than ours
- **Author's source-material text** (verbatim from 2026-05-21 conversation; preserved as drafting reference, not as locked prose):
  > Four traditions: Marx, Ostrom, Daly, Hartwick. Each addressed part of
  > what economics is for — who labors, who governs the commons, who
  > lives inside the biosphere, who inherits what is left. None priced
  > permanent foreclosure. And neither does the everyday model the rest
  > of us reason inside — supply and demand at the moment of sale. Supply
  > and demand clears the transaction between the parties present at it.
  > Permanent foreclosure is not present at the moment of sale; the
  > generation that pays for it is not seated at the table. The price
  > clears without them. The decision gets made. The bill comes due in
  > their lifetimes rather than ours.
- **Proposed placement in the essay:**
  - **Primary:** opener of §5 (universal-claim section). Transitions naturally from §4 (three cross-parameter cases demonstrating range) → this analytical context (what tradition misses + what supply-and-demand structurally can't) → §5 universal claim (pricing-framework-first analytical-lens-second; many-multiples claim) → §6 honest-accounting close.
  - **Alternative:** as deepening within §3 (six-patterns section's analytical framing). Less natural fit — sits more cleanly before the universal claim than before the pattern enumeration.
- **Why it's stronger than what the pitch carries:** The pitch lists six patterns by which costs get hidden (geographic distance, temporal asymmetry, etc.) without naming the *specific gap* — *permanent foreclosure*. This entry crystallizes the analytical category and gives the essay a named concept the pitch only describes.
- **Caveats to address when drafting:**
  1. **Define "permanent foreclosure" explicitly.** This is a CRYSTALLIZATION move — the essay names what the pitch only described. The term needs one defining gesture. Sketch: *"What none priced — and what supply and demand structurally cannot — is permanent foreclosure: the cost of an action whose damage is irreversible by the time anyone could pay for it."* (Verify if the term is already established in Tech Appendix or Ch 9; if so, use as established term and skip the defining gesture.)
  2. **Marx-tradition completeness flag.** *"None priced permanent foreclosure"* is defensible scoped to original Marx, but **John Bellamy Foster's metabolic-rift extension** within the Marxian tradition addresses ecological pricing. A Foster-reader might push back. Options when drafting: (a) scope to original-tradition founders (clean but Foster-blind); (b) parenthetical *"Foster's metabolic-rift extension within the Marxian tradition gestures toward this, but does not formalize it as a pricing move"*; (c) name as near-miss extension worth acknowledging.
  3. **Hartwick rule attribution accuracy.** Hartwick rule (1977, "Intergenerational Equity and the Investing of Rents from Exhaustible Resources") + Hotelling rule lineage. Verify *"who inherits what is left"* gloss matches Hartwick's actual framing if any reader fact-checks.
  4. **Daly attribution accuracy.** Daly's framing is throughput-and-scale (steady-state economics; ISEE founding). *"Who lives inside the biosphere"* is accurate-and-poetic — verify that's the gloss that lands.
  5. **Ostrom attribution accuracy.** Elinor Ostrom's commons governance / design principles. *"Who governs the commons"* — accurate.
  6. **Present-day analog vs. literal future generations.** *"The generation that pays for it is not seated at the table"* reads literally as future-people (Parfit / Hartwick / Mulgan tradition). But the pitch's McDowell coal case is a PRESENT-DAY case where the affected community was present but their voice in pricing was structurally suppressed. The essay's surrounding context should reconcile these two readings: literal-future-generations + present-day-structurally-excluded. The passage as written prioritizes the literal-future-generations reading; the essay can extend to acknowledge the present-day analog (the McDowell case + the *"communities downstream"* framing already in the pitch).
- **Audience-read quick check** (will re-verify at essay Stage 3):
  - **Aeon editor (Haselby) + Mulgan/Sandel/MacAskill cluster:** strong fit. Lineage-engaging analytical-philosophy register Aeon publishes.
  - **Mazzucato/Raworth cluster:** strong fit. Both authors do tradition-naming as intellectual-genealogy practice.
  - **Coasean economist:** mixed. Will push back on *"supply and demand … structurally cannot"* — would argue Coasean bargaining or property-rights extension solves the externality. The structural-mechanics framing + analytical-lens positioning partially defuses; essay can engage Coasean response substantively.
  - **Public Choice / Rothbardian / Reactionary:** the four-tradition inclusion (with Marx) could trigger reactionary suspicion, but Hartwick + Ostrom + Daly are all mainstream-econ-respectable, which balances the lineup. Strong on analytical-rigor grounds.
