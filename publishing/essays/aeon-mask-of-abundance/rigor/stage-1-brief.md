## 2. Editorial brain map for Aeon specifically

What Aeon editors *want* (and what Noema doesn't equally demand):

- **Distinctive philosophical angle.** "Standard direction" pitches (Earth person on Mars worrying about resources) get rejected; "perspective reversal" (Mars resident visiting Earth, or *reader* visiting closed habitat) does the work.
- **Thought-experiment register.** Aeon publishes essays that posit a hypothetical and walk it carefully. Pure-reportage pitches don't fit.
- **Universalizability claim.** The framework being defended needs to apply across cases, not just one. Aeon's analytic philosophy adjacency expects this.
- **Cross-domain reach.** Aeon wants pitches that touch multiple fields. *Commons Bonds* hits philosophy + economics + ecology + healthcare + memoir — that range is on-brand.
- **Honest accounting / clean argument.** No defensive hedging. State the argument and outline how it'll be made.
- **AI-collaboration disclosure** — voluntarily offered under Section 3.2 of Aeon's Author Terms (conflict-of-interest provision); on-brand with the framework's own honest-accounting discipline.

What hurts Aeon submissions specifically (vs. Noema):

- **Pure reportage** (Aeon wants the philosophy).
- **Sci-fi-coded framing** that risks genre-misclassification (the title rigor pass v1.0.0 flagged this for *The Mars Test*).
- **Defensive register** ("I am addressing the obvious objection because…") — Aeon expects you to make the move, not announce that you're making it.
- **Apparatus jargon** in the pitch (full essays can carry it; pitches cannot).
- **Length over the published guidance** — Haselby specifically said simple opener + 200-word body; pitches running long signal misalignment with editorial expectations.
- **Standard direction** (Earth person on Mars; status-quo-cost-bearer thinking about cost). Aeon wants the angle most other writers don't reach.

## 5. Voice register (ratified)

- **Distinctive philosophical voice.** Not journalist, not academic, not memoirist. Aeon's house voice is closer to Mulgan + Sandel + MacAskill than to The Atlantic or NYT Magazine.
- **Show, don't tell.** Lead with a scene; let the philosophy emerge from it. Don't announce "this is a philosophical piece."
- **Concise sentences.** Pitches are read in seconds. Cut hedges. Cut "I will argue that…" Just argue.
- **Italicize key terms** sparingly — *cost severance*, *mask of abundance*, the philosophical anchor sentence.
- **No first-person memoir register** in the pitch body — that's the Noema pitch shape. Aeon's reader is in the thought experiment, not in the author's biography.
- **Bio paragraph is separate** and brief: "Chris Winn is a nursing student in Hampton, Virginia, and a former healthcare IT executive. He built the framework this essay applies across a decade of consulting work that took him through fifty-eight countries — until his son's birth and a family caregiving crisis made him reconsider what the work was actually costing. He is not an economist. The framework grew from inside the systems it engages, not above them." (Carry forward verbatim from Version A unless Stage 2 has reason to change.)
- **AI disclosure paragraph** is separate and follows the bio. (Carry forward verbatim from Version A unless Stage 2 has reason to change.)

## 7. Carry-forward inventory (locked content)

### 7.1 Title

**The Mask of Abundance** — ratified per `publishing/essays/aeon-mask-of-abundance/rigor/title-candidates_2026-05-08.md`. Cannot be changed.

### 7.2 Frame

**Reader visits closed habitat** (Mars, the Moon, an orbital station, the Earth-side analogs at Devon Island or HI-SEAS). The "perspective reversal" angle — reader inside the closed system, then back home — is what differentiates this pitch from standard-direction pieces.

### 7.3 Cultural-engagement commitment (load-bearing)

The "*The Mask of X*" construction puts the framework structurally inside the **Dunbar / Du Bois / Ellison / Fanon** Black-literary lineage (the work of *unmasking systemic harm to reveal who bears cost*). The pitch itself does not need to cite the lineage explicitly — but the engagement commitment is load-bearing for the eventual essay (post-acceptance) and the broader book. Bibliography §21 (`tools/back-matter/sources/bibliography.md`) holds the citation set.

The Stage 2 session should be aware of the commitment but should NOT attempt to fit a Dunbar/Du Bois/Fanon/Ellison reference into the 300-word pitch. The commitment is paratextual; it lives in the essay body and Ch 8 (McDowell coal miner) per the title rigor pass.

### 7.4 Central claim

**The framework is universal — a pricing framework first, a critique second.** Most discussions of resource pricing are political; this one is structural. If the framework is right, the price tag on commons is many multiples of what we have been paying as a species.

### 7.5 Structural anchor sentence (optional but valued)

**"The closed habitat is the limiting case that exposes Earth's open system."** This sentence was the load-bearing addition that made Version C stronger than Version B. It is the Kantian-universalizability move that gives the pitch its philosophical depth. Stage 2 may use this sentence verbatim, may rewrite it, or may construct a different philosophical-anchor sentence — but the *function* (philosophical anchor that makes the thought experiment universalizable) is required.

### 7.6 Three cross-parameter cases

The framework's range is demonstrated by three cases that span different parameters:

- **Asteroid iron** (no community or ecosystem to bear the cost: extract freely). Demonstrates the framework is not anti-extraction.
- **Coal in McDowell County in 1960** (severely mispriced; community + ecosystem both bear cost). Demonstrates the historical extraction case.
- **Self-imposed commute lease whose hours of life were never computed** (no external bad actor; structural test the same). Demonstrates the framework operates without a villain.

These three cases together show the framework is universal across parameter space (cost-bearer present/absent; bad actor present/absent). Stage 2 should preserve the function of three cross-parameter cases — though the specific cases or framing may evolve.

## 11. Comparison protocol — Stage 3 scoring against Pitch A (Version C)

After Stage 2 produces Pitch B, run both pitches through this audience-load framework. Output:

1. **Per-element scoring.** For each load-bearing element across both pitches (¶1 hook, ¶2 framework + range, ¶3 universal-claim close, philosophical anchor, three cases, six patterns, voice register), evaluate against each of the 14 audiences. Tabulate winners.
2. **Aggregate scoring.** Per-audience: which pitch wins overall? Per-tier: which pitch wins overall? Across all tiers: which pitch is closer to Aeon-acceptable?
3. **Path B compliance verdict.** Run Path B audit on both. Pitch B should pass cleanly if the preemptive policy held. Pitch A's relationship to Ch 7 / Versions A is known (it's a refined Version B which descended from Version A).
4. **Length check.** Did Pitch B hit ~270–300w without cut-to-fit? Did the audience-blind drafting respect the budget?
5. **Editor-3-second-read test.** Read the first sentence of each pitch in isolation. Which is stronger? The first sentence is what an Aeon editor reads before deciding to keep reading.
6. **Universal-claim landing.** Does Pitch B's ¶3 close land the universalizability move as cleanly as Pitch A's *"any world"*?

**Methodology cross-validation:** If Pitch B wins on Aeon-load similarly to how Essay B (when it exists) wins on Noema-load, the two-stage discipline is independently confirmed across two different artifact types and two different venues. If Pitch B wins on Aeon-load but Essay B does not win on Noema-load (or vice versa), we have a partial-confirmation result that requires diagnosis. If neither wins, the discipline is falsified for these artifact types.
