## §1. Process protocol

### §1.1 Hybrid review structure

**Stage 1 — Whole-body diff review** (this script's primary mode):
1. Read the **complete baseline pitch** (§2) **ALOUD** before starting any candidate review. Listen for rhythm baseline.
2. For each of 8 candidate edits in §3:
   a. Read current text aloud.
   b. Read alternate text aloud.
   c. Disposition: PRESERVE / DISCUSS / RESTORE / NEEDS-AUTHOR-INPUT.
   d. If RESTORE — note alternate-version choice (Option 1 / 2 / etc.).
3. After all 8 candidates: tally cumulative word-budget impact of RESTORE set; verify within 320w hard cap.
4. If cumulative restoration busts hard cap, prioritize the highest-impact restorations; defer the rest.

**Stage 2 — Per-paragraph restoration application** (only if any
candidate dispositions RESTORE):
1. Per RESTORE candidate, apply edit to the canonical submission file
   (essay.md if Aeon file-org fix has fired; else
   _archive/prior-versions/aeon-pitch-commons-bonds-winn_VERSION-C.md).
2. After all RESTORE edits land in a single paragraph, read the FULL
   paragraph aloud post-restoration. Tweak if rhythm broke at edges.
3. Update submission-day-package_2026-05-31.md §2 to match (the
   submission-day-package §2 is THE paste-source on submission day,
   so changes to the canonical file must mirror there).
4. Re-fire compressed-cadence Pass 3.2 + 3.3 ONLY on changed
   paragraphs (not full-pitch re-audit). Pass 3.4 re-fire only if
   restorations re-introduce adversarial-probe surface area (rare).

### §1.2 Read-aloud test calibration

When reading alternates aloud, listen for:
- **Rhythm** — does the cadence carry the meaning, or does it stumble?
- **Breath** — where does the natural pause want to land? Does the
  punctuation match the natural-pause location?
- **Inversion clarity** — for parallel constructions (billed vs.
  freed, asteroid vs. coal vs. commute, etc.) — does the parallel
  land in the ear?
- **Punch** — for closing fragments and load-bearing emphases — does
  the punch land?
- **Echo** — for title-phrase echoes ("mask of abundance") and
  cross-paragraph callbacks — does the echo register?

**The version that sounds right is usually right.** When in doubt
between current + alternate, the read-aloud verdict carries primary
weight over the diff-text logical case.

## §2. Baseline text (297w current Aeon submission)

**Source of truth:** `publishing/essays/aeon-mask-of-abundance/submission-day-package_2026-05-31.md` §2

```
Imagine landing at a closed habitat — Mars, the Moon, an orbital station, an analog like Devon Island or HI-SEAS. At the airlock, you are asked how you will be paying for your air. You understand viscerally, then and there, what you have been taking for granted all your life — someone else has been paying.

Then you come home. The abundant heat, light, water and even the air is free again. All of it suddenly costless. That first morning back, you laugh at the absurdity of it. Then you discover, slowly, that the costs are still being paid. Just not by you.

This essay walks a pricing framework through what you discover. Built outside the academy across a decade, the framework names patterns through which costs get hidden, including geographic distance, temporal asymmetry, informational asymmetry, power asymmetry, normative routinization, and abundance masking. The closed habitat is the limiting case that exposes Earth's open system. These patterns route the costs that stopped at the airlock to communities downstream, to atmospheres a generation has not yet been born to breathe, to ecosystems that cannot send a bill. The framework is not anti-extraction. Asteroid iron, with no community or ecosystem to bear the cost: extract freely. Coal in McDowell County in 1960: severely mispriced. A self-imposed commute lease whose hours of life were never computed: same framework, no external bad actor.

The central claim is that the framework is universal: a pricing framework first, an analytical lens second. Most discussions of resource pricing are political. This one is structural. If the framework is right, the price tag on commons is many multiples of what we have been paying as a species. The math, finally done, is what a thoughtful reader on any world — including this one — would recognize as honest accounting.
```

**🔊 READ ALOUD — full pitch, end-to-end, at editor's-screen-read pace (~60-90 seconds). Listen for rhythm baseline. Mark any places where you stumble or where the rhythm flattens.**
