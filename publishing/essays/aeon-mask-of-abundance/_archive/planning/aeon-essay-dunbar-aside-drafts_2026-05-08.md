## Three depth-option drafts

These are alternatives, not additions. Pick one for the essay (or revise during essay-drafting).

### Light — footnote (compressed; minimal disruption to essay flow)

> *On Mars the mask of abundance is removed — every resource counts against finite supply.†*
>
> *† The phrase has a structural kinship to Paul Laurence Dunbar's "We Wear the Mask" (1896): in both, the mask hides systemic harm from the dominant gaze.*

**Use case:** Essay is tight on space; editor prefers compressed engagement; the lineage is acknowledged but not foregrounded.

### Medium — in-text aside (recommended depth)

> *On Mars the mask of abundance is removed — every resource counts against finite supply. The framework's debt is older than economics: Paul Laurence Dunbar named the structural move in 1896, when the mask hid not the cost of extraction but the pain of being extracted from. Both masks hide systemic truth from the dominant gaze. The framework extends the tradition into pricing.*

**Use case:** Essay has standard space allowance; lineage gets explicit acknowledgment without dominating; reader sees Dunbar named once. Most likely the right choice for an Aeon essay between 2,500-5,000 words.

### Heavy — integrated (substantive engagement; for if essay structure permits)

> *...When the McDowell miner says the dust is better now, half believing it, the mask he wears is the same mask Dunbar named in 1896. We Wear the Mask — the foundational poem of Black American literature — described masks that hide pain from the dominant gaze. The mask the miner wears does the same work. It is the framework's subject. The framework's claim is that the mask is not his alone — it belongs to the system that puts him in front of the X-ray. Dunbar named the strategy of survival; Du Bois named the structural condition that required it; Fanon extended the analysis into the colonial-economic register the framework's extraction-community cases sit closest to; Ellison gave the long-form narrative of life under it. The framework names the cost-accounting mechanism by which the mask remains structurally necessary. Each of these writers performed an act of naming. The framework follows.*

**Use case:** Essay has space for substantive integration; editor wants the engagement explicit; the lineage becomes a load-bearing argument moment rather than a brief acknowledgment. **Note:** this version is similar to what was applied in book Ch 8 — for the Aeon essay it might read as too book-derivative; medium version is probably better for the essay.

## Decision criteria when essay gets drafted

1. **Essay length** — light if <3,000 words; medium if 3,000-4,500; heavy possible at 4,500-5,000
2. **Editor preference** — Sam Dresser (philosophy desk) likely wants medium-to-heavy; Sam Haselby may prefer light-to-medium for compression
3. **Position in essay** — light works as footnote; medium works mid-essay near abundance-mask passage; heavy works as a dedicated paragraph
4. **Avoid Ch 8 duplication** — heavy version closely mirrors Ch 8's substantive engagement; if heavy is chosen for essay, the essay is mostly reusing Ch 8's prose. Probably better to use medium for the essay so essay and Ch 8 don't duplicate too closely.

**Default recommendation when essay-drafting begins:** Medium version. It's the cleanest size for an Aeon essay; doesn't duplicate Ch 8's heavier engagement; keeps the essay's prose distinct from the book's.
