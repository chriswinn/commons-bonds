## Phase 2 — Wait (8 weeks; through end of July's portal window)

**Wait window:** ~8 weeks (Jun 1 submission → ~Aug 7 decision point).

**Why 8 weeks specifically:** Most publications take 4-8 weeks to respond when accepting. Silent decline is common. Aeon's monthly portal cadence creates two opening windows in this 8-week period (June + July). If we haven't heard by the end of the second window, treating it as soft decline is reasonable.

**During the wait — parallel tracks continue running:**

- Op-ed cascade (FT Opinion → Bloomberg → NYT → WaPo → LA Times): SCC/coal arithmetic + eleven-cent-difference op-eds drafted and submitted independently of Aeon outcome
- Noema track: continue drafting Phase A; interview outreach to Mazzucato + Darity Jr in flight
- Agent batch: build best-fit list via acknowledgments method; queries can go out independently of Aeon timing
- Book chapter work continues regardless of pitch outcome

**Decision criterion at 8 weeks:**

- If Aeon responds positively (any time during window): proceed to essay drafting per [Aeon's editorial timeline]
- If Aeon declines explicitly: review feedback, follow Phase 3
- If silent for 8 weeks: assume soft decline, proceed to Phase 3

## Phase 3 — Resubmit a *different* pitch (NOT a shorter Version C)

**Critical strategic discipline:** Do **not** resubmit Version C in shortened form, even if word-count concern is the suspected reason for decline. Some editors view "shorter version of declined pitch" as not respecting the silent decline. Better to pitch a genuinely different angle next cycle.

**Why not resubmit:**
- The substance was already evaluated once
- "Shorter same idea" reads as not understanding what got declined
- Aeon expects writers to pitch multiple ideas over time, not re-attack the same target

**What TO do instead:** Pitch one of the alternative angles below in a future opening window (July, August, September).

## Alternative pitch ideas for Aeon

Ranked by Aeon-fit. All draw from existing book material; none requires new research beyond polishing.

### Tier 1 — Strongest Aeon-fit (philosophical / pure essay)

1. **"When Not to Decide"** — Kantian-philosophical essay on the framework's Asymmetric Regret Rule (ARR). When the cost of being wrong is unbounded, defer. Applies to climate decisions, AI risk, biotechnology, irreversible-resource extraction. Pure philosophical essay; connects to Parfit, Pearce, the precautionary principle. Aeon's philosophy desk (Sam Dresser, Nigel Warburton) would read this as native register. **Source material:** Tech Appendix (ARR formal apparatus); Ch 7 (Mars / Europa / off-world cases as ARR test scenarios).

2. **"The Math We Owe Our Grandchildren"** — Intergenerational Pricing Gap (IPG) focused essay. The Weitzman declining-rate question + the philosophical question of what we owe future generations. The framework's claim that current discounting practices are mathematically tractable but morally indefensible. Connects to Parfit *Reasons and Persons*, Ramsey, Stern Review. Pure philosophy. **Source material:** Ch 9 (pricing honestly); Ch 10 (model's limits).

3. **"On Wind Tunnels and AI"** — Already drafted as the book's Author's Note. Philosophical meditation on what model-makers do — accuracy as a moral discipline — applied to AI-collaboration disclosure. Distinctive angle on AI ethics through a craftsman's frame. Could be reworked as standalone Aeon essay. **Source material:** existing Author's Note (`manuscript/chapters/_AUTHORSNOTE_ON_WINDTUNNELS_AND_AI.md`).

### Tier 2 — Strong Aeon-fit (specific case-walks with philosophical lift)

4. **"What McDowell County Cost"** — Deep-dive into the coal economy of McDowell County applying the framework to historical extraction. Opens with a scene from the coal town, walks through the SCC/coal arithmetic, ends with the framework's claim about generational debt. Different from Version C in that it leads with a specific case rather than the cosmic-Mars thought experiment. **Source material:** Ch 8 (SCC/coal arithmetic); op-ed 1 source material expanded.

5. **"Who Pays for Death"** — Healthcare-system cost-severance analysis. The framework applied to medical bills, insurance asymmetries, end-of-life care decisions. Connects to Aeon's existing mortality/medicine essay tradition (Atul Gawande-adjacent). **Source material:** Ch 5 (Accountability Gap); the eleven-cent-difference analysis from op-ed 2.

### Tier 3 — Moderate Aeon-fit (depends on framing)

6. **"The Crab and the Atmosphere"** — Chesapeake Bay extraction-community essay. Slow-and-watery essay on the Bay watermen's structural bind. Becomes more pitchable after the Colden interview lands (week of May 12). Personal-narrative-adjacent; could fit Aeon if framed philosophically rather than journalistically. **Source material:** Ch 10 *Common Bonds* (Chesapeake material); upcoming Colden + Sherfinski + DiMarzio interviews.
