## Pitch body (283w)

Imagine landing at a closed habitat — Mars, the Moon, an orbital station, an analog like Devon Island or HI-SEAS. At the airlock, you pay for your air. You understand viscerally, then and there, what you have been taking for granted all your life — someone else has been paying.

Then you come home. The abundant heat, light, water and even the air is free again. All of it suddenly costless. That first morning back, you laugh at the absurdity of it. Then you discover, slowly, that the costs are still being paid. Just not by you.

The closed habitat is the limiting case that exposes Earth's open system. A pricing framework, built outside the academy across a decade, names patterns through which costs get hidden: geographic distance, temporal asymmetry, informational asymmetry, power asymmetry, normative routinization, and abundance masking. These patterns route the costs that stopped at the airlock to communities downstream, to atmospheres a generation has not yet been born to breathe, to ecosystems that cannot send a bill. The framework is not anti-extraction. Asteroid iron, with no community or ecosystem to bear the cost: extract freely. Coal in McDowell County in 1960: severely mispriced. A self-imposed commute lease whose hours of life were never computed: same framework, no external bad actor.

The framework is universal: a pricing framework first, an analytical lens second. Most discussions of resource pricing land in policy debate. This one lands in arithmetic. If the framework is right, the price tag on commons is many multiples of what we have been paying as a species. The math, finally done, is what a thoughtful reader on any world — including this one — would recognize as honest accounting.

## What was considered + rejected in the trim+switch design

- **Dropping "else" from "someone else has been paying" (¶1 reveal).** Author explicitly declined after second-read (carried through from β + α). Variant γ retains "else" verbatim per author direction.

- **Skipping the β announce-the-move trims (test action-frame in isolation against V-D).** Considered, rejected. The test's purpose is to isolate the combinatorial gain — Variant β tests trims in isolation against V-D; Variant γ tests trims + action-frame compounded. The three-variant matrix (α = V-D control / β = + trims / γ = + trims + action-frame) is what isolates each effect cleanly.

- **Verb choice "you pay" vs "you are asked to pay" vs "payment is taken" vs "pay or die" et al.** Considered. "You pay for your air" is the cleanest action-frame: present tense, second person, no implied agent ("who's asking" question is dissolved), parsimonious. "Pay or die" reads as polemic; "payment is taken" reads as passive-voice; "you are asked to pay" preserves the question-frame the switch is trying to dissolve.

- **Action-frame in ¶1 sentence 1 ("you land at a closed habitat" vs "Imagine landing").** Considered, rejected. "Imagine landing" is the thought-experiment register signal that Stage 1 §2 specifies Aeon publishes for. Switching it would lose the Aeon-house-voice posit-and-walk register entirely; γ's switch is intentionally narrow (¶1 sentence 2 only), so the thought-experiment frame at sentence 1 is preserved.

- **Compound switch at airlock-realization line.** Considered, rejected. The airlock-realization em-dash punch ("what you have been taking for granted all your life — someone else has been paying") was specifically credited at V-D INDEPENDENT-AUDIT §2 T1.1 as load-bearing for the Aeon editorial brain; modifying it carries higher risk than the surrounding sentence.
