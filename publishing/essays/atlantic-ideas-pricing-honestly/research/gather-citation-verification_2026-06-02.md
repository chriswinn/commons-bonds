The 2026-06-02 new-pipeline test run drafted from the existing Stage-1 brief,
which defers figure-verification to Stage-3 fact-check. That meant the **Gather
phase + two-state citation model were skipped** — the visible cost being two
citations the drafter (correctly, per no-invented-claims) wrote around or
omitted. This artifact runs the Gather verification retroactively and records
each citation's state. It directly answers the author's two questions
(2026-06-02): *why wasn't this verified before drafting, and why was the Darity
longevity citation omitted?*

**Headline (updated 2026-06-02):** the Gather phase now **confirms 3 citations**

initially omitted, was upgraded to CONFIRMED after a deeper search found the

the Gather phase first validated the drafter's caution, then *recovered* a
citation worth including — exactly the abundance-then-verify discipline.

## What this demonstrates

- The **two-state model works as designed**: harvested citations are validated
  *before* they can be stated as confirmed; those that don't verify stay
  tentative and are written-around or omitted rather than asserted.
- Running Gather *first* (as the real pipeline prescribes) would have produced
  the *same* draft decisions on #1–#4 — confirming that the test draft's
  citation handling was sound, not lucky. The only difference: it would have
  been a deliberate, recorded verdict rather than an in-draft judgment call.
- Net for the essay: it already states the one solidly-verifiable figure
  ($14T) and correctly avoids the two that don't hold up. No fabrication; no
  over-claim.
