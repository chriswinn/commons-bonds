## §6. Pass 3.5 — developmental-edit (architectural integrity at 382w scale)

Per Amendment B Pass 3.5 codification (2026-05-18), restoration polarity
(3.2 cuts, 3.5 restores). At 382w cover-letter scale, restoration
polarity is **bounded** — there is no budget for restoring scene-anchor
density or sensory-detail flow. The developmental-edit at this scale
audits **architectural integrity**:

(a) Does the hook earn the pitch?
(b) Does the close arc-complete back to the hook?
(c) Do the four paragraphs compound rather than stack?
(d) Are paragraph transitions load-bearing or merely sequential?

### §6.1 Hook earns the pitch

**¶1 hook:** McDowell coal $4.50 (1960) → $520–600 honest cost; "the
price was wrong by two orders of magnitude"; "four moves to make, in
order."

**The hook earns the pitch** by performing four moves in 109w:
- Names a specific American case (McDowell County, West Virginia).
- Quantifies the cost-severance arithmetic (two orders of magnitude).
- Generalizes from the case ("It is wrong now, for coal and for
  almost everything else").
- Signals the four-step mechanism architecture that the rest of the
  letter expands.

The hook does not waste a word. There is no setup-paragraph; the
arithmetic is the setup, the diagnosis, and the lever-into-mechanism all
at once. **✓✓✓.**

### §6.2 Close arc-completes back to the hook

**¶4 close:** *"My forthcoming book *Commons Bonds* develops the
framework at length. Full essay available on request."*

The close does NOT explicitly arc-complete back to the McDowell hook
(no "back to the McDowell ton" or "the four moves can be made; the
essay shows how"). The arc-completion is implicit: ¶4 is
bio-and-comp-titles-and-courtesy-close, the cover-letter convention,
NOT the essay-convention rhetorical-arc-close.

**Is this a problem?** Under Amendment D's editor-facing-pitch lens:
**no**. A cover letter that performs essay-style rhetorical
arc-completion at the close reads as overworked / performative; the
editor reads "this author thinks the cover letter is a small essay."
The convention is: cover letter ends with professional courtesy + clear
call-to-action ("Full essay available on request"). The cover letter
performs this convention cleanly. **✓✓✓ at editor-facing-pitch scale.**

(At end-reader-facing essay scale, the analogous architecture would be
the essay's own §VIII closing. The essay does carry arc-completion
back to the McDowell hook via the "what a framework does" §VIII
closing. That arc-completion is essay-load-bearing; cover-letter-load
is non-arc-completing-courtesy-close. The two artifacts have different
architectures because they have different reception chains. Amendment D
naturally captures this distinction.)

### §6.3 Four-paragraph compounding vs stacking

¶1 hook → ¶2 mechanism → ¶3 cases → ¶4 bio + comps + close.

- **¶1 → ¶2 transition:** Hook ends with "four moves to make, in
  order." ¶2 opens with "The moves are classification, reservation,
  investment, and reassessment." The transition is hand-off-clean:
  ¶1's promise (four moves) is ¶2's content (the four named).
  **Compounding, not stacking. ✓✓✓.**

- **¶2 → ¶3 transition:** ¶2 ends with "Border-adjustment ... runs as a
  cross-cutting concern of the four, not as a fifth step." ¶3 opens
  with "The not-utopian claim is anchored in working cases." The
  transition is from-mechanism-to-cases — load-bearing but adjacent;
  the editor reads it as "and here is how the mechanism is not
  hypothetical." **Compounding. ✓✓.**

- **¶3 → ¶4 transition:** ¶3 ends with "Mancur Olson's ... and Mian and
  Sufi's ... anchor the political-economy reading of why honest
  pricing has not been done." ¶4 opens with "The essay reads in the
  lineage of Mazzucato, Christophers, Pistor, and Susskind." The
  transition is from-political-economy-anchors-to-comp-titles-cluster
  — the editor reads it as moving from one named-author register
  (political-economy founding) to another (current trade-press
  comp-cluster). The hand-off is competent but **stacking-flavored**;
  ¶3's Olson/Mian-Sufi citation and ¶4's Mazzucato/Christophers/Pistor/
  Susskind cluster both function as author-name signaling in adjacent
  paragraphs, which can read as comp-shopping. **Acceptable at
  cover-letter scale.** Minor candidate spot-fix flagged in §7 below
  for completeness, but **not recommended for application** per
  Amendment D directness-vs-mediation discipline.

### §6.4 Paragraph transitions load-bearing or sequential

- ¶1 hook → ¶2 mechanism: **LOAD-BEARING** (mechanism is the
  hook's promise).
- ¶2 mechanism → ¶3 cases: **LOAD-BEARING** (cases are the
  mechanism's not-utopian anchor).
- ¶3 cases → ¶4 close: **SEQUENTIAL-LOAD-BEARING** (transition from
  argument to bio/comps/close is convention-driven, not
  argumentatively load-bearing; this is correct for cover-letter
  scale).

### §6.5 Pass 3.5 aggregate

**Architectural integrity verdict: PASS.** Hook earns the pitch
(¶1 → ¶2 hand-off clean); close arc-completes appropriately for
cover-letter convention (not for essay convention; this is correct);
four-paragraph compounding holds with one minor stacking-flavor at
¶3 → ¶4 (author-name register adjacency); paragraph transitions
load-bearing where they need to be and convention-sequential where
they should be.

**No restoration-polarity findings of HIGH or MEDIUM rank.** One LOW
candidate flagged in §7 for completeness; see directness-vs-mediation
