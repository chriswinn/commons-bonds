## 2. Editorial brain map for Atlantic Ideas specifically

**What Atlantic Ideas editors want** (versus Noema or Boston Review):

- **Argument / analysis / commentary register.** Not reportage (Noema's spine) and not institutional-measurement (BR's spine). The Ideas section publishes thesis-driven essays that take a position and defend it with mechanism + cases + comparative-historical anchoring.
- **American examples lead; international examples accent.** The Atlantic's audience is primarily American; foreign-policy material lands when it sharpens the American argument (US 2008 vs. Sweden 1932 contrast — *yes*; pure-international scope without American stake — *no*).
- **Mechanism + arithmetic + cases.** The four-step Classify→Reserve→Invest→Reassess sequence is exactly the kind of mechanism the section publishes. The McDowell $4.50/$500–600 arithmetic + Alaska Permanent Fund + Norway GPFG + Mondragon / Vienna / Chattanooga entering-wedges are exactly the kind of cases.
- **Named comp-titles fluency without name-dropping.** Mazzucato / Christophers / Pistor / Susskind / Olson / Coase / Ostrom / Buchanan + Tullock / Darity + Mullen / Mian + Sufi — Atlantic Ideas audience reads these signals as serious-membership rather than as showing off. But three names per paragraph reads as showing off; the discipline is selective deployment, with each citation doing a specific argumentative job.
- **Hook a smart-but-busy reader will finish.** The Atlantic's reader is busy and surrounded by competing reads. ¶1 has to earn ¶2; ¶1 + ¶2 have to earn ¶3. The mechanism-and-arithmetic opening (McDowell coal $4.50/$500–600) is the natural hook.
- **Clear thesis sentence in the first or second paragraph.** Not buried at the essay's end as a slow reveal. Atlantic Ideas readers want to know what claim the essay is defending by the time they reach the first section break.
- **Length discipline.** Target ~3,500–4,500w. Hard ceiling ~5,000w per Atlantic's verified web-submission norm. Pieces over 5,000w are by assignment, not unsolicited.

**What hurts Atlantic Ideas submissions** (versus Noema or BR):

- **Pure thought-experiment** (Aeon's register, not Atlantic's).
- **Memoir-led opener** (Noema's register, not Atlantic's). Personal anchors are fine if they ground the argument; they cannot carry the argument.
- **Apparatus jargon** — "existential substitutability gap" repeated as a term-of-art; RCV-as-acronym; "cluster-γ"; "Foreclosure Bond" / "Restitution Bond" as named instruments. The Atlantic Ideas editorial brain is general-intellectual, not academic.
- **Manifesto register** — "We must..." / "It is time to..." / "The future demands..." The Atlantic Ideas section reads manifesto as a sign of weak argument. The framework's posture is mechanism-and-arithmetic; preserve it.
- **Partisan brief register** — "Republicans have failed to..." / "The Democratic agenda must..." The non-partisan framing discipline is load-bearing.
- **Defensive author register** — preemptive-rebuttal-of-objections at length. (The Nordic-objection section in Ch 9 is exactly this risk; treat with discipline if it makes the essay cut.)
- **Op-ed throat-clearing** — "Last week, the news cycle was once again dominated by..." Atlantic Ideas is not the op-ed page.
