### F-DE-Atlantic-1 (HIGH) — Architectural inconsistency: §I thesis "four moves" vs §V ¶1 "third move" vs §VI ¶1 "four-item border-included list"

**Location:** §I ¶4 (line 9) + §V ¶1 (line 55) + §VI ¶1 (line 65).

**Current draft text:**

§I ¶4 thesis statement (line 9):
> If a society decided to price its extractions honestly, counting every cost the current price misses, it would have four moves to make, in order: classify what cannot be replaced, reserve a fraction of it, invest in substitutes and in alternative sources, and reassess as it learns.

§V ¶1 (line 55):
> The third move is the one that runs into the international system the way it actually works.

§VI ¶1 (line 65):
> The classification, the reserve, the investment, and the border describe accounting decisions of a kind countries make all the time.

**Restoration consideration:** The essay's structural promise has an internal inconsistency that a careful reader (Tier 1 #1 Atlantic Ideas senior editor; Tier 2 #6 Christophers-cluster) will catch on close-read:
- **§I ¶4 thesis lists FOUR moves:** classify (1), reserve (2), invest (3), reassess (4). Border is not named.
- **§V ¶1 opens with "The third move is the one that runs into the international system the way it actually works"** — claims Border is "the third move." But the third move per §I thesis is INVEST, not Border. And the §V section is about Border / CBAM / cost-severance-across-borders, not about invest-in-alternative-supply-sources.
- **§VI ¶1 names "classification, reserve, investment, and the border"** — four items, but with Border substituted for Reassess (which was the fourth move per §I thesis). Reassess is not in this list.

The essay actually delivers six sections of structural content: §II Classify + §III Reserve + §IV Invest (with Reassess folded into ¶5) + §V Border + §VI political-economy of resistance + §VII what works already + §VIII what a framework does. The thesis promise of "four moves" only covers §II–§IV + Reassess. §V (Border) is a fifth structural move that the §I thesis doesn't announce; §VI ¶1 partly acknowledges this by renaming the fourth-position item from Reassess to Border but in doing so it drops Reassess from the inventory.

The structural inconsistency isn't fatal — readers follow the essay's actual content — but the careful-read mismatch between thesis-statement promise (4 moves) and section-delivery (5 distinct moves + 3 framing sections) is a real architectural integrity gap.

**Options:**
- **(a) Add Border to §I thesis as a fifth move; remove "third move" framing from §V ¶1 (Recommended).** Make §I thesis name five moves explicitly: "...it would have five moves to make, in order: classify what cannot be replaced, reserve a fraction of it, invest in substitutes and in alternative sources, adjust the trade architecture to price what crosses borders, and reassess as it learns." Then §V ¶1 opens without numbering: "The next move runs into the international system the way it actually works." §VI ¶1 already lists Border parallel to classification + reserve + investment so it's consistent. Cost: ~+8w in thesis sentence; ~-3w in §V opener; net ~+5w. Benefit: structural-promise matches section-delivery; close-read integrity restored.
- **(b) Keep §I thesis at "four moves" but reframe §V ¶1 to drop "third move" language entirely.** §V ¶1: "Honest pricing runs into the international system the way it actually works." Border becomes a cross-cutting concern that complicates all four moves, not its own fifth move. Cost: ~-5w in §V opener. Benefit: keeps thesis statement compact + signals Border as concern rather than discrete move. Risk: §VI ¶1's "classification, reserve, investment, and the border" then doesn't list Reassess; needs separate adjustment.
- **(c) Reframe both §V ¶1 and §VI ¶1 to position Border as the international-dimension OF the four moves rather than a separate move.** §V ¶1: "Each of the four moves runs into the international system the way it actually works. Pricing honestly inside one country while trading partners do not price honestly hands the honest-pricing country a competitive disadvantage." §VI ¶1: "Classification, reserve, investment, and reassessment describe accounting decisions of a kind countries make all the time. The international dimension complicates each." Cost: ~+30w distributed across §V + §VI openings. Benefit: full structural integrity; Border framed as cross-cutting concern rather than discrete move. Risk: dilutes Border's section-level treatment.

**Recommendation:** **(a)** — add Border to §I thesis as fifth move; remove "third move" framing from §V ¶1.

**Reasoning:** The essay's section-level delivery actually treats Border as a discrete move (§V gets its own ~340w section with CBAM + Smith citation + protectionism distinction). The §VI ¶1 "classification, reserve, investment, and the border" listing already treats Border as a fourth item parallel to classify/reserve/invest. The cleanest fix is to acknowledge in §I thesis that there are five moves, not four. The two-word adjustment ("four moves" → "five moves" + adding "adjust the trade architecture to price what crosses borders" as item 4) restores structural-promise integrity with minimal text disturbance.

Option (b) preserves §I thesis but creates two new problems (§V opening still needs reframing + §VI ¶1 needs to add Reassess). Option (c) is the most architecturally-clean but expands word-count more than the integrity-restoration warrants. Option (a) is the minimal-restoration spot-fix.

This is HIGH severity because thesis-statement integrity is load-bearing for the Tier 1 #1 Atlantic Ideas senior editor's close-read; an editor who notices the mismatch on first close-read may form a "structural-issues-in-the-draft" impression that lowers acceptance probability. Pass 3.5 RESTORATION is the right pass to catch this (Pass 3.1 fact-check + Pass 3.2 voice-polish + Pass 3.3 acceptance + Pass 3.4 robustness all read the structural promise without scrutinizing internal consistency between thesis-statement and section-delivery).

> Four traditions: Marx, Ostrom, Daly, Hartwick. **Restoration consideration:** Per Pass 3.4 §3 Thread A4.2 + F-3.4-Atlantic-1 RECOMMENDED (Pass 3.4 RATIFIED 2026-05-27): the bare compression "None priced permanent foreclosure" overstates Hartwick's omission. Hartwick (1977) formally priced the case where natural capital and reproducible capital are substitutable (his rule: invest exhaustible-resource rents in reproducible capital → sustain consumption indefinitely). The case Hartwick assumed away is the IRREPLACEABILITY case — where substitutability fails because natural capital cannot be replaced by reproducible capital. The essay's framework operates precisely at that irreplaceability case (§II classify defines "the irreplaceables"). So the precise claim is: "None reached the irreplaceability case" or "None priced the case where substitution fails permanently."

Norway GPFG (cited at §VII ¶2) is an explicit Hartwick-rule operationalization (capture petroleum rents → invest in reproducible capital → maintain consumption). The Hartwick-aware reader will notice that Hartwick's rule DOES price the petroleum-foreclosure case (Norway operationalizes it; the essay's §VII ¶2 acknowledges the architecture); what Hartwick's rule does NOT reach is the case where the foreclosed resource has no substitute (rare earths in the magnets that turn wind turbines; phosphorus for agriculture; helium in industrial concentrations; fossil aquifers; the carbon budget).

**Options:**
- **(a) Replace "None priced permanent foreclosure" with "None reached the irreplaceability case" (Recommended).** Precision-tightening: the irreplaceability case is what the essay's §II Classify section then unpacks. Cost: 0w (word-substitution). Benefit: closes A4.2 + F-3.1-3 cleanly; aligns the diagnostic-set-up with the §II Classify content.
- **(b) Replace with "None priced the case where substitution fails permanently."** Alternative wording; more explicit about the substitutability-failure mechanism. Cost: +3w. Benefit: explicit-mechanism naming; Hartwick-aware reader reads it as direct engagement.
- **(c) Replace with "None reached the case the next move makes visible."** Forward-reference to §II Classify; the next-move pivot. Cost: 0w. Benefit: signals the essay's structural progression; restoration-polarity move that connects ¶3 to §II.
- **(d) Keep as-is.** The bare compression is defensible per Pass 3.4 ROBUST verdict (compression-defensible at essay length). Cost: 0w. Risk: residual Hartwick-aware-reader thread-pull that Pass 3.4 already documented.

**Recommendation:** **(a)** — replace with "None reached the irreplaceability case."

**Reasoning:** This is the precise diagnostic claim the §II Classify section delivers; the precision-tightening aligns ¶3 (diagnostic) with §II (delivery). Option (b) is slightly more explicit but adds 3w that the essay doesn't need; (c) is forward-referential which is clever but reads as more meta-rhetorical than the rest of the essay; (a) is the cleanest restoration. Per Pass 3.4 F-3.4-Atlantic-1 RATIFIED, this spot-fix is already ratified as RECOMMENDED for Pass 3.5 application.
