## §3. Per-finding inventory

### F-3.5-N2-M1 — §VIII closing-list 5-fold anaphora structural-choice (MED, KEEP-ALL-5)

**Severity:** MED (load-bearing for closing pivot's analytical weight; structural-choice question worth surfacing for author
ratification rather than implicit-acceptance).

**Site.** §VIII closing paragraph (essay-body line 226 of V-D hybrid; the second sentence after "Return now to the coastline
with which the reading began...").

**Observation.** V-D restores a 5-fold anaphora at the §VIII closing position, expanding the locked-cut's single-anchor
formulation ("The next contamination, on the next coastline...") into five "the next X that [does Y]" clauses:

1. "the next sovereign default that prices a debtor nation's fiscal policy in a courtroom on another continent" (Pistor —
   choice-of-law module + *NML Capital v. Argentina* case)
2. "the next pharmaceutical molecule priced for extraction rather than dissemination" (Mazzucato — Sovaldi case)
3. "the next renewable-energy project that fails to clear the asset-manager hurdle rate" (Christophers — hurdle-rate failure)
4. "the next family trust that survives the political-economic system that produced its wealth" (Pistor — trust module)
5. "the next contamination on the next coastline in whatever extractive sector or financialized arrangement or intergenerational
   obligation it happens to operate" (locked-cut framing-rhyme preserved as 5th anchor)

The main clause then arrives: "will pass through the same modules, register the same value-extraction signature, fail to be
priced by the same mechanism, and disappear into the same aggregate metric that hides its cost."

The structural question is COMPOUND (KEEP-ALL-5) vs CROWD (TRIM-TO-3-or-4 vs REVERT-TO-LOCKED-CUT-SINGLE-ANCHOR).

**Pro-COMPOUND arguments (KEEP-ALL-5).**
- Each anchor 1–4 maps to a specific reviewed book's case material, making the cumulative review of all four books concrete
  at the rhetorical close. This is exactly the analytical work a strong NYRB review-essay does at close — anchor the synthesis
  back to specific case material rather than leaving it gestural.
- The 5th anchor preserves the opening-scene framing-rhyme discipline (Stage 1 brief §I → §VIII coastline-return).
- The 4-fold main clause ("modules / value-extraction signature / mechanism / aggregate metric") maps the four-book schema
  (location / activity / mechanism / metric). The 5+4 asymmetry is purposeful: 4 reviewed-book anchors + 1 closing-image anchor
  → 4 schema-elements consequence.
- The asymmetric Pistor double-coverage (#1 sovereign default + #4 family trust) is justified because Pistor's modules are the
  institutional-location anchor for the architecture-of-accountability frame — Pistor's specifics warrant bilateral framing.

**Pro-CROWD arguments (TRIM consideration).**
- 5 subjects sits one above the comfortable English-prose anaphora threshold (the "rule of four" is the upper edge before
  anaphora starts feeling catalog rather than sentence).
- The 5th clause's length (22w including its internal rule-of-three "extractive sector or financialized arrangement or
  intergenerational obligation") is disproportionate to anchors 1–4 (avg 9w each), creating a reader-fatigue spike right before
  the main clause arrives.
- Total sentence runs ~120w before reaching the main clause; reader-load is high.
- LLM-cadence risk at 8,000+ words is highest at exposed rhetorical positions (the close); 5-fold anaphora is exactly the
  cadence pattern Pass 3.2 voice-polish discipline flags as risk.

**Resolution.** The compound DOES analytical work — anchors 1–4 perform a cumulative case-material recap that the locked-cut
left as gesture. The work-done justifies the length. The 5th-clause length disproportion is a Pass 3.2 voice-polish concern
(cut polarity), not a Pass 3.5 restoration concern (restore polarity); flagging it for awareness rather than as a Pass 3.5
finding.

**Options:**
- **Option A — COMPOUND-KEEP-ALL-5 (recommended).** Accept the V-D restoration as drafted. The 4-book-case-material recap is
  load-bearing for the closing pivot's analytical weight; reverting to locked-cut would forfeit a meaningful restoration.
- **Option B — CROWD-TRIM-TO-4.** Drop one anchor (likely #4 family trust, since Pistor double-coverage is the asymmetry that
  most reads as catalog). Retains the cumulative-recap reach while reducing reader-load.
- **Option C — CROWD-TRIM-TO-3.** Keep one anchor per reviewed-book schema-element (sovereign default for Pistor; pharma for
  Mazzucato; renewable for Christophers; drop Susskind-coverage — but Susskind's metric IS the contamination-on-coastline 5th
  anchor's implicit consequence). Risks losing the Pistor double-coverage that the schema warrants.
- **Option D — REVERT-TO-LOCKED-CUT-SINGLE-ANCHOR.** Roll back the V-D restoration entirely. Loses the cumulative-case-material
  reach the V-D restoration achieves.

**Recommendation: Option A — COMPOUND-KEEP-ALL-5.**

**Reasoning.** The V-D restoration converts the locked-cut's single-anchor close from gesture to specific cumulative case-material
recap. For a literary-critical NYRB review-essay where the closing rhetorical position is the synthesis crescendo, the analytical
work the compound does outweighs the reader-load it imposes. The 5th clause's internal rule-of-three is the only sub-component
worth flagging for possible Pass 3.2 re-fire consideration, but that's deferrable and not Pass 3.5's polarity. Drop-one
alternatives (Options B/C) would forfeit either the Pistor double-coverage (which the institutional-location anchor warrants)
or the framing-rhyme discipline (which Stage 1 §I coastline-opening commits the essay to honor).

**Cross-political-spectrum check.** None of the 5 anchors signals partisan register; each is a structural diagnosis of the
architecture the essay's reviewed books describe. Condition NR-1 unaffected.

**Pass 3.4 robustness check.** None of the 5 anchors introduces new adversarial-surface risk. Pass 3.4 re-fire NOT required.

**Pass 3.2 voice-polish flag for awareness only.** If a Pass 3.2 re-fire is convened (not required by this Pass 3.5 finding), the
5th clause's internal rule-of-three "extractive sector or financialized arrangement or intergenerational obligation" could be
tightened to a doublet or single anchor. This was present in the locked-cut (already ratified by Pass 3.2 2026-05-27) and becomes
more conspicuous as the tail of a 5-fold compound vs being the single anchor; whether the locked-cut ratification carries forward
unchanged or warrants re-evaluation is a Pass 3.2 question outside this audit's scope.
