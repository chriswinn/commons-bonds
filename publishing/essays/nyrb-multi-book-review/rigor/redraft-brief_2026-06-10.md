## 6. Prose rules (hard requirements; embed in the drafting session verbatim)

1. **ZERO em-dashes and en-dashes in prose.** This includes date ranges: write "1936 to 1948," "between 1980 and 2020," never "1936–1948." Verify with grep before returning the draft.
2. **Antithesis budget:** "Not X. It is Y." / "is not A; it is B" constructions at most ~1 per 1,500 words (≈5 in this essay). Vary paragraph endings; some paragraphs simply end; NO aphorism-epigram at every paragraph or section close.
3. **NO self-narration:** never narrate what the essay/book/framework is doing or about to do ("Hold onto that...", "The chapter has done its work", "I want to be specific...", "this essay's argument is..."). The disclaimer "the framework does not legislate" may appear AT MOST once (here: zero; "the framework" never appears at all per §7). "The framework" is never the subject of consecutive sentences (moot here; it is banned outright).
4. **NO white-paper register:** terminology defenses = one sentence + a pointer at most, never a digression (here: no Technical Appendix pointer exists; elide instead); no Greek-letter regime names or parameter intervals in running prose; numbers as numerals (no spelled-out number walls); no workshop vocabulary (steelman, pressure-test); no archival/process vocabulary ("named ... in the record").

This essay is the portfolio's purest lit-positioning instrument: it reviews the adjacent literature itself.
