### 3.2 Reveal-discipline risk surface

The risk is NOT that the essay reaches 8% by direct apparatus surface. The risk is that a **6,000–10,000-word review-essay reviewing 4 books on pricing/value/extraction naturally wants to surface the framework's analytical apparatus to make the review's positive contribution legible.** A reviewer-essay that critiques four books has to do more than say "these four books are all good" — it has to point at what they're each missing or what the cumulative pattern reveals. The framework's "what they're each missing" punchline IS its apparatus. Translating that apparatus to plain prose means losing the structural-clarity force that makes the apparatus distinctive — which then risks the essay reading as "yet another synthesis review" rather than "the synthesis review that names what the others have been circling."

Two scope-discipline calibrations protect the ≤8% target:

- **Calibration 1 — Negative-space construction.** The essay constructs the "what's missing" punchline as a **negative-space observation** (across these four books, no one has yet operationalized the corrective at the per-extraction-event mechanism level) rather than as a positive-naming move. The reader infers that someone could build the corrective; the essay does not name the builder or the apparatus. This is the discipline that holds reveal at concept-only.
- **Calibration 2 — Reviewer-not-architect register.** The essay's voice is reviewer-synthesizing-across-four-books, NOT framework-architect-summarizing-his-own-work. NYRB readers expect the reviewer to have intellectual depth that informs the synthesis — that depth IS what makes the review distinctive — but the depth shows up as reading-acuity, not as authorial-system-construction.

Per Stage 1 brief discipline (if Stage 0 verdict converts to GO): apparatus-exclusion list embedded explicitly; concept-only register for cost-severance + pricing-gap + accountability-architecture; reveal-target ≤8% verified at Pass 3.3 with cumulative-portfolio audit.

#### Two critique-registers (the load-bearing brief decision)

**Register A — Signal-positioning critique (RECOMMENDED).** The essay positions each reviewed author's work as a *diagnostic foundation* that the framework's analytical move *extends* or *operationalizes*. The "what's missing" across the four books is framed not as critique-of-each-author but as **what no one has yet built across all four diagnoses cumulatively**: an accountability architecture that prices the severance the diagnoses identify. Each author's analysis is treated as load-bearing; the essay's contribution is to notice that the four diagnoses *cumulatively point at a corrective none of them constructs*. This is the **"diagnose-AND-price-AND-design"** posture explicitly recognized in the framework's Darity bibliography entry (`research/literature/bibliography.md` line 549) — Darity & Mullen exemplify it; the four reviewed books each do parts of it; none of them does all three.

  - **Why this works for blurbing:** Each reviewed author can read the essay as a generous engagement that *positions their work as foundational to the constructive move the essay names*. Mazzucato's value-extraction critique becomes the platform the framework's pricing instrument stands on; Pistor's legal-architecture diagnosis becomes the institutional location the bonds attach to; Christophers' price-failure argument becomes the mechanism-level evidence the framework's RCV corrects; Susskind's growth-redirection becomes the macro analog of the framework's per-extraction-event redirection. Blurb path stays open — likely strengthened — because each author sees their work as part of the framework's intellectual ancestry.

**Register B — Naive critique (NOT RECOMMENDED).** The essay critiques each book's limits as failures or oversights — "Mazzucato names the disease but doesn't prescribe; Pistor names the modules but doesn't price; Christophers names the failure but doesn't fix; Susskind names the metric but doesn't operationalize." This register lands the same observation but in a tone that codes as superior-judgment-of-the-reviewer.

  - **Why this fails for blurbing:** Each reviewed author can read the essay as condescending or as positioning their work as inadequate to the corrective the reviewer claims. Mazzucato's already-declined substantive ask 2026-05-21 makes her especially sensitive to register; a public review read as condescending forecloses blurb path. Pistor + Christophers + Susskind similarly. The essay loses the comp-title-blurb base that Wave 1 platform paragraph depends on.

**Critique-register guidance for Stage 1 brief (PM-recommended):**

> The essay's critique-register MUST be Register A — signal-positioning. Every paragraph that engages a reviewed author's argument MUST position the author's contribution as foundational + analytically necessary + extending in a direction the essay's synthesis names. Phrase patterns: "Mazzucato shows X — the question is then how to operationalize the corrective her diagnosis demands" rather than "Mazzucato shows X but stops short of Y." The latter phrasing is BANNED in the brief.
>
> Verification at Pass 3.3: include each of the four reviewed authors as a pressure-test character (alongside the standard center-right policy reader + heterodox-econ + literary-essay readers). Pressure-test verdict for each reviewed author MUST return INCLUDE before the essay clears Stage 3.

This is the **single most load-bearing Stage 1 brief specification** for this candidate. If the brief leaves critique-register to Stage 2 drafting discretion, the risk is non-trivial that the essay drifts toward Register B — review-essays naturally want to take positions; the discipline of taking positions WITHOUT taking-positions-against the reviewed authors needs to be specified explicitly, not left to drafter judgment.

- **Net direction:** **HELPS book-success criteria #1 + #2** (blurb-network attraction strengthens; Wave 1 platform paragraph gets a "review-essay engaging with my work landed in NYRB" line for each of the four reviewed authors) — **IF** Register A holds throughout Stage 1 + Stage 2 + Stage 3.
