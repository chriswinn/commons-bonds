## Carry 1: John Broome into the lineage-of-near-misses (L73 / §"A lineage of near-misses")

### What it is

The essay's lineage section ("A lineage of near-misses," and its
recap at L73) surveys serious economic and philosophical thought that
circled the residual-commons-value plot without building on it: Marx
(surplus), Ostrom (renewable governance), Daly (throughput), Hartwick
(saving the rent), Rawls / Sandel / Parfit (future-generations claim),
Stern / Nordhaus / Weitzman (the discount-rate fight).

**John Broome is the missing near-miss on the moral-discounting axis.**
He is the philosopher-economist who made the cleanest case that the
discount rate is a moral choice rather than a technical measurement,
and who pressed the further step toward a *duty to make restitution*
for emissions. He sits exactly between the Rawls / Sandel / Parfit
cluster (the future holds a claim no market price discharges) and the
Stern / Nordhaus / Weitzman cluster (the discipline argues about the
worth of the distant future). His work is the bridge between those two
groups the essay already names, and including him tightens the claim
that the discipline "circled" this without arriving.

Why he is a *near-miss* and not the thing itself (the load-bearing
point, which must be preserved if added): Broome prices, and argues we
owe restitution for, the **damage flow** from emissions, meaning the
harm a burned barrel does on its way into the future. That is the same
axis Stern and Nordhaus are on. He still does not price the **barrel's
plain absence**, the present fact that a finite, non-renewable thing is
permanently foreclosed, distinct from the damages its combustion
causes. So Broome strengthens the lineage without collapsing the
essay's central distinction (residual commons value lives outside the
discount-rate / damage-flow axis). He gets *closer* than most precisely
because he already treats discounting as moral and already reaches for
restitution, which is why he belongs in the recap, and why naming the
one step he still does not take sharpens the gap rather than blurring it.

### Citation (substrate-safe; real cited works only)

- John Broome, *Counting the Cost of Global Warming* (report to the
  Economic and Social Research Council; with David Ulph), 1992. The
  early, rigorous statement that the choice of discount rate is an
  ethical judgment, not a fact of nature.
- John Broome, *Climate Matters: Ethics in a Warming World* (W. W.
  Norton, 2012). The popular-register statement of the
  discount-rate-is-moral argument plus the individual / collective
  **duty to offset and make restitution** for one's emissions.

Use whichever fits register; *Climate Matters* (2012, Norton) is the
trade-press-legible one and pairs naturally with the essay's existing
trade cadence. The 1992 ESRC report is the precision anchor if a
fact-checker wants the earliest formal statement.

### Where to insert (one line, two joints)

The lineage appears twice and a clean version adds Broome in both, or
just the recap. Minimal version: add Broome to the **L73 recap roll
call** only. Fuller version: add a sentence in the body section too.

**Recap (L73), drop-in pattern (PROPOSED prose, author to redline):**

The current L73 roll call reads (paraphrase of structure):
"…Rawls and Sandel and Parfit established that the future holds a
claim no market price discharges; Stern and Nordhaus and Weitzman
taught the discipline to argue about the worth of the distant
future…"

Broome's natural slot is **between** those two clauses, because he is
the bridge:

> …Rawls and Sandel and Parfit established that the future holds a
> claim no market price discharges; John Broome showed that the
> discount rate by which we weigh that claim is itself a moral choice,
> and went as far as a duty to make restitution for the harm; Stern and
> Nordhaus and Weitzman taught the discipline to argue about the worth
> of the distant future, even as they argued about the wrong quantity.

(That single clause is the whole Carry-1 footprint in the recap. It
keeps the "wrong quantity" payoff intact, because Broome too argues
about the damage flow, not the foreclosed barrel.)

**Body (optional, fuller version):** a short paragraph in "A lineage
of near-misses," placed after Rawls / Sandel / Parfit and before the
discount-rate "battleground" section, stating: the discount rate is a
moral choice (Broome), and a duty of restitution follows from it
(Broome), but restitution for the *damage*, still not a price on the
*absence*. Keep it to two or three sentences; it must land the same
"near-miss, not arrival" beat the other figures land.

### Polarity / guardrails

- Pure addition; nothing is cut.
- No new figures, no dollar amounts. Broome is a conceptual / lineage
  cite, not a quantitative one.
- Preserve the "argued about the wrong quantity" close so the

## Carry 2: Fanning & Hickel 2023 / CARICOM / Loss & Damage as nearest-kin priced instrument (L75 / §"What remains to be built" close)

### What it is

The essay's close (L75) already does the deeper-frame move: Polanyi's
fictitious commodities (1944), Fraser's widening to nature / care /
futures, and the **bidirectional** point, namely two missing prices,
the backward-looking bill for damage already done (restitution, making
it right) and the forward-looking worth of what we are about to
foreclose (choosing well). That bidirectional structure is the perfect
joint for the nearest-kin instrument, because the **backward-looking
column** is exactly where a priced climate-debt instrument already
lives in the real world.

**The nearest kin to the essay's novelty is the priced-climate-debt
literature and the Loss & Damage policy track.** The essay says these
two columns "can be filled"; the honest move in a future version is to
note that one of them is *already being filled* by serious people,
which strengthens the "this is real, not a hunch" claim, while the
essay's own contribution (pricing the *absence*, the forward-looking
foreclosure column, distinct from the damage column) remains the
unbuilt piece. This is a **credit-the-field** extension, not a
concession.

Two real referents, both public-record:

- **Fanning & Hickel (2023)**, "Compensation for atmospheric
  appropriation," *Nature Sustainability*. The most rigorous recent
  attempt to *price* the backward-looking climate debt (compensation
  owed for historical over-appropriation of the atmospheric commons).
- **CARICOM and the Loss & Damage track.** The political-institutional
  expression of the same backward-looking claim: the Caribbean bloc's
  long-standing reparations and climate-justice advocacy, and the Loss
  & Damage fund adopted at COP27 (2022) and operationalized at COP28
  (2023), the first multilateral instrument to name and fund the bill
  for harm already inflicted on those who did not cause it.

These map onto the essay's *first* missing column (the damage already
done, "what it would take to make it right"). They do NOT touch the
*second* column (the worth of what we are about to foreclose, the
present absence of the barrel), which remains the essay's distinctive,
unbuilt contribution. That asymmetry is the whole reason this is a
lineage extension and not a scoop: the field is filling one column; the
essay is the one naming the other.

### Citation (substrate-safe)

- Andrew L. Fanning and Jason Hickel, "Compensation for atmospheric
  appropriation," *Nature Sustainability* 6 (2023).
- CARICOM climate-justice / reparations advocacy (public-record bloc
  position); Loss & Damage fund, UNFCCC COP27 (2022) decision plus
  COP28 (2023) operationalization (public-record multilateral process).

### Where to insert (one to two sentences, at L75)

The L75 paragraph already names the two columns (damage already done /
damage not yet done) and says "Both columns have been left blank, and
the argument of this essay is only that they can be filled." The carry
adjusts only that last beat: the backward-looking column is **not
entirely blank**. It is being filled, imperfectly and against
resistance, by the priced-climate-debt and Loss & Damage work, while
the forward-looking column remains the essay's own unbuilt piece.

**Drop-in pattern (PROPOSED prose, author to redline):**

Replace / extend the "Both columns have been left blank" beat with
something like:

> The backward-looking column is no longer entirely blank. The Loss
> and Damage fund agreed at the 2022 climate summit, and the work of
> economists like Andrew Fanning and Jason Hickel to price the
> compensation owed for a century of atmospheric over-appropriation,
> are, whatever one makes of their particular figures, the first
> serious attempts to fill it, to put a number on the bill for harm
> already done to people who did not cause it. The forward-looking
> column, the worth of what we are about to foreclose, is the one this
> essay is about, and it is the one still written as zero.

Then the existing final beat ("the argument of this essay is only that
they can be filled" leading to "stop writing zero") follows cleanly and
is in fact *strengthened*: the future version can say the first column
proves the second is fillable, since the same discipline is already
filling one.

### Polarity / guardrails (CRITICAL: coercion/reparations figure)

- **NO coercion / reparations dollar figure in author-voice prose.**
  Per the ratified discipline (`feedback_coercion_pricing_first_person_abstention.md`),
  the author declines, first-person, to assign a coercion / reparations
  dollar figure. That guardrail governs this carry absolutely.
- **Credit the field; do NOT adopt its number.** Fanning & Hickel's
  headline figure (a historical-compensation estimate in the
  hundred-trillion-dollar range circulated in 2023 coverage of the
  paper) **must not be imported into author-voice prose as an endorsed
  figure.** The drop-in above is deliberately built to credit the work
  ("the first serious attempts to fill it") while the hedge "whatever
  one makes of their particular figures" holds the author's
  first-person abstention. Keep that hedge or an equivalent. The
  author may *credit the field* (name the authors, the instrument, the
  attempt); he does not *fill that column* with a number of his own and
  does not adopt theirs.
- **No standing-gatekeeping language.** Do not write "theirs to enter"
  or "only the affected" framing. The author takes no position on who
  else may price the backward-looking column; he simply notes it is
  being priced and that his contribution is the forward-looking one.
- Pure addition to architecture already present; the Polanyi / Fraser /
  bidirectional close is untouched in structure. This lands inside the
  bidirectional move it already makes.
- Source-manifest follow-on: add a Fanning & Hickel (2023) row and a
  Loss & Damage / CARICOM row to `source-manifest.md` (a new
  "Nearest-kin priced instruments" sub-table) when applied; flag the
  figure as **cited-as-others'-work-not-adopted** so a fact-checker
  does not read it as an author claim.
