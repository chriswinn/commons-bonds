## 1. Methodology and verdict format

**Pass 3.3 (acceptance) verdict format:** Per [Stage 1 brief §12](stage-1-brief.md) and [`audience-pressure-test-construction.md`](../drafting-templates/audience-pressure-test-construction.md) §3.3:

- ✓✓✓ — Strong INCLUDE; this character would not just accept the piece, they'd recommend it onward
- ✓✓ — INCLUDE; the piece lands cleanly for this character
- ✓ — Weak INCLUDE; the piece passes but doesn't excite
- NEUTRAL — Neither INCLUDE nor EXCLUDE; the character would read it without reaction
- ⚠ — Weak EXCLUDE-risk; minor concerns that could be addressed
- ⚠⚠ — EXCLUDE-risk; the piece has substantive problems for this character
- ⚠⚠⚠ — Strong EXCLUDE-risk; this character would reject or pan the piece

**Condition 1 dispositive test:** Per Q1 rigor pass §6 + Stage 1 brief §9, Tier 1 character #3 (center-right policy reader) is the GATING audience. Any ⚠⚠ or ⚠⚠⚠ on character #3 = ship-blocking. Adversarial A4 (reactionary intellectual reader) is the second venue for the same Condition 1 test.

**Pass 3.4 (robustness) verdict format:** Per [`audience-pressure-test-construction.md`](../drafting-templates/audience-pressure-test-construction.md) §3.4 thread-pull synthesis:

- **ROBUST** — Adversarial threads don't compound into a structural problem; spot-fixes (if any) are register-level
- **CONDITIONALLY ROBUST** — Adversarial threads identify specific spot-fixes; piece ships after spot-fix application
- **REQUIRES STRUCTURAL ENGAGEMENT** — Adversarial threads identify a structural pattern that needs more than spot-fixes; piece returns to Stage 2 or a cross-chapter workstream

**Methodology disclosure:** Per-character profiles are not re-described here; reference Stage 1 brief §1 Tier 1–3 + adversarial set §1. Each character entry below opens with the draft-A reading, draft-B reading, and the comparative diff + per-draft verdict. Roughly 250–350w per character entry; ~4,000w per pass; ~1,500w for book-strategy §; ~1,500w for aggregate verdict.

## 2. Pass 3.3 — Tier 1 (gating; 5 characters)

### 2.1 Phenomenal World editorial brain (Stage 1 brief §1 character #1)

**Draft A reading.** Opens with a concrete pump-price line ("crept up again... past where it sat last year") and pivots fast to the thought-experiment. The §I move to *what does the hundred dollars represent?* runs through spot price minus extraction, demand forecasts, time-discount, and — most consequentially for PW — names Hotelling rent in the technical-literature register. This is a fluency signal. PW editors know Hotelling. They will register the move as "this writer is inside the resource-economics literature, not adjacent to it." The §V Stern-Nordhaus engagement is rich: UK Treasury named as commissioning body, Stern's moral-not-empirical positioning on the 1.4% rate quoted accurately, Nordhaus's market-rate-of-return justification for 4.3% engaged. The §VI inline definition of *cost severance* ("the party that captures the value of an extraction is not the party that absorbs the burden, and there is no instrument that bills the costs back") is the kind of mechanism-naming sentence PW publishes routinely; it's heterodox-econ-fluent without academic-jargon-dense. The over-thirty-billion-barrels-from-the-Niger-Delta figure adds the verifiable-specificity PW fact-checks for.

**Draft B reading.** Opens softer — salience-only ("the political class is arguing about it again on cable news"), no year-over-year directional claim, no Hotelling reference. The §V engagement covers the same ground but with less Stern-position-fidelity richness (no "moral position more than an empirical one" line; no "small probability that the future will not exist" gloss on Stern's reasoning). The Norway treatment includes the "modestly prosperous social democracy with a fishing fleet and a merchant marine and a population then under four million" vignette which Draft A doesn't have — that's a strong stage-setting detail. But *cost severance* is omitted entirely; the framework's mechanism vocabulary lands in Draft B as one term (*residual commons value*) rather than two. The Hotelling-rent absence is a soft cost — PW editors won't grade Draft B down for missing it, but Draft A's presence of it is a measurable plus.

**Diff + verdicts.** Draft A signals more resource-econ literature fluency (Hotelling, UK Treasury, AER citation, *cost severance* defined). Draft B signals more essayistic care (the Norway vignette, the softer hook, less apparatus). PW editors weight intellectual-fluency signals heavier than essayistic-elegance signals for the gating decision; the venue is heterodox-econ-and-political-economy, not literary-essay.
- **Draft A:** ✓✓✓
- **Draft B:** ✓✓

### 2.3 Center-right policy reader (Stage 1 brief §1 character #3 — **CONDITION 1 DISPOSITIVE TEST**)

This is the gating audience for the entire essay. A ⚠⚠ or ⚠⚠⚠ here converts the piece to NO-GO and reverts cross-thread item #4 to Atlantic Ideas. The test is whether a *Reason* / *National Review* / AEI-econ-register reader reads the piece as **(a)** scholarly inventory of competing economic schools + structural-observation move outside Stern/Nordhaus axis + honest-accounting-principle-of-honest-dealing → INCLUDE; **(b)** left-academic essay deploying scholarly inventory to derive left-policy conclusion → EXCLUDE.

**Draft A reading.** The Condition 1 disarming work is **LOUD**. The reader cannot miss it. §III paragraph 3 stops the prose to say it explicitly: "Note the move I have just made. I have placed Marx in the lineage map as one tradition among several. I am not endorsing his political program. I am noting where his analytical apparatus does and does not reach. The same discipline applies to the figures who come next." A skim-read center-right reader registers this paragraph as the author addressing them directly. §IV does the same job for Rawls: "I want to name what I am doing here. I am using Rawls as one frame among several that produce the same intuition." And then the most center-right-friendly sentence in either draft: "Readers who find Rawls unpersuasive should ask themselves whether they find Sandel and Parfit equally unpersuasive. The intuition that compounds across all three is what the essay relies on." This is the author saying, *I am not asking you to accept Rawls. I am asking you to notice three different traditions converging.* The §V positioning is equally explicit: "The framework I am sketching is not a fourth position on the discount rate." §VI lands the locked register-anchor at full volume: "The principle that whoever creates a cost should bear it... is not a left-wing argument. It is not a right-wing argument. It is a principle of honest dealing."

A center-right reader reading Draft A on a normal-attention pass cannot complete the piece while reading it as left-academic-essay-deploying-scholarly-inventory-to-derive-left-policy-conclusion. The disarming work is too visible. The reader may not agree with the framework's substantive conclusions, but they will read the piece as the author intended: as scholarly inventory + structural observation + honest-accounting principle.

**Draft B reading.** The Condition 1 disarming work is **PRESENT BUT QUIET**. §III paragraph 2 contains the disarming work in flowing prose: "Marx is in the lineage map here as a matter of intellectual inventory: a major nineteenth-century critic of how surpluses are captured. The question of whether his analysis of labor is correct, or whether his political conclusions follow, is a separate question from the one this essay is asking. The point is that even a thinker who pressed hard on what capitalism extracts, and from whom, did not press on what extraction permanently removes from the world that comes after." This is good prose. It accomplishes the disarming work substantively. But a center-right reader on a skim-read may not notice that the move was made. The disarming is folded into a paragraph that mostly does other work (Marx-as-surplus-of-labor-critique).

§IV does the same: "Rawls is one philosopher producing this intuition. Michael Sandel, in *What Money Can't Buy*, argues from a different starting point..." Again, the work is done. But the work is not announced. A skim-read center-right reader registers "Rawls + Sandel + Parfit named" but may not register "the writer is signaling that no single tradition is being privileged."

§V is the most parallel between the two drafts: "the foreclosure cost survives even Stern's already-low discount rate... The honest accounting move is not to win the discount-rate debate from one side; it is to notice that the debate, as currently structured, is about a different thing." This is good and clear. §VI preserves the locked register-anchor verbatim.

The Draft B problem isn't that the disarming work is absent. It's present in every section. The problem is **visibility on skim-read**. The center-right reader audience is, by stipulation, predisposed to read PW pieces as tribe-coded. They are skimming for tribe-signals. Draft B's disarming work requires close reading to be noticed; Draft A's announces itself.

**Diff + verdicts.** Draft A wins decisively on the dispositive Condition 1 test. The trade-off is real — Draft A's explicit-meta paragraphs are slightly closer to LLM-tic throat-clearing on the prose-craft axis, and a confident PW reader may find them slightly labored. But the cost is on the PW reader (#2, already INCLUDE for both) and on the literary-agent audience (#4, where Draft B wins; see below). The gating audience is dispositive. Draft B is not failed here, but the margin is narrower than Draft A's.

- **Draft A:** ✓✓✓ — passes Condition 1 with margin to spare. The center-right reader walks away with the intended read.
- **Draft B:** ✓ — passes Condition 1 on close reading; ⚠ risk on skim-read. The disarming work is present but not visible enough to guarantee the intended read on a skim. **This is the load-bearing risk for Draft B at Stage 3.**

## 7. Strategic lens — Does publishing this essay help the book?

The user's second question: which essay is the better article, **and** does publishing that essay help the book? Both questions are real; they're not the same question. The first is local (which prose wins the audience tests). The second is portfolio-level (does adding this artifact to the public record advance the book's trade-publisher pipeline).

### 7.1 The portfolio context

Per [cascade plan](../../publishing/essays/_pipeline/archive/cascade-plan_2026-05-06.md) + Stage 1 brief §13 + [cross-thread-todos](../../publishing/essays/_pipeline/cross-thread-todos.md):

- The PW essay sits in **slot 3** of the essay portfolio (after Noema + Aeon). Firing the PW essay de facto resolves cross-thread item #4 (PW vs Atlantic Ideas for slot-3).
- **Wave 1 platform paragraph** (late July / early August 2026 agent queries) cites essays in the form "under consideration at" or "published in" each venue. PW credit lands in the Wave 1 platform paragraph regardless of which draft ships.
- **Center-of-gravity monitoring** flag: if Wave 1 reviewer reads platform paragraph as academic-left-tilted, calibrate by emphasizing Aeon + Noema + BR + op-eds' centrist register. PW is the venue most exposed to this read.

### 7.2 Net asset to the book — yes (either draft)

Publishing a PW essay on residual commons value is a net asset to the book's pipeline, for four reasons:

1. **Comp-cluster legibility for trade publishers.** The book sits in the Mazzucato / Pistor / Christophers / Sandel / Tooze / Boyce / Susskind cluster. PW publication places the author visibly inside this cluster. Trade acquisitions editor at Random House / Crown / Norton / Knopf reading the platform paragraph: "they've published in PW — they're in the Mazzucato / Pistor / Christophers conversation." This is the single highest-leverage register signal in the platform paragraph.

2. **Review-network compatibility.** PW publication signals to reviewer-of-record network that the book will be reviewed by reviewers fluent in Stern-Nordhaus + Hartwick + Ostrom + Daly + Mazzucato territory. That review network is exactly the network the book wants.

3. **Public naming of the framework.** Once *residual commons value* is named in PW under the author's name, the framework has a defensible public origin. The book's longer treatment references the PW essay as the public-debut artifact. This is how Mazzucato (*Project Syndicate* and *Foreign Affairs* essays before *The Value of Everything*), Pistor (*Project Syndicate* before *Code of Capital*), and Boyce (multi-decade essay run before *Economics for People and the Planet*) all worked.

4. **Pipeline-strengthening for Atlantic Ideas / Harper's / NYRB.** A PW publication credit on the platform paragraph improves the readability of subsequent submissions to Atlantic / Harper's / NYRB by signaling "this person has been published in serious heterodox-econ venue." The PW credit doesn't replace those venues — it strengthens the case for getting in.

### 7.3 Concerns to mitigate (apply to either draft)

1. **Platform-paragraph tribe-coding risk.** PW masthead reads as academic-left-tilted to some Wave 1 agents (Wylie cluster). Mitigation per Stage 1 brief §13: emphasize Aeon + Noema + BR + op-eds' centrist register in the agent-query letter; treat PW as one register-of-record alongside others rather than as the lead venue.

2. **Op-ed pipeline consistency.** Op-eds use "honest pricing / parallel transaction" framing without naming the framework. PW essay names *residual commons value* (and Draft A also names *cost severance*). If op-eds and PW essay fire near-simultaneously, an attentive reviewer might wonder about the framework's vocabulary discipline. Mitigation: stage op-eds before or after the PW essay rather than simultaneously; if simultaneous publication is unavoidable, ensure the op-ed pipeline lands the same framework-implicit register.

3. **Cross-thread item #4 disposition.** Firing the PW essay resolves slot-3 toward PW. The author still has the option to pursue Atlantic Ideas later as a separate Ch 9 pitch. If Atlantic Ideas is preserved for a future slot, the centrist-balance concern (#1 above) is partially mitigated by the future centrist credit.

### 7.4 Draft-A vs Draft-B book-strategy comparison

**Draft A book-strategy advantages:**
- Two framework concepts publicly named (*residual commons value* + *cost severance*) — signals "this person has built more than one piece of the framework" to trade-publisher track. The two-named-concepts pattern reads as book-length material more confidently than one-named-concept.
- Hotelling rent reference + AER citation specificity + UK Treasury detail signal resource-econ-credentialed engagement; strengthens reviewer-network compatibility with the academic-econ side of the comp cluster.
- The "constitutional in a way nothing else has" Norway framing is the most quotable single sentence in either draft; trade-publisher editors and book reviewers quote phrases like this when positioning the book.

**Draft B book-strategy advantages:**
- *cost severance* held in reserve for book debut; the book retains a naming-move on this term.
- Trade-press voice register reads more naturally; less risk of platform-paragraph reading as academic.
- The "modestly prosperous social democracy with a fishing fleet and a merchant marine" Norway vignette is the kind of vivid trade-press writing trade-publisher reviewers cite.

**The trade-off:**
- Draft A is the better essay for the **PW editorial brain test, the Condition 1 dispositive test, the academic comp-cluster signaling, and the orthodox-econ adversarial defense.** It's the better essay full stop on the audit's most heavily-weighted dimensions.
- Draft B is the better essay for the **literary agent / trade-acquisitions register, working-class / first-gen accessibility, and the prose-craft-elegance dimension.** It's competitive on the book-strategy axis only insofar as the trade-publisher track weights voice register heavier than framework-vocabulary specificity.

The trade-publisher track historically weights both — Mazzucato's *Project Syndicate* essays carry framework vocabulary (*The Value of Everything*'s "creator vs extractor" dichotomy was named in essays before the book) and Mazzucato is read as both academic-econ-credentialed and trade-press-essayistic. The pattern in the comp cluster is: name the framework vocabulary in essays, polish the voice register in essays, and use the book to elaborate both. Draft A fits this pattern more directly.

**Net book-strategy verdict:** Draft A serves the book's trade-publisher pipeline better. The framework-vocabulary specificity (two named concepts) signals book-length material; the resource-econ-credentialed engagement (Hotelling + AER) strengthens academic comp-cluster signaling; the loud Condition 1 disarming work protects against the platform-paragraph tribe-coding risk that PW publication carries. Draft B's voice-register advantage is real but does not outweigh these four asymmetries.

### 7.5 What publishing does *not* do for the book

- It does not substitute for the book's substantive framework-building. The essay names a gap; the book has to fill it.
- It does not lock in any framework vocabulary as permanent. The book can refine, rename, or recompose the vocabulary as needed. The essay's role is debut-the-gap, not lock-the-language.
- It does not resolve the trade-publisher acquisition decision. PW publication strengthens the case for acquisition; it does not produce the acquisition.
- It does not produce review-network alignment by itself. The platform paragraph + the comp-cluster legibility + the review-network compatibility all compound; PW publication is one input among several.
