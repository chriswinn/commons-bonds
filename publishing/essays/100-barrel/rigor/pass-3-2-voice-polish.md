### F-VP-Barrel-2 — §II three-fold "may" parallel structure — MEDIUM

**Location:** §II ¶4 (line 88).

**Verbatim current text:**
> But it is, even so, an assumption. It is not a guarantee. The substitution may not arrive in time. The substitute may be inferior in ways that matter. The substitution may itself depend on a different non-renewable input that runs into the same wall a generation later.

**Finding:** Three parallel sentences opening with "The substitution may..." / "The substitute may..." / "The substitution may..." — a rule-of-three triplet at the syntactic level. Each sentence makes a distinct analytical point (timing failure / quality failure / shifting-the-problem failure), so the content is doing real work, but the parallel structure reads as LLM-tic cadence at the prose level.

**Proposed correction (verbatim replacement prose):**
> But it is, even so, an assumption. It is not a guarantee. The substitution may not arrive in time, or the substitute that arrives may be inferior in ways that matter. The substitution may itself depend on a different non-renewable input that runs into the same wall a generation later.

(Combine the first two "may" sentences into a single comma-joined sentence; the third "may" stands alone as a separate structural point. Triplet collapsed to one-sentence-with-conjunction + one-standalone-sentence. Both analytical points preserved.)

**Cross-pass impact:** None on Pass 3.1 (no factual change). Marginal Pass 3.3 lift for Tier 1 #1 PW editorial brain + Tier 3 #18 first-gen reader (less obvious triplet density reads more PW-essayistic). No Pass 3.4 impact.

### F-VP-Barrel-6 — "the work of decades, not paragraphs" refrain (§VI ¶2 + §Close ¶1) — LOW

**Locations:** §VI ¶2 (line 160) and §Close ¶1 (line 178).

**Verbatim current text (§VI ¶2):**
> The hard work of constructing a number an economist can defend and a policymaker can implement is the work of decades, not paragraphs.

**Verbatim current text (§Close ¶1):**
> The hard work of producing a number an economist can defend and a policymaker can implement is the work of decades, not paragraphs.

**Finding:** Near-identical refrain — same sentence-structure, same "the work of decades, not paragraphs" tail, same "an economist can defend and a policymaker can implement" inner clause. The only difference is the lead verb ("constructing" vs "producing"). This may be intentional refrain (the framework's "naming the gap is the work of decades" thesis crystallizing at both the close of §VI and the open of §Close) or unintentional duplication (the §VI opening and §Close opening accidentally landing the same construction).

**Proposed corrections (author disposition):**

**Option (a) — preserve the refrain.** Keep both. The duplication is intentional rhetorical bookend: the §VI "naming the work" opens the existence-proof + counter-case + architecture-fix close; the §Close "naming the work" recapitulates the thesis at the essay's final pivot. Some PW readers will register this as deliberate refrain (Mazzucato uses similar bookend devices in *The Value of Everything*).

**Option (b) — vary the §VI instance to reduce duplication.** Change §VI ¶2 to a structurally different sentence (e.g., "I will not pretend in this essay that I have produced the answer. The hard work of constructing a defensible number runs to decades, not paragraphs."). Drops the parallel "an economist can defend and a policymaker can implement" inner clause from §VI; preserves it for §Close as the climactic landing. Reduces literal duplication while preserving thematic refrain.

**Option (c) — vary the §Close instance to reduce duplication.** Change §Close ¶1 to a structurally different sentence (e.g., "The hard work of producing a number that can stand up to economic defense and policymaker scrutiny is the work of decades, not paragraphs."). Drops the parallel inner clause from §Close; preserves the §VI form as primary.

**Recommendation:** Option (a) — preserve the refrain. The duplication reads as deliberate bookend on close reading, especially given the essay's other structural-refrain devices ("each tradition stopped at..." in §Close; "behind the veil" in §IV). Author disposition.

**Cross-pass impact:** None on Pass 3.1 or 3.4. Pass 3.3 reads either way — refrain reads as deliberate to attentive PW readers (Tier 1 #1, #2); duplication reads as casual repetition to skim-read general-policy economist (Tier 3 #14). Option (a) is the default low-friction choice.
