## 1. The chapter's irreducible job / spine

Ch 7 is the **universality / falsifiability chapter**. Its single load-bearing job is to convert the framework from "a political critique of Earth's particular extraction arrangements" into "a general pricing instrument whose outputs change *only* because parameters change." It does this by a falsification gambit: take the framework off Earth, run it through scenarios where the right answer is intuitively unambiguous, and show it lands the right answer every time — including landing on "do not extract," "extract freely," and "wait, I cannot price this" depending purely on parameters.

The spine, stated minimally, is four moves:

1. **The Mars-water case** — every framework term pinned at an extreme, recommendation obviously correct (calibration anchor).
2. **The one-number-at-a-time sweep** — Mars and Earth are the same system with different parameters; the instrument doesn't change, the parameters do (the universality claim proper).
3. **The cross-scenario sweep + table** — asteroid iron / He-3 / regolith / comet / Europa / Mars rare earths / commute lease — proving parameter-sensitivity in BOTH directions (not conservation-biased; not extraction-biased) and surfacing the chapter's one genuinely new construct.
4. **The objection-handling bookend** — "Mars is a false analogy" (frictionless-plane defense) + "it's science fiction" (thought-experiment defense) + "it reduces to market pricing" (calibration-is-a-feature defense).

Everything else in the chapter is either (a) a NEW conceptual contribution the chapter smuggles in (the *existential substitutability gap*; the six concealment patterns + their Mars-collapse), or (b) supporting apparatus (the across-time pyramids/Qin/feudal section; the immune-response section; the delivery-cost note). The job does not strictly *require* (a) or (b) — and that is where most of the cuttable mass lives.

**What this chapter must carry that no other chapter carries:** Landing-points **L (falsifiability)** and **M (domain-of-applicability / boundary)**. This is the falsifiability chapter. If a cut weakens L or M, it is a TRADE, not SAFE. Everything else here is either delivered better elsewhere or is genuinely surplus.

## 5. LANDING POINTS CARRIED (A–P) + THIN / DROPPED

**Carried well:**

- **M (domain-of-applicability / boundary)** — Europa case (93) is the explicit boundary-naming: "It says, honestly, that it cannot price this particular externality." Plus the "bounded-system-with-distance-compressed" scope statement (19). Co-signature deliverable. PROTECT.

- **C (objections to survive)** — engages Mars-not-transferable, science-fiction/unfalsifiable, just-Pigou-when-it-reduces-to-market-price, coercion-flattening (power asymmetry pattern). See §7.
- **E (worked demonstrations)** — eight scenarios + the summary table. The chapter's empirical-demonstration vehicle.
- **N (concealment taxonomy / covers)** — the six patterns (geographic / temporal / informational / power / normative-routinization / abundance-masking) + their Mars-collapse is the cleanest statement of the concealment taxonomy in the book.

**Thin:**
