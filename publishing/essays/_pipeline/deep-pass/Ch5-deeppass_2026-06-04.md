## 1. The chapter's irreducible job / spine

Ch5 is the book's **diagnosis chapter** and its **hinge**: it is the last chapter before the methodology (Ch6) and it carries the entire empirical weight that the framework's apparatus is built to answer. Its irreducible job has exactly four load-bearing moves:

1. **Cost severance is one mechanism, not six anecdotes.** A vermiculite mine, an oil platform, a tailings pond, a financial crisis, a retirement system, and a dying body are *the same object* — value extracted by one party, costs severed and transferred to a party with less power to refuse. This is the **structural-commons / fictitious-commodity** meta-claim made empirically. (The chapter's title claim, stated in the opener and the "Pattern Made Visible" synthesis.)
2. **The gap is empirically large and the existing architecture cannot reach it.** Libby 40:1, Deepwater 40% recovery, Social Security $108T foregone, GoFundMe 88% failure. Measurement, not new diagnosis: the value-add is *magnitude*, court-validated and conservative. The legal architecture (criminal acquittal, bankruptcy discharge, weak cross-border claims) systematically fails to recognize the mechanism — which is the Pistor seam.
3. **The framework answers bidirectionally with two instruments.** Restitution Bond (backward, harm realized) + Foreclosure Bond (forward, harm not yet realized) = the aggregate bond in CS = RCV − Bond. This is the configurational-novelty claim previewed at the instrument level.
4. **The instruments sit inside established lineages, not invented from nothing.** Coates → Darity-Mullen → Hamilton → Conley (reparations/restitution); Hartwick → reclamation bonds → Norway → Parfit → Pettit/Skinner (foreclosure); Pistor (legal-architecture diagnosis). The framework *joins* these traditions rather than rebranding externality theory.

Everything else — the four nested 2008 objection-rebuttals, the two nested Social Security objection-rebuttals, the Swedish-1930s excursion, the rent-seeking/Public Choice section, the four bond-naming etymology essays — is **supporting apparatus around those four moves**. The spine is: *here is the pattern six times (move 1), here is how big it is and why the law can't see it (move 2), here is the two-instrument response (move 3), here is whose shoulders it stands on (move 4).*

The chapter is currently running roughly **twice the length its spine requires** (~12K words; the spine lands clean at ~6–6.5K). The bloat is not in the case studies — those are doing the irreducible work — it is in three places: (a) an **inline objection-rebuttal apparatus** that has migrated out of the rigor passes and into the prose; (b) a **terminology-justification layer** (four bond-naming etymologies) that reads as the author defending word choices to a hostile committee; and (c) a **paragraph-final structural-finding restatement tic** that reasserts "the mechanism is identical / the structural finding survives" after nearly every section.

**One-line spine:** *Six cases prove cost severance is a single structural mechanism the existing legal architecture cannot reach; the gap is empirically large and conservative; the framework answers it with two coordinated bonds standing inside the reparations and environmental-bond traditions.*
