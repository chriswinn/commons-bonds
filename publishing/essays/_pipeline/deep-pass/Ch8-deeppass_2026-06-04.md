## 1. The chapter's irreducible job / spine

Ch8 is the book's **single worked number** — the keystone that every prior chapter forward-promised (Ch2 deferred the combustion tail and the IPG; Ch5 said the coal case is "developed at depth in Ch8"; Ch6 supplied the Pocahontas-seam basis; Ch7 referenced "the honest invoice for one ton"). Its irreducible job is four moves, in order:

1. **Take one extraction (McDowell coal) and price it cost-component by cost-component**, walking the Four Gates' admitted Cᵢ, conservatively (low end every time; omit the contested), to a defensible floor.
2. **Land the keystone number** ($510/ton honest floor vs $4.50 market) and the **IPG (33–122×)** as the per-ton time-snapshot of the framework's integral — with the carbon/foreclosure term ($496) shown to dominate and to exceed every era's market price on its own.
3. **Demonstrate the pattern is not coal-specific** (Deepwater, Libby, Baotou, Exxon Valdez, tar sands, the rent-burdened household, 2008) — same mechanism, same structural gap.
4. **Disarm the three predictable misreadings** (never-should-have-happened / market-is-fake / pull-up-the-ladder) and **explicitly fence the scope** (this is accounting, not policy; the civilizational figure is gestured, not argued).

Everything that does not serve one of these four moves is a candidate for compression. The spine is the eight-component walk + the sum + the pattern + the three disclaimers. The chapter currently buries move 2 (the keystone number) under a $8 → $504–518 → $510 running-total relay that forces the reader to reconcile across three section breaks, and it over-builds move 3 (the housing/YIMBY digression is ~1,100 words — disproportionate to every other pattern case).
