## 1. The chapter's irreducible job / spine

**Job:** Convert Chapter 8's diagnosis ("here is the gap between price and honest cost") into a *sequenced, institution-compatible program* that a society could start tomorrow without resolving every contested philosophical question — and then hand the baton to Chapter 10's closing reflection.

**Spine (load-bearing, must survive any cut):**
1. **Classify** — sort resources by existential-substitutability-gap into low/medium/high bins (Step 1).
2. **Reserve** — for high-gap resources, leave a fraction in the ground; the ground is free permanent storage; Asymmetric Regret Rule sizes it (Step 2).
3. **Invest** — renewable substitution + alternative (off-world) supply; "extract from the lowest-RCV source first" (Step 3).
4. **Reassess** — classification is a living document on a 5–10-year cadence (Step 4).
5. **The "what a framework does" hinge** — the honest answer to "show me the legislative pathway": frameworks change what becomes thinkable; name the entering wedges; hand off to Ch10.

Everything else in the chapter is **objection-survival apparatus** appended to the spine. It is valuable, but it is not the chapter's irreducible job, and its current volume (~8,000 words, ~70% of the chapter) inverts the figure-ground relationship.
