## 1. The chapter's irreducible job / spine

Ch 2 is the **first worked human case** in the book. Ch 1 ("The Quiet Math")
introduces the framework abstractly; Ch 2 is where the reader first *feels* it
against a real place. Its irreducible job is a four-beat spine:

1. **Establish the empirical anchor.** McDowell County: richest coal, richest
   output, poorest outcome (the Kennedy "wrong defendant" frame). The paradox
   that the framework exists to explain.
2. **Name the mechanism.** Define **cost severance / severed cost / value
   extraction** — the book's load-bearing vocabulary, used "in every chapter
   that follows." This is the chapter's single most important deliverable: it
   is the term-coining chapter.
3. **Decompose the bill into priced-vs-unpriced columns** (black lung; AMD/
   reclamation gap; community collapse; lineage/brain-drain; the deferred
   combustion tail) and hand the *magnitude* claim (33–122×) forward to Ch 8
   without computing it here.
4. **Pre-empt the three reflexive objections** the human case invites —
   companies-were-evil (no: mechanism-not-motive), miners-were-passive (no:
   agency-with-structural-limits), and culture-not-structure (no: the Purdue
   second-cycle refutation) — and bridge to Ch 3 (the Waterman) via the
   "same structure, different details" move.

Everything load-bearing serves one of those four beats. The spine is sound and
well-sequenced. The chapter's problem is **not** structure; it is **accretion**:
several passages do their job and then keep going — re-explaining the mechanism
the chapter has already named, or front-loading lit-positioning and etymology
that belong in lighter touches or later chapters. The cuts below protect all
four beats while removing the over-delivery.

## 2. ISSUE LIST — every place it goes beyond what it needs

Ordered by severity (buried-spine / overload first).

### ISSUE A — The etymology disquisition on "severance" (line 144, second half)
- **Where:** §The Vocabulary, the paragraph beginning "The framework's choice of
  *cost severance* as the name…" (~232 words).
- **Type:** Premature depth / overload.
- **Why it exceeds:** The chapter has *already* defined cost severance, severed
  cost, and value extraction cleanly in the three preceding paragraphs (lines
  136–142) with concrete McDowell anchors. This paragraph then re-litigates the
  *word choice* against four rejected alternatives (externality / displacement /
  transfer / appropriation) and unpacks the severance-pay payroll metaphor. This
  is a glossary/Technical-Appendix-§14 register intruding into a narrative human
  chapter. The reader who just absorbed the term does not need a 232-word defense
  of the naming decision before the chapter has even finished its case. It reads
  as the author reassuring himself, not the reader. Worse, the severance-pay
  metaphor ("the employer pays the worker for the cut tie") **flirts with the
  cost-severance-metaphor-commodifies-labor objection** — it analogizes the
  human commons to a payroll transaction at the exact moment the chapter should
  be holding moral-weight register.

### ISSUE B — Mazzucato + Harvey lit-positioning block (lines 148–152)
- **Where:** The two paragraphs after §The Vocabulary (~227 words combined).
- **Type:** Front-loaded lit-positioning / digression from the human spine.
- **Why it exceeds:** Naming the *value extraction* lineage "once at the moment
  we adopt it" is legitimate (Matrix cell: Mazzucato = activity; Harvey =
  measurement-tool). But the execution is over-scaled for Ch 2: it summarizes
  *The Value of Everything*'s three financial-capital cases (asset managers,
  pharma patents, platforms) and *The New Imperialism* Ch 4's accumulation-by-
  dispossession in full mini-abstract form. That is a lit-review register. Ch 2's
  job is the *case*, not the *positioning map* — the matrix work belongs
  concentrated in Ch 1/Ch 5 and the Technical Appendix §14. Here it interrupts
  the momentum between the vocabulary payoff and the §Distance escalation.
  **Note:** the Harvey paragraph also asserts "Harvey's analysis is the lineage
  behind Mazzucato's diagnosis" — a strong intellectual-history claim presented
  flatly, which a hostile academic reader will contest. It is not load-bearing
  for Ch 2.

### ISSUE C — "The Numbers" section restates what the columns already showed (lines 120–128)
- **Where:** §The Numbers (~310 words).
- **Type:** Redundancy / buried spine.
- **Why it exceeds:** The five preceding subsections (black lung / orange creek /
  community / children / the deferred sentence) already itemize every cost
  category. §The Numbers then re-enumerates the *same* categories in a
  hypothetical "if you add… if you add… if you add…" anaphora before delivering
  the only genuinely new content: the **33–122× hand-off to Ch 8**. The
  category recap is a third pass over material the reader has now seen twice.
  The section earns its place ONLY for the magnitude-hand-off sentence and the
  "externality is technically accurate and practically insufficient" turn; the
  recap can compress to a transitional clause.

### ISSUE D — The "agency" rebuttal runs three paragraphs where two would land (lines 190–196)
- **Where:** §The Mechanism, Not the Motive — the three paragraphs handling
  miner agency, constrained choice, information asymmetry, and the
  contemporary-renters generalization (~440 words across 190/192/194/196).
- **Type:** Overload + premature cross-chapter reach.
- **Why it exceeds:** The agency point is important (objection: paternalism /
  treating-workers-as-victims) and the UMW/Black Lung Association/Miners-for-
  Democracy history is the strongest credibility move in the chapter. But the
  section then layers FOUR distinct sub-arguments: (1) miners had agency; (2)
  company-town structural limits; (3) information asymmetry + tobacco/climate-
  playbook analogy; (4) the 2024 cost-burdened-renters / "company town returned
  in distributed form" generalization. Sub-argument (4) is a *Chapter 8 / Ch 9*
  move (the JCHS 2024 rental-housing material lives in Ch 8, line 190) imported
  into Ch 2, and it pulls the chapter off McDowell County into a contemporary-
  housing thesis the reader is not yet equipped to evaluate. It is the single
  clearest "premature depth" in the chapter.

### ISSUE E — Community-collapse paragraph closes with a mini-framework-dump (line 100)
- **Where:** §The community that emptied out, final paragraph ("Costs that
  aren't priced are still costs… The architecture that would have closed the
  gap — capturing the rents at extraction, routing them to the bearers, holding
  them across the full life cycle of the externality tail…").
- **Type:** Premature depth.
- **Why it exceeds:** Mid-chapter, before the vocabulary is even formally
  defined (that comes 40 lines later), this paragraph introduces "capturing the
  rents at extraction" + "routing them to the bearers" + "externality tail" with
  a parenthetical gloss — i.e. the Ch 9 bond architecture and the ARR/long-tail
  apparatus. It also pre-announces "subsidy" and "order of magnitude" before
  §The Numbers delivers them. The reader meets the *solution shape* before the
  *problem* is fully built. The plain-accounting "subsidy" naming is good and
  should stay; the rents/routing/life-cycle clause is apparatus the chapter
  hasn't earned yet.

### ISSUE F — Black-lung subsection double-counts the deficit reveal (lines 70–74)
- **Where:** §The man who developed black lung — the $44B / $4.6B-debt / $15B-
  by-2050 paragraph followed immediately by the "Forty-four billion dollars…
  and it still wasn't enough" emotional restatement (lines 70–72).
- **Type:** Redundancy (emotional restatement immediately re-states the figures
  just given).
- **Why it exceeds:** Line 70 delivers the numbers; line 72 re-delivers "$44B
  …and it still wasn't enough… running a deficit for forty-five years." Both
  paragraphs are strong individually, but back-to-back they say the same thing
  twice — once in ledger register, once in elegy register. One restatement of
  "$44B and it still wasn't enough" is powerful; the figures need not be
  re-walked.

### ISSUE G — Second-Cycle / Purdue section over-documents the litigation record (lines 208–212)
- **Where:** §The Second Cycle (~620 words).
- **Type:** Premature depth / citation over-scaling.
- **Why it exceeds:** The Purdue second-extraction argument is genuinely
  valuable and is the chapter's freshest contribution (the culture-not-structure
  refutation; Matrix-adjacent to Fraser/expropriation). But the prose loads the
  full litigation chronology — 2007 felony misbranding plea + three executives'
  misdemeanor counts + 2020 felony plea on three federal counts + Keefe's
  *Empire of Pain* + Sackler $11B + Big Three $21B/2022 — in dense apposition.
  Two of those data points (the 2007/2020 plea chronology granularity) are
  Technical-Appendix / endnote register; the *argument* needs only "documented
  in the public litigation record" + the target-map characteristics + the two
  headline settlement figures. The over-documentation is defensive (anticipating
  "numbers-are-invented") but it slows the rhetorical climax.

### ISSUE H — Triple "It does not prove…" scaffolding is visible (lines 180, 182, 190)
- **Where:** §The Mechanism, Not the Motive opens with "It does not prove that
  the coal companies were evil" / "It does not prove that industrialization was
  a mistake" / (later) "It does not prove that mining communities were helpless."
- **Type:** Buried spine / structural scaffolding showing through.
- **Why it exceeds:** This is the anaphoric "what this proves and does not
  prove" rhetorical frame; it works, but the three negations are separated by
  long paragraphs so the parallelism is felt as repetition rather than
  structure. Minor — flag, not a cut. Tightening the connective tissue would
  let the three land as a deliberate triad.

### ISSUE I — Two waterman/miner pride restatements (lines 24, 220)
- **Where:** Line 24 ("not an argument that those people were wrong to be
  proud") and line 220 ("proud of their work in the same way the miners were
  proud of theirs").
- **Type:** Redundancy (the "pride is real / we are not disputing it" beat
  appears at line 24, line 182, line 184, line 190, and line 220).
- **Why it exceeds:** "The work was dignified and the pride was real" is
  asserted at least five times. It is an important defensive move (objection:
  condescension-toward-Appalachia), but five touches is two or three too many.
  Consolidate to: once up front (line 24), once in the agency section (line
  190), once in the Chesapeake bridge (line 220).
