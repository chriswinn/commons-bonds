## (2) THE TEST VERDICT — Where Lens-Driven Drafting HELD vs STRAINED

**Headline: the matrix layer substantially reduced chat-then-draft iteration, but did not eliminate the author chat. The reduction is real and the residual is small, specific, and consistent across all 7 chapters.**

### Where it HELD (memo + lens were enough to draft confidently, no chat)

The lens was decisive — unanimously across all 7 chapters — on four classes of decision:

1. **Structural surgery / cut lists.** Every drafter reports the memo's SAFE/TRADE-tagged issue list mapped cleanly onto concrete prose moves. The heaviest cutters (Ch5 −3,171w, Ch6 −4,328w, Ch9 −3,110w) ran the entire restructure with no open structural question. "The deep-pass memo's issue list told me exactly which scenes did not earn their place" recurs near-verbatim in Ch5, Ch6, Ch8.

2. **Figure resolution.** The canonical-figure ledger resolved every figure question without a chat — what to keep, what is TA-only ($555, RCV $580–620, $496/$510 carbon-vs-total), and the "late 1980s" / "wound down across the 1980s" phasing that prevents the US Steel hard-year drift. Ch9 called figure-correction "mechanical once the ledger was in hand"; Ch6 said the owner-map line "Ch6 uses the canonical figures" settled the dual-basis digression outright. Ch8 and Ch9 actively *corrected* source drift ($550/$40 era-muddle → $510/$4.50) by ledger authority.

3. **Owner-map relocations.** Redundancy/relocation routing was pre-adjudicated. Housing→Ch9, Social Security→Ch5, financialization→Ch9, mask-veil→Ch1, McDowell carbon→Ch8 all resolved without judgment calls. This is the single biggest iteration-saver: under the old regime each of these is a "where does this live?" round-trip.

4. **The Polanyi/Fraser ADD — *placement and content*.** The matrix meta-claims told drafters not just *that* the structural-commons naming belonged but *what it had to say* (same-object claim + "price what the tradition only named" + double-movement re-embedding). Critically, the same re-embedding polarity answered the Pistor/financialization AND commodify-labor objections in one move (Ch9 explicit: "both fell out of the SAME re-embedding polarity rather than needing separate ad hoc answers"). This is the matrix layer's clearest value-add over a plain deep-pass memo.

### Where it STRAINED (author chat still needed) — three recurring failure modes

The residual need-for-chat clusters into exactly three patterns, and they repeat across chapters rather than being one-offs:

1. **The Polanyi/Fraser DEPTH and HOME boundary (6 of 7 chapters).** This is the dominant strain. The lens flags Polanyi/Fraser as "the biggest ratified gap" and says *name them where the chapter is the right home* — but it does not adjudicate **how much weight** each chapter carries vs. its neighbors, nor **which chapter gets the first full exposition**. Result: Ch2 wrote a full ~150w paragraph, Ch5 a synthesis beat, Ch6 a keystone, Ch8 a full new section, Ch9 a ~900w section — and *every one of these drafters independently flagged that the scale was the author's call*. Ch8 is the sharpest: it judged itself the right *demonstration* home but noted the canonical home is listed as Ch1/Ch5/Ch6/TA, so "if it lands in Ch1 first, my section should shrink to a callback." **The matrix told them what to say but not how loud, and the cross-chapter allocation is genuinely unsettled.** This needs one book-level positioning decision, not seven.

2. **Figure-RISK disclosure (not figure-value) — Ch5's $108T.** The ledger gives values; it cannot decide how to *present a model output that risks reading as invented*. The Social Security $108T foregone-return figure is a counterfactual-model output, flagged as the single highest "numbers-are-invented" exposure. Ch5 was explicit: "I could flag it but I cannot resolve it" — the author must decide disclose-assumptions-inline vs. demote-to-footnoted-band. No lens input substitutes for this editorial call.

3. **Cross-chapter cascade *status* — the lens describes a target state it cannot verify.** Relocations are flagged as MOVES, not executions. Ch6 cannot confirm Ch8's main-repo copy carries the post-fix $496/$510 (its forward-reference may point at a number that "does not yet exist there"). Ch8's YIMBY/2008/Public-Choice relocations "assume a coordinated Ch9 pass that has not happened yet." Ch9 couldn't verify Ch4 doesn't already carry the healthcare section it removed. **This is not a lens deficiency per se — it is the structural limit that a per-chapter lens describes a coherent end-state but a parallel-session pipeline has to actually reconcile the seams.**

### Did the matrix layer reduce chat-then-draft iteration? — Yes, measurably.

The honest signal: **the matrix collapsed the per-chapter chat from "structural + figure + relocation + positioning" (4 dimensions) down to a residual of ~2-3 narrow editorial forks per chapter, and those forks are largely the SAME fork repeated (Polanyi/Fraser depth) plus a handful of genuinely chapter-specific risk calls.** Three chapters (Ch6, Ch7, Ch9) report the residual chat is "concentrated entirely at cross-chapter seams, not at anything internal." Ch6 is the strongest hold ("ratify-ready on spine, cuts, figures, gap-fills without a chat").

What the matrix did NOT do, and arguably *cannot*: settle book-level positioning weight (Polanyi/Fraser), make figure-risk disclosure judgments, or verify that parallel sibling sessions have actually absorbed the flagged moves. These are precisely the calls that require either the author or a cross-session reconciliation pass — they are not per-chapter drafting decisions, so a per-chapter lens was never going to discharge them.
