**Verdict: COHERENT.** Three-artifact vocabulary register:

- Harper's V-D §VIII names *spatial cost severance* as a **named category** with a one-sentence definition (line 141). This matches Ch 7's policy-instrument register (line 131 "spatial cost severance pricing" implies the existence of a spatial-cost-severance category to be priced) and substantively extends the chapter's geographic-distance mechanism (line 111) into named-category register for the essay reader.
- Aeon pitch uses *geographic distance* (one of six patterns enumerated as comma-separated list) consistent with Ch 7 line 111 vocabulary. The pitch does NOT name *spatial cost severance* as a separate category — pitch's apparatus-reveal budget is tight (~289 words for the whole pitch). This is the venue-tailored compression, not an incoherence.

**Verbatim/scene-level corroborating coherence:** Ch 7 line 111 reads "McDowell County → Chicago" as the illustrative anchor for the geographic-distance pattern. Harper's V-D §VIII line 139 extends this to "New York apartments, Pittsburgh steel mills, Chicago streetcars, Norfolk shipyards" — four-cities-from-one-county.
