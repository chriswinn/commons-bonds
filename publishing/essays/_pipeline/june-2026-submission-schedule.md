## What "ready to submit" means per venue

### Aeon (pitch-first model)

- Pitch body (297w; locked); bio; AI disclosure; brief structural outline; working title.
- **Comps NOT required at pitch.** Aeon evaluates pitch on philosophical-angle + voice; comps fold into eventual essay → editor collaboration → essay-derived book proposal (separate agent-pipeline track).

### Noema (full-essay submission model)

- Full essay (~4,500w target per Noema writing guidelines).
- Pitch letter is not required; Noema reads full drafts.
- Named living sources with cited public-record attribution required (per Stage 1 brief audience analysis).
- AI-collaboration disclosure offered proactively per Aeon-precedent (verify Noema's submission terms for any specific AI-disclosure requirement).

### Phenomenal World (heterodox-econ academic-adjacent model)

- Full essay (~3,500-4,500w; verify PW length conventions at submission prep).
- Pitch + essay typically; PW's submission process less portal-formal than Aeon's.
- $100 Barrel essay specifically targeting PW per cross-thread-todos #4 resolution (2026-05-19; commit-anchored).

### Boston Review (institutional / policy-centrist model)

- Full essay (~4,500w typical).
- Pitch typically included; Boston Review accepts both.
- Center-right-policy-reader pressure-test is dispositive per Stage 0 Condition 1 carry-forward (per `audience-pressure-test-construction.md` §5).

### Atlantic Ideas / Foreign Policy (long-form magazine model)

- Full essay (~4,000-5,500w typical for Atlantic Ideas; ~2,500-4,000w for Foreign Policy).
- Pitch + sample paragraphs; can be queried before full draft.
- Foreign Policy alternate reframe outline (commit `516baa8`) provides the reframing seed if Atlantic Ideas declines.

### Op-eds (FT / Bloomberg / NYT / WaPo / Guardian Long Read)

- ~800-1,200w (varies by venue).
- News-peg required for most; op-ed editors prioritize current-events relevance.
- Both drafts (Norway sovereign-wealth + McDowell true-cost) pipeline-ready per commit `5167edd`.
