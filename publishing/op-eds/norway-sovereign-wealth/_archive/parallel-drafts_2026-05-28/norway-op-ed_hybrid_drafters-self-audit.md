## §0. Methodological caveat (explicit drafter-bias flag)

**Drafter-auditing-own-work bias applies.** This audit was produced by the same session that drafted Version D in the same prompt cycle. Self-assessment of the drafter's own output is systematically biased toward over-crediting the drafter's choices, because the drafter (a) knows the reasoning behind each move and unconsciously scores against that reasoning rather than the prose-as-written, (b) has not read Version D in a fresh frame after a context-reset, and (c) is incentivized to produce a result that justifies the hybridization work.

The Aeon Version D precedent (2026-05-28) makes this concrete. The same session that drafted the Aeon hybrid produced a self-audit claiming 5 Tier 1 ✓✓✓ verdicts versus the locked Version C's 1 ✓✓✓ — a 4-position jump. An independent fresh-session Pass 3.3 + 3.4 audit ([rigor/pass-3-3-and-3-4-bundled-audience-load_VERSION-D_INDEPENDENT-AUDIT_2026-05-28.md](https://example.invalid/aeon-rigor) — not URL-bound, repo-internal) confirmed NET INCLUDE DECISIVE but flagged specific drafter-overcredit claims (e.g., the "lands in arithmetic" defensive-gain-across-4-of-5-adversarial-characters claim was tested and partially walked back; Rothbardian "load-bearing defensive gain" was flagged as potentially overstated). The pattern: self-audit gets the direction right but overstates magnitude. Independent audit is the correction layer.

Treat every verdict below as the drafter's claim, not as rigor-equivalent finding. Independent fresh-session audit before submission promotion is the appropriate next step — especially because Version D will eventually be the source for a news-peg-activation cut that goes to FT/Bloomberg/Project Syndicate editors.
