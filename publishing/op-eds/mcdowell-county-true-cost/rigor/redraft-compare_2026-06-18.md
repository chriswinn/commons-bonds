# McDowell op-ed — 5-draft comparison (PROPOSED 2026-06-18)

**The experiment:** A + B were drafted under a 750–900 **maximum** band; min-500 / min-750 /
min-800 were drafted with that floor and **no ceiling**. The point was to see what beats a
maximum suppresses. Per the minimum-only prose rule, length below is **information, not a
gate**.

## The headline finding
The complete McDowell argument, unconstrained, wants **~1,300–1,600 words**. The ~900-word
band is ~55–60% of that — so the maximum forced A/B to defer roughly **40% of the substance**,
and they compressed beats rather than tightening prose. The three minimum-only drafts barely
differ from each other (1,325 / 1,555 / 1,588) — all carry the **full** beat set; the higher
floors just add felt-pressure detail. **min-800 is the richest and strongest.**

| Draft | Floor | Body | What it is |

| **A** | band ≤900 | ~900 | max-compressed; rounds figures, names presidents, elegiac close |
| **B** | band ≤900 | ~897 | max-compressed but precise figures + non-partisan; +1969-strike beat |
| **min-500** | ≥500, no max | **1,325** | full argument; all 3 columns + SCC graf + both harvest lines + foreclosure/Coase |
| **min-750** | ≥750, no max | **1,555** | same full set + more felt detail (Rennert handled, resurgence) |

## Hard rules (all 5 pass)
Every draft: figures correct ($4.71/2.61/$496/$510, $44B-through-2009 + $5.1B), **zero retired
forms**, **zero em-dashes**, no invented facts, no coercion figure, no bare IPG multiple
(min-drafts use one lens-explicit "a hundred times" = $496 vs $4.71, the single permitted use).
Figure precision improves with length (min-drafts carry the exact sourced figures; A rounds).
