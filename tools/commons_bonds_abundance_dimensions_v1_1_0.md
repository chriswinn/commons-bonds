## 1. AIT positioning (canonical, from C9 patch §3)

The Abundance Interference Taxonomy is the framework's epistemic core. **Dimensions are organizational scaffolding, not load-bearing structure.** Dimension additions, mergers, or renames are organizational revisions, not framework-integrity events. The AIT operates on any cost-scarcity relationship independently of a containing taxonomy — its validity is a property of the cost-scarcity relationship itself, not of any dimension set.

Dimensions provide four services that AIT alone does not:
- **Systematic coverage** — ensuring no structural allocation asymmetry is invisible for lack of a category to see it through.
- **Vocabulary portability** — giving the framework's key terms labels that travel (Harvey / Klein / Raworth compression test).
- **Pedagogical shape** — letting the book introduce the framework one scarcity axis at a time.
- **Scholarship seed points** — giving future work citeable footholds per dimension.

When tension arises between dimension clarity and AIT rigor, AIT wins. Dimension revisions are cheap; AIT revisions are structural. The current 10-dimension set has stabilized across multiple revisions (v1.24.0's regressed 7 → v1.21.0 canonical 10 → v1.2.1-ratified per Part 2 gap-close → Part 5 naming cohort with three locks landed as of 2026-04-22).

## 2. The 10 canonical abundance dimensions

Each dimension is defined by (a) its scarcity axis, (b) its paradigmatic allocation asymmetry, (c) its canonical cases, and (d) its current naming status. Merger/primitiveness gates cleared per v1.21.0 and v1.2.1; two-path and multi-scale × 2 env cleared per Part 2. Detailed per-dimension pressure assessments in `commons_bonds_rigor_pass_2026-04-22_v1_0_1.md` §3 (Part 2) and `commons_bonds_rigor_pass_2026-04-22_v1.0.2.md` (Kindred lock).

### 2.1 Habitability *(locked 2026-04-22)*

**Scarcity axis:** breathable, stable atmospheric medium — shared, non-excludable on capture, excludable on cost-imposition. The broader biophysical substrate within which ecosystem services (§2.6 Ecosystem) operate.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture emissions-externalized value; fenceline communities, workers, and future generations bear respiratory and climate cost. Path 2 shield is spatial + temporal distance — pollution travels, harm lags.

**Canonical cases:** occupational respiratory exposure; fenceline-community air quality; emissions-led climate foreclosure (overlapping with Ecosystem on climate content).

**Scope boundary:** Habitability is the shared-medium dimension (air-as-carrier and habitable-substrate). Ecosystem (§2.6) is the services-from-biosphere dimension and is **nested under Habitability** — habitable substrate is the precondition for ecosystem services. Overlap at climate / emissions is real; chapter-level case selection handles assignment. Geographic (§2.2) is place / distance, distinct.

**Naming status:** LOCKED 2026-04-22 (was "Atmospheric, rename pending"). "Habitability" resolves Test 20/23 compression failure of "Atmospheric cost" and strengthens off-Earth multi-scale coverage (life-support / engineered-habitat contexts read naturally as "habitability cost"). Note: "Life-support" was a prior candidate disqualified under abundance-naming pattern review.

### 2.2 Geographic *(rename pending)*

**Scarcity axis:** location — place-based access, stranded-asset cost, distance-to-market, place-specific extraction.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture locational advantage; stranded communities and regions with low bargaining power bear cost. Path 2 shield is distance itself.

**Canonical cases:** extractive-region sacrifice zones (Appalachian coal, mountaintop-removal-adjacent communities); housing-affordability-in-expensive-metros; infrastructure deserts; climate-migration foreclosure.

**Scope boundary:** Geographic is place / distance. Ecosystem is biosphere services. Habitability is shared medium.

**Naming status:** rename pending (Part 5 Priority-2). "Spatial" is the primary candidate — resolves the off-Earth multi-scale name failure documented in v1.0.0 stress test. Current "Geographic cost" compresses acceptably on Earth; strained for orbital-habitat / Lagrange-point contexts.

### 2.3 Temporal *(stable)*

**Scarcity axis:** time — temporal separation between generations, compounding effects, deferred / accelerated cost-transfer.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** present captures; future bears. Tier 3 (Dynastic Labor Cost) is the canonical worked example — evaporates under no-temporal-separation abundance. Path 2 shield is temporal distance itself — future generations cannot advocate.

**Canonical cases:** pension / retirement cost-shifting; climate as temporal cost; deferred maintenance / infrastructure; **120+ hour-week consent-normalization case** (Part 1 §5 canonical case for consent-normalization shield type); early-career trade-off compounding.

**Scope boundary:** Temporal is time-as-separator. Agency covers consent-under-constraint (overlaps at 120+ hour-week case); Institutional covers organizational structure (overlaps at pension governance).

**Naming status:** stable. No rename. Compresses cleanly on Tests 20/23. Strongest Goal-2 direct-contribution of any dimension.

### 2.4 Institutional *(stable)*

**Scarcity axis:** organizational coordination, hierarchical position, collective-action infrastructure.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms / orgs capture via organizational position; labor, subordinates, external stakeholders bear. Path 2 shield is institutional opacity — decisions made in rooms unobservable to those bearing cost.

**Canonical cases:** corporate hierarchy under up-or-out dynamics (consulting / finance / law-firm cases); company-town historical material; CEO-era organizational decisions with subordinate cost-bearing; NGO / university governance opacity.

**Scope boundary:** Institutional is organizational / hierarchical. Political is governmental / regulatory. State institutions sit at the boundary; case-level assignment follows whether the allocation-conflict is organizational (Institutional) or distributional-governmental (Political).

**Naming status:** stable. No rename.

**Pre-drafting gates:** NDA review; subordinate anonymization protocol. CEO-era case content is load-bearing for this dimension; gates address Test 13 career-risk exposure (secondary per v1.2.1) at the case level.

### 2.5 Kindred *(locked 2026-04-22)*

**Scarcity axis:** specific non-substitutable attachment-bonds — bonds to specific people, kin, cultures, or origins whose loss cannot be closed by substitution. Includes biological-family bonds, chosen-family bonds, ethnic-cultural-linguistic identity bonds, indigenous cultural-community bonds, and diasporic kinship networks on equal footing. The dimension prices the cost of severance of *specific* non-substitutable attachments, regardless of whether the bond is blood-related, legally-recognized, or relationally-constituted.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** dominant-culture institutions (assimilation-requiring employers, legal / medical / state frameworks that naturalize one kinship form, majority-language school systems, extraction industries displacing indigenous communities) capture via cultural-identity flattening and specific-attachment substitution; minorities, diaspora, indigenous communities, linguistic minorities, and chosen-family-reliant populations (LGBTQ+, adoptees, estranged-from-family, divorced-and-remarried, immigrant second-generation, biological-family-rejected) bear assimilation / bond-loss cost. Path 2 shield is cultural distance (majority cannot see what it costs minorities) **plus naturalization-of-dominant-kinship-form** (lay-English "family = blood" default that makes chosen-family bonds invisible in legal / medical / institutional accounting) **plus consent-normalization** at the social-accounting terminus (an assimilated individual's "choice" treated as terminally closing the accounting).

**Canonical cases:** indigenous cultural-loss under development pressure; diaspora kinship-bond foreclosure; minority-language displacement; ethnic-cultural identity cost under majority cultural dominance; **chosen-family severance cases** (LGBTQ+ chosen-kin loss through legal non-recognition; adoptee specific-bond severance; estranged-from-family individuals losing chosen-family through workplace relocation or institutional non-accommodation — Weston 1991 canonical grounding).

**Scope boundary:** Kindred is specific non-substitutable attachment (identity-deep, specific-person / specific-culture / specific-origin). Cohesion (§2.8) is civic trust / collective-action infrastructure (general, non-identity-deep). Bilateral independence confirmed v1.21.0 and re-verified under the Kindred name per `commons_bonds_rigor_pass_2026-04-22_v1.0.2.md` §4.3 (Bowling Alone suburbs — kindred abundance in tight-knit ethnic congregations, cohesion scarcity in atomized suburbs; third-generation immigrant communities — cohesion abundance via civic participation, kindred scarcity via attenuated heritage transmission). The Kindred / Cohesion split maps to the Tönnies (1887) gemeinschaft / gesellschaft distinction; framework extends Tönnies with tier-decomposable pricing. Language preservation sits at Kindred ↔ Informational scope edge; current assignment is Kindred (language-as-kinship-carrier) with Informational cross-reference where language is epistemic-transmission specific.

**Naming status:** LOCKED 2026-04-22 (was "Demographic, rename pending"; prior working candidate "Kinship" failed Chris's too-familial test in lay English). "Kindred" passes Test 20/23 strongly (courtroom-usable, organizer-native), expands target-reader population via chosen-family scope (LGBTQ+, adoptees, estranged-from-family, divorced-remarried, immigrant second-generation), and is grounded in Weston 1991 and extended chosen-family literature (Stacey / Nardi / Karner / Jones).

**Pre-drafting items surfaced (per Kindred lock pass):**
- Dimension-intro prose must include explicit chosen-family scope statement (Weston 1991 primary anchor), at least one chosen-family canonical case alongside indigenous / diasporic / ethnic cases, and Tönnies gemeinschaft reference (methodology-appendix depth; brief in-text).
- Methodology-appendix prose must pair "Kindred" with precise technical language ("specific non-substitutable attachment-bond as the scarcity axis"); engage Schneider / Carsten fictive-kinship anthropology briefly; credit Tönnies for the Kindred / Cohesion split; credit Weston for the chosen-family scope grounding; state the framework's contribution as tier-decomposable pricing.
- Counterargument prose address: (a) Kindred-as-blood-naturalizing reading (queer-theorist pressure); (b) Kindred-as-aesthetic (analytic-philosopher pressure); (c) Why-not-Kinship-or-Demographic (brief naming-rationale passage).

### 2.6 Ecosystem *(locked 2026-04-22)*

**Scarcity axis:** ecosystem services — pollination, hydrology, soil fertility, biodiversity-enabled regulation, climate stability (partial overlap with Habitability).

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture ecosystem-service value; downstream communities, future generations, non-human species bear. Tier 6 (Ecological Carrying Cost — name retained for the *tier* per eight-tier canonical) is the canonical worked example — services evaporate under ecosystem-service abundance, tier evaporates. Path 2 shield is ecosystem-service distance — cost lags, harm accumulates.

**Canonical cases:** mountaintop-removal water / soil foreclosure; pesticide / pollinator collapse; climate-as-ecosystem-service foreclosure; fisheries collapse; natural-commons enclosure.

**Scope boundary:** Ecosystem sits **nested under Habitability** (the broader biophysical condition — habitable substrate is the precondition for ecosystem services). Overlap with Habitability on climate / emissions is real — Habitability is air-as-medium and habitable-substrate, Ecosystem is services-from-biosphere; climate sits at the intersection.

**Naming status:** LOCKED 2026-04-22 (was "Ecological, stable"). "Ecosystem" compresses better on Tests 20/23 than the adjectival "Ecological" ("ecosystem cost" > "ecological cost" in brief-usable register; noun-form aligns with other dimension names).

**Coordination note:** the nested-under-Habitability relationship is now explicit in both dimension intros.

### 2.7 Political *(stable with US-partisan-connotation watch)*

**Scarcity axis:** distributional conflict over scarce allocations — who decides what gets allocated to whom, under what legitimacy claim.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** concentrated interests capture regulatory / legislative allocation; diffuse publics bear captured-regulation cost. Tier 8 (Political Capture) is the canonical worked example. Path 2 shield is political opacity + access asymmetry.

**Canonical cases:** regulatory capture by industries with interlocking healthcare-financing networks; extractive-industry capture of state regulatory apparatus (Ch. 2 content); pharmaceutical lobbying networks. Per v1.2.1 §7 Test 13 correction, career-risk is load-bearing at dimension level via canonical cases.

**Scope boundary:** Political is governmental / regulatory distributional conflict. Institutional is organizational / hierarchical. State institutions in their organizational mode (NASA operations, agency administration) sit Institutional; state institutions in their distributional mode (regulatory capture, legislative allocation) sit Political.

**Naming status:** stable with US-partisan-connotation watch retained. Current name passes Test 20/23 but reads partisan-valenced to US readers — positioning prose in dimension intro must mitigate.

**Pre-drafting gates:** canonical-case presentation protocol for regulatory-capture-in-healthcare-financing content (Test 13 + Character 7 + Character 21 convergent pressure).

### 2.8 Cohesion *(name TBD)*

**Scarcity axis:** civic trust, collective-action infrastructure, social-fabric density — capacity for non-kin coordination.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** platforms / employers / markets capture from density and proximity without contributing to cohesion maintenance; workers and individuals bear isolation-at-density cost. Path 2 shield is individual invisibility — cohesion erosion is collective; no individual lens sees the full loss.

**Canonical cases:** gig-economy atomization; knowledge-worker isolation at urban concentration; **NYC consulting-years isolation** (canonical non-co-occurring case demonstrating Cohesion scarcity at Kindred abundance — pedagogical requirement for dimension intro per v1.21.0); suburban Bowling-Alone pattern.

**Scope boundary:** Cohesion is civic / collective trust. Kindred is cultural / kinship / identity-deep specific-attachment. Bilateral independence confirmed v1.21.0 and re-verified under both names per `commons_bonds_rigor_pass_2026-04-22_v1.0.2.md` §4.3. Social / relational cross-dimension concept subsumes here (Part 2 §5.1). The Kindred / Cohesion split maps to Tönnies gemeinschaft / gesellschaft — use in methodology appendix only; dimension names stay English.

**Naming status:** name TBD (Part 5 Priority-2). Candidates: Cohesion / Social-Fabric / Civic-Ties / Communion. Current "Cohesion" reads borrowed from Putnam (Test 18 overlap pressure). "Social-Fabric" reads most organizer-usable.

### 2.9 Informational *(name TBD)*

**Scarcity axis:** evidentiary access — information-as-precondition for allocation evaluation and consent.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture information-advantage; consumers, regulators, workers, future generations bear evidentiary-asymmetry cost. Path 2 shield is suppression, complexity, deliberate opacity.

**Canonical cases:** **tobacco internal-research suppression; pharma data suppression; opioid-death geography mapping to Appalachian counties** (primary Test 13 load-bearer per v1.2.1 §7 — canonical-case presentation protocol required); data-commons enclosure; AI-era epistemic-asymmetry (explicit Test 19 decade-out handling).

**Scope boundary:** Informational is evidentiary asymmetry. Agency is coercion-under-information-deficit (Kantian pair — see §5). Political is regulatory-informational capture (cross-dimension cases exist; canonical-case assignment follows whether the primary asymmetry is evidence-access or distributional-allocation).

**Naming status:** name TBD (Part 5 Priority-2). Candidates: Informational / Epistemic / Veridical / Evidentiary. "Evidentiary" reads strongest in the labor-lawyer-2039 test (courtroom-native); "Informational" bland; "Epistemic" philosophical; "Veridical" unusual.

**Pre-drafting gates:** canonical-case presentation protocol (tobacco / pharma / opioid). Career-risk routes through nursing-adjacent professional channels per Test 13.

### 2.10 Agency *(name TBD)*

**Scarcity axis:** non-coerced choice — capacity to allocate one's own labor, time, and participation without structural compulsion.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** coercer (employer, platform, state, information-asymmetric counterparty) captures; coerced bears non-consent cost. Path 2 shield is **consent-normalization** (v1.2.1 §5.2): individual consent, when treated by the surrounding social / legal / institutional framework as terminally closing the accounting, operates as a structural shield even when individual awareness is complete. Strongest primitiveness result of the v1.21.0 candidates.

**Canonical cases:** gig / contract labor coercion-under-information-deficit; employment-relationship power asymmetry; company-town consent-under-constraint; **120+ hour-week consent-normalization case** (Part 1 §5 — canonical for the shield type itself, shared with Temporal); behavioral / psychological coercion (cognitive / psychological cross-dimension concept subsumes here per Part 2 §5.2).

**Scope boundary:** Agency is coercion-structure. Informational is evidentiary asymmetry (Kantian pair — see §5). Institutional is organizational hierarchy (overlaps at employment-relationship cases; Agency prices the coercion, Institutional prices the hierarchy).

**Naming status:** name TBD (Part 5 Priority-2). Candidates: Agency / Autonomy / Non-coercion. "Agency" carries principal-agent baggage in economics (Test 20/23 + Character M1 pressure). "Autonomy" cleaner philosophically; "Non-coercion" awkward in compound-noun form.

**Cross-ideological positioning is load-bearing** — libertarian non-coercion and leftist anti-exploitation both land on this dimension; Character 3 (right critic) specifically flagged that libertarian appropriation creates misread risk that the cross-spectrum traction itself mitigates.

## 5. Methodology notes from the Part 2 pass

### 5.1 Canonical cases define a dimension's pressure profile

A dimension's canonical cases are not illustrations attached to an abstract definition — they are what the dimension means in practice. Career-risk, legal exposure, and professional-retaliation pressures route through specific dimensions via their canonical cases, not through the dimension abstraction.

Empirical confirmation from Part 2:
- Informational's canonical cases (tobacco / pharma / opioid) make it the primary Test 13 career-risk load-bearer.
- Political's canonical cases (regulatory capture in healthcare financing) make it the secondary Test 13 carrier.
- Institutional's canonical cases (CEO-era content) make it a tertiary Test 13 case-level concern.
- The other seven dimensions are low on Test 13 at dimension level because their canonical cases do not involve industries with professional-retaliation capacity.

Further confirmation from the Kindred lock pass: the Kindred dimension's chosen-family scope surfaces only because the canonical-cases-set was extended to include chosen-family cases; the scarcity axis was always specific-non-substitutable-attachment, but the Demographic name obscured the extension. Name + canonical cases co-define the dimension's reach.

**Book implication:** when introducing a dimension, its canonical cases must be named alongside its scarcity axis. Scope boundary statements at dimension intros should reference canonical cases, not abstract definitions alone.

### 5.2 Character 25 (NASA Langley grandfather) is a system-wide ethical anchor

The grandfather-at-NASA-Langley character functions as an existence-proof counterweight across at least five dimensions (Habitability, Institutional, Kindred, Ecosystem, Informational). Without this dimension visible in the book's positioning, the framework reads purely critical — which Test 25 (steelmannability) flags as fragile.

Re-confirmed in the Kindred lock pass: the Hidden-Figures / Langley-community existence-proof reads strongly under the Kindred name, where NASA Langley appears as an institution that built specific-identity-deep professional kindred networks rather than only as a counterpoint to organizational opacity (Institutional).

**Book implication:** the existence-proof framing — that honest institutions and principled allocation do exist and that the framework's rigor is diagnostic rather than universally condemnatory — should be visible in early book positioning, not only in a closing gesture.

### 5.3 Kantian consent-precondition pair (Informational ↔ Agency)

Informational and Agency form a Kantian consent-precondition pair: consent requires information; information-structural coercion is itself an Agency-dimension case. The two dimensions cross-reference each other cleanly.

**Book implication:** each dimension intro should surface the pair relationship. Chapters that use 120+ hour-week case content (Temporal + Agency) may cross-reference Informational where the consent-normalization shield operates through information asymmetry as well as through social framework.

### 5.4 Single-dimension cost severance is cost severance

The framework is additive and compositional, not conjunctive. A case that activates one dimension is a full instance of cost severance — the framework does not require multi-dimension co-occurrence to identify cost-bearing. Dimensions price their own scarcity axis independently.

Canonical single-dimension examples:
- A retiree discovering that their pension was restructured on terms they never understood — **Temporal** alone.
- A knowledge worker recognizing the consent-normalization pattern in their own employment — **Agency** alone (or Agency + Temporal).
- An LGBTQ+ individual whose chosen family is legally unrecognized in medical-decision contexts — **Kindred** alone.
- The NYC consulting-years isolation case — **Cohesion** scarcity at Kindred abundance — is canonically a single-dimension case by design. The non-co-occurrence is the pedagogical mechanism for distinguishing Cohesion from Kindred (per v1.21.0 ratification, re-anchored under the Kindred name here).

Multi-dimension activation (Ch. 7 asteroid-miner activating 9–10 dimensions; Ch. 8 coal activating many) demonstrates framework coverage and universality. Goal-2 direct-contribution work — "accept a job with open eyes and honest accounting" — often succeeds at single-dimension precision: a worker pricing their own tier on one axis, because that is the only axis their specific situation is severing on, is the framework working as designed.

**Book implication:** dimension chapters should not require readers to locate themselves in multiple dimensions before the framework earns its keep. A single-dimension identification is a successful use. Chapter-closing examples may show multi-dimension cases, but the framework's directly-useful floor is one-dimension recognition.

1. A new dimension candidate passes both gates (merger + primitiveness) and clears AIT + two-path + multi-scale × 2 env under the current 10.

3. A scope-boundary statement is revised based on empirical drafting experience.

Rejected candidates remain revisitable per protocol §17.6.
