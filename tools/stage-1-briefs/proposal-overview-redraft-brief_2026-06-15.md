## 1. Why redraft (the diagnosis driving this)

The ratified overview has three gaps, identified against the live text:

- **The most arresting demonstration is present but flattened.** The Mars closed-habitat appears only as a clause ("as a universality test… off-Earth extraction") and the **Commons Inversion Test** is a lone bullet. The single most vivid way to make cost-severance *felt* — Earth's free commons (air, water, CO₂ removal) become metered line-items; the bill arrives with every breath — is absent. **Mars *is* the Commons Inversion Test made physical** (imagine the commons absent; watch the cost appear). It is the hook, currently used as a parenthetical.
- **The biggest "so what" is missing.** The overview frames the framework as a backward-looking *measurement / pricing / accounting* apparatus — the same register as the comps. It does **not** frame the framework's largest benefit: a **forward, runnable decision procedure that scales** — an individual, a firm, a community, a nation (a species) can run it on a choice *not yet made* to see what it severs and what restoration it owes. This is the "why it matters today" with teeth: the reader gets a *tool*, not just a diagnosis. (Origin: the insight a senior reparations economist reached for, unprompted, on hearing the individual-decision use. **Attribution is consent-gated — see constraints. Carry the idea as the author's own framing.**)
- **The literature positioning is actually strong** (§"What this book uniquely does" — three directions + Sandel + Pigou/Coase/Ostrom/Hotelling). **Preserve it.** The redraft adds the decision-tool as the *headline* contribution, not a replacement.

**Net goal:** keep the strengths; lead with voltage and the so-what; deploy Mars; elevate the scalable decision tool to the spine.

## 3. Beat blueprint (ordered — the spine of the redraft)

**B1 — HOOK: Mars as the Commons Inversion Test made physical.** Open inside the closed habitat where the air is manufactured and metered, every litre counted, breathing itself on the bill — the priceless commons of Earth reduced to line-items by removing the planet underneath. Render it viscerally (thought-experiment register; see constraints). Name the move: this is the Commons Inversion Test — imagine the commons absent, see what cost appears. Then the turn: the same costs exist on Earth; we simply never had to write the number down. *Source the rendering from the Aeon habitat prose (substrate-safe, already written).*

**B2 — EMPIRICAL ANCHOR: McDowell coal, so it isn't a parlor trick.** Pivot from the thought experiment to a real number. 1960 McDowell County coal sold at the mine mouth for just under five dollars a ton; walked through every documented cost at the low end, the floor is roughly $510. The line for the difference has been blank for a century because no one was made to write it down. (Pairing the vivid hypothetical with a hard real figure is the strongest possible open.)

**B3 — THESIS (plain language): cost severance.** Value drawn from a shared resource, cost severed from the beneficiary onto a party who cannot refuse. Distance is the engine; the modern economy manufactures distance for a living. Structural, not partisan; no bad faith required.

**B4 — THE FRAMEWORK / THE BOOK: the named instruments, cumulative.** Cost Severance → Residual Commons Value (the pricing apparatus) → the Restitution Bond (backward, person-affecting) → the Foreclosure Bond (forward, impersonal/Parfit) → the Commons Inversion Test (already introduced via the hook — close the loop). The deliberately scattered case set (one demonstration = anecdote; many = pattern). State the order-of-magnitude gap finding and that direction never flips across lenses.

**B5 — WHAT THIS BOOK UNIQUELY DOES (the so-what — restructured around the decision tool).**
  - **Headline (new):** the framework is not only a better backward-looking *accounting* of past extraction; it is a forward **decision procedure that scales** — runnable by any agent at any level (individual → firm → community → nation → species) on a choice not yet made. *This is the sharpest differentiation from every comp: they diagnose; this hands you a tool you can use.* Frame conservatively — claim only what the manuscript demonstrates (see open question Q1).
  - **Preserve (already strong):** the three extension directions (financial→physical-resource + universality; diagnosis→instrument; answering Sandel's priceless-into-a-transaction objection — "the choice is between two numbers, because the thing already has a number, and that number is zero"); the precise location vs Pigou / Coase / Ostrom / Hotelling (the Hotelling Identity); the Polanyi/Fraser pricing-to-reserve-not-to-sell lineage.
  - **Preserve:** non-partisan positioning (Public Choice complement; AEI/Cato/Mercatus/GMU invitation; not pro- or anti-capitalism; a state mine and a co-op that sever costs are still severing costs). Consider upgrading the stated success criterion from vocabulary-adoption-only to **also** decision-procedure adoption, consistent with the new headline.
  - **Preserve:** "names what it cannot do" (discount rate; pricing a life the model can't recognize; predicting breakthroughs; present-vs-future tension) and the **first-person deferral of direct coercion pricing** (the reparations field carries it; the book does not).

**B6 — WHY THIS AUTHOR (preserve; it's strong).** Outsider credibility: NIH/Human Genome → World Bank/IMF consulting → CEO → nursing student on a sailboat; the framework built after the cost landed on him; grandfather LaVern E. Winn (NASA Langley model maker + inventor); the Ostrom/Desmond/Raworth "outsiders see the gap" frame; the honest AI-collaboration disclosure (keep as in the ratified overview — it is a conscious decision to disclose).

**B7 — WHY NOW (preserve three currents; sharpen with the decision-tool lens).** Intellectual (the Mazzucato/Pistor/Christophers/Susskind/Darity-Mullen wave + appetite); practical (CBAM definitive phase 2026, IRA, reparations commissions, sovereign-wealth funds); the cost-of-living crisis ("what things actually cost" is now a kitchen-table question). The decision-tool framing sharpens this: decision-makers at every scale face extractive choices *now*.
