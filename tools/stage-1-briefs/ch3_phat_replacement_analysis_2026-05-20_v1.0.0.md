## 1 — Scene inventory

The Ch 3 v1 draft places the (now anonymized) Phat sequence at
lines 129–138, immediately after the Biggie passage and before
the place-paragraph (Tangier / Smith Island / Hampton). The
sequence runs five paragraphs. Each plays a distinct structural
role.

### Para 1 (line 130) — Lineage bridge
> "There is a man on Fox Hill I am leaving unnamed here. He is
> alive, still on the water… He is from the same Fox Hill world
> Biggie was from, and his work and his life have unfolded in the
> next stretch of the same arc."

**Role:** Geographic + generational bridge. Carries the reader
from Biggie (deceased oysterman; the older accounting worked) to
a living crabber from the same community (the current accounting
has broken). The phrase "same Fox Hill world" is the load-bearing
clause; it links the deceased/named anchor (Biggie, courtesy-notify
discipline) to the living/unnamed anchor (this man, anonymized).

**Load-bearing details preserved post-anonymization:** Fox Hill
specificity; large man, not tall; on the Bay since boyhood; same
arc as Biggie.

### Para 2 (line 132) — Cost-compression observation
> "He is a crabber and a fisherman… What he has watched, across
> his decades on it, is that the math for a man like him has
> changed. The catches have come back thinner. The costs of fuel
> and gear and the slip have moved in the other direction. The
> species he came up working are not all of them still running."

**Role:** The Bay-wide structural condition rendered through one
man's body. Translates the macro data the chapter has been
running (oyster 99% decline; rockfish YOY 2.0 vs 11.0; blue-crab
238M; menhaden cap) into the embodied observation of a single
working life. Sets up the second-economy hinge in Para 3–4.

**Load-bearing details preserved:** decades on the water near
Fox Hill and Poquoson; the three-vector cost compression
(catches thinner / costs higher / species not running); the
distinction between "general conditions" and "what is particular
to him."

### Para 3 (line 134) — Second-economy scene (THE CORE)
> "He still gets up at two or three in the morning… He goes to
> the mechanic shop he owns, on the Peninsula, where his name is
> on the sign and his crab pots are stacked behind the locked
> fence in the back lot, behind the cars he is working on for
> customers. He works the shop until five or six in the evening."

**Role:** This is the chapter's central illustration of cost-
severance — the second-economy claim rendered in physical, scene-
level detail. The mechanic shop with the name on the sign + crab
pots stacked behind the locked fence in the back lot is the most
material image the chapter produces. The reader can see it. The
image embodies the chapter's argument about what the surrounding
economy has been accepting from watermen in lieu of pricing the
externalities.

**Load-bearing details preserved:** the 2/3 a.m. predawn run;
crab pots; the mechanic shop on the Peninsula; name on the sign;
crab pots stacked behind the locked fence; cars he is working on
for customers; full second-shift through the working day. **All
intact under anonymization** — the only detail removed is the
personal name on the sign (the reader still knows that *a* name
is on it; just not which name).

### Para 4 (line 136) — Second-economy hinge
> "He did not quit the water. He could not. He has spent decades
> building, on his own time and his own dime, the second economy
> that has allowed him to not quit it. The accounting that made
> the older men's lives possible — the accounting in which the
> crab and the boat and the long days were enough — does not work
> for him. He has filled the gap with the only thing he had left
> to fill it with: more of himself."

**Role:** Argument hinge. Names the accounting mechanism the
chapter is making visible: cost-severance from the surrounding
economy onto the worker's own body and time. Dependent on Para 3
for its scenic anchor — without the shop-with-the-sign image,
the "more of himself" claim is abstract.

### Para 5 (line 138) — Reflective frame
> "I have not asked him what he would say about all this, and the
> chapter does not have his answer to put on this page… The
> accounting this book is trying to make visible is not,
> necessarily, the accounting he would put on himself. It is what
> the surrounding economy has been accepting from him."

**Role:** Author-as-narrator epistemic-humility turn. Acknowledges
that the framing the chapter is doing is the chapter's framing,
not the subject's. Offloads moral authority from the man to the
system. Tied tightly to the author's first-person Fox Hill
connection (set up at line 18 — "My father, who is from Fox Hill…
We are zero generations removed from the community.").

## 3 — Per-appearance evaluation

### Para 1 (lineage bridge) — KEEP anonymized

**Substantive load-bearing capacity vs anonymized scene.** The
Fox Hill–to–Fox Hill lineage clause ("same Fox Hill world Biggie
was from") is the bridge that makes the Biggie → living-crabber
sequence work. *Every* named candidate in the inventory is from
elsewhere — Tangier (Eskridge, McMann, Parks, Rudisail, Evans,
Pruitt, Cannon), Tilghman Island MD (Whewell, Fisher, Schwartz,
Gowe), Piankatank River (Hudgins), or Fort Monroe (O'Connor).
None can carry the Fox Hill lineage clause without breaking the
geographic continuity that the chapter is using to anchor the
author's first-person standing (line 18: *"My father, who is from
Fox Hill… We are zero generations removed from the community."*).

**Gain from naming a public-record subject:** none specific to
this paragraph's role. Reader verification would be available
elsewhere via Biggie's name (already present) + Tangier voices
named further down.

**Loss from replacement:** the Fox Hill lineage clause itself.
Replacing the man with a Tangier waterman would force a re-
sequencing of the chapter — Biggie's Fox Hill anchor would lose
its "next stretch of the same arc" payoff.

**Verdict:** KEEP anonymized.

### Para 2 (cost-compression observation) — KEEP anonymized; consider augmentation

**Substantive load-bearing capacity vs anonymized scene.** The
paragraph is the chapter's voice rendering one man's lived
observation, not a quoted voice in its own right. The anonymized
scene carries it cleanly.

**Gain from substitution:** J.C. Hudgins's *"$150 in your pocket
per day. And it ain't no easy job. You're freezing your butt off.
It's cold."* would carry the cost-compression observation in a
named waterman's own voice, with public-record citation and
professional credit to Hudgins as president of Virginia
Watermen's Association. That is a substantive gain in
verifiability.

**Loss from substitution:** the geographic continuity with the
Fox Hill anchor — Hudgins is Piankatank River, not Fox Hill —
plus the embodied across-decades observation register that the
anonymized paragraph uses to set up Para 3. Hudgins's quote is
SHORT and PITHY; the anonymized paragraph is OBSERVATIONAL and
SETUP-CARRYING. Different registers, different jobs.

**Verdict:** KEEP anonymized as the chapter's spine paragraph for
Para 3's scene. SEPARATELY, augment the chapter ELSEWHERE with
Hudgins's $150 quote — likely upstream, near the existing
"asymmetry of margins" paragraphs (around line 63) where the
chapter is currently doing the Bay-wide cost-compression
observation. Augmentation-not-replacement keeps both voices in
play.

### Para 3 (second-economy scene — THE CORE) — KEEP anonymized

**Substantive load-bearing capacity vs anonymized scene.** None
of the named candidates in the inventory carry a second-economy
scene at this granularity. The candidates carry: cultural-
survival (Eskridge), island-fate (Cannon, Evans, Pruitt), daily-
margins (Hudgins), regulatory-record (O'Connor), photo-caption
presence (Whewell, Fisher, Schwartz, Gowe), or institutional /
scientific voice (Tarnowski, Nesslage, Latour, Wilberg, Ogburn,
Scheld, Colden, Moore, Goldsborough, Kurtz, Harris, Ruth). None
of those carry a *mechanic-shop-with-the-name-on-the-sign-and-
crab-pots-stacked-behind-the-locked-fence* scene. The image is
particular to this man. It is the chapter's most material
illustration of cost-severance because it is physical — the
reader can see the sign, can see the crab pots in the back lot.

**Gain from substitution:** none. There is no candidate in the
inventory whose published record contains this scene. The closest
analogues — Swift's *Chesapeake Requiem* portraits of working
Tangier watermen, Fletcher's 2009 Smithsonian profiles — are
about-the-water scenes, not second-economy scenes; the watermen
in those sources do not run mechanic shops in their off-hours.

**Loss from substitution:** the entire chapter's most material
cost-severance image. Replacing it with a Tangier-cultural-
survival scene or a Hudgins-daily-margins quote would weaken Para
4's argumentative hinge ("more of himself" depends on Para 3's
scene to be concrete).

**Verdict:** KEEP anonymized. The scene cannot be carried by a
public-record substitute. This is the load-bearing scene the
discipline's worked-example reasoning was protecting — the
anonymization preserves the scene's substance with only the
personal name removed.

### Para 4 (second-economy hinge) — KEEP anonymized

**Substantive load-bearing capacity vs anonymized scene.** This
paragraph is the argument synthesis of Para 3's scene. It depends
on Para 3.

**Gain from substitution:** none. Substituting the synthesis
without the scene would leave the chapter making the cost-
severance claim abstractly. Substituting both Para 3 and Para 4
loses the chapter's core scene without an equally material
replacement.

**Loss from substitution:** the cost-severance argument's most
concrete embodiment.

**Verdict:** KEEP anonymized (downstream of Para 3's verdict).

### Para 5 (reflective frame) — KEEP anonymized

**Substantive load-bearing capacity vs anonymized scene.** The
paragraph's posture *depends* on the author not having asked the
man — "I have not asked him what he would say about all this."
This is the chapter's epistemic-humility turn: the framing is
the chapter's, not the subject's; the surrounding economy is
what is being indicted, not the man's understanding of himself.

**Gain from substitution:** none of the candidates carry this
posture in their public record. Eskridge gives a framed-up
cultural-survival claim ("saving a culture"); Cannon gives a
spiritual-sermon claim ("Why do you think we're on this Earth?");
Hudgins gives a kitchen-table-economics claim; O'Connor gives a
regulatory-failure-record claim. None of them carry the *author's
restraint about speaking-for*. Substituting a named subject would
require either having asked them (which the chapter has not done
for this role) or quoting one of their existing framed-up claims
(which would *not* be the same paragraph).

**Loss from substitution:** the chapter's epistemic-humility
turn — the gentlest, most necessary moment in the second-economy
sequence, where the author acknowledges that the accounting the
book is making visible is the book's accounting, not the
subject's.

**Verdict:** KEEP anonymized.

## 4 — Net judgment

The anonymized Phat sequence carries scene-level second-economy
material that no public-record subject in the inventory can
provide. The mechanic-shop-with-crab-pots-behind-the-locked-fence
is the chapter's most material illustration of cost-severance,
and the load-bearing image survives anonymization intact (the
only detail removed is the personal name on the sign).

The named candidates surfaced by the post-petition expansion all
carry **something else** — cultural-survival (Tangier voices),
daily-margins (Hudgins), regulatory-record (O'Connor), scientific
gap (Nesslage, Latour, Tarnowski), market-collapse statistics
(Maryland Matters / Banner sources), institutional posture
(Colden, Moore, Goldsborough). They do work the anonymized
sequence does not and should not try to do. Replacing the
anonymized sequence with one or more of them would weaken the
chapter — losing the second-economy scene without gaining an
equally material substitute.

The correct move is **augmentation, not replacement.** Keep the
anonymized sequence as the chapter's second-economy spine. Add
the named public-record voices at *other* points in Ch 3 where
the chapter is currently doing structural / regulatory / market
work that those voices can carry verifiably and at substantive
weight. This is consistent with the worked-example reasoning in
`tools/memory/feedback_named_subject_consent.md` — the
anonymization was ratified *precisely because* the post-petition
expansion had made the voice inventory substantively rich at the
public-record level. That richness lands ELSEWHERE in the chapter,
not in the second-economy scene the anonymized sequence is
holding.

The worked-example logic is symmetric here. The post-petition
expansion made anonymization a low-cost choice because the
chapter does not need this scene to be named to be substantively
rich — the chapter is *already* rich with named voices in the
other roles. The anonymized scene's job is to carry the second-
economy spine, and on that job it is irreplaceable.
