# Audience-Pressure-Test Character Roster (v1.0.0)

**Date drafted:** 2026-05-19
**Status:** Living document. Captures the current 40-character chapter-level full-rigor pressure-test set with construction discipline + adaptation guidance.
**Portability:** Designed to be lift-and-reused. The character set is partially project-specific (some characters are calibrated to a specific manuscript's content) but the construction discipline + tier structure + many characters are general. Could be extracted to its own repository as a standalone audience-testing reference.

---

## §0. What this is + how it's used

When you draft a publisher-facing artifact, you need to know whether it will land with the audiences you intend to reach **before** it ships. An audience-pressure-test character set is the operational answer: a roster of imagined readers, each with a specific voice and concern, whom you simulate as a reader of your draft.

The roster has two distinct functions:

1. **Acceptance testing (Pass 3.3 of the rigor pipeline).** For each character, does the artifact return INCLUDE / NEUTRAL / EXCLUDE? "INCLUDE" means this character will continue reading, recommend the work to peers, or in the case of editorial gatekeepers, advance the artifact through their pipeline. The acceptance set captures the audiences you want to land.

2. **Robustness testing (Pass 3.4 of the rigor pipeline).** For each adversarial character, what thread do they pull on? The robustness set captures hostile readers. The diagnostic question is not "do they like it" but "where do they break it" — and especially, "do multiple adversaries pull on the same thread?" Common load-bearing threads across multiple adversaries = real artifact vulnerability. Scattered procedural threads = robust artifact with isolated cosmetic-flag exposure.

These are different questions with different answers. **Pass 3.3 + Pass 3.4 must be separated.** Bundling acceptance + robustness into a single character set produces confused verdicts.

## §1. Tier structure

Acceptance characters split into three tiers by their relationship to artifact success:

| Tier | Definition | Verdict mode | Count in canonical set |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — gating audiences** | Audiences whose INCLUDE verdict is required for the artifact to succeed. EXCLUDE here = artifact-level fail. | INCLUDE required; EXCLUDE disqualifies | 7 |
| **Tier 2 — pipeline-strengthening audiences** | Audiences whose INCLUDE verdict strengthens reach and pipeline value (essay venues; intellectual-tradition signals; comp-shelf peers). NEUTRAL acceptable; EXCLUDE is a warning but not gating. | INCLUDE strengthens; NEUTRAL acceptable | 11 |
| **Tier 3 — cultural-resonance + practitioner audiences** | Audiences whose INCLUDE verdict signals authenticity, broad reach, and practitioner adoption. High authenticity sensitivity at some sub-characters. | INCLUDE = authenticity passes | 12 |
| **Tier A — adversarial / robustness** | Hostile readers. Used for thread-pull synthesis, not pass/fail. | EXCLUDE-as-expected; track thread-pull patterns | 10 |

**Total: 40 characters (30 acceptance + 10 adversarial).**

## §2. Acceptance characters (30)

### Tier 1 — gating audiences

#### 1. Trade-press editor pressure-test character

**Reads for:** first-page hook + comp-shelf placement + voice signature. The question "is this a book I would publish?"

**Cares about:** opening-paragraph weight; comp-cluster fit (does this fit my list?); voice integrity through the close; absence of disqualifying tics; whether the author can sustain 250+ pages at this register.

#### 2. Layman-but-engaged reader (artifact's primary intended reader)

**Reads for:** plain-language register; whether the artifact delivers without academic decoder-ring; emotional + intellectual carry through the close.

**Cares about:** scene-grounded prose; jargon explained on first use; analytical claims anchored in concrete instances; whether the artifact respects the reader's time and intelligence simultaneously.

#### 3. Free-market-economist / center-right policy reader

**Reads for:** good-faith engagement with free-market objections; absence of partisan ideological coding; analytical-not-advocacy posture.

**Cares about:** no-villain framing; structural critique routed through architecture register rather than blame register; absence of left-coded scholarship signals; whether the framework is presented as accounting rather than as political program.

**Three sub-readings to track:**
- *Literary-knowledgeable conservative* (Will / Brooks / Levin / National Review book-section calibrator) — reads scholarly attribution as appropriate intellectual-history.
- *Reflexively-skeptical conservative* (the "wokeness alert" calibrator) — reads any naming of left-coded writers as political-positioning.
- *Pure-policy conservative* (Cato libertarian / AEI fellow / pure-free-market economist) — focused on framework-architecture, third-order on literary signals.

#### 10. Literary agent (trade-press calibrator — e.g., Wylie / Bohan / Chalfant cluster)

**Reads for:** sustainable book-author signals; commercial-arc evaluator; comp-titles fit; first-paragraph hook; subsidiary-rights potential (magazine excerpts / foreign / audio); "can I sell this to an acquisitions editor?"

**Cares about:** voice integrity; commercial-arc trajectory; comp-shelf placement; whether the author can sustain a book; subsidiary-rights material visibility (excerpt-able passages; foreign-rights interest).

#### 11. Trade publisher acquisitions editor

**Reads for:** commercial viability; comp-cluster placement; "can my marketing team find the audience for this book?"; bookstore-buyer hook; "would I make an offer?"

**Cares about:** marketing-positioning legibility; jacket-flap-copy hooks; the reader-trust foundation across the sample chapter; whether the manuscript signals 250+ pages of sustained authorial voice.

#### 21. Adjacent-practitioner peer team member (e.g., Doughnut Economics Action Lab / Raworth-team)

**Reads for:** tradition-companionship signals; whether the artifact's central work is compatible with peer-organization work; whether the memoir-opener pattern matches peer norms.

**Cares about:** intellectual-history fluency; analytical-rigor-without-utopianism positioning; comp-team-recognition signals.

#### 22. Business-press end reader (Bloomberg / FT / Economist subscriber)

**Reads for:** analytical-rigor signals; actionable framework-promise; absence of disqualifying political-coding; usability in professional contexts.

**Cares about:** practitioner-grounded analytical work; honest scope-discipline; analytical-tool-not-political-program register; whether the framework will give them tools usable at their desk.

### Tier 2 — pipeline-strengthening audiences

#### 4. Tier-1 left-policy reader

**Reads for:** methodological seriousness about structural-critique lineage; explicit intellectual-tradition commitments.

**Cares about:** whether the artifact makes its structural-critique commitments explicit at chapter-opener positions or carries only loose framing.

#### 5. Anti-capital / structural-critique reader

**Reads for:** whether structural-critique routes through architecture register rather than through individual-blame register.

**Cares about:** Foreclosure Bond intellectual lineage signals; no-villain framing as architecture-level diagnosis rather than as evasion of structural critique.

#### 6. Reparations-economics-tradition reader (Coates / Hamilton / Mullen / Darity cluster)

**Reads for:** Coates-tradition companionship; "name the specific X" argument architecture; explicit lineage anchors.

**Cares about:** named-lineage moments in body prose at load-bearing positions; methodological inheritance from the reparations-economics tradition.

#### 12. Venue editor for derivative works (composite across multiple venues)

**Reads for:** the artifact as a sample of the author's voice + intellectual-tradition commitments when evaluating derivative-work pitches (excerpts; related essays; op-eds; methods notes).

**Sub-venue calibrations to track:**
- *Long-form literary-philosophical magazines* — values memoir-history-theory weave; intellectual-history fluency; restrained authorial register.
- *Heterodox / political-economy magazines* — values structural-critique routing; cross-tradition lineage-engagement.
- *Centrist-policy magazines* — values cross-political-spectrum engagement; absence of partisan coding.
- *Right-of-center policy magazines* — values free-market objection engagement; absence of left-coded ideological positioning.

#### 13. Civic-republican / political-philosophy reader (Pettit / Skinner tradition)

**Reads for:** architecture-not-blame frame compatible with freedom-as-non-domination; consent + standing-obligation questions.

**Cares about:** institutional-architecture analysis at the level non-domination requires; explicit framing-discipline at structural moves.

#### 14. Heterodox-economics academic-adjacent reader (Mazzucato / Raworth / Stone Center / Jain Family Institute / IIPP-network cluster)

**Reads for:** memoir-substrate-into-framework comp-shelf pattern; serious-enough-to-engage-academically.

**Cares about:** intellectual-history fluency; analytical-cred via memoir-substrate; disciplined scope-naming; cross-tradition citation comfort.

#### 23. Mainstream / Coasean economist (devil's advocate)

**Reads for:** whether the framework is alternative-to-Coasean (will critique) or Coasean-extension (may grant); relationship to transaction-cost analysis.

**Cares about:** whether the artifact will engage transaction-cost theory and property-rights bargaining mechanisms directly; reserves judgment until analytical sections deliver.

#### 24. Hayekian / Austrian-school economist (knowledge-problem reader)

**Reads for:** distributed-knowledge analysis; whether structural-architecture frame is compatible with price-system-as-knowledge-aggregation.

**Cares about:** institutional-failure framings that respect path-dependence and spontaneous-order; absence of central-designer assumptions; epistemic-humility signals.

#### 25. Ecological-economics reader (Daly / Meadows lineage)

**Reads for:** conversation with steady-state economics + biophysical limits + throughput-not-GDP tradition.

**Cares about:** ecological-economics conceptual territory; analytical-rigor-without-policy-prescription positioning; cross-tradition intellectual-history fluency.

#### 26. Corporate / regulatory / fiduciary-duty lawyer

**Reads for:** whether the artifact's accountability-instruments can operate as legal architecture; how aggressive the enforcement-mechanism proposals are.

**Cares about:** scope-disciplined methodology-promise vs. litigation-architecture-claim; admin-law-compatibility; fiduciary-duty implications.

#### 27. Constitutional / administrative-law scholar

**Reads for:** compatibility with admin-law's accountability-without-fault doctrines; strict-liability + system-design-accountability + state-action doctrines.

**Cares about:** architecture-not-blame frame as admin-law-recognizable structural-accountability analysis.

### Tier 3 — cultural-resonance + depth-test + practitioner audiences

#### 7. Literary reader (memoir-prose calibrator)

**Reads for:** memoir-prose voice integrity; cadence; em-dash density; literary-lineage moves that don't break memoir flow.

**Cares about:** sustained authorial voice; restrained-register at heavy emotional moments; specific-rather-than-generic sensory detail; intellectual-tradition attribution that fits memoir register.

#### 8. Identity-specific resonance reader (e.g., Black-Studies-resonance, women's-studies-resonance, LGBTQ-resonance — whichever applies to artifact)

**Reads for:** whether the artifact names specific lineage moments in body prose vs. carries only loose framing about the tradition.

**Cares about:** explicit lineage-anchoring at load-bearing positions; absence of generalization that erases specificity; cross-citation discipline.

#### 9. Quantitative-policy reader

**Reads for:** quantitative-rigor signals; apparatus-light-narrative-frame-to-quantifiable-framework promise.

**Cares about:** forward-pointers to where methodology lives; honest scope-discipline; promise-not-overclaim.

#### 15. Specific-community-resonance reader (e.g., McDowell County / Appalachian extraction-community — calibrated to the artifact's content)

**Reads for:** whether community-references land as honest material-constraint-comparison vs. appropriative outsider-flattening.

**Cares about:** reference-mode use vs. identity-claim; cross-chapter scope-distribution (does the artifact engage the community substantively elsewhere or only in passing); absence of community-as-rhetorical-leverage.

#### 16. Parent / caregiver reader (NICU-parent; aging-parent caregiver; estranged-parent; or other heavy-emotional-stakes caregiver register)

**Reads for:** authenticity of personal-stakes rendering; whether personal pain is instrumentalized for rhetorical effect.

**Cares about:** specific-detail authenticity; restrained register at heavy moments; analytical-routing-not-wound-display posture; refusal of easy-redemption-arc.

**This is typically the highest-authenticity-sensitivity character in the set.** Calibrate per artifact's emotional content; if the artifact has no significant emotional content, this character may be omitted.

#### 17. General working-class reader

**Reads for:** plain-language register; absence of jargon-tripwires; whether artifact delivers without privileged-decoder-ring background.

**Cares about:** scene-grounded rather than abstract; named-concept rather than coded-concept; appropriate-context-for-acronyms; absence of class-coded vocabulary.

#### 18. First-generation / immigrant-experience reader

**Reads for:** whether ethnic / cultural anchors land as authentic biographical specificity vs. ornamental ethnic-flag.

**Cares about:** narratively-functional vs. decorative ethnic detail; appropriate consent-discipline (anonymization where required); cross-generation specificity.

#### 19. Contemplative-tradition / spiritual reader

**Reads for:** whether contemplative elements engage tradition territory authentically.

**Cares about:** practice-language accuracy; restrained register; refusal of soteriology where the artifact's job is accounting-not-redemption; cross-tradition fluency without claim.

#### 20. Tech-industry / engineering-management reader

**Reads for:** authenticity of professional-experience scenes; whether industry burnout culture + inherit-undocumented-systems realities + management-of-large-deals scenes ring true to lived experience.

**Cares about:** specific-detail recognition (canonical first-question patterns; realistic emergency-rescue accommodations; senior-engineering-leader observation-marks); workshop-mechanic-to-engineering-management bridge.

#### 28. Policy think-tank fellow (composite across spectrum)

**Reads for:** whether the framework / analytical work will be usable as policy-paper-writing input.

**Sub-readings to track:**
- *Left-center think-tank fellows* (Brookings / Urban / RFF) — read cross-tradition scholarly attribution as appropriate.
- *Right-leaning think-tank fellows* (AEI / Cato / Manhattan / Heritage) — may read left-coded lineage commitments as positioning.

#### 29. ESG / sustainable-finance practitioner

**Reads for:** analytical tools for cost-severance accounting in portfolio construction; Scope-4 (Avoided Emissions) + ESG materiality relevance.

**Cares about:** structural-accounting tools; framework-promise relevance to portfolio-stewardship analysis.

#### 30. Foundation / philanthropy program officer

**Reads for:** funder-adoptability; whether the artifact's evidence-architecture is credible for grant-eligible work.

**Cares about:** personal-stakes credibility; framework-promise scope-discipline; political-non-alignment for adoptability across foundation types.

## §3. Adversarial / robustness characters (10)

**Adversarial verdict scale:**
- **⚠ EXCLUDE** — would push back; the artifact holds against the push-back; thread is acknowledged.
- **⚠⚠ EXCLUDE** — would actively reject; finds load-bearing thread; artifact's structural moves don't disarm.
- **⚠⚠⚠ EXCLUDE** — would weaponize the artifact against itself; finds artifact-disqualifying thread.

The aggregate verdict is determined by **whether adversarial readers find COMMON load-bearing threads** (= real artifact vulnerability) vs. scattered procedural threads (= robust artifact with isolated cosmetic-flag exposure).

### 31. Industry-funded sector economist (oil/coal/pharma/etc.)

**Thread sought:** direct threats to industry economics; commons-pricing or cost-accounting as policy-attack vector.

**Calibration:** customize to industry the artifact most directly threatens (energy / health / finance / agriculture). May fork into multiple sub-characters if the artifact touches multiple sectors.

### 32. Industry-funded health economist

**Thread sought:** threats to pharma / hospital / insurance cost-shifting accounting.

**Optional:** fold into #31 if artifact doesn't engage health sector substantively.

### 33. Public-choice theorist (Buchanan / Tullock tradition)

**Thread sought:** whether the artifact's framing brackets or skips rent-seeking analysis. Public Choice argument: institutions don't design themselves; actors with rent-seeking incentives design them.

**This is typically the most intellectually-serious adversarial thread.** It tests whether the artifact's no-villain framing (if it has one) genuinely engages or evades a real analytical question.

### 34. Pure-libertarian / Rothbardian reader (Mises Institute / FEE / Cato-libertarian-wing)

**Thread sought:** framework as expanded-state-power proposal; any accountability-instrument as state-enforcement.

**Calibration:** predisposed-ideological; the artifact does not need to land this reader, but the thread-pull pattern is worth surfacing.

### 35. Climate / energy-realist reader (shale-cluster / fossil-realist)

**Thread sought:** commons-pricing for fossil-fuel externalities or similar policy-attack vectors.

### 36. Reactionary intellectual reader (cultural-conservative cluster — e.g., National Review / National Affairs / Ferguson / Stephens cluster)

**Thread sought:** Frankfurt-school-adjacent or left-coded scholarship signals; intellectual-tradition lineage commitments read as political-positioning.

**Often the most-pulled thread across adversarial reads** when the artifact engages left-coded scholarly tradition explicitly.

### 37. Trade-press hostile reviewer

**Thread sought:** material to amplify in a hostile review for major-press venues (NYT / WSJ / Atlantic / New Republic).

**Calibration:** assumes adversarial assignment; threads they amplify are derived from the artifact's chosen material rather than from artifact-flaws. Chapter-level mitigation is limited; reception-cycle mitigation (supportive reviews from non-hostile venues) is the real defense.

### 38. Business-press conservative reader (Wall Street Journal editorial-board / Forbes / business-press conservative)

**Thread sought:** "this artifact threatens business interests" signals; structural-economic-critique framing.

### 39. Tort-reform / fiduciary-protection lawyer (defense-bar / corporate-counsel)

**Thread sought:** litigation-amplification concerns; any accountability-instruments as breach-of-duty-architecture preparation.

### 40. Tabloid / populist-skeptic reader (NY Post / Daily Mail / Fox News op-ed page)

**Thread sought:** material for populist character-attack on the author or the work.

**Threads commonly amplified:** cosmopolitan-elite signals; estrangement-as-credibility-attack; memoir-as-tear-jerker-manipulation; class-coded biographical detail.

**Calibration:** chapter-level mitigation is limited (the biographical material is real); reception-cycle mitigation is the defense (populist hostile attention is bounded; doesn't gate the artifact's commercial / policy / adoption success criteria except in worst-case-amplification scenarios).

## §4. Construction discipline

How to build a pressure-test character set for your own artifact.

### §4.1 Character-set sizing

| Artifact scale | Recommended size |
|---|---|
| **Short-form (op-ed, blog post, short essay <1500 words)** | 5-8 acceptance characters + 2-3 adversarial = 7-11 total |
| **Mid-form (long essay, methods note, 2000-5000 words)** | 10-15 acceptance + 4-6 adversarial = 14-21 total |
| **Long-form (chapter, novella-length essay, 5000-15000 words)** | 15-25 acceptance + 6-10 adversarial = 21-35 total |
| **Book-level / full-rigor canonical** | 25-30 acceptance + 8-10 adversarial = 33-40 total |

### §4.2 Tier 1 (gating) construction

For Tier 1, identify the audiences whose INCLUDE verdict is **required** for artifact success. For a book: literary agent + acquisitions editor + the book's primary intended reader. For a magazine essay: the magazine's editor + the magazine's typical subscriber. For a policy paper: the funder + the practitioner who will deploy the work + the policy-counterpart who will critique it.

Be honest about gating. Some audiences would be nice to land but are not gating; those go to Tier 2. Tier 1 is the "if these don't INCLUDE, the artifact has failed" set.

### §4.3 Tier 2 (pipeline-strengthening) construction

Tier 2 audiences signal intellectual-tradition fit, comp-shelf placement, derivative-pipeline reach. For each candidate Tier 2 character, ask: "if this character returns INCLUDE, does the artifact's reach genuinely expand?" If yes, include. If no (INCLUDE is nice-to-have but doesn't change anything), it's a Tier 3 character.

### §4.4 Tier 3 (cultural-resonance + practitioner) construction

Tier 3 captures cultural-resonance authentication and practitioner adoption. These characters tell you whether the artifact lands authentically with the readers who carry its emotional / cultural / practitioner-domain content.

**Authenticity-sensitivity calibration:** for any character who reads with heightened authenticity sensitivity (typically: parent/caregiver if artifact has emotional content; community-resonance reader if artifact engages a specific community; identity-resonance reader if artifact engages identity-tradition material), test the character's read with particular care. These characters will catch instrumentalization-of-pain or appropriation-of-experience flags that other characters won't.

### §4.5 Adversarial set construction

For adversarial characters, identify:
1. **Predisposed-hostile-by-financial-incentive** — industry-funded experts whose financial interest the artifact threatens. Cover the sectors the artifact engages.
2. **Predisposed-ideological** — adversaries whose ideological priors clash with the artifact's structural commitments. Pick the strongest exemplars (the most intellectually-serious version of the critique).
3. **Predisposed-cultural** — reactionary / cultural-conservative readers if the artifact engages identity-tradition material or structural-critique tradition.
4. **Predisposed-commercial** — hostile reviewers / business-press conservatives if the artifact threatens commercial interests or carries reputational-attack surface.
5. **Predisposed-legal** — defense-bar / corporate-counsel readers if the artifact engages accountability-instrument terrain.
6. **Predisposed-populist** — tabloid / populist-skeptic readers if the artifact has biographical material vulnerable to populist character-attack.

**Note:** the adversarial set is not meant to be balanced or fair. It's meant to find threads. The strongest, most intellectually-serious version of each critique is what you want — not strawmen, not caricatures.

### §4.6 Character-construction discipline (per character)

For each character, articulate:
- **Role / position** — what they do; what their gatekeeper-or-reader function is.
- **Reads for** — the specific question they bring to the artifact.
- **Cares about** — the specific features that determine their verdict.
- **Calibrations** — sub-readings if the character has internal heterogeneity (e.g., #3 free-market-economist has three sub-readings; #12 venue editor has four sub-venue calibrations).
- **Verdict mode** — INCLUDE/NEUTRAL/EXCLUDE (acceptance) or ⚠ / ⚠⚠ / ⚠⚠⚠ (adversarial).

### §4.7 Character-set evolution

Sets grow iteratively. You will not get the set right on the first pass. Expect:
- **v1** — initial set (~5-10 characters), based on what feels obvious.
- **v2** — expanded set after the first round of pressure-testing reveals gaps (~12-20 characters).
- **v3** — full-rigor set after multiple audit cycles (~25-40 characters).

**Adversarial set construction often comes late.** It's common to start with acceptance-only testing and add adversarial characters only after a pattern of misses surfaces. The split between Pass 3.3 (acceptance) and Pass 3.4 (robustness) in the rigor pipeline is the formalization of this two-phase character-set evolution.

## §5. How to use the set

### §5.1 Pre-audit setup

Before running a pressure test:
1. Lock the character set at Stage 1b of the rigor pipeline (do not modify mid-pass; if a new character is identified, route through Stage 1b and re-run from the appropriate prior pass).
2. For each character, document the **verdict scale** you'll use. Three-tier INCLUDE (✓✓✓ / ✓✓ / ✓) gives finer-grained discrimination than binary INCLUDE/EXCLUDE.
3. Choose your **verdict-aggregation rule.** Common: Tier 1 must all INCLUDE; Tier 2 may have NEUTRAL but not EXCLUDE; Tier 3 must mostly INCLUDE with EXCLUDE permissible only with clear cause; adversarial set verdicts aggregate to thread-pull synthesis, not character-by-character.

### §5.2 Per-character audit

For each character:
1. Imagine the character reading your artifact cold (no advance context).
2. Read the artifact through their voice.
3. Render verdict: INCLUDE / NEUTRAL / EXCLUDE (acceptance), or ⚠ / ⚠⚠ / ⚠⚠⚠ EXCLUDE with thread-pull description (adversarial).
4. Note specific load-bearing passages: where did the character's verdict land? Which lines / paragraphs / structural moves moved the verdict?

### §5.3 Aggregate verdict

Compose the aggregate verdict from per-character verdicts. Track:
- Per-tier tallies.
- Verdict-level shifts vs prior audit baseline.
- Cross-character thread-pull patterns (especially for adversarial).
- Highest-authenticity-sensitivity character verdicts (these are particularly load-bearing).

### §5.4 Cross-pass impact

Note where the audience-load audit interacts with other rigor-pipeline passes:
- Pass 3.1 fact-check corrections may shift acceptance verdicts (cleaner facts strengthen INCLUDE).
- Pass 3.2 voice-polish corrections may shift acceptance verdicts (cleaner voice strengthens literary-reader / parent / contemplative INCLUDE).
- Pass 3.5 developmental-edit corrections (restoration of richness) typically strengthen Tier 3 cultural-resonance verdicts; light Pass 3.3 re-fire is recommended post-Pass-3.5.

## §6. Limits + honest disclosures

The discipline is not a substitute for actual audience response. It is a discipline for **predicting** audience response with structured care.

- **Predictive accuracy.** Pressure-test verdicts are best-effort simulations. Where you have real audience data (early reader feedback; agent rejections; editor pushback), use it to recalibrate the character set.
- **Construction bias.** The set reflects the author's priors about who audiences are. If your audience-set misses an important audience class, the pressure test will miss the verdict that audience would have rendered. Periodic external review of the character set itself (an editor or peer reading the character roster, not the artifact) is the mitigation.
- **Identity-resonance characters require care.** Characters who read with heightened authenticity sensitivity (parent/caregiver; community-resonance; identity-tradition) are doing critical work, and the discipline is most valuable when those characters' verdicts are taken seriously even when they conflict with Tier 1 / Tier 2 commercial verdicts.
- **Adversarial-set construction is not a defensive crouch.** The strongest adversarial reading is the one most likely to surface real artifact vulnerabilities. Build the strongest version of each critique, not the most easily-dispatched one.
