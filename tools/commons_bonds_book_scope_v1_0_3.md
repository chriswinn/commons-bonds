## Purpose

This document is the canonical scoping decision for the *Commons Bonds* project. It defines what belongs in Book One, what is deferred to Book Two, what is horizon work for Book Three, why the split is drawn where it is drawn, what "done" looks like for Book One, and under what conditions the scoping itself should be revisited.

It exists so that every downstream decision — what to add, what to trim, whether a new layer or chapter belongs in scope, whether the book is trying to do too much — can be resolved by reference to a single document rather than re-argued case by case. When in doubt, this is the reference point.

This document is **not** a chapter outline, a publication timeline, or a writing plan. Those live in separate working files.

---

## Canonical decisions (summary)

1. **Three books, scoped separately.** The *Commons Bonds* project is three books, not one. Book One names the mechanism. Book Two prices the invisible at civilizational scale and names beneficiaries. Book Three is horizon work — pricing extraction before the institutions exist to enforce it (space law, off-Earth migration).
2. **Write Book One now. Defer Book Two. Hold Book Three as note-taking only.**
3. **Book One carries the framework, the vocabulary, the math, and one fully-worked case.** It does not carry the full-economy simulation. It does not name specific beneficiaries.
4. **The Abundance Inversion Test is canonical in Book One** as the methodology that generates tiers rather than a static list of tiers.
5. **The scoping decision is grounded in the operating core**, not in taste or caution. The Book One / Book Two split passes the two-path rigor test applied reflexively to authorship. This is worked in §6 below.

## 1. Book One scope — *Commons Bonds*

### 1.1 What it is

A framework-naming book. Its job is to make visible the costs that every extraction transaction in human history has excluded from its price, told through human stories and validated by formal mathematics, written so that the vocabulary enters discourse and the reader cannot unsee what the book shows them.

Book One names a **mechanism** (cost severance), prices an **unpriceable residual** (residual commons value), and demonstrates **universality** (the mechanism scales from one worker to one civilization). It does not name beneficiaries. It does not prescribe a comprehensive policy architecture. It hands tools.

### 1.2 Structural spine

**Mechanism → Math → Universal Validation → Actionable Policy.**

This spine is load-bearing and holds across the ten-chapter structure:

1. **The Plane** — non-physical commons (labor, attention, dynastic labor cost); expanded to carry the knowledge-worker framework
2. **The Miner** — physical extraction; the $100 barrel passage
3. **The Waterman** — renewable commons and renewable mispricing
4. **Norway** — existence proof; cost severance is a policy choice, not an inevitability
5. **The Accountability Gap** — industrial cases (Libby, Deepwater Horizon, Exxon Valdez, Baotou) **plus** Social Security as the fifth case demonstrating cost severance at national financial scale
6. **Three Ways of Counting** — the analytical chapter; RCV decomposition (now canonical as eight-tier via Decision A, 2026-04-19); **RCV Decomposed section carries two parallel individual-level worked applications at full tier depth: a worker-perspective case (drawn from the author's IT consulting / knowledge-worker experience, bearing severed costs from above) and a CEO-perspective case (drawn from the author's healthcare-IT executive experience, allocating severed costs downward while himself bearing them from investors and competitive pressure).** The dual position is the chapter's analytical payload: it demonstrates allocation symmetry (same person, different structural positions, different tier activations, same mechanism) and provides first-person authorial grounding for the framework's architectural claim that cost severance is structural rather than personal. See §1.10 for the book-wide worked-applications distribution across scales.
7. **The Colony Administrator** — the Universality Test across space-mining scenarios; **carries the abundance-fully-stripped worked application at full tier depth** (asteroid-miner case with all eight tiers simultaneously active because no abundance hides any tier). Serves as the clearest operating point for the Abundance Inversion Test — demonstrating what happens when every abundance layer is stripped at once. Verification item: confirm during ratification pass whether current Ch. 7 scaffold applies full eight-tier depth or only the universality validation; if the latter, §1.10's scale-distribution claim requires either scaffold expansion or revision.
8. **What Things Actually Cost** — one fully-worked example (coal, continuing from Ch. 2) priced across all eight tiers, then a single-paragraph aggregate signpost ($10–15T/yr pattern) pointing toward Book Two. Coal is the rigor-based choice because it activates all eight tiers with empirical grounding, preserves architectural continuity with Ch. 2, and serves as the paradigmatic case readers trust the method transfers from. **Not** the full-economy simulation. Fallback case (lithium extraction) named in §4 for drafting-stage contingency only — see §1.9.
9. **The Renewable Imperative** — four-step policy framework; Community Transition Reserve as a named recommendation
10. **Common Bonds** — the closing; the one-letter shift (Commons Bonds → common bonds) as the emotional payload

### 1.3 Load-bearing apparatus

- **Technical Appendix** — RCV integral, convergence theorem, structural cost severance theorem, three-model convergence demonstration, eight-tier decomposition (per Decision A), **and the Abundance Inversion Test as formal methodology** (new in v1.0.0 scope).
- **Vocabulary Glossary** — the 15 canonical terms plus four additions from the Apr 19 integration pass: Full Generational Cost, Dynastic Labor Cost, Community Transition Reserve, Political Capture Cost (see §1.5 on the last of these).
- **Author's Note on AI** — methodological transparency about tool use.
- **Counterargument work** woven through every chapter; no counterargument stitched in as a separate chapter.

### 1.4 The Abundance Inversion Test (canonical in Book One)

Stripping abundance is the methodology that makes invisible tiers visible. Abundance is the mechanism of invisibility; stripping abundance is therefore the method of detection. The test is stated plainly in the Technical Appendix: to identify hidden tiers in any extraction transaction, strip each abundance dimension in turn and observe what becomes visible.

Abundance dimensions to strip: habitability, geographic, temporal, institutional, kindred, ecosystem, political, cohesion, informational, agency. Each strip reveals specific tiers. The asteroid miner scenario strips all of them simultaneously, which is why Chapter 7 is clarifying rather than speculative.

**Why this belongs in Book One (not deferred).** The test passes both gates:

- **Primitiveness gate.** It does not decompose cleanly into existing dimensions; it is the *meta-method* by which dimensions are identified. It operates one level up.
- **Merger gate.** It is bilaterally independent from the tier decomposition itself — the tiers could exist as a static list without the test (and be accused of proliferation), and the test could be articulated without the current tier set (and would still be generative). Neither entails the other.

Without the test, the tier set is a list that invites "why not more / why these" challenges. With it, the tier set is the product of a falsifiable method: any proposed tier must survive the inversion. That is the rigor anchor Book One needs.

### 1.5 Political Capture Cost — shielding condition, not tier

Political Capture Cost is **a shielding condition**, not a tier. It describes the mechanism by which political abundance hides other tiers from view — the cost of operating in a system where suppression of honest accounting is profitable. PCC does not have first-order cost content of its own; it has second-order content about how other costs get hidden.

**Rigor derivation (v1.0.1 rigor pass):**

- **Primitiveness gate fails.** PCC decomposes into meta-level description of shielding operations on existing tiers. It is not a primitive cost in its own right.
- **Merger gate (bilateral independence) fails in one direction.** Other tiers exist without PCC (a natural disaster causes cost severance without political capture being operative). PCC does not exist without other tiers (alone it has no referent — it is always the suppression *of* something).
- **Two-path test on PCC itself.** Path 1 (allocation asymmetry): confirmed. Path 2 (shield absence): *PCC is itself a shield*. Shield-absence fails.

These results together establish PCC's architectural location: the political-abundance dimension of the Abundance Inversion Test. Stripping political abundance reveals the tiers PCC was hiding.

**Where PCC appears in Book One:**

- **Glossary** — as a named concept. The term is useful even if it is not a tier.
- **Abundance Inversion Test / Technical Appendix** — as the political-abundance shielding mechanism.
- **Ch. 9 (Renewable Imperative)** — may be referenced when discussing why honest pricing faces specific structural resistance.

**Where PCC does not appear in Book One:**

- The eight-tier RCV decomposition. PCC is not a tier.

**Where PCC's applied work lives:**

- Book Two. Naming specific industries, documented lobbying histories, dated regulatory capture events, and identified executives is the applicative political work PCC's structural description enables. That work requires the authorial configuration Book Two's preconditions specify.

This resolves the verification item flagged in v1.0.0 §1.5. The rigor pass resolved it.

### 1.6 What Book One does not contain

- The full-economy simulation (Book Two)
- Resource-by-resource transition mechanics (Book Two)
- Named industry beneficiaries with documented suppression histories (Book Two)
- Differentiated-timeline framework for developing nations (Book Two)
- International coordination mechanics (Book Two)
- Comprehensive space-law drafting (Book Three)
- A policy prescription book (Book Two)
- Reader checklists, self-assessment tools, or pre-built application worksheets. **The framework is the tool.** Book One teaches the framework so that readers build their own applications of it — a labor lawyer applies it to a case, a community applies it to a negotiation, an individual worker applies it to a contract. Handing pre-built checklists substitutes the author's instance of the framework for the reader's, which weakens the reader's ability to extend the framework to situations the author didn't anticipate.

Book One's manuscript is "done" when:

1. All ten chapters are drafted with the Apr 19 integrations applied (Social Security in Ch. 5; eight-tier decomposition in Ch. 6; IT stories in Ch. 1; Community Transition Reserve in Ch. 9; Chapter 8 trimmed to the single worked example + one-paragraph aggregate signpost).
2. The Technical Appendix formally includes the Abundance Inversion Test.
3. The Glossary contains the 15 canonical terms plus the four additions.
4. Every chapter has its specific counterarguments woven in (the existing counterargument work is preserved, not redone).
5. The Noema essay has completed its publication cycle (accepted and published, or declined and redirected via the cascade).
6. Book proposal is ready for a publisher with a legal department capable of supporting the manuscript.
7. No scope creep from Book Two or Book Three material has been smuggled back in. Test: if a claim requires naming a specific beneficiary or running a full-economy simulation, it does not belong in Book One.

### 1.9 Chapter 8 story choice — rigor pass results

Chapter 8's worked example is coal. This is a rigor-based choice, not a default. The v1.0.1 rigor pass evaluated six candidates (coal, Social Security, IT/knowledge work, Libby asbestos, asteroid miner hypothetical, and a fresh case represented by lithium extraction) against eight criteria: full-eight-tier activation, empirical record, cross-spectrum positioning, paradigmatic status, individual-reader applicability, publisher/agent appeal, Noema/Berggruen placement fit, and architectural cohesion.

**Coal wins on three criteria that matter most for Ch. 8's specific job:**

1. **Full eight-tier activation.** Most candidates exercise only four-to-six tiers cleanly. Social Security has weak Tiers 3, 7, 8. IT work has weak ecological and civilizational tiers. Libby is narrower than coal. Coal is one of the few extractions where all eight tiers are meaningfully present with empirical grounding. For a chapter whose job is demonstrating the framework produces a legible result across all eight tiers, this is decisive.
2. **Architectural continuity with Ch. 2.** Ch. 8 deepens rather than starts fresh. This preserves narrative cohesion and lets the reader carry context forward rather than re-contextualize.
3. **Paradigmatic status.** If the method works on the most familiar extraction case, readers trust it transfers. Starting with an unfamiliar case (lithium, opioids, data extraction) forces readers to learn the case *and* the method simultaneously, which weakens the method's legibility.

**Coal's real weakness — low individual applicability for non-miner readers — is not Ch. 8's problem to solve.** It is solved by Ch. 1 (IT / knowledge-worker story providing individual applicability) and Ch. 6 (tier decomposition handing readers the method to apply to their own situation). Ch. 8's job is different: produce a fully-costed paradigmatic result. Coal is better suited to that specific job than any alternative.

**Fallback case (drafting-stage contingency only): lithium extraction.** If drafting reveals that Ch. 2's coal treatment cannot be deepened in Ch. 8 without producing redundant-feeling content — a discovery possible only during actual writing — lithium is the fallback. Lithium scores high on paradigmatic (demonstrates method portability to a fresh case), cross-spectrum (green transition paradox is genuinely non-partisan), and contemporary (the discourse is forming now). Weakness is emerging empirical record and architectural cohesion cost. Switching to lithium is a drafting-stage contingency, not a scope-stage change, and would require re-running this scoping decision through rigor before ratification of the switch.

**No other candidate rose above coal under rigor.** Social Security's tier coverage is incomplete. IT work is architecturally redundant with Ch. 1. Libby is redundant with Ch. 5. Asteroid miner is redundant with Ch. 7 and hypothetical. Fresh cases other than lithium (opioids, agricultural extraction, data extraction) did not score meaningfully above lithium on any dimension.

### 1.10 Worked-applications distribution across scales (Configuration C)

Book One carries worked framework applications at four distinct scales across four chapters. This distribution is the result of the v1.0.2 rigor pass on Configuration C (full 25-test + 25-character suite per the rigor protocol document). Each chapter's worked application does work the others cannot do:

| Chapter | Scale | Worked application | Distinctive analytical function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ch. 2 | Individual physical (narrative) | The Miner — McDowell County, $100 barrel | Narrative grounding; introduces cost severance in a concrete case without formal tier analysis |
| Ch. 6 | Individual non-physical (analytical, dual-position) | Worker-perspective + CEO-perspective cases from author's career | Demonstrates allocation symmetry — same person, different structural positions, different tier activations, same mechanism. First full formal tier application in the book. |
| Ch. 7 | Hypothetical maximum-stripped | Asteroid miner / Colony Administrator | Demonstrates the Abundance Inversion Test at its clearest operating point (all abundance stripped, all tiers simultaneously active). Provides universality validation. |
| Ch. 8 | Civilizational | Coal (continuing Ch. 2's grounding at formal tier depth) | Demonstrates the framework producing a legible result at full societal scale; signposts Book Two's civilizational-scale application work. |

**What this distribution does for the book:**

1. **Pattern matching for readers.** Readers pattern-matching their own situation find a worked case close to their scale — individual worker, individual manager, hypothetical maximum, civilizational. Four patterns, four chances for recognition, versus one.
2. **Method portability demonstrated, not asserted.** The framework shows it works across the full scale range. This is a stronger claim than one application at one scale.
3. **Allocation symmetry visibly demonstrated.** Ch. 6's dual-position cases make the framework's structural claim visible in biographical form. Cost severance is shown to be structural (not moral) when the same person occupies both sides of the mechanism across a career.
4. **The "villain who isn't a villain" frame.** Ch. 6's CEO case refuses the easy victim-or-villain narrative. It demonstrates that extraction happens because of structural incentives (investors, competitive pressure, market dynamics), not because CEOs are evil. The same person extracted from below while bearing extraction from above. That is the framework's architectural thesis in narrative form.

### 1.11 Pre-drafting items surfaced by the Configuration C rigor pass

The v1.0.2 rigor pass's character suite surfaced three pre-drafting requirements that must be addressed before Ch. 6 drafting begins:

**1. NDA review from CEO-era engagements.** Whether NDAs from the author's healthcare-IT CEO period constrain writing about the work itself needs confirmation. Most NDAs permit high-level description of work and prohibit specific internal details, client-identifiable information, and trade-secret content — but this must be verified case-by-case for the author's specific agreements. Resolution required before drafting the CEO-perspective case.

**2. Subordinate anonymization protocol.** Character 23 (former subordinate from CEO days) surfaced a critical flag: if any specific person from the author's CEO history could identify themselves in the Ch. 6 CEO-case worked example, there is both ethical exposure (analyzing an individual without consent) and legal exposure (defamation risk even when factually accurate). Canonical mitigation order:

- **Preferred:** pattern description without identifiable specifics. Describe structural position and tier activations without identifying specific engagements, projects, or workers. The framework does not require named specifics to demonstrate tier activation; rigor comes from the tier treatment, not from identifiability.
- **Secondary:** composite fictionalization with explicit author's note ("Details drawn from multiple engagements; no single subordinate is depicted"). Preserves analytical richness at the cost of some first-person specificity.
- **Tertiary:** explicit permission from any identifiable subordinate. Highest authenticity, highest friction; used only if the case specifically requires it.

Under no circumstance is a specific subordinate named or described identifiably without their explicit permission.

**3. First-person discipline across three touchpoints.** Ch. 1 (narrative), Ch. 6 (analytical), and Ch. 8's closing signpost (possibly referential) are three places the author appears as subject. Discipline requirement: Ch. 6's analytical voice must equal the rigor of Ch. 8's coal treatment — no softer analysis on the author's own case. Specifically:

- All eight tiers must be worked for both Ch. 6 cases (worker-perspective and CEO-perspective), with explicit documentation where a tier activates weakly and why.
- Register separation: Ch. 1 is narrative voice, Ch. 6 is analytical voice. The author appears in both but as different functional roles (narrator in Ch. 1, analytical subject in Ch. 6).
- Counterargument coverage in Ch. 6 must include "this is memoir dressed as framework" and "this is self-serving reframing of one's own past behavior" — both must be addressed structurally, not dismissed.

## 2. Book Two scope — *The Subsidy Economy* (working title)

### 2.1 What it is

An applied, politically charged book that prices the invisible at civilizational scale and names the beneficiaries of current mispricing. It presupposes the *Commons Bonds* framework and applies it.

Working spine: the modern global economy is a systemic transfer from the powerless to the powerful, priced at roughly $10–15T per year, and honest accounting reveals both the subsidy and the infrastructure required to replace it.

### 2.2 What it contains (scope signal, not a manuscript)

- Resource-by-resource full-economy simulations (coal, oil, gas, rare earths, agriculture, pharmaceuticals, healthcare, finance, labor, housing, attention)
- Differentiated-timeline framework for developing-nation transitions
- International coordination and enforcement mechanics
- Distributional analysis; the universal-floor insight
- Policy architecture for an extraction-honest economy
- Applied Political Capture Cost: named industries, documented lobbying histories, dated regulatory capture events
- Political economy of the subsidy's defenders

### 2.3 Why it is a separate book — deferral rationale

Five reasons, ordered by weight:

1. **Lone-author vulnerability.** Book Two's claims implicate fossil-fuel companies, pharmaceutical companies, extractive mining operations, defense contractors, federal fiscal history, and concentrated financial interests. A lone author without institutional backing, legal infrastructure, PR support, or platform presence is structurally vulnerable to organized discrediting (the Carson / Piketty attack pattern). The nursing career adds specific professional exposure that a tenured academic writing the same book does not face.
2. **Two attack surfaces become one attack surface.** If Book One and Book Two share an author and release too close together, any successful attack on Book Two's specific claims retroactively reframes Book One as "the framework book by the advocacy author," diminishing Book One's cross-spectrum reception among readers who would have engaged with a neutral framework.
3. **Framework work vs. advocacy work is an asymmetry.** Framework work names mechanisms and can be done safely by individuals. Advocacy work names beneficiaries and is done more safely by coalitions, institutions, or individuals with substantial resources. These are different kinds of work with different risk profiles.
4. **Vocabulary must circulate before application.** If Book One establishes the framework for three to five years before Book Two applies it, Book One's vocabulary becomes a standard that Book Two's opponents must engage with rather than dismiss. Sequencing compounds.
5. **The second book may not need to be Chris's.** If Book One succeeds in placing the vocabulary, other authors — economists with institutional backing, journalists with publisher infrastructure, policy researchers with think-tank cover — are better positioned to do the application work. Being the source of the framework is more impactful, and substantially safer, than also being the author of the application.

### 2.4 Preconditions for writing Book Two

Book Two should not be drafted — only noted — until the preconditions below are met. The v1.0.1 rigor pass distinguishes **necessary** preconditions (structurally required; without them, Book Two fails the two-path rigor test applied to authorship — see §6.1) from **supporting** preconditions (strengthen Book Two's reception and safety but are not structurally required).

**Necessary (both must be met):**

1. **Coauthor relationship *or* institutional affiliation.** An economist, journalist, or policy researcher as coauthor — *or* a research fellowship, PhD program, policy organization, or commons-economics institute affiliation. Either restores the allocation symmetry and shield-absence that a lone-author configuration fails. Without at least one of these, Book Two's authorship configuration fails the rigor test applied reflexively to Book Two itself (§6.1), and the book should not be written in that configuration.
2. **Publisher with strong legal infrastructure has acquired the manuscript.** Book Two's defamation and retaliation surface requires publisher-side legal protection. Self-publishing Book Two is structurally prohibited by the rigor test — not merely inadvisable.

**Supporting (strengthen the project; not individually required):**

3. **Book One has circulated for three-to-five years with measurable vocabulary uptake.** The circulation establishes framework legitimacy that Book Two then applies. Sequencing compounds.
4. **No other author has already produced a substantively overlapping book.** If Book Two's application work has been done by someone else with institutional cover, Chris is freed from that particular labor.
5. **Material life-circumstance stability.** Book Two's draft process should not coincide with a period of unusual life-stage exposure (early nursing career, unstable housing, new dependents).

**Threshold rule:** Both necessary preconditions must be met. Supporting preconditions strengthen the project's case but are not required individually. The v1.0.0 "three-of-five" heuristic was too loose — it permitted configurations that failed the two-path rigor test. v1.0.1 tightens this.

### 2.5 Narrower vs. wider versions of Book Two

If Book Two is written, a scoping sub-decision is required at that point: the **industry-aggregate** version (safe, moderate power) versus the **named-company** version (high power, high exposure). Middle path canonical: name the *patterns* and reference specific industries in aggregate; do not document individual companies' lobbying histories with named executives without serious institutional and legal backing.

## 3. Book Three scope — horizon / speculative

### 3.1 What it is

A book (or a contribution to a framework being drafted by others) that prices extraction before the institutions exist to enforce it. Space law, asteroid mining, lunar resources, off-Earth migration, pricing-at-distance. The Chapter 7 material in Book One is a seed; a full treatment would expand it into its own volume.

### 3.2 Why it is horizon work

Three reasons:

1. **Relevance horizon.** Space-law frameworks are being drafted now but operational extraction is decades out. The vocabulary needs to exist before the institutions form; the institutions need to form before the book matters.
2. **Audience.** The readership is policymakers shaping space law, not general readers; this is a different kind of book with different distribution mechanics.
3. **Authorship question.** It may not be Chris's to write. If Book One's Chapter 7 successfully places the Universality Test into the space-law discourse, others with closer institutional proximity to the drafting process may do this work more naturally.

### 3.3 Current status

Note-taking only. No drafting. No scope claim on Book One beyond Chapter 7's existing treatment.

## 5. Decision heuristic for future scope questions

When considering whether to add, expand, or preserve something in Book One, apply in order:

1. **Does it sharpen the framework, or expand its scope?** Sharpen: usually add. Expand: usually question.
2. **Does it help a reader recognize the mechanism in their own situation?** Yes: prioritize. No: question.
3. **Does it add vocabulary the discourse needs, or duplicate vocabulary already established?** Only novel terms that do distinct work should be added.
4. **Does it require Book One to make claims that would be stronger if held for Book Two?** If yes, hold.
5. **Does it increase Book One's legal or political exposure without proportionate increase in Book One's framework utility?** If yes, reconsider.

**When in doubt, trim.** Book One's job is to make the invisible visible and hand the vocabulary to the discourse. Anything beyond that job risks weakening the book's ability to do its actual job.

## 6. Scoping logic grounded in the operating core

The scoping decisions above are not stylistic preferences. They follow from the framework's own rigor test applied reflexively.

### 6.1 Two-path rigor test applied to authorship

- **Path 1 (allocation symmetry).** For Book One as framework work by a lone author: the intellectual contribution and the authorship cost are borne by the same party. **Symmetric.** For Book Two as advocacy work by a lone author: political costs (reputational, legal, career) are borne by the author while political benefits (policy change, redistribution) are distributed to downstream users. **Asymmetric.**
- **Path 2 (shield absence).** For Book One: the framework names mechanisms, not entities. Distance from named beneficiaries functions as the shield. **Shield present.** For Book Two by a lone author: the book names beneficiaries directly, with no institutional distance between author and named parties. **Shield absent.**

Book One passes both paths (symmetric + shielded). Book Two by a lone author fails both paths (asymmetric + unshielded). **Coauthorship and institutional affiliation are exactly what restore symmetry (shared cost-bearing) and provide shielding (institutional distance).** The preconditions in §2.4 are not conservative taste; they are what the rigor test requires.

This is why the scoping is load-bearing rather than optional. The framework's own logic says: do not publish Book Two until the two-path test passes for its authorship configuration.

### 6.2 Merger and primitiveness gates applied to the Abundance Inversion Test

Both gates were checked in §1.4 and passed. The test belongs in Book One because it is the primitive methodology that generates tiers — without it, the tier set is a list rather than the product of a method. Removing it weakens Book One structurally.

### 6.3 Operating discipline

"Push harder so we only rewrite once." The scoping decision in this document is the single most drift-expensive decision in the project. Every chapter decision, every integration question, every "is this trying to do too much" moment for the next year will resolve by reference to §1 and §2 above. Drift here propagates everywhere. ## 7. Success criteria — Book One

### 7.1 The canonical sentence

If, thirteen or more years from now, a labor lawyer uses "severed cost" in a brief or in open court and the judge does not need it explained — and the author whose name is on the book never knows this happened — the book succeeded.

That is the benchmark. Not royalties. Not prizes. Not revolution. Vocabulary used by people solving real problems, without attribution, because the vocabulary is useful.

### 7.2 Concrete success signals, ordered by likelihood

**Most likely (primary success tier):**

- A labor lawyer, community organizer, or public-interest attorney uses "severed cost" or "residual commons value" in a brief, negotiation, or publication.
- A journalist covering a mine closure, a pension crisis, or an environmental-justice case uses "intergenerational pricing gap" or equivalent framework vocabulary as a headline concept.
- A graduate student builds on the framework in a dissertation.
- An individual reader — a coal miner, a nurse, a gig worker, an IT consultant — reads the book and renegotiates a contract with one internal question that wasn't there before: *am I being asked to donate unpriced tiers?*
- A community negotiating an extraction agreement uses the framework to request specific tier-priced protections that would not otherwise have been on the table.

**Possible, less likely (secondary success tier):**

- A commons-economics course at a university includes *Commons Bonds* on its reading list.
- Scholarship integrating Ostrom's commons governance with *Commons Bonds*' pricing framework appears within a decade-plus horizon.
- A state, tribal nation, or small country adopts the Community Transition Reserve (or a functionally similar ring-fenced intergenerational fund with legibility requirements) as a demonstration case.
- Space-law frameworks being drafted for asteroid mining and lunar resources incorporate tier-priced accountability because someone in the drafting process read Chapter 7.
- A second author publishes a book applying the framework to a specific domain (Book Two written by someone else).
- Framework vocabulary enters general press without explanation.

**Not goals, stated explicitly:**

- Personal financial independence from book sales.
- Systemic replacement of existing economic arrangements.
- Academic prizes.
- Immediate policy adoption at scale.
- Measurable short-term behavior change at population level.

### 7.3 Alignment with Chris's two stated goals

**Goal 1: Future generations might be compensated more fairly.** Book One's contribution is vocabulary and visibility. Compensation requires institutional infrastructure Book One cannot build alone, but institutional infrastructure does not get built until the thing it protects is nameable. The contribution is upstream of the outcome, but real.

**Goal 2: People might accept a job or project with open eyes and honest accounting.** Book One accomplishes this directly, reader by reader. The eight-tier decomposition in Ch. 6 and the IT / knowledge-worker framework in Ch. 1 are the primary vehicles. This is the goal the book is best equipped to serve without intermediaries.

Both goals are compatible with Book One's scope as defined. Neither requires Book Two to be written. Book Two would extend Goal 1 at institutional scale; Book One alone is sufficient for Goal 2.

## 8. Publishing path — integral enablement of success criteria

The v1.0.0 draft mischaracterized the Noema / Berggruen / cascade strategy as "orthogonal" to Book One's scope. The v1.0.1 rigor pass corrects this: **the publishing path is integral.** It is the vocabulary's entry vector into discourse, and it is load-bearing on every success criterion in §7.

### 8.1 Why the publishing path is load-bearing

The causal chain that makes §7's success criteria possible:

**Essay placement → vocabulary enters intellectual discourse → literary agent notices → book publisher acquires manuscript → Book One published with non-trivial distribution → vocabulary adoption becomes possible at the scale §7 assumes.**

Without essay placement, the chain breaks at step one. A self-published book by an independent author without prior essay circulation reaches an order of magnitude fewer readers than a publisher-backed book preceded by a Noema or Boston Review essay. The 13+-year labor-lawyer success criterion assumes the vocabulary has reached labor lawyers. That reach depends on the publishing path.

### 8.2 The cascade (as previously established)

1. **Noema** — primary submission (submitted; currently in response window). Noema's cross-spectrum intellectual-policy reach is the optimal first-mover venue for this specific framework.
2. **Boston Review** — immediate fallback (24–48 hours after any Noema decline). Forum format fits dialogue-oriented reception; no simultaneous-submission restriction once Noema responds.
3. **The Atlantic Ideas** and further cascade venues — beyond these two, per the consolidated Publishing Strategy document.
4. **Berggruen Prize independent rewrite** — parallel credential path, AI-free, August 17, 2026 deadline. This track is independent of the Noema cascade and provides a second institutional anchor regardless of the cascade's outcome.

Specific agent and publisher names identified as next-stage targets live in the consolidated Publishing Strategy document (Apr 18, 2026) and are not reproduced here — this document references the strategy; it does not replace it.

### 8.3 What this means for scope

The integration does **not** expand Book One's scope. It documents a dependency. The scope decisions in §1–§3 hold independently; they are not revised by the publishing path's load-bearing status. What changes is the recognition that Book One's success criteria (§7) depend on the publishing path succeeding at non-trivial circulation.

Specific implications:

- **Scope questions under §5's decision heuristic should not factor in "what increases publishing appeal"** — that would be reverse-fitting the framework to the market. The framework's integrity is primary; the publishing path's job is to carry the integrity-preserved framework to readers.
- **Timing of Book Two** is downstream of the publishing cascade's reception, not independent of it. §2.4's supporting precondition #3 (Book One circulation for three-to-five years) assumes the cascade succeeds at some level. If the cascade fails entirely, §9's revisit trigger #1 fires.
- **Scope revisions** triggered by cascade outcomes (e.g., essay reception revealing a framework weakness that needs addressing in the book) go through §9's revisitability process, not §5's decision heuristic.

### 8.4 Separation of concerns

This scoping document handles **what the book is**. The Publishing Strategy document handles **how the book reaches readers**. Both are load-bearing. Neither replaces the other. The relationship is: scope decides content; publishing strategy decides distribution; success criteria depend on both.

## 11. Gate-check process — post-drafting rigor application

The scoping decisions in this document are proposed-canonical before drafting. Scoping decisions made in advance of writing can fail empirically when writing happens — a chapter that was supposed to fit its scope may not, a framing that seemed right in the abstract may read wrong in the chapter voice, or a rigor pass conclusion may need revision in light of actual drafted content. Gate-check is the process for catching these failures before they compound.

### 11.1 When gate-check runs

Gate-check applies after each chapter draft is complete. It does not apply during drafting (which would paralyze writing), and it does not apply only after all ten chapters are complete (which would defer rewrites too long). One gate-check per chapter, when that chapter's draft reaches a complete-enough state to be read for rigor.

Additional gate-checks run at:

- The midpoint (after Ch. 5 draft complete) — re-read Ch. 1 through Ch. 5 together for cumulative effect and arc coherence.
- The full-draft point (after Ch. 10 draft complete) — whole-book gate-check for cross-chapter consistency, vocabulary consistency, and the close's payoff.
- Any point where an ordering question, scope question, or architectural question surfaces that earlier rigor passes deferred to drafting-stage.

### 11.2 What gate-check does

For each chapter:

1. **Full rigor pass per `commons_bonds_rigor_protocol_v1_0_0.md`.** All 25 framework tests + all 25 characters applied to the actual drafted chapter.
2. **Test against this document's scoping claims for that chapter.** Does the chapter do what §1.2 said it would? Does it avoid what §1.6 / §1.9 said it should avoid? Does it carry the pre-drafting items (§1.11) through to drafted form?
3. **Failure identification.** Which tests failed, which characters surfaced pressure that the draft didn't mitigate, which scoping claims are contradicted by the draft?
4. **Rewrite protocol selection.** Scope revision, chapter revision, or drafting-only adjustment depending on failure type.

### 11.3 Failure modes to watch for

- **Scope boundary violation.** Chapter tries to do what was deferred (e.g., Ch. 8 begins the full-economy simulation, Ch. 6 names specific industry beneficiaries). Response: trim or rewrite to restore boundary.
- **Tone failure.** Chapter reads in a register that contradicts positioning. Examples: Ch. 2 reads as attack on miners, Ch. 6's dual case reads as memoir rather than analytical demonstration, Ch. 7 reads as science fiction rather than rigorous methodology. Response: revise voice / framing without changing content.
- **Rigor insufficiency.** Framework elements asserted but not demonstrated (e.g., eight tiers named but not worked, Abundance Inversion Test described but not applied). Response: expand chapter or restructure to put demonstration in foreground.
- **Counterargument coverage gap.** Obvious counterargument exists that the chapter doesn't address. Response: add counterargument treatment; if the counterargument is structural rather than tactical, scope-level revision may be warranted.
- **First-person discipline failure.** Ch. 1, Ch. 6, or Ch. 8 touchpoints let authorial voice drift across registers (narrative vs. analytical vs. signpost). Response: sharpen register boundaries, possibly consolidate touchpoints.
- **Pre-drafting item unresolved at draft time.** E.g., subordinate anonymization not actually applied in the Ch. 6 CEO-case, NDA review not completed before drafting started. Response: hold publication, complete the pre-drafting work, revise draft accordingly.

### 11.4 Rewrite protocols by failure severity

- **Drafting-only adjustment.** Failure is tactical (phrasing, specific example choice, paragraph structure). Revise within the existing chapter scope and scoping claims. No scope document change.
- **Chapter revision.** Failure is structural within the chapter but doesn't affect scope. Significant rewrite of the chapter; scope doc remains canonical. Example: Ch. 7 needs fuller tier-depth treatment than current scaffold provides, so Ch. 7 expands within its existing scope claim.
- **Scope revision.** Failure surfaces a scoping decision that was wrong. Scope doc updates (v1.0.4 or later); affected chapters revised accordingly. Example: if Ch. 2 + Ch. 7 drafts in current order read poorly, reordering may become warranted — that's scope revision.
- **Architectural revision.** Failure surfaces a framework-level issue (e.g., a tier is not actually bilaterally independent; the Abundance Inversion Test doesn't generate the tier it was supposed to). Rigor protocol v1.0.x update may be needed; multiple chapters affected. High-cost revision; apply the full operating discipline before committing.
