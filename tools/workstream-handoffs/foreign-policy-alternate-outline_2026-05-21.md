## Reframe cut — international-dimension / border-adjustment spine

The Atlantic Ideas essay carries the four-step Classify → Reserve → Invest → Reassess sequence as its spine, with the international-dimension section (§V Border) as one piece of the architecture. The Foreign Policy reframe **inverts the priority:** the international-dimension becomes the spine; the four-step sequence collapses to one paragraph of background context. Why this cut:

- FP's audience is foreign-policy establishment (State / DoD / think tanks / foreign correspondents / diplomatic readers). Their natural question is *how does this engage with countries and trade*, not *what's the optimal extraction-pricing mechanism*.
- The Atlantic Ideas essay's §V Border + Ch 9's CBAM material + Clarion-Clipperton Zone material + rare-earth / Baotou / DRC transboundary cases together make a coherent ~1,200w argument standalone.
- The cost-severance-as-trade-mechanism observation (Ch 9 line 94 beats) is the *distinctive* contribution this essay would make to FP discourse.

## Proposed structure (5 paragraphs, ~1,200w)

| ¶ | Function | Anchor | Word target |
|---|---|---|---|
| ¶1 | News-pegged hook + thesis | CBAM Phase II fully operational 2026 — the EU has now built a working transboundary-pricing mechanism for carbon. The next question is what other transboundary commons get the same treatment. | ~200w |
| ¶2 | The cost-severance trade observation | International trade as cost-severance delivery system. The Baotou tailings pond (7 sq mi radioactive slurry) supplies the rare earths in an iPhone sold in California; the cobalt in the iPhone's battery was, until recently, mined by an estimated ~40K children in DRC; the oil in the diesel a European truck burns may have come from the Niger Delta, where 50 years of spillage has rendered thousands of square kilometers uninhabitable. The beneficiary does not share a border (or, often, a continent) with the bearer. | ~250w |
| ¶3 | What CBAM proved + what it doesn't yet do | CBAM's mechanism: a tariff scaled to the carbon a foreign producer didn't price. It demonstrates that transboundary cost-pricing is legally, administratively, and diplomatically feasible when the jurisdiction doing it is large enough to matter. What CBAM does NOT yet do: extend the same logic to other transboundary commons. Rare earths. Cobalt. Phosphorus. Fossil aquifers (Ogallala / North China Plain / Guarani). The Clarion-Clipperton Zone polymetallic-nodule deposits, currently under International Seabed Authority commercial-mining-code negotiation. | ~300w |
| ¶4 | The substitutability-gap classification frame | A generalized border adjustment scaled to *substitutability gap* — how irreplaceable a resource is, how long the externality tail runs, how concentrated the deposits are. EU Critical Raw Materials Act 2023 (34 materials) is a partial implementation; Japan's post-2010 rare-earth response funding Lynas; South Korea's 2019 reserves; the US MP Materials Mountain Pass 2018 acquisition — all are *uncoordinated national responses* to the same transboundary commons problem. Coordinated border adjustment is the next institutional step. | ~250w |
| ¶5 | Close — what this is and isn't | Not a treaty; not a multilateral negotiation; not a Bretton Woods 2.0 proposal. A pricing principle that scales by ordinary mechanics of market access: large enough jurisdictions start; the cheapest path to retaining market access is to internalize costs at the source and capture the revenue domestically. Over time the floor rises. Carbon pricing followed this path — handful of jurisdictions in early 2000s; more than 70 today. The substitutability-gap analog can follow. | ~200w |

**Total: ~1,200w.**

## Apparatus exclusion — sharper than Atlantic Ideas

FP's accessibility-language rule is explicit. Drop:

- "Existential substitutability gap" / "ESG" — translate to "irreplaceability gap" or just "how hard the material is to substitute."
- "Residual commons value" / "RCV" — never use; replace with "the cost the price misses" or "the unpriced cost."
- "Asymmetric Regret Rule" — never use; replace with "cheap insurance against bigger-than-we-think downside."
- "Cost severance" — use sparingly; can define once but not load-bear as term-of-art.
- "Accountability bond" / "Foreclosure bond" / "Restitution bond" — never use; translate to structural shape.
- "Cluster-γ" — never.

The FP cut's distinctive contribution is the *observation* (international trade as cost-severance delivery; CBAM as proof of concept; substitutability-gap as classification frame). The framework apparatus stays in the book.
