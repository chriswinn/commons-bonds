## §A — Atlantic Ideas essay (`publishing/essays/atlantic-ideas-pricing-honestly/essay.md:80`)

**Issue: standing-assignment drift in the coercion-deferral closer.** The author's ratified stance (2026-06-09) is **first-person abstention with NO gatekeeping of standing**: he declines to price coercion himself, credits the reparations field as already doing the work, and takes no position on who is *permitted* to enter the number. Phrasings that assign or qualify standing drift off-target.

**Current closer (the last sentence is the drift):**
> "…The framework has room for accounting of this kind, and the people best placed to do it are already doing it. Putting a price on the costs of coercion borne by a group I do not belong to is not mine to do, and I leave that work to those who are continuing it. What the framework offers is the ledger. **What belongs on it, looking backward, is theirs to enter.**"

**Keep:** "is not mine to do, and I leave that work to those who are continuing it" — that is exactly the target (first-person abstention + descriptive field-crediting).

**Fix in the redraft:** soften or drop the final sentence so the passage does not assign the ledger entry to a defined "they." Two directions (redraft's choice):
- (a) End on "What the framework offers is the ledger." (drop the assignment sentence entirely — cleanest).
- (b) Keep a closing beat but in first-person abstention: e.g. "What the framework offers is the ledger; the backward accounting is not a column I will fill in."

**Why it matters beyond the standing point:** "theirs to enter" also **collides with the book's signature closer**, Ch 1's "The numbers are yours to enter" (addressed to the *reader*, second person). The essay hands the same "enter the ledger" cadence to a third-party group instead. Reconciling the standing drift also resolves that collision.

**Cross-reference for consistency:** the book side now uses first-person abstention — Ch 5:192 was fixed today (commit `99f8270`: "one the reparations-economics field is already carrying forward and one this book does not undertake"), and TA §1.10:598 was already clean ("methodologically unresolved … outside this volume's scope"). The essay should land in the same register.

**Hard constraint (unchanged):** never compute or insert a coercion/reparations dollar figure; credit the field (Darity, Mullen, et al.) without gatekeeping. Per the guardrail memory linked above.

**Fix in the redraft (optional):** if the redraft keeps this passage, consider "rivalrous and depletable" or "drawn down by use" instead of "finite" for the coordination commons, to keep the word off the Definition 1.1 floor. Purely precautionary; defensible as-is.
