**For the Redraft Campaign PM / coordination layer — NOT to be handed to the prose-drafting agents directly.**

## What changed
The scope claim "the book only prices non-renewable commons, and renewables extracted past their regenerative capacity" was author-flagged as false: it is too narrow and contradicts the framework's own ten-category commons taxonomy (the TA §3 restriction even excluded information/network commons the framework prices). Author-ratified corrected framing (descriptive only):

> The framework prices cost severance, the foreclosure of commons value, which is positive whenever consumption forecloses future access faster than the commons can restore itself: always for non-renewables (restoration is zero); only when harvest outpaces regeneration for renewables; and for non-resource commons (institutional, civic, epistemic) when degradation outruns recovery. Where use stays within what the commons can restore, the price is correctly zero.

The normative inference "if we used only renewables within regeneration the ecosystem would be in harmony" is **deliberately excluded** (political / attack-surface). Keep the scope statement strictly descriptive.
