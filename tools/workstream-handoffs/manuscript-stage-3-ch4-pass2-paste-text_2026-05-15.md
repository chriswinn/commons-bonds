**File path:** manuscript/chapters/Chapter__4_THEEXISTENCEPROOF__Draft.md

**Voice register:** analytical-comparative policy prose. NOT memoir
(Ch 1 register). NOT first-person-narrative (Ch 2 / Ch 3 register).
Ch 4 is third-person-analytical with embedded comparative scaffolding

**Voice-polish emphases for Ch 4 specifically (per chapter register):**

- **Cross-country comparison structures.** Three-state and four-state
  enumerations of petroleum-state outcomes are the chapter's primary
  argumentative scaffolding. Rule-of-three and tidy-parallel patterns
  will appear; many will be substantively-earned (the comparison itself
  IS three or four cases). Flag where the cadence reaches beyond the
  substance — e.g., "Some did X. Some did Y. Some did Z." formulations
  where the three options are stylized rather than literally surveyed.
- **Date-heavy chronologies.** The institutional-architecture argument
  rests on chronological sequencing (1969 Ekofisk → 1990 fund founding
  → 2001 fiscal rule → 2006 rename → subsequent administrations).
  Date-heavy prose risks cadence-flatness — declarative-three-in-a-row
  with no sentence-length variance ("X happened in Year. Y happened in
  Year. Z happened in Year."). Flag any chronology paragraph that flat-
  lines.
- **Counterfactual scaffolding.** "If Norway had done X, then Y"
  constructions; "Other countries chose differently"-type setups. These
  are policy-essay-defensible but can read as expository-flatness or
  meta-commentary when the framing-phrase replaces concrete-naming.
- **Apparatus residue.** Ch 4 prefaces formal apparatus introduction
  in Ch 5 + Ch 6. Per Ch 4 Pass-1 §"Apparatus regression check," Ch 4
  permits lowercase prose use of "cost severance," "accountability bond
  B," "residual commons value," "foreclosure component," "externality
  tail," and a single capitalized "Intergenerational Option Value" at
  Ch 4:121 (introduction site). Verify no NEW apparatus terms have been
  introduced in body prose since Pass-1 baseline (no Greek letters; no
  subscripts; no flagship-term name-defenses that belong to other
  chapters). Verify the lowercase usages still match canonical
  apparatus register decisions.
- **Connective-tissue clichés.** Higher risk in analytical prose than
  in memoir. Watch for "in short," "ultimately," "moreover,"
  "furthermore," "that being said," "what this means is," "the point
  is" — Ch 4's argumentative voice may reach for these as paragraph-
  binders. Each instance should earn its space; default disposition
  for unearned instances is cut.
- **Hedge phrases.** "I will argue that…" / "It seems likely that…" /
  "Perhaps…" — chapter is declarative-policy-argument register; hedge
  phrases are register-breaks. Flag any.
- **Expository flatness.** "The plain definition is this:" / "Here is
  what I think:" / "The argument is simple:" — Ch 4 is making an
  existence-proof argument; flag any expository-frame phrase that
  could be replaced by direct claim.
- **Named-figure register.** Norwegian PMs (Stoltenberg, Bondevik II,
  Solberg, Støre) named in official capacity per standard journalistic
  practice; Ken Saro-Wiwa named with historical-record status (Pass-1
  consent-check clean). No living named subjects requiring consent
  gating. Verify the register holds — verify no informal nicknames
  applied to political figures; verify no biographical detail beyond
  what the argument requires.

- **Rule of three** — "A. B. C." constructions across sentence-units.
  HIGH risk for Ch 4 (comparative-case scaffolding).
- **Em-dash crutches** — em-dashes as connective tissue rather than
  deliberate parenthetical extension or punch. Watch paragraph-level
  density (Ch 1's F-V4 + F-V8 flagged paragraphs with 3-4 em-dashes

  / institutions / time periods. HIGH risk for Ch 4.
- **Hedge phrases** — register-break for analytical prose.
- **Connective-tissue clichés** — paragraph-binder phrases. HIGHER
  risk in analytical voice than in memoir.
- **Meta-commentary** — "That is the whole sentence." / "What I mean
  to say is…" / "Let me explain…"
- **Expository flatness** — "The plain definition is this:" / "Here
  is what I think:" / "The argument is simple:"
- **Declarative-three-in-a-row** — three short declaratives in
  succession with no sentence-length variance. HIGH risk for Ch 4
  (date-heavy / event-heavy chronology passages).
- **Nostalgia / sentimentality tics** — "There are not many places
  like that anymore" / "Back when…" — lower risk in analytical voice
  but possible at the Ekofisk-discovery scene-setting.
- **Cadence repetition** — "It changed everything. It humbled the
  industry. It made…" patterns.
- **Apparatus residue** — see emphases above; verify no premature
  formal apparatus.
- **Register-break to corporate / bureaucratic phrasing** — Ch 1
  flagged "at this time" as a register-break (F-V13). Analytical prose
  may reach for "in this context," "going forward," "at the end of
  the day," "to that end" — flag any.
- **Tense-consistency** — Ch 4 is past-tense for historical
  chronology + present-tense for current-state analytical claims +
  conditional for counterfactual scaffolding. Verify shifts are
  scene-cued; no leakage.
