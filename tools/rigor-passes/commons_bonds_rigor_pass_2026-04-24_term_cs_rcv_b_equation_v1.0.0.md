## §2. The question under test

### §2.1 The equation

**CS = RCV − B**

- **CS** — Cost Severance (mechanism as quantified deficit).
- **RCV** — Residual Commons Value (the true cost the mechanism leaves unpriced).
- **B** — Accountability Bond (the cost the system has actually priced onto the extractor).

Equation's claim: **the Cost Severance in a transaction is precisely the gap between true residual commons value and actually-priced accountability.**

### §2.2 Why the equation earns separate named-object status

Compare two framings:

**Framing A (without named equation):** *"The framework has three quantities — CS, RCV, B — that relate in some structurally important way. Readers will pick up the relationship from context."*

**Framing B (with named equation):** *"The framework's central relational claim is CS = RCV − B. This single equation does three things: (i) defines Cost Severance quantitatively; (ii) names B = RCV as the honest-accounting ideal; (iii) makes CS > 0 the empirical claim the book is about."*

Framing B is stronger because:
- Ability to cite the equation as a single object ("Per the framework's CS = RCV − B equation...") is rhetorically and intellectually more economical than reconstructing the relationship each time.
- Critics, reviewers, and adopters engage the equation as a claim-object — it either holds or it doesn't.
- Education: readers who internalize the equation have acquired the framework's central assertion in one expression.
- Mathematical-form discipline: naming the equation forces the framework to maintain consistency between its terms. If a later rigor pass weakens one term, the equation surfaces the consequence immediately.

### §2.3 Options

- **A — CONFIRM Ring 2.** Named relational claim; distinct record from component records.
- **B — DEMOTE** to "just math" among named components.
- **C — PROMOTE to Ring 1.**
- **D — MERGE** into Cost Severance's formal definition.

### §2.4 Falsifiers

**Option A falsified if:**
- The equation collapses into a trivial consequence of component definitions (no independent content).
- Named status produces no pedagogical, rhetorical, or rigor-infrastructure benefit over the alternatives.

**Option C falsified if:**

## §3. Principle #3 derivation check (decisive module)

Is the equation a TRIVIAL consequence of component definitions, or does it carry INDEPENDENT CONTENT?

### §3.1 Component definitions (as ratified)

- **CS (Cost Severance):** the mechanism by which extraction separates value capture from cost bearing; quantified as the deficit between RCV and B.
- **RCV:** ∫ᵗ₀^∞ {[1 − S(t|t₀)] · U(R, t, Q(t)) + E(R, t)} · D(t, t₀) dt — integrand over future time.
- **B (Accountability Bond):** the total dollars forced onto extractors via accountability instruments.

**Observation:** CS's definition already says "quantified as the deficit between RCV and B" — so is the equation trivially implied?

**Counter-observation:** CS's definition mentions the relationship in words; the equation specifies HOW. "CS is a deficit between RCV and B" ≠ "CS = RCV − B" (the latter specifies arithmetic subtraction, not ratio, percentage, difference-weighted, etc.). The equation's specific arithmetic form is independent content beyond the words in CS's definition.

**Counter-counter-observation:** but "deficit" in economics usually means arithmetic subtraction...

**Resolution:** the equation is a LIGHT non-triviality — it fixes the specific arithmetic form (subtraction). Without the equation, CS's definition could be interpreted as ratio, logarithm, multiplicative, or other deficit-like relationships. The equation eliminates that ambiguity.

**Verdict:** Principle #3 derivation check: PASSES (light non-triviality sufficient). Equation carries independent content beyond its components' definitions.

### §3.2 The relational-claim content

Beyond the arithmetic form, the equation asserts:
1. **B and RCV are the only quantities that matter for computing CS.** Not substitutability, not externality tail in isolation, not abundance masking — those are captured via RCV's integrand. CS is specifically a function of RCV and B.
2. **The accountability gap is linear in B.** Increasing B by $X reduces CS by $X. This is a policy-design claim: accountability-instrument scaling is well-behaved.
3. **B = RCV is the framework's ideal state.** CS = 0 ↔ B = RCV. The equation defines what "honest accounting" means quantitatively.

These are substantive framework claims, not trivial consequences. The equation earns its keep.

## §4. M12 — Intellectual honesty / Prior-art audit

### §4.1 Exact-equation search

"CS = RCV − B" as a specific equation — not found in environmental economics, Pigouvian-tax literature, or commons-governance literature. Framework-specific notation.

### §4.2 Concept-level search (equation structure)

**Gap/deficit accounting equations** are established in several adjacent domains:

- **Pigouvian gap:** externality − tax = unpriced amount. Structural parallel to CS = RCV − B (true cost − priced cost = gap). Pigou 1920 + subsequent environmental-economics literature.
- **Stern Review intergenerational framing:** present-cost-to-future-generations minus current-priced amount = intergenerational transfer. Stern 2007. Structural parallel.
- **DICE social-cost-of-carbon framework:** cumulative marginal damage − market carbon price = social-cost-of-carbon gap. Nordhaus. Parallel.
- **Accounting identity:** economic-cost minus recorded-cost = unrecorded-cost. Standard accounting.

**Finding:** the STRUCTURE (true cost − priced cost = gap) is established across multiple adjacent frameworks. The framework's contribution is the SPECIFIC DECOMPOSITION — what RCV and B are specifically — not the equation-form.

### §4.3 Classification

**Independent specialization of established gap/deficit-accounting structure.**
- Not original coinage (gap-form equations are ubiquitous).
- Not adoption of specific established equation.
- Framework novelty is the specific RCV definition (abundance-inversion-grounded integrand over future time) + specific B definition (full Severed-Cost spectrum) + specific CS role (mechanism that produces the gap).

### §4.4 Required action per M12 action ladder

The framework's contribution is not the gap-form but the specific decomposition: RCV integrates over future time with abundance-inversion-grounded cost terms, and B spans the full Severed Cost spectrum rather than single-project or single-externality scope."*
