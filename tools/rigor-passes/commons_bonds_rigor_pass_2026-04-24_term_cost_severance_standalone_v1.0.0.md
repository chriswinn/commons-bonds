## §3. Test 1 — Name-mechanism match (standalone analysis)

### §3.1 Each word does work

**"Cost":** the substrate being severed. Not value, not risk, not price — specifically the cost (the bill, the consequence, the price-not-charged). Naming the severed substrate as "cost" explicitly is deliberate; it frames the framework as accounting.

Contrast with alternatives:
- "Value Severance" — severs the wrong thing. Value isn't being severed; it goes with the capturer. Cost is what's left behind, severed from the capturer.
- "Price Severance" — market-price-centric; misses the framework's critique that markets don't price what the framework prices.
- "Risk Severance" — risk is subtly different (uncertain costs vs definite costs). Cost is more direct.

**"Severance":** the act of separating. Active, definite, structural.

Contrast with alternatives:
- "Displacement" — too neutral; doesn't capture the severing act.
- "Offloading" — corporate-strategy register; doesn't carry the moral weight.
- "Externalization" — economics jargon; neutral, missing the structural-violence framing.
- "Transfer" — too neutral; implies consent + destination.

**"Severance":** implies cutting-apart. The word has weight. Severance pay. Severed ties. Severed limbs. Structural, consequential.

### §3.2 Compound coherence

"Cost Severance" — the act of severing cost from those who capture value. Structural, moral-weighted, specific, accounting-aligned.

No rename improves on all four: structure + moral-weight + specificity + accounting-register-alignment.

### §3.3 Principle #3 check

Does the term accurately name the mechanism? YES — severance names the act; cost names what's being severed. PASSES.

## §5. Test 3 — Terminological distinctiveness

### §5.1 Non-collision with adjacent economic vocabulary

**Externality:** names the flow of costs to non-parties (Pigou). Adjacent but distinct — externality is the flow; cost severance is the structural mechanism that creates the flow.

**Rent-seeking:** names the pursuit of economic rents. Adjacent but distinct — rent-seeking is behavior; cost severance is structure.

**Structural violence:** Galtung / Farmer. Adjacent but distinct — structural violence is the broader phenomenon; cost severance is a specific economic mechanism within it.

**Accumulation by dispossession:** Harvey. Adjacent but distinct — AbD names the accumulative process; cost severance names a specific structural feature (separation of capture from cost-bearing).

**No collision.** Cost Severance names something specific that other economic-vocabulary items don't name.

### §5.2 Originality

Chris's original coinage. Not a pre-existing academic term being repurposed. The compound is distinctively the framework's.

### §5.3 Verdict on §5

PASS. Distinctive, non-colliding, original.
