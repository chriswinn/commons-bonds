## §2. Pass-3 scope reminder

Pass 3 applies the audience pressure-test character set built for Ch 1's specific role as the book's memoir opener (general-reader-calibrated rather than packet-recipient-calibrated). For each character the test asks:

- What does this character read for?
- What lands for this character in the post-Pass-2-applied prose?
- What flags for this character?
- Does the chapter hold this character's attention through the close and into Ch 2?

Score per character on the canonical include-vs-exclude scale:

- **✓✓✓ INCLUDE** — strong yes; chapter lands decisively for this character.
- **✓✓ INCLUDE** — yes; chapter lands.
- **✓ INCLUDE** — yes with minor reservations.
- **NEUTRAL ~** — neither alienated nor strongly engaged.
- **⚠ EXCLUDE** — may disengage; flag specific reason.
- **⚠⚠ EXCLUDE** — actively rejects; flag specific reason.
- **⚠⚠⚠ EXCLUDE** — disqualifying for this character at Tier 1; would require structural revision.

Tier-1 ⚠⚠⚠ at any character = STRUCTURAL REVISION NEEDED (re-fire Stage 2). Tier-1 EXCLUDE at lesser severity = SPOT-FIX NEEDED. Tier-2/3 EXCLUDE = recommended spot-fix unless author rules otherwise.

In addition, Pass 3 produces explicit verdicts on the **nine items** Pass 2 §6 + the post-Pass-2 PM session flagged forward.

**Ch 1-specific audience-load lens.** Unlike the analytical chapters, Ch 1 carries no apparatus, no quantitative case-studies, no formal lineage prose. The audience-load question is whether the memoir-register lands as **honest-and-load-bearing** for the book's reader-stack rather than as sentimental-and-self-indulgent — and whether the personal stakes named in the chapter (NICU son, aging parents, no-contact-with-son, NIH-era work-cost, paternity-leave-end pivot) earn the reader's trust into the framework the book will develop.
