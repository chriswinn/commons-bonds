### F-3.1-H1 (HIGH) — Bailey biographical fabrication: "went underground at seventeen... fifty years later... thirty-six of those years actively mining"

**Location:** essay.md line 35 (§IV Bailey opening sentence).

**Current draft text:**
> "Robert Bailey went underground at seventeen and came out for the last time, on a stretcher, fifty years later. He worked thirty-six of those years actively mining, first in McDowell County and then, after the McDowell mines closed, in Boone County, because Boone was where the work had gone and Bailey had nothing else."

**§7 canonical specification:** §7.3: "**Mining tenure:** **36 years underground.** Started in McDowell County; finished in Boone County after McDowell's mines closed." §7.3 also anchors Bailey's death at 28 days after his February 2019 final interview, with essay line 37 confirming his age at that interview as 63.

**Corroborating Ch 2 source:** Ch 2 line 30: "Robert Bailey spent thirty-six years underground. He started in McDowell County and finished in Boone County after McDowell's mines closed and he had nowhere else to go." Chapter does NOT specify (a) starting age of 17; (b) "fifty years later" total span; (c) "thirty-six of those years actively mining" (vs the total span). The chapter's 36-year figure is the total tenure underground, not "active mining years inside a longer 50-year span."

**Issue:** The essay invents three biographical specifics that are not in §7.3 and not in Ch 2:
1. Started at age 17.
2. Came out for the last time, on a stretcher, fifty years later (i.e., at age 67).
3. Thirty-six of those fifty years were "actively mining" (implying 14 years underground but not actively mining).

These three invented details are **internally inconsistent** with the essay's own §IV chronology. Essay line 37 confirms Bailey was 63 at his February 2019 final interview and died 28 days later. If Bailey started underground at 17, he was a miner for 46 years (17 → 63 at death), not 50, and the "came out for the last time at age 67" framing places his last underground entry four years after his documented death. The "thirty-six of those years actively mining" elaboration (which would imply a 14-year gap between active mining and final underground entry) is contradicted by both the §7.3 canonical "36 years underground" total and the chapter's "started in McDowell County and finished in Boone County" continuity-of-employment framing.

For Harper's specifically the fact-check bar is calibrated for **named-subject biographical precision** (Tier 1 #1 Christopher Beha + Tier 1 #2 Harper's reader recognize invented biographical detail as the LLM-tic the venue is most sensitive to in long-form essays). A fact-checker verifying Bailey against the WV Public Broadcasting Inside Appalachia interview transcript + Bailey's published obituary would flag the "seventeen / fifty years later / on a stretcher" elaboration as unsourced; the cleaner posture is the §7.3 + chapter canonical "thirty-six years underground" total without the invented age/span specifics.

**Options:**
- **(a) Strip to §7.3 + chapter canonical: "Robert Bailey spent thirty-six years underground, first in McDowell County and then, after the McDowell mines closed, in Boone County."** Minimum-prose-change; removes the invented "seventeen," "fifty years later," "on a stretcher," and "thirty-six of those years" specifics; preserves the McDowell → Boone employment continuity from §7.3 + Ch 2. The "on a stretcher" image, if author wants it preserved, can land at the end-of-mining moment ("...and was carried out on a stretcher the last time") without the invented "fifty years later" anchor.
- **(b) Restructure to preserve "on a stretcher" sensory anchor + canonical 36-year tenure: "Robert Bailey spent thirty-six years underground. He started in McDowell County and finished in Boone County, after the McDowell mines closed and there was nowhere else to go; the last time he came out, he came out on a stretcher."** Two-sentence form; preserves the load-bearing 36-year canonical + the McDowell → Boone continuity + the "on a stretcher" image; drops "seventeen," "fifty years later," "thirty-six of those years actively mining."
- **(c) Hold the current draft text and defer to fact-checker verification at Harper's submit.** **NOT recommended** — the elaboration is internally inconsistent with the essay's own age-at-death chronology and is unsourced against §7.3 + Ch 2; Harper's fact-check team will catch it.

**Recommendation:** **(b)** — restructure to preserve the "on a stretcher" sensory anchor + the canonical 36-year tenure + McDowell → Boone continuity, dropping the invented age + span + active-vs-total breakdown.

**Reasoning:** The "on a stretcher" image is genuine Harper's-register sensory anchoring (the kind of physical-detail Tier 1 #1 Beha + Tier 1 #2 Harper's reader read for); preserving it requires only minor restructuring. The "seventeen" + "fifty years later" + "thirty-six of those years actively mining" specifics are unsourced and internally inconsistent — they read as Stage 2 audience-blind drafter's confabulation, the discipline-specific failure mode the Noema test (commit `5167edd`, 2026-05-10) documented as the v2.0 Amendment A motivation.
