## §3. Principle #3 derivation check (decisive module)

### §3.1 Is CSG a distinct concept from S?

**Definition of S:** S(R, t | t₀) — probability that a substitute for resource R exists at time t, given baseline t₀.

**Definition of S_max:** ceiling / supremum of S over some evaluation range.

**Definition of CSG:** CSG(R) = S_max(R, industrial) − S_max(R, existential).

The operation "take the difference between two S_max evaluations" doesn't introduce a new concept — it applies S in two contexts and compares. Analogous framework derivations NOT named separately:
- S(t) − S(t−1): change in substitutability over time (no dedicated term).
- S evaluated for rival baselines (no dedicated term).
- Maximum of S over uncertainty range (no dedicated term).

**Asymmetry check:** what distinguishes CSG from these other derivations? Answer: the INTUITION that industrial vs existential contexts can diverge dramatically. But the intuition isn't the concept — the concept is the scalar quantity. The intuition is pedagogical / rhetorical.

**Verdict:** CSG is a derived scalar. Principle #3 derivation check: FAILS as a distinct concept. Concept reduces to S + comparison operation.

### §3.2 Re-running the meta-pass semantic test with v2 accuracy

Claims to re-test:
- "Rare earths have high CSG." → "Rare earths have a large industrial-existential substitutability gap." OR "S_max(rare earths, industrial) is much higher than S_max(rare earths, existential)."
- "CSG analysis shows the Mars colony needs in-situ preservation." → "Industrial-existential substitutability gap analysis shows the Mars colony needs in-situ preservation." OR "Comparison of S_max(industrial) vs S_max(existential) for Mars resources shows in-situ preservation warranted."
- "High-CSG resources warrant Asymmetric Regret Principle application." → "Resources with large industrial-existential substitutability gaps warrant Asymmetric Regret Principle application."

All rewrites work. Prose is ~2-3× longer but semantically equivalent.

**Verdict:** all CSG usage rewrites without semantic loss. Option A's SC-A3 (prose compression earns name) is weak — compression exists but doesn't reach Ring-2 load-bearing threshold.

### §3.3 Asymmetric Regret Principle pairing check

V2 says ARP *"Applies across all uncertainty ranges for high-CSG resources."*

Does ARP need CSG named to function? Rewrite test:
- Original: "Apply ARP for high-CSG resources."
- Rewrite: "Apply ARP for resources with large industrial-existential substitutability gaps."

Rewrite works. ARP can reference the GAP CONCEPT without needing CSG as capitalized named concept.

**Contrast with Abundance Masking:** AM pairs with AIT such that AIT's definition REQUIRES AM to be named ("what does AIT detect?"). ARP doesn't require CSG named in the same way — ARP's pairing is TOPICAL (applies to high-gap cases) not STRUCTURAL (definition depends on CSG). Weaker pairing.

**Verdict:** ARP-CSG pairing doesn't reach Ring-2 load-bearing for CSG.
