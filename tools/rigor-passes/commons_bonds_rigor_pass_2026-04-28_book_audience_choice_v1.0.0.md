## §1. Executive summary

**RECOMMENDED:** Option **B + supplementary D** — write the book to the **academic-trade hybrid crossover audience** (Mazzucato / Raworth / Anderson / Sen / Acemoglu-Robinson / Stiglitz / Hickel register; Princeton / Yale / Harvard / MIT / U-Chicago press landing) as primary, with **policy-practitioner supplementation** in chapters where legal/policy adoption-travel is the success-criterion (notably Ch 9 *Pricing Honestly*; possibly Ch 7 + Ch 10 to lesser degrees).

**Decisive findings:**

1. **The framework's actual content profile makes B the only viable primary.** The book carries (a) a mathematical apparatus (CS = RCV − B; the RCV integrand; the Substitutability Function S; the Hotelling Identity); (b) lineage citations across welfare economics (Pigou, Stern, Nordhaus, Hotelling), intergenerational ethics (Rawls, Parfit, Brown Weiss), real options (Henry, Arrow & Fisher, Dixit-Pindyck), political economy (Harvey, Polanyi, Ostrom); (c) cross-political-tradition Option-C'-ratified accommodation; (d) personal narrative grounded in Hampton roots + NASA Langley + lived knowledge-work experience; (e) 18 case studies spanning McDowell, Norway, Libby, Indigenous land dispossession, Egypt, Mondragon, Deepwater Horizon, etc. **No other audience option carries this content profile without sacrificing one component for another.** Trade (A) sacrifices the math + lineage. Pure academic (C) sacrifices the narrative + cross-tradition. Public-intellectual (E) sacrifices the framework apparatus. Cross-tradition pure (F) sacrifices economics-defensibility. Only B threads all five.

2. **Author intuition confirmed by content-profile match.** *"Approachable for someone that doesn't have economic degrees, yet anyone that does also shouldn't turn their nose up"* IS the academic-trade-hybrid register's defining brief. The intuition tracks.

3. **Success criterion (severed-cost into legal/policy vocabulary) requires D supplementation.** Pure B yields scholarly-citation adoption (academic books cite the framework) but weaker regulatory-text adoption (regulators write *severed cost* into law). D-supplementation in chapters where instrument-design + regulatory-text-vocabulary are load-bearing closes that gap without changing the primary register.

4. **Chris's voice fits B (and D-supplemental).** The drafted chapters' prose register — Ch 1 personal-narrative + framework-introduction + emotional weight + technical apparatus woven in — already operates in B's register. Continuing in B is consistent with the book's existing draft state; switching to A or E would require substantial voice rewrite. C, D, F primary would require even larger rewrite.

5. **Realistic publisher landing supports B.** The book's pitch profile (cross-disciplinary apparatus + author-voice + cases) maps to Princeton / Yale / Harvard / MIT / U-Chicago presses — the classic academic-trade hybrid landing. Pure trade (A) is unlikely to acquire a book carrying this apparatus weight. Pure academic (C) press won't market the narrative.

6. **Literary-agent fit narrows under B.** Agents who represent academic-trade hybrid authors (e.g., Mazzucato, Raworth, Sen) are a specific subset — not generalist agents, not pure-academic-imprint reps. The pitch needs to read as "intellectually rigorous yet accessible to engaged generalist readers" in one sentence.

7. **M11 critic-survival STRONGEST under B.** The strongest critic against the framework will be a welfare-economics-trained reviewer asking "is this defensible?" — that critic reads B's register comfortably. The strongest critic against the *book* (commercial-viability angle) will be an acquisitions editor asking "who reads this?" — the answer "engaged generalists who follow Mazzucato/Raworth" is credible only under B.

**Net verdict:** **Option B + supplementary D.** Strengthens M3 + M6 + M10 + M11 + M12 + author-voice fit + success-criterion-reachability. Confirms author intuition with refinement (D supplementation for adoption-travel chapters).

## §3. Methodology

### §3.1 Audience options enumerated (§4)

Six audience options (A-F) representing the realistic register space the framework's content could land in. Each option specifies: register exemplars, publisher landing, voice characteristics, vocabulary register, narrative pacing, success-criterion fit.

### §3.2 Per-option evaluation (§5)

Each option scored against ten criteria:

1. **M3 (book-content fit)** — does the audience register accommodate the framework's actual mathematical apparatus + lineage citations + cross-tradition accommodation + personal narrative + case-study density?
2. **M6 (academic defensibility)** — does the register read as defensible specialization to a welfare-economics-trained academic reviewer?
3. **M8 (test-of-time durability)** — does the register survive the next 20-50 years; what shelf-life pattern does the chosen-audience exemplar set show?
4. **M10 (publishing realism)** — which actual publishers acquire books in this register at this content profile?
5. **M11 (critic survival)** — strongest critic against the framework + book; does that critic land?
6. **M12 (lineage strength)** — does the register accommodate the multi-discipline lineage citations the framework requires?
7. **Commercial viability** — realistic sales / reach trajectory; advance + royalty profile.
8. **Author-voice fit** — does Chris's existing voice + drafted chapter material already operate in this register, or would substantial voice-rework be required?
9. **Success-criterion-reachability** — does the register support adoption of *severed cost* / framework vocabulary into legal/policy/academic discourse?
10. **Literary-agent representability** — is there a clear class of agents who represent books in this register at this content profile?

Per-option verdict: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK / DISQUALIFIED.

### §3.3 Cross-option comparison matrix (§6)

Side-by-side scoring for direct comparison.

### §3.4 Hybrid options tested (§7)

The realistic answer may be a hybrid (e.g., B+D, B+E, B+F). Hybrids tested where they earn their complexity cost.

### §3.5 M11 critic-survival probes against recommended verdict (§8)

The strongest critic arguments tested against the recommended option.

### §3.6 Verdict + ratification (§9)

Net recommendation with explicit trade-off acknowledgments.

## §4. Audience options enumerated

**Register exemplars:** Naomi Klein *No Logo* + *This Changes Everything*; Richard Heinberg *The End of Growth*; Matt Stoller *Goliath*; Daniel Susskind *World Without Work*; David Graeber *Bullshit Jobs*; Anand Giridharadas *Winners Take All*.

**Voice characteristics:** Narrative-driven; high accessibility; minimal jargon density; case-studies-as-spine; authorial voice prominent; argument-by-illustration not argument-by-derivation.

**Vocabulary register:** Minimal framework coinage; existing English vocabulary preferred; technical terms heavily glossed in-line; reader doesn't need to track many named objects.

### §4.2 Option B — Academic-trade hybrid (author intuition)

**Vocabulary register:** Selective framework coinage with explicit lineage citations; non-specialist accessible; academic-defensible; named framework objects tracked through prose with first-introduction context.

**Narrative pacing:** Medium; chapter-as-substantive-argument; ~300-500 page count.

**Marketing positioning:** Cross-disciplinary economics / political-economy / ethics; academic-trade reviews (*Project Syndicate*, *Foreign Affairs*, *Boston Review*, *LRB*, *NYRB*); plus general-interest reviews.

## §8. M11 critic-survival probes against the recommended verdict

### §8.1 Critic — "Why not pure academic? You're losing rigor by writing for generalists."

**Response:** The framework's success criterion (severed-cost adoption into legal/policy + academic vocabulary) requires MORE than academic-only reach. Academic monographs cite sources but rarely produce vocabulary that escapes academia. Mazzucato's *entrepreneurial state* did escape; pure-academic-Mazzucato wouldn't have. The framework's success requires the same escape. Pure academic *kills* the success criterion; B + D *enables* it. The framework's rigor isn't reduced — the math, the lineage, the apparatus all stay in B; the prose is calibrated for accessibility without dropping defensibility.

### §8.2 Critic — "Why not pure trade? You're killing reach by writing for academics."

**Response:** Trade can't carry the framework's apparatus. Trying to write the framework in trade register means dropping the math (kills CS = RCV − B as the central claim), dropping the lineage (kills M6 + M12), or pushing both to appendix (most readers won't follow). The book's content profile EXCLUDES trade-primary. B can be acquired by academic-trade presses with crossover marketing; A would need a heavy rewrite that no longer fits the framework's arguments.

### §8.3 Critic — "Mazzucato sells well; she's not academic-trade-hybrid, she's accessible-academic. The distinction you're drawing doesn't exist."
