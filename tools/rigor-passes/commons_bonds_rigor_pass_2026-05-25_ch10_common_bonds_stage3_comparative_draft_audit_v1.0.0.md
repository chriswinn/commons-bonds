## 1. Methodology and verdict format

**Pass 3.3 (acceptance) verdict scale per [`audience-pressure-test-construction.md`](../drafting-templates/audience-pressure-test-construction.md) §1.2:**

- ✓✓✓ — Strong INCLUDE; this character would not just accept the chapter, they'd recommend it onward.
- ✓✓ — INCLUDE; the chapter lands cleanly for this character.
- ✓ — Weak INCLUDE; the chapter passes but doesn't excite.
- NEUTRAL — Neither INCLUDE nor EXCLUDE; the character would read it without strong reaction.
- ⚠ — Weak EXCLUDE-risk; minor concerns that could be addressed.
- ⚠⚠ — EXCLUDE-risk; substantive problems for this character.
- ⚠⚠⚠ — Strong EXCLUDE-risk; this character would reject the chapter.

**Dispositive characters for Ch 10:**
- **Indigenous-rights / Lakota-policy reader (Tier 1 #3)** is the gating audience for the Black Hills concession — the chapter's most consequential framework-modesty passage. A ⚠⚠ or ⚠⚠⚠ verdict here is ship-blocking and would route back to Stage 1c structural revision.
- **Cross-tier political reader (Tier 1 #4)** is the second dispositive test — the chapter's not-for-or-against-capitalism positioning must land as bridge-building, not triangulation, for both left-critic and conservative-friends adversarial reads.

**Pass 3.4 (robustness) verdict scale per [`audience-pressure-test-construction.md`](../drafting-templates/audience-pressure-test-construction.md) §3.4 thread-pull synthesis:**

- **ROBUST** — Adversarial threads don't compound into a structural problem; spot-fixes (if any) are register-level.
- **CONDITIONALLY ROBUST** — Adversarial threads identify specific spot-fixes; chapter ships after spot-fix application.
- **REQUIRES STRUCTURAL ENGAGEMENT** — Adversarial threads identify a structural pattern that needs more than spot-fixes; chapter returns to Stage 2 or a cross-chapter workstream.

**Character set adaptation.** Standard chapter-scope acceptance set per `audience-pressure-test-construction.md` §8 is 25-30 characters. Per the comparative-draft mode the author scope-ratified, this audit uses an 18+5 set tuned to Ch 10's specific synthesis-closing function — matching the $100 Barrel comparative pattern's character economy. Tier 1 character #1-4 are taken directly from the ALT-draft prompt's Ch-10-specific Tier 1 enumeration. Tier 2 + Tier 3 + adversarial sets are adapted from the canonical 30+10 set used at the Ch 1 REAUDIT v3 baseline.

**Methodology disclosure.** Per-character profiles are not re-described here; reference [`audience-pressure-test-construction.md`](../drafting-templates/audience-pressure-test-construction.md) §2-§3 + the cited canonical examples. Each character entry below opens with the Draft A reading, Draft B reading, and the comparative diff + per-draft verdict. ### 2.1 Trade-press editor / acquisitions editor (Tier 1 #1)

Acquiring editor at trade imprint (Random House / Crown / Norton / Knopf / FSG) reading the closing chapter as: (a) does the synthesis close the book? (b) does the prose register match the comp cluster (Mazzucato / Pistor / Christophers / Sandel / Desmond / Tooze)? (c) does the literary-memoir register hold across the synthesis pivots?

**Draft A reading.** Substantively rich. The model-as-wind-tunnel metaphor extends across two paragraphs and lands precisely ("If the model was wrong, the data coming out of the tunnel was wrong, and people a decade later would fly an aircraft that killed them"). The harbor opening is dense with watermen-register attention. The cross-cut figures section gives the McDowell miner a full dignifying paragraph ("Somewhere under the ground in southern West Virginia, a man roughly my age is pulling on boots... He has a brother who went to Charlotte for work and a cousin who stayed and took a job at the Dollar General"). The Sisyphus reflection is the richest in either draft ("The man is not happy because the work is complete. The man is happy because the work is his. He has chosen it"). These are all editorially valuable.

But the editor will notice the cumulative voice issues. Em-dash density across the chapter is high (~32 em-dashes in body prose vs Draft B's 0); this is editor-visible at the page-skim level. The line "I needed a place where the sentence of my life was short and the sentence of my days was long, and a boat on a slip is that" reads as overwrought-literary wordplay that lands wrong; an experienced editor would flag both halves. The four-in-a-row "I came because" structure across paragraph 16 reads as rhetorical mannerism. The wife passage ("my wife had died once on a hospital bed and I had eventually realized that even after she came back the hospital had taken something I would not get back either") is buried mid-sentence in a long compound construction; an editor reading for cumulative voice will mark this as a candidate for restructuring even without knowing the fact-check issue.

**Draft B reading.** Cleaner voice register throughout. Em-dash count zero in body prose. The wife passage has its own paragraph structure with substantial sentence-length variance: "My wife's heart stopped on an operating table. It was nearly twenty seconds. Two handfuls of seconds. She was never officially pronounced dead; the hospital was careful about the word." The breath that consequential claims need is preserved. The harbor opening matches Draft A's substance compactly. The cross-cut figures section is more compressed — the McDowell miner is a single-clause sketch ("A miner in McDowell County, sixty-three years old, who can no longer climb a flight of stairs without sitting down at the top") rather than Draft A's full dignifying paragraph. The Sisyphus reflection is shorter, lacking Draft A's "the man is happy because the work is his" reflection. Substantively less dense; voice substantially cleaner.

**Diff + verdicts.** Trade-press editor weights voice discipline at the closing-chapter scope substantially — closing chapters set the manuscript's final-page-impression. Draft B's voice baseline is what an acquiring editor expects to see from a closing chapter that doesn't need Phase C voice-cleanup. Draft A's substantive richness is real but compounds with editor-visible voice issues that would require Pass 3.2 + Pass 3.5 work before the chapter is publisher-ready. Both pass; Draft B has the cleaner publisher-readiness baseline.

- **Draft A:** ✓✓ (substantive richness; voice issues are Pass 3.2 spot-fix territory; wife-fact is Pass 3.1 critical-path)
- **Draft B:** ✓✓ (clean baseline; Pass 3.5 restoration recommended to import Draft A's richer substantive moments)

### 2.2 Memoir reader (Desmond *Evicted* / Berry / Macy *Dopesick* lineage; Tier 1 #2)

Trade-press literary-memoir reader who buys nonfiction in the Desmond / Berry / Macy / Krakauer cluster. Reads for plainspoken declarative prose + scene-anchor specificity + restraint at consequential moments + authorial restraint that lets the subjects breathe.

**Draft A reading.** The harbor opening has the right scene-anchor density. The McDowell miner paragraph is exactly the kind of imagined-detail dignifying move Desmond does in *Evicted*. The grandfather model-maker passage carries the canonical "work he was proud of" phrase. These are substantive memoir-register moves that land well.

But the memoir reader in this lineage reads carefully against three specific tics this draft has: (1) overwrought literary wordplay (the "sentence of my life / sentence of my days" line is precisely the kind of move Desmond / Berry / Macy do NOT make; their plainspoken declarative register cuts wordplay that doesn't earn its difficulty); (2) forced summary closers ("a boat on a slip is that" is the same kind of construction memoir editors in this lineage flag for cutting); (3) burying load-bearing personal claims in subordinate clauses (the wife passage as drafted does this — the consequential medical event is part of a long compound construction rather than given its own breath). The four-in-a-row "I came because" structure compounds the literary-essay register at the expense of the memoir register.

**Draft B reading.** Plainspoken declarative throughout. The wife passage has the breath consequential claims need: "My wife's heart stopped on an operating table. It was nearly twenty seconds. Two handfuls of seconds. She was never officially pronounced dead; the hospital was careful about the word. But the floor of every hour after that morning is a different floor than the floor of any hour before. She came back. She is here. The mark the morning made did not heal." This is closer to the Macy / Berry register than Draft A's compound-clause buried framing. No wordplay tics. No forced closers. The why-I-came section is structured as separate paragraphs per reason rather than as a rhetorical four-in-a-row.

**Diff + verdicts.** Draft B wins clearly on memoir-register discipline. The Desmond / Berry / Macy lineage specifically rewards the discipline Draft B has and specifically penalizes the tics Draft A has. The substantive memoir-material (NICU son in Ch 1 echo, grandfather, Hampton family detail) is present in both drafts; the register discipline differentiates.

- **Draft A:** ✓ (passes on substance; voice tics are flagged-pattern matches with this audience's specific dislikes)
- **Draft B:** ✓✓✓ (cleaner memoir register; lands closer to the comp cluster's prose discipline)

### 2.3 Indigenous-rights / Lakota-policy reader (Tier 1 #3 — **DISPOSITIVE**)

This is the gating audience for the chapter's most consequential framework-modesty passage. A reader inside the Indigenous-rights tradition (Coulthard / Simpson / Kimmerer / Deloria network; *Tipping Points* / *Yes!* magazine / *Briarpatch* / *Indian Country Today* reader) testing whether the Black Hills concession reads as: (a) appropriate settler-author marking-of-limits with substantive engagement → INCLUDE; (b) settler-author appropriation of indigenous suffering for framework-validation → EXCLUDE; (c) settler-author token-handling that gestures at humility without substantive engagement → EXCLUDE.

**Draft A reading.** The Lakota section runs five paragraphs. Para 1 frames as concession ("There is one more concession I owe the reader, and it sits differently from the others"). Para 2 walks canonical facts (1868 Fort Laramie + 1874 Custer + 1877 Act + 1980 *United States v. Sioux Nation* + ~$1.5B in escrow + refused for more than four decades). Para 3 names what the framework does NOT propose ("It does not have standing to propose it"). Para 4 makes the settler-author acknowledgment, names the broader indigenous-dispossession case, and lists the reading list (Kimmerer, Simpson, Coulthard, Dunbar-Ortiz, Deloria) WITHOUT tribal affiliations. Para 5 — the substantive move that distinguishes Draft A — directly engages the most-serious decolonial critique: that treating indigenous dispossession as one case among many flattens what is being engaged. The engagement is substantive: "The mechanism's name does not flatten the indigenous case; the absence of the mechanism's name is what permits the case to be treated as historical aberration rather than as the system's foundation."

The Indigenous-rights reader reads para 5 as evidence the author has engaged the obvious decolonial critique head-on rather than gesturing past it. The omission of tribal affiliations in the reading list is a small naming-discipline issue but does not undo the substantive engagement.

**Draft B reading.** The Lakota section runs six paragraphs. Para 1 frames as limit-naming ("There is one accounting the framework does not reach, and I want to set it down carefully before the chapter goes any further") — the structural-placement signal here is stronger than Draft A's "concession" frame because it announces that limit-naming gates everything that follows. Para 2 walks canonical facts (matching Draft A; uses comma-flanked "Ȟe Sápa" rather than em-dashes). Para 3 names the substitutability limit cleanly: "The reason is simple in a way the framework cannot reach. The land is not substitutable by money. Not at any amount. Not for any purpose. What was taken cannot be returned in dollars." Para 4 combines settler-author acknowledgment + the structural-not-moral-equivalence statement in one careful paragraph. Para 5 names the reading list WITH tribal affiliations: "Robin Wall Kimmerer (Citizen Potawatomi); Leanne Betasamosake Simpson (Michi Saagiig Nishnaabeg); Glen Coulthard (Yellowknives Dene); Roxanne Dunbar-Ortiz; and Vine Deloria Jr. (Standing Rock Sioux)" — and reframes the work as "a different shape of the question the book is asking, and a deeper one." Para 6 names WHY the limit is at the front of the closing chapter: "a framework that does not name its limits at the close has not done its work. The Black Hills account is the account that holds the framework honest."

The Indigenous-rights reader reads the tribal-affiliations naming as a discipline signal — small but real evidence the author has done specific work to honor naming conventions. The "different shape... deeper" framing reframes the reading list as substantively prior rather than supplementary. But Draft B is missing the substantive flattening-defense paragraph that Draft A has.

**Diff + verdicts.** This is the hardest single call in the audit. Draft A has the substantive flattening-defense engagement (load-bearing for the most serious decolonial critique). Draft B has the tribal-affiliations naming-discipline + the "before the chapter goes any further" structural-placement signal + the "different shape... deeper" reading-list framing. Both pass. Different audiences inside the Indigenous-rights tradition would prefer different drafts. A reader who reads for substantive critique-engagement leans Draft A; a reader who reads for naming-discipline + structural-placement-of-modesty leans Draft B.

The most defensible reading is that both drafts pass the dispositive test at ✓✓ or better — neither has the ship-blocking ⚠⚠ verdict that would route back to Stage 1c. Draft A is slightly stronger on substance; Draft B is slightly stronger on discipline. **The clean Pass 3.5 restoration move is to port Draft A's flattening-defense paragraph INTO Draft B's structure, producing a synthesis that has both the substantive engagement AND the disciplined framing.** This is the single most important Pass 3.5 carry-forward identified by the audit.

- **Draft A:** ✓✓✓ (substantive flattening-defense engagement; structural placement late; tribal-affiliations omitted from reading list)
- **Draft B:** ✓✓ (disciplined framing + tribal-affiliations + earlier structural placement; missing flattening-defense substantive engagement) → ✓✓✓ after Pass 3.5 restoration

### 2.4 Cross-tier political reader (left-critic + parents'-conservative-friends; Tier 1 #4)

Two-faced gating audience. The left-critic position: reader at *Jacobin* / *Dissent* / *n+1* / *New Left Review* who reads the framework as reformist-not-radical and tests whether the not-for-or-against-capitalism passage lands as serious engagement or as triangulation. The conservative-friends position: reader the author's parents' generation knows personally — *National Review* subscribers, retired federal civil servants, naval-architect professional class — who reads the not-for-or-against-capitalism passage for tribal-coding signals.

**Draft A reading.** The not-for-or-against-capitalism section runs four paragraphs. Para 1 names the objection from the left-critic position with care: "The objection, made carefully, is that this book does not go far enough... That the critique I am making is a reformist critique, compatible with a capitalism that will swallow it." Para 2 — the substantive move — engages the objection directly: "I want to say, plainly, that I have thought about this objection, and that the people who make it are not wrong about what they see. The framework can be captured. It can be softened." This is the most direct engagement with the left-critic position in either draft and is precisely the move *Jacobin* / *n+1* readers reward. Para 3 makes the structural-not-political claim with strong opening: "But I want to say something else too, and I want to say it without triangulating, without hedging, without retreat. This framework is not an argument for capitalism. It is not an argument against it." Para 4 closes with the dual-naming acknowledgment: "I understand this will not fully satisfy the most careful critics on the left. I understand that my parents' conservative friends, who have mostly agreed with the diagnosis so far, will find that this last clarification makes them nervous in a different direction."

The left-critic reads this as a serious engagement — the preamble paragraph specifically validates the objection before pivoting. The conservative-friends reader reads the explicit "not an argument for capitalism. It is not an argument against it" + "principle of honest dealing" register as the bridge-building move it intends to be. The "thought about this objection" preamble is the load-bearing substantive move; without it, the pivot would read as triangulation. With it, the pivot reads as informed bridge-building.

**Draft B reading.** The capitalism passage runs three paragraphs. Para 1 opens cleanly: "It is also worth saying, clearly and without hedging, what the framework is not. The book is not an argument for capitalism. The book is not an argument against capitalism." Para 2 develops the structural-not-political claim with the same cross-civilization examples Draft A has (state-owned mines + worker cooperatives + feudal lords + pharaohs + Qin emperors). Para 3 closes with the dual-naming acknowledgment: "I have friends on the political left who will read the book and find me too modest about the institutional reform the framework implies. I have parents whose conservative friends will read it and find me too generous to the institutional reform the framework implies. Both readings are misreadings of the same mistake."

Substantively similar to Draft A. The differential is the absence of the "I have thought about this objection" preamble paragraph. Without that preamble, the left-critic reads the opening as positional-declaration rather than as informed-engagement. Draft B's version is cleaner but reads as bridge-building without the substantive-engagement preamble that Draft A has.

**Diff + verdicts.** Draft A wins this test clearly. The preamble paragraph engaging the left-critic position is load-bearing for both faces of the cross-tier audience — it validates the left-critic objection before pivoting (which the left-critic specifically rewards) and it demonstrates the conservative-friends-friendly pivot is informed-engagement rather than triangulation (which the conservative-friends reader specifically reads for). **The clean Pass 3.5 restoration move is to port Draft A's preamble paragraph engaging the left-critic position INTO Draft B's capitalism section.** This is the second most important Pass 3.5 carry-forward identified by the audit.

- **Draft A:** ✓✓✓ (substantive engagement with left-critic preamble + the dual-naming acknowledgment; bridge-building reads as informed)
- **Draft B:** ✓✓ (clean structural-not-political claim + dual-naming acknowledgment; missing the substantive-engagement preamble) → ✓✓✓ after Pass 3.5 restoration
