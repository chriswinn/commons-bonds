## 2. Editorial brain map for Atlantic Ideas specifically

**What Atlantic Ideas editors want** (versus Noema or Boston Review):

- **Argument / analysis / commentary register.** Not reportage (Noema's spine) and not institutional-measurement (BR's spine). The Ideas section publishes thesis-driven essays that take a position and defend it with mechanism + cases + comparative-historical anchoring.
- **American examples lead; international examples accent.** The Atlantic's audience is primarily American; foreign-policy material lands when it sharpens the American argument (US 2008 vs. Sweden 1932 contrast — *yes*; pure-international scope without American stake — *no*).
- **Mechanism + arithmetic + cases.** The four-step Classify→Reserve→Invest→Reassess sequence is exactly the kind of mechanism the section publishes. The McDowell $4.50/$500–600 arithmetic + Alaska Permanent Fund + Norway GPFG + Mondragon / Vienna / Chattanooga entering-wedges are exactly the kind of cases.
- **Named comp-titles fluency without name-dropping.** Mazzucato / Christophers / Pistor / Susskind / Olson / Coase / Ostrom / Buchanan + Tullock / Darity + Mullen / Mian + Sufi — Atlantic Ideas audience reads these signals as serious-membership rather than as showing off. But three names per paragraph reads as showing off; the discipline is selective deployment, with each citation doing a specific argumentative job.
- **Hook a smart-but-busy reader will finish.** The Atlantic's reader is busy and surrounded by competing reads. ¶1 has to earn ¶2; ¶1 + ¶2 have to earn ¶3. The mechanism-and-arithmetic opening (McDowell coal $4.50/$500–600) is the natural hook.
- **Clear thesis sentence in the first or second paragraph.** Not buried at the essay's end as a slow reveal. Atlantic Ideas readers want to know what claim the essay is defending by the time they reach the first section break.
- **Length discipline.** Target ~3,500–4,500w. Hard ceiling ~5,000w per Atlantic's verified web-submission norm. Pieces over 5,000w are by assignment, not unsolicited.

**What hurts Atlantic Ideas submissions** (versus Noema or BR):

- **Pure thought-experiment** (Aeon's register, not Atlantic's).
- **Memoir-led opener** (Noema's register, not Atlantic's). Personal anchors are fine if they ground the argument; they cannot carry the argument.
- **Apparatus jargon** — "existential substitutability gap" repeated as a term-of-art; RCV-as-acronym; "cluster-γ"; "Foreclosure Bond" / "Restitution Bond" as named instruments. The Atlantic Ideas editorial brain is general-intellectual, not academic.
- **Manifesto register** — "We must..." / "It is time to..." / "The future demands..." The Atlantic Ideas section reads manifesto as a sign of weak argument. The framework's posture is mechanism-and-arithmetic; preserve it.
- **Partisan brief register** — "Republicans have failed to..." / "The Democratic agenda must..." The non-partisan framing discipline is load-bearing.
- **Defensive author register** — preemptive-rebuttal-of-objections at length. (The Nordic-objection section in Ch 9 is exactly this risk; treat with discipline if it makes the essay cut.)
- **Op-ed throat-clearing** — "Last week, the news cycle was once again dominated by..." Atlantic Ideas is not the op-ed page.

## 5. Voice register (ratified)

- **First person sparingly.** Atlantic Ideas tolerates first-person grounding (e.g., one or two memoiristic notes if they ground the argument) but is not memoir register. The essay's voice is third-person analytic with occasional first-person punctuation, NOT first-person-throughout (which is the Noema register).
- **Plain, unsentimental, mechanism-and-arithmetic.** Concrete numbers + named institutions + dated events. No nostalgia register; no manifesto register; no op-ed throat-clearing.
- **Short paragraphs OK but vary sentence length within them.** Counter the declarative-three-in-a-row LLM cadence by mixing 5-word and 25-word sentences.
- **No italicization for foreign or technical terms** unless quoting verbatim. Italicize remembered direct speech if the essay carries any (likely sparingly).
- **Avoid LLM tics**: rule of three; em-dash crutches; tidy parallels; *in short* / *ultimately* / *what this means is* connective tissue; *It changed me. It humbled me. It made...* cadence; *There are not many people like that anymore* nostalgia; "Show me the legislative pathway" hypothetical-objector apostrophe (Ch 9 carries one of these — at line 252 — and it lands in Ch 9 but it would read as a tic in a shorter essay; do not import).
- **Specific concrete details over abstract claims.** The handoff doc principle: "recliner, drill bits, fifty-eight countries." For this essay: $4.50/ton (1960), 7 sq mi tailings pond, 1956 Arizmendiarrieta founding, 1919 Red Vienna, 2001 fiscal rule.
- **Read each paragraph aloud before showing.**
- **Drop the apparatus.** No "existential substitutability gap" as a term-of-art (use "irreplaceability gap" or "substitution gap" in plain language, defined once and then used naturally); no "RCV" / "Residual Commons Value" (use "the unpriced cost" or "the cost the price misses"); no "Asymmetric Regret Rule" (use "cheap insurance against bigger-than-we-think downside"); no "Cost Severance" (use "the cost that gets handed off"); no "Foreclosure Bond / Restitution Bond / Accountability Bond" naming (translate to the structural shape: rent-capture at extraction + community-designation + life-cycle-matched disbursement). The framework's only load-bearing claim for this essay is: *honest pricing means the price reflects every cost the extraction produces — including the costs that currently get handed off to communities, ecosystems, and the future.*

### 7.2 Resource economics + classification

- **Japan post-Senkaku Islands rare-earth-export-restriction crisis:** **2010** crisis triggered Lynas Corporation Australian-Malaysian processing infrastructure funding.

- **Helium-3 / lunar regolith:** Helium-3 effectively absent from Earth's atmosphere; abundant in lunar regolith. Reservation argument hinges on fusion-application uncertainty.
- **Phosphorus:** workable concentrations at a few global locations; no substitute for biological role in agriculture.
- **Fossil aquifers (millennial-regeneration timescales):** Ogallala (US); North China Plain; Guarani (South America).

### 7.4 Political economy of resistance

- **Mancur Olson — *The Logic of Collective Action*:** **1965**. Concentrated-vs-diffuse interest dynamic; named-tradition for the resistance section.
- **US 2008 financial crisis architecture:**
  - **Approximately $11 trillion** US household wealth destroyed.
  - **Approximately 10 million foreclosures** over the following five years.
  - **Approximately $700 billion** direct authorization (Troubled Asset Relief Program — TARP).
  - Plus trillions of dollars in Federal Reserve emergency liquidity + guarantees + AIG / Citigroup bailouts.
  - **Almost all deployed capital went to bank-balance-sheet recapitalization**, not to household-debt restructuring.
- **Mian + Sufi — *House of Debt*:** **2014**. Welfare-cost calculation: household-debt restructuring at the scale the crisis required would have produced substantially more consumption recovery in 2009–2011 than the bank-rescue architecture did, with redistributive effects benefiting the bottom 40% of the income distribution by a factor of **three to four** relative to top-quintile households. **Verify exact Mian + Sufi welfare-calculation figures against Ch 9 line 122 and *House of Debt* primary text at Pass 3.1 fact-check** — Ch 9 carries "three to four" and "bottom forty percent" but these are at Pass-1-PROPOSED stability.
- **Sweden 1932–1937 / 1992 architecture:**
  - **1933:** Currency devaluation reduced real debt burdens.
  - **1933–1937:** Mortgage-modification programs wrote down nominal mortgage debt for distressed households; public-works employment programs absorbed unemployed labor.
  - **1938:** Saltsjöbaden Agreement institutionalized labor-employer wage-bargaining at consumption-maintaining level. Survived World War II, 1973 oil shock, 1992 Swedish banking crisis, into present.
  - **1992 Swedish banking crisis:** Several major Swedish banks collapsed; government recapitalization captured equity; recovered most deployed capital within five years; combined with countercyclical fiscal policy maintaining household consumption.
