### HIGH-1: Mars mission chronology out-of-order — Mars 3 placed AFTER Viking I/II

**Location:** [Chapter__7_OnOtherWorlds.md:19](manuscript/chapters/Chapter__7_OnOtherWorlds.md:19)

**Chapter text:**
> "Mars is used here because it is the off-world body human civilization has been actively trying to reach for half a century — Viking I and II, Mars 3, Pathfinder, Spirit and Opportunity, Phoenix, Curiosity, InSight, Perseverance, with sample return and crewed proposals on the active near-term agenda..."

**Canonical truth (NASA NSSDCA + Wikipedia mission pages, verified May 2026):**

| Mission | Agency | Launch | Arrival/landing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mars 3 | Soviet | 28 May 1971 | 2 Dec 1971 (first soft landing on Mars; 110 sec operations) |
| Viking 1 | NASA | 20 Aug 1975 | 20 July 1976 |
| Viking 2 | NASA | 9 Sept 1975 | 3 Sept 1976 |
| Mars Pathfinder | NASA | 4 Dec 1996 | 4 July 1997 |
| Spirit (MER-A) | NASA | 10 June 2003 | 4 Jan 2004 |
| Opportunity (MER-B) | NASA | 7 July 2003 | 25 Jan 2004 |
| Phoenix | NASA | 4 Aug 2007 | 25 May 2008 |
| Curiosity (MSL) | NASA | 26 Nov 2011 | 6 Aug 2012 |
| InSight | NASA | 5 May 2018 | 26 Nov 2018 |
| Perseverance | NASA | 30 July 2020 | 18 Feb 2021 |

**Mars 3 preceded Viking I and II by four years on the launch calendar, and by nearly five years on the arrival calendar.** It is widely cited as the first spacecraft to achieve a soft landing on Mars (Dec 2, 1971), four and a half years before Viking 1's 1976 touchdown. Placing Mars 3 between Viking I/II (1975/76) and Pathfinder (1996) is a chronological inversion.

**Why this is the HIGH-1 finding:** The chapter's framing is "Mars is the off-world body human civilization has been actively trying to reach for half a century" — the half-century framing IS anchored by Mars 3 as the FIRST of the enumeration; placing it second misrepresents the chronological start. A space-history-literate reader (planetary scientist; aerospace historian; SF-literate trade reader) will catch the error on first reading, and a publisher pre-publication fact-checker will flag. The chapter does not signal "in chronological order" explicitly, but a sequenced enumeration with "for half a century" framing reads as chronological by default. The error is small in word-count terms (one item out of place) but reads as authorial unfamiliarity with the mission history the chapter's argument leans on.

**Recommended spot-fix (single-edit):** Re-order Mars 3 to the head of the enumeration.

```
Mars is used here because it is the off-world body human civilization has
been actively trying to reach for half a century — Mars 3, Viking I and
II, Pathfinder, Spirit and Opportunity, Phoenix, Curiosity, InSight,
Perseverance, with sample return and crewed proposals on the active
near-term agenda...
```

**Severity rationale:** HIGH not CRITICAL because the underlying claim (these missions were attempts; the half-century framing is right) is correct — only the sequencing is wrong. HIGH not MEDIUM because the workstream handoff specifically called out "Mars mission chronology" as a per-chapter audit emphasis (handoff line 69 + per-chapter context block in framing), the chapter cites mission chronology as load-bearing evidence for the "half a century" claim, and the inversion is exactly the kind of drift a publisher fact-checker checks.

**Recommended spot-fix (paired across Ch 7 + bibliography §13):** Two options.

Author judgment between A/B/C; mirror in bibliography §13 if A or B is ratified.

### MEDIUM-3: Sun timeline "red-giant phase that fully engulfs the planet roughly 5 billion years out"

**Location:** [Chapter__7_OnOtherWorlds.md:197](manuscript/chapters/Chapter__7_OnOtherWorlds.md:197)

**Chapter text:**
> "The Sun's main-sequence luminosity is increasing on a timescale that mainstream solar-physics models project will make Earth uninhabitable for biological civilizations on the order of 500 million to 1 billion years from now — well before the red-giant phase that fully engulfs the planet roughly 5 billion years out."

**Canonical truth (standard solar-physics models per Schröder & Smith 2008 *MNRAS*; Sackmann/Boothroyd/Kraemer 1993; *Future of Earth* canonical Wikipedia treatment):**
- Sun reaches hydrogen-exhaustion / red-giant branch (RGB) entry at **~5 billion years** from now (this is when the Sun begins post-main-sequence evolution).
- Sun reaches maximum red-giant radius and engulfs Earth at approximately **~7.5 billion years** from now (mainstream models; Schröder & Smith 2008 project ~7.59 Gyr).
- "Earth uninhabitable for biological civilizations" — chapter's "500 million to 1 billion years" is on the conservative end of canonical projections (~600 Myr loss of complex multicellular life via runaway greenhouse; ~1.0-1.3 Gyr loss of human-habitable conditions per moist-greenhouse onset; ~2.1 Gyr loss of all water-based life). Defensible within range.

**Chapter's conflation:** "Red-giant phase that fully engulfs the planet roughly 5 billion years out" conflates two events that are ~2.5 billion years apart in canonical solar-evolution models:
1. **~5 Gyr from now:** Sun exits main sequence; red-giant phase begins.
2. **~7.5 Gyr from now:** Sun reaches engulfment radius (full envelope expansion past Earth's orbit).

The chapter's "red-giant phase that fully engulfs" reads the two events as simultaneous; they are not.

**Why this is the MEDIUM-3 finding:** An astrophysics-literate reader (e.g., a Tier-1 quantitative reader or any reader familiar with Schröder/Smith or *Future of Earth* canonical chronology) will catch the conflation. The chapter's deeper claim (off-world habitation is methodologically coherent given the long-term Sun timeline) does not depend on the precise engulfment date; only on Earth becoming uninhabitable well within Sun-main-sequence lifetime — which 500 Myr-1 Gyr cleanly satisfies. MEDIUM because the precision drift is small (two events conflated; both are far in the future) and easily fixed with a phrase swap. Not HIGH because the load-bearing claim is unaffected.

**Recommended spot-fix:** Two options.
- Option A (preserve "5 billion years" as the entry-to-red-giant marker): `well before the red-giant phase begins, roughly 5 billion years out` — drops "fully engulfs the planet."
- Option B (precision per Schröder/Smith): `well before the red-giant phase that fully engulfs the planet roughly 7.5 billion years out` — keeps the "engulfs" framing, corrects the number.
- Option C (collapse to entry-marker, single number): `well before the Sun exits its main sequence, roughly 5 billion years out` — sidesteps the engulfment specificity entirely.

Option A is most conservative (smallest prose change); Option C is most defensible (no implicit precision claim).

**Severity rationale:** MEDIUM. Astrophysics-literate readers will catch; the load-bearing methodological claim is unaffected; spot-fix is local.

### LOW-6: Asteroid-mining cost projections "five to seven orders of magnitude below current science-mission economics"
