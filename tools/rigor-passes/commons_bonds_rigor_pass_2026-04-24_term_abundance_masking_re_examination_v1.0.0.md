## §2. The question under test

### §2.1 v2 glossary definition

> *"The mechanism by which abundance makes costs invisible until scarcity forces them into view. Oxygen feels free because Earth's atmosphere is abundant. Ecological services feel free because ecosystems haven't collapsed yet. **Abundance Masking is the mechanism; Abundance Dimension is the dimension along which it operates. These are distinct entries: masking is a process, dimension is a category.**"*

**Key finding:** v2 explicitly calls Abundance Masking a "mechanism" and distinguishes it from Abundance Dimension (category). AIT exists to detect this mechanism.

### §2.2 Earlier demotion and why it was wrong

Earlier recommendation (meta-pass §13.2.c, commit `46600bc`): *"DEMOTE to in-prose description. Abundance Masking names the phenomenon that AIT detects — abundances mask costs by making them invisible to pricing. But the phenomenon is what AIT is FOR. Naming it as a separate term creates a term-per-mechanism bloat."*

**The Principle-#2 failure:** I recognized masking is what AIT detects but concluded this made masking REDUNDANT with AIT. That's backwards. It's like saying "we have 'severance mechanism detection,' so we don't need the term 'severance mechanism' itself." No — we need both the mechanism-name AND the detection-methodology-name.

Correct framing:
- Mechanism: Abundance Masking (what happens in the world that hides costs)
- Methodology: AIT (how we detect the hidden costs)

Both earn their place.

### §2.3 Options

- **A — Ring 2 (internal load-bearing mechanism-name)** — recommended.
- **B — Demote to lowercase prose description** (earlier recommendation).
- **C — Retire entirely** (no framework-specific naming).
- **D — Merge into AIT entry** (describe masking as part of AIT definition).
- **E — Rename** (e.g., "Scarcity Illusion," "Cost Invisibility").

### §2.4 Sub-claims

**Option A:** distinct mechanism needs a name; pairs with AIT; prevents "what does AIT detect?" ambiguity.

**Option B / C / D:** break the mechanism-methodology pairing; lose framework-internal precision.

**Option E:** rename candidates weaker than current; "Abundance Masking" is concise + accurate.

### §2.5 Falsifiers

**Option A falsified if:**
- The mechanism is truly not distinct (it's just AIT-result restated).
- Chapter-usage patterns show the phrase has no operational role.
- A rename candidate materially improves precision.

**Verdict below (§5):** Option A survives.

## §3. Pairing with AIT (the decisive test)

### §3.1 Framework pair structure

Consider parallel pairs in the framework:

| Mechanism | Methodology/Detection | Both earn names |
|---|---|---|
| Cost Severance | (no dedicated detection methodology; implicit via AIT + formula) | One side named |
| Abundance Masking | Abundance Inversion Test (AIT) | **Both earn names** |
| Value Extraction | (economic-accounting detection) | One side named |

The Abundance Masking + AIT pair is distinctive because the detection methodology is itself a framework contribution. The pair deserves symmetric naming.

### §3.2 "What does AIT detect?" test

Ask an adversarial reader: *"What does the Abundance Inversion Test test for?"*

**Without Abundance Masking term:** "It tests whether a candidate cost is scarcity-grounded." But what makes a cost scarcity-grounded vs not? Because SOMETHING makes non-scarcity-grounded costs LOOK like costs of extraction when they're actually costs of the activity (not the scarcity). What's that something? We don't have a name for it if Abundance Masking is retired.

**With Abundance Masking term:** "It tests whether a candidate cost survives abundance inversion — i.e., whether the cost is genuinely hidden by Abundance Masking or is intrinsic to the activity regardless of scarcity."

The second version is cleaner because it names the phenomenon AIT is designed to detect.

### §3.3 Verdict on §3

Retaining Abundance Masking as Ring-2 mechanism-name preserves AIT's explanatory clarity. Demoting breaks the explanation.
