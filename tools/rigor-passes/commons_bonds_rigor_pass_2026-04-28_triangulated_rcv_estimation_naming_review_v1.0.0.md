## §1. Executive summary

**Summary of findings:**

1. **The framework currently has 3 names for the same thing:**
   - **"Triangulated RCV Estimation"** (formal terms_index entry name)
   - **"Three Ways of Counting"** (pedagogical alias; chapter 6 title)
   - **"the triangulated three-method range"** (inline result description used in Ch 6 prose)

2. **Author's intuition** that this is over-complex is correct under Working Principle #1 (parsimony) + vocabulary strategy v1.0.1 §3 four-move tier framework. The framework has multiple Ring-1 + Ring-2 ratified terms whose names are simultaneously academically-citable AND reader-accessible (Cost Severance; Severed Cost; Accountability Bond; Commons Inversion Test; Asymmetric Regret Rule). "Three Ways of Counting" can be the same kind of term — pedagogical-and-formal in one name.

3. **"Triangulated RCV Estimation" doesn't pass the four-move ladder strict-fit threshold:**
   - **Tier A check (verbatim academic):** Triangulation is a standard scientific-methodology term (Webb et al. 1966 *Unobtrusive Measures*; Denzin 1978 *The Research Act*; Patton 1999 *Health Services Research*); the framework adopts the standard concept. Tier A would be "triangulation of RCV estimates from three independent methods" — but "Triangulated RCV Estimation" as a proper-noun framework-coinage is heavier than what Tier A requires.
   - **Tier B check (academic + specialization):** The framework specializes triangulation to RCV estimation; light specialization-footnote suffices. But the framework already specifies the three methods (Method 1 Replacement Cost; Method 2 Revealed Preference; Method 3 Scarcity-Adjusted Option Value) as named items. Tier B would be the methods themselves carrying lineage citations; the framework's contribution is the SPECIFIC THREE METHODS chosen + convergence/divergence diagnostic, not the triangulation discipline itself.
   - **Tier C check (descriptive prose):** "the triangulated three-method range" + "the framework's three methods of estimation" + "Three Ways of Counting" — all DO serve as descriptive prose for the result + methodology + chapter. Tier C is in scope.
   - **Tier D check (coinage justified):** Coinage is JUSTIFIED if the framework needs a distinction no existing vocabulary supports. The framework's contribution is the SPECIFIC THREE METHODS + convergence/divergence diagnostic — not the triangulation methodology itself. So Tier D coinage would have to be on the THREE-METHOD-COMBINATION-AS-A-WHOLE, which is what "Three Ways of Counting" already accomplishes pedagogically.

4. **Per the four-move ladder decision rule** (default to lowest tier serving B-register reader): the methodology lands at Tier C with **"Three Ways of Counting" as the framework's accessible-and-academically-defensible name** for the three-method combination; "the triangulated three-method range" as inline result description; the three Methods themselves at Tier B (academic lineage cited per terms_index entry §4.5).

5. **Suffix-convention coherence:** "Triangulated RCV Estimation" uses the `-Estimation` suffix, which is one-off in the framework's suffix vocabulary (parallel to Hotelling Identity's `-Identity` and Externality Tail's `-Tail`). One-offs are allowed when they pattern-match academic-source conventions per vocabulary strategy v1.0.1 §6. "Estimation" suffix doesn't strongly pattern-match a specific academic convention (econometric-estimation literature uses "estimator" / "estimation method" / "estimation procedure" interchangeably). Dropping the formal-name removes the one-off without loss.

6. **"Three Ways of Counting" as the primary methodology name passes the four-move tier ladder:**
   - Tier C/D borderline. As a colloquial phrase it's Tier C-ish; as a chapter title + framework methodology handle it earns Tier D-ish status.
   - Pattern-matches accessible academic-trade titles: Mazzucato's *The Value of Everything*; Raworth's *Doughnut Economics*; Sen's *The Idea of Justice*; Anderson's *Private Government*. These are simultaneously approachable AND academically-defensible.
   - Suffix: no role-bearing-suffix, but pattern-matches accessible-formal-handle convention per vocabulary strategy v1.0.1 §6.4 RECONSIDER allowance for one-offs.

**Net verdict:** Drop "Triangulated RCV Estimation" as a separate Ring-2 term; promote "Three Ways of Counting" as the framework's primary methodology name; preserve "the triangulated three-method range" as the inline result description. Vocabulary footprint reduces by one term; pedagogical accessibility preserved; academic defensibility preserved (the three methods carry their own academic lineage).

## §4. Per-name tier evaluation

### §4.1 "Triangulated RCV Estimation" (current formal name)

**Four-move tier verdict:** Tier C-or-D-borderline; recommend DROP.

**Tier A check:** Triangulation is established academic methodology (Webb et al. 1966; Denzin 1978; Patton 1999). Tier A would adopt "triangulation" verbatim. The framework's specialization — three SPECIFIC methods chosen + convergence/divergence diagnostic — pushes toward Tier B.

**Tier B check:** Tier B would be "triangulation of RCV estimates" with academic-lineage footnote citing Webb / Denzin / Patton + framework's specialization on the specific three methods. **This works** — the methodology IS triangulation specialized to RCV; light footnote suffices.

**Tier C check:** Tier C would be descriptive prose: "the framework's three methods of estimating RCV" + result described as "the triangulated three-method range." **Also works** — and is the lighter tier.

**Tier D check:** Coinage justified only if no existing vocabulary supports the distinction. Triangulation IS established vocabulary; the framework's contribution is the specific methods. NOT Tier D justified.

**Verdict under decision rule (default to lowest tier):** Tier C suffices. "Triangulated RCV Estimation" as a proper-noun framework-coinage is over-elevated.

**Audience-tier evaluation:**
- **B-register reader (Mazzucato/Raworth profile):** "Triangulated RCV Estimation" reads as econometric-jargon — feels heavier than the methodology actually is. The reader can decode "three methods of estimating RCV → triangulated range" without specialist gloss. The proper-noun framework-coinage adds vocabulary footprint without adding decode-clarity.
- **D-modulation reader (regulatory-vocabulary-fluent):** Triangulation is recognizable; the formal name is acceptable but not load-bearing for adoption-travel.

**Suffix-convention check:** `-Estimation` is one-off. Doesn't pattern-match a specific academic convention strongly. Drop without loss.

**M11 probe:** *"You're claiming a methodology that's just standard triangulation."* — yes, the framework adopts standard triangulation methodology; the framework's contribution is the specific three methods chosen, not the triangulation discipline. Dropping the proper-noun coinage REMOVES this critique surface.

### §4.2 "Three Ways of Counting" (current pedagogical alias; chapter title)

**Four-move tier verdict:** Tier D — accessible-and-formal coinage; the framework's primary methodology name.

**Tier A check:** No academic-literature term carries this exact meaning. Not Tier A.

**Tier B check:** "Three methods of valuation" exists in environmental economics literature but doesn't capture the specific Replacement Cost / Revealed Preference / Scarcity-Adjusted Option Value combination + the convergence/divergence diagnostic. Not Tier B.

**Tier C check:** Tier C would be "the framework's three methods of counting RCV" — descriptive prose. But "Three Ways of Counting" is already operating as a chapter title + cross-chapter methodology handle. Promoting to Tier D earns its keep.

**Tier D check:** Justified. The framework's specific three-method combination + convergence/divergence diagnostic + reporting discipline is a coined methodology. "Three Ways of Counting" is the coined name; the chapter is named after it; the methodology handle travels.

**Audience-tier evaluation:**
- **B-register reader:** "Three Ways of Counting" reads as accessible — explicit signal that there are three methods + the verb "counting" is concrete + pedagogical. Pattern-matches "Doughnut Economics" / "The Value of Everything" / "The Idea of Justice" — accessible-academic-trade titles.
- **D-modulation reader:** Adoption-travel adequate — regulatory text could reference "the framework's three ways of counting" without translation.

**Suffix-convention check:** No role-bearing-suffix; one-off. Pattern-matches accessible-formal-handle convention per vocabulary strategy v1.0.1 §6.4 RECONSIDER allowance.

**M11 probe:** *"'Three Ways of Counting' is too colloquial for academic citation."* — can be cited as: "the framework's *Three Ways of Counting* methodology (Method 1 Replacement Cost / Method 2 Revealed Preference / Method 3 Scarcity-Adjusted Option Value)." The methods themselves carry full academic lineage. The methodology-handle is approachable but the method citations are academic. M11 SURVIVES.

### §4.3 "the triangulated three-method range" (current inline result description)

**Four-move tier verdict:** Tier C descriptive prose. KEEP as inline phrasing.

**Tier A/B/C/D analysis:** This is descriptive prose, not a coined term. "Triangulated" is a descriptor; "three-method range" describes the form. No proper-noun status; no glossary entry; no terms_index promotion needed. Stays in chapter prose as the natural way to describe the methodology's output.

**Audience-tier:** Strong on both B and D. Reads naturally as descriptive English.

**Verdict:** KEEP as inline phrasing.

## §6. M11 critic-survival probes

### §6.1 Critic — "'Three Ways of Counting' is too colloquial; you're sacrificing academic credibility for accessibility."

**Response:** "Three Ways of Counting" is the chapter title; promoting to formal methodology name pattern-matches academic-trade-hybrid convention (Mazzucato *The Value of Everything*; Raworth *Doughnut Economics*; Anderson *Private Government*). The methods themselves carry full academic lineage citations; an academic citation reads "the framework's *Three Ways of Counting* methodology (Replacement Cost; Revealed Preference; Scarcity-Adjusted Option Value)." The colloquial accessibility is the brief, not a sacrifice. M11 SURVIVES under audience-tier B (which the rigor pass ratified 2026-04-28 as the book's primary audience).

### §6.2 Critic — "Triangulation is a real methodology with a real academic name. Why would you drop the formal name?"

**Response:** Triangulation IS a real academic methodology — Webb et al. 1966 *Unobtrusive Measures*; Denzin 1978 *The Research Act*; Patton 1999. The framework adopts standard triangulation practice. The framework's contribution is the SPECIFIC THREE METHODS chosen for RCV estimation, not the triangulation discipline. Dropping "Triangulated RCV Estimation" as a coined proper-noun does NOT lose the triangulation-methodology lineage — it preserves it as descriptive prose ("the methodology triangulates three independent methods"). The framework's contribution is more honestly framed as method-choice than as triangulation-coinage. M11 SURVIVES under M12 honesty discipline.

### §6.3 Critic — "By dropping a Ring-2 term you're shrinking the framework's vocabulary."

**Response:** Per Working Principle #1 Corollary B (RATIFIED 2026-04-24; CSG retirement origination), usage frequency is not a rigor argument for retention. The framework's vocabulary should be no larger than necessary to do the work the framework needs to do. "Triangulated RCV Estimation" + "Three Ways of Counting" doing the same work is exactly the proliferation that Working Principle #1 disciplines. Dropping the redundant term is parsimony in action. The framework retains "Three Ways of Counting" as the primary handle; the methodology is preserved; vocabulary footprint reduces. M11 SURVIVES.

### §6.4 Critic — "What about Tech Appendix references that already use 'Triangulated RCV Estimation'?"

**Response:** Phase 3 Tech Appendix v2.0.0 rebuild derives cleanly from terms_index per S1 schema (architecture rigor pass §10 implementation plan). Renaming the terms_index entry → Tech Appendix v2.0.0 inherits the new name automatically. Current Tech Appendix v1.0.0 references can be swept now or carried until v2.0.0 lands. M11 SURVIVES.

### §6.5 Critic — "The chapter title 'Three Ways of Counting' is already the methodology name in everything but formal records — you're just ratifying the existing usage."

**Response:** Yes. That's the recommendation's strength: the framework already operates this way; the formal-name `Triangulated RCV Estimation` was the redundant overlay; ratifying the existing pedagogical-and-formal usage is the parsimony move. M11 SURVIVES.
