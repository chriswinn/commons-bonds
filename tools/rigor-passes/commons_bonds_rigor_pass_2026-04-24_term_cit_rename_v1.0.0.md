### §3.3 Concept-level audit: the "invert the commons state to test cost" move

Adjacent concepts in literature:
- **Counterfactual reasoning** (broad philosophy-of-action + decision theory). CIT is a specific counterfactual ("imagine commons absent/unconsumed"). Framework-specific application.
- **Substitutability testing** (resource economics). Adjacent — substitutability tests ask "can something else fill the role?"; CIT asks "what's revealed if the commons isn't there?" Different question.
- **Ostrom's boundary design principle** (commons governance). Ostrom asks "what defines the commons boundary?"; CIT asks "what costs depend on the commons?" Different question.
- **Abundance Inversion (Alletto 2024 SSRN)** — different domain (AI + public-sector efficiency); not applicable.

**Concept is framework-specific** — no established test-methodology uses this specific operational move.

## §8. Term Provenance Record (CIT)

### Commons Inversion Test (CIT)

**Working definition:** Framework-specific Ring-1 methodology for admitting candidate costs to RCV. Operates on commons categories: imagine the commons inverted (either absent or unconsumed); if the candidate cost becomes visible as commons-dependent, admit to Cᵢ via Four Gates. Two sub-forms: **Commons-Absence Inversion** (imagine commons not existing; paradigm: oxygen in space; habitability destroyed) + **Commons-Consumption Inversion** (imagine commons not consumed; paradigm: commute-time returned; forest uncut). Specialized extension of commons-governance methodology (Ostrom lineage) to extraction-contexts.

**Status:** `CURRENT` at Ring 1 (recommended by this pass; pending author ratification). Supersedes AIT.

**Term-spec version:** v1.0 (first sanctioned spec).

**M12 classification:** Original specialization within counterfactual-reasoning methodological family. No prior-art collision on phrase or concept; acronym collisions in unrelated domains only (spell-out-at-first-use convention mitigates).
