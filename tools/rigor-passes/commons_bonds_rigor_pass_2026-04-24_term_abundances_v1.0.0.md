## §1. Executive summary

**RECOMMENDED: Option A — CONFIRM Ring 2 (scaffolding construct) + RATIFY "Abundance" as the canonical label (keep current; retire "Dimension" as primary construct name).** The 10-element scaffolding is load-bearing framework architecture under the Path F reframing already ratified (variables AIT has discovered, not ontological taxonomy). "Abundance" (as label) wins over "Dimension" on three grounds: (a) M5 dinner-table clarity — "abundance" describes what's actually true about the scarcity-grounding condition; (b) travel-durability — "abundance" echoes widely-understood vocabulary while "dimension" is technical-ambiguous; (c) Path-F semantic alignment — "the 10 abundance variables" reads as empirically-grounded; "the 10 dimensions" suggests ontological fixity Path F already rejected.

**Key findings summarized:**

1. **The 10-element scaffolding IS load-bearing for the framework.** Removing it would leave AIT without a domain over which to operate. Dropping to "costs span many categories, examined case-by-case" would dissolve the framework's pedagogical + convergence-demonstration apparatus. Retain at Ring 2.

2. **"Abundance" wins over "Dimension" as label — terminology decision ratified in this pass.**
   - "Dimension" suggests orthogonality, canonical ontology, mathematical fixity. Path F explicitly withdraws that claim.
   - "Abundance" describes the substantive condition (a state of abundance that abundance-inversion operates on). Semantic alignment to AIT.
   - Keep "Abundance" everywhere as primary construct name. "Dimension" retained only as lowercase prose fallback where semantic context demands it.

3. **M12 classification: Independent framework-specific scaffolding.** "Abundance" in economics usually means "plenty" or "post-scarcity rhetoric" (Desai & Lemley 2022; abundance-economics literature). Framework's specific use — "the variable domain over which AIT operates to admit scarcity-grounded costs to RCV" — is framework-distinctive. Tech Appendix footnote recommended.

4. **Scope-creep risk is HIGH and must be actively managed (per Open Insight #13).** Each of the 10 abundances has rich-profile content from v1.2.0 naming-cohort rigor. Pushing all that depth into Book 1 would bloat toward textbook-mode. Recommendation: Ch 6 introduces the 10-count scaffolding + 1-2 canonical abundance applications; Tech Appendix carries the fuller treatment; Book 2 territory is per-abundance industry-specific implementation.

5. **Epistemic-honesty reinforcement (Path F already ratified but worth restating):** the 10 are "what we found across these 18 cases" — not "canonical," not "complete," not "ontological." Future applications of AIT to new contexts could surface additional abundance categories or refine existing ones. Book should present with this humility.

**Options tested:**
- **A — CONFIRM Ring 2 + ratify "Abundance" label.** **RECOMMENDED.**
- **B — CONFIRM Ring 2 + rename all to "Variable" (per Path F literal reading).** REJECTED — "variable" is too generic, loses Path-F-compatible substantive label.
- **C — CONFIRM Ring 2 + keep both "Abundance" and "Dimension" contextually.** REJECTED — inconsistent vocabulary creates reader friction.
- **D — DEMOTE scaffolding to prose (no named construct; just list 10 categories as needed).** REJECTED — breaks AIT's domain, breaks convergence-table pedagogy, breaks Ch 6 structure.

## §3. Principle #3 / structural check

### §3.1 Is the 10-element construct load-bearing?

**Test:** suppose we remove the construct and talk about costs directly without the scaffolding.

Consequences:
- AIT has no domain over which to operate ("abundance inversion test" — inversion of WHAT?). AIT becomes "inversion of whatever you're looking at," which is operationally weaker.
- Ch 6 convergence-table pedagogy (4 approaches → IPG ≫ 1) depends on the cases being unified by the 10-category scaffolding. Without scaffolding, convergence claim weakens.
- Case-study audits (chapter audit v1.0.6 + case-study audit v1.0.6) use per-abundance activation as a methodology. Loss of scaffolding unsettles this.

**Verdict:** scaffolding is load-bearing. Option D rejected.

### §3.2 Is the 10-element specification load-bearing, or just the scaffolding concept?

Path F already answers this: the SCAFFOLDING is load-bearing (framework needs a variable domain for AIT); the SPECIFIC 10 are "what's been found across 18 cases," not "canonical." Adding an 11th through AIT application is framework-legitimate.

**Verdict:** scaffolding concept Ring-2; specific 10 are empirically-grounded; framework is generative not closed.

## §4. "Abundance" vs "Dimension" terminology decision

### §4.1 Current usage

- Doc title: "Abundance Dimensions" (compound).
- Per-element usage mixed: "Habitability dimension," "Habitability abundance," "Habitability variable" all appear.
- Glossary v2 has "Abundance Dimension" as standalone entry (distinct from Abundance Masking).
- Meta-pass flagged as T1 terminology decision ("Dimension vs Abundance").

### §4.2 "Abundance" case

- Semantic alignment with AIT (inversion test operates on abundance states).
- M5 dinner-table: "abundance" > "dimension" for non-expert.
- Travel-durability: "abundance" echoes widely-understood vocabulary; "dimension" is technical-ambiguous.
- Path F alignment: "the 10 abundance variables" reads as empirically-grounded; "the 10 dimensions" suggests ontological fixity.
- Each abundance is a KIND of abundance-condition (Habitability is an abundance-of-habitability; Spatial is an abundance-of-spatial-conditions). Works grammatically.

### §4.3 "Dimension" case

- Mathematical register (dimensions in vector space).
- Allows orthogonality claims (dimensions are independent).
- But orthogonality is NOT a framework claim under Path F — Path F explicitly rejects dimension-as-canonical-ontology.
- Register is heavier than the framework's accounting-bookkeeping voice warrants.

### §4.4 "Variable" case (Option B)

- Path F literal terminology.
- Too generic — "variable" is any quantity that varies; loses substantive label.
- Lacks adoption-durability vs "abundance."

### §4.5 Verdict

**"Abundance" ratified as primary construct name.** "Dimension" retired as primary construct name; retained only as lowercase prose fallback where semantic context demands it (rare — mostly reader-orientation sentences like "these 10 dimensions of the problem" in introductory passages). "Abundance Dimension" compound retired (glossary v3 → single "Abundance" entry, not "Abundance Dimension").

This terminology decision is ratified in this pass (resolves meta-pass §12.3 T1).

## §5. M12 — Intellectual honesty / Prior-art audit

### §5.1 Exact-phrase search

"The 10 abundances" as specific enumeration — framework-specific; no collision.

### §5.2 Concept-level search

- **Abundance economics literature** (Desai & Lemley 2022 *Scarcity, Regulation, and the Abundance Society*; abundance-economics broader tradition) — uses "abundance" in rhetorical-policy sense about scarcity-abundance-regulation relationships. Adjacent but different use — framework's "abundance" means "scarcity-grounded-cost variable category," not "post-scarcity political-economy claim."
- **Rawls primary-goods framework**, Sen capabilities, Nussbaum central capabilities — enumerate categories of human good; adjacent precedent for "framework lists N categories."
- **Ostrom design principles** — Ostrom enumerated 8 design principles for commons governance; adjacent precedent for named-enumeration in commons-governance literature.
- **Maslow's hierarchy / Max-Neef human needs** — psychology-adjacent enumerations.

**Finding:** the PATTERN (framework enumerating N categories of human-relevant variable) is established across multiple adjacent traditions. The SPECIFIC 10 and the specific framing (variable categories AIT has discovered) is framework-specific. No named-enumeration collision.

**Independent specialization** with established-enumeration-pattern lineage. Framework's contribution is (a) the specific 10 + their AIT-convergent discovery; (b) the "abundance" framing that semantic-aligns with AIT; (c) the generative architecture (not a closed set).

## §6. Scope-creep monitoring (Open Insight #13)

This is the heaviest section of the pass. Abundances is a prime scope-creep trigger because each of 10 categories has rich-profile v1.2.0 content that could push Book 1 toward textbook-mode.

### §6.1 What's in Book 1 scope

- Ch 6 introduces the 10-count scaffolding + how AIT operates on it + 1-2 canonical abundance applications (e.g., Habitability for McDowell; Spatial for Chesapeake; Temporal/intergenerational for Social Security).
- Tech Appendix carries the fuller methodology (what each abundance names; how AIT is applied; per-case activation records from chapter audit v1.0.6 + case-study audit v1.0.6).
- Per-abundance rich profiles (v1.2.0 content) stay in the methodology doc (`core/dimensions/commons_bonds_abundance_dimensions_v1_3_0.md`), NOT migrated into Book 1 chapter prose.
- One-paragraph signposts per abundance in Ch 6 sufficient.

### §6.2 What's OUT of Book 1 scope (Book 2 territory)

- Per-abundance industry-specific implementation (e.g., "how to price Habitability for coal mining in Appalachia").
- Comprehensive per-abundance case library (e.g., 10+ cases per abundance).
- Named industries with lobbying histories that suppress specific abundances.
- Per-abundance policy instrument design.

### §6.3 Trigger indicators

- Chapter drafting tries to cover all 10 abundances at equal depth (→ compress; prefer 1-2 canonical applications in main text + Tech Appendix for rest).
- A passage names specific industries with lobbying histories per abundance (→ Book 2).
- An abundance's rich profile migrates wholesale into chapter prose (→ compress; link to Tech Appendix).

### §6.4 Mitigation

- Ch 6 structure: "here are the 10 abundance categories; here's AIT applied to Habitability in McDowell at worked-example depth; the other 9 follow the same methodology and are catalogued in Tech Appendix."
- Tech Appendix absorbs the depth.
- Per-abundance rich profiles stay in the methodology doc (not chapter prose).
