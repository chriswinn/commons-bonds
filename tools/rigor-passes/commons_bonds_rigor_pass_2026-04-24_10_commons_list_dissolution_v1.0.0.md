## §3. Axis 1 — Does dissolution strengthen or hurt the book?

### §3.1 Argumentative structure impact

Book 1's argumentative structure depends on:
1. Teaching CIT as methodology.
2. Showing CIT applied to 18+ cases.
3. Claiming the methodology generalizes (universality).

**Under Option B (dissolution):**
- Methodology stands central (teaching CIT primary).
- Cases demonstrate methodology; commons emerge naturally.
- Universality claim rests purely on case-diversity + methodology-applicability.

**Under Option C (middle path):**
- Methodology stands central.
- Cases demonstrate methodology; 10 as reference shows what's been found.
- Universality claim rests on methodology + case-diversity + "here's 10 commons categories we've surfaced so far."

**Finding:** both support the argumentative structure. Option C gives readers MORE (methodology + cases + reference) for trivial structural cost.

**Finding:** Option C serves more reader modes without compromising the other modes.

### §3.3 Strengthen or hurt?

- **Option B (full dissolution):** hurts pedagogy + reader-adoption marginally; strengthens academic-rigor marginally. Net slight hurt.
- **Option C (middle path):** strengthens on all axes without material loss. Net strengthen.
- **Option A (keep scaffolding):** current state; scope-creep vulnerability.

**Verdict on Axis 1:** Option C strengthens.

## §4. Axis 2 — Does dissolution hold up to academic rigor?

### §4.1 Completeness-claim attack under each option

**Option A (keep scaffolding):**
- Attack: *"The framework names 10 but admits they're not canonical. What's load-bearing — the 10 or the methodology?"* Under Path F framing, defensible but requires careful framing.
- Risk: scaffolding-as-load-bearing language can slip into completeness-implication.

**Option B (dissolution):**
- Attack: *"How do I apply this to a new case? Where do I start?"* Response: CIT methodology; case-emergent commons. Defensible.
- No completeness claim to defend.
- Trade-off: no per-commons rigor work visible — could read as "method without worked-out application."

**Option C (middle path):**
- Attack: *"Why list 10 if not complete?"* Response: *"These are the 10 we've identified across 18 cases. Reference-illustration of the methodology's application record. Apply CIT to your case — you may find others."*
- Clean academic answer. Reference-status doesn't claim completeness.

### §4.2 Intellectual-history adjacency audit (M6 academic)

Adjacent frameworks with enumerated categories:
- **Ostrom — 8 design principles.** Enumerated; widely accepted; never claimed complete.
- **Sen/Nussbaum — 10 capabilities (Nussbaum).** Enumerated; contested but influential.
- **Maslow — 5-level hierarchy.** Enumerated; widely taught; empirically contested.
- **Rawls — primary goods list.** Enumerated; debated.
- **Polanyi — 3 fictitious commodities.** Enumerated; canonical.

**Finding:** enumerated frameworks are COMMON in adjacent traditions. Academic rigor doesn't demand dissolution; it demands honest framing of enumeration status.

**Option B's "dissolve because enumeration is rigor risk" is OVERCORRECTING.** Academic rigor accepts enumerations that are honestly framed (reference, not canonical). Option C achieves this without dissolution.

### §4.3 Ratified Principle #1 Corollary B compliance

*"Usage frequency alone is not a rigor argument for retention."* Applies to whether retention serves ADOPTION rather than RIGOR. Under Option C, the 10-as-reference serves pedagogy + adoption without making rigor claims. Compliant.

### §4.4 Verdict on Axis 2

- **Option B:** MORE academic rigor (zero completeness-attack surface).
- **Option C:** ADEQUATE academic rigor (enumeration honestly framed as reference).
- **Option A:** ADEQUATE but vulnerable (scaffolding-language can slip into completeness-implication).

**Net:** Option C is sufficient. Option B's rigor gain is marginal over Option C.

**Verdict on Axis 2:** Option C holds up to academic rigor without dissolution cost.

## §11. Author-ratified resolutions

**Ratified 2026-04-24 by Chris Winn — Option C' (refined middle path).** Option C base recommendation (demote 10-list to reproducibility-reference) ratified WITH political-philosophical acknowledgment refinement surfaced in author exchange:

**Option C' specific changes vs Option C base:**

1. **Framing shift:** "examples of commons categories the framework has examined" rather than "the framework's commons categories." Reframes 10 from reproducibility-record to *worked-example record across cases* — the methodology produces these in our examined cases; reader's cases may produce different/additional ones.

2. **Explicit political-philosophical acknowledgment:** the framework doesn't claim universal ontology of what IS a commons; it shows HOW cost severance operates across whatever commons are identified in a given context. Different political traditions (classical liberalism, civic republicanism, socialist-communitarian, Marxist, lived-oppression, dominant-class) make different commitments about what is shared vs individual. The framework works within any of those commitments; what it doesn't accept is extraction without accountability regardless of which commons is being extracted from.

3. **Politically-contested commons treatment:** Autonomy (especially) + Political + Kindred + Epistemic explicitly framed as commons CAN function in those modes; other political traditions may treat them differently AND framework still applies. Tech Appendix names contestation; invites reader application with their own commons-identification.

4. **Universality claim sharpened:** universality is about METHOD (CIT applies anywhere extraction operates on commons-like resources), not about enumeration canonicity. Matches Raworth's Doughnut + Ostrom's commons-governance pattern.

5. **Authorial-voice positioning** (recommended Ch 1 or Ch 6 prose):
> *"This framework doesn't argue that autonomy is universally a commons, or that habitability is, or that time is. It argues that when any of these operate AS commons in a given society — shared, depended-on, collectively enabling — extraction can sever cost from that commons, and this methodology measures the severance. Different political traditions have different commitments about what is shared and what is individual. The framework works within any of those commitments. What it doesn't accept is extraction without accountability, regardless of which commons is being extracted from."*
