### 3.2 Reveal-discipline risk surface

**Risk 3.2.A — Concept-density spillover.** A 6,000–10,000-word review-essay of Swift's book using cost-severance as analytical lens can drift past 10% effective because the lived-Chesapeake register surfaces multiple framework-concepts simultaneously across Swift's chapter-arcs (oyster collapse → cost severance; menhaden reduction-fishery → value-extraction-to-upstream-industries; Tangier shoreline loss → foreclosure-cost; rockfish moratorium-vs-now → intergenerational-pricing-gap). The concepts accumulate as a *cluster pattern* the reader registers — reading the framework even without the framework being named.

**Risk 3.2.B — Lived-author-voice slippage.** Ch 3 lines 137–146 (the anonymized-Phat passage) demonstrate exactly the register the review-essay would draw on — first-person observation from inside the waterfront, with cost-severance language emerging via accumulated lived detail rather than formal naming. That register is *the framework's most natural plain-prose carrier*. Importing it into a NYRB review-essay risks landing the framework's *voice* before the framework's *vocabulary* in print — voice-pre-revelation that pre-warms (rather than thunder-steals) but warrants Stage 1 brief discussion.

**Three calibrations protect the ≤10% target:**

- **Calibration 1 — Negative-space construction** (carried forward from multi-book NYRB Stage 0 §3.2): "Swift gives us the year on Tangier in extraordinary depth; what no one writing on the working waterfront has yet articulated is the accounting that would let the cost-bearers be compensated" — negative-space observation, not positive-naming move.
- **Calibration 2 — Reviewer-from-inside-the-territory register, not framework-architect register.** The essay's voice is *waterman-adjacent-reviewer-reading-Swift*, NOT framework-architect-summarizing-his-own-work. Lived-Fox-Hill credentials anchor the reading; author's framework remains visible as analytical posture without being named.
- **Calibration 3 — Cap concept-density at ≤4 cost-severance-adjacent concepts surfaced.** Cost severance + foreclosure cost + value-extraction-to-upstream-industries + intergenerational-pricing-gap. The brief specifies these four and STOPS — no additional CIT, RCV-by-name, Three Ways, Four Gates, bond decomposition, Norway-as-existence-proof, Tech Appendix reference.
