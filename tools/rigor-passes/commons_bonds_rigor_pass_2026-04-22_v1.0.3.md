## 4. Per-candidate light rigor

### 4.1 Informational → Evidentiary

**Context (from abundance-dimensions doc §2.9):** Scarcity axis = evidentiary access; canonical cases include tobacco / pharma / opioid suppression + data-commons enclosure + AI-era epistemic asymmetry. Candidates: Informational / Epistemic / Veridical / Evidentiary. Evidentiary leading per "courtroom-native" Test 23 pressure.

**Merger gate under "Evidentiary":**

- **vs. Agency → Autonomy (Kantian pair).** Case A (Evidentiary scarcity, Autonomy abundance): a well-informed free-agent contractor faces no coercion but bears evidentiary cost (supplier withholds performance data). Constructible. Case B (Autonomy scarcity, Evidentiary abundance): a fully-informed worker is coerced by hierarchy despite having access to all relevant information (employment-relationship power asymmetry). Constructible. Bilateral independence holds. **PASS.**
- **vs. Political.** Case A (Evidentiary scarcity, Political abundance): private-party information asymmetry in a non-regulatory-capture context (consumer vs. firm data imbalance in a well-regulated market). Case B (Political scarcity, Evidentiary abundance): regulatory capture operating transparently (publicly-disclosed lobbying, well-documented capture) — harder case; much regulatory capture operates via informational opacity. However, pure-coordination capture (e.g., revolving-door personnel policy, transparent) can exist with full evidentiary access. Bilateral independence holds, with the Political case tighter. **PASS (marginal).**

**Primitiveness gate under "Evidentiary":** Does evidentiary-access-as-scarcity decompose into existing dimensions? No — evidentiary asymmetry is a distinct primitive (not Temporal, not Institutional, not Political). Evidentiary access is the precondition for consent and allocation-evaluation; it does not reduce to other scarcities. **PASS.**

**Test 20 (vocabulary portability):** "Evidentiary cost" / "severed evidentiary access" / "evidentiary asymmetry" all compress to citable phrases. Strictly stronger than "Informational cost" (bland). Passes Harvey / Klein / Raworth compression test. **PASS (strong).**

**Test 23 (labor-lawyer-in-2039):** "Evidentiary severance" / "evidentiary cost" reads natively in a brief. Courtroom-native — the word "evidentiary" has unambiguous legal register. A judge does not need it explained. Stronger than "Informational cost." **PASS (strong).**

**Character 10 (labor lawyer 2039):** Strong alignment. "Evidentiary asymmetry" is legal-practice-native; "evidentiary cost" reads naturally in employment, consumer-protection, and regulatory briefs. **PASS.**

**Character M1 (analytic philosopher):** "Evidentiary" is technically precise (bearing-on-determining-truth), distinct from "informational" (generic) or "epistemic" (broader, includes knowledge-ecology questions). Definitional work clean. **PASS.**

**Character 11 (community organizer):** "What evidentiary asymmetry is this contract hiding?" — works. Slightly academic register vs. "what are they hiding from us?" but organizer-usable without translation. **PASS (moderate).**

**Scope concern (potential full-rigor escalation):** Does "Evidentiary" narrow the dimension's scope vs. "Informational"? Canonical cases include AI-era epistemic-asymmetry (training-data opacity, model unexplainability, attention-economy surveillance) and data-commons enclosure. These read more "epistemic" / "informational" than pure "evidentiary" in lay reading.

**Resolution:** The scarcity axis is **access-to-evidence-for-consent-or-allocation-evaluation**. This is the primitive. AI-era cases fit — training-data opacity is evidentiary asymmetry about how models were built; attention-economy surveillance is evidentiary asymmetry about what platforms extract. The word "Evidentiary" in the framework is used in the broad legal-philosophical sense (bearing-on-determining-truth), not narrowed to courtroom-only usage. A dimension-intro scope statement must make this explicit — i.e., "Evidentiary scope covers all cases where access-to-evidence-for-consent-or-allocation-evaluation is structurally asymmetric, including but not limited to courtroom contexts. AI-era epistemic asymmetry (training-data opacity, model behavior), attention-economy surveillance, data-commons enclosure, and regulatory-disclosure failures are all Evidentiary-dimension cases."

**Verdict:** **LOCK Evidentiary.** Light rigor sufficient; no scope-clarification escalation needed. Dimension-intro scope-statement addition queued as pre-drafting item (parallel to Kindred's chosen-family scope statement).

### 4.2 Agency → Autonomy

**Context (from abundance-dimensions doc §2.10):** Scarcity axis = non-coerced choice; canonical cases include gig / contract-labor coercion, employment-relationship power asymmetry, company-town consent-under-constraint, 120+ hour-week consent-normalization case. Candidates: Agency / Autonomy / Non-coercion. Autonomy leading per Character M1 pressure on "Agency" (principal-agent baggage in econ).

**Merger gate under "Autonomy":**

- **vs. Evidentiary (Kantian pair).** Re-verified above (§4.1). Case A (Evidentiary scarcity, Autonomy abundance) and Case B (Autonomy scarcity, Evidentiary abundance) both constructible. Bilateral independence holds. **PASS.**
- **vs. Institutional.** Case A (Autonomy scarcity, Institutional abundance): worker in a well-governed corporate hierarchy still subject to coercive employment terms (non-compete, at-will-with-retaliation-risk). Constructible. Case B (Institutional scarcity, Autonomy abundance): freelance contractor with full non-coercion but in organizational chaos (freelance-collective dysfunction, cooperative-management breakdown). Constructible. Bilateral independence holds. **PASS.**
- **vs. Temporal.** Case A (Autonomy scarcity, Temporal abundance): coerced employment with secure retirement and long horizon (classic mid-century union-era corporate employment, post-pension). Constructible historically. Case B (Temporal scarcity, Autonomy abundance): freelance contractor with full non-coercion but no retirement security or foreshortened horizon (gig-economy, startup-founder). Very constructible. **PASS.**

**Primitiveness gate under "Autonomy":** Does non-coerced-choice-as-scarcity decompose? No — capacity-for-non-coerced-choice is primitive, distinct from Evidentiary (information access), Institutional (hierarchy), Temporal (time-separator), etc. Same primitiveness verdict as under "Agency." **PASS.**

**Test 20 (vocabulary portability):** "Autonomy cost" / "severed autonomy" / "autonomy-bond" all compress. "Autonomy" is a well-established philosophical and legal term (Kant, Mill, Sen, medical autonomy, employment autonomy). Stronger than "Agency" which carries principal-agent baggage. **PASS (strong).**

**Test 23 (labor-lawyer-in-2039):** "Autonomy cost" / "autonomy severance" in a brief reads naturally. Employment law already uses "autonomy of the individual worker" as doctrinal vocabulary. Stronger than "Agency cost" (which conflates with legal-agent / power-of-attorney usage). **PASS (strong).**

**Character 10 (labor lawyer 2039):** Strong — employment law doctrinally uses autonomy. "Autonomy severance" / "autonomy cost" reads native in employment, gig-economy, and consumer-protection briefs. **PASS.**

**Character M1 (analytic philosopher):** "Autonomy" is philosophically precise (Kantian self-legislation; Mill's self-regarding liberty; Sen's capability-as-freedom). Resolves the principal-agent baggage that made "Agency" imprecise. **PASS (strong).**

**Character 11 (community organizer):** "What autonomy is this gig-economy contract stripping?" — organizer-usable. Stronger than "agency" (econ-jargon register). Possibly less-strong than "non-coercion" (direct organizer vocabulary) but "autonomy" travels further into policy and legal registers. **PASS.**

**Scope concern:** Does "Autonomy" narrow from "non-coercion-structure" to "individual-independence"? Medical-autonomy, patient-autonomy, age-of-consent-autonomy all carry specific registers. Could the framework's collective-coercion cases (company-town, cultural coercion, population-scale behavioral coercion) read awkwardly as "Autonomy" cases?

**Resolution:** "Collective autonomy" and "group autonomy" are standard phrases (political self-determination, indigenous self-governance). The dimension prices any cost imposed by structural compulsion regardless of whether the compulsion operates at individual or collective scale. Cross-ideological positioning (libertarian non-coercion + leftist anti-exploitation) is load-bearing — both read "Autonomy" naturally.

**Verdict:** **LOCK Autonomy.** Light rigor sufficient.

### 4.3 Cohesion → Social-Fabric (vs. retain Cohesion)

**Context (from abundance-dimensions doc §2.8):** Scarcity axis = civic trust / collective-action infrastructure / social-fabric density; canonical cases include gig-economy atomization, NYC consulting-years isolation (non-co-occurring with Kindred), Bowling-Alone suburbs. Candidates: Cohesion / Social-Fabric / Civic-Ties / Communion. Social-Fabric leading per Part 2 "organizer-usable" read; Cohesion-name pressure from Test 18 Putnam-overlap.

**Merger gate under "Social-Fabric":**

- **vs. Kindred.** Case A (Social-Fabric abundance, Kindred scarcity): third-generation immigrant community with rich civic participation but thinned heritage-kinship transmission. Constructible. Case B (Kindred abundance, Social-Fabric scarcity): tight-knit ethnic religious congregation in an atomized suburban context. Constructible. Bilateral independence holds, parallel to Cohesion-vs-Kindred under Part 2. **PASS.**
- **vs. Institutional.** Case A (Social-Fabric scarcity, Institutional abundance): well-run organization operating in a civic-depleted locality. Constructible. Case B (Institutional scarcity, Social-Fabric abundance): civically-rich community with collapsing organizational infrastructure (nonprofit funding collapse, shuttered public institutions). Constructible. **PASS.**
- **vs. Autonomy.** Case A (Social-Fabric scarcity, Autonomy abundance): free-agent-contractor population in civic-depleted locality. Constructible. Case B (Autonomy scarcity, Social-Fabric abundance): cohesive collective with coercive internal norms (sectarian religious community, union-era company town). Constructible. **PASS.**

Merger structure under Social-Fabric works — no boundary issues.

**Primitiveness gate under "Social-Fabric":** Does civic-trust-as-scarcity decompose? No — same primitiveness verdict as under Cohesion. Civic-general collective-coordination-capacity is primitive. **PASS.**

**Test 20 (vocabulary portability):** "Social-fabric cost" — hyphenation is awkward in a brief; prose would likely drop the hyphen ("social fabric cost") which then introduces ambiguity (is "social fabric" a technical term or a generic phrase?). "Severed social fabric" compresses but reads metaphorical. "The social-fabric severance" — hyphenation awkwardness persists. Compared to "Cohesion cost" / "civic cohesion cost" which compress cleanly. **MARGINAL — Social-Fabric compresses less cleanly than Cohesion.**

**Test 23 (labor-lawyer-in-2039):** "Social-fabric severance" / "social-fabric cost" in a brief: the hyphen itself forces the reader to stop; un-hyphenated "social fabric cost" reads metaphorical rather than technical. A judge might need it explained (what specifically is meant by "social fabric" in this formal context?). "Cohesion cost" reads cleaner in legal register despite Putnam association. **WEAKER than Cohesion — Social-Fabric fails the courtroom-native test.**

**Character 10 (labor lawyer 2039):** Hesitates on Social-Fabric. Would likely rephrase to "social cohesion cost" or "community cohesion cost" in briefs, reverting to the Cohesion vocabulary. **FAIL — the rename doesn't survive Character 10 adoption.**

**Character M1 (analytic philosopher):** "Social-Fabric" is a metaphor, not a technical term. Social capital (Bourdieu, Coleman, Putnam) is the technical term; "social fabric" is a journalism-register phrase. **FAIL — definitional work weaker under Social-Fabric than under Cohesion.**

**Character 11 (community organizer):** "The social fabric of this neighborhood is being torn" — organizer-native. Strong positive. The organizer-register is Social-Fabric's one clear advantage. **PASS.**

**Cross-character analysis:** Two of three specified-light-rigor characters (10, M1) prefer Cohesion; one (11) prefers Social-Fabric. The rename improves organizer-usability but weakens courtroom-usability and analytical-precision. Net: **rename is a lateral move, not an improvement.**

**Alternative evaluation — Civic-Ties:** Same hyphenation concern. Narrower scope ("ties" vs. "fabric") — loses the broader collective-capacity reading. **Weaker than both Cohesion and Social-Fabric.**

**Alternative evaluation — Communion:** Religious register forecloses non-religious framings. Narrower. **Rejected.**

**Key finding:** The reason "Cohesion" was flagged in Part 2 was Test 18 Putnam-overlap — a positioning concern, not a precision concern. Test 18 is a framework-positioning test, resolvable by **methodology-appendix prose engaging Putnam as intellectual ancestor** (same pattern as Weston engagement for Kindred, Ostrom engagement for Kindred / Cohesion split, Tönnies engagement for Kindred / Cohesion distinction). The rename to Social-Fabric addresses the positioning concern by evading Putnam altogether — but at the cost of creating new precision problems (hyphenation + metaphor).

Methodology-appendix Putnam-engagement is the better fix. It preserves Cohesion's clean compression on Tests 20/23/Character-10/Character-M1 while directly engaging the positioning concern.

**Verdict:** **RETAIN Cohesion as canonical name.** Queue methodology-appendix prose engaging Putnam (social-capital literature) as intellectual ancestor; position framework as extending Putnam with tier-decomposable pricing (pattern parallel to Tönnies engagement for Kindred, Ostrom for commons governance). Social-Fabric vocabulary may still appear in intuitive prose passages when discussing the dimension colloquially, but Cohesion is the formal dimension name.

**Follow-up candidate revisitability:** Rejected candidates remain revisitable per protocol §17.6. If Putnam-engagement prose proves inadequate in drafting practice, Cohesion rename returns to candidacy with expanded alternatives (not Social-Fabric / Civic-Ties / Communion, which this pass found weaker — candidates like "Solidarity" or "Conviviality" would need separate testing).

### 4.4 Geographic → Spatial

**Context (from abundance-dimensions doc §2.2):** Scarcity axis = location / place-based access / stranded-asset cost; canonical cases include extractive-region sacrifice zones, housing-affordability metros, infrastructure deserts, climate-migration foreclosure. "Spatial" rename resolves off-Earth multi-scale naming failure documented in v1.0.0 layer/tier stress test ("Planetary" / "Geographic" candidates strained at orbital-habitat / Lagrange-point contexts).

**Merger gate under "Spatial":**

- **vs. Habitability.** Case A (Spatial scarcity, Habitability abundance): breathable-air-abundant locality with place-based access constraint (suburban metro with excellent air, extreme housing-stranded cost). Constructible. Case B (Habitability scarcity, Spatial abundance): fenceline-community air-quality foreclosure in a geographically unconstrained region. Constructible. **PASS.**
- **vs. Ecosystem.** Case A (Spatial scarcity, Ecosystem abundance): stranded-region with functioning pollination / hydrology but no market access (remote agricultural zone). Constructible. Case B (Ecosystem scarcity, Spatial abundance): ecosystem-service collapse in a well-connected metro (urban-watershed pollution in an accessible city). Constructible. **PASS.**

**Primitiveness gate under "Spatial":** Does location-as-scarcity decompose? No — spatial-relations (distance, place-specificity, stranded-asset) is primitive, distinct from Habitability (medium) or Ecosystem (services). Same primitiveness verdict as under "Geographic." **PASS.**

**Test 20 (vocabulary portability):** "Spatial cost" / "spatial severance" / "spatial-asymmetry" all compress. "Spatial inequality" is an established social-science term (economic geography, urban studies, environmental justice). Slightly more abstract than "Geographic" but compresses equivalently. **PASS.**

**Test 23 (labor-lawyer-in-2039):** "Spatial cost" in a brief reads natural. Employment / housing / environmental-justice law already uses "spatial" (spatial mismatch, spatial segregation, spatial justice). Slight register-abstraction vs. "geographic" but judge does not need it explained. The off-Earth resolution ("spatial cost" works for orbital-habitat / Lagrange-point contexts where "geographic cost" strains) is the Test 23 tiebreaker in favor of Spatial. **PASS.**

**Character 10 (labor lawyer 2039):** Both work. Slight preference for "spatial" in framework-consistency register (Spatial fits the abstract-axis naming pattern with Temporal, Autonomy, etc.). **PASS.**

**Character M1 (analytic philosopher):** "Spatial" is precise (the spatial axis of allocation, parallel to the temporal axis). Clean definitional pair with Temporal. **PASS.**

**Character 11 (community organizer):** "The spatial cost of this extraction" reads slightly more abstract than "the geographic cost of this extraction." Organizers already use "spatial justice" in environmental-justice organizing, but "geographic" reads more immediate. Minor concern — mitigable by prose using "place-based" or "geographic" in passages where immediacy matters, while "Spatial" remains the formal dimension name. **PASS (moderate).**

**Scope concern:** Does "Spatial" broaden too much into pure-geometry / abstract-topology territory? Physics and mathematics use "spatial" in formal abstract senses that could confuse the framework reader expecting social-allocation content.

**Resolution:** Framework-context is social-allocation; "spatial" in that context maps to economic-geography / spatial-inequality / spatial-justice discourse, not abstract geometry. The dimension-intro scope statement clarifies: "Spatial names location-as-scarcity-axis — place-based access, distance-to-market, stranded-asset cost, place-specific extraction — at scales from individual commutes and migrations to off-Earth contexts."

**Off-Earth resolution (the Test 23 tiebreaker):** "Geographic" strains at orbital-habitat, Lagrange-point, and multi-planet contexts (where "geography" presupposes a planet-surface referent). "Spatial" reads cleanly across all scales: Earth individual / community / government / corporate AND off-Earth individual / community / government / corporate. This matches the universality-claim requirement of protocol §5.4.

**Verdict:** **LOCK Spatial.** Light rigor sufficient.

## 5. Cross-cutting findings

### 5.1 Kantian consent-precondition pair under new names

The Informational ↔ Agency pair becomes Evidentiary ↔ Autonomy. Re-verified under new names:

- Consent requires **evidence** (Evidentiary axis) — access-to-evidence-for-consent.
- Consent requires **non-coercion** (Autonomy axis) — capacity-for-non-coerced-choice.
- Evidence-structural coercion is itself an Autonomy-dimension case.
- Autonomy-deficit generated by evidentiary asymmetry is the pair's central instance.

The pair reads **more philosophically precise under the new names** than under Informational / Agency. "Evidentiary" + "Autonomy" is standard Kantian-ethics vocabulary; "Informational" + "Agency" conflated with information-theory / principal-agent-economics usages. Rename strengthens the Kantian-pair cross-reference in dimension intros and methodology appendix.

### 5.2 Scope-boundary impacts

- **Habitability ↔ Spatial:** The Habitability / Geographic boundary was shared-medium vs. place. Under Spatial the boundary is unchanged (shared-medium vs. place-location). No intro-prose revision needed.
- **Ecosystem ↔ Spatial:** Services-from-biosphere vs. place. Unchanged.
- **Kindred ↔ Cohesion:** Identity-deep specific vs. civic-general. Unchanged (Cohesion retained).
- **Evidentiary ↔ Political:** Evidentiary asymmetry vs. distributional-conflict. The boundary is where *the primary asymmetry* is evidence-access (Evidentiary) vs. allocation-conflict (Political); cross-dimension cases (regulatory-informational capture) exist and are assigned per canonical-case routing. Unchanged.
- **Autonomy ↔ Institutional:** Coercion-structure vs. organizational-hierarchy. Unchanged.
- **Autonomy ↔ Temporal:** 120+ hour-week case retains its cross-reference (Temporal + Autonomy).

No scope-boundary prose revisions required beyond the name propagation itself and the Evidentiary scope-statement addition flagged in §4.1.

### 5.3 Off-Earth universality re-verified

Spatial rename specifically addresses the off-Earth scale-locking failure documented in the v1.0.0 layer/tier stress test. Evidentiary, Autonomy, and Cohesion all travel off-Earth cleanly without rename (verified at abstract level in Part 2 §3). The cohort as a whole strengthens the framework's multi-scale × 2-environments universality claim per protocol §5.4.
