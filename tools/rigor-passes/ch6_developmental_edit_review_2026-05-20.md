## §1. What this pass IS (and what it is NOT)

**This pass IS** the whole-chapter developmental-edit lens applied to a
methodology chapter: emotional-arc continuity (insofar as a methodology
chapter has an analytical arc the reader's attention rides); scene-anchor
density across the chapter (Ch 6 has zero scenes — flagged at §4 LOW); breath
around the chapter's analytical pivots; reader-engagement at the chapter's
load-bearing apparatus surfaces; cumulative-cadence residue after per-paragraph
Pass-3.2 + Phase C-γ + Phase C-δ + cascade-followup fixes; whether the
chapter's central empirical claim (the three-method convergence finding) lands
with the structural prominence its load-bearing role demands.

**This pass is NOT:**

- Pass 3.1 fact-check (no new facts surfaced; no re-litigation of Pass 1
  dispositions; Pass 1 Phase C-α + Phase C-β + cascade-cleanup closed).
- Pass 3.2 voice-polish (no per-paragraph LLM-tic findings; Pass 2 caught
  what it was designed to catch via Phase C-γ + Phase C-δ + cascade-followup
  — this pass operates at the scale where cumulative-pacing effects show up).
- Pass 3.3 acceptance / Pass 3.4 robustness (no per-character audience
  verdicts; the Pass 3.3 verdict 7 INCLUDE / 0 EXCLUDE is honored; the
  Pass 3.4 CONDITIONALLY ROBUST verdict is honored; this pass tests
  something different — whether the chapter's *experience-of-reading* a
  methodology chapter matches what the character-set tests verified).
- Stage 4 render-integrity audit (render-side concerns are pipeline-side
  and tracked separately; see `render_pipeline_comparison_ch6_2026-05-18.md`).
- Stage 5 sign-off (pre-publication; not this pass's scope).

The developmental-edit lens for a methodology chapter is different from the
lens for a memoir chapter (Ch 1). A methodology chapter is *designed* to be
apparatus-dense; the dev-edit lens does not ask whether the apparatus
should be removed but whether the apparatus is *paced*. The diagnostic
question becomes: **after four rigor passes + multiple Phase C cycles, where
does the chapter's whole-chapter rhythm still leave a load-bearing claim
landing without breath, or compress a structurally-important section into a
density that the reader processes too quickly to feel?** Pass 3.2 catches
the tic at the sentence; this catches whether the cumulative effect of
multiple tic-fixes — combined with the chapter's substantive
apparatus-density — leaves any structural moment under-weighted.

The defining diagnostic posture per the workstream class is: *does the prose
still feel flat / over-compressed / clipped-after-cutting?* For Ch 6 the
answer is more nuanced than for Ch 1. Ch 6 does not feel "flat / unemotional"
in the way Ch 1 did — Ch 6 is intentionally analytical and the analytical
register is the chapter's substantive contribution. What Ch 6 does carry, in
several specific places, is **over-compression at load-bearing structural
moments** — the chapter knows what its load-bearing moments are (it explicitly
names them: "This is the chapter's central finding, and it is worth sitting
with for a moment" at line 185) but does not always *enact* the breath around
them that its own naming announces.

See §5 (whole-chapter synthesis) for the cumulative diagnosis. The HIGH
findings concentrate on the convergence-finding pacing, the Norway Backtest
structural underweight, and the chapter-opening anchor; the MEDIUM findings
catalogue compression-without-breath patterns at the four-gates, Approach 1
walkthrough, Contribution section, What Is Owed section, and one
name-defense-mid-orientation location.
