## §1. Executive summary

**The framework's vocabulary footprint is too large for its success-criterion bet.** Twenty-plus named concepts compete for reader adoption bandwidth when the framework's single-term adoption goal ("severed cost" used by labor lawyers) requires sharpness and concision. The pass recommends a **tiered vocabulary architecture** with three rings:

- **Ring 1 — Public load-bearing (5 terms):** terms the book's adoption-durability bet rests on. These travel outside the book into legal / policy / academic discourse. *Cost Severance · Severed Cost · Residual Commons Value (RCV) · Abundance Inversion Test (AIT) · Cost (Cᵢ).*

- **Ring 2 — Internal load-bearing (7 terms):** terms load-bearing for the framework's internal coherence but not adoption targets. Readers encounter these; non-readers don't adopt them. *Accountability Bond (B) · Cost Severance equation (CS = RCV − B) · Four Gates (as collective) · Abundances (as organizational scaffolding) · Substitutability Function (S) · Externality Tail (E) · Intergenerational Pricing Gap (IPG).*

- **Ring 3 — Methodological elaboration (retire, merge, or demote to context):** terms that do methodological work but may be expressible in already-load-bearing vocabulary. *Full Generational Cost (FGC), Civilizational Substitutability Gap (CSG), Abundance Masking, Abundance Dimension (as synonym for abundance — redundant), Universality Test, Asymmetric Regret Principle, the 9 tier entries, Spatial Cost Severance, Temporal Cost Severance, Value Capture, Foreclosure Cost (as standalone glossary entry alongside Cᵢ convention), Cost Bearing.*

**Aggregate finding:** ~12 Ring-1 and Ring-2 terms deserve individual deep-rigor; ~11 Ring-3 terms warrant retirement / merger / subsumption rather than individual rigor; ~3 cluster-level rigor passes cover the remaining structural-consistency questions (severance cluster, gate-naming cohort, tier dissolution follow-through). Reshaped queue below in §12.

**What this means for the 31-pass queue ratified earlier today:** the queue contains substantial redundancy. Individual passes on retirement-candidate terms reproduce the meta-pass's retirement finding without adding insight. Cluster passes replace several individual passes each. Reshaped queue is ~10–14 passes instead of 31.

**Core tension identified:** the framework's current vocabulary was built additively — each term was added to solve a specific problem as it arose. Additive growth without periodic pruning produces vocabulary proliferation. This pass is the first pruning pass.

**The five meta-modules + §22.4:**

| Test | Finding |
|---|---|
| **M1 Framework CORE** | CORE survives any vocabulary footprint; CORE defends against over-pruning (5 Ring-1 terms are irreducible). |
| **M4 Craft** | Prose quality degrades as footprint grows past ~12 terms; book chapters become term-heavy and accessibility suffers. |
| **M5 Dinner-table** | Non-expert cognitive load is the sharpest constraint — readers tap out around 8–10 named concepts. |
| **M6 Academic rigor** | Over-proliferation signals under-theorized framework; precision-per-term diminishes as term count grows. |
| **M11 Critic pressure** | Adversarial critic asks "why N concepts rather than N-k?" for every gap between footprint and load-bearing count. |
| **§22.4 Success-criterion alignment** | Vocabulary adoption bandwidth is finite; proliferation trades against the single-term adoption bet. |

"Is the framework's active vocabulary — taken as a system — serving its success criterion, publishing path, and rhetorical strategy? Or is it proliferated to a degree that costs more than it contributes?"

### §2.2 Sub-questions

**SQ-1:** Which terms are load-bearing for the framework's CORE (AIT methodology + RCV formula + CS = RCV − B equation + accountability bond + cost severance mechanism)?

**SQ-2:** Which terms are load-bearing for the book's argument (distinct from CORE — terms the chapters need to make their arguments)?

**SQ-3:** Which terms are methodological elaboration that can be replaced by already-load-bearing vocabulary?

**SQ-4:** Which terms are redundant (duplicates, near-synonyms, lower-order entries of higher-order concepts)?

**SQ-6:** Is the gate-naming cohort internally consistent, or does it need coordination?

**SQ-7:** Is the severance vocabulary cluster internally consistent?

### §2.3 What falsifies the recommendations

The tiered-ring architecture is falsified if:
- A term classified Ring-3 (retirement candidate) turns out to be load-bearing in a way the pass missed.
- A term classified Ring-1 (public load-bearing) turns out not to support the success-criterion vocabulary-adoption goal.
- The proposed cluster passes fail to produce coordinated recommendations when executed.
- Reshaping the 31-pass queue leaves coverage gaps.

**Verdict below (§11–§12):** provisional classifications survive the pass's own tests; falsification trigger would come from executing reshaped passes and finding they produce inconsistent verdicts.

## §4. M1 — Framework CORE

The framework's mathematical + methodological CORE does not change under any vocabulary decision. The formula `CS = RCV − B` with `RCV = ∫ (Σ Cᵢ) · D dt` operates on symbols that are agnostic to their human-readable names. AIT's role (identify which costs admit through the four gates) is agnostic to naming choices.

**What CORE requires:**
- A name for the mechanism (cost severance).
- A name for the computed quantity (RCV).
- A name for the posted instrument (accountability bond / B).
- A name for the gap-equation relation (CS = RCV − B).
- A name for the identification method (AIT).
- A name for the atomic unit of measurement (cost / Cᵢ per Variable-vs-Cost pass).
- A name for the collective admission apparatus (four gates).

That's 7 terms for CORE itself. Everything else is elaboration or instance.

**CORE defense against over-pruning:** if a rigor pass recommends retiring any of the 7 CORE terms, the pass has made an error. CORE is the irreducible minimum.

**CORE defense against under-pruning:** if a rigor pass recommends keeping a term that is not part of CORE and not load-bearing for a specific chapter's argument, the pass has not applied the discipline.

**Verdict on M1:** The vocabulary footprint is over-sized relative to CORE. Pruning can remove ~11 terms without affecting CORE. Pruning cannot remove the 7 CORE terms.

## §5. M4 — Craft

Prose quality under the current vocabulary footprint.

### §5.1 Term density in chapter drafts

Sampling method: count distinct framework-technical terms per 1000 words of chapter prose.

- Ch 2 (McDowell coal, ~6200 words): ~7 distinct framework terms encountered. Primarily: cost severance, severed cost, accountability bond, externality tail, RCV (referenced but not defined), AIT (referenced). Reader load manageable.
- Ch 6 (current state, ~9500 words): ~18 distinct framework terms. Reader load high.
- Ch 8 (pending variable-walkthrough restructure): will introduce all named Cᵢ. If 20+ specific costs get named explicitly, Ch 8 becomes the most term-dense chapter by far.

**Observation:** Ch 6 is already near the cognitive-load ceiling. Ch 8 (restructured) will push past it unless specific-Cᵢ naming absorbs some of the vocabulary budget that abstract terms currently occupy.

### §5.2 Prose-economy cost of term proliferation

Each introduced-and-defined term costs ~50–100 words of chapter prose for its definition + worked use. Ten additional terms = 500–1000 words of definition overhead across the book. That's 2–4% of total book word count spent on defining methodological elaboration terms that may not be load-bearing for the argument.

### §5.3 Register discipline under proliferation

The book's register commitment is accounting / bookkeeping voice. Terms like "Civilizational Substitutability Gap" and "Asymmetric Regret Principle" pull toward technical-academic register. Each register-intrusion is a micro-break in the book's voice.

**Verdict on M4:** Prose quality degrades as footprint grows past ~12 terms. Current footprint (~33 terms in inventory) produces visible strain in Ch 6 and will produce more in Ch 8. Pruning to Rings 1+2 (~12 terms) restores prose economy.

## §6. M5 — Dinner-table

Non-expert reader cognitive load.

### §6.1 Working-memory limit

Cognitive-science consensus places working memory at 4–7 items for abstract concepts. A non-expert reading the book holds in mind: (a) the characters + settings of each chapter, (b) the framework's named concepts, (c) the argument being made. Each framework term consumes one working-memory slot.

With ~12 Ring-1/Ring-2 terms, the reader's slots for framework concepts are mostly filled but manageable across a chapter. With ~33 terms, the reader cannot hold all of them; the book must spend cognitive budget re-introducing terms chapter-by-chapter or accept that readers will fail to retain some.

### §6.2 Specific-vs-abstract balance

Specific terms ("black-lung cost," "acid-mine-drainage remediation") are easier to hold than abstract terms ("civilizational substitutability gap," "asymmetric regret principle"). Shifting the vocabulary balance toward specifics reduces cognitive load.

### §6.3 Dinner-table test sample — framework introduction in 3 minutes

**Current (full footprint):**
> "The framework identifies cost severance — the mechanism by which extraction separates value capture from cost bearing — and prices it through Residual Commons Value or RCV, which is calculated against an Accountability Bond B via the equation CS equals RCV minus B. The Abundance Inversion Test or AIT admits new costs to the integrand through four gates — AIT itself, dimensional consistency, boundedness, and independence — and the whole apparatus operates over ten abundance dimensions..."

*Non-expert tap-out point: AIT introduction.*

**Pruned (Ring-1 + Ring-2):**
> "When extraction separates who profits from who pays the cost, we call that cost severance — and the unpaid part is a severed cost. The framework computes how big those severed costs are by adding up specific costs a community bears — health, community-transition, political-capture, environmental — that the extractor never paid for. We use a method called the Abundance Inversion Test to decide which costs count, and we compare the total to whatever the extractor did pay."

*Non-expert tap-out point: no tap-out; description held through to end.*

The Ring-1+Ring-2 version is more accessible. Dinner-table test PASSES under pruning; FAILS under current footprint.

**Verdict on M5:** Current footprint materially fails dinner-table accessibility. Pruning is necessary, not optional.

## §7. M6 — Academic rigor

### §7.1 Precision-per-term

Academic audiences judge frameworks on precision — each term should have a distinct, defensible meaning. Proliferation can produce terms whose meanings overlap, weakening precision.

**Overlapping pairs identified:**
- *Cost Severance / Severed Cost:* mechanism vs result. Distinct enough.
- *Cost Severance / Spatial Cost Severance:* general vs subtype. Is subtype load-bearing? Same for Temporal Cost Severance. Likely subsumable into the general.
- *Abundance Dimension / Abundance:* under Dimension-vs-Abundance rigor pass, these collapse to one.
- *Foreclosure Cost (as C₁) / Foreclosure Cost (as Tier 4):* duplicate, resolves under tier dissolution.
- *Full Generational Cost (FGC) / RCV:* under dissolution, FGC was "total generational cost across tiers"; without tiers, FGC = Σᵢ Cᵢ = the integrand of RCV. Redundant.
- *Universality Test / Rigor Protocol M1 framework CORE:* is "Universality Test" a distinct thing or is it the M1 rigor pass applied to universality claims? Likely subsumable.
- *Civilizational Substitutability Gap (CSG) / Substitutability Function (S):* CSG is a specialized framing of S applied to civilization-scale contexts. Probably subsumable.

### §7.2 Acronym overload

The framework has: RCV, CS, AIT, B, IPG, CSG, FGC, and has informally used others. Seven+ acronyms is heavy; academic readers tolerate this but non-academic readers struggle.

### §7.3 Terminological drift across docs

The Tech Appendix uses some terms ("Canonical v10") the rigor passes have since dissolved. This is fixable by the audit sweep but it's a signal: when docs proliferate terms, keeping them synchronized is harder.

**Verdict on M6:** Precision-per-term degrades under proliferation. Retiring overlapping pairs + acronym-heavy elaboration terms tightens the framework's academic defense.

### §8.2 The "concept-inflation" attack

**Critic:** "Every time you encountered a new argumentative need, you added a term. FGC for tier-total, CSG for civilization-scale, Asymmetric Regret for decision rules, Universality Test for cross-context applicability. This is concept-inflation — each problem gets its own named category. Frameworks that mature tend to prune; yours hasn't."

**Response under current footprint:** must defend each term independently. Weak.

**Response post-pruning:** "This pass is exactly that pruning. Here's what we kept and why."

### §8.3 The "orphan terms" attack

**Critic:** "Abundance Masking. Asymmetric Regret Principle. These appear in the glossary but hardly anywhere else. If a term isn't doing work in the book, what's it doing in the glossary?"

**Response:** under the tiered-ring architecture, orphan terms are candidates for retirement or demotion to a footnote-level reference. Glossary earns its entries by chapter-work demonstrated.

**Verdict on M11:** Current footprint invites three distinct adversarial attacks, each weak to defend. Pruning to rings removes the attack surfaces.

## §9. §22.4 — Usefulness-vs-Goals Alignment

### §9.1 Success-criterion alignment

**Success criterion:** "severed cost" adopted as legal/policy vocabulary.

Single-term adoption benefits from vocabulary concentration. When a reader encounters one strong term (severed cost) in a dense conceptual field of thirty other terms, the one term has to fight for attention. When the reader encounters one strong term in a sparse conceptual field of five-to-seven other supporting terms, the one term stands out.

**Pruning serves the success criterion directly.** Every retired term reduces vocabulary-adoption competition for "severed cost."

## §10. The tiered-ring architecture

### §10.1 Ring 1 — Public load-bearing (5 terms)

Terms the book's adoption-durability bet rests on. These travel outside the book. A labor lawyer encountering these terms should parse them and possibly adopt them.

1. **Cost Severance** — the mechanism. Flagship phrase (in compound with "severed cost"). Book title is "Commons Bonds" but the phrase that gets adopted is "cost severance" / "severed cost."
2. **Severed Cost** — the phenomenon. Success-criterion target.
3. **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** — the quantity. Needs to survive because without it, "severed cost" has no quantified backbone. Usage: "The RCV of McDowell coal exceeded the accountability bond by..."
4. **Abundance Inversion Test (AIT)** — the method. Possibly expressible in simpler prose ("the scarcity test") but AIT is already in use across all rigor passes + Ch 6 + Tech Appendix. Durability favored.
5. **Cost (Cᵢ)** — the atomic unit. Per Variable-vs-Cost rigor pass.

### §10.2 Ring 2 — Internal load-bearing (7 terms)

Readers encounter these; external non-readers don't necessarily adopt them but the framework's internal argument requires them.

1. **Accountability Bond (B)** — the posted instrument. Appears in CS = RCV − B equation; load-bearing for quantification.
2. **CS = RCV − B equation** — the core equation. Named as a single concept or used as "the cost-severance equation."
3. **Four Gates (collective)** — AIT + units + boundedness + independence as the admission apparatus. Individual gate names may be adjusted per Gate-naming-cohort rigor pass.
4. **Abundances** — organizational scaffolding (the 10 per Path F, pending Dimension-vs-Abundance rigor).
5. **Substitutability Function (S)** — the framework's most novel single component. Can't be dissolved because the substitutability move is what makes RCV differ from pure Pigouvian accounting.
6. **Externality Tail (E)** — the second canonical cost term. Load-bearing for the RCV formula structure.
7. **Intergenerational Pricing Gap (IPG)** — IPG = RCV / P_market. Used in convergence tables; could arguably demote to Ring 3 as "ratio of RCV to market price" in prose.

## §11. Ring-3 disposition recommendations

### §11.1 Full Generational Cost (FGC)

**Status:** RETIRE.

**Rationale:** FGC was defined as the total cost across tiers. Under tier dissolution, "total cost across tiers" becomes just "total cost" — which is the RCV's integrand sum. FGC collapses into RCV. Continuing to name FGC as a distinct concept creates spurious distinction.

**Replacement language:** where FGC currently appears, use RCV or "the sum of all cost terms."

### §11.2 Civilizational Substitutability Gap (CSG)

**Status:** DEMOTE or RETIRE.

**Rationale:** CSG is a framing applied to the Substitutability Function at civilization scale. It's not a new concept — it's S evaluated in a specific context. Keeping CSG as a named term implies it's distinct from S; it isn't.

**Replacement language:** "civilization-scale substitutability" in prose; reference S (not CSG) in technical contexts.

### §11.3 Abundance Masking

**Status:** DEMOTE (from glossary entry to in-prose description).

**Rationale:** Abundance Masking names the phenomenon that AIT detects — abundances mask costs by making them invisible to pricing. But the phenomenon is what AIT is FOR. Naming it as a separate term creates a term-per-mechanism bloat. The phenomenon can be described in prose without a capitalized named term.

**Replacement language:** describe the masking phenomenon as "an abundance hides the cost of its scarcity-grounded violation until AIT is applied."

### §11.4 Abundance Dimension

**Status:** RETIRE (collapses under Dimension-vs-Abundance rigor pass).

**Rationale:** "Abundance Dimension" is a compound that the Dimension-vs-Abundance pass will resolve to "Abundance" or "Dimension" (TBD). Either way, the compound goes.

### §11.5 Universality Test

**Status:** DEMOTE or RETIRE.

**Rationale:** Universality Test is currently a glossary entry but functions as the M1 rigor-pass question applied to universality claims ("does the framework work across scales?"). Under rigor protocol v2.2.0, universality is tested by M1 + §22 path-comparison; it doesn't need its own named test.

**Replacement language:** reference M1 or context-universality in technical passages; drop standalone term.

### §11.6 Asymmetric Regret Principle

**Status:** RETAIN IF LOAD-BEARING FOR SPECIFIC CHAPTER; otherwise demote.

**Rationale:** Asymmetric regret is a decision-theoretic principle (precautionary pricing — err on the side of over-pricing scarce resources because under-pricing produces asymmetric regret). If Ch 9 or another chapter uses it as a load-bearing argument, keep. If it's a glossary-only entry that doesn't do chapter-work, demote.

**Replacement language:** if demoted, use "precautionary pricing" in prose.

### §11.7 Value Capture · Cost Bearing

**Status:** RETAIN (implicit structural role) but don't elevate to glossary-prominent.

**Rationale:** Value capture and cost bearing are the two halves of the severance gap. They're not framework-technical terms in the way RCV is; they're general English phrases the book uses. Glossary entry adds little.

**Replacement language:** keep in prose; remove from glossary or reduce to one-line entries.

### §11.8 Spatial Cost Severance · Temporal Cost Severance

**Status:** DEMOTE.

**Rationale:** These are cost-severance-with-a-specific-scope labels. The book's argument doesn't require them as named variants; "cost severance across space" or "cost severance across generations" describe the same phenomenon in prose without needing a capitalized compound term.

**Replacement language:** describe the spatial / temporal dimensions in prose; don't name them as distinct severance variants.

### §11.9 The 8 remaining tier entries

**Status:** RETIRED as tiers (per tier-reframing rigor pass dissolution ratification 2026-04-24). Their *content* persists as specific named Cᵢ (C_community_transition, C_political_capture, etc.). Their *glossary entries as tier X* disappear.

**Replacement:** specific Cᵢ names in prose + Tech Appendix variable catalog (Section C.4 restructure under Phase A3).

### §11.10 Foreclosure Cost (standalone glossary entry)

**Status:** MERGE with the formula-term entry.

**Rationale:** Duplicate in glossary v2 (once under Mathematical terms as the C₁ formula term, once as Tier 4). Tier entry dissolves; Mathematical entry stays as the single source.
