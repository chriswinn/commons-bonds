## §1. Executive summary

**RECOMMENDED (unchanged from prior pass): Option B — RETAIN "Units Consistency."**

**What the deep M6 changes vs. the prior pass:** the verdict stands, but the rationale is now more precisely articulable. **Three findings shift in detail:**

1. **"Commensurability" is stronger than the prior pass credited.** Kuhn 1962 (*The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*) is the most recognized contemporary academic use of the term-pair (incommensurability / commensurability) — and the framework's positive form ("the gate enforces commensurability of units") could read as a clean extension of Kuhn's category rather than as collision. This is **internal collision** (within the framework's own citation network), not just external collision. Option A loses harder than the prior pass said.

3. **Three additional candidates worth ruling out for completeness:** "Commensurate" (adjective; grammatically odd as gate name), "Comparability" (more accessible but less precise), "Homogeneity" (technical physics-tradition term; less accessible). All three weaker than B; surfaced for option-space breadth.

**Established use of "consistency":** Hilbert's program (1900s–1930s); Gödel's incompleteness theorems (1931); axiomatic-systems consistency = absence of contradiction ("a system is consistent if no statement and its negation are both derivable").

| Candidate | Reception by Hilbert/Gödel-trained reader |
|---|---|
| **A Consistency** | **Direct collision.** Reader's strongest prior is "system-without-contradiction." Reader has to override that prior to read framework's gate as unit-commensurability. Cost: prior-override per encounter. |
