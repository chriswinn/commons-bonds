### Probe A — "Three lineages in one paragraph might overload the reader."

The per-component structure (residual / commons / value, each with one tradition + one alternative-rejection) is the chapter's load-bearing argumentative pattern; the paragraph extends it cleanly. Length runs ~310 words; not over-stuffed at this register. Ch 6 is the methodology chapter where readers come prepared for argued vocabulary.

### Probe B — "Hartwick is already cited at Ch 6 line 537 and Ch 5 line 200. Doesn't a third citation overlap?"

The existing citations are case-context (Norway-SWF) and bond-instrument-context (Foreclosure Bond lineage). The proposed citation is term-defense-context (RCV's *residual* component). Three citations across three different argumentative purposes — parallel discipline, not redundancy.

### Probe C — "Why is *commons* the right term when some Indigenous traditions resist commons-as-property?"

The paragraph's Ostrom citation engages commons-as-institutional-form, not commons-as-property. The Indigenous-tradition concern is a broader-book question (Ch 4 cases + Ch 10 closing carry cross-tradition material). The paragraph's domain-of-applicability honesty leaves room for cross-tradition complementarity.

### Probe D — "Why is *residual* not just *unpriced* or *uncaptured*?"

*Residual* carries the Hartwick / residual-rents discipline: the framework's residual is what is *left* after standard accounting has done what it does, not what *escapes* standard accounting. The semantic difference matters: residual signals continuity with Hartwick's framework; unpriced or uncaptured would signal a competing framework. The paragraph names "not a substitute for either" precisely on this point.

### Probe E — "Mazzucato's value-creation distinction is contested in mainstream economics."

The paragraph's Mazzucato citation is for the value-as-extracted vs value-as-created distinction at the term-vocabulary level; it does not require the reader to accept Mazzucato's full thesis. The framework's value is named in the Mazzucato register the comp-cluster reader expects; mainstream-econ skeptics may push back at Mazzucato but their pushback is on Mazzucato's broader argument, not on the framework's term-defense.
