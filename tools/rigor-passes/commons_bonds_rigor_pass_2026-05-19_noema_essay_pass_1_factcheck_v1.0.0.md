### F-FC-Noema-1 — Norway fiscal-rule percentage attributed to 2001 — HIGH

**Location:** Line 99.

**Verbatim current text:**
> In 2001 the parliament adopted a fiscal rule capping annual government spending out of the fund at approximately three percent of expected returns. Every successive government, regardless of which coalition held power, has held to that rule.

**Source of truth (external citation):** Norway's fiscal rule (*handlingsregelen*) was adopted by the Stoltenberg I government in March 2001 with an **expected real return target of 4 percent**. The rate was **reduced to 3 percent in February/spring 2017** by the Solberg government, following analyses from the Thøgersen commission, the Mork commission, and Norges Bank that lowered expected long-run real return assumptions in a lower-global-interest-rate environment. (Sources verified 2026-05-19: regjeringen.no — The Norwegian Fiscal Policy Framework; NHH — "When Norway rewrote the fiscal rule"; Wikipedia — The budgetary rule.)

**Stage 1 brief baseline:** §7.4 says "2001 fiscal rule — held by every government since regardless of coalition — caps annual spending at ~3% of returns." The Stage 1 brief itself **conflates the 2001 adoption with the 2017 revision** — Amendment A canonical-truth-gap.

**Proposed correction (verbatim replacement prose):**
> In 2001 the parliament adopted a fiscal rule capping annual government spending out of the fund at the expected real return — initially set at four percent, lowered to three percent in 2017 as long-run return expectations declined. Every successive government, regardless of which coalition held power, has held to that rule.

**Cross-pass impact:** No impact on Pass 2 voice-polish (correction adds ~15 words; preserves cadence; no LLM-tic introduction). Negligible Pass 3.3 audience-load impact except that the **Coasean economist** + **Mazzucato-cluster reader** characters benefit from the rate-change context (signals familiarity with the policy mechanism); the Tier-1 Noema editor + literary agent benefit from the precision (fact-check-readable).

**Empirical anchor:** This is exactly the genre of factual drift the op-ed pipeline session (commit 5167edd) caught at Stage 3.1 (GPFG founding-date conflation + Bondevik-coalition-timing drift). The same regime applies here.

**Amendment A canonical-truth-gap flag:** Stage 1 brief §7.4 needs a corresponding repair to reflect the 4%→3% rate-change history. Recommend brief amendment as part of Phase C application.

"River herring" is a separate informal grouping referring to alewife (*Alosa pseudoharengus*) and blueback herring (*Alosa aestivalis*). Shad and river herring are both anadromous fish in genus *Alosa*, both run Bay tributaries in spring, but they are distinct species and a fisheries scientist (e.g., Allison Colden, Chris Moore — both named in this same section) would treat the conflation as a taxonomic error. (Source: standard fisheries literature; ASMFC species pages; NOAA Fisheries.)

**Stage 1 brief baseline:** §7.3 says "Shad — anadromous river herring whose runs once spawned up every Bay tributary." **Stage 1 brief contains the same error.** Amendment A canonical-truth-gap at the brief level.

**Proposed correction (verbatim replacement prose):**
> The shad — the anadromous fish whose spring runs once filled every Bay tributary — have effectively disappeared from the regional commercial fishery for decades.

(Drops "river herring" — keeps "anadromous" + "spring runs" + "every Bay tributary" which are all accurate for shad.)

**Cross-pass impact:** Pass 3.3 — **Chesapeake regional reader** (Tier 3) is the most likely to catch this; fisheries scientists in the Bay region routinely distinguish shad from river herring. Correction protects the essay's reportage authority in the same section where Colden + Moore are named sources. Pass 3.4 — adversarial reader (fisheries-management skeptic, ASMFC-aligned industry reader) would seize the species conflation to challenge the broader credibility of the species block.

**Amendment A canonical-truth-gap flag:** Stage 1 brief §7.3 needs the same correction (shad ≠ river herring).
