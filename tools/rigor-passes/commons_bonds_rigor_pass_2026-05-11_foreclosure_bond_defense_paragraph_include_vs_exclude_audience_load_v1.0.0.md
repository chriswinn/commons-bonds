## M11 critic-survival probes against INCLUDE

### Probe A — "Engaging the mortgage-foreclosure association directly might amplify the wrong association rather than dispel it."

The paragraph's structure inverts the risk: the association is named in the opening sentence + reframed as cousin-mechanism in the next two sentences + grounded etymologically in the following sentence. The reader who carries the mortgage association into the term reads it acknowledged + reframed + structurally re-anchored within four sentences. The acknowledgment is what makes the reframing legible; without it, the brief disambiguation at line 200 leaves the misroute open.

### Probe B — "The cousin-mechanism extension might read as flattening racial-economic specificity of 2008."

Tier-B residual flag on character #15 (Black readers). Mitigation: the racial-and-class concentration is named as fact ("disproportionate concentration in Black and working-class communities"). The cousin-relation is structural ("Same mechanism. Different substrate.") — explicitly NOT magnitude-equivalence. The "What was deliberately NOT done" section in the draft file makes the no-equivalence-claim explicit.

### Probe C — "Five alternatives is more than the bond-defense's three or the cost-severance's four. Is that bloat?"

Each rejection does specific structural work:
- *Future-Generations Bond* — descriptive but loses bar-from feature.
- *Sustainability Bond* — co-opted by ESG markets; loss of specificity.
- *Reclamation Bond* — exists in SMCRA; subset, not umbrella.
- *Conservation Bond* — preserves resource without bar-from feature.
- *Intergenerational Bond* — temporal direction without mechanism.

The number is substance-driven; the term carries more plausible alternatives than tax/fee/fund or externality/displacement/transfer/appropriation because *foreclosure* is a less-conventional economic term than the others, and reasonable readers will reach for more alternative-naming options.

### Probe D — "Old French etymology may read as scholarly affectation."

The italicized *forclore* signals etymological seriousness; the gloss ("to bar from") is plain English. Working-class reader (#18) verdict supports accessibility holds. The etymology is the structural anchor for the cousin-mechanism reframing (without it, the cousin claim is asserted; with it, the claim is grounded).

### Probe E — "Why doesn't the paragraph engage the Indigenous-tradition future-generations registers more directly?"

That work is at Ch 4 + Ch 10's broader cross-tradition material. The paragraph stays at name-defense scope; Indigenous-tradition direct engagement is broader-book scope.
