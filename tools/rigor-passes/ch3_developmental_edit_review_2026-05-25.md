### F-DE-Ch3-1 — Hampton seafood-anchor paragraph (L18) is info-dense without narrative breath (HIGH)

**Location:** Line 18 (the F-V21 + Phase C-edited Hampton historical paragraph).

**Verbatim current text:**
> "I am writing from Hampton, which once held one of the largest oyster-packing operations in the country. I grew up on this bay. The firm of J.S. Darling and Son took that name in 1884, when Frank Darling joined his father's existing oyster business. At its peak, 160 shuckers handled 200,000 bushels of Chesapeake oysters every year. The mountains of oyster shells out behind the plant rose more than four stories high. The Darlings also ran a menhaden factory at Factory Point in Fox Hill. **Recommendation:** **Option A.** Lightest-touch restoration that resolves the scene-anchor gap without compromising the historical-density.
