## 4. Structural decisions

**A. TA §15.1.6 — restructure into two movements + a rationale (replaces the current blended ¶s at `:6794–6868`).**
- *Movement 1 — the structural account, and what stays open.* The framework DOES give a structural account of a commons (Def 1.1: a shared good subject to scarcity dynamics; for material commons a stock Q with any regeneration rate ρ ≥ 0). What it declines is to *close the substantive enumeration*: the ten categories are illustrative examples surfaced across the eighteen cases, open at both ends — future-unimagined commons, and goods long taken for granted revealed as commons once scarce (air is the anchor). Ostrom/Sen/Nussbaum/Rawls lineage.
- *Movement 2 — open in content, disciplined in form.* The utility-theory / decision-theory analogy. The discipline lives in CIT + the Four Gates, not in the word "commons" or the shape of the thing. Reject the slogan "anything assigned a dollar is a commons" — the dollar is the output of admission, never the criterion.
- *Rationale (demoted from co-equal move):* the six-tradition catalogue (`:6824–6862`) becomes the *reason* both movements are made (tradition-neutrality), explicitly signposted as rationale.

The methodological point may remain as secondary support only. **HARD: no coercion/reparations dollar figure produced; first-person abstention only; no standing-gatekeeping** (see §8 + `feedback_coercion_pricing_first_person_abstention`).

**C. TA §5.5 (`:1385`) — light alignment only.** It already runs the freedom inversion correctly. Ensure terminology matches Movement 1's floor language (scarcity dynamics, not "finite"); no substantive rewrite.

**D. `:1755` gloss — split the two jobs.** Keep the methodological domain-routing ("the candidate commons comes from the tradition, not from the gates"); move/separate the political non-closure so the methodological fact and the political non-commitment stop sharing one clause.

## 6. Carry-forward inventory (canonical ground truth — all verified 2026-06-09)

**Framework structure:**
- **Definition 1.1 (Commons Resource), TA `:326–327`:** "a resource unit R ∈ 𝒞 … drawn from a shared stock Q with regeneration rate ρ ≥ 0. The framework prices extraction for all values of ρ." ρ = 0 → strictly non-renewable/irreversible; ρ > 0 → renewable, depletion only above the regeneration rate. **Therefore "finite" is the WRONG floor-word — it would exclude renewables. Use "subject to scarcity dynamics," not "finite."**
- **Autonomy commons (#10), TA `:592`:** "Non-coerced-choice capacity … Under abundance, choice is genuinely uncompelled …; under scarcity, costs of coercion-shield … become legible." No stock Q, no physical form — a commons purely by the inversion. (Companion: Epistemic commons `:586`.) Lineage: Kant 1785; Mill 1859; Sen 1999; Nussbaum 2011; Anderson 2017 *Private Government*.
- **§5.5 freedom inversion, TA `:1385`:** "the abundance of freedom masks coercion costs until freedom becomes scarce. The Commons Inversion Test's structural move — invert abundance to reveal masked costs — runs both directions." Backward application = legacy-effects pricing (Methods 1+2+3 applied backward).
- **§1.10 scope boundary, TA `:596–598`:** coerced extraction priced via the *legacy-effects pathway* (longevity gaps, wealth gaps, cultural-knowledge transmission disruption, intergenerational trauma); "the direct pricing of the coercion vector itself … remains methodologically unresolved in the reparations-economics field … outside this volume's scope."
