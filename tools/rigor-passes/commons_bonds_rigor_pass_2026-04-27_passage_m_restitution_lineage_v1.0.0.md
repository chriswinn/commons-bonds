A book whose section headings use restitution-vocabulary survives political-vocabulary regime shifts that would date a book whose section headings use reparations-vocabulary.

## §6. Axis 4 — The test-of-time / book-greatness question

### §6.1 Will inclusion (I-A) make the book stand the test of time?

**Methodological engagement durability:**
- Darity-Mullen wealth-gap-against-comparable-households methodology is the contemporary state of the art; this is genuinely the academic field the framework's CSD/B₁ extends. The methodology has been peer-reviewed, debated, refined across the 2010s-2020s.
- Even if specific-numerical-estimates change as new data accumulates, the METHODOLOGY (accumulating measurable mechanisms; separating accounting from political feasibility) is durable.
- The framework's specialization (extraction-community-context application) is a structurally distinct contribution that doesn't depend on the political moment.

**Risk: methodology obsolescence.** If a successor methodology supersedes wealth-gap-against-comparable-households (e.g., a more granular generational-mobility framework), Passage M's specific Darity-Mullen anchor could date. Mitigation: the multi-tradition framing (S-B') buffers this — restitution-law tradition + truth-and-reconciliation are also cited; methodological lineage isn't single-source-dependent.

### §6.2 Will the section make the book GREAT?

The framework book's greatness depends on whether it produces durable analytical leverage on extraction-economics problems. Passage M contributes to greatness by:

1. **Establishing intellectual humility** — "is not new methodology dropped into the literature without lineage" — reader trusts a framework that names its predecessors.
2. **Demonstrating cross-tradition engagement** — engaging reparations economics, restitution-law tradition, truth-and-reconciliation scholarship, environmental-justice damages literature shows the framework integrates across multiple academic communities rather than competing with them.
3. **Closing M11 Char 19** — the reparations economist (one of four most-load-bearing external-review audiences per academic-rigor full test pass §6.3) sees their literature engaged; their endorsement is more likely.
4. **Anchoring the methodology-vs-politics discipline** — passage's third paragraph (separating *what is owed* from *what is politically feasible to collect*) is load-bearing for Option C' political-philosophical-accommodation discipline; this discipline is cross-cutting framework vocabulary that Ch 9 (policy economy) builds on.

The section's REMOVAL would weaken book-greatness on all four axes. Inclusion contributes positively to greatness.
