## §1. Subject

**Candidate #7 — Deep-seabed mining as extraction-frontier case.**

Surfaced 2026-04-26 during Ch 7 holistic sweep. Deep-seabed polymetallic-nodule mining sits as a near-term real-world case that exercises the same structural features Ch 7's Mars-water and Europa-ocean scenarios stress-test in fiction:

- **High industrial-existential substitutability gap inputs**: cobalt, nickel, manganese, rare earths — exactly the renewable-transition critical-minerals Ch 9 Step 1 lists as high-IES-gap
- **Externality tail genuinely hard to bound**: deep-sea ecosystems whose recovery rates from disturbance are unknown on multi-decadal-to-multi-century timescales (Smith et al. 2008 *Nature Geoscience*; Jones et al. 2017 *Nature Ecology & Evolution* on Clarion-Clipperton-Zone ecosystems)
- **Cost-severance distance structural**: extraction in international-waters areas legally outside any state's territorial accountability framework
- **Asymmetric-regret pressure**: irreversibility of disturbance versus reversibility of waiting-and-studying

The case is also a **live policy front**:
- Clarion-Clipperton Zone (CCZ) — 4.5 million km² international-waters area in the eastern Pacific holding the largest accessible deposits
- International Seabed Authority (ISA) granted exploration contracts since 2014; held authority over commercial-mining-code negotiation since UNCLOS 1982
- The Metals Company (TMC) / Nauru-sponsored 2-year-rule trigger 2021 (Nauru-Ocean-Resources-Inc. invoked the UNCLOS provision requiring ISA to finalize regulations within 2 years of a sponsoring state's request, forcing the timeline)
- ISA mining-code negotiations active through 2024-2025 with deadline pressure
- Major mining states (China, Russia, Norway domestically engaging the question) signaling interest in proceeding

Q1: does the addition belong in the book at all? Q2: which Ch 9 placement (or alternative)?
