## §5. Notable findings

**Finding 1 — A "The Renewable Imperative" fails on M9 + M10 + M11 + §22.4 simultaneously.** The political-charge concern is module-supported across multiple axes: M9 (risk / capture by environmentalist-advocacy framing); M10 (Berggruen / academic-press review may flag as advocacy-leaning); M11 (pre-formed objections from political pre-categorization); §22.4 (applied-advocacy register inconsistent with chapter's actual Book-1 scope content). M3.4 (counterargument coverage) is also a fail because the title pre-loads counterargument before the chapter can engage. The author's "carry-over from before Book-1/Book-2 split" diagnosis is rigor-confirmed.

**Finding 2 — H "The Pricing Imperative" is the surprise leader at +12.** It preserves the original title's imperative-mood narrative weight (★★★ on M5 + M4.3) while replacing the politically-charged "renewable" with framework-derived "pricing." The result: zero ✗ marks, ★★★ across the rigor / publishing-path / critic-pressure / scope axes, ★★★ on the chapter-fit and framing-resonance modules. It is also the most direct natural pair with Ch 8's "What Things Actually Cost" — the two chapters together describe the diagnosis (Ch 8) and the imperative (Ch 9) of pricing.

**Finding 3 — J "The Honest Pricing Imperative" is academically strongest** (★★★ on M6 + M10 + M11) but slightly long for narrative impact (★★ on M5 + M2.5). Choose if academic-press review preference dominates over reader-engagement.

**Finding 4 — F "What a Society Would Do" is the strongest descriptive title** (★★★ on M2.4 + M4.7 + M5 + M2.5). Uses the chapter's own opening line ("the set of practical moves a society would make") as title. Slight academic-rigor weakness (★★ on M6 — informality).

**Finding 5 — B "Closing the Gap" + C "The Reachable Horizon" are strong second-tier candidates.** Both tied at +8. "Closing the Gap" wins on direct Ch 8 connection; "The Reachable Horizon" wins on chapter-internal-phrase-resonance. Either would work.

**Finding 6 — D "What the Arithmetic Demands" is academically strong** (★★★ on M6) but cool register (★★ on M5 dinner-table, M2.5). Choose if framework-arithmetic-emphasis matters most.

## §6. Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: H "The Pricing Imperative."**

Rationale:
- **Highest aggregate score (+12).** Strongest across all 14 modules combined.
- **Preserves the original title's narrative weight.** "Imperative" is doing real work in the original title — the chapter argues for an imperative response to Ch 8's diagnosis. Replacing only the politically-charged adjective preserves what worked while fixing what didn't.
- **Direct Book 1 alignment.** "Pricing" is the framework's central operation; the chapter is about what the framework's pricing logic demands. Title and content match.
- **Natural Ch 8 pairing.** "What Things Actually Cost" → "The Pricing Imperative" reads as a tight diagnosis-and-response pair.
- **Politically neutral.** "Pricing" carries no environmentalist / progressive register; engages free-market and skeptical readers on equal terms.
- **Minimal carry-over risk.** No applied-advocacy connotation; no ambiguity about Book 1 scope.

**Secondary recommendation: J "The Honest Pricing Imperative"** if academic-press / Berggruen-window submission preference dominates. Adds "Honest" for explicit framework-anchoring at the cost of slightly longer title and slightly weaker narrative impact.

**Tertiary recommendation: F "What a Society Would Do"** if the descriptive-rather-than-prescriptive register is preferred. Uses the chapter's own opening framing.

**Tertiary alternative: C "The Reachable Horizon"** if the literary-essayistic register is preferred. Uses the chapter's internal phrase; pairs with "entering wedges" framing.

**Not recommended:**
- **A "The Renewable Imperative"** (current) — fails M3.4 / M6 / M8 / M9 / M10 / M11 / §22.4 on political-charge / Book-1/Book-2-split-carry-over grounds.
- **E "From Diagnosis to Architecture"** + **G "Acting on the Number"** — both at +5; serviceable but not module-leaders.
- **I "The Accountability Imperative"** — narrower than H "The Pricing Imperative" without compensating advantage.
