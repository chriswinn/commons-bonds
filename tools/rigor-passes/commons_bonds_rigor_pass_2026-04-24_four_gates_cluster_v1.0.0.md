## §1. Executive summary

**RECOMMENDED: Option A — CONFIRM the four gates as cohort + RATIFY "Units Consistency" (T2 terminology decision) + ACKNOWLEDGE the naming inconsistency as load-bearing structural distinction (CIT is a TEST; the other three are CONDITIONS).** The Four Gates are not four parallel objects — CIT is the *content gate* (does this candidate cost satisfy commons-grounding?); Boundedness, Independence, and Units Consistency are *structural/mathematical conditions* the cost-function must satisfy. The naming asymmetry (acronym vs property-words) reflects the structural asymmetry; forcing uniform naming would obscure the distinction.

**Three rationales:**

1. **Structural asymmetry justifies naming asymmetry.** CIT is a test (action; methodology); the other three are mathematical conditions (property; state). Renaming for parallelism (e.g., "Boundedness Test" / "Independence Test" / "Units Consistency Test") would falsely suggest all four are tests of the same kind.

2. **"Units Consistency" wins T2 over "Dimensional Consistency."** "Units" is accounting-bookkeeping register (matches book voice); "Dimensional" is physics-register (academic but heavier). Both are correct mathematically; "Units" is craft-aligned. Resolves T2 decision.

3. **M12 audit: no prior-art collisions on gate names.** "Boundedness" + "Independence" are general mathematical-analysis terms (not framework-specific) — the framework uses them as common-mathematical-property labels, not as framework-coined terms. No M12 exposure.

## §3. Structural distinction: CIT vs the other three (decisive module)

### §3.1 What each gate does

| Gate | Object of test | Output | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CIT** | candidate cost | does it depend on a commons? (admissible / not) | **Methodological test** |
| **Units Consistency** | candidate cost | does it have the right units (dollars × time)? | **Mathematical condition** |
| **Boundedness** | candidate cost integrand | does the integral converge? | **Mathematical condition** |
| **Independence** | candidate cost vs. already-admitted Cᵢ | is it linearly/correlationally independent? | **Mathematical condition** |

CIT operates at the CONTENT level (is this thing a commons-grounded cost?). The other three operate at the FORM level (mathematical conditions for the cost-function to participate in the integral cleanly).

This is structurally analogous to the classical distinction in mathematics between *axiom of substance* (what objects we admit) and *axioms of form* (what mathematical conditions admitted objects must satisfy).

### §3.2 Why naming asymmetry honors this

- CIT named as a TEST (action) because it's an *operational methodology* applied at admission time. Reader runs CIT against a candidate cost.
- Boundedness, Independence, Units Consistency named as PROPERTIES because they're *static mathematical conditions* — the cost function either has the property or doesn't. There's no procedural test the reader runs; the property is a structural fact.

Forcing parallel naming ("Boundedness Test" + "Independence Test") would falsely suggest each is a procedural test of the same kind as CIT. This misframes what the gates do.

### §3.3 Verdict

The cohort works as four gates because they all gate-keep cost admission, but they do so at different layers (content + 3 mathematical-form). Naming honors the layer distinction.

## §4. T2 — "Dimensional Consistency" vs "Units Consistency"

### §4.1 The question

Both phrases describe the same mathematical condition: the cost must be expressed in commensurable units with the rest of the RCV integrand.

### §4.2 "Dimensional Consistency" case

- **Pro:** physics-register precision (dimensional analysis); academic gravitas.
- **Pro:** technically correct (dimensional analysis is the formal discipline).
- **Con:** physics-register heavy for accounting-bookkeeping book voice (M4 craft).
- **Con:** "Dimension" was the old construct-label for the (now-) commons categories; reusing the word in a different sense creates near-confusion.

### §4.3 "Units Consistency" case

- **Pro:** accounting-bookkeeping register (matches book voice).
- **Pro:** "units" is the universally-understood word for this concept; lay reader parses immediately.
- **Pro:** disambiguates from "dimensions" of commons (which under commons-reframing are now commons categories).
- **Con:** slightly less academically formal than "dimensional consistency."

### §4.4 M5 dinner-table test

- "Units Consistency" — *"the cost must be in dollars and time, like the rest of the equation."* Parses immediately.
- "Dimensional Consistency" — *"the cost must satisfy dimensional analysis with the rest of the equation."* Requires reader to know what "dimensional analysis" is.

### §4.5 M6 academic test

Both pass. Tech Appendix §L formal specification can use full mathematical-formalism vocabulary internally; main-text Ch 6 uses "Units Consistency" with no academic-rigor cost.

### §4.6 Verdict on T2

**"Units Consistency" wins on craft + dinner-table + register-coherence grounds.** "Dimensional" abandoned in favor of "Units." Resolves T2 decision.
