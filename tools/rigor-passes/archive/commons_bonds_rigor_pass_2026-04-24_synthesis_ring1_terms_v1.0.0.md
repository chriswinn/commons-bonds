## §4. Causal-chain end-to-end test

The framework's rhetorical spine per author's 2026-04-24 statement: *"the causal chain not landing means I lose the reader, reviewer, editor, et al at the very beginning."*

### §4.1 The full chain

```
Value Extraction (event)
        ↓
[AIT identifies Cᵢ that passes the four gates]
        ↓
Cost Severance (mechanism) operates — specific Cᵢ are severed
        ↓
Σᵢ Cᵢ integrated over time produces Residual Commons Value (RCV)
        ↓
CS = RCV − B (equation)
        ↓
Severed Cost (result) is what remains unaccounted-for
```

### §4.2 Narrative reading

**Chapter-level prose example (how the chain reads in book text):**

> *"When a coal company extracts value from McDowell County — coal itself, land use, community labor, future economic opportunity — the extraction event creates cost severance. Cost severance is the mechanism by which the costs of extraction get separated from the value-capturer and stay with the community, the ecosystem, and future generations.*
>
> *Which costs specifically? The framework uses the Abundance Inversion Test — AIT — to identify them. Each candidate cost is tested: if the underlying scarcity condition were inverted to abundance, would the cost vanish? If yes, it's scarcity-grounded and admits. Black-lung cost, community-transition cost, environmental-remediation cost, political-capture cost — each tested, each admits.*
>
> *The admitted costs (Cᵢ) are integrated over future time, weighted by substitutability and discounted, to produce the Residual Commons Value — RCV — of the extraction. For McDowell coal that's roughly $550/ton.*
>
> *The accountability bond posted by the extractor — B — covers some portion of that cost (reclamation bonds, worker's compensation, etc.). The gap is what the framework calls cost severance: CS = RCV − B.*
>
> *What remains unaccounted-for is the severed cost — the bill the community bears."*

**Chain-test verdict:** reads as a tight causal sequence. Each step flows from the previous. No inference gaps. The reader — labor lawyer, editor, reviewer, non-specialist — can follow the logic without requiring framework-internal jargon beyond the defined terms.
