## 4. Framework-meta pressure (applied once across full set)

### 4.1 Character 2 — Left critic ("reformed capitalism")

**Pressure:** "Why these 10 layers instead of a class-based structure? Your taxonomy is capital's way of reorganizing itself without naming the real division." Uniform pressure across the full set; does not differentiate per layer.

**Response:** The framework is not organized around who owns versus who works; it is organized around what gets captured and what gets severed across structurally-asymmetric allocations. A class-based taxonomy names one set of relationships (labor / capital). The abundance-layer set names the scarcity axes across which allocation asymmetries form, *including* labor / capital but also temporal, institutional, cohesion, informational, and agency asymmetries that a class-only lens under-prices. Cross-ideological traction (left + libertarian on Agency; cross-spectrum on Political, Informational, Ecological) is structural evidence that the layers are not capital-reorganizing — if they were, cross-spectrum critics would not find them useful.

**Implication for book:** Introduction must include one paragraph acknowledging the class-critique and positioning the framework as extending rather than replacing class-based analysis. Methodology appendix carries the full case.

### 4.2 X1 — Meta-Critic

**Pressure:** "Why is the framework's architecture this and not that? Is the 10-layer count load-bearing, or could it be 8 or 12?" Uniform across the set.

**Response:** AIT is load-bearing; the layer count is scaffolding per C9. The count emerged from repeated merger/primitiveness testing — 7 was the v1.24.0 state before Cohesion, Informational, and Agency were ratified via the full battery in v1.21.0. The set-completeness sweep in §5 below tests five additional candidates and finds four collapse to existing layers; one (cosmic/physical-law) survives primitiveness but sits outside Book One's human-allocation scope. The count is principled, empirical, and revisitable — rejected candidates remain testable under new framings per protocol §17.6.

**Implication for book:** Methodology appendix carries the empirical trace — which candidates passed or failed which gate, and why. Main text does not belabor the count; layer chapters introduce each layer's contribution without arguing for the full set each time.

## 5. Set-completeness sweep — §6.2 candidates

Each candidate run through merger gate (bilateral independence against nearest-adjacent layer[s]) and primitiveness gate (do revealed costs decompose into existing layers?). Candidates from Part 1 §6.2.

### 5.1 Social / relational

**Tested against:** Cohesion (primary), Demographic (secondary).

**Merger gate:** Case A (social/relational abundance, cohesion scarcity) — strong close friend networks in communities with low civic trust. Construction difficulty: strong interpersonal networks typically correlate with civic trust; cases like this exist but are uncommon and arguably are cohesion-present at a different scale (dyadic vs. collective). Case B (cohesion abundance, social/relational scarcity) — civically-trusting community with weak close friendships. Similar construction difficulty — research suggests cohesion-rich contexts generate relational opportunity. Bilateral independence FAILS under clean construction.

**Primitiveness:** Social/relational costs decompose into Cohesion (collective trust / civic infrastructure) + Demographic (kinship / cultural identity bonds). Revealed costs — isolation, relationship erosion, dyadic trust breakdown — are priced under these two layers.

**Verdict:** **MERGER with Cohesion (primary); Demographic covers kinship-specific cases.** Social/relational is a valid cross-layer concept, not a standalone layer.

### 5.2 Cognitive / psychological

**Tested against:** Agency (primary), Informational and Temporal (secondary).

**Merger gate vs. Agency:** Case A (cognitive/psychological abundance, agency scarcity) — impaired cognition or manipulation without external coercion. This is the behavioral-economics nudge scenario, which the Agency layer already scopes as coercion-via-choice-architecture. Case B (agency abundance, cognitive/psychological scarcity) — free choice under cognitive deficit. Decision under cognitive deficit is itself a scope instance of Agency (consent-under-constraint). Bilateral independence FAILS.

**Primitiveness:** Cognitive costs decompose into Agency (behavioral/choice-architecture coercion) + Informational (information to process) + Temporal (attention-as-time).

**Verdict:** **SUBSUMPTION under Agency (primary); Informational and Temporal secondary.**

### 5.3 Information / data

**Tested against:** Informational.

**Merger gate:** Information/data is a subset of Informational's scope (evidentiary asymmetry includes data asymmetry). No bilateral independence — any information-cost case is an Informational-layer case.

**Primitiveness:** Trivially decomposes into Informational.

**Verdict:** **SUBSUMPTION under Informational.**

### 5.4 Biological / species

**Tested against:** Ecological.

**Merger gate:** Case A (biological/species abundance, ecological scarcity) — high biodiversity with collapsed ecosystem services. Construction fails: biodiversity presupposes functioning ecosystems. Case B (ecological abundance, biological/species scarcity) — functioning ES with species collapse. Construction fails: ES depend on biodiversity (pollination, pest control). Bilateral independence FAILS.

**Primitiveness:** Biological/species costs decompose into Ecological (ecosystem services) + Temporal (evolutionary timescales for extinction-foreclosure).

**Verdict:** **SUBSUMPTION under Ecological; Temporal for extinction-horizon framing.**

### 5.5 Cosmic / physical-law

**Tested against:** all 10 layers.

**Merger gate:** No existing layer prices thermodynamic-irreversibility directly. No existing layer prices light-speed as an allocation constraint. Against Ecological (closest conceptual neighbor): Case A (physical-law scarcity, ecological abundance) — cosmic-heat-death horizon with intact ecosystems; constructible. Case B (ecological scarcity, physical-law abundance) — damaged ecosystems with intact thermodynamic conditions; trivially constructible. Bilateral independence PASSES.

**Primitiveness:** Thermodynamic entropy does not decompose cleanly into Temporal (time-structure) + Ecological (services). It is a distinct scarcity axis at the physical-law level.

**AIT check:** Under thermodynamic abundance (no entropy), all allocation is trivial and the tier evaporates. Under light-speed abundance (instantaneous communication/travel), spatial costs evaporate but that is Geographic territory. Thermodynamic passes AIT as scarcity-grounded.

**Scope check:** Book One is human-allocation-scale. Cosmic/physical-law costs operate at civilization / cosmological timescales where tier-decomposition is intellectually valid but does not serve the book's success criteria (labor lawyer 2039, individual worker with open eyes) at any useful resolution. Ch. 9 civilization-scale Foreclosure extension (rare-earth / off-Earth migration, deferred queue) is the closest book-scope touchpoint.

**Verdict:** **PASSES primitiveness; OUT-OF-SCOPE for Book One canonical layer set.** Held as revisitable — valid primitive layer candidate whose scope-appropriate integration is Ch. 9 extension territory or future Book Three.
