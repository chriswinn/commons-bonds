## §1. Executive summary

### §1.1 Hohfeld 1913 fundamental legal conceptions

Wesley Hohfeld 1913 *"Some Fundamental Legal Conceptions as Applied in Judicial Reasoning"* establishes the foundational right/duty + claim/obligation jural-correlative framework that political-philosophy + legal-philosophy + economics-of-rights have operated under for over a century. The framework's parallel usage of "intergenerational obligations" + "intergenerational claims" deploys Hohfeld's framework deliberately:

- **Right/Claim** = entitlement-side. Future generations *have a claim on* present extraction.
- **Duty/Obligation** = correlative duty-side. Present generations *have an obligation toward* future generations.

These are **jural correlatives** in Hohfeld's framework: every right corresponds to a duty; every claim corresponds to an obligation. Using both in framework discourse:
- Allows distinguishing duty-side analysis (Ch 6 ethics-philosophy framing) from entitlement-side quantification (Ch 4 + Ch 5 + Ch 9 + terms_index).
- Aligns with established political-philosophy + legal-philosophy + economics-of-rights tradition.

### §5.3 What this preserves

- **Parsimony** — minus 1 vocabulary item.
- **Single-term consistency** — chapter prose always uses one term.

### §5.4 Verdict for (β) Drop claims

**REJECTED.** Parsimony gain illusory (both terms are Tier A established vocabulary; +0 framework-vocabulary cost to keep both). Hohfeldian-dual loss substantial.
