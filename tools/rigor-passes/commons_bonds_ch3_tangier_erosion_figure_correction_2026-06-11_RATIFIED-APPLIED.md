## Verification against the primary source (done this session, 2026-06-11)

**Primary source:** Schulte, Dridge & Hudgins (USACE), "Climate Change and the Evolution and Fate of the Tangier Islands of Chesapeake Bay, USA," *Scientific Reports* 5:17890 (2015). Verified via the PMC full text (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4675185/; Nature URL https://www.nature.com/articles/srep17890 refused connection this session).

What the paper reports (1850–2013 record):

| Figure | Paper value | Conversion |
|---|---|---|
| Eastern Tangier Island shoreline retreat | **0.53 m/yr** | **≈ 1.74 ft/yr** ← the register's "~1.7 ft/yr" |
| Eastern Uppards shoreline retreat | 1.32 m/yr | ≈ 4.3 ft/yr |
| Interior tidal creeks | 0.301 m/yr | ≈ 1.0 ft/yr |
| Mean annual land loss, all islands | 3.41 ha/yr | ≈ 8.4 ac/yr |
| Landmass lost since 1850 | **66.75%** | — |
| Remaining land, 2013 (island group) | **319.35 ha** (from 875.33 ha in 1850) | **≈ 1.23 sq mi** ← the register's "~1.2 sq mi" |
| Abandonment projection | ~50 yr (mid-range scenarios); as few as 25 yr (high) | — |

**A 15 ft/yr (~4.5 m/yr) rate appears nowhere in the paper** (explicitly checked).

**Provenance of the "15 ft/yr" figure:** USACE-via-press, for the **exposed western shore** — Bay Journal (Aug 2022, "Tangier Island may receive $25 million...") reports "severe erosion of 16 feet per year" on the **western edge of Uppards** (north of the navigation channel, unprotected); press accounts also report the western shore of Tangier proper lost 25–30 ft/yr **before** the 1989–90 USACE seawall, and essentially nothing where the seawall stands since. So 15 ft/yr is a real, sourceable figure for the worst unprotected stretch — it is not the island-wide rate, and it is roughly 9× the peer-reviewed long-term average for the inhabited island's shoreline.

**Verdict: the register/ledger correction is RIGHT; the chapter's unqualified "fifteen feet a year" is WRONG as written (both occurrences), and the same sentence's "two square miles" overstates remaining land ~60%.** The projection clause ("uninhabitable within the lifetime of children currently in the school") is supported by the paper and stands as written.

*Precision note on the ledger's shorthand:* the ledger parenthetical "Tangier ~1.7 ft/yr exposed-max" compresses two figures awkwardly — ~1.7 ft/yr is the **paper average** (eastern Tangier proper); "exposed-max" is the register's instruction to reframe the 15-ft figure as the exposed-shore maximum. Optional Phase C bookkeeping: clarify that line to "Tangier ~1.7 ft/yr paper-avg / 15 ft/yr exposed-shore-max only."

*Lineage note:* both chapter occurrences pre-date the 2026-06-11 promotion (archive `Chapter__3_TheWaterman_REGRESSED_2026-06-11.md` lines 156 + 218). The register (2026-06-08, "Ch 3:156") flagged the figure but its STATUS block correctly says corrections were **not yet applied to chapter prose**; the promotion then carried the uncorrected figures into the canonical chapter. ## FINDING F-1 (HIGH — shippable factual error, publisher-facing)

**Occurrence 1 — line 169 (Tangier portrait paragraph):**
> "Tangier Island, in the middle of the Lower Bay, is **two square miles** of low-elevation marsh and sand, continuously inhabited since the 1770s. ... The island is also losing **about fifteen feet of shoreline a year** to erosion, and the projections suggest most of it will be uninhabitable within the lifetime of children currently in the school."

**Occurrence 2 — line 262 (the not-paid-for litany):**
> "...or for the menhaden the reduction fleet is removing from the food web his life depends on, or for **the Tangier shoreline going under at fifteen feet a year**, or for the daughter who has gone to nursing school in Norfolk because there was no boat for her to come back to."

### Options

**Option A — paper-average correction (minimal).** Replace the rate with the peer-reviewed average and fix the area. Line 169 becomes "...a bit over a square mile of low-elevation marsh and sand..." and "...losing, on the long-term average, nearly two feet of shoreline a year to erosion — a rate that never takes a year off — and the projections suggest..." Line 262: same substitution ("going under at nearly two feet a year").
*Cost:* loses the vivid press figure entirely; "nearly two feet" in the litany is rhythmically weaker.

**Option B — dual-figure reframe at line 169 (matches the Atlantic-essay execution).** Keep the 15-ft figure but attribute it honestly as the exposed-shore maximum, anchored by the paper's average and cumulative loss. Directional draft (exact wording is the author's per the author-redline discipline):
> "Tangier Island, in the middle of the Lower Bay, is a bit over a square mile of low-elevation marsh and sand, continuously inhabited since the 1770s. ... The island is also going under. The figure that travels in the press — fifteen feet of shoreline a year — describes the worst of the exposed western shore; the Army Corps scientists who reconstructed the islands' full record put the long-term average closer to two feet a year, with about two-thirds of the landmass already gone since 1850. The average never takes a year off. The projections suggest most of the island will be uninhabitable within the lifetime of children currently in the school."
*Gain:* keeps the strong number without overclaiming; both in-flight Atlantic redraft candidates (A + B, 2026-06-11) already execute exactly this reframe, so chapter and essay stay consistent. Conservative per the peer-review-defense discipline (anchor on the sourced average; the qualified max is still available).

**Option C — line 262 litany treatments** (pick one alongside A or B for line 169):
- **C1 — drop the numeral:** "...or for the Tangier shoreline going under, or for the daughter..." (cadence preserved; no rate claim at all)
- **C2 — accurate numeral:** "...or for the Tangier shoreline going under at nearly two feet a year, or for the daughter..."
- **C3 — keep fifteen with qualifier:** "...or for the unprotected Tangier shoreline going under at as much as fifteen feet a year, or for the daughter..." (defensible but the qualifier load is heavy for a litany beat)

### Recommendation

**Line 169 → Option B. Line 262 → C1.** (Plus the "two square miles" → "a bit over a square mile" fix, which rides whichever option is chosen — it is part of the same register item §2.10.)

### Reasoning

1. **The primary source is unambiguous.** No 15 ft/yr rate exists in the peer-reviewed record; the paper's averages are 0.53–1.32 m/yr. An unqualified island-wide "fifteen feet a year" is a factual error in publisher-facing nonfiction under the no-invented-facts rule, and Ch 3 is queued to ship externally.
2. **Option B keeps the rhetorical asset honestly.** The 15-ft figure is real (USACE-via-press, exposed western shore) and vivid; attributing it as the exposed-shore maximum alongside the ~2 ft/yr average and the two-thirds-since-1850 cumulative loss is *more* frightening, not less — the average never takes a year off. This is the execution both Atlantic redraft candidates already carry, so ratifying B keeps the book and its derivative essay telling the same story with the same numbers.
3. **C1 over C2/C3 at the litany.** The litany's power is its unbroken register; a qualifier (C3) breaks it and an accurate-but-small numeral (C2) deflates it. Once line 169 establishes the corrected frame, "the Tangier shoreline going under" needs no rate — and repeating an unqualified "fifteen feet" at 262 would silently re-assert the wrong island-wide figure two sections after the text corrected it.
