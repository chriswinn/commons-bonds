## §1. What this pass IS (and what it is NOT)

**This pass IS** the whole-chapter developmental-edit lens: emotional-arc
continuity; scene-anchor density across the chapter; sensory-detail
restoration where compression read as flatness; voice-flow continuity
across section breaks; cumulative-LLM-cadence residue after per-paragraph
Pass-2 fixes; reader-engagement drops at the chapter's load-bearing
analytical pivots.

**This pass is NOT:**

- Pass 3.1 fact-check (no new facts surfaced; no re-litigation of Pass 1
  dispositions).
- Pass 3.2 voice-polish (no per-paragraph LLM-tic findings; Pass 2 caught
  what it was designed to catch — this pass operates at the scale where
  cumulative effects show up).
- Pass 3.3 acceptance / Pass 3.4 robustness (no per-character audience
  verdicts; the REAUDIT v3 verdict READY TO SUBMIT AS-IS is honored; this
  pass tests something different — whether the chapter's *experience-of-reading*
  matches what the character-set tests verified).
- Stage 4 render-integrity audit (render-side concerns are pipeline-side
  and tracked separately; see `render_pipeline_comparison_ch1_2026-05-18.md`).
- Stage 5 sign-off (pre-publication; not this pass's scope).

The developmental-edit lens is the closest thing the existing pipeline has
to a trade-press editor's full-chapter read. Pass 3.2 catches the tic at
the sentence; this catches whether the **cumulative effect** of multiple
tic-fixes produced flatness even though each fix was individually
justified. Pass 3.3 / 3.4 tests whether characters INCLUDE the chapter;
this tests whether the chapter holds reader attention through its
analytically-loaded passages.

See §6 (doctrine-amendment candidate flag) for whether this lens warrants
formal codification as a Pass 3.5 or as a Stage 4-equivalent
developmental-editing gate.

### F-DE-Ch1-1 — Section 4 (free-market-economist / quiet-math) is
analytical-overlay between two rich scene-sections; reader attention
plausibly slips here (HIGH)

**Lines 78–86 (the whole §4 cluster):**

> "I was thirty-eight years old and on a plane heading north because the
> company I worked for had said paternity leave was over and FMLA leave
> had run out, and it was time to do what I was good at again. It was
> time to do my job. So I did.
>
> My employer was not cruel. My colleagues were not indifferent. I had
> built a career on showing people how the value of what I sold aligned
> with what they needed and how it would solve the issue they had walked
> into the meeting with. That ability was valuable to the company. The
> company did what its institutional protocols said to do — paternity
> leave ends; FMLA leave exhausts; back to work. The system did exactly
> what the system was designed to do. There was no villain in it.
>
> A free-market economist would say I made voluntary trade-offs. I did.
> I had options a coal miner in McDowell County does not have. I had
> elite technical skills, multiple employers, geographic mobility, and
> significant compensation. And I still ended up, that morning, on a
> plane heading north, trying not to think about a NICU in Savannah
> where my son was waiting to be held by a human, while my parents —
> eight hundred miles north of him and a continent away from me in any
> way that mattered — were visibly moving slower these days.
>
> I have done the quiet math we all eventually do. If I disappear for
> another few years, surfacing only for weddings and Thanksgivings, the
> odds are growing that the next flight home will be for something I
> cannot bear to name. These are the years I have with them. I intend
> to be present with them, to laugh with them, and to be here for them
> when there is no laughter left.
>
> I did not understand, at thirty-eight, on that plane, that this was
> the math the framework would later let me put a number on. I just
> knew the math was there."

**Why this flags.** Five paragraphs of analytical-overlay sit between
the chapter's two most-sensory sections — §3 closes with the dense
NIH-era + cable-TV-station compression (line 74 *"I did not understand
any of this as a framework when I was doing it"*), and §5 opens with
the NICU-mornings scene (line 90's single-pointed-meditation passage).
Between them, the chapter does substantial thesis-work: the no-villain
framing; the free-market-economist direct-address; the quiet-math
title-naming moment; the framework-foreshadow.

The chapter HAS scene-elements here (*"on a plane heading north"*;
*"that morning"*; *"on that plane"*) — but they appear as adverbial
anchors decorating thesis-statements rather than as scenes the reader
inhabits with the protagonist. The reader is told the protagonist is on
the plane; the reader is not invited back into the cabin's sensory
detail (sun, seatback, grip-under-the-seat, garment-bag-in-the-overhead
from line 11's opening).

The cumulative effect: the chapter's TITLE-NAMING moment ("quiet math")
arrives as authorial declaration of theme rather than as a discovery the
protagonist arrives at on the plane. This is the section where the
author's "flat / unemotional" diagnosis most plausibly lands — the
reader has just exited richly-sensory NIH/CEO material and is asked to
absorb five paragraphs of theme-work before re-entering scene at §5.

**Why this matters for emotional arc.** The chapter's structural design
is plane-bookend (opens line 11 plane-scene; closes line 106-108
plane-bookend "the plane was past the cloud line. The sun was up."). §4
sits at the precise mid-point and is the chapter's analytical climb to
the title-naming moment. If §4 detaches from the plane-cabin, the
plane-bookend loses force — the reader returns at line 106 to a
plane-cabin she had effectively left at line 78.

**Severity rationale.** (a) This is the chapter's analytical climb to the
title-naming moment — the highest-attention-load passage in the chapter;
(b) the reader plausibly disengages between the NIH/CEO density and the
NICU-mornings restoration — exactly where the author's flatness
diagnosis most likely originates; (c) every Pass-2 individual fix to
this section (F-V10 tautological-closer cut; F-V11 chiastic-coda held)
was individually justified, but the cumulative effect after Pass 2's
chiseling was a section that reads as theme-statement rather than
scene-grounded realization.

**Proposed restoration (author selects one option):**

- **A. Re-anchor §4 in the plane-cabin with one or two sensory beats
  woven into the analytical content.** Specifically: between the
  free-market-economist paragraph (line 82) and the quiet-math paragraph
  (line 84), add one beat of the cabin — the slight tilt of the wing as
  the plane banked, the second cup of coffee the flight attendant set
  down, the way the morning light from the window had moved across the
  seatback opposite, the unopened email on the laptop screen, the grip
  still wedged under the seat. The analytical work continues; but the
  reader stays in the cabin with the protagonist. Estimated addition:
  one paragraph (3–5 sentences) inserted between current paragraph 3 +
  current paragraph 4 of §4.

- **B. Convert the "quiet math" paragraph (line 84) from theme-statement
  to inner-monologue.** Currently the paragraph names the theme
  authorially (*"I have done the quiet math we all eventually do"*).
  Replacement-in-spirit: open the paragraph with the protagonist
  thinking specifically rather than declaring generically — e.g., *"I
  was doing the math in my head. If I disappeared for another few years
  — five years; ten — surfacing only for weddings and Thanksgivings,
  the odds were growing that the next flight home would be for something
  I could not bear to name. These were the years. I would be present.
  I would laugh with them. I would be there when there was no laughter
  left."* The math is happening in the protagonist's mind, on the plane,
  in inner-monologue register, rather than as authorial naming-of-the-
  theme. The chapter still earns the title from this passage; the
  reader earns it WITH the protagonist.

- **C. Both A + B applied together.** Restores cabin-anchor AND converts
  title-naming to inner-monologue. Heaviest rewrite; restores §4 most
  fully to scene-grounded register. Recommended if author wants the
  developmental-edit pass to produce its most substantive restoration.

**Cross-pass flag.** Restoration would NOT affect Pass 1 fact-check

### F-DE-Ch1-2 — Grandfather section (§2 first half) is under-built for
its lineage-load; the AuthorsNote names a faithfulness-theme Ch 1 barely

**Lines 26–33 (the grandfather first-half of §2):**

> "The man my son would never meet was my grandfather. They called him

> hours outside of work there. He worked his entire career at NASA

> he passed. He was a model maker in the wind tunnels — a title he was
> proud of — and an inventor with his name in the patent record. He had
> a way of solving things, my father said, things that other people
> would just give up on, walk past, or simply avoid because they were
> too difficult.
>
> My father visited him once and noticed the air compressor was moved
> from its normal location. […air-compressor + drill-bits scene…]
>
> Years after this, my sister had a son. […Pou-nickname-passing
> scene…]"

**Why this flags.** The grandfather is the chapter's lineage-source for
two things the book carries: (a) the see-clearly-and-do-the-work skill
the author later names as the bridge from NIH-era to cable-TV-rescue at
line 72 (*"the one my grandfather used when he sharpened the drill
bits"*), and (b) the FAITHFULNESS-of-the-MODEL theme the AuthorsNote
names explicitly:

> *AuthorsNote line 13:* "Model-making at Langley meant building scale
> aircraft and spacecraft components — wood, metal, resin, accurate to
> a thousandth of an inch — so the wind tunnel would tell the truth
> about full-size designs that did not yet exist. … His tools and
> systems had to work in spaceflight, where reality is unforgiving and
> the cost of being wrong is borne by people who cannot recover from
> the error."

The AuthorsNote then makes the book-level claim: *"This book is a model.
The framework I have built across ten chapters is an instrument for
measuring what extraction actually costs. Like the work my grandfather
did, its value depends on whether it was built faithfully enough that
the world will not lie when run against it."*

The AuthorsNote is paratextual and lands the connection; the chapter
should establish the FAITHFULNESS-of-the-MODEL theme at memoir-register
in body prose, so the AuthorsNote's later claim has its lineage-anchor
already on the page. Currently the chapter mentions wind tunnels in
passing (*"a model maker in the wind tunnels — a title he was proud
of"*) but does not render WHAT model-making at Langley meant or WHY the
faithfulness discipline mattered. The chapter's grandfather is rendered
through ONE anecdote (the air-compressor + drill-bits scene) — which
demonstrates the willingness-to-do-the-work skill but does not
demonstrate the faithfulness-to-the-truth-of-the-model skill that the
AuthorsNote names.

**Source-substrate available.** The author's raw-dump material
(`research/story-drafts/ch1_becoming-ceo_2026-05-01.md` lines 50–52)
includes vivid grandfather material that was cut during compression:

- The wind-tunnels at Langley + speed-of-sound work (line 52: *"he is
  one of the people that created the wind tunnels at Langley… and
  actually figure out how to make one of the one tunnels for testing
  things going faster in a speed of sound"*).
- The model-making practice itself (line 52: *"he was a model maker
  for making model models in the wind tunnel, and he would always
  design different things and see how they react inside the [wind]
  tunnel at different speeds"*).

Either or both of these substrate beats could anchor a one-paragraph
addition to the grandfather section that establishes the
faithfulness-of-the-model theme.

**Why this matters for emotional arc.** The chapter teaches the reader
to care about the grandfather through one anecdote (air-compressor +
drill-bits) before the §-break to the father-pedagogy section. The
reader is asked to absorb the lineage-source quickly. Adding one
paragraph of texture — specifically the model-making + wind-tunnel
discipline that the AuthorsNote later names — would (a) give the
grandfather more emotional weight as a lineage source; (b) plant the
faithfulness-of-the-model seed at body-prose register, so the
AuthorsNote's book-as-model claim has its memoir-anchor on the page;
and (c) restore the grandfather's substantive contribution to the
chapter's framework-precursor lineage (currently the chapter shows
grandfather-as-problem-solver but not grandfather-as-faithful-measurer).

**Severity rationale.** (a) The grandfather is the chapter's lineage-
source for the FAITHFULNESS theme that the book itself depends on
(AuthorsNote frames the entire AI-rigor-pipeline rationale through
this metaphor); (b) the chapter's current rendering is the most
compressed of the chapter's character-sections (one anecdote-per-
generation; father gets three scenes; grandfather gets one); (c) the
register-split with the AuthorsNote (formal name; full bio) makes the
chapter's anonymized-Pooh rendering even more dependent on showing
WHAT the grandfather did, since the chapter can't lean on the
patent-record + L. E. Winn formal-naming the AuthorsNote provides.

**Proposed restoration (author selects one option):**

- **A. Add one paragraph between the wind-tunnels mention (line 28) and
  the air-compressor anecdote (line 30) — establishing what model-making

  cannot recover from the error."* This anchors the FAITHFULNESS-of-the-
  MODEL theme at body prose register. Mirrors AuthorsNote line 13
  closely but compresses to memoir-register; preserves the register-
  split (no formal name; "Pooh" intimate-nickname-only in Ch 1).

- **B. Add the model-making-wind-tunnel beat at lighter weight (1–2
  sentences only).** Less load on the chapter's §2 opening; preserves
  compression-discipline. Draft: *"What he did in the wind tunnels was
  build the world in miniature, accurate to a thousandth of an inch, so
  the tunnel could tell the truth about a full-size design that did not
  yet exist."* The faithfulness-of-the-model is implied through the
  *"so the tunnel could tell the truth"* phrasing; the discipline-of-

- **C. Hold as-is.** Defensible: the AuthorsNote carries the

**Recommended:** Option A (add the faithfulness-of-the-model paragraph).

**Cross-pass flag.** Restoration would NOT contradict Pass 1 fact-check

analytical pivot's emotional weight is absorbed into the next
paragraph's substrate texture.

chapter's analytical pivot. Without breath around it, the reader
processes it as one-beat-among-many in a fast-moving section. With
breath (a §-break; a one-sentence paragraph; or a return to the plane
where the protagonist is having the thought), the reveal becomes the
load-bearing pivot it is. Similarly, the cable-TV-station-rescue +
grandfather-bridge cluster at lines 70–72 is a substantive scene that
deserves its own space rather than appearing as a parallel to NIH.

**Severity rationale.** (a) The both/and reveal is the chapter's most
important analytical move; (b) §3 is the chapter's longest section by
substrate-count; (c) the compression appears load-bearing for the
chapter's overall length-discipline but cumulatively reads as
narratively-flat (lots of memorable beats in fast succession; none
fully landing); (d) the author's "over-compressed" diagnosis most
likely originates here as much as at §4.

  > That pattern I learned at the NIH would, twenty years later, cost me
  > my marriage and the early years with my son.
  >
  > ---
  >
  > I billed over a hundred and twenty hours a week for stretches…"
