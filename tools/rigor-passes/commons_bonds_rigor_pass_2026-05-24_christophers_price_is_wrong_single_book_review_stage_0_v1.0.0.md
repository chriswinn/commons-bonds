### 3.2 Reveal-discipline risk surface — SHARPER than sibling

The risk for THIS candidate is materially sharper than for the sibling multi-book essay. Three compounding factors:

**Risk factor (i) — Argument-depth pressure.** A 4,000–8,000-word single-book review-essay reviewing *The Price Is Wrong* will engage Christophers' mechanism-level argument at chapter-by-chapter depth (the renewable-finance case-material; the asset-manager interviews; the BlackRock-vs-Vanguard analysis; the structural-vs-cyclical investment-incentive critique; the public-vs-private capital allocation argument). The reviewer's analytical position — to be worth reading — must extend or critique Christophers' argument at the same depth. **That depth is precisely where the framework's apparatus would naturally surface.** The reviewer who reads Christophers' "profit, not price" diagnosis and writes a generous, depth-engaged review faces direct pressure to name the corrective instrument the diagnosis demands. Christophers names the disease (pricing alone cannot redirect capital). The framework names the cure (accountability instruments that internalize the cost severance the price-and-profit gap creates). Sticking to plain-prose-corrective-vocabulary-only at this depth requires explicit Stage 1 brief discipline.

**Risk factor (ii) — Single-author engagement focuses attention.** The sibling multi-book essay distributes reviewer-attention across four authors; no single author's reception receives 100% of the engagement. THIS candidate's reception IS Christophers' engagement. Any reveal slippage lands with full weight on the Christophers-as-blurber relationship (per §4(e) below). The blurber-risk surface is concentrated rather than distributed.

**Risk factor (iii) — Review-essay genre convention.** Single-book reviews in NYRB / LARB / Public Books / Phenomenal World long-form follow a register where the reviewer's positive contribution is foregrounded more aggressively than in a multi-book synthesis (single-book reviews are expected to take a position on the book under review; multi-book essays can hide behind synthesis). The reviewer's position on *The Price Is Wrong* will be visible. If the position is "Christophers' diagnosis demands a corrective instrument he does not construct, and here is where one would begin" — the reviewer is closer to naming the framework's territory than the sibling's "across these four diagnoses no one has yet operationalized the corrective" framing.

**Three scope-discipline calibrations protect the ≤8% target:**

- **Calibration 1 — Negative-space construction (tighter than sibling).** The essay constructs the "what's missing in Christophers" punchline as a **negative-space observation** (Christophers' diagnosis identifies the price-and-profit gap; the question of what instrument would internalize the gap is named but not built; reasonable people building such an instrument would face the per-extraction-event mechanism-design questions Christophers' macro-level argument leaves open). The reader infers that someone could build the corrective; the essay does not name the builder or the apparatus.
- **Calibration 2 — Reviewer-not-architect register (tighter than sibling).** The essay's voice is reviewer-engaging-Christophers-at-depth, NOT framework-architect-using-Christophers-as-foil. The single-book genre's register-pressure toward architect-voice is the dominant risk; Stage 1 brief must specify this explicitly.
- **Calibration 3 — Christophers' own corrective moves carry the constructive register.** Christophers' constructive moves (public ownership, public investment, public-purpose capital allocation) are themselves substantial; the essay's constructive close engages those as the corrective register, NOT the framework's per-extraction-event mechanism. The reviewer can note that Christophers' macro-architectural moves are necessary but ALSO note that the per-extraction-event mechanism question Christophers' diagnosis raises is a complementary site for corrective-instrument design — without naming what such instruments would look like at apparatus depth.

Per Stage 1 brief discipline (if Stage 0 verdict converts to GO): apparatus-exclusion list embedded explicitly; concept-only register for cost-severance + pricing-gap + accountability-architecture; reveal-target ≤8% verified at Pass 3.3 with cumulative-portfolio audit; **tighter than sibling on Calibration 1 + 2 + 3 to absorb the higher-depth pressure**.
