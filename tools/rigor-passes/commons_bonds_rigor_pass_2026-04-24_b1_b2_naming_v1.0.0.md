## §1. Executive summary

**RECOMMENDED:**
- **B1 — adopt "Restitution Bond"** as Ring-2 sub-instrument name. Replaces "Reparations Bond" working label.
- **B2 — adopt "Foreclosure Bond"** as Ring-2 sub-instrument name. Replaces "Resource-Foreclosure Bond" working label.

**Decisive rationale:**

1. **B1 = "Restitution Bond" outperforms "Reparations Bond" on M11 critic-survival + adoption-bandwidth.** "Reparations" carries strong political-loading that narrows framework's adoption channels — many readers (especially center-right + libertarian + politically-cautious institutional audiences) tune out at "reparations." "Restitution" is legal-register-clear, captures the same backward-looking-compensation function, and has broader cross-political-tradition adoption potential. Per Option C' political-philosophical-acknowledgment ratification 2026-04-24: framework accommodates rather than asserts political-traditional commitments.

2. **B2 = "Foreclosure Bond" outperforms "Resource-Foreclosure Bond" on M4 craft + M5 dinner-table + framework-coherence.** "Foreclosure" already operates in framework vocabulary (Foreclosure Cost C₁ in RCV integrand). Naming the bond after the cost-component it internalizes creates clean term-pair coherence (Foreclosure Cost paired with Foreclosure Bond). Shorter; cleaner; pairs with existing framework vocabulary.

3. **Pair-coherence across the framework:** the recommended names create a clean conceptual/grammatical structure:
   - **Backward-looking pair:** Cost Severance Damages (CSD) ↔ Restitution Bond (B1).
   - **Forward-looking pair:** Residual Commons Value (RCV) ↔ Foreclosure Bond (B2).
   - **Aggregate:** Cost Severance (CS) = (CSD − B1) + (RCV − B2) = (CSD + RCV) − (B1 + B2).

4. **M12 audit:** both proposed names are framework-specializations within established legal/economic vocabulary (restitution = legal-economics term for restoring; foreclosure = framework's own term per Foreclosure Cost C₁). Honest extension claim.

5. **Framework's adoption strategy preserved:** B1 + B2 sub-instrument names are Ring-2 (framework-internal); adoption-bet stays focused on Ring-1 flagships (Severed Cost, Cost Severance, RCV, AIT/CIT, etc.).

**Options tested per sub-instrument:** detailed in §3 (B1) + §4 (B2) below.

### §3.3 Top-tier B1 candidates analysis

Three candidates emerge as defensible: **Reparations Bond**, **Restitution Bond**, **Damages Bond**.

**Reparations Bond:**
- Pro: strongest direct lineage to active scholarly tradition (Darity & Mullen 2020); makes framework's relationship to reparations economics maximally explicit.
- Con: **politically loaded** — narrows framework's adoption to politically-progressive audiences; center-right + libertarian + politically-cautious institutional audiences may tune out before engaging the framework's mechanism-level claim.
- Per Option C' political-philosophical-acknowledgment discipline (ratified 2026-04-24): framework accommodates political traditions rather than asserting commitments.

**Restitution Bond:**
- Pro: legal-register-clear; restitution = "restoring what was taken" — captures the same compensation-for-realized-harm function; cross-political-tradition adoption potential.
- Pro: restitution-law has its own lineage (legal economics; toxic-tort + class-action-damages tradition; truth-and-reconciliation processes); framework can cite multiple traditions.
- Pro: narrower political-loading; reaches conservative + libertarian audiences who'd disengage from "reparations" while still pulling reparations-economics readers into engagement.
- Con: less direct lineage to Darity & Mullen specifically (still cited in Tech Appendix; but name is less of a beacon).
- Con: may be perceived by politically-progressive readers as politically-cautious dilution of "reparations" framing — risk of looking like sanitization.

**Damages Bond:**
- Pro: cleanest legal-register; toxic-tort + class-action-damages tradition is the most-applicable established methodology.
- Pro: low political-loading.
- Con: "damages" is narrower than "restitution" — restitution can include rights-restoration, dignity-restoration, structural-correction beyond pure monetary damages.
- Con: framework's CSD is broader than monetary damages (life-expectancy gap, intergenerational mobility loss, community-cohesion erosion — not all monetizable as classical damages).

### §3.4 B1 verdict

**RECOMMENDED: Restitution Bond.**

Rationale:
- **M5 + M6 strong.** Legal-register-clear; cross-political-tradition adoption.
- **M11 critic survives.** Restitution is broader legal concept; framework's CSD-restoration-spanning-monetary-+-non-monetary-harms fits restitution scope better than damages scope.
- **Political-loading optimal.** Per Option C' discipline: framework accommodates political traditions. "Restitution" reaches both reparations-economics audiences (who recognize the restitution-tradition connection) AND politically-cautious audiences (who'd disengage at "reparations"). Wider adoption-bandwidth.
- **M12 honest.** Restitution-law tradition + reparations-economics + truth-and-reconciliation + toxic-tort all cited as adjacent lineages; framework's specialization is the extraction-community-context application.

**Fallback options:** "Damages Bond" (cleaner legal-register; narrower scope) OR "Reparations Bond" (strongest direct-lineage signal but politically narrower).
