## 2. Findings

### F-FC-AeonC-1 [LOW] — "the analog at Devon Island or HI-SEAS"

- **Severity:** LOW (tightens defensibility; fact-checker would flag in
  pre-publication review; Aeon editor unlikely to catch on first-screen).
- **Location:** Para 1, line 41 (Version C file), verbatim:
  > *"Imagine landing at a closed habitat — Mars, the Moon, an orbital
  > station, the analog at Devon Island or HI-SEAS."*
- **Source-of-truth.** Stage 1 §7.2 carry-forward inventory:
  > *"Mars, the Moon, an orbital station, the Earth-side analogs at Devon
  > Island or HI-SEAS."*
- **Diagnosis.** Two specific drifts from Stage 1 canonical wording:
  (a) the **"Earth-side"** qualifier is dropped. Without it, a literal reader
  scanning the list may parse "the analog at Devon Island or HI-SEAS" as a
  fourth closed-habitat *type* parallel to Mars / Moon / orbital station,
  rather than as a category-shift to terrestrial analog facilities.
  (b) **"analogs" (plural) → "analog" (singular)**, while two distinct
  facilities are then named. Devon Island hosts FMARS (Flashline Mars Arctic
  Research Station, Mars Society, est. 2000, Nunavut, Canada). HI-SEAS is the
  Hawaii Space Exploration Analog and Simulation, run by University of
  Hawaiʻi at Mānoa on Mauna Loa, est. 2013. These are two separate Mars-
  analog facilities, not one analog with two locations or two names. A reader
  fluent in the Mars-analog program (likely overlap with the Aeon Philosophy
  Prize judges and the Mulgan/Parfit-adjacent Aeon-reader cluster) could note
  the imprecision.
- **Proposed correction (preferred — zero-word-cost; preserves 300w total):**
  > *"Imagine landing at a closed habitat — Mars, the Moon, an orbital
  > station, an analog like Devon Island or HI-SEAS."*

  ("the analog at" → "an analog like" — 3 words → 3 words; net 0.)
- **Alternative correction (more faithful to Stage 1; +2w cost):**
  > *"Imagine landing at a closed habitat — Mars, the Moon, an orbital
  > station, an Earth-side analog like Devon Island or HI-SEAS."*

  (Net +2w → total 302w. Still under Stage 1 hard cap 320w; over Stage 1
  target 300w by 2.)
- **Cross-pass impact.**
  - **Pass 3.2 (voice-polish):** the preferred correction "an analog like"
    avoids the singular/plural mismatch and reads cleaner cadence-wise; voice-
    polish unlikely to revisit either replacement.
  - **Pass 3.3 (audience-load acceptance):** unaffected — neither the
    Mazzucato/Raworth-cluster reader (Tier 2 #7) nor the McDowell County
    resident (Tier 3 #12) reads this line as load-bearing.
  - **Pass 3.4 (audience-load robustness):** unaffected — adversarial
    characters (industry-funded; Public-Choice; ideologically-opposed) do not
    weaponize this line.

### F-FC-AeonC-2 [LOW] — "Built outside the academy across a decade"

- **Severity:** LOW (paraphrase-precision tightening; defensibility flag for
  the analytic-economics audience).
- **Location:** Para 2, line 45 (Version C file), verbatim:
  > *"Built outside the academy across a decade, the framework names patterns
  > through which costs get hidden..."*
- **Source-of-truth.** Stage 1 §7.8 bio (which is itself the canonical-truth
  source for the author's framework-development context):
  > *"He built the framework this essay applies across a decade of consulting
  > work that took him through fifty-eight countries... He is not an
  > economist. The framework grew from inside the systems it engages, not
  > above them."*
- **Diagnosis.** Version C paraphrases the bio's "not an economist" +
  "framework grew from inside the systems it engages, not above them" as
  **"outside the academy."** The paraphrase is broader than the bio strictly
  supports — "outside the academy" implies zero academic affiliation across
  the decade, whereas the bio claims only (i) non-economist status and (ii)
  framework-origin from inside the systems engaged. A literal reader (e.g.,
  the Coasean economist at Stage 1 §1 Tier 2 #8 — the audience explicitly
  flagged for testing the "voluntary trade-offs" defense and universalizability
  claim) might press: outside *which* academy? Economics specifically? All
  scholarly fields? If the author held any adjunct, executive-education, or
  consultancy-affiliated academic role during the decade, the literal "outside
  the academy" claim could be challenged.
- **Proposed correction (author-affirmation gate first; remediation only if
  affirmation negative):** This is an *author-affirmable* fact. If the author
  can confirm zero academic affiliation during the framework-building decade,
  **no fix needed** and this finding can be closed. If any academic
  affiliation existed during that decade, tighten to one of:
  - *"Built across a decade outside academic economics, the framework names
    patterns..."* (+2w; total 302w)
  - *"Built across a decade of work outside the academy, the framework names
    patterns..."* (+1w; total 301w; mild rephrasing)
- **Cross-pass impact.**
  - **Pass 3.2 (voice-polish):** unlikely to revisit; current phrasing has
    good cadence ("Built outside the academy across a decade" is rhythmic);
    if remediated, voice-polish may have a preference between the two
    alternatives.
  - **Pass 3.3 (audience-load acceptance):** unlikely to flip any audience
    verdict.
  - **Pass 3.4 (audience-load robustness):** the Coasean-economist /
    Public-Choice / industry-funded adversarial set might note the
    imprecision but unlikely to weaponize it (the bio's clarifying context
    is paratextual to the pitch). LOW-severity adversarial flag, not a
    structural thread.
