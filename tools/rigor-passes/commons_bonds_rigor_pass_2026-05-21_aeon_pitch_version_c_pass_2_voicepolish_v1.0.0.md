## §1. Pass 3.2 scope reminder

Per Stage 3 template, the named LLM-tic + voice-issue inventory:

- **Rule of three** — sentence-level "A. B. C." constructions; flag if >2.
- **Em-dash crutches** — em-dashes used as connective tissue rather than
  deliberate parenthetical extension or punch.
- **Tidy parallels** — "He did X. She did Y. They did Z." structures.
- **Hedge phrases** — "I will argue that…" / "It seems likely that…"
- **Connective-tissue clichés** — "in short" / "ultimately" / "moreover" /
  "furthermore" / "that being said"
- **Meta-commentary** — "That is the whole sentence." / "What I mean to
  say is…" / stage-direction phrasing
- **Expository flatness** — "The plain definition is this:" / "The
  argument is simple:"
- **Declarative-three-in-a-row** — three short declaratives in succession
  with no sentence-length variance.
- **Nostalgia / sentimentality tics** — "There are not many people like
  that anymore" / "Back when…"
- **Cadence repetition** — "It changed me. It humbled me. It made…"
  patterns.
- **Apparatus residue** — Greek letters, subscripts, integrals, acronyms
  where Stage 1 §10 prohibits.
- **Grammar / register-break flags** — subject-verb agreement; tense
  consistency; pitch-register breaks toward query-letter or
  academic-defensive register.

  defensibility-tightening option; cumulative-cadence concern.
- **LOW** — style preference; tightening option; substantively-earned
  named pattern; hold-as-is defensible.

### F-V-AeonC-1 — Singular-verb-with-compound-subject in Para 1 (MEDIUM) [Pass 3.1 §5.4 carry-forward]

- **Location:** Para 1 visual second paragraph (file line 43), verbatim:
  > *"The abundant heat, light, water and even the air **is** free again
  > — all of it suddenly costless."*
- **Diagnosis.** Compound subject (*"heat, light, water and even the
  air"* — four enumerated items) governs singular verb (*"is"*). Strict
  prescriptivist subject-verb agreement expects plural verb *"are."* The
  trailing coda *"all of it suddenly costless"* (with singular pronoun
  *"all of it"*) confirms the construction reads the four items as a
  unified collective abstraction (the ambient-resource mass) — the
  **collective-singular** literary construction is the deliberate move,
  not a grammar error. But the construction is visible: a careful reader
  scans the verb-and-list mismatch before parsing the collective-singular
  defense; trade-press copyeditors are calibrated to flag the pattern.
  Aeon editor (Haselby — literary-philosophical, thought-experiment-
  tolerant) likely reads as deliberate; pre-publication copyeditor would
  flag for review.
- **Options.**
  - **A. Hold as-is.** Collective-singular construction; the *"all of
    it"* coda confirms unified-mass reading; defensible as deliberate
    literary move. Aeon editor reads as on-register; copyeditor concern
    sits in post-acceptance editing territory, not pitch-screening
    territory. Word count: 300w preserved.
  - **B. Pluralize the verb.** *"The abundant heat, light, water and
    even the air **are** free again — all of it suddenly costless."*
    Strict subject-verb agreement; loses the collective-mass unity
    signaled by the singular verb. Tension: the coda *"all of it"*
    (singular) now disagrees with the head verb (plural), shifting the
    grammar-strict question one step downstream rather than resolving
    it. Word count: 300w preserved.
  - **C. Restructure to sidestep the verb.** *"The abundant heat, light,
    water and even the air — all of it free again, all of it suddenly
    costless."* Drops the verb entirely; uses em-dash to lift the list
    into the appositive; preserves singular *"all of it"* throughout.
    Resolves grammar question; adds a sixth em-dash to the body (see
    F-V-AeonC-2 cross-pass impact). Word count: 300w + 1w = 301w (still
    well under hard cap 320w).
  - **D. Restructure with parallel singulars.** *"The abundant heat is
    free, the light, the water and even the air — all of it suddenly
    costless."* Repeats the singular structure across each item; reads
    as deliberate cadence-rhythm; resolves the verb question by aligning
    each subject as singular. Heavier rewrite; changes the sentence's
    feel; minor word-count effect (+1w to 301w).
- **Recommendation.** **A — Hold as-is.** The collective-singular
  construction is on-register for the literary-philosophical Aeon editor
  and substantively earns its place (the *"all of it suddenly costless"*
  coda only lands cleanly under singular reading of the antecedent). The
  construction is the kind of move Mulgan, Sandel, or MacAskill would
  make in trade-philosophy prose: a deliberate departure from strict
  agreement that signals collective-abstraction.
- **Reasoning.** Per memory `feedback_dual_audience_test.md`, test both
  audiences. Layman (Mazzucato/Raworth-cluster trade reader) parses
  *"heat, light, water and even the air"* as a unified ambient-resource
  mass and registers the singular verb as natural collective-singular;
  specialist (Coasean economist; Aeon Philosophy Prize judges) registers
  the construction as deliberate-literary, not solecism. Per the prior
  comparison §1.6, Version C wins decisively on voice-register / LLM-tic
  exposure against B — and the singular-verb construction is part of what
  produces that win (the construction's literary intentionality is a
  Mulgan/Sandel/MacAskill-register signal). Changing the construction at
  this stage risks register-drift toward neutral-copyedit register.
  Submission gate: Aeon editor screen-read is the load-bearing audience,
  not the eventual copyedit pass.
- **Cross-pass impact.**
  - **Pass 3.3 (acceptance):** unaffected — neither
    Mulgan/Sandel/MacAskill-cluster reader nor McDowell County resident
    reads this line as load-bearing. Aeon editorial brain reads on-
    register either way.
  - **Pass 3.4 (robustness):** unaffected — adversarial characters
    (industry-funded; Coasean; Public-Choice) do not weaponize a
    collective-singular literary construction.
- **Author disposition required.** This is the explicit Pass 3.1 §5.4
  carry-forward; author should ratify Option A explicitly to close the
  carry-forward, or select B/C/D if the grammar-strict reading is
  preferred.

### F-V-AeonC-4 — "structural test the same" tautological-echo coda in case 3 (LOW)

- **Location:** line 45 (Para 3 visual, case 3 close), verbatim:
  > *"A self-imposed commute lease whose hours of life were never
  > computed: same framework, no external bad actor, **structural test
  > the same**."*
- **Diagnosis.** The coda *"same framework, no external bad actor,
  structural test the same"* contains a tautological-echo: *"structural
  test the same"* repeats the *"same"* of *"same framework"* at the
  coda's opening. The Ch 1 Pass 2 F-V10 precedent (*"The architecture
  was the architecture"*) flagged the X-is-X tautological circular-
  framing as the named LLM-tic move where prose reaches for resonance
  through circular phrasing. Here the construction is milder — *"same
  framework... structural test the same"* is not quite X-is-X but is
  echo-of-X, repeating *"the same"* at frame-open and frame-close. A
  reader scanning for tics may register the echo as the reach-for-
  resonance move.
- **Options.**
  - **A. Hold as-is.** The three-clause coda is doing real substantive
    work (each clause demonstrates a property: same framework verdict
    applies → no external bad actor present → structural-test logic
    returns same result); the echo gives rhythmic emphasis to the
    universality-conclusion. Trade-press editors may read as deliberate.
    Word count: 300w preserved.
  - **B. Drop the third clause.** *"A self-imposed commute lease whose
    hours of life were never computed: same framework, no external bad
    actor."* Cuts the tautological echo; the *"same framework, no
    external bad actor"* two-clause coda still demonstrates universality
    (framework applies even with no bad actor). Loses the rhythmic
    emphasis; tightens the cadence. Word count: 300w - 4w = 296w.
  - **C. Replace third clause with substantive content.** *"A self-
    imposed commute lease whose hours of life were never computed: same
    framework, no external bad actor, no excuse."* Trades the
    tautological echo for a moral-register two-word punch closer; reads
    as deliberate-emphasis without circular phrasing. Tension: *"no
    excuse"* introduces moral-register the rest of the pitch avoids
    (per Stage 1 §7.4 *"pricing framework first, critique second"*).
    Word count: 300w - 2w = 298w.
  - **D. Rephrase to avoid the echo.** *"A self-imposed commute lease
    whose hours of life were never computed: same framework, no
    external bad actor, structural verdict identical."* Replaces *"the
    same"* with *"identical"*; preserves the three-clause structure;
    drops the echo. Word count: 300w preserved.
