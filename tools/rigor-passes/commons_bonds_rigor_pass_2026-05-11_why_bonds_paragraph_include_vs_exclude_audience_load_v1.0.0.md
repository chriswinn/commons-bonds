## Evaluation dimensions

For each character, the test asks: what reading experience does State A produce, what reading experience does State B produce, and which is better for that character?

**Symbol shorthand:**
- `INCLUDE ✓✓✓` = strong gain from inclusion (gating-audience-load-bearing)
- `INCLUDE ✓✓` = real gain from inclusion
- `INCLUDE ✓` = mild gain from inclusion (accessibility / nice-to-have)
- `NEUTRAL ~` = no load-bearing change either way
- `EXCLUDE ⚠` = paragraph hurts this audience (would push toward dropping)

## M11 critic-survival probes against INCLUDE

### Probe A — "The paragraph is over-defended. Why argue for *bond* when no chapter has been pressing on the choice?"

The choice is foundational; the title carries it; the entire apparatus depends on it. A foundational choice that's never argued is a load-bearing assertion. Mazzucato argues "value" in body prose (Ch 1 of *The Value of Everything*); Raworth argues "doughnut" (Ch 1 of *Doughnut Economics*); Sen argues "capabilities" (multiple essays). Defending the umbrella concept is what serious institution-design books do.

### Probe B — "The defense is too brief — one paragraph isn't enough."

The defense is appropriately scoped to one paragraph because (a) the chapters and Tech Appendix do extensive concrete work with the bond instrument elsewhere; (b) the paragraph slots into a chapter that's already long; (c) a longer treatment would crowd out the lineage paragraphs that follow. One paragraph that names alternatives, rejects them structurally, exhibits operations, and names traditions is sufficient.

### Probe C — "The paragraph names *sovereign wealth fund* but the framework's term is *Foreclosure Bond* — terminological mismatch."

This is exactly the point. The paragraph argues that the various traditional terms (sovereign wealth fund, reclamation bond, decommissioning bond, reparations) all converge on "bond" as the structural form. The paragraph closes the conceptual gap between heterogeneous tradition-vocabulary and the framework's umbrella term.

### Probe D — "The paragraph might be read as Western/extraction-economy parochial — it grounds *bond* in Western legal architecture (SMCRA + Norway + civic-republican)."

Real flag for Indigenous + non-Western audience reception. Mitigation: the surrounding chapter at line 202 names Niger Delta + global externalities; Ch 4 + Ch 10 carry cross-tradition material. The paragraph itself is appropriately scoped to defending the umbrella term against the strongest Western-economics objections; broader cross-tradition work belongs elsewhere in the book.

### Probe E — "*Borrows the term in good faith* reads as defensive."

Soft spot. Tweak candidates:
- *"The framework uses the term in continuity with these traditions"*
- *"The framework adopts the term where each tradition has prepared the ground for it"*
- *"The framework uses the term in the sense each tradition has built into it"*

Author judgment call. The current phrasing is acceptable but Tier-B.

### Probe F — "*Posted before the full harm has materialized* is forward-looking; how does it cover the Restitution Bond?"

Soft spot. Restitution Bond is posted against documented historical harm — the triggering event has already occurred. But the COST continues to be borne (intergenerational disadvantage, ongoing health costs, lineage-labor compounding). The first operation could be tightened to:

> *posted before the full cost has been borne*

This covers both bond directions cleanly. Tweak candidate, not removal.
