## M11 critic-survival probes against INCLUDE

### Probe A — "The defense engages three alternatives (reversal, recovery, counterfactual test). Does the prose handle all three cleanly, or does one read as filler?"

Each rejection does specific structural work:
- *Reversal* — implies action on the resource; the test does not act on the resource.
- *Recovery* — implies the resource is restored; the test does not restore.
- *Counterfactual test* — structurally accurate but loses the directional specificity (one specific premise-flip, not generic counterfactual reasoning).

Three distinct failures, three distinct alternatives. None reads as filler.

### Probe B — "The Lewis 1973 citation grounds *counterfactual*. Does the framework's claim that CIT specializes Lewis hold up to a Lewis scholar?"

The paragraph claims "the David Lewis counterfactual-reasoning tradition specialized to one specific premise-flip." Lewis's counterfactual-conditional semantics (closest-possible-world) is the philosophical foundation for counterfactual reasoning. The framework's specialization claim is that CIT runs *one specific counterfactual* (inversion of the scarcity premise), not arbitrary counterfactuals. This is consistent with Lewis-tradition methodology (counterfactual conditionals are evaluated by holding most of the world fixed and varying one antecedent). A Lewis scholar reads the specialization claim as defensible at body-prose register.

### Probe C — "The revealed-preference engagement at line 699 already does alternative-rejection work. Is the new paragraph redundant with line 699?"

Line 699's revealed-preference rejection operates at the methodology-alternative level — *why not revealed preference as the gate*. The proposed paragraph operates at the name-choice level — *why* inversion *as the name*. The two paragraphs run parallel disciplines: name-defense first, methodology-alternative-rejection second. The bond-defense paragraph established the pattern (bond-umbrella defense at line 192, then bond-instrument lineage at line 194). The two disciplines are distinct; the parallel-discipline pattern reinforces rather than redundant.

### Probe D — "The closing line foregrounds the name (*Inversion* names what the test does). Is that scope-creep into the methodology argument?"

The closing line ("*Inversion* names what the test does, and what no other available name does as precisely") closes the term-defense by naming what the choice secures — parallel to the bond-defense paragraph's closing ("The framework uses the term in continuity with these traditions") and the cost-severance defense paragraph's closing ("Naming the missing architecture is what makes the bond proposing it possible"). The framework's apparatus is named in the closing only as the discipline the term carries; the methodology + instrument material lives in the surrounding chapter prose. Scope-discipline holds.
