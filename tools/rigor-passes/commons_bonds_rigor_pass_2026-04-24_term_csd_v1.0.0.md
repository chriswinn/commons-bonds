## §3. Principle #3 distinctness check (decisive module)

### §3.1 Is CSD distinct from already-ratified Cost Severance + Severed Cost?

**Cost Severance (Ring 1):** the structural mechanism by which extraction separates value capture from cost bearing. Mechanism noun.

**Severed Cost (Ring 1):** a specific cost that has been severed. Result noun.

**CSD (proposed Ring 2):** measurement instrument for the backward-looking, realized-human-harm component of severed costs. Instrument noun.

**Distinction:** Cost Severance names the MECHANISM; Severed Cost names the RESULT (a quantified outcome); CSD names a SPECIFIC MEASUREMENT INSTRUMENT for the human-harm component of the result.

**Test:** is CSD just "Severed Cost measured backward-looking"? Partially — CSD is the backward-looking measurement of the human-harm portion of Severed Cost. But the instrument-vs-result distinction is structurally important:
- Severed Cost is the QUANTITY (the cost that got severed onto the source).
- CSD is HOW you measure the human-harm component of that quantity.
- RCV is HOW you measure the resource-foreclosure component of that quantity.

These are different conceptual layers — quantity vs measurement-method. Frameworks routinely have separate names for both layers (e.g., "income" as quantity; "GDP" or "household-survey-income" as specific measurement methodologies).

**Verdict:** CSD is distinct from Cost Severance + Severed Cost at the measurement-method layer. **PASSES Principle #3 distinctness.**

### §3.2 Is CSD distinct from RCV?

Block 1 Hole 5: *"You could fully compensate every descendant of every Appalachian coal miner — make them whole on health, education, housing, income — and the coal is still gone."*

CSD measures realized human harm (compensable in principle via reparations). RCV measures resource permanent-foreclosure (NOT compensable via reparations — resource is gone).

**Verdict:** Orthogonal measurements. **PASSES distinctness from RCV.**

### §3.3 Could CSD be subsumed under Cᵢ?

Cᵢ are the cost variables admitted to RCV via the Four Gates. The Cᵢ framework operates on RCV's components.

CSD operates on a DIFFERENT instrument — backward-looking realized human harm, not RCV's forward-looking integrand.

**Verdict:** Not subsumable. CSD is its own measurement instrument with its own admission/calculation methodology (reparations-economics-grounded).

## §4. M12 — Intellectual honesty / Prior-art audit

### §4.1 Exact-phrase audit

### §4.2 Concept-level audit

The CONCEPT (backward-looking measurement of human harm from a structural-extraction process) is established across multiple traditions:
- **Reparations economics** (Darity & Mullen 2020 + earlier Darity work) — wealth-gap-against-comparable-households methodology.
- **Environmental-justice damages** — Bullard 1990 *Dumping in Dixie* + subsequent literature on measurable environmental-health damages concentrated by race/class.
- **VSL (Value of Statistical Life)** — established cost-benefit-analysis methodology in regulatory economics.
- **DALY (Disability-Adjusted Life Years)** — global-burden-of-disease methodology in health economics.
- **Intergenerational mobility** (Chetty et al. — already in bibliography §4) — empirical mobility-gap measurement.
- **Toxic-tort damages** in legal-economics — established compensation methodology for environmental health damages.

**Finding:** the CONCEPT (backward-looking realized-human-harm measurement of a structural-extraction process) is well-established. The framework's CSD term is original specialization that names the cluster of measurement methods + applies them specifically to the cost-severance mechanism.
