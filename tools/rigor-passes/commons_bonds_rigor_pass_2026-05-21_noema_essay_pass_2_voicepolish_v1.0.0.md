## §1. Pass 3.2 scope reminder

Pass 3.2 audits prose for the LLM tics + voice issues trade-press editors flag. The named-inventory categories applied below (template §"Pass 3.2"):

- **Rule of three** — "A. B. C." constructions
- **Em-dash crutches** — em-dashes used as connective tissue rather than as deliberate parenthetical extension or punch
- **Tidy parallels** — "He did X. She did Y. They did Z." structures
- **Hedge phrases** — "I will argue that…" / "It seems likely that…" / "Perhaps…"
- **Connective-tissue clichés** — "in short" / "ultimately" / "moreover" / "furthermore" / "that being said"
- **Meta-commentary** — "That is the whole sentence." / "What I mean to say is…" / "Let me explain…"
- **Expository flatness** — "The plain definition is this:" / "Here is what I think:" / "The argument is simple:"
- **Declarative-three-in-a-row** — three short declaratives in succession with no sentence-length variance
- **Nostalgia / sentimentality tics** — "There are not many people like that anymore" / "Back when…"
- **Cadence repetition** — "It changed me. It humbled me. It made…" patterns
- **Apparatus residue** — Greek letters, subscripts, integrals, acronyms appearing in body prose where Stage 1 brief §5 prohibits

Noema-specific Stage 1 §5 emphasis: counter declarative-three-in-a-row by mixing 5-word and 25-word sentences; italicize remembered direct speech + key terms; drop the apparatus.

**Diagnosis.** The phrase *"In plain language:"* is a substituted-variant of the named-inventory expository-flatness tic *"The plain definition is this:"* / *"Here is what I think:"* / *"The argument is simple:"*. Trade-press editors who scan for "AI-prose definitional throat-clearing" will still catch this. The substantive cost-severance definitional sentence that follows is load-bearing — it is what the Stage 1 brief §7.4 names as the framework's only definitional sentence the essay needs — so the move IS the substance; the question is whether the frame-phrase earns its space at the most editor-scrutinized definitional moment of the essay.

**Severity rationale.** (a) Named-inventory substituted-variant verbatim match against template-§Pass-3.2 *"Expository flatness — 'The plain definition is this:' / 'Here is what I think:' / 'The argument is simple:'"* category. - **B. Re-route the framing through the prior paragraph as a continuation.**

  *"There is a name for what I was inside of, and it took me much longer than it should have to find it. The framework is *cost severance* — the structural mechanism by which the value created by an activity is captured by one party while the costs of that activity are absorbed by another party who cannot bill back."*

Frame-phrase removed; the cost-severance label and its definition co-occur without expository scaffolding.   Keeps the definition as its own sentence (preserving the load-bearing register-clarity move) but replaces "In plain language:" with a register-bridging sentence that points forward to the cases-section rather than down to the definition itself.

- **D. Hold as-is.** Frame-phrase reads as register-clarity-move; not a tic-stopper for the editorial-brain audience.

**Recommendation: A.** Cut "In plain language:" entirely.

**Reasoning.** The definition sentence is strong enough to stand alone — it carries the framework's only load-bearing definitional claim per Stage 1 brief §7.4, and the sentence's syntax already foregrounds the plain-language register without needing the frame-phrase to flag it. Option B would compress the naming-and-defining into one paragraph but introduces a new em-dash where the chapter's cumulative em-dash density is already high (see F-V-Noema-14). ### F-V-Noema-3 — Section-I five-declarative-cluster with tautological "X was the X" pair (MEDIUM)

**Severity:** MEDIUM

**Location:** Line 25, Section I father-Appalachians-drive paragraph (5th-of-5 sentence cluster):
> He did not talk much on the way out. He did not need to. The drive was the drive. The morning was the morning. He did not see the categories as separable.

**Diagnosis.** Five short declarative sentences in succession (10, 4, 5, 5, 8 words — varied just enough to evade strictest *declarative-three-in-a-row* tripwire) at the paragraph's interior pivot. The middle two sentences (*"The drive was the drive. The morning was the morning."*) are tautological *"X was the X"* constructions — a cadence-formula that introduces a recurring essay-level rhetorical habit. The same *"X was the X"* construction recurs at line 65 (*"The work was the shape it was because the architecture was the shape it was."*) and line 67 (*"The architecture was the architecture."*) — three occurrences across the essay. The recurring construction performs intentional content work (it asserts the inseparability of categories — the father's worldview at line 25; the architecture's self-justifying closed-loop at line 65 + 67) and is recognizable as memoir-cadence enacting scene-substance. The risk: a tic-scanning trade-press editor reading three "X was the X" tautologies inside ~3,800 words may read the construction as a cadence-habit rather than as deliberate echo.

**Severity rationale.** (a) Declarative-five-in-a-row variant of *declarative-three-in-a-row* named-inventory. (b) Tautological *"X was the X"* pair flagged inside a single paragraph reads as cadence-formula even with the surrounding sentence-length variance. (c) Cumulative essay-level recurrence (line 25 + line 65 + line 67) compounds visibility; this is the FIRST occurrence and trains the editor to notice the next two. (d) Substantive load is content-real (father's drive-as-morning-as-one-thing is the chapter's first memoir-content). MEDIUM rather than HIGH because the variance-by-word-count IS present and the cadence enacts scene-content; flagged for cumulative-visibility review at F-V-Noema-8 (the line 67 cumulative instance) rather than as standalone HIGH.

**Options:**

- **A. Collapse the tautological pair into one sentence.**

  *"He did not talk much on the way out. He did not need to. The drive and the morning were the same thing — the categories were not separable to him."*

  Drops both tautologies; folds the substantive claim into one closing sentence. Removes the "X was the X" construction at this location entirely. Cumulative essay-level recurrence reduces from three to two (line 65 + line 67 carry the remaining instances; whether to address those is F-V-Noema-8's question).

- **B. Drop one of the tautologies; vary cadence on the other.**

  *"He did not talk much on the way out. He did not need to. The drive was the drive, and the morning was the drive too. He did not see the categories as separable."*

  Keeps one *"the drive was the drive"* construction; turns the second into a connecting clause; reads as one tautology + one elaboration rather than as cadence-pair. Less aggressive cut than A.

- **C. Restructure with longer sentence-length variance.**

  *"He did not talk much on the way out, and he did not need to. To him the drive was the drive and the morning was the morning — the categories were not separable, and the silence belonged to both."*

  Compresses five sentences into two (one short-pair-merged + one long-with-em-dash). Removes the declarative-five pattern; preserves the tautological-pair as content-load by quoting it as the father's view. (Adds em-dash; see F-V-Noema-14.)

- **D. Hold as-is.** Cadence-enacts-content; the five-declarative + tautological-pair IS the father's quiet way of seeing the drive-and-morning as one thing; cutting it weakens the chapter's first memoir-content moment.

**Recommendation: A.** Collapse the tautological pair into one sentence.

**Reasoning.** The tautological *"X was the X"* construction is recognizable enough that three occurrences across the essay (line 25 + line 65 + line 67) will register as a cadence-habit to a careful reader. Reducing to two occurrences (line 65 + line 67, the two architecture-related instances) keeps the construction as a deliberate Section-III/IV-architecture-echo rather than as a Section-I-memoir-cadence-that-recurs. Option A preserves the substantive memoir-content (father's worldview) while removing the most editor-detectable instance of the construction. Option B keeps a single tautology at this location, which keeps the cumulative count at three. Option C restructures into a long sentence that may read as over-explanatory. Cross-pass impact (Pass 3.3 / Pass 3.4): the rewrite preserves all Section-I-anchored scene-detail (father's silence + the drive's primacy + the categorical-inseparability claim) so acceptance + robustness verdicts on Section-I-anchored characters (Tier-1 literary-philosophical; Tier-3 narrative-driven; adversarial scientific-reductionist) should not shift.

**Cross-pass impact:** Pass 3.3 acceptance — no expected shift; Section-I memoir-substance is preserved. Pass 3.4 robustness — no expected shift; this is memoir-content, not framework-claim. F-V-Noema-8 sequencing — applying A here keeps line 65 + line 67 as the remaining tautological-construction instances; F-V-Noema-8's disposition then determines whether the construction is preserved as a deliberate echo or whether the third instance (line 67) is also reduced.
