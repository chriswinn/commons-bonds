## 3. Candidate placements analyzed

### Candidate A: After line 63 (end of "what the model can do" enumeration), before the `---` at line 65 — RECOMMENDED

**Insertion point:** after the "insurance is usually cheap" paragraph closes with "…the framework asks that we apply the same logic to the planet." Inserted paragraph becomes the closing paragraph of section 4; then `---` to section 5 (Lakota).

**Rationale for fit:**
- Section 4 is the chapter's natural home for meta-reflection on framework scope. The inserted paragraph IS a scope-reflection — it names the framework's reach (bidirectional) as a structural property, complementing the section's affordances enumeration.
- The transition into section 5 (Lakota) becomes load-bearing: the Lakota case is precisely a backward-direction case the framework can in principle reach but the book has not yet computed. The bidirectional paragraph primes the Lakota concession by naming that the framework runs in reverse, and Lakota then lands as the most striking instance of "the legacy effects whose abundance-masking has only just begun to fail."
- Voice continuity: section 4 already closes on a meta-reflective register ("The framework asks that we apply the same logic to the planet."). The inserted paragraph deepens that register rather than fighting it.
- The "It can / It cannot" affordances list closes with "insurance is cheap"; the inserted paragraph is not a fifth "It can" — it's a scope/coda that names a different axis (directional reach). Adding it as a coda paragraph preserves the list's rhythm without forcing a structural rewrite.

**Stakes vs. other candidates:**
- Loses the option of giving the bidirectional paragraph its own section break (Candidate B).
- Gains the bridge from affordances → Lakota concession, which is the strongest argumentative payoff.

### Candidate B: As its own one-paragraph section between the existing `---` at line 65 and the Lakota opener at line 67

**Insertion point:** new section break inserted, paragraph stands alone, then another section break to Lakota.

**Rationale for fit:**
- Gives the meta-reflection more visual weight; reads as a deliberate pivot move.
- Avoids tonally shifting the close of section 4 from affordance-list voice to meta-reflective voice mid-section.

**Stakes:**
- Single-paragraph sections are not a pattern Ch 10 otherwise uses; other sections carry multiple paragraphs. Introducing a one-paragraph section breaks the chapter's structural rhythm.
- Visual choppiness — two section breaks bracketing a five-sentence paragraph reads as overweight scaffolding for the content.

### Candidate C: After the Lakota section closes (line 75), before the "objection from the left" opener at line 79

**Insertion point:** between section 5 and section 6.

**Rationale for fit:**
- Lakota is a backward-direction case the book explicitly defers ("remains ahead of me"). The bidirectional paragraph could close out the Lakota engagement by saying the framework that priced things forward can in subsequent work price them backward.

**Stakes:**
- Awkward because the paragraph mentions "the book by some other author with the right tools" — generic; reads as competing with the Lakota section's emphasis on **indigenous scholars and sensitivity readers** specifically. Risks flattening the Lakota section's epistemic-humility move into a generic future-work gesture.
- The Lakota section's closing "remains ahead of me" already does the future-work move locally; doubling it weakens both.

### Decision

**Recommend Candidate A.** Strongest fit because:
1. The paragraph functions as a scope-coda to section 4 (framework affordances), not as a bridge that competes with the Lakota section's local closing.
2. The bidirectional paragraph PRIMES the Lakota section's "this book has not fully done" framing — the Lakota case becomes one specific instance of the backward direction the framework reaches but this book did not compute. The argumentative payoff is strongest here.
3. Voice continuity preserved.
4. No new section structure introduced; chapter rhythm intact.
