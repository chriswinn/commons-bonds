## M11 critic-survival probes against INCLUDE

### Probe A — "The defense engages three alternatives (asymmetric risk / one-sided regret / irreversibility-weighted). Does the prose handle all three cleanly?"

Each rejection does specific structural work:
- *Asymmetric risk rule* — fails because expected-utility risk presupposes probabilities the framework doesn't have.
- *One-sided regret rule* — fails because the asymmetry is not one-sided in the casual sense (both directions have regret); it is asymmetric in the specific bounded-vs-unbounded sense.
- *Irreversibility-weighted decision rule* — fails because it loses the regret-theory lineage (Savage 1951) that grounds the framework in established decision theory rather than as ad-hoc construction.

Three distinct failures, three distinct alternatives. None reads as filler.

### Probe B — "The Savage 1951 citation grounds *regret*. Does the framework's claim to specialize Savage hold up to a decision-theory scholar?"

The paragraph claims "Leonard Savage's 1951 minimax-regret discipline... The discipline does not require knowing the probabilities. It requires only knowing the possible futures and the irreversibility structure across them." Savage's minimax-regret discipline (Savage 1951) is the foundational specification of regret-theoretic decision-making; the framework's specialization claim (commons-extinction direction as the unbounded-regret direction) is consistent with Savage-tradition methodology. A decision-theory scholar reads the specialization claim as defensible at body-prose register; the formal specification is in Tech Appendix §8.

### Probe C — "The closing line claims the framework 'joins the regret-theory tradition.' Is that scope-creep into the broader decision-theory literature?"

The closing line ("The framework joins the regret-theory tradition and names what its specialization adds: the asymmetry the irreversibility of commons-extinction produces.") closes the name-defense by naming what the choice secures — parallel pattern with bond/cost-severance/CIT defenses. The framework's claim is specific (joining regret theory at the commons-extinction-direction specialization); the broader decision-theory literature is invoked only as the parent tradition the framework specializes. Scope-discipline holds.

### Probe D — "The 'probabilities not knowable in the sense expected-utility theory requires' framing engages a deep epistemological claim. Does the paragraph's body-prose register support that claim?"

The framing aligns with the deep-uncertainty / Knightian-uncertainty literature (Knight 1921; Lempert-Popper-Bankes 2003). The paragraph names the substantive claim ("the probabilities the framework would need to know — probability of a fusion breakthrough, probability of a substitute material being discovered, probability of an unanticipated technological transition — are not knowable in the sense expected-utility theory requires") at body-prose level without citing Knight 1921 explicitly. A welfare-economics reviewer or epistemologist might want the citation; the Tech Appendix §15.1.7 carries the formal engagement. Body-prose register holds; deeper engagement is in the appendix.
