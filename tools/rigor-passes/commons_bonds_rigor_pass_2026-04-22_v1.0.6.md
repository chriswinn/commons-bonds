### 5.1 Tier 1 — Lifetime Survival Cost

**Summary.** Tier 1 prices the cumulative biological, psychological, and social cost imposed on those whose embodied capacity is consumed by extraction — borne across the full lifespan including latent consequences that emerge after the transaction ends. Not just contract-period provisioning (food, water, shelter, healthcare) but the long-horizon impact on life expectancy and health-adjusted lifespan of the population absorbing the cost.

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Habitability + Ecosystem (functionally infinite breathable medium + self-restoring ecosystem services). Under abundance, respiratory disease from industrial exposure evaporates; cardiovascular stress from workplace conditions evaporates; asbestosis / pneumoconiosis / knowledge-worker burnout-cardiac-disease all evaporate. *The extraction still occurs; workers still work; but the lifetime-health decrement vanishes.*

**Two-path.** Path 1: firms capture labor value at contract-period wages; workers bear cost across full lifespan + latent-consequence horizon + post-retirement healthcare. Shield (Path 2): Habitability/Ecosystem abundance on Earth is the structural shield — the provisioning that underwrites the worker's embodied capacity is invisible because it's ambient.

**Primitiveness vs. adjacent tiers.** Bilateral independence vs. Tier 2a: worker with acute workplace injury (Tier 2a active, no chronic-disease Tier 1). Worker with latent chronic disease across decades (Tier 1 active, low acute-risk Tier 2a). **Distinct.** Vs. Tier 3: Tier 3 is intergenerational-compensation-asymmetry (lineage cost); Tier 1 is within-lifetime-health-decrement (own body across own years). Different axes. **Distinct.**

**Multi-scale × 2 env.** Individual (worker's own lifetime health). Community (aggregate life-expectancy gap in extraction-dependent regions — McDowell County life-expectancy is ~10 years below national median). Firm (long-tail workers-comp / insurance-premium / litigation liability). Government (Medicare / Medicaid / disability concentration in extraction-regions; tax-base reduction from shortened working lives). Off-Earth: asteroid-miner radiation exposure across long-duration contract; Mars-colony atmospheric-engineering substrate dependency. **PASS all 4 scales × both environments.**

**Key character pressures.** Character 14 (Appalachian reader) — DIMENSION-CRITICAL via black-lung / pneumoconiosis canonical case. Character 16 (former IT consultant) — DIMENSION-CRITICAL via knowledge-worker burnout / cardiovascular-disease canonical case. Character 10 (labor lawyer 2039) — "lifetime survival cost" compresses in briefs cleanly. Character 15 (young worker) — pattern-match immediate (early-career-health-trade-off). **No structural pressure.**

**Earning-its-place test.** **STRONGLY EARNS PLACE.** Tier 1 is load-bearing on:
- Ch. 2 (coal case) — pneumoconiosis is the canonical extraction-cost-on-human-body case; un-removable from the chapter.

"Worker health cost" exists in other frameworks (workers' comp, OSHA) but only for acute / contract-period coverage; Tier 1's full-lifespan + latent-consequence + aggregate-population scope is the framework's extension. This is book-worthy canonical content.

### 5.2 Tier 2a — Actuarial Risk Cost (rename from Individual Risk Cost)

**Summary.** Tier 2a prices the true actuarial price of personal harm from extraction work — acute injury, chronic disease, and mortality risk — priced across the full exposure timeline rather than only the contract period. Distinct from Tier 1 in that Tier 2a prices *risk-as-stochastic-variable* (probability-weighted expectation), where Tier 1 prices *realized lifetime health decrement*. They are adjacent but distinct: Tier 2a is ex-ante pricing of the expected harm; Tier 1 is ex-post measurement of the cumulative cost.

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Institutional (organizational transparency abundance). Under abundance, firms cannot absorb or suppress risk data into organizational opacity; actuarial-risk data flows freely to workers, regulators, insurers; risk is properly priced at contract formation. *The work itself continues; but the risk-pricing reflects full-timeline exposure and the firm cannot capture the actuarial-premium-it-failed-to-charge.*

**Two-path.** Path 1: firms capture the difference between (a) the actuarial premium that would properly compensate the risk and (b) the contract-period compensation actually paid; workers bear the underpriced risk. Path 2: institutional shield — firms' organizational opacity absorbs risk data before it reaches workers, insurers, regulators. Workers' comp insurance masks this partially but the pricing feedback loop is broken.

**Primitiveness vs. adjacent tiers.** Vs. Tier 1: Tier 2a is ex-ante risk pricing; Tier 1 is ex-post realized cost. Bilateral independence: some workers have high actuarial risk (Tier 2a) with good outcomes (low Tier 1 realization); others have moderate actuarial-risk profiles with bad realizations (high Tier 1). **Distinct.** Vs. Tier 2b: Tier 2a is personal-harm pricing; Tier 2b is operation-failure pricing (stranded-cost, retry-cost). Clean separation. **Distinct.**

**Multi-scale × 2 env.** Individual (actuarial-risk pricing at contract formation). Community (aggregate risk-pricing for extraction-adjacent populations — fenceline community premium adjustments). Firm (workers' comp trends, insurance-premium drift, latent-litigation exposure). Government (actuarial-basis for worker-protection-policy, Medicare risk-adjustment in extraction regions). Off-Earth: asteroid-mining insurance pricing at mission formation. **PASS.**

**Key character pressures.** Character 9 (Economics chair) — actuarial framing is economics-native; framework's contribution is full-timeline scope, not methodology. Positive engagement. Character 10 (labor lawyer) — actuarial-risk-pricing is employment-law territory. Character 15 (young worker) — relevant but less pattern-match-immediate than Tier 1. Character 7 (Legal counsel) — pre-drafting relevance for named-case content. **Moderate pressure; no structural flags.**

**Earning-its-place test.** **EARNS PLACE with scope-clarification required.** The risk of Tier 2a being scaffolding-adjacent: actuarial pricing is well-developed by existing insurance / workers-comp / actuarial-science frameworks. Framework's unique contribution is *extending actuarial pricing to cover full-lifetime exposure rather than contract-period only*, and specifying the *Institutional opacity mechanism* that prevents proper actuarial pricing.

Specific book-worthy contributions:
- **Ch. 6** (Three Ways of Counting) — Tier 2a is a concrete case where "actuarial price of full-exposure timeline" produces a different number than "actuarial price of contract period." This is the kind of mathematical legibility that makes RCV decomposable to non-economist readers.
- **Ch. 5** (accountability gap) — workers' comp's systematic underpricing IS the aggregate Tier 2a signature.
- **Ch. 9** (policy) — actuarial-risk-pricing reforms are policy-actionable (mandate full-timeline actuarial basis for extraction-industry workers' comp).

The framework without Tier 2a would conflate ex-ante risk-pricing with ex-post realized health decrement (Tier 1), losing analytical precision at exactly the place economists are strongest. **EARNS PLACE with explicit Tier-1-vs-Tier-2a scope differentiation in v11.**

**Scaffolding-vs-book-worthy judgment.** v10 body text still uses "Individual Risk Cost" despite ratification-box authorization of "Actuarial Risk Cost" rename. The rename IS the rigor finding being executed — strip "Individual Risk Cost" throughout v11. The rename is execution-of-prior-decision, not scaffolding. The rest of Tier 2a content is book-worthy. **CLEAN BOOK-WORTHY after rename execution.**

**Verdict.** **LOCK Tier 2a (renamed from Individual → Actuarial).** Dimension mapping stable (Institutional). Scope-clarification note required: Tier 2a prices ex-ante actuarial risk; Tier 1 prices ex-post realized lifetime decrement. v11 scale-abstract + manifestations treatment per WS02/WS03 authorization.

### 5.3 Tier 2b — Mission Failure Reserve

**Summary.** Tier 2b prices the cost of an operation not returning its expected value, borne by workers and communities who planned around it. Includes stranded-cost (asteroid miner without fuel to return; McDowell miner without savings to relocate when mine closes), mine-underperformance cost (community whose economy depended on projected revenues), and the compounding contingency stack (cost of needing to try a second, third time). Distinct from Tier 4 (Foreclosure) — Tier 2b is about *this specific operation failing*; Tier 4 is about *resource option-value across all future uses*.

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Spatial (frictionless relocation) + Temporal (no time-separation between planning and failure-recognition). Under abundance, worker can leave stranded-mission context freely; retry has no compounding cost; community can relocate without structural dependency. *The operation still fails; but the failure doesn't cascade through immobility or retry-stack.*

**Two-path.** Path 1: extraction-operator captures upside-case expected value; workers / communities who planned around operation bear downside-case stranded cost + retry cost. Path 2: Spatial (distance-as-trap) + Temporal (planning-horizon-to-failure gap) shields; a population that cannot freely relocate or retry absorbs the contingency.

**Primitiveness vs. adjacent tiers.** Vs. Tier 1: Tier 2b is mission-failure cost; Tier 1 is health cost. Distinct. Vs. Tier 4: Tier 2b prices *this operation's failure*; Tier 4 prices *option-value foreclosure across all future uses of the extracted resource*. Both are about future but at different granularity. Bilateral independence: operation that succeeds (low Tier 2b) but forecloses future uses permanently (high Tier 4). Operation that fails (high Tier 2b) but the resource remains available for future uses (low Tier 4). **Distinct.**

**Multi-scale × 2 env.** Individual (worker stranded / owed severance on failed operation). Community (McDowell community whose economy depended on mine continuing). Firm (contingency-reserve requirements; multi-attempt mission-planning). Government (extraction-region support after operation failure). Off-Earth: **asteroid miner scenario is the canonical Tier 2b case** — mission failure with no return option is the prototype that made Tier 2b legible. **PASS strongest off-Earth alignment of any tier.**

### 5.4 Tier 3 — Dynastic Labor Cost

**Summary.** Tier 3 prices the cost imposed on a worker's lineage by extraction of productive capacity at below Full Generational Cost. Includes lost earnings trajectory, lost intergenerational wealth accumulation, the compounding cost of a generation's productive contribution consumed in the present rather than held for the future. *Capital owners have always understood their transactions affect their lineages; "Dynastic" names the same claim for workers.*

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Temporal (no intergenerational separation; present-generation cost is future-generation cost). Under abundance, the worker's children would feel the cost of below-FGC compensation in the present, forcing renegotiation. Firm cannot capture by deferring compensation-deficit to lineages that cannot advocate for themselves.

**Two-path.** Path 1: firm / extraction-operator captures present-generation labor-value at below-FGC pricing; worker's descendants bear compounding intergenerational-wealth-trajectory asymmetry. Path 2: Temporal shield — future generations cannot advocate; consent-normalization applied temporally ("worker agreed to wages then") closes the accounting even as generational cost compounds.

**Primitiveness vs. adjacent tiers.** Vs. Tier 1 (Lifetime Survival): Tier 1 is within-lifetime; Tier 3 is across-lifetimes. Distinct. Vs. Tier 4 (Foreclosure): Tier 4 is resource-option-value foreclosure; Tier 3 is labor-compensation intergenerational asymmetry. Both Temporal but different subject-matter. **Distinct.** Vs. hypothetical Tier 9 (Autonomy / Coerced-Consent — see §9.2): Tier 3 operates through temporal-opacity; Autonomy-coercion operates through present-compulsion. Related at 120-hour-week case where both activate but distinct primitives.

**Multi-scale × 2 env.** Individual (own wealth trajectory vs. FGC baseline). Community (Appalachian coal regions' multi-generational wealth-gap). Firm (long-horizon lineage-claims — rare in existing law but growing). Government (Social Security as 88-year quantitative Tier 3 demonstration). Off-Earth: multi-generation-ship crew compensation structures; Mars-colony labor pricing across settler-generations. **PASS.**

**Key character pressures.** **Character 10 (labor lawyer 2039) — DIMENSION-CRITICAL via vocabulary portability:** "dynastic labor cost" in a brief works powerfully ("The claimant was paid below Full Generational Cost; this is a Dynastic Labor claim"). Character 12 (grad student) — citable across labor economics, intergenerational ethics, Piketty-successor work. Character 15 (young worker) — pattern-match-immediate for early-career trade-off. Character 16 (former IT consultant) — 120-hour-week case activates Tier 3 (pay-now-at-future-health-cost). Character 17 (Ostrom scholar) — intergenerational commons-governance engagement. ### 5.5 Tier 4 — Foreclosure Cost

**Summary.** Tier 4 prices the permanent elimination of all future uses an extracted resource might have served, valued at conservative compound rate from moment of extraction forward, weighted by probability that no substitute exists for each future use. **This is the mathematical centerpiece of RCV** — the integral the formal framework integrates.

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Temporal (future-uses become present and visible). Under abundance, all future possible uses of the extracted resource are priced into present decision; if future option-value exceeds extraction revenue, extraction does not occur. Firm cannot capture present revenue by foreclosing future option-value invisibly.

**Two-path.** Path 1: extraction-operator captures present revenue; future generations (or future-self) bear the foreclosed option-value. Path 2: Temporal shield — future uses are unimagined, undervalued, discounted steeply. Pre-discounting future uses at 3-7% (common practice) makes multi-decade options invisible.

**Primitiveness vs. adjacent tiers.** Vs. Tier 3 (Dynastic Labor): Tier 3 is labor-compensation temporal asymmetry; Tier 4 is resource-option-value temporal asymmetry. Different subject-matter. Bilateral independence. **Distinct.** Vs. Tier 6 (Ecological Carrying): Tier 4 is extracted-resource option-value; Tier 6 is secondary-damage-to-ecosystem from extraction process. **Distinct.**

**Multi-scale × 2 env.** Individual (rarely pertinent — resource extraction is typically community+ scale). Community (Appalachian coal foreclosing future uses of specific seams + landscape). Firm (reserve-depletion and resource-optionality trade-offs). Government (national-strategic-reserve policy). Off-Earth: rare-earth mining foreclosing future off-Earth construction options; asteroid mining foreclosing comet-water uses. **PASS.**

### 5.6 Tier 5 — Community Transition Reserve

**Summary.** Tier 5 names a pre-committed, independently held fund sized to finance the complete economic transition of any community whose existence became structurally dependent on an extraction operation. Calculated at inception of extraction, held outside the control of the extracting entity, required to fund full transition of the dependent community when extraction concludes. *Distinct from decommissioning funds (physical cleanup only) and severance packages (individual workers only).*

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Spatial (frictionless relocation) + Kindred (specific-attachment bonds recognized universally regardless of location). Under abundance, community can relocate freely while preserving specific attachment-bonds across the relocation. Transition cost absorbs into trivial relocation decision. Extraction-operator cannot capture by externalizing community-transition cost to populations stranded by immobility + broken kindred-networks.

**Two-path.** Path 1: extraction-operator captures resource-revenue; community bears structural-dependency cost when extraction concludes. Path 2: Spatial shield (community's immobility — relocating costs everyone connections, housing equity, specific-place attachments) + Kindred shield (relocating severs specific-identity-deep bonds that can't be substituted in the new location). **Cohesion cross-shield:** civic-infrastructure that makes the community functional is lost in relocation — Klinenberg's *Palaces for the People* territory.

**Primitiveness vs. adjacent tiers.** Vs. Tier 2b (Mission Failure): Tier 2b is operation-specific-failure pricing; Tier 5 is community-as-entity transition pricing. A successful operation still produces Tier 5 cost if community is structurally dependent. **Distinct.** Vs. Tier 7 (Knowledge and Culture): Tier 5 is economic-transition financing; Tier 7 is knowledge-transmission-threshold-breach pricing. Related at canonical-case level (McDowell) but distinct primitives. **Distinct.**

Firm (long-horizon contingent liability — rarely booked but functionally existing). Government (extraction-jurisdiction transition-planning; energy-transition policy). ### 5.8 Tier 7 — Knowledge and Culture Cost

**Summary.** Tier 7 prices the cost of accumulated community knowledge, skill, and cultural continuity that is lost when an extraction-dependent community disperses below the threshold needed to maintain its collective expertise. Traditional fishing knowledge, mining expertise, land management practice, language, oral history. **Threshold dynamic is Tier 7's primitive contribution:** once carrying-community disperses below transmission-threshold, reconstruction is often impossible.

**AIT under canonical dimensions.** Strip Kindred (specific non-substitutable attachment bonds universally recognized and preserved across relocation). Under abundance, community dispersion doesn't fracture knowledge transmission because specific kin/cultural bonds persist regardless of location. Knowledge transmission continues through Weston-style chosen-kin networks even as community geographically disperses.

**Two-path.** Path 1: extraction-operator captures resource revenue; community bears knowledge-transmission-threshold-breach cost. Path 2: Kindred shield — specific-identity-deep bonds are invisible to dominant-culture accounting; their severance is unnamed and unpriced. **Epistemic cross-shield:** knowledge itself becomes inaccessible once transmission threshold breaches (cross-dimension with Epistemic, but distinct — Epistemic is present-tense evidentiary-asymmetry; Tier 7 is transmission-threshold collapse across generations).

Bilateral independence: community with full Tier 5 funding that still disperses below knowledge-threshold (Tier 7 active despite Tier 5 provision); community preserves knowledge via diaspora networks despite economic transition failure (Tier 5 active, Tier 7 low). **Distinct.** Vs. Kindred-dimension cost (not a tier per se): Tier 7 is the specific *threshold-dynamic* contribution — knowledge-loss as function of transmission-population-density below critical threshold. Kindred-dimension prices broader specific-attachment severance; Tier 7 adds the threshold-mathematics.

**Multi-scale × 2 env.** Individual (native-language speaker whose transmission context is lost). Firm (industry-specific craft knowledge lost when firms exit — shipbuilding, certain manufacturing). Government (extraction-region-specific land-management practice erosion).
