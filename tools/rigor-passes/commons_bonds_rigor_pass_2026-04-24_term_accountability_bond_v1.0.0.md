The pass recommends Ring 2 primarily (to preserve adoption-bet focus on Severed Cost + Commons Bonds + Cost Severance flagships) with explicit travel-monitoring annotation. ## §2. The question under test

### §2.1 V2 definition

> *"Accountability Bond (B): Every mechanism that forces extractors to bear costs: reclamation bonds, sovereign wealth funds, carbon taxes, insurance requirements. The formal quantity that should equal RCV for honest accounting. Under current extraction regimes, B reflects only identified, legally-enforceable costs; RCV includes intergenerational foreclosure costs that no existing B mechanism prices."*

### §2.2 CORE-math role

B is the right-hand-side term in the framework's flagship equation:
- **CS = RCV − B**

Cost Severance (CS) equals the gap between the true Residual Commons Value (RCV) and what the Accountability Bond (B) actually covers. When B = RCV, CS = 0 (no severance). When B < RCV, CS > 0 (severance exists and equals the gap).

Structural role: **B makes the equation work.** Removing B leaves CS = RCV − [unknown], which would force the framework to redefine severance without the accountability counterpart.

## §3. M12 — Intellectual honesty / Prior-art audit

### §3.1 Exact-phrase search

Search: *"accountability bond"* in environmental / policy / legal literature.

Findings:
- **Environmental Impact Bond** (EIB) has "accountability features" prominently discussed (Balboa 2016).
- "Accountability bond" itself is NOT an established term in environmental-bonding literature.
- Social Impact Bonds + Pay-for-Success contracts have accountability-outcome structures but don't use the phrase.

**Exact-phrase finding:** the phrase "Accountability Bond" as a standalone instrument name is novel / close to original coinage. Low collision risk at the phrase level.

### §3.2 Concept-level search

Underlying concept: a financial/legal instrument forcing cost-bearing by the extractor/actor who would otherwise externalize cost.

Established adjacent instruments:
- **Reclamation Bond** — established in mining / oil-gas (OSMRE; GAO-17-207R; Yang & Davis 2021). Specific to physical reclamation.
- **Financial Assurance** (US federal term) — umbrella for reclamation + closure + post-closure instruments.
- **Environmental Impact Bond (EIB)** — pay-for-success outcome-based contract (Balboa 2016).
- **Pigouvian Tax** (Pigou 1920) — tax priced to externality.
- **Social Impact Bond** — outcome-based contract in social-policy; adjacent structure.
- **Decommissioning Bond** — renewable-energy end-of-life assurance.
- **Sovereign Wealth Fund** (e.g., Norway's GPFG) — accumulation of extraction rents; related but different mechanism.

**Concept-level finding:** the CONCEPT (instrument forcing extractors to bear costs) is established under multiple existing names. Accountability Bond's framework novelty is (a) scope — spans full Severed Cost spectrum, not just reclamation — and (b) equational role in CS = RCV − B.

### §3.3 Classification (per M12 §16.5.4c)

**Independent specialization + extension.** Framework's Accountability Bond is:
- Not original coinage (adjacent instruments well-established).
- Not independent rediscovery (framework consciously extends established lineage).
- Not lexical collision (phrase itself isn't collision-prone).
- Not adoption (framework specifies a broader scope than any single established instrument).
- **Adjacent term (extension).** Framework extends reclamation-bond + EIB + Pigouvian-tax lineages with full-Severed-Cost scope + equational-closure role.

### §3.4 Required action (per ratified M12 action ladder — Principle #6 Corollary D)

**Level 4 — Terms Index extension-positioning statement** + **Level 3 — Ch 5 prose citation** + **Level 1 — Tech Appendix methodological footnote.**

Ratified 2026-04-24 by Chris Winn: cite reclamation-bond / financial-assurance literature in Tech Appendix + Ch 5 at first use. Bibliography entries already added (commit `f643e59`).

### §3.5 Canonical citation form (recommended for Ch 5 + Tech Appendix first uses)

> *"This framework's Accountability Bond (B) extends the established tradition of environmental-bonding instruments — reclamation bonds required by the U.S. Office of Surface Mining Reclamation & Enforcement (SMCRA 1977; GAO-17-207R; see Yang & Davis 2021 on systematic under-coverage), Environmental Impact Bonds in outcome-based environmental finance (Balboa 2016), and the Pigouvian tradition of pricing externalities (Pigou 1920) — from their current project-specific or single-externality scope to the full Severed Cost spectrum.
