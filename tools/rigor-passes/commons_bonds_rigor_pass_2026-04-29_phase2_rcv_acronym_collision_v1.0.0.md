### §5.1 Insurance: Replacement Cost Value (HIGH collision)

**Domain:** Property and casualty (P&C) insurance.

**Definition:** the cost to replace a damaged item with a new item of like kind and quality, without deduction for depreciation. Distinguished from "Actual Cash Value" (ACV; depreciated value).

**Sources:**
- Insurance Information Institute (III) — *Replacement Cost vs. Actual Cash Value* — canonical industry-reference.
- Casualty Actuarial Society — actuarial-textbook standard.
- Most U.S. homeowner / renter / auto policies — vernacular usage in policy paperwork.

**Audience overlap with framework:**
- Tier 1 (lay readers with property-insurance experience): HIGH overlap.
- Tier 2c (policy-practitioner): HIGH overlap (insurance regulation is policy-practitioner territory).
- Tier 2a (academic-economist): MEDIUM overlap (insurance-economics specialty).
- Tier 2e (working-quant in finance): MEDIUM overlap (actuarial-finance overlap).

**Misread mechanism:** reader sees "RCV" → infers "Replacement Cost Value" → expects per-item insurance-replacement quantity → encounters CS = RCV − B equation → "Replacement Cost Value − Bond" reads as some bizarre insurance-bond formula, not framework's primitive equation → reader pauses, re-reads, may resolve to framework reading via context but with reading-friction cost.

**Verdict:** **HIGH collision risk.**

### §5.2 Voting: Ranked-Choice Voting (MEDIUM-HIGH collision; increasing salience)

**Domain:** Electoral systems / civic engagement / policy reform.

**Definition:** voting method where voters rank candidates by preference; if no candidate has majority, lowest-vote candidate is eliminated and votes redistributed per next-preference until majority reached. Also called Instant-Runoff Voting (IRV) or Alternative Vote (AV).

**Sources:**
- FairVote.org — authoritative U.S. organization for ranked-choice voting advocacy.
- Maine 2018 (statewide adoption); New York City 2021; Alaska 2022; multiple state ballot initiatives 2024.
- Reilly 2001 *Democracy and Diversity: Political Engineering in the Asia-Pacific* (Oxford UP).

**Audience overlap with framework:**
- Tier 2c (policy-practitioner): HIGH overlap (electoral reform is direct policy-practitioner territory).
- Tier 1 (lay readers in U.S. RCV-adopting states): HIGH overlap (Maine, NYC, Alaska, etc.).
- Tier 2a (academic-economist with public-choice / political-economy specialty): MEDIUM overlap.
- Tier 2b (academic-philosopher): LOW overlap.

**Misread mechanism:** reader (especially policy/civic-engagement) sees "RCV" → infers "Ranked-Choice Voting" → expects voting-methodology discussion → encounters CS = RCV − B equation → "Ranked-Choice Voting − Bond" makes no sense → reader pauses, may resolve via Tech Appendix §B but with substantial reading-friction.

**Salience trajectory:** RCV-as-Ranked-Choice-Voting has gained substantial U.S. mindshare since 2018; the trend is upward through 2024+. By the framework's planned publication window, this collision risk will be more salient than at framework's original 2024-2025 vocabulary-adoption window.

**Verdict:** **MEDIUM-HIGH collision risk; increasing.**
