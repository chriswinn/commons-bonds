## §0. What changed from v1.0.0

Two findings drove this re-evaluation:

**Finding 1 — class-coded baggage.** v1.0.0's recommended title "Inheritance" carries class signaling (British literary tradition; gentry/estates association; risks excluding readers descended from generations denied inheritance). Procedural lesson: pressure-test character sets must explicitly include reader-archetypes from positions the book's substantive content engages. **v2.0.0 expands the pressure-test set with four new characters.**

**Finding 2 — length/register constraint.** v1.0.0's runner-up "What I Learned to Call It" (6 words, first-person reflective) didn't align with the rest of the book's chapter-title set. **v2.0.0 enforces a length and register constraint** matched against the existing chapter-title baseline:

## §1. Title candidates

### §1.1 Candidate A — "What It Took"

**Setup.** Three-word interrogative-completion phrase. Voice-aligns directly with Ch 8 "What Things Actually Cost."

**Connotation cluster.** Common English idiom; accessible across class lines; carries dual reading (the effort the work required + what was extracted from him); humble; emotional weight without dramatic register; not pompous; voice-fit with chapter's reflective tone.

### §1.2 Candidate B — "What He Passed"

**Setup.** Three-word phrase pulled from chapter line 69 ("He passed it to me the same way"). Earned weight from chapter's own voice.

**Connotation cluster.** Inherited from chapter's voice; understated; requires pronoun resolution ("who is he?"); emphasizes the transmission dimension; humble register; lighter inheritance signaling than "Inheritance" itself.

### §1.3 Candidate C — "The Reckoning"

**Setup.** Two-word definite-article + noun phrase. Matches "The Miner" / "The Existence Proof" / "The Accountability Gap" register.

**Connotation cluster.** Biblical/legal/spiritual register; carries weight; universally accessible; risk of reading religious or dramatic in some contexts; could telegraph the framework's arrival rather than letting the chapter discover it.

### §1.4 Candidate D — "Patterns"

**Setup.** Single-word plural. Framework-vocabulary adjacent.

**Connotation cluster.** Framework-vocabulary adjacent; signals analytical posture; would be the only one-word title in the book; risk of thinness; risk of M6 overclaim (introduces framework-vocabulary at chapter-title level before reader has earned it).

### §1.5 Candidate E — "The Quiet Math"

**Setup.** Three-word definite-article + noun phrase. Matches "The Miner" / "The Existence Proof" register. Combines two chapter motifs.

**Connotation cluster.**
- *"Quiet"* threads through the chapter — the father's voice ("a hair below normal volume," speaking only when he had something worth saying); the grandfather's understated competence (sharpened the drill bits, "did not mention it"); the inheritance "passed it to me the same way."
- *"Math"* threads forward — the commute passage's math without a calculator (1,300 hours); the no-villain-but-the-math-was-there beat; the framework's central concern (the cost the math reveals); pairs forward with Ch 8's worked-example math and Ch 9's "Pricing Honestly."

Combined: humble + framework-adjacent + chapter-motif weight + class-neutral + voice-fit + length-aligned + register-aligned.

### §1.6 Candidate F — "The Pattern"

**Setup.** Two-word definite-article + singular-noun. Matches "The Miner" register. Framework-vocabulary adjacent (singular vs. plural variant of D).

**Connotation cluster.** Framework-vocabulary adjacent; singular emphasis implies a specific pattern is being shown; risk: definite article + abstract noun reads slightly grandiose if the chapter doesn't deliver the singular-pattern claim; risk of M6 overclaim.

## §8. Recommendation

**Lock E — "The Quiet Math"** as Ch 1 chapter title.

**Reasoning ranked by load-bearing weight:**

1. **Strongest originality across the candidate set (M7).** "The Quiet Math" is a distinctive phrase with no obvious cliché collision; "The Reckoning," "Patterns," and "What It Took" all carry mild-to-moderate cliché risks the distinctive phrase doesn't.

2. **Combines two chapter motifs that thread through the book.** *"Quiet"* runs through the lineage scenes (father's voice spoken below the room's volume; grandfather's understated competence; "passed it to me the same way"). *"Math"* runs forward through the commute passage's 1,300-hour calculation, the no-villain-but-the-math-was-there beat, the framework's central concern, and pairs with Ch 8's worked-example math and Ch 9's "Pricing Honestly." The two motifs together carry both the inheritance dimension and the framework dimension without invoking either explicitly.

3. **Class-neutral; survives the v1.0.0 procedural concern.** Strong across all four added reader-archetypes (Black reader / indigenous reader / working-class reader / first-generation immigrant). The "math" framing is universally recognized — accounting in any tradition; the math of arrival, sacrifice, what was added up. The "quiet" framing is universally recognized — the quiet competence; the math being done without announcement. No class signaling.

4. **Length and register aligned with existing chapter-title set.** Three words; definite article + adjective + noun phrase; matches "The Miner" / "The Existence Proof" / "The Accountability Gap" structure exactly. Pairs with Ch 10's "Common Bonds" as: *the math nobody was doing → the bonds that tie us*.

5. **Voice-fit with the rest of the book.** Pairs cleanly with Author's Note (the wind-tunnel discipline + grandfather's quiet inventiveness); pairs with Ch 8 + Ch 9 as the math becomes explicit in later chapters; pairs with Ch 10's closing reflection.

6. **Pitchable to agents and editors.** Distinctive phrase that signals both narrative content (lineage motif) and analytical content (framework-arrival math). Agents respond to titles with this dual-load structure.

**Runner-up:** A — "What It Took." Strong on most modules but slightly less distinctive (M7) than E. If E proves problematic in some way during further review, A is the strong fallback that carries dual reading and class-neutral access.
