## 1. AIT positioning (canonical, from C9 patch §3)

The Abundance Interference Taxonomy is the framework's epistemic core. **Layers are organizational scaffolding, not load-bearing structure.** Layer additions, mergers, or renames are organizational revisions, not framework-integrity events. The AIT operates on any cost-scarcity relationship independently of a containing taxonomy — its validity is a property of the cost-scarcity relationship itself, not of any layer set.

Layers provide four services that AIT alone does not:
- **Systematic coverage** — ensuring no structural allocation asymmetry is invisible for lack of a category to see it through.
- **Vocabulary portability** — giving the framework's key terms labels that travel (Harvey / Klein / Raworth compression test).
- **Pedagogical shape** — letting the book introduce the framework one scarcity axis at a time.
- **Scholarship seed points** — giving future work citeable footholds per layer.

When tension arises between layer clarity and AIT rigor, AIT wins. Layer revisions are cheap; AIT revisions are structural. The current 10-layer set has stabilized across multiple revisions (v1.24.0's regressed 7 → v1.21.0 canonical 10 → v1.2.1-ratified per Part 2 gap-close).

## 2. The 10 canonical abundance layers

Each layer is defined by (a) its scarcity axis, (b) its paradigmatic allocation asymmetry, (c) its canonical cases, and (d) its current naming status. Merger/primitiveness gates cleared per v1.21.0 and v1.2.1; two-path and multi-scale × 2 env cleared per Part 2. Detailed per-layer pressure assessments in `commons_bonds_rigor_pass_2026-04-22_v1_0_1.md` §3.

### 2.1 Atmospheric *(rename pending)*

**Scarcity axis:** breathable, stable atmospheric medium — shared, non-excludable on capture, excludable on cost-imposition.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture emissions-externalized value; fenceline communities, workers, and future generations bear respiratory and climate cost. Path 2 shield is spatial + temporal distance — pollution travels, harm lags.

**Canonical cases:** occupational respiratory exposure; fenceline-community air quality; emissions-led climate foreclosure (overlapping with Ecological on climate content).

**Scope boundary:** Atmospheric is the shared-medium dimension (air-as-carrier). Ecological is the services-from-biosphere dimension. Overlap at climate / emissions is real; chapter-level case selection handles assignment.

**Naming status:** rename pending (Part 5 priority-1). Candidates: Atmospheric / Life-support / Habitability / Biophysical. Current name fails Test 20/23 compression. "Life-support" flagged as disqualified per roster unless the abundance-naming pattern is revisited concurrently.

### 2.2 Geographic *(rename pending)*

**Scarcity axis:** location — place-based access, stranded-asset cost, distance-to-market, place-specific extraction.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture locational advantage; stranded communities and regions with low bargaining power bear cost. Path 2 shield is distance itself.

**Canonical cases:** extractive-region sacrifice zones (Appalachian coal, mountaintop-removal-adjacent communities); housing-affordability-in-expensive-metros; infrastructure deserts; climate-migration foreclosure.

**Scope boundary:** Geographic is place / distance. Ecological is biosphere services. Atmospheric is shared medium.

**Naming status:** rename pending (Part 5 priority-2). "Spatial" is the primary candidate — resolves the off-Earth multi-scale name failure documented in v1.0.0 stress test. Current "Geographic cost" compresses acceptably on Earth; strained for orbital-habitat / Lagrange-point contexts.

### 2.3 Temporal *(stable)*

**Scarcity axis:** time — temporal separation between generations, compounding effects, deferred / accelerated cost-transfer.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** present captures; future bears. Tier 3 (Dynastic Labor Cost) is the canonical worked example — evaporates under no-temporal-separation abundance. Path 2 shield is temporal distance itself — future generations cannot advocate.

**Canonical cases:** pension / retirement cost-shifting; climate as temporal cost; deferred maintenance / infrastructure; **120+ hour-week consent-normalization case** (Part 1 §5 canonical case for consent-normalization shield type); early-career trade-off compounding.

**Scope boundary:** Temporal is time-as-separator. Agency covers consent-under-constraint (overlaps at 120+ hour-week case); Institutional covers organizational structure (overlaps at pension governance).

**Naming status:** stable. No rename. Compresses cleanly on Tests 20/23. Strongest Goal-2 direct-contribution of any layer.

### 2.4 Institutional *(stable)*

**Scarcity axis:** organizational coordination, hierarchical position, collective-action infrastructure.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms / orgs capture via organizational position; labor, subordinates, external stakeholders bear. Path 2 shield is institutional opacity — decisions made in rooms unobservable to those bearing cost.

**Canonical cases:** corporate hierarchy under up-or-out dynamics (consulting / finance / law-firm cases); company-town historical material; CEO-era organizational decisions with subordinate cost-bearing; NGO / university governance opacity.

**Scope boundary:** Institutional is organizational / hierarchical. Political is governmental / regulatory. State institutions sit at the boundary; case-level assignment follows whether the allocation-conflict is organizational (Institutional) or distributional-governmental (Political).

**Naming status:** stable. No rename.

**Pre-drafting gates:** NDA review; subordinate anonymization protocol. CEO-era case content is load-bearing for this layer; gates address Test 13 career-risk exposure (secondary per v1.2.1) at the case level.

### 2.5 Demographic *(rename pending)*

**Scarcity axis:** cultural / kinship / identity bonds — specific cultural attachments that are not infinitely substitutable.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** majority cultural-group captures (assimilation norms, cultural dominance); minorities, diaspora, indigenous communities bear assimilation / loss cost. Path 2 shield is cultural distance — majority cannot see what it costs minorities.

**Canonical cases:** indigenous cultural-loss under development pressure; diaspora kinship-bond foreclosure; minority-language displacement; ethnic-cultural identity cost under majority cultural dominance.

**Scope boundary:** Demographic is cultural / kinship / identity. Cohesion is civic trust / collective-action infrastructure. Bilateral independence confirmed v1.21.0 (Bowling Alone suburbs — demographic abundance, cohesion scarcity; small religious communities — demographic scarcity, cohesion abundance). Language preservation sits at Demographic ↔ Informational scope edge; current assignment is Demographic (language-as-kinship-carrier) with Informational cross-reference where language is epistemic-transmission specific.

**Naming status:** rename pending (Part 5 priority-1). Candidates: Communal / Cultural / Kinship / Belonging / Fellowship. "Common Bonds" meta-confusion risk with book title. Current name fails Test 20/23 (reads census-clinical, not cultural-lived-experience).

### 2.6 Ecological *(stable)*

**Scarcity axis:** ecosystem services — pollination, hydrology, soil fertility, biodiversity-enabled regulation, climate stability (partial overlap with Atmospheric).

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture ecosystem-service value; downstream communities, future generations, non-human species bear. Tier 6 (Ecological Carrying Cost) is the canonical worked example — services evaporate under ecosystem-service abundance, tier evaporates. Path 2 shield is ecosystem-service distance — cost lags, harm accumulates.

**Canonical cases:** mountaintop-removal water / soil foreclosure; pesticide / pollinator collapse; climate-as-ecosystem-service foreclosure; fisheries collapse; natural-commons enclosure.

**Scope boundary:** Ecological sits **nested under Habitability** (the broader biophysical condition). Overlap with Atmospheric on climate / emissions is real — Atmospheric is air-as-medium, Ecological is services-from-biosphere; climate sits at the intersection.

**Naming status:** stable. No rename.

**Coordination note:** if Atmospheric renames to "Habitability," the nested relationship becomes an explicit cross-reference in both layer intros.

### 2.7 Political *(stable with US-partisan-connotation watch)*

**Scarcity axis:** distributional conflict over scarce allocations — who decides what gets allocated to whom, under what legitimacy claim.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** concentrated interests capture regulatory / legislative allocation; diffuse publics bear captured-regulation cost. Tier 8 (Political Capture) is the canonical worked example. Path 2 shield is political opacity + access asymmetry.

**Canonical cases:** regulatory capture by industries with interlocking healthcare-financing networks; extractive-industry capture of state regulatory apparatus (Ch. 2 content); pharmaceutical lobbying networks. Per v1.2.1 §7 Test 13 correction, career-risk is load-bearing at layer level via canonical cases.

**Scope boundary:** Political is governmental / regulatory distributional conflict. Institutional is organizational / hierarchical. State institutions in their organizational mode (NASA operations, agency administration) sit Institutional; state institutions in their distributional mode (regulatory capture, legislative allocation) sit Political.

**Naming status:** stable with US-partisan-connotation watch retained. Current name passes Test 20/23 but reads partisan-valenced to US readers — positioning prose in layer intro must mitigate.

**Pre-drafting gates:** canonical-case presentation protocol for regulatory-capture-in-healthcare-financing content (Test 13 + Character 7 + Character 21 convergent pressure).

### 2.8 Cohesion *(name TBD)*

**Scarcity axis:** civic trust, collective-action infrastructure, social-fabric density — capacity for non-kin coordination.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** platforms / employers / markets capture from density and proximity without contributing to cohesion maintenance; workers and individuals bear isolation-at-density cost. Path 2 shield is individual invisibility — cohesion erosion is collective; no individual lens sees the full loss.

**Canonical cases:** gig-economy atomization; knowledge-worker isolation at urban concentration; **NYC consulting-years isolation** (canonical non-co-occurring case demonstrating Cohesion scarcity at Demographic abundance — pedagogical requirement for layer intro per v1.21.0); suburban Bowling-Alone pattern.

**Scope boundary:** Cohesion is civic / collective trust. Demographic is cultural / kinship identity. Bilateral independence confirmed v1.21.0. Social / relational cross-layer concept subsumes here (Part 2 §5.1).

**Naming status:** name TBD (Part 5 priority-2). Candidates: Cohesion / Social-Fabric / Civic-Ties / Communion. Current "Cohesion" reads borrowed from Putnam (Test 18 overlap pressure). "Social-Fabric" reads most organizer-usable.

### 2.9 Informational *(name TBD)*

**Scarcity axis:** evidentiary access — information-as-precondition for allocation evaluation and consent.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** firms capture information-advantage; consumers, regulators, workers, future generations bear evidentiary-asymmetry cost. Path 2 shield is suppression, complexity, deliberate opacity.

**Canonical cases:** **tobacco internal-research suppression; pharma data suppression; opioid-death geography mapping to Appalachian counties** (primary Test 13 load-bearer per v1.2.1 §7 — canonical-case presentation protocol required); data-commons enclosure; AI-era epistemic-asymmetry (explicit Test 19 decade-out handling).

**Scope boundary:** Informational is evidentiary asymmetry. Agency is coercion-under-information-deficit (Kantian pair — see §5). Political is regulatory-informational capture (cross-layer cases exist; canonical-case assignment follows whether the primary asymmetry is evidence-access or distributional-allocation).

**Naming status:** name TBD (Part 5 priority-2). Candidates: Informational / Epistemic / Veridical / Evidentiary. "Evidentiary" reads strongest in the labor-lawyer-2039 test (courtroom-native); "Informational" bland; "Epistemic" philosophical; "Veridical" unusual.

**Pre-drafting gates:** canonical-case presentation protocol (tobacco / pharma / opioid). Career-risk routes through nursing-adjacent professional channels per Test 13.

### 2.10 Agency *(name TBD)*

**Scarcity axis:** non-coerced choice — capacity to allocate one's own labor, time, and participation without structural compulsion.

**Paradigmatic asymmetry:** coercer (employer, platform, state, information-asymmetric counterparty) captures; coerced bears non-consent cost. Path 2 shield is **consent-normalization** (v1.2.1 §5.2): individual consent, when treated by the surrounding social / legal / institutional framework as terminally closing the accounting, operates as a structural shield even when individual awareness is complete. Strongest primitiveness result of the v1.21.0 candidates.

**Canonical cases:** gig / contract labor coercion-under-information-deficit; employment-relationship power asymmetry; company-town consent-under-constraint; **120+ hour-week consent-normalization case** (Part 1 §5 — canonical for the shield type itself, shared with Temporal); behavioral / psychological coercion (cognitive / psychological cross-layer concept subsumes here per Part 2 §5.2).

**Scope boundary:** Agency is coercion-structure. Informational is evidentiary asymmetry (Kantian pair — see §5). Institutional is organizational hierarchy (overlaps at employment-relationship cases; Agency prices the coercion, Institutional prices the hierarchy).

**Naming status:** name TBD (Part 5 priority-2). Candidates: Agency / Autonomy / Non-coercion. "Agency" carries principal-agent baggage in economics (Test 20/23 + Character M1 pressure). "Autonomy" cleaner philosophically; "Non-coercion" awkward in compound-noun form.

**Cross-ideological positioning is load-bearing** — libertarian non-coercion and leftist anti-exploitation both land on this layer; Character 3 (right critic) specifically flagged that libertarian appropriation creates misread risk that the cross-spectrum traction itself mitigates.

## 5. Methodology notes from the Part 2 pass

### 5.1 Canonical cases define a layer's pressure profile

A layer's canonical cases are not illustrations attached to an abstract definition — they are what the layer means in practice. Career-risk, legal exposure, and professional-retaliation pressures route through specific layers via their canonical cases, not through the layer abstraction.

Empirical confirmation from Part 2:
- Informational's canonical cases (tobacco / pharma / opioid) make it the primary Test 13 career-risk load-bearer.
- Political's canonical cases (regulatory capture in healthcare financing) make it the secondary Test 13 carrier.
- Institutional's canonical cases (CEO-era content) make it a tertiary Test 13 case-level concern.
- The other seven layers are low on Test 13 at layer level because their canonical cases do not involve industries with professional-retaliation capacity.

**Book implication:** when introducing a layer, its canonical cases must be named alongside its scarcity axis. Scope boundary statements at layer intros should reference canonical cases, not abstract definitions alone.

### 5.2 Character 25 (NASA Langley grandfather) is a system-wide ethical anchor

The grandfather-at-NASA-Langley character functions as an existence-proof counterweight across at least five layers (Atmospheric, Institutional, Demographic, Ecological, Informational). Without this dimension visible in the book's positioning, the framework reads purely critical — which Test 25 (steelmannability) flags as fragile.

**Book implication:** the existence-proof framing — that honest institutions and principled allocation do exist and that the framework's rigor is diagnostic rather than universally condemnatory — should be visible in early book positioning, not only in a closing gesture.

### 5.3 Kantian consent-precondition pair (Informational ↔ Agency)

Informational and Agency form a Kantian consent-precondition pair: consent requires information; information-structural coercion is itself an Agency-layer case. The two layers cross-reference each other cleanly.

**Book implication:** each layer intro should surface the pair relationship. Chapters that use 120+ hour-week case content (Temporal + Agency) may cross-reference Informational where the consent-normalization shield operates through information asymmetry as well as through social framework.

### 5.4 Single-layer cost severance is cost severance

The framework is additive and compositional, not conjunctive. A case that activates one layer is a full instance of cost severance — the framework does not require multi-layer co-occurrence to identify cost-bearing. Layers price their own scarcity axis independently.

Canonical single-layer examples:
- A retiree discovering that their pension was restructured on terms they never understood — **Temporal** alone.
- A knowledge worker recognizing the consent-normalization pattern in their own employment — **Agency** alone (or Agency + Temporal).
- The NYC consulting-years isolation case — **Cohesion** scarcity at Demographic abundance — is canonically a single-layer case by design. The non-co-occurrence is the pedagogical mechanism for distinguishing Cohesion from Demographic (per v1.21.0 ratification).

Multi-layer activation (Ch. 7 asteroid-miner activating 9–10 layers; Ch. 8 coal activating many) demonstrates framework coverage and universality. Goal-2 direct-contribution work — "accept a job with open eyes and honest accounting" — often succeeds at single-layer precision: a worker pricing their own tier on one axis, because that is the only axis their specific situation is severing on, is the framework working as designed.

**Book implication:** layer chapters should not require readers to locate themselves in multiple layers before the framework earns its keep. A single-layer identification is a successful use. Chapter-closing examples may show multi-layer cases, but the framework's directly-useful floor is one-layer recognition.

## 8. Revisitability

This document is revised when:

1. A new layer candidate passes both gates (merger + primitiveness) and clears AIT + two-path + multi-scale × 2 env under the current 10.
2. An existing layer is formally renamed via Part 5 naming cohort ratification.
