The book's framework register, as ratified 2026-05-02 (Ch 10 line 125 cost-component pass):

**The move:** Commons Bonds follows Elinor Ostrom's methodological path. Ostrom did not claim to catalog every commons-governance arrangement that exists; she picked a small set of empirical cases and demonstrated patterns through them, leaving the universe of commons-arrangements explicitly open. Commons Bonds takes the same stance: it picks a couple of handfuls of commons and a small set of Cᵢ cost-components and uses them illustratively, making no claim to have enumerated every commons or every component a different extraction might surface.

**Vocabulary consequence — drop "canonical":** Stop calling the Cᵢ list "canonical." Stop calling the commons enumeration "canonical." The framework's lists are illustrative, not closed/canonical/exhaustive. Use phrases like "Ch 8's illustrative cost-component list," "the framework's named cost-components," or "the components McDowell County coal surfaces" rather than "the canonical Cᵢ schema."

**How to apply this when editing or auditing:**
- When a chapter names a cost-component not in Ch 8's eight (currently: Direct Health, Environmental Degradation, Community Disruption, Foreclosure, Lineage Labor, Knowledge and Cultural, Political Capture, Temporal Displacement), don't auto-flag it as a vocabulary regression. The book is allowed to gesture at unenumerated components.
- When such a gesture appears, ask whether it (a) tightly maps to one of the eight (just shorthand — usually fix to the named component), (b) gestures at a real-but-unnamed component (the writer's prerogative under the Ostrom-path register), or (c) reads as an ad-hoc reach that confuses more than it earns. Apply judgment per the local prose, not a list-conformance rule.
- For closing-chapter recognition passages where the prose names a sequence of components, prefer the Ch 8 illustrative names (so a reader who tracked Ch 8 recognizes the list) — but this is a stylistic-coherence preference, not a hard rule.
- The Cᵢ list is open; new components can be admitted via the Four Gates (Commons Inversion Test, units consistency, boundedness, independence). The eight in Ch 8 are case-specific to McDowell coal; a different extraction surfaces a different set.

**Why this matters:** "Canonical" implies a closed taxonomy that the framework deliberately does not claim. Using "canonical" in editing/audit framing pushes the writer toward enforced list-conformance, which contradicts the book's Ostrom-path stance. The register move is meaningful and should be visible in editorial reasoning.
