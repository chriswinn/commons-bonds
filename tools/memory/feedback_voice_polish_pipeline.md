When working on Commons Bonds (Book 1) prose:

**The pipeline:** Author dumps scattered notes/thoughts → Claude sifts for relevance + book-worthiness → Claude polishes into publish-ready prose. Author relies on Claude heavily across all three stages.

**Voice expectation:** Do not preserve author voice exactly when transitioning from substrate to publisher-facing prose. Make sure content flows as good grammar and good prose. The author voice in raw dumps is not the target voice for the published book; it is the source material to be shaped.

**Why:** The author has limited writing time and energy and is generating substrate at scale. The author has explicitly framed his role as substrate-generator and Claude's role as sifter + polisher. Preserving raw author voice in published prose would lose the polish the book needs. Author confirmed 2026-05-04 going into Ch 1 + Ch 3 β.2 prose drafting.

**How to apply:**

1. **Substrate capture (verbatim preservation still applies):** When the author dumps raw notes into chat, capture them to `research/story-drafts/` files preserving voice + typos as dumped. This is internal scaffolding (WP#10) and the verbatim preservation is for the author's reference, not the book.

2. **When drafting publisher-facing prose from substrate:** Polish actively. Fix grammar. Restructure sentences for flow. Re-order content for argumentative coherence. Cut what doesn't serve the chapter's framework argument. Translate raw images into book-register prose. The dump is raw material; the prose is the work.

3. **Apply the framework-not-memoir filter actively, not as confirmation:** Author flagged 2026-05-04 that Ch 1 needs to establish credibility + show that he noticed cost severance + show why he wrote a framework — NOT to be a memoir. Apply this filter when sifting substrate. Cut beats that are biographical color without framework relevance. Don't just defer those calls back to the author.

4. **What still goes back to the author:**
   - Architectural calls about the chapter's spine and beat-order against drafted prose
   - Scenes that are trauma-adjacent (son-NICU, ex-wife adjacent material) — needs author's clear-headed engagement
   - Decisions about what private material crosses publisher-facing gates (consent, NDA, wife-medical-privacy)
   - Final review before any prose is treated as locked

5. **What does NOT go back to the author:**
   - Grammar fixes
   - Sentence-level prose flow
   - Cutting biographical color that doesn't serve the framework argument
   - Translating raw images into book-register prose
   - Smoothing structure across multiple substrate dumps that share material

**Net:** lean into active editorial work. The author is reliable for substrate + judgment + final decisions; rely heavily on Claude for the sifting + polishing in between.

**Compatible with:**
- WP#10 two-layer content origination discipline (substrate stays internal; published prose is shaped)
- Substance-drives-length feedback (no word-count targets; polish to what content needs)
- Pattern 2 register (demonstrate the affordance through the polished prose; do not codify methodology)
- Ostrom-path illustrative register (no canonical-completeness claims)
