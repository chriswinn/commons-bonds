**Date ratified:** 2026-05-25
**Type:** project (project-phase framing)

## Summary

The *Commons Bonds* book is content-complete as of 2026-05-24 (all 10 chapters drafted + Tech Appendix + AuthorsNote). Active work is iterative polish (chapter Stage 3 + Stage 4 + Stage 5 cycles in flight) AND an essay/op-ed marketing strategy aimed at landing an agent and a trade-publisher contract for the complete book. The essays are downstream marketing instruments, not platform-builders writing-the-book-as-they-go.

## Why

Claude (this assistant), during the 2026-05-24 Chesapeake Requiem NYRB review-essay Stage 0 session, defaulted to "platform-builds-the-book" reasoning when applying the Stage 0 rigor template. The framing conflated two distinct project phases:

1. **Writing-while-platform-building** — where chapter derivatives serve TWO functions: stress-testing the manuscript AND building audience.
2. **Marketing-a-finished-book** — where essays serve only positioning + reach.

The cascade-plan v2 §7 nomenclature ("Wave 3 candidates") implicitly carries the platform-building framing forward into a phase that doesn't need it. Without correction, future sessions would repeat the framing error.

Empirical anchor: the Chesapeake Requiem Stage 0 verdict ("Path (c) DEFER to Wave 3 Q2–Q3 2027") was correctly rebid by the author as a category error — Q2–Q3 2027 isn't "Wave 3" in the platform-building sense; it's pre-launch or post-launch in the marketing sense. Honest disposition was PARK, not Wave 3 commitment.

## How to apply

When evaluating essay/op-ed candidates (Stage 0 rigor passes; cascade-plan amendments; venue selection; sequencing; bandwidth-tradeoff judgments), treat the master criterion as **marketing the complete book**, not **platform-building while writing it**. Specifically:

- **Dimension (a) thunder-stealing** is cleaner arithmetic — apparatus is fixed in the manuscript; the question is reveal-pacing pre-launch.
- **Dimension (d) framework-positioning lock-in** is much less acute — framework framing is locked in the manuscript; essays cannot "drift" anything not already drifted.
- **Dimension (f) source-material self-sufficiency** is less acute — chapters are fixed; essay drafts don't risk drifting the chapter's framing.
- **Dimension (g) strategic portfolio value** becomes *marketing-cell coverage* (which audience cell does this venue + form reach), not *platform-cell coverage* (what does this teach me about the book's framing).
- **Q2–Q3 2027 candidates** sit in either (i) pre-publication launch window (publisher-coordinated; not free author choices) IF Wave 1+2 converted to contract, OR (ii) continued platform-deepening IF Wave 1+2 underperformed. The "Wave 3 candidates" framing in cascade plan v2 §7 implicitly assumes (ii); it doesn't coherently describe (i).
- **PARK as a verdict** is legitimate when a candidate's value depends on which scenario lands — pre-stage the analysis as cheap insurance, decide when the publishing situation clarifies.
- Distinguish *pre-publication platform essays* (build audience for an unsold book) from *pre-publication launch essays* (coordinate with publisher marketing 3–6 months pre-pub) from *post-publication backlist essays* (extend visibility after launch). Different functions, different selection criteria, different bandwidth tradeoffs.
