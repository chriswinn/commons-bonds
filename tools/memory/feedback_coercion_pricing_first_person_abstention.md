The author declines, in his own first-person voice, to compute or assign a dollar
figure to the coercion vector or to reparations owed to a group he does not belong
to. He finds it improper for *him* to do so in this context.

**This is a first-person abstention only.** The author takes NO position on who may
or may not enter such a number and does not qualify anyone's standing. Do not frame
the position as "only the affected may price it," "theirs to enter," or any wording
that assigns/restricts standing — that would make the author an arbiter of who is
permitted, which is the opposite of what he intends. The framework offers the ledger;
he simply does not fill in that column himself.

**Operational rule for Claude/AI internal sub-agents** (drafting in the framework's
or author's voice): never compute or insert a coercion or reparations dollar figure
into publisher-facing prose. You MAY name and credit the reparations-economics
scholars who do this work (Darity, Mullen, Hamilton, Coates, Conley, et al. — cite
exact forms from the book's bibliography), but never produce the number on the
author's behalf. "Sub-agents" here means Claude internal-scaffolding agents, not
people.

**Why:** Ratified 2026-06-09. The author's ledger ethic is already published-draft
in the Atlantic Ideas essay (`publishing/essays/atlantic-ideas-pricing-honestly/essay.md`):
"Putting a price on the costs of coercion borne by a group I do not belong to is not
mine to do, and I leave that work to those who are continuing it. What the framework
offers is the ledger." Moral + reputational: it is not his to price, AND he refuses
to arbitrate others' standing. Note: the essay's existing "is theirs to enter" tail
edges toward assignment; flagged to the author for possible softening, his call.

**How to apply:** When any publisher-facing prose touches coercion, slavery, forced
labor, or reparations, the framework establishes that the commons (freedom/autonomy)
is real and has a real, person-revealed price (e.g., the manumission/self-purchase
price), but the author records the structure and declines to assign the backward
number himself, crediting the reparations field as those carrying the work. Related:
