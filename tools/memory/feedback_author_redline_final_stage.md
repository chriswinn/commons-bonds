The prose pipeline's last step is the author reading the promoted text and
redlining whatever catches his eye. Author statement (2026-06-11): "the best
last step is to have me read and redline whatever catches my eyes as that so
far results in our best prose."

**Why:** Two independent lines of evidence converged 2026-06-10/11. (1) The
Ch 1–3 Fable bake-off showed that wherever the author's hand had recently
been, Fable variants *regressed* it — both variants re-edited the
author-held Tubman paragraph the day after his own pass and lost his rhythm;
the Ch 1 verdict was KEEP-CANONICAL largely because the variants' apparent
freshness was his own pending redline. Fable's verified strength is coverage
restoration and factual discipline; the author's is voice. (2) The author
revealed the design intent: the long Noema/portfolio pause was not
procrastination but a deliberate breakdown-and-rebuild (TA → repo refactor →
prose pipeline), and pushing Fable to write better prose was *intended to
push the author to write better prose* — it worked. The redline stage is
where that compounding happens.

**How to apply:** (1) Pipeline order for every chapter/essay promotion:
blind draft from brief → judge/assembly → **author read+redline** → light
Pass 3.3 refire → Stage 4/5. Do not mark anything submission-ready that has
not had the redline pass. (2) Author redline files
(`research/story-drafts/*redline*`, `*author-edits*`) are the strongest
voice signal in any drafting substrate — drafters write *toward* them.
(3) NEVER auto-edit a passage the author personally redlined (the Tubman
lesson); treat such passages as HELD. (4) A long-idle PROPOSED state may be
a deliberate rebuild pause, not a stall — surface it, but don't pressure it
as overdue. Related: [[feedback_no_invented_factual_claims_in_publisher_facing_prose]],
[[feedback_substrate_critical_editorial_input]], [[feedback_voice_polish_pipeline]].
