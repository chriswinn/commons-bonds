**Status tracker:** [`research/outreach/_pipeline/consent-tracker.md`](../../research/outreach/_pipeline/consent-tracker.md) — single source of truth for "where are we with subject X today?" queries across the full publisher-facing portfolio. This file is the POLICY; the tracker is the per-subject OPERATIONAL STATE. Update the tracker when consent state changes; this policy file changes only when the discipline itself amends.

For external publisher-facing prose (Commons Bonds book chapters, Noema / Aeon / other essay submissions, op-eds), apply these defaults whenever a real, identifiable person is named:

- **Living subjects → "named-pending-consent."** Anonymize in the working draft as the safe default. Pursue signed consent in parallel. If consent lands before submission, swap the name back in. If consent does not land before the submission window, submit anonymized; restore the name in future printings or downstream artifacts (book chapter, reprint) once consent lands. Anonymization should preserve the load-bearing details (cost-severance specifics, mutual-aid patterns, biographical contour) — the trade-off is the personal name, not the substantive material.
- **Deceased subjects → "named with courtesy-notify-surviving-family-if-reachable."** Name in the draft. Pre-submission, make a good-faith effort to identify and notify reachable surviving family. If unreachable after that effort, proceed with naming. The "courtesy notify" is not a veto; it is an act of respect.
- **Place names** (Fox Hill, McDowell County, Libby, etc.) → safe to name without per-person consent.
- **Public-record exception → public officials / institutional spokespersons quoted from on-record speech are attributable by default.** Press releases issued under their name, signed Congressional or legislative testimony, on-the-record interviews granted to journalists, named op-eds — all of these are public utterances made in institutional capacity and do not require consent for attribution. Standard journalism names them. Anonymizing such quotes reads evasive and denies the subject professional credit for positions they have publicly staked. **Courtesy notification before publication** (a heads-up to the subject or their communications coordinator listing the verbatim quotes attributed to them, asking only for citation-accuracy verification) is the right discipline — not consent. This exception does NOT extend to *characterizations* of the subject's personal views, motivations, or beliefs beyond what the on-record quotes themselves say; for that, the living-subject consent default applies.

**Why:** Ratified 2026-05-09; public-record exception added 2026-05-12 in the context of two Fox Hill watermen — Phat's (alive, easily identifiable: owns a mechanic shop with his name on the sign and crab pots stacked behind the locked fence) and Biggie (deceased 30+ years, oysterman; author's father knew him from boyhood). Author wants publisher-facing prose to honor named subjects — anonymization is a draft-time fallback, not a values-aligned end state. Consent collection is an upgrade window, not a precondition for submission. Symmetric with the substitution-hypothesis pattern already in use for Darity / Colden interviews (live conversation = upgrade opportunity, not block).

**Worked example — Phat anonymization ratified 2026-05-20.** Author chose to anonymize Phat in working draft per the discipline's safe default, after post-petition research expansion (Tonya O'Connor VMRC Petition 449 + WHRO Oct 2025 + Maryland Matters Apr 2026 + Banner May 2026 + Sherfinski 2022 + Earl Swift 2018 + Smithsonian 2009 + 1,266 public commenters) made Ch 3's voice inventory substantively rich at the public-record level (~30+ named-quotable subjects). The Fox Hill anchor is preserved via anonymized identifier — all load-bearing details (mechanic shop; crab pots; multi-decade work on the water; specific community contour) survive without the personal name. Consent pursuit moves to relationship-pace, not publishing-gate; outreach package at `research/outreach/subjects/phat/` remains available; name restores in future printings if consent lands later. Biggie continues under courtesy-notify-family discipline. **This worked example demonstrates the discipline operating as designed: living-named-pending-consent → anonymize safely without losing chapter substance.** The Noema submission gate that had been open ≥6 days collapsed in one decision once the author saw that the expanded source inventory made the anonymization choice substantively low-cost.

**How to apply:** When introducing a named real person in any publisher-facing artifact, classify as living vs. deceased and apply the relevant default. Track consent / notify status in the relevant source-material file under `research/story-drafts/` (or the per-subject folder under `research/outreach/subjects/`) and add the consent step to the artifact's pre-submission checklist. Before each new draft, check status — do not silently re-anonymize a subject whose consent has already been collected. The default applies regardless of who is doing the drafting (Claude, author, collaborator).
