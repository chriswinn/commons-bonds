## 1. HEADLINE — the highest-impact independent findings

**H1 — The bidirectional atmospheric-climate-debt literature is the single most
dangerous genuine gap, because it occupies the book's exact configurational-
novelty joint and is verifiably absent.** Fanning & Hickel 2023 ("Compensation
for atmospheric appropriation," *Nature Sustainability*), CARICOM's reparations
plan, and the COP27/28 Loss & Damage Fund already price an atmospheric commons
as a debt running *both* forward (foreclosing future foreclosure) and *backward*
(historical emissions owed), on an SCC spine — which is precisely the "one
apparatus, both directions" claim the book stakes as novel. Confirmed grep=0
corpus-wide (Fanning/CARICOM/loss-and-damage/atmospheric-appropriation all 0;
Hickel present only as degrowth). The reviewer line ("what does the
Foreclosure + Restitution pair add to SCC-based climate-debt accounting beyond
renaming it?") has no answer on the page. The defensible answer exists
(per-extraction-event micro-instrument vs national-aggregate macro-accounting;
the reserve-as-optionality move) but must be **written and the kin literature
named.** This is the convergence of two lenses (reparations + resource-econ) on
the same joint and it is real.

**H2 — The analytic climate-ethics + libertarian-rights flank is genuinely
empty, and one absence is embarrassing: John Broome.** Broome=0 everywhere. The
book names its instrument the *Restitution* Bond, argues the discount rate is a
moral not technical choice, and argues future goods should not be discounted for
pure time preference — these are Broome's positions (*Climate Matters* 2012;
*Counting the Cost* 1992), uncited. A philosophy reviewer reads this as the book
reinventing the ethics-of-discounting wheel without the wheelmaker. Same flank:
**Gardiner** (the tyranny-of-the-contemporary threat to the whole theory of
change) and **Nozick** (the missing *rights*-flank objection, asymmetric to the
Marxist objection the book *does* engage in Ch10 — and winnable via Nozick's own
Lockean proviso). All three confirmed absent. This is the highest-confidence,
cleanest-to-fix cluster in the entire verification.

**H3 — The Dasgupta 2021 Review (not the 1979 textbook) and Solow-in-the-
S_max<1-prose are real but narrower than the lens framed them.** The book is not
ignorant of Dasgupta or Solow (both in TA). The defensible gap: (a) the 2021
HM-Treasury Review — the field's most visible recent statement of pricing the
natural commons — is unengaged, and a one-paragraph "instrument vs.
national-wealth-accounting" distinction would inoculate the book cheaply; (b)
Solow 1974 lives only in the TA, while the *S_max<1 stance it underwrites is
taken in Ch6 prose* without naming the weak-vs-strong-sustainability debate the
stance enters. Fix both at low cost; do **not** dramatize them as "the book
doesn't know its field" — that version is false and would mislead the author.

**H4 — The Code-of-Capital / rentier-creation objection is the most dangerous
*genuinely-contested* vulnerability — but the matrix UNDERSELLS the book's
existing answer.** Verified: Ch5 L164–L178 already builds the financialization
rebuttal explicitly ("pricing the commons is just financialization by another
route... Accounting a cost is not coding an asset. Pistor's modules code assets;
this book accounts costs"), and fuses it with the Sandel and flattening
objections into one defended position "from one position, the position Polanyi
and Fraser built." So the charge is **engaged and answered in Ch5**, not
unanswered. The live exposures are narrower: (a) the rebuttal leans on the
*assertion* "this book accounts costs" without naming Pistor's four legal
attributes (priority/durability/universality/convertibility) and showing the
bond deliberately lacks transferability/convertibility — the move that would
make it a *demonstration* not a *claim*; (b) Christophers is engaged only via
*The Price Is Wrong* (2024), not *Rentier Capitalism* (2020), the book that
actually threatens the instrument. Strengthen the existing Ch5 answer; don't
treat it as missing.

**H5 — Cross-lens structural pattern: the book's real failure mode is
attribution-distribution, not absent engagement.** The magnitude-work lands in
the narrative chapters (Ch2/Ch3/Ch8) with the informing thinkers *unnamed*,
while the attribution lands in Ch5/Ch6/Ch9. Every lens hit a version of this
(reef Restitution Bond runs in Ch3 with Darity/Coates silent; Nigeria leakage
runs in Ch4 with no Zucman/Ndikumana; worked ton in Ch8 with no Costanza/
Hotelling). A reviewer who reads any single magnitude chapter without the
attribution chapters sees uncredited application. This is a higher-yield, lower-
cost fix surface than any single new citation.

## 3. OVER-CLAIMS TO FIX — where the matrix asserts a relation the prose can't (yet) defend

Ordered by exposure. **Note:** two of the matrix's *own* "MISSING" cells are
themselves over-claims-in-reverse (the matrix under-claims what the prose does);
those are corrected in §0 and not repeated as flaws here.

1. **"aggregate Bond < aggregate Residual Commons Value is a theorem" (meta-claim 3)
   and the Hotelling Identity (RCV − Hotelling rent = CS per unit) — both at risk
   of being definitional tautologies dressed as results.** If B is constructed as
   a ≤1 share of RCV, B<RCV is true by definition and proves nothing; if RCV is
   *defined* as the sum of the other two Hotelling terms, "CS>0" is true by
   construction and is the opposite of a robustness result. The book claims both
   as substantive (a "bridge to standard theory"; a "near-theorem"). **This is the
   deepest exposure to a quantitative methodologist and the synthesis cannot
   resolve it from the matrix.** TA-owning session must confirm against TA §4/§14.1
   that the three quantities are *independently measured and reconcile* (empirical
   content), not rearranged. Until confirmed, do not call either a theorem/near-
   theorem in chapter prose. *(Cross-lens: flagged independently by resource-econ;
   I am elevating it because both formal claims share the same failure mode and it
   is the one finding I could not verify either way.)*

2. **"EXTENDS Pigou + Ostrom into a setting neither addressed" — the broad version
   is false; only the narrow version is defensible.** The Hartwick/genuine-savings/
   Dasgupta tradition HAS addressed pricing the drawn-down resource commons; Ostrom
   HAS studied appropriation-from-a-depletable-stock (groundwater). The true claim
   is the *level and instrument form* (per-extraction-event, bidirectional, reserve-
   against), not the *idea* of pricing natural capital or governing stock commons.
   The matrix sometimes states the broad version; a Dasgupta/Ostrom-literate
   reviewer rejects it. **Make the narrow version consistently.**

3. **Harvey "we add the number to Harvey's diagnosis" is asserted in the meta-claim
   but landed in ZERO chapters per the matrix's own status (Ch9 keeps only "changed-
   the-conceivable"; Ch2 cut it; Ch3 does the number-work with Harvey unnamed).**
   Verified: Harvey appears 2× in chapters, 6× in TA — present, but the *magnitude*
   relation is not where the meta-claim says it is. Either land it (name Harvey at
   the Ch3 reef-number work) or drop "Harvey diagnoses; RCV measures" from the
   meta-claim. *(The "needs an outside" spatial-fix parallel to Ch4 L137 is a
   genuine unexploited alliance.)*

4. **Reparations economics is wrongly inside the "they diagnosed, we measure" meta-
   claim.** Darity-Mullen ALREADY did the accounting (the *From Here to Equality*
   wealth-gap + longevity-gap computation). The honest relation is **method-
   portability / specialize** ("apply their method to extraction communities"), NOT
   "add the magnitude they lacked." The canonical Ch5/Ch6 prose gets this right
   ("methodological specialization," Ch6); the *meta-claim* over-generalizes the
   Mazzucato/Harvey "magnitude is the value-add" framing onto a tradition that
   priced. **Carve reparations economics out of that meta-claim explicitly.** This
   is a positioning fix, not a citation.

5. **Civic-republican non-domination is doing unearned FORWARD/intergenerational
   work in meta-claim 2.** Non-domination is a relation between contemporaries
   (Anderson's is workplace/private-government); future people can't be "dominated"
   in the republican sense while the dominator exists alongside them. Republicanism
   is strong for *within-generation* extraction (miner, community) but weak as the
   *forward* grounding — that load should rest on Broome/Meyer/just-savings.
   **Re-assign the forward grounding; keep republicanism for the within-generation arm.**

6. **Rawlsian just savings coded "operationalize" may over-claim.** Just savings is
   a savings-*rate* principle behind the veil; the bond does not derive or implement
   that rate — it shares the intuition. Safer relation: "parallel / shares-the-
   intuition," OR do the work to show the bond rate IS a just-savings rate (a real
   contribution if defensible). Currently TA/essay-side only, not chapter prose.

7. **Parfit non-identity "honest, unresolved" is treated as discharging the burden
   when it only acknowledges it.** The backward Restitution Bond survives non-
   identity (those people existed); the FORWARD Foreclosure Bond's core moral claim
   is exactly what non-identity threatens, and "conceded as unsolved" leaves it
   with no answer beyond a shrug. The Meyer/Page threshold route (§2 #13) is the fix
   — without it the forward bond has no identifiable wronged party.

**NOT over-claims (matrix is right / prose defends it — recorded so the author
doesn't over-correct):** Sandel IS engaged and answered (Ch5 L164–178; the matrix
cell is stale, the prose is fine); the financialization rebuttal IS in Ch5 (L172),

with the author's standing abstention; the SCC IS triangulated (TA L907).
