## Edit 2 — NEW paragraph (after the backward-application paragraph): CSD as structured range

> **CSD is a structured range: a provable floor and a moving ceiling.**
> `CSD_floor` = the Method-1 cure-cost (documented, realized inputs — the number a
> hostile reviewer cannot knock over), corroborated where restoration is already
> underway by the Method-2 realized-B₁ reading (actual restoration spend per unit of
> realized loss). The ceiling is generated by machinery the framework already
> contains: the Four Gates (§7) admit which Cᵢ may enter; the Commons Inversion Test
> toggles each admitted cost from the $0 its commons's abundance masked to the value
> scarcity makes legible (Theorem 10.3); and the §17 sum-of-costs generalization is
> exactly this moving-ceiling machinery run backward:
> `CSD_ceiling = CSD_floor + Σ (gate-admitted Cᵢ, including the Method-3 option
> value)`. The framework's contribution is making the range — and exactly what drives
> its ceiling — explicit and gate-disciplined, not forcing consensus on one complete
> computation.

## Edit 3 — NEW paragraph: three states of a cost slot

> **Three states of a cost slot — never collapsed.** A gate-admitted cost variable is
> in exactly one of three states. *Zero*: under abundance the cost genuinely is $0
> (the CIT toggle; Theorem 10.3). *Filled*: a value is entered, with its derivation
> shown. *Open*: a real, gate-admitted slot deliberately left empty. Open ≠ Zero, and
> Open ≠ unpriceable. An unfilled admitted Cᵢ means the computed CSD is a *lower
> bound* — pure arithmetic, a sum missing a non-negative term. That is the only thing
> the mathematics says about an empty slot; it says nothing about why the slot is
> empty or who should fill it. (This is the state the coercion vector occupies per
> Definition 1.10's scope-of-applicability boundary, and the state any
> admitted-but-unentered ceiling variable occupies in a worked backward case.)

## Edit 4 — NEW paragraph: CSD versus the Restitution Bond B₁

> **CSD versus the Restitution Bond B₁.** CSD is the range above; B₁ is the
> enforceable instrument actually posted against it. The bond anchors at the provable
> floor — a polity bonds what it can prove — and the affected polity chooses where in
> the CSD range to set it. `(CSD − B₁) > 0` measures unrestituted severance; equally
> diagnostic is *who posted* the realized B₁: where repair is publicly funded while
> the extractive beneficiary contributes nothing (the Black Lung Trust Fund's
> federal-general-fund share is the documented analogue, §11.6), the Method-2 reading
> reveals a mis-assigned bond — the instrument exists, at scale, charged to the wrong
> party.
