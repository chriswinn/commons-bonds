  appropriation" = **0 across all 10 chapters + TA**. Hickel present only as *Less Is More*

  directions, on a social-cost spine" novelty claim.
- **Home:** Ch5 backward-direction lineage (beside the existing Polanyi/Fraser/Sandel
  load-bearing passage, [Ch5:172–178](../../manuscript/chapters/Chapter__5_TheAccountabilityGap.md))
  + TA §5.5 backward-method lineage (beside the Darity-Mullen credit, TA L1565) + essays
  (Foreign Affairs; $100 Barrel, which already seeds Polanyi/Fraser).
- **Relation:** add-to / parallel — **concede macro-priority, claim per-event
  micro-operationalization.**
- **Proposed engagement:** Add a named-kin paragraph (Ch5 + a matching TA §5.5 lineage
  entry) crediting the bidirectional atmospheric-debt program — **Fanning & Hickel,
  "Compensation for atmospheric appropriation," *Nature Sustainability* (2023); CARICOM's
  reparations plan; the COP27/28 Loss and Damage Fund** — as the field that *already*
  prices an atmospheric commons as a debt running both forward (foreclosing future
  appropriation) and backward (historical over-appropriation owed) on a social-cost spine.

  operates at **national-aggregate, macro-accounting** grain (who owes whom across the
  global carbon budget); the framework's contribution is the **per-extraction-event
  micro-instrument** — pricing a single ton / site / decision and *reserving* against it
  rather than netting national balances — plus the reserve-as-optionality move. The
  framework supplies the ledger structure for the backward atmospheric direction; **per
  the standing first-person abstention it does not fill the coercion/reparations dollar
  column for that direction** (no atmospheric-reparations figure produced).
- **⚠ Coercion flag — credit-the-field only.** This is the most flag-sensitive finding.
  The engagement names scholars/programs and what *they* argue/compute; it produces no
  dollar figure of its own and takes no position on who owes whom.
- **Ratification ask:** Approve (a) crediting Fanning-Hickel / CARICOM / L&D as kin with
  no figure, and (b) the macro-aggregate-vs-micro-instrument distinction as the on-page
  answer to the "renamed climate-debt" objection?

### H2 — Analytic climate-ethics + libertarian-rights flank (highest-confidence cluster)

A three-work cluster; can ratify as one or split (Broome / Nozick / Gardiner). All
confirmed **0 everywhere**. No coercion flag on any of the three.

- **H2a — Broome** *(home: Ch6, co-located with Parfit/Sen at [Ch6:259–273](../../manuscript/chapters/Chapter__6_ThreeWaysofCounting.md); TA §14.3/§14.4; essay: $100 Barrel lineage-of-near-misses)*
  - **Relation:** extends / ally — and **co-grounds the forward arm** (the audit §3(e)
    re-assignment: republicanism strains under intergenerational load; Broome/Parfit carry
    the forward grounding, republicanism keeps the within-generation arm).
  - **Engagement:** Name **John Broome** (*Climate Matters* 2012; *Counting the Cost of
    Global Warming* 1992; "Discounting the Future" 1994) as the philosopher whose three
    positions the framework already enacts — that the discount rate is a moral, not merely
    technical, choice; that we owe *restitution* for harm imposed on future people; and that
    future wellbeing should not be discounted for pure time preference. The book names its
    instrument the *Restitution* Bond and argues exactly these three things; crediting Broome
    converts an apparent reinvention-of-the-wheel into a stated lineage and gives the forward
    arm a philosophy co-grounding. **This is the cleanest, highest-confidence fix in the
    whole verification — a straight ally-credit.**
  - **Ratification ask:** Approve naming Broome as the forward-arm philosophy co-grounding
    (alongside Parfit/Sen and the civic-republican grounding)?
- **H2b — Nozick** *(home: Ch9, beside the Coasean/P1 efficiency objection and symmetric to
  the Marxist objection engaged in Ch10; essay: Atlantic Ideas center-right disarm)*
  - **Relation:** extends / turns-against-itself.
  - **Engagement:** Add the libertarian **rights-flank** objection via **Robert Nozick**
    (*Anarchy, State, and Utopia* 1974) — the flank the book currently leaves empty while
    engaging the left/Marxist objection (Ch10) and the statism objection (Ch10). The
    justification is **argumentative completeness, not audience-courting**: a book that
    answers two major objections to an instrument but is silent on the third, deepest one
    (the deontological claim of right) has an unanswered-objection gap any rigorous reader
    notices, regardless of politics. (The "center-right reviewer reads it as a left lean"
    framing belongs to the *Atlantic Ideas essay*'s Condition-1 audience, not the book chapter.)
    State it at full strength (the bond burdens legitimately-held entitlement), then win it
    on Nozick's own ground: the **Lockean proviso** licenses appropriation only when "enough
    and as good" is left for others, and permanent foreclosure of a commons is exactly the
    case where it is not — so Nozick's framework, not merely the framework's critics',
    condemns unpriced permanent foreclosure. Claims the rights flank as an **ally**.
  - **Ratification ask:** Approve adding the Nozick/Lockean-proviso rights-flank objection
    and resolving it via the proviso (turning the flank into a license, not a threat)?
- **H2c — Gardiner** *(home: Ch9 + Ch10 honest-objections; Norway/Ch4 as the empirical
  counter)*
  - **Relation:** engage-as-threat → precommitment-device design rationale. (Borderline
    MUST per handoff.)
  - **Engagement:** Engage **Stephen Gardiner**'s *A Perfect Moral Storm* (2011)
    "tyranny of the contemporary" as a named threat to the theory of change: the generation
    that would post the Foreclosure Bond is the one that decides whether to post it, with no
    future party able to sanction refusal — an intergenerational buck-passing the no-villain
    concession is adjacent to but does not address. Then turn it: the bond is a
    **precommitment device** against exactly this buck-passing, and Norway's fund (Ch4) is
    the existence-proof that a polity can bind itself this way — vulnerability becomes design
    rationale and Norway is the empirical answer to Gardiner's pessimism.
  - **Ratification ask:** Approve engaging Gardiner's tyranny-of-the-contemporary as a named
    threat answered by the precommitment-device framing (+ Norway as counter)?
