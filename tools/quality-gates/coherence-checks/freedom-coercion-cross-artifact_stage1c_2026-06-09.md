## Drift / tensions

**3a. Standing-assignment drift (two real fixes):**
- **Ch 5:192** — "leaving the moral weight of that question to those who hold the standing to carry it." Clearest standing-assignment; also in institutional (not first-person) voice. Soften to TA §1.10:598 register (methodologically-unresolved / field-already-doing-the-work). **HIGH.**
- **Atlantic Ideas:80 closer** — "What belongs on it, looking backward, is theirs to enter." Keep the on-target opening ("not mine to do… those who are continuing it"); soften the closer. Also **collides with Ch 1:136's "The numbers are yours to enter"** (reader vs third-party antecedent). **HIGH.** (End-user-facing prose; check submission status before editing.)
- TA §1.10:598 is already clean — the model, not a fix.

**3b. Ch 1 boundary vs the manumission/price point — strengthens only if kept on the right axis.**
A naive manumission-number insertion would DISTURB Ch 1:56's refusal to put a commute and a stolen life "on the same line," because it would (1) import a magnitude into the no-equation paragraph, (2) risk the author computing the coercion figure himself, and (3) conflate the *exit-from-coercion* (self-purchase) price with the *coercion-vector* price the framework explicitly defers. It STRENGTHENS only if framed as: the number was **never zero and was person-revealed** (refuting "you can't price the sacred"), with computation handed to the field (first-person abstention) and the no-equation refusal preserved verbatim. Canonical structural home for the price-claim is **TA §5.5**; Ch 1 should at most carry a one-clause evidentiary reference, never a computed figure or a new section.

**3c. "finite" / over-narrow:** only Ch 6:279 ("The commons is finite") — and it describes the coordination-commons class, not Autonomy/freedom. Defensible in context; optional LOW tidy ("rivalrous / depletable / drawn-down") to keep it off the Definition-1.1 floor. Autonomy treatments are otherwise clean (capacity, no physical form, scarcity-dynamics register).
