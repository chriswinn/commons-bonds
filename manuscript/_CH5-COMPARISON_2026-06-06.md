The second ratified blocker — the total absence of the Polanyi/Fraser/Sandel exposition, the book's single biggest gap, with Ch5 as its ratified home and a Ch2 Fraser seed waiting to be paid off — is fully resolved. This is not a gesture — it is the load-bearing philosophical spine the chapter previously lacked, and it is the strongest single stretch of prose in either draft.

Across all three lenses NEW wins. The wins are not close on fidelity (NEW has the ratified content CURRENT structurally lacks) or prose-voice (153 → 0 em-dashes; framework-vocabulary scrubbed). Reader-arc is a clear-but-narrower NEW win. The only reason the verdict is PROMOTE-NEW-AFTER-FOLDIN rather than PROMOTE-NEW (clean) is that NEW dropped one substantive CURRENT section wholesale — the Public Choice / rent-seeking analysis of *who shaped* the architecture — and softened a handful of CURRENT's matched-comparison specifics.
