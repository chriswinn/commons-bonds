## Structural gaps (require new beat / re-architecture, not a line edit)

**SG-1 — The reef is the book's strongest restitution case and is never named as one (obligations #2 + #21).**
Ch3 builds the entire backward/Restitution logic in prose — the reef ground down across a century, "you have
to put something back, or accept that what was lost stays lost" (line 44) — and the restoration paradox (lines
186–202) shows recovery-without-honest-accounting leaving the cost-bearers still paying. This is *exactly* the
Restitution Bond's empirical terrain. But the chapter never names it backward/restitution, leaves the Bay's
instrument identity explicitly OPEN, and its close points only at the FORWARD Norway/trust model. The
contribution-matrix-lens and cross-chapter-ledger both independently flag this as the book's missing
backward-worked-case (the forward instrument gets a full worked number in Ch8 coal; the backward instrument is
asserted as symmetric but never discharged). **Fix options (author craft decision):** (a) add a short beat in
Ch3 explicitly naming the reef as the restitution archetype (the Bay is *backward* — what was ground down has to
be put back — while Norway answers the *forward* question), OR (b) hand the backward/restitution identity cleanly
to Ch9/Ch10 with an explicit signpost so the reader knows the Bay is the restitution case being deferred.
At minimum the chapter must stop leaving the instrument identity silently open. **Not redline — this is a new
analytical beat or a re-architected close.**

**SG-2 — The Ch3→Ch4 oyster-fund promise has no home (obligation #3; dangling-ref B-1, SUBMISSION-BLOCKING).**
