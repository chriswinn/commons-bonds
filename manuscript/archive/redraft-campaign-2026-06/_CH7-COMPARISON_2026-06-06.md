## PER-LENS SCORING

**Lens 1 — reader-arc: 50/50 (true tie; CURRENT edges it on the close).**
Both drafts run the same escalation: wrong-claim hook → settle-the-question promise → Mars walk → Earth convergence → brisk sweep → taxonomy → reversal → ladder → table → objections → deep time → across-time → immune response → coal handoff. The arc is identical because the architecture is identical. NEW's "Running the test" framing ("run briskly") and CURRENT's ("run quickly") are equivalent. The one arc-level difference is the close: CURRENT ends on the canonical, concrete "four-dollar-and-fifty-cent figure ... Look at the number. Look at the price on the invoice. Notice the gap." NEW ends on "just under five dollars a ton ... Look at the number ..." — same beat, but the softened number slightly blunts the gut-punch that the whole close is built to deliver (a precise small number set against a huge honest invoice lands harder than a vague one). Edge: CURRENT.

**Lens 2 — fidelity-structure: 60/40 CURRENT (the decisive lens).**
Thought-experiment architecture: preserved in both; NEW adds one explicitly-signaled disclaimer paragraph (a gain). Signaled-register discipline: intact in both; neither slides into asserting invented specifics as fact — both keep the habitat/administrator/consortium overtly hypothetical, both keep the launch-economics appendix scrupulously hedged ("actor-stated targets, not validated operational figures"; "not offered as a delivery-cost forecast"). Figure correctness: **CURRENT wins decisively.** CURRENT's coal handoff uses the corpus-canonical $4.50 point value; NEW drifts to "just under five dollars a ton," contradicting the 2026-06-04 canonical-sweep instruction to harmonize toward $4.50. NEW also drops two pieces of formal scaffolding CURRENT carries: the explicit math notation "S_max(industrial) − S_max(existential)" (NEW prose-paraphrases it as "the difference between S_max for industrial use and S_max for existential use") and the parenthetical "(RCV)" / "B" glosses that tie the result table's column headers ("Severed cost" in NEW vs "Cost Severance" in CURRENT; "large bond" in NEW vs "large B" in CURRENT) back to the Technical Appendix variables. These are small, but in an analytical chapter whose credibility rests on instrument-precision, CURRENT's tighter coupling to the formal apparatus is the better fidelity posture. Net: CURRENT is more faithful to the canonical figure and the formal apparatus; NEW's only fidelity gain is the one disclaimer paragraph.

**Lens 3 — prose-voice: 50/50 (genuine wash; trade-offs cancel).**
Em-dash hygiene: NEW is noticeably more disciplined — it systematically converts CURRENT's em-dashes to commas/periods/restructures (e.g. CURRENT's opener "wrong — not in its moral framing, not in its sympathy ..." becomes NEW's "wrong. Not wrong in its moral framing, and not wrong in its sympathy ..."). Per the project's em-dash-overuse memory (2026-05-21), this is the correct direction and a real NEW win. BUT CURRENT is *already* within acceptable em-dash range for a cascaded chapter, so the gain is marginal, and NEW occasionally pays for it with slightly flatter, more clause-chopped cadence ("Not wrong in its moral framing, and not wrong in its sympathy ... but wrong in its technical claim" is wordier than CURRENT's parallelism). Framework-free: not applicable as a constraint here — this is an explicitly framework-forward analytical chapter in both versions (RCV terms, Asymmetric Regret Rule, Commons Inversion Test, Theorem 10.3 are load-bearing and correctly present in both). Cadence tics: both are clean; neither shows runaway rule-of-three or anaphora pile-ups. The em-dash win for NEW is real but small and offset by NEW's occasional cadence flattening and its figure/apparatus losses. Wash.
