PROCESS-VALIDATION NOTE

Proceed firing the analytical chapters with the whole-cloth-from-a-brief process. The comparison is a clear validation, with specific guardrails.

What it validates: Whole-cloth-from-a-brief beat a month of iterate-in-place on the things that are hardest to fix by iteration and easiest to get wrong — argument architecture (the four-movement spine with a real hinge vs. a memoir braid that never escalates the concept), reader-arc (paradox opener + enacted accelerando + reader-as-ledger-keeper vs. a single elegiac tempo), and constraint-compliance (framework-free execution, near-zero em-dashes, the load-bearing slavery weights-discipline passage that the month of iteration never produced at all). Iteration-in-place is good at polishing what exists but tends to entrench an inherited structure; the OLD's plane-frame is the tell — a month of work made it more beautiful but never made it argue. Whole-cloth-from-a-brief is structurally free to build the right spine because it starts from the brief's intent, not from accumulated prose. For analytical chapters — where argument architecture is the dominant value and the emotional-scene density of Ch1 is lower — this advantage should be even more pronounced and the rescue burden lower.

What it does NOT do, and the guardrail: The whole-cloth process systematically TELLS where dramatized scenes SHOW, and it reproduced two known drift failure modes (air-compressor compression; a changed author-supplied specific — the passport). Both are predictable and both are catchable by the existing pipeline: the air-compressor compression is precisely what Pass 3.5 developmental-edit (restoration polarity) exists to catch, and the passport/$400K drifts are exactly what Pass 3.1 fact-check against substrate exists to catch. So the process validation is conditional on running the full explicit-gate cascade (3.1 fact-check + 3.5 developmental-edit especially) on every whole-cloth analytical draft — the whole-cloth step produces the superior foundation, and the cascade is non-optional cleanup for its two characteristic weaknesses, not optional polish.

Concrete recommendation: Adopt whole-cloth-from-a-brief as the default for the analytical chapters. Bake two process additions into the brief/cascade for each: (1) a "scene-anchor inventory" carried in the Stage 1 brief — an explicit list of the dramatized scenes/sensory anchors the chapter must SHOW rather than report, so the whole-cloth drafter aims at them rather than summarizing them (this front-loads what the Ch1 fold-in list had to recover after the fact); and (2) a mandatory Pass 3.1 substrate-fidelity check specifically targeting newly-introduced documentary specifics (figures, dates, biographical details) that the whole-cloth process is prone to hardening into "facts" — the passport and $400K findings are the empirical anchors for why this is non-negotiable. With those two additions, fire the analytical chapters on this process. For Ch1 itself: one whole-cloth revision on the NEW spine, recomposing fold-ins 1-5 and clearing the must-fix fidelity items, produces the chapter you would want to publish — which neither the OLD nor the current NEW is yet.
