## 3. Hard constraints

### 3.1 Path B preemptive policy

No sentence-level reuse from Ch 1. No structural close-paraphrases of Ch 1 paragraphs. Generate fresh prose from beats and concrete details supplied in the Stage 1 brief. The discipline's load-bearing test: produce a draft that passes a Path B audit on first try.

### 3.2 Locked Section I closing paragraph (verbatim)

This paragraph was ratified verbatim in a prior session. It must appear in Essay B exactly as written. It is original prose generated for the essay; it is NOT a Ch 1 paraphrase. Reuse it word-for-word:

> That morning on the plane, the recliner beside my son's NICU bed sat empty. The nurses kept the routines — they fed him, they cleaned him, they did the same for every other baby in the unit — but the holding he needed was not part of any routine, and there was never enough staff for it. I thought about my own father in a way I had not before. Every father knows this math. Come what may, you are there for your family. My father had gotten that part. I did not yet have a name for what pulled me away.

Why these word choices were ratified:
- **"holding"** (not "human contact" / "connection"): concrete, physical, irreplaceable for a NICU newborn.
- **"Come what may, you are there for your family"**: load-bearing — names the unwritten code.
- **"what pulled me away"**: names the active force.

### 3.3 Length budget

Target ~3,800w. Hard ceiling 4,000w. If you find yourself heading past 4,200w, stop and flag — don't cut-to-fit (substance-drives-length applies; cut-to-fit kills flow).

### 3.4 Voice register (from prior author ratifications)

- First person, plain, unsentimental.
- Vary sentence length deliberately — mix 5-word and 25-word sentences. Counter declarative-three-in-a-row.
- Italicize remembered direct speech and key terms (em-dash + italics for spoken lines).
- Avoid LLM tics: rule of three, em-dash crutches, tidy parallels, "in short" / "ultimately" connective tissue, "It changed me. It humbled me. It made..." cadence, "There are not many people like that anymore" nostalgia.
- Specific concrete details over abstract claims: recliner, drill bits, fifty-eight countries.
- Read each paragraph aloud before showing.
- No formal apparatus. No acronyms. No "RCV." No "cluster-γ." No three-component breakdowns. The framework's only definition the essay needs is the plain-language one.
