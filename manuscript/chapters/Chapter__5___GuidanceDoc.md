CHAPTER 5: THE ACCOUNTABILITY GAP

From our conversations, what lives here:

All non-McDowell-County case studies, told briefly:

Libby, Montana:

- W.R. Grace mine, 80% of world vermiculite supply
- Asbestos contamination known to the company since at least 1963
- 400+ deaths, 3,000+ diagnosed with asbestos-related disease
- Mortality from asbestosis 40-80x the national average
- $600M+ in EPA cleanup, over 1 million cubic yards of contaminated soil removed
- $4 billion bankruptcy trust
- 35 million American homes potentially containing contaminated insulation
- First and only Public Health Emergency declared by EPA (2009)
- Mine lifetime revenue ~$100 million vs. costs >$4 billion. IPG ≈ 40-80x

Deepwater Horizon:

- Projected well lifetime revenue ~$4.25 billion
- $15 billion cleanup costs
- $20 billion economic damages
- $5.5 billion Clean Water Act penalty
- $8.8 billion natural resource damages
- $4 billion criminal fines
- Total >$65 billion. IPG ≈ 15x
- Largest environmental settlement in U.S. history, and costs continue

Exxon Valdez:

- 11 million gallons spilled, market value ~$5.5 million
- Total social costs $5.5-9.5 billion
- 250,000 seabirds, 3,000 otters, 300 seals, 250 bald eagles, 22 killer whales killed
- Sea otter recovery took 25 years
- Herring never recovered
- Oil still present in sediments decades later

Baotou rare earths:

- 2,000 tons of toxic waste per ton of rare earth mined
- Cancer rates 70x national average near tailings lake
- 300 abandoned mines in Ganzhou alone
- 200 million tons of tailings, 100 square kilometers of forest destroyed
- Recovery estimated at 50-100 years
- "Cancer villages" with elevated cancer and birth defects

Social Security (added to Ch 5):

- Counter-case: shows what cost-severance LOOKS LIKE when the accountability instrument exists (intergenerational pooling + dedicated revenue stream)
- Demonstrates by contrast what's missing in extraction cases (no analogous instrument prices intergenerational foreclosure)

Healthcare commons (added to Ch 5):

- Cost-severance via for-profit insurance intermediation + denial economics
- Profits captured by intermediaries; costs borne by patients + medical providers + uncompensated emergency care
- Demonstrates cost-severance mechanism in service commons (not just resource extraction)

**Restitution + Foreclosure two-instrument apparatus (added to Ch 5; ~1,800 words structural payoff):**

- Ch 5 introduces the two-instrument framework (Restitution Bond for past-tense accountability gaps; Foreclosure Bond for forward-looking pricing of permanent loss)
- This is the chapter's load-bearing structural payoff — the cases cumulate into demonstration that two distinct instruments are needed because two distinct accountability gaps exist
- Phase 2 Foreclosure Bond housing-crisis collision (Insight #38, ratified 2026-04-29) — bridge prose at first introduction; v1 DRAFT awaits author voice refinement

The pattern statement: Every case, every resource, every geography, every decade — value extracted, costs severed, market price capturing a fraction of true cost. Not individual bad actors. Structural absence of the instruments needed to price what's being lost.
