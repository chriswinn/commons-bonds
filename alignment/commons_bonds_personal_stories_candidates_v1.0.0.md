## §0. Why this file exists

Personal stories surface organically during working sessions — often when a framework concept reminds Chris of a lived experience that demonstrates the mechanism at individual scale. Without a capture doc, these stories risk being lost between sessions or showing up as ad-hoc references in scattered guidance docs. This file is the collection point.

Each candidate is captured with:
- Story summary (enough to recognize and draft from later)
- Framework connections (where in the framework this story demonstrates mechanism)
- Potential placements in the book (which chapters could host it)
- Status (candidate / drafted / integrated / rejected)
- Author notes (anything Chris wants to remember when drafting)

Per the project's personal-stories-drafting gate: Chris drafts under Path F (no pre-fit-to-framework concern) — stories come first, framework-fit is tested per-story once 3–5 drafts exist.

### Status definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `candidate` | Captured but not drafted. |
| `drafted` | First draft exists (at a path noted in the entry). |
| `integrated` | Story has been absorbed into the book at a specific placement (chapter or appendix). |
| `rejected` | Considered and rejected (with reason). Preserved for record. |

## §2. Active candidates

### Candidate #1 — The commute trade

- **Captured:** 2026-04-24 by Chris Winn
- **Status:** candidate
- **Story summary:** During Chris's IT consulting career, he moved to an apartment that saved $300/month in rent. He'd driven the route at 1am and the drive took 40 minutes. He didn't realize that during actual working hours the commute would stretch to 2.5 hours each way — totaling 5 hours per day of commute time for a 5–7 day/week job over the full year of the lease. The $300/month "savings" cost approximately 1800 hours of his year — enough time to learn a language, take a college class, or learn an instrument. At the moment of signing the lease, past-Chris didn't have the information that future-Chris would need. The value-capturer and cost-bearer were the same person, separated by an information gap across time.
- **Framework connections:**
  - **Spatial abundance mechanics at individual scale.** The story demonstrates the exact phenomenon Chris's "Spatial Cost Severance was misnamed" insight (2026-04-24) points at — spatial abundance (commute distance) producing abundance-dependent costs (time) that weren't visible at the moment of decision. Worked example that the abundance-cost mechanics operate at individual scale, not just extraction-economy scale.
  - **Information-asymmetric self-severance.** The Cost Severance mechanism's core definition (value capture separated from cost bearing) operates here across an information gap within a single agent. Past-self captures value; future-self bears cost. Novel worked example for reaching knowledge-worker readers.
  - **Time as the most severe commons.** Illustrates why time-as-resource extraction is particularly devastating — time cannot be recovered even if the framework is applied retrospectively.
- **Potential placements:**
  - **Ch 1 — the 120-hour-week thread.** Fits naturally alongside the billable-hours arc. Could extend the Ch 1 consent-problem passage: "you had options a coal miner in McDowell County does not have — and you still ended up on a plane while your wife recovered from dying. You had information a coal miner doesn't have either — better information about your employment contract, your drive time, your apartment specs — and you still got extracted from because the information you had wasn't the information that would have changed the decision."
  - **Ch 7 — Mars colony parallel.** The asteroid miner's 40-minute teleport to work is the inverse of Chris's 2.5-hour Earth commute. Same mechanism (spatial abundance producing cost severance), vector reversed. Ch 7 guidance doc could reference this as an Earth-analog paragraph.
  - **Ch 8 — individual-scale worked example.** When Ch 8 walks through specific named Cᵢ, the commute story could serve as a non-resource-extraction case demonstrating the framework's universality.
- **Author notes:**
  - Personal narrative; no consent or privacy issues.
  - Likely candidate for early drafting if Chris wants to hit the 3–5-draft threshold that triggers Path K revisit.
  - Ch 1 placement should coordinate with other Ch 1 guidance-doc items (wife's illness, plane, co-founded company, nursing pivot).
- **Drafting status:** not started.

## §6. Operating discipline

### §6.1 When to capture a candidate

When a personal story surfaces during working sessions that (a) demonstrates a framework mechanism at individual scale, (b) has cross-chapter placement potential, or (c) Chris wants to remember for later drafting. Capture immediately — don't wait for the next session.

### §6.2 Per-project-memory integration

Per `project_personal_stories_drafting.md`: when 3–5 drafts exist, the testing suite evaluates per-story using M3 + M4 + M5 + M9 + M11. At that point we revisit Path K integration depth. This file supports that workflow by holding candidates before drafting + drafts as they accumulate.

### §6.3 Relationship to other docs

- **Open Insights Log** — if a personal story also surfaces a framework-level insight, the insight lands in the Open Insights Log; the story entry here cross-references.
- **Chapter guidance docs** — this file feeds into guidance-doc integration points (e.g., Ch 1 guidance already has a "120-hour-week thread" — candidate #1 references that thread as placement target).
- **Working Principles** — if a pattern emerges about how personal stories work in the framework, capture as candidate principle.
- **AI Statement draft** — not typically; personal stories are Chris's alone, not collaboration artifacts.

### §6.4 Privacy + consent discipline

Per Ch 1 guidance §Pre-drafting gates: wife's medical privacy, parents' privacy, Hampton community observations, and CEO-era NDA restrictions apply to any personal story. Each candidate's *Author notes* field should flag relevant privacy/consent issues.
