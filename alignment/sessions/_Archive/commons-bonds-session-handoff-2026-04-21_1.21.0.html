What changed v1.20.0 → v1.21.0 (full session summary)

  • AIT scaffolding-vs-load-bearing ratified. User asked whether the framework needs specific layers or whether AIT stands on its own. Worked out: AIT operates on any cost-scarcity relationship independently of a containing taxonomy. The test's validity is a property of the cost-scarcity relationship itself, not of any layer set. Layers provide systematic coverage, vocabulary portability, pedagogical shape, and scholarship seed points, but they are scaffolding, not load-bearing. A framework with an imperfect or incomplete layer set still has valid AIT application to individual cost proposals. Corollary: future layer set changes (additions, mergers, renames) are organizational revisions, not framework-integrity events. This explicitly absorbs the project's three prior rounds of layer-naming iteration rather than presenting them as instability.

Proposed protocol v1.2.0 additions (drafted inline, not yet shipped)

§3a — Merger and primitiveness gates for layer claims. Any proposed layer addition, split, or merger must clear two gates before ratification:

  • Merger check (outward): does the candidate substantially overlap with any existing layer such that they should be merged? Test by searching for bilateral independence cases — real-world instances where one is abundant and the other is scarce, in both directions. If independence cases don't exist in both directions, merger is indicated.
  • Primitiveness check (inward): does the candidate reduce to a composition of existing layers? Test by decomposing the candidate's revealed costs and checking whether each cost is already priced by an existing layer (or composition thereof). If all revealed costs decompose cleanly, the candidate is valid as a concept but redundant as a layer.

A layer passes the distinctness gate when it clears AIT, passes both merger and primitiveness checks, and passes two-path and multi-scale. A candidate that fails either gate may still be useful as a cross-layer concept, manifestation-level framing, or tier-level attribution — but not as a standalone layer.

Also: §12.7 addition — Rejected candidates remain revisitable. A candidate concept that fails AIT or two-path at one framing may succeed under another framing as understanding of its underlying scarcity sharpens. Failure is a finding about the tested form, not a permanent verdict on the concept space. Re-test is warranted whenever new cases surface that existing layers don't price cleanly.
