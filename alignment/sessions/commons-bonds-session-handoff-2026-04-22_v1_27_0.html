Commons Bonds operating core (canonical — updated v1.27.0)

AIT positioning (canonical, from C9 patch §3)

The Abundance Interference Taxonomy is the framework's epistemic core. Layers are organizational scaffolding, not load-bearing structure. Layer additions, mergers, or renames are organizational revisions, not framework-integrity events.

Two-path rigor test — apply before ratifying any architectural claim

  • Path 1 — Allocation symmetry: Is the structural value-capture / cost-bearing asymmetry present?
  • Path 2 — Shield absence: Is the distance condition absent? Shield typology: epistemic, institutional, political, fiduciary, spatial, temporal, consent-normalization (v1.2.1).

Both paths must confirm. Structural allocation is the mechanism of cost severance. Distance is the shielding condition.

Merger gate — apply before ratifying any new or renamed layer

Demonstrate bilateral independence with cases in both directions. If independence fails in either direction, merger is indicated.

Primitiveness gate — apply before ratifying any new layer

The layer's revealed costs must not decompose cleanly into compositions of existing layers. If they do, the candidate is a cross-layer concept, not a new layer.

Single-layer cost severance (added v1.27.0)

The framework is additive and compositional. A case activating one layer is a full instance of cost severance — multi-layer activation is not required. Goal-2 direct-contribution work often succeeds at single-layer precision. See commons_bonds_abundance_layers_v1_0_0.md §5.4 for canonical examples (Temporal-alone pension case, Agency-alone consent case, Cohesion-alone NYC isolation case).

Canonical cases are layer-inherent (v1.26.0)

A layer's canonical cases are not incidental — they are what the layer means in practice. Career-risk, legal exposure, and reception pressures route through specific layers via their canonical cases. Empirically confirmed across Part 2 rigor pass (Test 13 load-bearing on Informational, Political, Institutional via their canonical cases).

Revisitability

Rejected candidates remain revisitable. A candidate concept that fails one framing may succeed under another as understanding sharpens. Failure is a finding about the tested form, not a permanent verdict.
