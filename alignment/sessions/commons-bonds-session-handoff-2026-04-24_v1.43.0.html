Ch 7+9 CSG acronym collision — needs dedicated M12 rigor pass

Finding: Ch 7 (TheColonyAdministrator) and Ch 9 (TheRenewableImperative) use the acronym CSG to mean "Civilizational Substitutability Gap" — a chapter-specific resource-classification property:

  • Ch 7 line 113: "The book uses the term civilizational substitutability gap, abbreviated CSG, for this property. A resource's civilizational substitutability gap is the difference between its substitutability for current industrial applications and its substitutability for possible long-term civilizational applications..."
  • Ch 9 line 5 contains explicit disambiguation note: "CSG, not ESG. The acronym changed (Civilizational Substitutability Gap). I used CSG throughout."

Collision: the framework-retired CSG = "Cost Severance Gap" (retired on parsimony grounds — derivations of primitives shouldn't be named; see core/terms/terms_index.md). Per M12 / Working Principle #6 + Corollary D 7-level action ladder, this is at minimum a Level-3 lexical-collision-disambiguated finding, possibly Level-4 collision-rename-required.

Three resolution options to evaluate in dedicated rigor pass:

  1. Disambiguate. Keep CSG = Civilizational Substitutability Gap; add explicit chapter-opening footnote disambiguating from retired Cost Severance Gap; update terms_index entry for retired CSG to note the chapter-domain re-use.
  2. Rename. Rename Ch 7+9 acronym to avoid collision (e.g., CSubG, CivSubGap, or full-spelled term).
  3. Spell out. Use "civilizational substitutability gap" without acronym throughout Ch 7+9.

Recommendation: add to next-session M12 sweep agenda; resolve before academic-rigor full test (M12 finding will surface during M6 review).
