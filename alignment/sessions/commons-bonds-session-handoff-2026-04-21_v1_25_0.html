Commons Bonds operating core (canonical — updated v1.25.0)

AIT positioning (canonical, from C9 patch §3)

The Abundance Interference Taxonomy is the framework's epistemic core. Layers are organizational scaffolding, not load-bearing structure. Layer additions, mergers, or renames are organizational revisions, not framework-integrity events. The AIT operates on any cost-scarcity relationship independently of a containing taxonomy — its validity is a property of the cost-scarcity relationship itself, not of any layer set. Layers provide systematic coverage, vocabulary portability, pedagogical shape, and scholarship seed points.

Two-path rigor test — apply before ratifying any architectural claim

  • Path 1 — Allocation symmetry: Is the structural value-capture / cost-bearing asymmetry present?
  • Path 2 — Shield absence: Is the distance condition (the shielding mechanism) absent?

Both paths must confirm. Structural allocation is the mechanism of cost severance. Distance is the shielding condition.

Merger gate — apply before ratifying any new or renamed layer

Demonstrate bilateral independence with cases in both directions: a case where Layer A costs occur without Layer B costs, and vice versa. If independence fails in either direction, merger is indicated.

Primitiveness gate — apply before ratifying any new layer

The layer's revealed costs must not decompose cleanly into compositions of existing layers. If they do, the candidate is a cross-layer concept, not a new layer.

Revisitability

Rejected candidates remain revisitable. A candidate concept that fails one framing may succeed under another as understanding of its underlying scarcity sharpens. Failure is a finding about the tested form, not a permanent verdict on the concept space.

Operating discipline

"Push harder so we only rewrite once." Applies to layer set, protocol, AIT positioning, chapter framing, book scope, the abundance-layer set itself, and the rigor protocol's own lineage. This session's regression findings are an instance of the discipline catching drift before it propagated further.
