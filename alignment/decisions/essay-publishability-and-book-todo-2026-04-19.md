## What would make the Commons Bonds essay publishable at Noema/Boston Review level

Five requirements identified in the April 19 session:

### 1. An original theoretical contribution, precisely defined

Applying Harvey's framework to new examples is competent scholarship. What gets published is naming something that wasn't named before. The concepts developed in Commons Bonds — cost severance, residual commons value, departure liability — must be stated with the precision of a legal definition, not as descriptive shorthand. Departure liability in particular: what is it, what legal/moral framework should govern it, why does it currently have no standing, and who benefits from its absence.

### 2. Making the current arrangement feel contingent, not natural

The Rousseauian move is not just naming the mechanism — it's making hierarchy feel like a choice rather than a fact of nature. The essay needs to name the specific historical moments when the current arrangement was constructed: Taft-Hartley, the defeat of Truman's universal healthcare proposal, the 1978 Revenue Act that created the 401(k), the 2005 Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention Act that made student loans non-dischargeable. Contingency requires specificity. The arrangement was built. It can be rebuilt.

### 3. Original reporting or primary evidence

Every major essay that has changed the conversation on inequality is grounded in specific, observed, named human experience. The essay currently argues from secondary sources. The personal opening (wife's hospitalization, paternal leave, the plane) is the beginning of this — but it needs at least one additional original scene or observation from nursing program experience, from healthcare IT work inside institutions like Johns Hopkins and NIH, or from community college context.

### 4. Engagement with the strongest counterargument

The serious opposing argument is not "people make bad choices." It is: extractive arrangements are universal, the Nordic model depends on ethnic homogeneity and oil wealth and historical contingencies not replicable in a continental democracy of 330 million people, and markets allocate resources more efficiently than any demonstrated alternative. This argument must be engaged and defeated on its own terms — not dismissed.

### 5. A reachable horizon

Work that successfully delegitimizes the existing order without offering a coherent alternative generates despair or rage, both more easily captured by authoritarianism than by democratic reform. The essay doesn't need a policy platform. It needs what might be called a *reachable horizon*: one domestic example of community-captured resource value that already exists and forecloses the "it could never work here" objection. Candidates: Alaska Permanent Fund, municipal broadband cooperatives, Mondragon-model worker cooperatives in former extraction zones.

### The Rousseau question

Rousseau's *Discourse on Inequality* gained traction because it provided intellectual scaffolding for factions that already wanted to dismantle the existing order. But the more precise mechanism: Rousseau made hierarchy feel *contingent* rather than *natural*. Before Rousseau, hierarchy was the way things simply were. After Rousseau, it was a specific historical arrangement that had been constructed and could be reconstructed differently.

The Commons Bonds project does something similar but hasn't fully committed to the move. It shows the mechanism. It names the ideology. But the Rousseauian move — making the reader feel in their bones that this is not the way things have to be — requires the contingency argument (item 2 above) to be front and center, not buried.

On whether a path forward is required: Rousseau named the problem and the French Revolution followed, producing both the Declaration of the Rights of Man and the Terror. Naming without a constructive vision generates energy that existing political imagination can't contain. The essay needs a reachable horizon (item 5) — not a policy program, but a destination that makes the alternative imaginable.

## Structured book/essay todo list

Sequenced by dependency in the April 19 session. Note: several of these items have been materially advanced since April 19 — flagged below with status.

### Foundation work — do first

- **Define "departure liability" as a precise original concept.** Write a working definition of 300 words or less that could stand alone in an academic footnote. Answer: what is it, what legal/moral framework should govern it, why does it currently have no standing, who benefits from its absence. Write it as if presenting to a law review audience — not because you're publishing there, but because that discipline forces precision. What would a departure liability statute actually require? What existing legal frameworks (CERCLA, pension guarantee law, environmental remediation law) provide partial precedent?

- **Develop "residual commons value" and "cost severance" to the same definitional precision.** Each needs a working definition tight enough to stand alone. Then write one paragraph showing how all three concepts relate as a system — they should feel like parts of a single original framework, not three separate observations. *[Status 2026-04-23: substantially advanced via rigor protocol v1.3.0 + dimensions doc v1.2.0; the 10-dimension framework + 8-tier decomposition + AIT canonical source carry this work. Departure liability specifically remains open.]*

- **Write the contingency argument.** 500 words for yourself, not for the essay yet. Answer: at what specific historical moments were the key choices made that produced the current arrangement? Name the dates, the votes, the people. Taft-Hartley. The defeat of Truman's universal healthcare proposal. The 1978 Revenue Act. The 2005 Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention Act. Contingency requires specificity. This becomes the spine of the "how we got here" chapter.

### Evidence and reporting — do second

- **Find one domestic example of community-captured resource value that already works.** Must be American, functional, and defensible under scrutiny. Candidates: Alaska Permanent Fund, municipal broadband cooperatives, Mondragon-model worker cooperatives in former extraction zones, the Appalachian Citizens' Law Center's black lung legal work, any post-extraction community that used severance tax structures to fund genuine restoration. This forecloses the "it could never work here" objection.

- **Develop one original scene or observation from personal experience.** Three candidate sources: healthcare IT work inside institutions like Johns Hopkins and NIH (inside view of how cost structures actually operate), nursing program experience (proximity to patients absorbing externalized costs), community college SGA context (proximity to the people the essay describes). Pick one. Write 400 words — specific, observed, named. This becomes the essay's second original scene after the opening.

- **Build the opioid case as standalone affirmative argument.** Stop framing it as a rebuttal to Vance. Source the Purdue deposition documents and the Keefe book more specifically. Map the county-by-county targeting data if available in the public litigation record. The argument: here is what deliberate corporate extraction of community vulnerability looks like when the documentation is in the public record. Vance then becomes a footnote rather than the frame. *[Status 2026-04-23: captured in `research/case-studies/opioid-extraction-appalachia.md`; affirmative-frame vs. rebuttal-frame decision pending drafting stage.]*

- **Make the 2008 double-transfer mechanism human.** Build a composite family — specific income, specific retirement account balance, specific home value, specific job. Walk them through both risk transfers on the same money. First transfer: pension replaced by 401(k) in 1995, risk moves from employer to individual. Second transfer: 401(k) loses 40% in 2008 on instruments the financial sector created. Same money, transferred twice. Then show what happened to the banker who structured the instruments. This is your strongest contemporary case and currently your thinnest treatment relative to its importance. *[Status 2026-04-23: analytical framing captured in `research/case-studies/2008-financial-crisis.md`; composite-family narrative treatment remains pending at drafting stage.]*

### Argument hardening — do third

- **Write the steelman counterargument.** 600 words. The serious version: extractive arrangements are universal, the Nordic model depends on ethnic homogeneity and oil wealth and historical contingencies not replicable in a continental democracy of 330 million, markets allocate resources more efficiently than any demonstrated alternative. Write it as well as its best proponent would. Then defeat it specifically, not generally. You cannot publish in serious venues without doing this.

- **Resolve the Rousseau question privately before writing further.** Write a private 300-word answer to: is it enough to name the mechanism, or does this project require a reachable horizon? Your instinct is that naming may be sufficient. But Rousseau wrote into a specific political moment with specific factions ready to act. What is the analogous political moment now, and who are the analogous factions? This answer determines the book's ending and the essay's final paragraph. *[Status 2026-04-23: substantially advanced via villain-analysis content now in `Chapter_10___GuidanceDoc.md`; the Ch. 10 closing formulation ("Establish the prior claim of future generations on commons resources as a first principle of political economy...") is the current working answer.]*

### For the essay draft — do when the above are done

- **Write a 4,000-word essay draft built around cost severance / departure liability as the central concept, the 2008 crisis as the primary case study, and one domestic alternative as the reachable horizon.** This is the Noema/Boston Review version. Every other case study becomes a one-sentence reference or a footnote. Ruthless focus. One argument, fully developed.

- **Identify target publications and read their last 12 months of relevant content before submitting.** Noema: long theoretical essays with original framing. Boston Review: political urgency married to intellectual rigor. The Atlantic ideas section: accessibility without sacrificing precision. Know which version you're writing before you write it.

### For the book — do last

- **Write the book's last paragraph before writing anything else.** Know where you're going. Rousseau knew his argument ended with natural man as the moral benchmark against which civilization would be measured and found wanting. Your equivalent endpoint — the emotional and intellectual destination you want the reader to arrive at — should be written before the first chapter is drafted. Everything else is navigation toward that point.

- **Map every concept and case study to a chapter after the essay draft exists.** The essay will clarify what the spine is. The mapping session happens after the essay draft, not before — because the essay will tell you what the book needs to amplify versus what was useful scaffolding that doesn't survive at full length. *[Status 2026-04-23: currently the project's operating sequence — "folders → case studies → tier decision → chapter guidance → chapter rewrites" is roughly this map-after-spine pattern applied to the book rather than the essay, with the rigor-passes + 10-dimension framework serving as the structural spine.]*
