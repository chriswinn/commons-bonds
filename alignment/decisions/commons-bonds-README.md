# Commons Bonds

**A framework for honest accounting of extraction costs**

By Chris Winn

---

## Overview

Commons Bonds provides a systematic approach to pricing the true costs of extracting resources from shared commons. The framework introduces two core concepts:

- **Cost Severance** - The mechanism by which extraction separates value capture from cost bearing
- **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** - A mathematical framework for pricing previously unpriced intergenerational costs

## Key Innovation: Eight-Tier Decomposition

The framework decomposes extraction costs into eight measurable tiers, making the abstract RCV formula accessible to non-economists:

1. **Lifetime Survival Cost** - Full biological/psychological impact across worker's lifetime
2. **Actuarial Risk Cost** - True actuarial price of personal harm  
3. **Mission Failure Reserve** - Cost when operations don't return expected value
4. **Dynastic Labor Cost** - Cost imposed on worker's lineage
5. **Foreclosure Cost** - Permanent elimination of future resource uses
6. **Community Transition Reserve** - Economic transition fund for dependent communities
7. **Ecological Carrying Cost** - Environmental damage across full recovery timeline  
8. **Knowledge and Culture Cost** - Lost community expertise when populations disperse
9. **Political Capture Cost** - Cost of suppressing honest accounting itself

## Methodology: Abundance Inversion Test

**Core insight:** Earth hides extraction costs behind layers of abundance. Strip away abundance (atmospheric, geographic, temporal, institutional, demographic, ecological, political) and hidden costs become visible.

**Example:** An asteroid miner pays the full cost of oxygen, water, food, return transport, mission failure risk, and political infrastructure because none of these are abundant in space. On Earth, these costs exist but are invisible, borne by parties with no mechanism to price them into the market.

## Theoretical Foundations

**Three methodological pillars:**
1. **Cost Severance** - Structural mechanism identification
2. **Residual Commons Value** - Mathematical pricing framework  
3. **Abundance Inversion Test** - Discovery and validation methodology

**Universality claim:** Framework produces correct answers for any extraction scenario (Earth, Mars, asteroids) by systematically revealing costs hidden behind abundance layers.
