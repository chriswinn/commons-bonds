## The retired alternate decomposition (April 18, 2026)

Framed as "Individual Level: Eight-Tier FGC Decomposition" in the Integrated Framework master document. The 8 tiers:

1. **Immediate Survival Cost** — Direct threats to life/functioning
2. **Lifetime Health Cost** — Long-term health impacts
3. **Community Stability Cost** — Local social infrastructure damage
4. **Relationship/Family Cost** — Strain on personal bonds
5. **Dynastic Labor Cost** — Impact on children/future generations
6. **Opportunity Cost** — What you can't do because you're doing this
7. **Environmental/Ecological Cost** — Local environment degradation
8. **Existential/Meaning Cost** — Damage to purpose, dignity, integrity

## What this alternate decomposition does differently

- **Individual-level framing** rather than RCV-level framing. These tiers are named from the perspective of a single worker / family / individual bearing cost, not from the perspective of the extraction transaction itself.
- **Includes existential/meaning cost** as a distinct tier — current decomposition does not carry this explicitly. Possibly relevant to the Autonomy-coverage gap flagged in v1.0.6.
- **Includes opportunity cost** as a distinct tier — current decomposition treats this implicitly across Foreclosure + Dynastic Labor rather than naming it.
- **Includes relationship/family cost** — current decomposition carries some of this under Kindred-dimension case-study work but no dedicated tier.
- **Missing from this alternate:** Mission Failure Reserve, Political Capture Cost, Community Transition Reserve, Knowledge and Culture Cost — all of which carry load in the current canonical decomposition.

## Questions for tier-structure review

1. Does the individual-level framing reveal an **organizing principle** the current RCV-level framing obscures? (E.g., "cost bearers as categories" vs. "extraction-transaction components.")
2. Do any of the retired tiers (Existential/Meaning, Opportunity, Relationship/Family) carry load that the current 8 don't, or is that load already absorbed elsewhere?
3. Is there value in a **two-lens decomposition** — one set of tiers at the extraction-transaction level (current), another at the individual-life level (this retired set) — with both earning their place under Test 28?
4. Does the Autonomy-coverage gap in v1.0.6 find partial resolution here? Existential/Meaning Cost and Opportunity Cost both carry Autonomy-dimension load.
