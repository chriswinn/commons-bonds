> "We are being forced to generate more humans not as slaves, but as consumers."

Slavery captures labor value directly from the body — brutal and visible, the accountability severance obvious. Consumer generation is subtler and therefore more durable. The child born into poverty generates value not through forced labor but through lifetime spending — on food, clothing, healthcare, housing, transportation, education, entertainment — across every consumption category.

Poverty is the more reliable consumption engine because wealthy people invest, hoard, and minimize consumption. Poor people spend every dollar they have, immediately, by necessity. The system that mandates birth, removes reproductive choice, eliminates social support, and keeps families poor is not a moral failure of the system. It is the system working as structured.
