The existing Drive documents framed the project primarily around extractive capitalism. The April 19 session produced a conceptual clarification that should be stated explicitly in Ch. 1 or the prologue:

> The mechanism is broader than capitalism. It predates capitalism. It will likely survive capitalism. Feudal lords did it. Colonial empires did it. Soviet industrial planners did it. A nonprofit can do it. A government agency can do it.
>
> The system is almost incidental — the mechanism is what matters. Capitalism is the current and most efficient delivery vehicle, not the root cause.

This matters strategically: it means the critique cannot be dismissed as socialist ideology. The mechanism indicts feudalism, Soviet planning, colonial empires, and extractive capitalism equally. No political camp can claim it, and no political camp can fully dismiss it.

## Existing thinkers who gesture at this without naming the severance directly

- Karl Polanyi, *The Great Transformation*
- James C. Scott — state extraction mechanisms
- David Graeber, *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* — traces obligation severance to ancient Mesopotamia
- David Harvey — describes what gets taken; does not name the knife

**The original contribution:** Harvey describes what gets taken. *Commons bonds* names the knife — the deliberate cutting of cost from profit as the active mechanism.
