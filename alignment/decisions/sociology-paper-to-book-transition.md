## Section 1 — Publishability Assessment (captured from sociology-paper source §6)

### The five items required for Noema / Boston Review-level publication

**1. An original theoretical contribution, precisely defined.**

> Applying Harvey's framework to new examples is competent scholarship. What gets published is naming something that wasn't named before. The concepts developed here — cost severance, residual commons value, departure liability — must be stated with the precision of a legal definition, not as descriptive shorthand. Departure liability in particular: what is it, what legal/moral framework should govern it, why does it currently have no standing, and who benefits from its absence.

**Current status (2026-04-22):** Substantially progressed. "Departure liability" was retired under Option C (per workstream 01 decisions). The remaining two pillars — **Cost Severance** and **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** — have precise definitions via the eight-tier decomposition (v10) and the rigor-protocol v1.2.1 apparatus. Still pending: legal-framework precision for RCV deployment (a Tech Appendix question, queued for post-cohort work).

**2. Making the current arrangement feel contingent, not natural.**

> The Rousseauian move is not just naming the mechanism — it's making hierarchy feel like a choice rather than a fact of nature. The essay needs to name the specific historical moments when the current arrangement was constructed: Taft-Hartley, the defeat of Truman's universal healthcare proposal, the 1978 Revenue Act that created the 401(k), the 2005 Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention Act that made student loans non-dischargeable. Contingency requires specificity.

**Current status:** Partially addressed. The 2008 financial crisis case (`research/case-studies/2008-financial-crisis.md`) references the 1978 Revenue Act. The tax-tradeoff case (`research/case-studies/tax-tradeoff-us-sweden.md`) implies the contingency but doesn't name the specific moments. Still pending: a compressed "contingency section" (500 words per §7 todo list) naming specific dates, votes, people — the Rousseauian move explicitly. Candidate placement: Ch 9.

**3. Original reporting or primary evidence.**

> Every major essay that has changed the conversation on inequality is grounded in specific, observed, named human experience. The essay currently argues from secondary sources. The personal opening (wife's hospitalization, paternal leave, the plane) is the beginning of this — but it needs at least one additional original scene or observation from nursing program experience, from healthcare IT work inside institutions like Johns Hopkins and NIH, or from community college context.

**Current status:** Chapter 1 guidance (`manuscript/chapters/Chapter__1___GuidanceDoc.md`) includes personal-narrative anchors from IT career / NASA / JH / NIH / wife's illness. The knowledge-worker extension merged this session (item 12) extends this. Still pending: **at least one additional original scene** (nursing program, healthcare IT institution, or community college context). Also pending: **interview-based material** across multiple chapters per workstream 11 (archived) — requires fieldwork.

**4. Engagement with the strongest counterargument.**

> The serious opposing argument is not "people make bad choices." It is: extractive arrangements are universal, the Nordic model depends on ethnic homogeneity and oil wealth and historical contingencies not replicable in a continental democracy of 330 million people, and markets allocate resources more efficiently than any demonstrated alternative. This argument must be engaged and defeated on its own terms — not dismissed.

**Current status:** Partial engagement in existing chapter drafts. Ch 2 handles "cheap energy lifted billions" and "miners chose that work" counters. Ch 4 handles the "Norway had advantages McDowell didn't" counter. Still pending: **the full "steelman" counterargument** (600 words per §7 todo list) — the Nordic-model-unrepeatable argument engaged at its strongest form. Candidate placement: Ch 9 or standalone counterargument section.

**5. A reachable horizon.**

> Work that successfully delegitimizes the existing order without offering a coherent alternative generates despair or rage, both more easily captured by authoritarianism than by democratic reform. The essay doesn't need a policy platform. It needs what might be called a reachable horizon: one domestic example of community-captured resource value that already exists and forecloses the "it could never work here" objection.

**Current status:** **Norway SWF** is the international reachable-horizon (Ch 4 extensively drafted). **Alaska Permanent Fund** is flagged in `tax-tradeoff-us-sweden.md` expansion notes as the natural domestic reachable-horizon. **Municipal broadband (Chattanooga)** and **Mondragon cooperatives** also flagged. Still pending: a developed standalone treatment of the domestic reachable-horizon (candidate: a case-study file for Alaska Permanent Fund alongside the existing Norway SWF file).

### The Rousseau question

The sociology-paper source framed Commons Bonds as operating in Rousseau's *Discourse on Inequality* register — making hierarchy feel contingent rather than natural. Status: the framework has committed to this direction (per workstream 01 Decision C: Chapter 10 lands on the villain-analysis / institutional-legibility-infrastructure prescription). Still pending: resolving how much "reachable horizon" is required before the delegitimization reads as responsible rather than destabilizing. ## Section 4 — Books 1 / 2 / 3 Strategy and Reasoning (consolidated)

*Consolidation requested 2026-04-22: "for the publishability assessment make sure we are including the book one, two & three strategy and reasoning as it might make sense for that information to all live in the same place." Previously scattered across workstream 01 Decision D, workstream 07, publishing strategy doc, scope doc, and the archived two-book strategic analysis. Consolidated here because the publishability assessment (Section 1) is inseparable from the books-split decision — the publishability requirements of each book differ, and the split is what makes each book's publishability achievable.*

### The three-book structure

**Book One — *Commons Bonds***

- **What it is:** Framework naming. Cost Severance + RCV + Abundance Inversion Test + Universality Test, applied to the canonical cases (Appalachian coal, Norway, Chesapeake watermen, industrial-accountability cases, eight-tier decomposition, colony administrator thought experiment, renewable imperative, closing synthesis).
- **Political positioning:** Non-partisan by design. Framework-integrity-first. Cross-spectrum engagement (Appalachian coal defender, left critic, right critic, tenured economics chair, community organizer all addressable within chapter counterargument architecture).
- **Legal exposure profile:** Minimal. No named beneficiaries of advocacy; no specific political actors identified as malicious; no industry-specific targeting beyond historical cases. The pre-drafting gates (NDA review, subordinate anonymization, canonical-case presentation protocols) keep exposure within publisher-legal-counsel acceptance range.
- **Success criterion:** 13+-year labor-lawyer sentence (per `tools/commons_bonds_guiding_constraints_v1_0_0.md` §1). Vocabulary adoption over policy adoption.
- **Publishing path:** Cascade — Noema primary, Boston Review fallback, Atlantic Ideas further cascade. Berggruen Prize (2026-08-17) parallel independent track. Per guiding constraints §2.
- **Timeline:** Near-term target (late 2026 / early 2027 publication target).

**Book Two — *The Subsidy Economy***

- **What it is:** Applied / policy / advocacy work. Full-economy simulation, named-industry critique, named-actor accountability, the $10–15 trillion annual subsidy argument, distributional crisis analysis, universal-floor structural necessity proof, political-economy-of-transition discussion.
- **Political positioning:** Explicitly advocacy. Takes positions on specific industries, specific policy architectures, specific named actors where the litigation record supports it (opioid case, 2008 crisis, healthcare-industry capture, etc.).
- **Legal exposure profile:** Substantial. Requires:
  - **Coauthorship** (shares author risk with someone with institutional credential).
  - **Institutional affiliation** (institutional backing against industry lawsuits; post-CRNA or post-PhD credentialing).
  - **Publisher with legal backing** (publisher legal team willing to defend against industry lawsuits; not a self-publishing or small-press route).
- **Content sources:** Seed material in `archive/_One Day Maybe/Book-Two-Subsidy-Economy/` (via workstream 10's reorganization plan, partially executed this session). Counter-Vance research (`archive/_One Day Maybe/Counter-Vance/`), democratic-socialism discussion (`archive/_One Day Maybe/Democratic-Socialism/`), satellite-essay material (`archive/_One Day Maybe/Satellite-Essays/`) also route here per the workstream 10 plan executed 2026-04-22.
- **Status:** **Notes-only for now.** Reevaluate in 3–5 years after the author's credentialing pathway (CRNA and/or PhD) completes and institutional affiliation becomes available.
- **Timeline:** Likely 2029+ target.

**Book Three — *Pricing the Final Frontier***

- **What it is:** Off-Earth / space-mining application of the framework. Asteroid mining, lunar resource extraction, orbital-habitat resource management, Lagrange-point infrastructure, Mars-colony-scale resource allocation.
- **Political positioning:** Framework-development and speculative-future mode; political exposure low because the actors and jurisdictions are partly hypothetical.
- **Legal exposure profile:** Low to moderate. The real constraints are scientific-plausibility and speculative-scenario-discipline, not legal exposure.
- **Connection to Book One:** Chapter 7 of Book One (*The Colony Administrator*) is the off-Earth universality-test chapter. Book Three expands this into a full book-length application. The Irreversibility / availability-absence material from v1.28.0 (currently methodology-appendix-territory and Ch 9 civilization-scale extension) is a natural Book Three topic.
- **Seed material:** `archive/_One Day Maybe/Book Three_ Pricing the Final Frontier.docx`.
- **Status:** Notes-only. Dependent on Book One establishing the framework first.
- **Timeline:** Post-Book Two in natural sequence; may shift earlier if Book One's Ch 7 generates substantial off-Earth-policy engagement during the publishing cascade.

### Why the three-book split (strategic reasoning)

**The uncomfortable truth the two-book analysis established** (archived at `archive/_One Day Maybe/commons-bonds-two-book-strategic-analysis 10.html`):

> Your intellectual contribution may be most powerful if you write the framework that lets others do the political work safely, rather than trying to do both the framework and the politics yourself. This reshapes the project identity from "I will fix this" to "I will build the instrument others use to fix this." Both are valid. They are different books.

**The strategic logic:**

1. **Book One protects Book Two by establishing vocabulary first.** Once "cost severance" and "residual commons value" are in intellectual circulation, a subsequent advocacy book using those terms is received as applying established framework rather than inventing rhetoric. Without Book One first, Book Two reads as partisan advocacy wrapped in novel terminology — easily dismissed.

2. **Book Two protects Book One by not preloading it with advocacy.** If Book One attempts to do the framework-naming work AND the specific-advocacy work simultaneously, the advocacy pulls the framework into its political register. Reviewers evaluate the framework as partisan; the vocabulary-adoption pathway narrows to readers who already agree. The 13+-year labor-lawyer success criterion becomes harder, not easier, because the vocabulary is politically-marked from the start.

3. **Book One's success criterion is not the same as Book Two's success criterion.** Book One succeeds when the vocabulary travels. Book Two succeeds when specific policy changes. Different instruments; different timelines; different authorship requirements.

4. **Book Three is an extension, not a protection.** Book Three extends the framework into off-Earth territory that Book One establishes in Ch 7 as universality test. It doesn't protect the other books; it completes the framework's applicability claim.

### Why this matters for publishability assessment

The three-book split is **integral to the publishability analysis in Section 1**, not orthogonal. Specifically:

- **Item 1 (original theoretical contribution):** Book One needs to be framework-naming to publish at Noema / Boston Review register. Advocacy content would dilute the theoretical contribution.
- **Item 4 (engagement with strongest counterargument):** Steelman engagement requires framework-level precision. Book One's scope keeps it framework-level; Book Two's advocacy scope would shift the engagement register.
- **Item 5 (reachable horizon):** Book One's reachable-horizon is the Norwegian / Alaskan / Mondragon / municipal-broadband existence proofs. Book Two's reachable horizon is specific policy architecture. Different scale, different publishability signatures.

The split is not just about timing — it is about **what each book's publishability requires** of the other.

### Content assignment rules (for chapter drafting decisions)

When material surfaces during drafting or research, the default routing:

- **Names individuals or specific corporations as primary advocacy targets?** → Book Two.
- **Names historical actors in historical cases (1950s–1990s McDowell County, 1989 Libby, 2010 Deepwater Horizon)?** → Book One acceptable; use pre-drafting gates (anonymization where warranted, publisher legal-counsel review, canonical-case presentation protocols).
- **Names currently-active political actors or currently-operating corporations as primary targets of advocacy?** → Book Two (or later Book Two companion essays).
- **Specific policy architecture proposal with named instruments, named jurisdictions, named implementation paths?** → Book Two.
- **Framework-level extensibility to off-Earth contexts?** → Book One (Ch 7 current scope) OR Book Three (fuller treatment).
- **Named beneficiaries or harmed parties whose identification could trigger defamation risk?** → Book Two.
- **Anonymizable pattern descriptions of the same phenomena?** → Book One acceptable per pre-drafting anonymization protocols.

For each case, the rigor pass evaluates:

**(a) Framework-integrity tests** (Group A per protocol §5):
- Does it demonstrate cost severance cleanly?
- Does two-path rigor hold (allocation symmetry + shield absence)?
- Does it survive AIT?
- Are the dimension(s) it activates cleanly identifiable?

**(b) Scope / placement tests** (Group B per protocol §6):
- Trying-to-do-too-much: does adding this case expand Book One scope beyond what it should cover?
- Cross-spectrum preservation: does the case preserve non-partisan framework positioning (Book One) or shift toward advocacy (Book Two)?
- Load-bearing vs. scaffolding: is the case structural or organizational?
- Displacement: what currently-planned content would be pushed out to include this case?

**(c) Publishing-strategy alignment** (per Section 4 content-assignment rules + `tools/commons_bonds_guiding_constraints_v1_0_0.md` §2):
- Does this case fit Book One's non-partisan framework-naming register, or does it push toward Book Two advocacy register?
- Would including this case strengthen or weaken the Noema / Boston Review / Atlantic Ideas cascade reception?
- Would it affect Berggruen Prize fit (independent framework work, not advocacy)?

**(d) Success-criteria alignment** (per `tools/commons_bonds_guiding_constraints_v1_0_0.md` §1):
- Does this case strengthen the 13+-year labor-lawyer-using-vocabulary criterion? (Goal 1 direction.)
- Does this case strengthen the individual-reader "accept work with open eyes" criterion? (Goal 2 direction.)
- Does this case dilute either goal?

**(e) Book assignment per Section 4 content-assignment rules:**
- Book One / Book Two / Book Three?
- If Book One: which chapter, with what anonymization / pre-drafting-gate requirements?
- If Book Two: move standalone case file to `archive/_One Day Maybe/Book-Two-Subsidy-Economy/` staging?
- If Book Three: move to Book-Three staging?
- If split between books (some content in Book One framework-register, deeper advocacy content in Book Two): document the split explicitly.
