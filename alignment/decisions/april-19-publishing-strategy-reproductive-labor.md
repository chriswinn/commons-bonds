---

The April 19 session produced a considered recommendation **not** to publish a standalone abortion / reproductive-labor application of commons bonds before the larger essay establishes the framework. The reasoning:

- **Branding risk:** The abortion application, regardless of how structurally argued, will be read by many editors as a pro-choice piece. This closes cross-spectrum reach — one of the project's greatest strategic assets.
- **Reveals the knife before the surgery:** The larger essay's power comes partly from demonstrating accountability severance across so many contexts simultaneously that no single ideological camp can claim it. Publishing the abortion case first hands readers a lens that colors every subsequent case.
- **Reduces novelty:** Editors at Noema and The Atlantic will ask what's new. If commons bonds has already appeared in an abortion piece, the answer becomes "an extension of earlier argument" rather than "a comprehensive original framework."

**Recommended sequence:** The abortion / reproductive-labor material should appear as one of the most fully developed sections of the larger essay — landing in context, surrounded by feudalism, oil, asteroid mining, and the Vance dismantlement. In that context it reads as one devastating application of a universal mechanism. Isolated, it reads as a position piece on abortion.

Once the essay is published and the framework is publicly established under the author's name, a standalone abortion application piece becomes possible — because by then the author is identified as the person who named commons bonds, not the person who wrote a piece about abortion.
