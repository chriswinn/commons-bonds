## Why this case might warrant full development

- **Indigenous-nation impact:** First Nations in the Athabasca region bear disproportionate cost from tar-sands extraction; links to the Indigenous-land-dispossession framework (separate stub file).
- **Ecological-carrying-cost specifics:** Boreal forest clearance, water use and contamination (Athabasca River), CO2-intensity far exceeding conventional oil. Distinct from Appalachian coal's ecological profile.
- **Political-capture patterns:** Alberta's political economy has distinct capture dynamics (oil-sands royalty structures, federal-provincial jurisdictional conflict, indigenous consultation requirements and their limits).
- **Climate-transition relevance:** Tar sands are among the most carbon-intensive oil sources; their economic viability is marginal and highly sensitive to carbon pricing, making them a natural case for "what does the framework say about extractive activities that are marginal under honest accounting?"
