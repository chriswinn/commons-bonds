## 2. Positioning-first findings (PRIMARY LENS, per author directive 2026-06-11)

The question: does the draft illustrate how the book SUPPORTS, CONTRADICTS,
and ADDS TO the adjacent literature named in brief §3?

**Variant A — 9/10.** Every required lineage appears with the brief's
relationship verb enacted in prose, not asserted:
- *Extends the formal canon:* "Pigou's corrective tax, Coase's bargain, and
  Ostrom's design principles are not being discarded; they are being extended
  into the three territories none of them was built for. Honest pricing, on
  this account, is what completing the market looks like." This is brief
  landing point 3 delivered in two sentences, with the Smith disarming
  sentence attached ("he trusted prices because he expected them to tell the
  truth about cost").
- *Supports + contradicts + adds to Public Choice:* takes Olson/Tullock
  "gratefully," grounds the mechanism concretely ("the handful who benefit
  attend every hearing and the millions who pay are never in the room"),
  parts company explicitly ("at its frequent conclusion, that the remedy is
  less public decision-making"), and names the addition ("What the tradition
  declined to compile... is the other half: the ledger of what the open bill
  cost, and who has been carrying it").
- *Adds to Christophers at depth:* fair to the stronger insufficiency claim,
  concedes it ("he is right about the limit, and the limit is where the
  instrument's real work begins"), converges on public-purpose capital.
- *Polanyi/Fraser written, not name-dropped:* "the severed costs of the early
  chapters are Fraser's hinterland with a dollar column attached... the
  double movement... becomes something a polity can do on purpose."
- Pistor diagnosis + financialization inoculation both land, Pistor named,
  correct Ch5 cross-reference. Glaeser/Gyourko + Schuetz at full strength
  with the interpretive-argument flag. Simon-Ehrlich conceded then
  redirected. Desmond softened; Susskind correctly dropped.

**Variant B — 8.5/10.** The per-engagement depth is the best in any of the
three texts: Christophers ("an honest price that no investor can profit by

named anywhere in the draft (the brief's landing point 3 and Beat 2 both

softer than A's ("the broader political project... stays where it is").

**Canonical — 3/10.** Public Choice is run as a long defensive standalone

/ GMU) that brief §3 explicitly bans from prose; Polanyi and Fraser do not
appear at all; Susskind is retained; Christophers is present but the

## 6. Three verbatim side-by-side excerpts

### Excerpt 1 — The opening arithmetic (Beat 1, the figure-pinning hot spot)

**Canonical:**
> "One ton of McDowell County coal, sold at the mine mouth in 1960 for four
> dollars and fifty cents, actually cost — conservatively, floor-estimated,
> cost-component by cost-component — somewhere between five hundred and six
> hundred dollars when every severed cost was priced honestly. The gap
> between those two numbers is not a rhetorical flourish. It is arithmetic.
> And it is replicated, with different parameters, across every extraction
> the economy runs."

**Variant A:**
> "In 1960 a ton of coal left a mine mouth in McDowell County, West Virginia,
> for just under five dollars. The Energy Information Administration's
> historical price tables put the figure at $4.71. ... The honest total came
> to $510. The difference between the two numbers moved off the invoice and
> onto McDowell County: into lungs, into groundwater, and forward in time
> onto people who had no say in the sale."

**Variant B:**
> "A ton of coal left a McDowell County mine mouth in 1960 for $4.71. ...
> Just under five dollars collected; more than five hundred owed. The gap is
> arithmetic. It does not soften with rechecking. I have tried."

*Reading:* the canonical opens on a wrong figure, an antithesis, and the
scope overclaim. Both redos pin every figure with a named authority and stay
declarative; B's "I have tried" is the sharper close, A's "no say in the
sale" carries more of the book's spine.

### Excerpt 2 — Norway (the TA-canon-critical wedge)

**Canonical:**
> "Norway's sovereign wealth fund is a larger, more disciplined national
> version operating on the same principle. The specific design of an
> architecture that sits between these two — not a dividend to every citizen,
> but a long-horizon fund structurally tied to the community whose commons
> was drawn from — is the work of separate applied-policy analysis..."

**Variant A:**
> "Norway, the existence proof of Chapter 4, is better read another way. ...
> read directly off the fund, Norway internalized about $48 on every
> barrel-equivalent it produced. That figure is a floor, what one polity
> demonstrably internalized, with the true residual value above it. And the
> fund's deeper work is what it does to irreversibility: a country holding
> that much restorative capacity has preserved the option of repair, and it
> is irreversibility that makes foreclosure expensive."

**Variant B:**
> "Norway is the deeper proof, and the book has already built it; only the
> reading needs restating. What the Government Pension Fund Global buys, in
> this book's terms, is reversibility. A nation holding roughly $2 trillion
> against the day the oil is gone... retains options that a nation that spent
> the royalties does not, and preserved options are precisely what collapse
> the foreclosure premium on extraction."

*Reading:* canonical reads Norway as the rents story (the v2 brief's named
correction). Both redos carry the irreversibility-reduction reading and the
M2 revealed-floor framing; B's rendering is the cleanest of the three and is
fold-in source material for A's wedge opener.

### Excerpt 3 — Public Choice positioning (support / contradict / add-to)

**Canonical:**
> "The practical implication for readers in the policy-economy traditions
> Public Choice has shaped — readers at AEI, Cato, the Mercatus Center,
> George Mason economics... — is that the framework here is not a competitor
> for the analytical space they occupy."

**Variant A:**
> "The cleanest answer in the literature comes from the public-choice
> economists, and this book takes it gratefully. ... A reclamation bond stays
> underpriced not because anyone wicked wills it but because the handful who
> benefit attend every hearing and the millions who pay are never in the
> room... Where this book parts company with the tradition is at its frequent
> conclusion, that the remedy is less public decision-making; what it keeps
> is the mechanism... What the tradition declined to compile, and what these
> chapters have compiled, is the other half: the ledger of what the open bill
> cost, and who has been carrying it."

**Variant B:**
> "Public Choice supplies the cleanest answer on offer, and it costs the book
> nothing to take it. ... The borrowing here is vocabulary; the broader
> political project that often travels with the school stays where it is.
> Public Choice names who shaped the rent-seeking arrangements and why the
> severance persists, with no villain required. This book supplies the ledger
> of what the arrangement cost, and to whom. The two accounts fit precisely
> because neither needs the other to be wrong."

*Reading:* the canonical talks ABOUT positioning (and names the banned think
tanks); both redos DO the positioning. A's is more grounded (the hearing
room) and contradicts more explicitly; B's closing line is the more elegant
formulation of the complement.
