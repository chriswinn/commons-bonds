## 5. Three verbatim side-by-side excerpts

### Excerpt 1 — the M2 recast (the chapter's most important structural beat)

**Canonical (retired spine; M2 as middle estimator):**
> "Method 2 sits as a revealed-preference-anchored middle of the triangulation but as a lower bound on true RCV for that reason."

**A:**
> "Method 2 is revealed preference, and it is not a third estimator. … A number cannot serve as your estimate of what the commons was worth and simultaneously as the payment you subtract from that worth; an apparatus that let it do both would be grading its own homework. So the framework assigns revealed preference the job it can actually do. It reads the bill."

**B:**
> "The same observable cannot serve as an estimate of the value and as the payment subtracted from it; treating revealed payments as a third estimate of RCV would be entering a figure on one side of a ledger because it appeared on the other, and the subtraction CS = RCV - B would quietly subtract a number from itself. … What was paid and what was owed are the two quantities this whole chapter exists to hold apart."

Both land it; B's "subtract a number from itself" is the cleanest statement of WHY the recast is forced, and "what was paid and what was owed" is the chapter's thesis in nine words.

### Excerpt 2 — Norway's mechanism (irreversibility-reduction, not rent-capture)

**Canonical (retired framing):**
> "Result: CS is small, but positive. The fund captures most of the financial value — for Norwegians."

**A:**
> "What the fund does is keep open the polity's capacity to restore and to compensate: to fund the transition, to build the substitutes, to pay for harms as they surface, on any timeline the future turns out to require. … The fund is an irreversibility-reduction machine, and irreversibility-reduction, not revenue capture, is what the backtest credits."

**B:**
> "The intuitive story says Norway captured the rent, and the intuitive story is wrong in a way that matters for anyone trying to copy it. The fund does not buy back the barrel; nothing manufactures subsurface petroleum a second time, and no sum in any account un-burns the carbon. What the fund changes is the irreversibility of the loss. … What Norway bought with the fund was not absolution and not a refund; it was the preservation of its own future capacity to make things right."

B explicitly names and disarms the wrong (rent-capture) story before replacing it, which is the safer pedagogy given the book's earlier chapters; A asserts the right story cleanly.

### Excerpt 3 — the who-paid finding (the brief's "strongest new sentence" slot)

**A:**
> "The bond this book keeps asking for is not missing. It exists, at scale, and it is charged to the wrong party."

**B:**
> "An accountability instrument for Appalachian coal exists. It operates at scale. It is charged to the wrong party. That is the mis-assignment finding, and it generalizes … The policy debate has spent decades arguing about whether to create accountability instruments. The reading says the instruments exist and the argument is actually about the addressee."

Both land the sentence the brief asked for; B's extension ("the argument is actually about the addressee") converts the finding into a positioning claim against the policy literature, which is exactly the primary lens.
