She walked into the ER under her own power. She had been telling me for five days that she couldn't sleep but she was ok and not to worry about it. I recall being livid when I went to the pharmacy and I realized the nurses forgot to bother calling in medication for what they thought was her upset stomach. After going over my wife's symptoms the head nurse stopped asking questions and told me to bring her in immediately. After waiting to be seen we were going through the usual pleasant chatter that accompanies doctors visits.. then the nurse stopped responding to us when she was looking at the monitor. By the time the surgical team arrived, the doctor was no longer talking to us — he was talking past her, fast, to the residents. She told him she was fine to wait if there were emergencies. She would later find out she was the emergency, she had been the emergency for days.
