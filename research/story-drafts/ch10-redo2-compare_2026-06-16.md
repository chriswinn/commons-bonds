## 3. FLATNESS (flattest + most-alive passage per variant, verbatim)

### NEW-A

**Flattest (verbatim):**
> "But here is the move that matters, the one I cannot leave out, because the
> honesty is only half the answer. The framework does not need to win that fight.
> The result holds across the whole range. … The model is honest about what it

This is FILL 4. The relations are correct, but the prose narrates its own
rhetorical bookkeeping ("here is the move that matters, the one I cannot leave
out"; "Both halves are true, and I have learned not to say only the first"). The
man stops thinking and starts explaining that he is being careful. This is the
flattest stretch in either variant and it sits inside the highest-stakes
positioning fill.

**Most alive (verbatim):**
> "Put a Mars administrator in that room too, the one I asked you to imagine,
> running a ten-thousand-person habitat… On Mars the cost severance is not
> impossible. Nothing about human nature changes on the way up. It is impossible

> who will absorb the loss out of sight. Everyone who would bear the cost is in
> the room, breathing the same recycled air."

"There is no downwind… breathing the same recycled air" is A at its best:
concrete, escalating, the abstraction (severance) made physical. This beats the
canonical's Mars beat.

B's close pulls the grandfather thread and the waterman thread into the final

**Flatness call:** NEW-A's flattest sits *inside a positioning fill* (FILL 4) and

## 4. NON-REGRESSION GATE (HARD) — both variants vs the CURRENT canonical

**The gate:** Ch10 is the book's register model. A variant may win ONLY if its
prose MATCHES OR BEATS the canonical grounded harbor register AND lands the
financialization fill. If both regress → KEEP-CANONICAL.

**Canonical register anchors (the bar):** the open observes rather than explains
("A man at the next pier says something to a man on a boat in a voice neither of
them needs to raise"); the single objection is introduced WITHOUT enumeration
machinery ("There is one objection I want to engage… because it is the objection I
find most serious and because the people who make it are, in a real sense, my
allies"); the close is spare and resolved.

**NEW-A vs canonical:**
- *Open:* MATCHES on cadence; very slightly REGRESSES on one move — replaces the
  canonical's observed human exchange with an explanatory gloss ("the way it
  carries everything here, the way it gives the harbor its own acoustics at this
  hour"). Minor.
- *Close:* MATCHES (sparest of the three; clean).
- *Objections section (the fill zone — decisive):* **REGRESSES.** The four fills
  are sequenced with visible checklist machinery — "That is one objection. There
  is a second one, and it cuts deeper… There is a third objection, and I am giving
  it the same care as the other two… There is one more objection, gentler than the
  others." Plus move-announcing inside the fills ("here is the move that matters,
  the one I cannot leave out"; "I have learned not to say only the first";
  "a symmetry asserted is worth nothing and a symmetry demonstrated is the
  point"). The canonical never enumerates or announces. This is the §4.4
  ("checklist of objections-and-answers") + §4.5 (no self-narration) failure mode.
  **NEW-A does NOT match the canonical register through the fill zone.**
- **GATE: NEW-A FAILS the non-regression gate** (lands the fills, but regresses
  the register where the fills live — the one place the gate most cares about).

**NEW-B vs canonical:**
- *Open:* MATCHES — keeps the lean observed cadence; "which in some way it is" is a
  quiet first-person grounding in-register; the short-line opening ("It is the men
  going to work.") is a legitimate register variation, not a regression.
- *Close:* MATCHES OR BEATS — pulls the grandfather/waterman threads into the final
  image; spare; resolved.
- *Objections section (the fill zone — decisive):* **MATCHES.** B opens the section
  with a register-grounded line ("Out here, before light, with no one to perform
  for, I find I can hear the objections more clearly than I can at a desk") and
  carries the four fills with lighter, characterful transitions ("The first comes
  from my allies, which is what makes it sharp"; "There is a near cousin to this
  objection"; "The third objection comes from the other direction, from my
  parents' world"). It EARNS the symmetry close by demonstration rather than
  announcing it. A reader cannot cleanly point at "the new objection-beats" — they
  read as the man working through the hardest things at the dock, which is the
  brief's success criterion #2 and the canonical's standard.
- **GATE: NEW-B PASSES the non-regression gate** — matches-or-beats the canonical
  grounded harbor register AND lands FILL 1 (financialization) at the point of
  maximum pressure.

**GATE RESULT: NEW-A FAILS the non-regression gate; NEW-B PASSES it.** Because at
least one variant (B) matches-or-beats the canonical register while landing the
financialization fill, the verdict is NOT KEEP-CANONICAL. **Promote B.** The
financialization beat ships via the promoted redraft (B), not via author redline.

## 5. THREE VERBATIM SIDE-BY-SIDES

### Side-by-side 1 — THE OPEN (register bar)

**CANONICAL:**
> A long time before the sun comes up at Old Point Comfort, the sound that arrives
> is not light but the sound of men going to work. A diesel coughs and settles. A
> line stops slapping. A man at the next pier says something to a man on a boat in
> a voice neither of them needs to raise, because at that hour sound travels across
> the water as if the water itself were carrying it.

**NEW-A:**
> A long time before the sun, the first thing that arrives is not light. It is the
> sound of men going to work. A diesel coughs somewhere down the line of slips,
> catches, and settles into itself. A line that has been slapping a mast all night
> stops slapping. The sound travels across the water as if the water were carrying
> it, the way it carries everything here, the way it gives the harbor its own
> acoustics at this hour.

**NEW-B:**
> It is a long time before sunrise. The first thing that comes is not light. It is
> the men going to work. / A diesel coughs somewhere down the line of slips,
> catches, settles into its idle. A line that has been slapping a mast all night
> goes quiet. The sound travels to me across the water as if the water itself were
> carrying it, which in some way it is.

*Read:* All three are in-register. A drops the canonical's human exchange ("a man
at the next pier… in a voice neither of them needs to raise") and substitutes an
explanatory gloss ("the way it gives the harbor its own acoustics"). B keeps the
lean observe-don't-explain discipline ("which in some way it is" is the most
restrained of the three glosses). B's open is closest to the canonical bar. **A's
lost human-exchange detail is a fold-in candidate (see §6).**

### Side-by-side 2 — THE FINANCIALIZATION FILL ANSWER (FILL 1, the book's #1 fill)

**NEW-A:**
> And the answer is a distinction, and the distinction is everything. Accounting a
> cost is not coding an asset. They are opposite operations that happen to pass
> near the same word. Pistor's modules code assets, things to be owned and traded
> and charged for. This book accounts costs, debts that must be paid and kept
> visible until they are. … Same word, opposite operation, and the whole framework
> lives on the far side of that distinction from the thing the objection accuses it
> of being.

**NEW-B:**
> Accounting a cost is not coding an asset. They are opposite operations that
> happen to share the word price. Pistor's modules code assets, things made to be
> owned and traded. This book accounts costs. The bond does not create a thing to
> own. It records a debt that has to be paid, and keeps that debt visible until it
> is. Pricing-to-sell turns a forest into board feet. Pricing-to-reserve names what
> the forest is worth so that its destruction can no longer be booked as free. The
> whole polarity of the instrument runs the other way from the one the objection
> fears.

*Read:* Both land the distinction faithfully and Ch5-consistently. A frames it with
"And the answer is a distinction, and the distinction is everything" — a small
move-announcement. B drops straight into "Accounting a cost is not coding an asset"
and lets the forest/board-feet image (Ch5's own) carry it, landing the polarity
without preamble. B is the cleaner delivery of the identical positioning content.

### Side-by-side 3 — THE CLOSE

**CANONICAL:**
> The morning is coming. The boats are going out. The dog is at the foot of the
> companionway, waiting for me to bring the kettle up and to begin the day she has
> decided is, like every day, a perfectly ordinary one to be alive for. / Let us
> begin.

**NEW-A:**
> The first work boat is well out into the channel now. The kettle is about to go
> on. The dog has come up to wait. / Let us begin.

**NEW-B:**
> The light is full enough now to see the far shore. The work boats are out past
> the marker. Somewhere down the line a man I will never meet is hauling pots in
> water my grandfather might have known. The kettle is on. The dog is up. The day
> is made of small attentions, and they are waiting. / Let us begin.

*Read:* All three land "Let us begin." A is the sparest (matches canonical's
economy). B braids the grandfather and waterman threads into the final image and
re-sounds "the day is made of small attentions" — a richer close that matches or
beats the canonical without over-writing. B's close is the strongest of the three.

## 6. A→B FOLD-IN LIST (NO canonical grafts; A-side moves to lift into B)

These are NEW-A inventions worth carrying into the promoted NEW-B. None is a
canonical paste; all originate in A.

1. **A's Mars "no downwind / recycled air" intensifier.** A's Mars beat —
   "There is no downwind. There is no later generation in another country who will
   absorb the loss out of sight. Everyone who would bear the cost is in the room,
   breathing the same recycled air" — is more physical than B's "In a sealed dome
   there is no away. Everyone breathes the same air; everyone drinks the same

   Mars paragraph.
2. **A's Pistor turn-the-diagnosis line.** A makes the Pistor reversal explicit and
   characterful: "I met Pistor earlier in the book as a friend, a diagnostician on
   my side. The honest thing is to admit that the same diagnosis can be turned

   friend-turned-objector framing into B's FILL 1 (it is the brief's explicit
   instruction that Pistor "is already met at Libby in Ch5 as a diagnostic ally;
   here she is turned into the objector").
3. **(Optional, register-light) A's "an hour from where I am sitting" vs B's "half
   a mile."** Keep B's default but have the AUTHOR adjudicate the Chesapeake
   distance at redline — "half a mile" is literally truer to Old Point Comfort's
   position on the Bay; "an hour" reads as drive-time to the oyster grounds.
   Recommend the author pick or trim; not a graft.

**Do NOT fold in** A's objection-enumeration scaffolding ("That is one objection.
There is a second one… There is a third objection, and I am giving it the same
