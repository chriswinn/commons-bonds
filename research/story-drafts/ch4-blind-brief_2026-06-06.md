## 1. CHAPTER ROLE / PURPOSE

Ch 4 is the **existence proof** - the chapter that converts the book's diagnosis (Ch 1-3: cost severance is real and it hurt real communities) into the book's enabling claim (the severance is not geological destiny; it is the output of an institutional architecture, and architecture is a choice that can be engineered differently). Everything the back half of the book proposes depends on this chapter establishing that the honest outcome is *reachable*, not utopian.

The chapter must do five things:

1. **Stage the proof.** Norway took petroleum out of the North Sea and, through deliberate institutional design held across decades, captured the rents for its whole population in perpetuity rather than letting them be severed. This is presented not as a fairy tale but as engineered, debated, deliberately defended institutional machinery.
2. **Bound the proof honestly.** Apply the framework to Norway and show it reduced cost severance dramatically but did NOT zero it out (the barrel is still gone; the externality tail persists). The framework's verdict on the best-managed extraction regime on earth is "much closer to honest pricing than anywhere else, and still not all the way there."
3. **Run the mirror.** Nigeria/Niger Delta - comparable oil, comparable market, opposite institutional architecture, opposite outcome. This is the chapter's proof that the framework DIFFERENTIATES (small severance for Norway, enormous for Nigeria, from identical geology) - which is what makes it a policy instrument and not a slogan that extraction is always bad.
4. **Defeat the "it could never work here" objection** with the reachable-horizon macro-cluster (Alaska, Chattanooga, Mondragon, Vienna - each at brief existence-proof depth) and the architecture-is-a-choice generalization (US Social Security as a same-problem-different-architecture comparison; the eleven-cent argument relocated in from Ch9).
5. **Hand the question forward** - name the four gaps Norway has NOT solved (the promissory note the back half is organized around), give the Ch3 oyster promise a brief home, and hand the industrial-cases baton to Ch5.

The chapter's animating thesis, stated and proved: **the same barrel, from the same kind of seabed, sold into the same market, produces radically different outcomes depending on the institutional architecture that decides who captures the value and who bears the cost - and that architecture is engineered, not given.**
