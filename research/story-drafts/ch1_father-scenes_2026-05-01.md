My father isn't the sort of father that gives a lot of advise. He doesn't really even talk a lot compared to most. So it is notable whenever he did offer some bit of advise on anything. I recall him saying more than once he didn't care what I decided to do with my life as whatever I picked as my career was my decision and mine alone as I would need to be the one who does the work. However he did give one bit of advise on work. He said that even if I decided to dig ditches for a living, at the end of the day I should be able to look at my work and know in my heart that no one has ever done better. He then suggested that even if nobody ever compliments you or your work, you taking your time and taking pride in your work and knowing in your heart that you did a good job will matter. He said every job has it's hard points and taking pride in your work makes the hard days a little more bearable. And he said often times work will go unnoticed, but as long as you know you did a good job... often that's enough to make the day go by a bit easier. My father isn't the sort of guy that walks around ordering people around, which is notable as he has been in managerial positions as long as I have been aware of what he did for a living with the Navy beyond the somewhat vague "naval architect" label. He often said he was around guys that are each Capitan's and Admirals each with command of their own ships, responsibilities, etc. so they are guys that are used to their words being followed to the letter without any further discussion so when in a meeting with others of the same or higher rank and differing opinions they would get in yelling matches trying to drown out other people while expressing their opinions. He had a simple rule he followed in meetings, don't say something in a meeting unless you really have something worth saying. He also would never raise his voice. It's amazing the reaction you'll get in a room of screaming adults when the person that only says something worth listening to starts talking and they start talking at a volume just a hair below normal speaking volume. Everyone shuts up so they can listen. Not because anyone told them to be quite, but because they stopped talking so they themselves could listen to what you had to say. As follows logically he would use the same sorts of things to guide my sister and I as we grew up. One instance I recall vividly, he would give us something to do like clean the living room or the backyard and of course being small kids my sister and I would dutifully rush through the work of cleaning cutting every corner we could think of sweeping piles of dust under the corner of the rug vs.  or raking the leaves, throwing the sticks from the tree under the old shed vs disposing of them properly. Then when we came back to say we had finished and asked if we could go play, obviously off on some grand (to us) adventure... He would start to rise out of his chair and then pause and say before I get up. I want to ask you, when I go check the work you have done will you be proud of the work I'm about to see? And my sister and I would of course say wait wait wait before you bother getting up, let us just do a quick walk through to make sure it's ready for your inspection. And at that point we would of course run to the rug with a broom & dustpan which would deposit it's load in the kitchen trash which we would then carry out to the big trash can outside, and we would crawl under the shed pulling out all of the sticks and break them down and throw them in the trash and then edge the lawn with the weed eater and sweep the sidewalks and collect everything into the trash can. By the time we were finished we would proudly walk back to our father and standing tall we would say we were finished and everything was ready for inspection.
