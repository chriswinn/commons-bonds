## 0. ROLE / PURPOSE / POSTURE

### What this chapter is

Chapter 9 is the book's operationalization chapter. The eight chapters before
it felt the mechanism, named it, cased it, proved it can be reduced (Norway),
generalized it, formalized it, stress-tested it across worlds, and landed a
single worked dollar number on one ton of coal. Chapter 9 answers the next
question a fair reader asks after seeing that number: what do we actually do
about it, using institutions that already exist?

### The one-sentence irreducible job

Deliver a clean, sequential, four-step instrument the reader can hold in
working memory, and prove that every component of it already exists somewhere
in the world right now, so the honest response reads as reachable rather than
utopian, while handing the closing chapter a clean spine.

### The #1 structural mandate

**FOREGROUND THE FOUR-STEP INSTRUMENT: Classify, Reserve, Invest, Reassess.**
It must be:

- stated explicitly and by name up front, in the opening, as a named sequence;
- given its own clean section per step, in order, each section doing one job;
- the single most prominent thing in the chapter, never buried.

A prior version buried the instrument under a stack of defensive and
illustrative essay-sections before the actionability payoff resurfaced. Do not
reproduce that shape. The chapter reads: bridge in -> four steps -> the
international / border step -> the housing application -> count the pieces
that already exist -> property rights and their limits -> what a framework
does -> hand-off. Objections that survive are folded as one-to-three-sentence
inoculations INSIDE the relevant step, not as standalone essays.

**Strengths to preserve (author direction 2026-06-10).** Three things the
prior chapter did well and the redraft must keep doing well:

## 2. REQUIRED BEATS + SECTION ARCHITECTURE

Eleven beats. The four-step instrument (Beats 2-5) is the load-bearing core.
Every beat is abstracted; generate fresh prose for all of it.

### Beat 1 - Bridge in (short)

- Carry forward the prior chapter's single worked number: one ton of
  mid-century McDowell County coal sold at the mine mouth for just under five
  dollars ($4.71 where a point value shows), while its honestly-priced cost
  ran past five hundred dollars per ton once every severed cost was counted
  ($510 total; $496 of it the carbon term, if decomposed). The gap is
  arithmetic, not rhetoric.
- **Scope-corrected universality (supersedes v1 phrasing).** The gap repeats
  **across every extraction that outruns restoration**: always for
  non-renewables (restoration is zero), for renewables only when harvest
  outpaces regeneration, and it prices to zero within restoration. Do NOT
  write "every extraction the economy runs" or any all-extraction-severs
  overclaim. Do NOT add the normative inference that renewables-within-
  regeneration would put the economy "in harmony" with anything; the scope
  statement is strictly descriptive.
- Pivot: the honest response to a gap like that is to ask what to do. Set
  expectations honestly: not a legislative program, not a manifesto; a
  sequence of practical moves operable with institutions that already exist.
- **Name the sequence explicitly and up front: Classify, Reserve, Invest,
  Reassess.** This is the chapter's thesis sentence. Keep the beat tight.
- **Antithesis warning (this beat is the historical hot spot).** A prior
  version fired "It is not X. It is Y." twice in the first four sentences.
  The whole chapter's budget is ~1 per 1,500 words; do not spend it in the
  opening. Let the arithmetic land declaratively.

### Beat 2 - Step 1: Classify

- Sort resources by foreclosure risk; not every resource carries the same
  risk and they should not be treated alike.
- Three bins by the existential substitutability gap (abbreviation note
  below): **low, medium, high.**
  - **Low:** abundant, substitutable in principle (iron, coal-as-fuel), plus
    the renewable commons (sunlight, wind, atmospheric oxygen, regional
    rainfall), which are low by construction because they are themselves the
    renewable substrate the substitute-investment of Step 3 rests on. The
    move: price the externality tail, require adequate reclamation bonding,
    let a corrected market allocate. (This renewable-commons framing is
    already aligned with the 2026-06-09 scope correction; keep it
    descriptive: within-regeneration use prices to zero.)
  - **Medium:** progressive extraction rates plus sovereign-wealth capture of
    rents.
  - **High:** geologically concentrated, chemically hard to substitute,
    required for technologies not yet invented but very likely needed (rare
    earth elements, platinum-group metals, helium / helium isotopes,
    phosphorus for agriculture, fossil-aquifer groundwater on millennial
    regeneration timescales). These get the move the instrument is organized
    around: reservation (Step 2).
- Classification is not mysterious: materials scientists, energy economists,
  agronomists, aerospace engineers, and hydrologists already hold most of the
  needed information. Existing apparatus does part of the work (the EU
  Critical Raw Materials list; the USGS annual mineral assessments). What
  does not exist is a single legible ranking integrating substitutability,
  stock dynamics, externality tail, and criticality into one score; producing
  one is a matter of months, not decades.
- A rough three-bin sort is enough to drive every decision that follows.
- **Fold-in inoculation (one or two sentences):** the Simon-Ehrlich
  substitution tradition: scarcity summons substitutes, so there is no
  missing price. Concede substitution usually wins, which is exactly why the
  instrument invests in substitution (Step 3) and reserves only the residual
  substitution may not reach. One honest sentence plus a Technical Appendix
  pointer; no digression.

### Beat 3 - Step 2: Reserve

- The idea that sounds strange on first hearing and obvious on the second:
  for high-gap resources, designate a fraction of known economic deposits as
  strategic reserve and **leave them in the ground.** Not
  extract-and-warehouse, not stockpile. The ground is free, permanent storage
  requiring no refrigeration, security, or insurance.
- The cost of in-situ preservation is just the foregone revenue on the
  reserved fraction, and it is small, because extraction is usually
  constrained by current demand and mining throughput, not total deposit
  size. (An illustrative reserves-versus-production sketch is permitted ONLY
  if built from canonical / substrate-safe figures and explicitly framed as
  illustrative ("say, ..."); do not invent reserve or production tonnages and
  typeset them to look sourced.)
- Frame the reserve as a **free option**: costs little to hold, appreciates
  as the non-reserved stock depletes, pays the full substitution value if
  scarcity develops, can be reclassified and released if substitution makes
  the resource unnecessary.
- No new international institution required: strategic petroleum reserves and
  mineral stockpiles already exist in most industrial countries. What is
  added is a coherent principle for what to reserve and how much: the Step-1
  ranking, with reserved fractions sized to the asymmetric regret of the
  decision.
- **Discharge the Asymmetric Regret Rule here, in the spine** (inherited from
  the formalization chapter; applied, not re-introduced). If the reserve
  proves unnecessary, the cost is modest foregone revenue spread across a
  century and a global economy; if it proves necessary and the resource was
  already dug up, the cost is civilizational and irreversible. When the
  insurance is cheap and the downside is unbounded, reserving is the rational
  move.
- **Fold-in inoculation (one or two sentences):** the market-oriented
  reader's objection that reservation is industrial policy / picking winners
  / a price distortion inviting capture. Reservation is not subsidy to a
  chosen producer; it is the decision not to foreclose an option, sized by
  regret, and it leaves the corrected market to allocate everything not
  reserved. One sentence, move on.

### Beat 4 - Step 3: Invest

- Reservation buys time; investment decides what to do with it. Two
  simultaneous priorities.
- **Renewable substitutes**: not only renewable energy but the full
  substitutability agenda (alternative battery chemistries for lithium and
  cobalt; recycled and synthesized flows for virgin rare earths; regenerative
  agriculture for fossil aquifers and phosphate; alternative proteins for
  collapsing fisheries). Structural claim: every dollar of substitution
  research raises the substitutability function (lowering the residual
  commons value of high-gap resources), preserves the reserve's optionality,
  and reduces the externality tail of future extraction. The rare expenditure
  reducing present harm, future harm, and future need simultaneously.
- **Alternative supply sources** ("space access"), framed as comparative
  residual-commons-value arithmetic, not science fiction: a lifeless body has
  no community bearing spatial cost severance, no cascading biosphere, an
  externality tail measured against empty rock; its gap is very low by
  construction. As launch costs decline, off-world material eventually
  competes on total honest cost with terrestrial deposits whose full residual
  commons value has been priced. The model says nothing about timing; only
  that the eventual destination of bulk extraction for the most abundant
  materials is off this planet.
- Governing principle: not "never extract" but **extract the
  lowest-residual-commons-value source first.** For most materials over the
  next century that is renewable substitution; for a narrow set,
  eventually extraterrestrial. Both fall out of the arithmetic, not politics.
  (Method description stays at chapter register: no Greek-letter regime
  names, no parameter intervals in running prose; one TA pointer if needed.)

### Beat 5 - Step 4: Reassess

- Nothing is static. A high-gap resource can become low-gap in thirty years
  if a substitution breakthrough matures (non-rare-earth magnet chemistry;
  grid-parity sodium-ion storage; designed-around helium). Reserved fractions
  then reclassify: some released, some held permanently as a hedge against
  the substitute's failure modes.
- Reassessment runs both directions. Two substrate-safe reclassification
  examples: **cobalt** (looked abundant a generation ago; now treated as
  strategically critical by most industrial nations) and **helium** (once so
  cheap the US government effectively gave it away; now priced with the
  seriousness its properties deserve).
- The classification is a living document, maintained by the same scientific
  bodies that produce it, on a roughly five-to-ten-year cadence. The
  machinery largely exists (USGS, the EU science apparatus, the IEA, national
  stockpile programs); what is missing is the structure within which
  reassessment matters, and the gap-driven reserve is that structure.
- **Fold-in inoculation (one sentence):** the discount-rate exposure. Name
  that the choice of discount rate is contested (Stern versus Nordhaus) and
  that the quantitative treatment lives in the Technical Appendix; do not
  re-import the debate.

### Beat 6 - The international / border step

- Cost severance crosses borders; borders are among the mechanisms by which
  it operates. AT MOST two substrate-safe cost-bearer images, kept tight: the
  rare-earth tailings near Baotou (**~4 square miles / ~10 square
  kilometers**; NOT "seven") and the DRC cobalt supply chain (~40,000
  children, **UNICEF 2014 via Amnesty International 2016**, with the "until
  very recently" hedge on DRC share). One compact paragraph, not a section;
  the book already built the premise.
- The problem for honest national pricing: a country pricing domestic
  extraction honestly while importing unpriced foreign extraction has merely
  relocated where severance enters its economy. The import gap is the new
  problem.
- The response is a principle, not a drafted treaty: apply the pricing to
  imports, mechanically a **border adjustment** scaled to the residual
  commons value the exporting country failed to price. The EU Carbon Border
  Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) is a partial, real, working-imperfectly version
  for carbon alone: proof the move is legally, administratively, and
  diplomatically feasible at sufficient scale.
- It spreads by market access, not treaty: it begins where any country or
  bloc decides to begin, the way carbon pricing spread from a handful of
  jurisdictions to many.
- **Fold-in inoculation (one clause):** CBAM faces WTO non-discrimination
  questions and retaliation risk; do not overstate feasibility.
