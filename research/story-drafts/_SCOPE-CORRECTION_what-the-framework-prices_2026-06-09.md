# Canonical scope correction — what the framework prices (2026-06-09)

**Supersedes** the framing "the book only prices non-renewable commons, and renewable commons extracted past their regenerative capacity." That framing is too narrow and is internally inconsistent with the framework's own ten-category commons taxonomy. Every blind redraft and every brief must adopt the canonical statement below.

## Canonical statement (use this verbatim or reinvent in your own prose; descriptive ONLY)

> The framework prices cost severance, the foreclosure of commons value. That foreclosure is positive precisely when consumption forecloses future access faster than the commons can restore itself. For a non-renewable resource it is always positive, because restoration is zero. For a renewable resource it is positive only when harvest outpaces regeneration. For non-resource commons, such as institutional, civic, or epistemic commons, it is positive when degradation outruns recovery. Where use stays within what the commons can restore, the price is correctly zero.

## Why the old framing fails

1. **It excludes the non-resource commons the book demonstrably prices.** The ten commons include Epistemic (information), Institutional, Political, and Cohesion, none of which are renewable/non-renewable resources with a regeneration rate. The TA's §3 Theorem-10.3 domain restriction even listed "information / non-rivalrous" and "network-effect" commons as out of scope, which contradicted the taxonomy. (TA §3 is now reframed: that restriction bounds Theorem 10.3, not the framework.)
2. **Sustainable renewable use is not out of scope; it is in scope and prices to zero.** CS = RCV − B yields no positive severance when extraction stays within regeneration. Framing that as a scope limit mistakes a clean result (zero) for inapplicability.
3. **Theorem 10.3's domain restriction is legitimate as a theorem, but was over-generalized into a whole-framework scope claim** in the prose (Ch6).

## Hard caveat (author directive)

Do NOT include the normative inference that "if we used only renewables within regeneration the ecosystem would be in harmony." It is a political/ethical conclusion the framework does not need, and it hands antagonists an attack surface. Keep the scope statement strictly descriptive: what the math computes, not what we ought to use.
