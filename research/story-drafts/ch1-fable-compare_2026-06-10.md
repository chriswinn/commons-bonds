## 3. Side-by-side excerpts (verbatim; same beat)

### Excerpt 1 — Tubman passage close (the author's freshest redline; canonical wins)

**Canonical:**
> The wrongness there is not a missing line on a ledger. It is the ledger torn up and the person who kept it sold. The shape repeats throughout this book. It is the same shape, value generated by one person and captured by another. But the weights are not the same and will never be the same. A commute and a stolen life do not belong on the same line, and they will not share one here. Naming the shape of them is not the same as making them equal.

**Variant A:**
> There, no line went missing from a ledger. The ledger itself was torn up, and the person who kept it was sold. The shape repeats throughout this book, value generated by one person and captured by another, but the weights are not the same and will never be the same. A commute and a stolen life do not belong on the same line, and they will not share one here. Naming the shape of them is not the same as making them equal.

**Variant B:**
> The wrongness there is not a missing line on a ledger; it is the ledger torn up and the person who kept it sold. The shape repeats throughout this book, value generated by one person and captured by another, but the weights are not the same and will never be the same. A commute and a stolen life do not belong on the same line, and they will not share one here.

A passivizes the torn-ledger sentences and fuses three author-crafted
sentences into one, losing the 06-09 rhythm. B drops the final guard
sentence the author kept on his own pass the day before this bake-off.
The redline said: do not auto-edit this paragraph. Both did.

### Excerpt 2 — the load-bearing wall (canonical wins)

**Canonical:**
> Take him out and the structure does not sag. It comes down.

**Variant A:**
> Take him out and nothing sags first; the whole structure comes down at once.

**Variant B:**
> Take him out and the building would not sag; it would come down.

Canonical: two short present-tense sentences, the punch isolated on "It comes
down." A buries it under a semicolon and "at once"; B softens it into the
conditional.

### Excerpt 3 — held-baby close (variants win the substance via the ratified breath-count rescue; canonical wins the cascade)

**Canonical:**
> That was the whole of it. A warm chest instead of a plastic bed. I have never in my life been more completely in one place than I was on those mornings, counting my breaths, his weight in my hands, with nowhere else I was and nothing else I was doing.

**Variant A:**
> That was the whole of it, a warm chest instead of a plastic bed. To stay there I counted breaths, a seven on the inhale and a seven on the exhale, or an eleven and an eleven, whichever I chose at the start held the same for the whole sitting, counting the breaths up as far as I could before my mind wandered, and then beginning again at one. I have never in my life been more completely in one place than I was on those mornings, his weight in my two hands, the count climbing, with nowhere else I was and nothing else I was doing.

**Variant B:**
> That was the whole of it: a warm chest instead of a plastic bed. I counted my breaths to stay inside those mornings, a seven on the inhale and a seven on the exhale, or an eleven and an eleven, whichever count I had chosen at the start held for the whole sitting, counting the breaths upward for as long as I could before some thought carried me off, and then beginning again at one. I have never in my life been more completely in one place than I was on those mornings, his weight in my hands, nowhere else I was and nothing else I was doing.

The breath-count is the author's ratified rescue and belongs in this
paragraph — but in canonical's frame: keep the fragment button ("That was the
whole of it. A warm chest instead of a plastic bed."), insert the count.
A's "the count climbing" is the best single phrase either variant
contributed here.

## 4. Why not PROMOTE-*-WITH-FOLDIN

Strip from either variant everything that is (a) the author's pending redline
or (b) a regression against the author's freshest pass, and what remains is a
handful of paragraph-splits and two or three phrases ("the count climbing";
B's declarative ceiling close). The fold-in list required to repair a
promoted variant (restore canonical's Tubman paragraph; restore "quiet
center"; restore the echo-repetition; strip semicolon register; strip
inversions/italics; strip 6 embedded structure-note comments; re-arbitrate
the UR appositive) is longer than the redline-application list canonical
already owes. Promotion would launder the author's queued work through a
Fable draft while importing new regressions. KEEP-CANONICAL is the honest
verdict.
