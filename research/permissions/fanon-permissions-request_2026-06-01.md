## D. Fair-use fallback statement

If all three letters return declines OR if response timing pushes past the Noema submission
window, the substrate-safe fallback is the V-D-prime hybrid as currently drafted (Fanon
paraphrase only). The audit finding F-DE-VD-M1 disposition Option A (HOLD as substrate-safety-
driven) + Option D (surface as Stage 5 pre-pub-queue acknowledgment) remain available and were
recommended as the conservative default in the audit. The permissions-request pathway is
an UPGRADE opportunity, not a submission gate.

**Standard US academic-essay fair-use threshold (for context, NOT legal advice):** the four-
factor test (Section 107 of US Copyright Act) typically protects brief quotation of ≤300 words
from a substantial work for purposes of literary-philosophical commentary in non-commercial or
scholarly contexts. *Noema* is a non-commercial magazine (Berggruen Institute is a 501(c)(3)
non-profit); the proposed quotation is brief (≤50 words); the use is critical-lineage
commentary, not market-substitution. A US copyright attorney would likely advise that a 25-40-
word quotation under these conditions falls within fair use. **But fair-use is risk-allocation,
not entitlement** — the rights-holder retains the legal right to challenge any quotation, and
permissions-clearance is the conservative discipline that avoids the risk entirely.
