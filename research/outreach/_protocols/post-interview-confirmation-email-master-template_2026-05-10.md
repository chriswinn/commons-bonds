# Post-interview confirmation email — master template

**Created:** 2026-05-10 by Claude per author direction (protocol-update session).
**Companion to:** [`interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md`](./interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md) §"Post-interview correspondence (belt-and-suspenders)" and [`pre-interview-privacy-notice-eu-uk_2026-05-10.md`](./pre-interview-privacy-notice-eu-uk_2026-05-10.md).
**Purpose:** Reusable master template for the belt-and-suspenders written confirmation the author sends within 1–3 days of every interview. Captures in writing what the verbal Q0(a) + Q0(b) already settled, gives the subject a chance to refine, and supplies the kind of documentation publishing-house permissions teams will appreciate when the trade-contract phase arrives.

**Tone.** Plain-language and relational. This is a personal email, not legal paperwork. Customize aggressively per subject — the grace notes and the substantive feedback line are what make it read as real correspondence rather than a form letter.

**Scope.** Confirms five things in writing — (1) attribution preference and any specific quotes the author plans to highlight, (2) recording-handling, (3) applicable-law / jurisdiction, (4) publication scope, and (5) any follow-up / warm-intro outcomes from the conversation. For EU/UK subjects, also (6) reaffirmation of the pre-interview privacy notice and subject rights.

**Does NOT:** do a full transcript review (that's a separate Category-3 deliverable if the subject chose Category 3).

> *Operational scaffolding, not legal advice.*

---

## How to assemble a per-interview email from this master

The template is modular. The author selects:

1. **One jurisdiction-variant block** based on where the subject is and where the author is (US one-party / US two-party / EU-UK).
2. **One attribution-category-variant block** based on what the subject said at Q0(b) (Category 1 / 2 / 3 / 4).
3. **Optional follow-up blocks** as relevant:
   - Warm-intro candidate-discovery block (if the subject surfaced names during the call).
   - Specific-quote-list block (Category 1 only — the author lists specific quotes they plan to use).
4. **Subject-specific grace notes and substantive-feedback line** — never skip these; they're what make the email feel like correspondence.

Concatenate the selected blocks in the order shown below, fill the placeholders, and send within 1–3 days of the call.

## Jurisdiction-variant blocks (select ONE)

### Variant J-A — US one-party-consent state (subject's end) / one-party-consent state (author's end)

*Applies when both parties are in US one-party-consent jurisdictions. Examples: Darity (NC) ↔ author (VA); Moore (VA) ↔ author (VA); Mian (NJ) ↔ author (VA).*

**On the recording.** At the start of the call I asked your verbal yes to recording the conversation, and you said yes. (Confirming for the record.) The audio file sits on my own working devices — not a cloud service — and is used only to produce an accurate working transcript for the materials above. Once your attribution review (if any) and the relevant manuscript work are complete, I'll delete the audio and let you know here that it's done. If you'd prefer it gone sooner, say the word and I'll delete it now.

**On applicable law.** The call took place under one-party-consent recording rules on both ends — you in \[SUBJECT US STATE\], me in Virginia — so the attribution and recording handling laid out above governs accordingly. If your institution or anyone on your side would prefer an additional written form for permissions records, tell me what's wanted and I'll provide it.

### Variant J-B — US two-party / all-party-consent state (one or both parties)

*Applies when one or both parties are in US two-party-consent jurisdictions. Examples: Colden (MD) ↔ author (VA); Boyce (MA) ↔ author (VA); Sufi (IL) ↔ author (VA); also any future Pennsylvania, California, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Hawaii, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Washington subject.*

**On the recording.** \[SUBJECT FIRST NAME\], your state (\[STATE NAME\]) is an all-party-consent jurisdiction for telephone recording, so at the start of the call I asked your explicit verbal yes to recording the conversation, and you said yes. **Confirming for the record: you said yes to my request to record at the start of our call. Thank you.** My own consent is on the audio file as well. The audio sits on my own working devices — not a cloud service — and is used only to produce an accurate working transcript for the materials above. Once your attribution review (if any) and the relevant manuscript work are complete, I'll delete the audio and let you know here that it's done. If you'd prefer it gone sooner, say the word and I'll delete it now.

**On applicable law.** The call took place under \[SUBJECT US STATE\] all-party-consent recording rules (your end) and Virginia one-party-consent rules (my end). Because the rules differ, I've defaulted to the stricter standard — explicit verbal yes from both parties on tape — and the attribution and recording handling laid out above governs accordingly. If your institution or anyone on your side would prefer an additional written form for permissions records, tell me what's wanted and I'll provide it.

### Variant J-C — EU / UK subject (GDPR / UK GDPR scope)

*Applies when the subject is in the EU or the UK, or is otherwise within GDPR / UK GDPR scope. Examples: Mazzucato (UK) ↔ author (VA); Dagan (Netherlands) ↔ author (VA); Buller (UK), Raworth (UK), Mondragon-press (Spain).*

**On the recording.** At the start of the call I asked your verbal yes to recording the conversation, and you said yes. (Confirming for the record.) The audio file sits on my own working devices — not a cloud service — and is used only to produce an accurate working transcript for the materials above. Once your attribution review (if any) and the relevant manuscript work are complete, I'll delete the audio and let you know here that it's done. If you'd prefer it gone sooner, say the word and I'll delete it now.

**On the data-protection note.** Ahead of our call I sent a short pre-interview privacy notice covering how I'd handle the audio, the transcript, and our correspondence under \[UK GDPR + the Data Protection Act 2018 / GDPR + the Dutch UAVG / GDPR + the Spanish LOPDGDD / etc. — fit to member-state\]. The journalism / literary exemption under Article 85 GDPR is invoked in good faith for the processing described there. Reaffirming here: I'll retain the audio and transcript through publication and a reasonable post-publication archive period; I'll respect your data-subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, objection) in good faith, modulated by the journalism / literary exemption where it applies; and you can lodge a complaint with \[ICO / Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens / Agencia Española de Protección de Datos / etc. — fit to member-state\] if you ever have one.

If anything in the privacy notice needs clarification or refinement now that we've talked, tell me and I'll respond. If you'd like the audio deleted sooner than the standard retention window, say the word.

## Attribution-category-variant blocks (select ONE)

### Variant C-1 — Freely quotable (Category 1)

*I'll quote you directly where it serves the writing, with attribution.* I'll note here the specific quotes I'm currently planning to use, so you have a record of the material I'm working with:

\[INSERT specific-quote-list block (see below) — 3–6 quote-fragments with surrounding context, 1–2 sentences each. Do not skip; the specific-quote-list is the whole point of the Category 1 variant.\]

If anything in this list doesn't sit right, let me know and I'll revise or drop it.

### Variant C-2 — Paraphrase + attribution; review direct quotes before publication (Category 2)

*I'll paraphrase our conversation freely with attribution to you. For any direct quotes, I'll send you the specific sentences I want to use, in their surrounding context, before they're published — book and essays both. The first batch will likely come from me when I have a draft of \[SECTION / CHAPTER / ESSAY WHERE THE HIGHEST-LEVERAGE MATERIAL FROM THIS SUBJECT SITS\]; I'll send that within a few weeks if everything is on schedule.*

### Variant C-3 — Full transcript review (Category 3)

*I'll send you the transcript when it's ready (allow me about a week to clean it up after the call), and you can redact, clarify, or strike anything before I use any of it. I won't quote or paraphrase you in the book or essays until you've signed off on the transcript material I'm drawing on.*

### Variant C-4 — Off-record entirely (Category 4)

*Our conversation will inform the framework but won't be cited in the book or essays. Your name won't appear as a source. I appreciate the time and the substance regardless; the conversation has already shaped my thinking.*

## Specific-quote-list block (Category 1 only)

The Category 1 variant requires this block. Each entry is 1–2 sentences of context plus the proposed direct quote in italics. Aim for 3–6 entries — enough to give the subject a real preview, not so many that the email becomes a transcript.

Sample structure:

> 1. **In \[CONTEXT — e.g., "Chapter 5's accountability-gap argument"\]:** "When I asked about the gap between the science and the regulation, you said \[*direct quote*\]." I plan to use this as \[USAGE — e.g., "the load-bearing voice in the section's opening paragraph"\].
>
> 2. **In \[CONTEXT\]:** "On the question of \[TOPIC\], you said \[*direct quote*\]." I plan to use this as \[USAGE\].
>
> *...etc.*

Include the surrounding context for each quote so the subject can see how it's being framed, not just the quote in isolation.

## Warm-intro block (optional — include only if applicable)

Use only when the subject actually surfaced names during the call. Don't pre-populate names the subject didn't raise.

**On follow-up / warm-intro indications.** Late in our conversation \[I asked / you surfaced\] who in your network you thought would find this framework interesting. \[ADAPT IN 1–3 SENTENCES TO WHAT THE SUBJECT ACTUALLY SURFACED — name the candidate(s) raised, quote or paraphrase the framing used, and acknowledge any nuance about who is or isn't a good fit right now. If the subject demurred or the question landed without specifics, say that plainly and note you'll revisit only if and when it makes sense.\]

Rather than guess at next steps, I've drafted short forwarding notes (for you to send) and direct-email versions (only if you'd rather I write directly) for the candidates likeliest to come up given your network. The checklist below is meant to be easy to mark up — please check, annotate, strike, or replace anything:

- [ ] **\[CANDIDATE NAME 1\]** *(\[BRIEF FRAMING — why this candidate, what their relationship to the subject is, what the warm-intro ask would be\])* — happy to send you a short forwarding note for your approval first.
- [ ] **\[CANDIDATE NAME 2\]** *(\[BRIEF FRAMING\])* — same handling.
- [ ] **\[CANDIDATE NAME 3 — IF APPLICABLE\]** — same handling.
- [ ] **Someone else you named:** \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ — same handling.
- [ ] **No warm-intro path right now** — happy to leave it; I'll revisit only if it surfaces naturally.

I won't write to anyone on this list until you've given me a green light (or forwarded yourself); if any of these are wrong for any reason, just strike them.

## Notes for the author

- **Customize aggressively.** This is a master template, not a form letter. The grace notes and the substantive feedback line are what make the per-interview email read as personal correspondence. If you can't think of a real grace note or a real manuscript-shift line, the call was thinner than you thought; rework rather than padding with flattery.
- **Send within 1–3 days of the call** while it's still fresh in both your minds.
- **If the subject chose between Category 2 or 3 verbally but you're unsure which:** lean toward Category 2 in the email and ask the subject to confirm or clarify in their reply. The subject gets to choose; don't lock them into something tighter than what they said.
- **For the warm-intro block, fill in only the candidates who actually came up** — don't pre-populate names the subject didn't surface. The checklist is a low-friction surface for them to refine, not a menu of asks.
- **Keep your records.** File the subject's reply at `research/outreach/subjects/<subject>/post-interview-correspondence_<DATE>.md` along with a note on the chosen attribution category, the recording-handling agreement, and any warm-intro outcomes. This feeds the project's interview-attribution + consent log.
- **EU/UK subjects:** the per-subject variant files in `research/outreach/subjects/<subject>/post-interview-confirmation-email_template_2026-05-10.md` pre-select Variant J-C and the relevant member-state language, so you don't have to assemble from scratch each time.

## When this template should be revised

- After any interview where the subject pushes back or refines the framing — capture the refinement and update the relevant variant block here.
- When a new pipeline subject's jurisdiction doesn't fit any existing variant (e.g., a first interview in a fundamentally new regime: Canada, Australia, Japan, Brazil) — add a new variant block rather than stretching an existing one.
- After the publishing/entertainment lawyer consultation (target late June 2026, cascade plan June D-track) — incorporate any refinements counsel suggests.
