# Interview attribution + consent-handling protocol

**Created:** 2026-05-06 by Claude per author direction.
**Date modified:** 2026-05-10 — v2 extension adds recording-consent Q0 layer, jurisdiction-aware variants, and an EU/UK pre-interview privacy-notice layer. See Update Log at bottom.
**Purpose:** Reusable discipline for handling attribution preferences AND recording consent AND EU/UK data-protection notice obligations across all interview-based outreach for *Commons Bonds*. Ensures every interview is conducted under clear ground rules — about both how the subject's words will appear in print and how the conversation itself will be recorded and handled — established explicitly before any substantive questions get asked.

> *Operational scaffolding, not legal advice.* The protocol below is reasonable-practice guidance for a working author. Substantive legal questions about US recording-consent jurisdictions, GDPR/UK GDPR processing, journalism exemptions, defamation, or model-release requirements should be addressed by a publishing/entertainment lawyer — see the cascade-plan lawyer-consultation milestone targeted for late June 2026 (before agent Wave 1).

The protocol now has **three distinct layers** at the start of every interview:

1. **Recording-consent Q0 (new — verbal, on tape).** Explicit yes/no to the act of recording. Legal floor in two-party-consent jurisdictions; best practice everywhere.
2. **Attribution-preference Q0 (existing, unchanged — Categories 1–4 below).** How the subject's words may appear in print.
3. **EU/UK pre-interview privacy notice (new — written, sent in advance).** Triggered when the subject is in EU/UK or otherwise within GDPR / UK GDPR scope. Sent with or before the pre-read brief. Does NOT replace the verbal Q0s; both are required.

Layer 1 and Layer 2 are sequential at the start of the call (recording-consent first, attribution-preference second). Layer 3 happens days in advance via email.

---

## When to handle attribution

**Pre-interview (in advance comms):** Mention in the pre-read brief or scheduling email that attribution preferences will be discussed at the start of the conversation. Phrasing example (from Darity pre-read brief, 2026-05-05):

> *On attribution: I'd like to quote you in the book and in any derivative essays. Scholars / institutional voices / [adapt per recipient] handle attribution differently — some prefer freely-quotable interview answers, others prefer that interview material be paraphrased + attributed unless reviewed in advance, others ask for transcript review before any quoting. Whatever works for you, please let me know at the start of our conversation; I'll abide by it across the book and essays.*

Function: primes the subject so they've thought about it before the call. Lets them reply in writing if they have a clear preference. Saves interview minutes.

**Live interview — sequence the two Q0s in this order, before any substantive question:**

**Q0(a) — Recording-consent (asked FIRST, before anything else, with the recorder already running):**

> *Before we get started, I want to ask if it's okay for me to record this conversation. I'll be using the recording for my own accuracy when I write — quotes, paraphrases, transcription if we go that route — not for any broader distribution. Is that okay with you?*

Function: captures explicit verbal consent (or refusal) to recording itself, on the audio file, before any substantive content is on tape. Distinct from attribution-preference, which is about how words may appear *in print*. Required by law in two-party-consent jurisdictions; best practice everywhere. See the jurisdiction-aware variants section below.

If the subject says no to recording, stop recording immediately and proceed by hand-note. Proceed to Q0(b) regardless.

**Q0(b) — Attribution-preference (asked SECOND, after recording-consent is settled):**

> *Before we get into the framework questions, I want to ask about your preferences for how I handle our conversation in print. The book and any derivative essays — there's a Noema-track essay in development, possibly an Aeon-track piece on a separate angle — would draw on this conversation. People handle attribution differently — some prefer freely-quotable answers, some prefer paraphrase + attribution unless reviewed first, others ask for full transcript review before any quoting. Whatever works for you, I'll abide by it across the book and essays. What would you prefer?*

Function: ensures it gets done; signals seriousness/preparation; creates clear ground rules for the rest of the conversation.

## Recording-consent Q0 — jurisdiction-aware variants

Recording-consent law in the US is **jurisdiction-dependent**. Roughly half the states (and federal law) follow a **one-party-consent** rule (only one party — typically the recorder — must consent). The rest follow a **two-party-consent** (a.k.a. **all-party-consent**) rule (every party on the call must consent). When the parties are in different jurisdictions, courts disagree on which governs; the safe default is to record consent on tape from every party regardless.

For *Commons Bonds* pipeline subjects:

| Jurisdictional category | Q0(a) phrasing | Pipeline subjects (current pipeline; non-exhaustive) | Legal status of Q0(a) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Two-party / all-party consent state** | Standard Q0(a) above. Note explicitly that every party on the call must consent: *"…and just for the record, since both ends of this call need to consent — that's a yes from me on my end, too."* | **Colden (MD), Boyce (MA, UMass/PERI), Sufi (IL, Chicago Booth)**, plus future subjects in CA, CT, DE, FL, HI, MT, NV, NH, PA, WA. | **Not optional.** Recording without all-party consent is a statutory violation. |
| **One-party consent state** | Standard Q0(a) above. No need to invoke jurisdictional reciprocity language. | **Darity (NC), Moore (VA), Mian (NJ)**, plus the federal-law floor for most other US states. | Best practice. The interviewer's own consent satisfies state law; the subject's verbal yes is still recommended for clean documentation and for the case where the subject's jurisdiction is unclear or in motion. |
| **Cross-jurisdictional (parties in different states; one or both two-party)** | Use the two-party variant. Treat as if the stricter rule governs. | E.g., a Chris Winn (VA, one-party) call with Allison Colden (MD, two-party) → MD rules govern Colden's end; explicit consent on tape from both sides is the safe and lawful approach. | **Default to stricter.** Courts disagree on which jurisdiction's law controls a cross-state call; explicit consent on tape from all parties moots the question. |
| **EU / UK subject** | Standard Q0(a) above. The pre-interview privacy notice (Layer 3 below) handles the data-protection layer; Q0(a) handles the recording-consent layer. | **Mazzucato (UK), Dagan (Netherlands), Buller (UK), Raworth (UK), Mondragon press (Spain).** | Verbal consent is required in addition to the written notice; member-state rules on call-recording vary, and consent on tape is the safe floor. |

**Operational note.** This table is reasonable-practice scaffolding for a working author, not legal advice. When stakes are high or jurisdictions are unclear (e.g., a subject who travels frequently, an institutional voice subject to additional employer constraints, or a call routed through an aggregator that complicates jurisdiction), consult a publishing/entertainment lawyer.

## EU / UK pre-interview privacy-notice layer

When the subject is in the EU, the UK, or is otherwise within GDPR / UK GDPR scope (EU/UK residency; processing of their personal data by an EU/UK-established controller; or a non-EU controller processing the data of an EU/UK data subject in a way that triggers Article 3(2)), a **pre-interview privacy notice** must be sent in advance of the interview — typically attached to or sent alongside the pre-read brief, a few days before the call.

Template at: [`research/outreach/_protocols/pre-interview-privacy-notice-eu-uk_2026-05-10.md`](./pre-interview-privacy-notice-eu-uk_2026-05-10.md).

Why required:

- **Article 13 / 14 information duty.** When personal data is collected from a data subject (audio recording + derivative transcript + notes = personal data), the controller must provide identity, contact details, purposes of processing, lawful basis, retention period, recipients, transfer information, and the subject's rights — at the time of collection. Sending the notice in advance of the interview satisfies "at the time of collection" reasonably and gives the subject a chance to read it without time pressure.
- **Article 6 lawful basis.** Combination of (a) **consent** (the subject's verbal yes at Q0(a) + their acknowledgment of the privacy notice) and (b) **legitimate interests** pursued by the author and readers in journalism / literary work. The notice should name both.
- **Article 85 journalism / literary exemption.** Article 85 GDPR requires member states to reconcile data protection with freedom of expression, including journalism and academic / artistic / literary expression. Most member states (and the UK under the Data Protection Act 2018) have implemented the exemption to varying degrees. The privacy notice invokes the exemption while still providing the Article 13 information — the exemption modulates data-subject rights (especially erasure) rather than abolishing them, and it is good-faith practice to provide the notice regardless.

Pipeline subjects under GDPR / UK GDPR scope:

- **Mazzucato** (UK, UCL/IIPP — UK GDPR + DPA 2018)
- **Dagan** (Netherlands — GDPR; Dutch implementation of Article 85 via UAVG)
- **Buller** (UK — UK GDPR + DPA 2018)
- **Raworth** (UK — UK GDPR + DPA 2018)
- **Mondragon press** (Spain — GDPR; Spanish implementation via LOPDGDD)

**Crucially:** the privacy notice does NOT replace the verbal Q0(a) recording-consent. Both are required for EU/UK subjects — the notice is the *written* information-and-acknowledgment layer; Q0(a) is the *verbal* consent-on-tape layer.

What the notice covers (full structure in the template):

- Author / data-controller identity + contact.
- Purpose, lawful basis, journalism / literary exemption invocation.
- Data categories collected (audio + derivative transcript + correspondence).
- Retention period (through publication + a reasonable post-publication archive).
- Subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, objection, complaint to supervisory authority — subject to the journalism exemption).
- International transfer note (data processed in the US where the author is located).
- Author contact for data-rights requests.

The notice is plain-language and relational in tone, not legalese. The subject is asked to acknowledge receipt before the call; the acknowledgment + Q0(a) verbal yes together form the consent record.

## The four preference categories

| # | Preference | What it requires from us | Setup implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Freely quotable** | Note in interview log; proceed normally. | Easiest. Recording + transcription optional but recommended for accuracy. |
| 2 | **Paraphrase + attribution; review before direct quotes** | Send any direct quotes to subject before publication for approval. Paraphrased material doesn't need pre-review. | Recording recommended for accurate paraphrase. Pre-publication review pass adds 1-2 weeks to manuscript timeline. |
| 3 | **Full transcript review before any use** | Send transcript to subject for review + approval; subject can redact / clarify / strike anything before we use it. | Recording + transcription required. Transcript-review pass adds 2-4 weeks to manuscript timeline. Subject's edits are binding. |
| 4 | **Off-record entirely** | Conversation informs the framework but is never directly cited. Subject's name doesn't appear. | No setup required, but lower book-leverage for the interview. |

## What to do if the subject hasn't decided

Offer the four categories as a menu. Most subjects will pick one quickly. If they're uncertain, default to **Category 2 (paraphrase + attribution; review before direct quotes)** — it's the most common preference for academic/institutional figures and gives the subject a review touchpoint without burdening the manuscript timeline as much as Category 3.

## What to do if preferences shift mid-interview

If the subject says something they want to retract or wants to put off-record, honor it immediately. Re-confirm the modified ground rules verbally. Note the redaction in the interview log.

## What to do for follow-up correspondence

If the subject sends a follow-up email, the contents of that email follow the ground rules established at Q0 unless they specify otherwise. Treat any explicit "off the record" annotation as binding.

## Documentation discipline

For each interview, record in the interview log:

- **Recording-consent.** Q0(a) yes/no, captured on the audio file. Jurisdiction of each party at the time of the call. If the call is hand-noted because the subject declined recording, note that explicitly.
- **Attribution-preference.** Subject's chosen preference category (1, 2, 3, or 4), captured on the audio file.
- **Any modifications agreed during the conversation** — to recording, attribution, or off-record handling.
- **Any post-interview adjustments** — including any written follow-up that confirms or refines the verbal record (e.g., the post-interview confirmation email per the master template at `research/outreach/_protocols/post-interview-confirmation-email-master-template_2026-05-10.md`).
- **For EU/UK subjects:** copy of the privacy notice sent + date sent + the subject's acknowledgment of receipt.

This protects both the subject and the manuscript: if any quote or recording is challenged later, we have a layered consent record on file.

## Post-interview correspondence (belt-and-suspenders)

The verbal Q0(a) + Q0(b) + (for EU/UK) the privacy-notice acknowledgment are sufficient consent on their own. A written **post-interview confirmation email** sent within 1–3 days of the call is a belt-and-suspenders move: it paraphrases the verbal record in writing, gives the subject a chance to refine, and supplies the kind of documentation a publishing-house permissions team will appreciate when the trade-contract phase arrives. The master template at `research/outreach/_protocols/post-interview-confirmation-email-master-template_2026-05-10.md` has jurisdiction-variant blocks (US one-party / US two-party / EU-UK) + attribution-category-variant blocks (1/2/3/4) that the author selects and combines per interview. Per-subject pre-filled variants live alongside each subject's prep stack.
