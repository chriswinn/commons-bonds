# Pre-interview privacy notice — EU / UK subjects (template)

**Created:** 2026-05-10 by Claude per author direction (protocol-update session).
**Companion to:** [`interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md`](./interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md) §"EU / UK pre-interview privacy-notice layer."
**Purpose:** Reusable template the author sends to EU/UK interview subjects in advance of the call — typically attached to or sent alongside the pre-read brief, a few days before the interview. Provides the GDPR / UK GDPR Article 13 information that data-protection law requires when personal data (audio recording, derivative transcript, correspondence) is collected from a data subject.

**Triggers (apply when ANY is true):**
- Subject is in the EU at the time of the interview.
- Subject is in the UK at the time of the interview.
- Subject is an EU/UK resident regardless of where the call takes place.
- The subject's data is otherwise within GDPR / UK GDPR scope (e.g., processing by an EU-established controller; or non-EU controller processing EU/UK data subject's data in a way that triggers Article 3(2)).

**Does NOT replace the verbal Q0(a) recording-consent at the start of the call.** The notice is the written information-and-acknowledgment layer; Q0(a) is the verbal consent-on-tape layer. Both are required.

> *This template is operational scaffolding, not legal advice. The notice is intended as good-faith reasonable practice under GDPR Article 13 / 14. For specific legal questions on a given interview, GDPR processing of journalistic material, or the journalism / literary exemption under Article 85 (and member-state implementations — UK DPA 2018, Dutch UAVG, Spanish LOPDGDD, etc.), consult a publishing/entertainment lawyer. The cascade-plan lawyer-consultation milestone is targeted for late June 2026 (before agent Wave 1).*

## How to use the template

1. Copy the template body below into the email (or a short attached document, your call).
2. Fill the **\[BRACKETED PLACEHOLDERS\]** — subject name, scheduled interview date, expected interview format, and any chosen attribution category (if the subject has already indicated a preference in advance comms; otherwise leave the category as "to be confirmed verbally at the start of the call").
3. Send a few days before the call so the subject has time to read without time pressure. Ask the subject to acknowledge receipt (a short "received, thank you" reply is sufficient).
4. File the sent copy + the subject's acknowledgment in `research/outreach/subjects/<subject>/correspondence_<DATE>.md` for the consent record.

## Template body

**To:** \[SUBJECT EMAIL\]
**Subject:** *Commons Bonds* interview — short data-protection note ahead of our call

\[SUBJECT NAME\],

Ahead of our conversation on \[INTERVIEW DATE\], I wanted to send a short note covering how I'll be handling the recording, transcript, and correspondence from the interview. This is partly because EU / UK data-protection law (GDPR / UK GDPR) asks me to share it in writing, and partly because I'd rather you have the picture in plain language before we talk, so we can spend the call itself on substance.

**Who I am.** I'm Chris Winn, writing *Commons Bonds* — a forthcoming trade book on cost-severance accountability, and a handful of magazine essays adjacent to it. I work independently, from Hampton, Virginia, in the United States. For any data-protection question or request, you can reach me at \[AUTHOR EMAIL\].

**What I'll be collecting.** With your verbal consent at the start of the call, I'll be making an audio recording of our conversation. From that audio I'll produce notes and (where useful) a working transcript. I also already hold the correspondence we've exchanged setting up the interview. That's the full set of personal data this interview generates on my end.

**Why I'm collecting it.** Two purposes, both journalistic and literary:

- *Source for the book and adjacent essays.* Quotes, paraphrases, and framework feedback drawn from our conversation, attributed to you under whatever attribution preference you choose at the start of the call (more on that below). The book is *Commons Bonds*; the essays are currently *Noema*-track and *Aeon*-track, with op-ed and academic / policy adaptations possible.
- *Accuracy.* The recording is so I can quote and paraphrase you correctly rather than from memory.

**Lawful basis for processing.** Under GDPR Article 6, I am relying on a combination of (a) **your consent** — your verbal yes to the recording at the start of the call, plus your acknowledgment of this notice — and (b) the **legitimate interests** I and my future readers have in journalism and literary work. The two together are the basis on which I process your data.

**Journalism / literary exemption.** Article 85 GDPR (and the UK Data Protection Act 2018, the Dutch UAVG, the Spanish LOPDGDD, and other member-state implementations) recognises that data protection has to be reconciled with freedom of expression — including journalism, academic work, and literary expression. The processing I'm doing falls within those purposes, and the exemption applies. Practically, that means some of your data-subject rights (especially erasure) are modulated by the exemption rather than absolute, *but* I will still respect them in good faith, and I will not invoke the exemption to refuse a reasonable request.

**How long I'll keep your data.** I retain the audio recording and any transcript through publication of the book and any adjacent essays, plus a reasonable archive period after publication. The archive period matters because my eventual trade publisher's contract is likely to impose specific retention requirements (sourcing and fact-check trails are commonly required), and because there may be reasonable cause to revisit material for second editions or downstream uses. If you'd like the audio file deleted sooner — for example, immediately after the attribution review concludes — say the word, and I'll delete it and confirm.

**Where your data lives.** The audio file and transcript sit on my own working devices in the US, not on a cloud service. When I move material into a managed service for transcription or backup, I use providers that operate under standard mechanisms for international data transfer (UK / EU adequacy or Standard Contractual Clauses where relevant). I do not share the audio or transcript with third parties beyond what's required for transcription, fact-checking, and the eventual publisher's permissions team.

**International transfer note.** Because I'm in the United States, your data is processed outside the EU / UK. Standard mechanisms apply (UK / EU adequacy where relevant, SCCs for any third-party processor). If you have specific concerns about transfer, I'd like to hear them — we can adjust the arrangement.

**Your rights.** Under GDPR / UK GDPR you have the right to:

- request access to the personal data I hold about you;
- ask me to correct anything inaccurate;
- ask me to delete data (subject to the journalism / literary exemption above, and subject to publication-record needs);
- object to processing;
- lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (the UK ICO, the Dutch AP, the Spanish AEPD, or your member-state equivalent).

I will respond to any request promptly and in good faith. If the journalism / literary exemption applies to part of a request, I will tell you which part and why.

**Attribution preference (preview).** Separate from the data-protection layer above, the attribution preference I'll ask about at the start of the call has four options:

1. *Freely quotable* — I quote you directly with attribution.
2. *Paraphrase + attribution; review direct quotes* — I send you direct quotes for sign-off before publication; paraphrases don't need pre-review.
3. *Full transcript review* — I send you the transcript; you can redact, clarify, or strike anything before any use.
4. *Off the record entirely* — the conversation informs the framework but is never cited.

You can pick any of these (or describe a different preference), and I'll abide by it across the book and essays. I'll plan to ask at the start of the call. \[OPTIONAL — IF ATTRIBUTION PREFERENCE INDICATED IN ADVANCE COMMS: "You'd already mentioned \[Category N — \<description\>\] in your earlier reply; I'll confirm at the start of the call but can plan around that as the working assumption."\]

**What I'd like from you before the call.** Just a short reply acknowledging this note — "received, thank you" is enough. I'll bring up the verbal recording-consent and attribution-preference questions at the start of the call itself.

Thank you again for the time. Looking forward to our conversation \[INTERVIEW DATE\].

Best,
Chris Winn

## About this notice

- *Not legal advice.* This notice is operational scaffolding for a working author, intended as good-faith reasonable practice under GDPR Article 13 / 14. It is not a substitute for legal advice on any specific interview or data-processing situation.
- *Reasonable-practice framing.* The notice draws on commonly-used journalist / academic / interviewer privacy-notice patterns, the UK ICO's published guidance for journalism, and the Article 85 implementations of the named member states. It is not a notice that has been reviewed by counsel.
- *If you (the subject) have a specific legal question about how your data is handled here, you may wish to consult a lawyer.* Likewise, if I (the author) face a specific challenge or request that this notice doesn't clearly address, I'll consult a publishing/entertainment lawyer rather than answering from a template.

## Authoring notes (not sent to the subject)

- **When to send:** ideally 3–7 days before the interview, alongside or shortly after the pre-read brief. Earlier than that risks getting lost in the subject's inbox; later than that doesn't leave time to read.
- **Tone:** plain-language and relational, not legalese. The subject is a busy practitioner / scholar; the notice should be readable in five minutes.
- **Acknowledgment record:** save the subject's "received, thank you" reply in `research/outreach/subjects/<subject>/correspondence_<DATE>.md` along with the date sent. This forms the written part of the consent record; the verbal Q0(a) yes captured on the audio forms the spoken part.
- **Member-state specificity:** the template invokes Article 85 GDPR generally and names UK DPA 2018, Dutch UAVG, and Spanish LOPDGDD because those are the implementations relevant to current-pipeline subjects (Mazzucato/Buller/Raworth UK; Dagan NL; Mondragon-press Spain). If a future subject is in a different member state, add or substitute the relevant implementation (e.g., German BDSG, French Loi Informatique et Libertés).
- **If the subject responds with concerns or rights-requests:** answer in good faith and file the exchange in the per-subject correspondence file. Don't invoke the journalism exemption defensively; the exemption modulates rights, doesn't abolish them, and operational good-faith engagement is the right baseline.
- **Cross-reference:** the pipeline subjects this template applies to are listed in the protocol's "Application across the current pipeline" table at [`interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md`](./interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md).
