## 1. Dagan's role at Amsterdam Donut Coalition + bio + CHANGENCY arc

**Current role.** Dagan Cohen is **Programme Director** of the Amsterdam Donut Coalitie (ADC) — a title he was using publicly by January 2025 (event listing for *Donut Night: Van droom naar doorbraak*, Pakhuis de Zwijger, 27 Jan 2025; [event listing](https://activiteiten.amsterdam.nl/activiteiten/2025/01/donut-night-droom-doorbraak/); [Duurzaam Ondernemen event page](https://www.duurzaam-ondernemen.nl/events/de-amsterdamse-donuteconomie-5-jaar-later-van-droom-naar-doorbraak/)). Earlier coverage variously called him "leader of ADC", "lead at the Amsterdam Donut Coalition", or simply "core team" ([Amsterdam Smart City profile](https://amsterdamsmartcity.com/members/34831); [TheNextSpeaker bio](https://thenextspeaker.com/en/experts/dagan-cohen/)). The internal scope is consistent across sources: **forging collaborations between neighbourhood initiatives, businesses, knowledge institutions and the municipality**, and — distinctively — **developing the shared narrative** that lets ordinary Amsterdammers picture Amsterdam as a "donut city" ([ADC profile of Dagan](https://amsterdamdonutcoalitie.nl/persoon/66873/dagan-cohen)).

**Tenure.** Dagan has been "part of the core team of the Amsterdam Donut Coalition since its founding in 2019" ([Pakhuis de Zwijger speaker bio for Donut Night Jan 2025](https://activiteiten.amsterdam.nl/activiteiten/2025/01/donut-night-droom-doorbraak/)). Founding meeting was December 2019 ([DEAL ADC page](https://doughnuteconomics.org/organisations-and-networks/amsterdam-donut-coalition)); Dagan was there from the start. The Director / Programme Director designation appears to have hardened later, after the original AUAS funding window closed at end of 2021 ([Designing the Doughnut: A Story of Five Cities, Kate Goodwin](https://matchboxstudio.medium.com/designing-the-doughnut-a-story-of-five-cities-8bad04ded5e3)) and the Coalition transitioned from a small AUAS-funded core (one FT + one PT, per Goodwin) to a much larger network (~1,400 members; 41 organisations; 100+ collaborative projects, per [UIIN July 2025 article](https://www.uiin.org/2025/07/10/rethinking-urban-sustainability-with-the-amsterdam-doughnut-coalition/)). Co-founder credit visible in public record: **Jennifer Drouin** (co-founder); **Rosa Tibosch** (coordinator). Dagan's ascent into the Programme Director role appears to track the network's scaling phase post-2021.

**Bio arc.** Three career chapters ([CHANGENCY about page](https://changency.amsterdam/en/about-us/); [TheNextSpeaker](https://thenextspeaker.com/en/experts/dagan-cohen/)):
1. **Visual-art / experiential design** (~12 years). Studied environmental art and design at the **Rietveld Academy**, Amsterdam. Worked as visual artist creating installations and theatre performances "experimenting with audience participation". In the early 1990s **co-founder and artistic director of Supperclub Amsterdam** — the boundary-pushing "restaurant for all senses" that became internationally notorious for staging dinner-as-performance ([Supperclub mention in his bio](https://www.facebook.com/Supper.nl/videos/supperclub-amsterdam-presents-colors-by-creative-agency-club-k/)).
2. **Advertising / creative direction** (20+ years). Creative Director at **Saatchi & Saatchi**, **Lowe**, and **Draftfcb**. Multiple national + international awards, especially in digital. Taught interactive art and design at the Rietveld Academy and branding at the **Willem de Kooning Academy** ([CHANGENCY about](https://changency.amsterdam/en/about-us/)).
3. **Design-for-societal-change** (current). Seven years at **What Design Can Do (WDCD)** (~2016–April 2023) running global design challenges and heading R&D — design research, acceleration programmes, impact measurement. Led the **Climate Action Challenge** and the **Make it Circular Challenge** (with the IKEA Foundation) ([WDCD speaker page](http://archive.whatdesigncando.com/amsterdam-2016/speaker/dagan-cohen/); [LinkedIn post on Make it Circular Challenge](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dagancohen_introducing-the-make-it-circular-challenge-activity-6985600343413817344-Qvzo)). Left WDCD April 2023; founded **CHANGENCY** in March 2024.

**CHANGENCY** ([website](https://changency.amsterdam/en/about-us/); tagline: *"Let's Make Shift Happen"*). Self-described as a **"creative agency for societal change"** that "connects front runners in the public and private sectors with committed designers and artists. Together, they imagine and design solutions for urgent social and ecological issues." Mission language explicitly opposes the advisory-report register: *"a shift away from advisory reports towards collectively designed visions of the future and solutions that inspire."* Methodologically anchored in **design fiction** (a discipline Dagan has practiced publicly going back to a 2016 WDCD talk; see also the 2026 *De Klerenpartij* speculative fictional political party project, [DesignDigger Feb 2026](https://www.designdigger.nl/2026/02/26/de-klerenpartij-design-fiction-als-politieke-daad/)) and **doughnut-economics-as-narrative-substrate**: the agency draws explicit inspiration from Doughnut Economics as one of its alternative-economic-models reference frame ([CHANGENCY about](https://changency.amsterdam/en/about-us/)).

**Relationship between CHANGENCY and the Coalition role.** CHANGENCY is Dagan's commercial vehicle for narrative-and-design-led societal-change work; the ADC role is the live laboratory in which the methodology gets exercised on the highest-profile civic case in Amsterdam. The two reinforce: Dagan is *literally being paid* (or at least underwritten on the ADC side) to do what the framework on his agency website promises — translate a complex framework into a story citizens can imagine.

**Narrative-translation specialty — what he says publicly.** Dagan's clearest public statement of the work appears in his ADC bio: *"With a background in art, communication and design, he is driven to make the donut narrative and all the beautiful practical examples accessible to all Amsterdammers"* ([ADC profile](https://amsterdamdonutcoalitie.nl/persoon/66873/dagan-cohen)). Recurring vocabulary: **"shared narrative"**, **"imagining Amsterdam as a donut city"**, **"making accessible"**, **"shift"** (the CHANGENCY tagline puns "make shift happen"). Public-record search 2026-05-08 surfaced *no* Dagan-authored long-form essay or op-ed laying out his methodology in detail; the public-record voice is event-and-speaker-bio-shaped rather than essayistic. **This is a gap the live interview can probe directly** — ask him to articulate the methodology in his own words, since the public record under-indexes it.

## 2. Amsterdam Donut Coalition's published work

**Founding.** December 2019 founding meeting; "officially" 2020 mission start; founded by Jennifer Drouin and others, with Dagan on the core team from the beginning ([DEAL](https://doughnuteconomics.org/organisations-and-networks/amsterdam-donut-coalition); [ADC bio](https://amsterdamdonutcoalitie.nl/persoon/66873/dagan-cohen)). Initial funding ran through end of 2021 from the **Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences (AUAS / HvA)**, where Kate Raworth was inaugurated as the first **Professor of Practice** (2020) ([Goodwin "Designing the Doughnut"](https://matchboxstudio.medium.com/designing-the-doughnut-a-story-of-five-cities-8bad04ded5e3); [Raworth tweet on the appointment](https://x.com/kateraworth/status/1167143112495554560)).

**Amsterdam's official adoption.** **8 April 2020** — the Municipality of Amsterdam adopted the Doughnut framework alongside its **Amsterdam Circular 2020-2025 Strategy**, becoming the first city in the world to do so ([Kate Raworth blog post 8 April 2020](https://www.kateraworth.com/2020/04/08/amsterdam-city-doughnut/); [Resilience.org republishing](https://www.resilience.org/stories/2020-04-09/introducing-the-amsterdam-city-doughnut/); [SmartCitiesWorld](https://www.smartcitiesworld.net/news/news/amsterdam-adopts-first-city-doughnut-model-for-circular-economy-5198)). Driving city official: **Marieke van Doorninck**, then deputy mayor for sustainability and urban planning, the political champion of the adoption ([RegenNarration podcast Ep 90 with van Doorninck](https://www.regennarration.com/episodes/090-becoming-the-worlds-first-doughnut-economy); [DEAL Amsterdam case study](https://doughnuteconomics.org/stories/local-government-case-study-amsterdam-nl)). She is a key public-record interlocutor adjacent to Dagan's work.

**The Amsterdam City Doughnut report (April 2020).** The first published downscaling of the global Doughnut to a city-scale "Portrait" — produced by **Kate Raworth + DEAL + Biomimicry 3.8 + Circle Economy + C40 Cities** (collaboration funded under the **KR Foundation Thriving Cities Initiative**) ([report PDF](https://doughnuteconomics.org/amsterdam-portrait.pdf); [DEAL ACD case study](https://doughnuteconomics.org/stories/amsterdam-city-doughnut)). The signature methodological contribution is the **four-lenses approach** (later canonicalized by DEAL as **Doughnut Unrolled**, April 2022; [DEAL Doughnut Unrolled tools](https://doughnuteconomics.org/tools/doughnut-unrolled-introducing-the-four-lenses)):

1. **Local-Social** — How are the people of Amsterdam faring? (housing affordability, healthcare access, community strength)
2. **Local-Ecological** — How is the health of Amsterdam's environment? (air quality, water, biodiversity within city boundaries)
3. **Global-Social** — How do Amsterdam's consumption patterns affect people worldwide? (labour conditions in textile factories, mining for metals in electronics)
4. **Global-Ecological** — How does Amsterdam contribute to overshooting planetary boundaries? (climate, ocean acidification, fertilizer use, land use)

**This is structurally important for the brief.** The Global-Social lens is precisely the place where **imported / upstream cost severance** is supposed to be visible in the existing Doughnut framework. Whether the four-lens vocabulary actually makes that visibility *binding* — i.e., translates into action-level instruments — is the question Commons Bonds is most directly poised to engage. (Note the asymmetry: the methodology *names* global-social impact as a lens, but the Coalition's published material on what to *do* about it appears thinner than the local-social and local-ecological work; this is one of the strongest hooks for the live interview.)

**Coalition's subsequent material** (post-2020):
- **Donut & Friends Festival** — annual since 2021 ([DEAL story page](https://doughnuteconomics.org/stories/donut-friends-festival-amsterdam-donut-coalition); city co-funded 2021–2022 [TimeOut](https://www.timeout.com/news/how-amsterdam-is-embracing-the-doughnut-model-and-why-other-cities-should-too-110121)). Nov 2024 edition celebrated "five years of doughnut economics in Amsterdam" with Kate Raworth, Hans Stegeman (Triodos), Thami Schweichler (United Repair Centre), Nathalie van Loon (AmsterDOEN), and Dagan giving the five-year retrospective.
- **Donut Night: Van droom naar doorbraak ("From Dream to Breakthrough")** — 27 Jan 2025, Pakhuis de Zwijger, ADC + Pakhuis de Zwijger ([Pakhuis de Zwijger programme](https://dezwijger.nl/programma/van-droom-naar-doorbraak)). Dagan was the framing voice for the five-year arc.
- **"Doughnut Economics in Amsterdam" report** (HvA / AUAS, executive report; [PDF](https://www.hva.nl/binaries/content/assets/subsites/cet/doughnut-economics-in-ams-executive-report-final.pdf)) — academic-stakeholder-shaped reflection, published via the Centre for Economic Transformation at AUAS (March 2023 download stamp on the PDF).
- **Donut Deal Dag, Amsterdam Donut Dag, Donut Pioniers** — recurring Pakhuis de Zwijger programme strand ([Donut Deal Dag](https://dezwijger.nl/programma/donut-deal-dag); [Donut Pioniers](https://dezwijger.nl/programma/donut-pioniers)).
- **Four programme areas** (per UIIN 2025 article): **Places & Cities**, **Research & Education**, **Art & Design**, **Government & Policy** — plus active business engagement.

**Coalition vocabulary.** In Dutch: *donutstad* (donut city); *binnen de donut* (within the donut); *donutpioniers*; *donut & friends*; *droom* and *doorbraak* (dream and breakthrough). In English: *making the donut narrative accessible*; *change-makers*; *living labs*; *city within planetary boundaries*. Notable absence: explicit cost-severance / accountability-instrument vocabulary; the Coalition's register is **aspirational + experiential + community-civic**, not regulatory or financial.

## 4. Cross-Doughnut-city comparative material

Per DEAL, **50+ local and regional governments** have engaged Doughnut tools as of 2025 ([Earth.org explainer](https://earth.org/what-is-doughnut-economics/); [DEAL Cities & Regions](https://doughnuteconomics.org/themes/cities-regions); [PD-Impulse English report](https://www.pd-g.de/assets/PD-Impulse/220811_PD-Impulse_Doughnut_Economics__English.pdf)).

- **Brussels** — adopted late September 2020, ~6 months after Amsterdam. **BrusselsDonut** consortium led by **Confluences** (specialist in co-creation projects); DEAL is an official member. Notably "deeply participatory dynamic" ([CNBC March 2021](https://www.cnbc.com/2021/03/25/amsterdam-brussels-bet-on-doughnut-economics-amid-covid-crisis.html); Goodwin "Five Cities").
- **Copenhagen** — within six weeks of Amsterdam's announcement, the city council's economic department was tasked with developing an implementation proposal (PD-Impulse report).
- **Nanaimo (Canada)** — voted to adopt December 2020 (PD-Impulse report).
- **Cornwall (UK)** — county adapted method; staff member given two months for adaptation (PD-Impulse).
- **Berlin** — Donut Berlin initiative since July 2020 (Goodwin "Five Cities").
- **Glasgow (Scotland)** — pilot site for C40's Thriving City Portrait downscaling methodology, 2022–2023; published in *The Lancet* applied case study ([Lancet 2023](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(23)02057-3/fulltext)).
- **Tokyo** — Tokyo Metropolitan Government Bureau of Environment launched **"Do!Nuts Tokyo"** online platform; first time a Japanese city officially showed the Doughnut diagram on its website ([DEAL Tokyo story](https://doughnuteconomics.org/stories/119)). Setagaya specifically not surfaced in the public-record sweep.
- **Sydney + Melbourne** — covered in Goodwin "Five Cities" (Australian engagement).
- **Wales** — country-scale localisation (Wikipedia / DEAL).

**Comparative academic / policy work.** Kate Goodwin's Medium series; Carnegie Endowment essay [*Can Cities Use the Doughnut Model to Hack Liberal Democracy?*](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2022/02/can-cities-use-the-doughnut-model-to-hack-liberal-democracy?lang=en&center=europe) (Feb 2022); a Springer 2025 [*Sustainability Science systematic review of Doughnut Economics 2012-2024*](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11625-025-01640-8); a Wiley *Earth's Future* [bibliometric analysis 2012-2024](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2024EF004638) (April 2025); UCL Bartlett event Oct 2024 on [*Strategic planning for degrowth: synergy, regions, and the doughnut of Amsterdam*](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/events/2024/oct/strategic-planning-degrowth-synergy-regions-and-doughnut-amsterdam).

## 5. Critical literature on Amsterdam Doughnut implementation

Substantial — and growing — body of critical work. Several distinct critique threads:

**a. The Willem van Winden line — citizen reach + power-relations.**
**Willem van Winden** (HvA / Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences research professor, lead of REGENALYZE) has written multiple LinkedIn pulse pieces critically engaging the Coalition. [*The Amsterdam Doughnut Coalition: some critical notes*](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/amsterdam-doughnut-coalition-some-critical-notes-willem-van-winden) is the most-cited; his [*Is Kate Raworth radical enough?*](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/kate-raworth-radical-enough-willem-van-winden) and [*Degrowth, green growth, or just another recession?*](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/degrowth-green-growth-just-another-recession-willem-van-winden) extend the analysis. The cross-published critical reflections via AUAS itself ([HvA News March 2023](https://www.amsterdamuas.com/cet/shared-content/news/news/2023/03/the-adc-some-critical-reflections.html)) suggest his critique has been institutionally received rather than dismissed. Headline van Winden findings: Amsterdam still substantially overshoots planetary boundaries; the doughnut is "hardly applied to address urban social and racial inequities"; participation is concentrated among "higher educated, quite vocal people"; "the doughnut community is still predominantly white". He hosted Stanford undergraduate **Sophia Manolis** (Urban Studies & Sociology), whose 2023 fieldwork interviewing ADC members, policymakers, citizens, and academics is the most specific empirical documentation of the implementation gap ([DEAL member page for Manolis](https://doughnuteconomics.org/members/6690); [Schumacher Center bio](https://centerforneweconomics.org/people/sophia-manolis/)).

**b. Jacobin / political-economy left.**
[*Doughnut Economics Has a Hole at Its Core*](https://jacobin.com/2021/09/doughnut-economics-raworth-amsterdam-capitalism-socialism), Jacobin Sept 2021. Argument: the framework's "apolitical" framing means it cannot confront the power relations that prevent the economy from serving most people's needs.

**c. The capitalism-or-degrowth crossroads critique.**
[*Amsterdam's circular economy at a world-ecological crossroads: postcapitalist degrowth or the next regime of capital accumulation?*](https://academic.oup.com/cjres/article/17/3/535/7710625) — *Cambridge Journal of Regions, Economy and Society*, vol. 17, no. 3, 535+, peer-reviewed. Argument: Amsterdam's Circular 2020-2025 Strategy discourse is progressive but implementation is "incipient and limited"; doesn't include explicit measures to reduce citizens' overconsumption equitably. The strategy could become a "next regime of capital accumulation" rather than postcapitalist degrowth.

**d. The strong-sustainable-consumption critique.**
[*The Amsterdam Doughnut: moving towards "strong sustainable consumption" policy?*](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/352786174_The_Amsterdam_Doughnut_moving_towards_strong_sustainable_consumption_policy) (ResearchGate; 2021).

**e. The degrowth-transformative-capacity assessment.**
[*Assessing degrowth transformative capacity of urban Doughnut Economics initiatives in Amsterdam*](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s42854-025-00087-5), *Urban Transformations*, 2025. Empirical study applying a DTC framework to ten Amsterdam DE initiatives; surveys + semi-structured interviews. Notes the Coalition's "40 supporting organizations, 722 members, and 84 projects" working with DE for transformational change — but with mixed transformative-capacity findings.

**f. Methodological / decolonial.**
[*Doughnut economics, degrowth and decoloniality. New directions for the Doughnut Portrait method*](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13549839.2026.2613377), *Local Environment*, 2026 (online first). Decolonial reframing of the Portrait method.

**g. Mainstream economics.**
**Branko Milanović** (CUNY Stone Center) — model ignores that bringing world to current rich-country median income would triple world GDP, an issue Raworth doesn't address. **Erik Schokkaert** (welfare economist) — Raworth mixes positive and normative considerations; lacks empirical grounding for prescriptions. (Both summarised on [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doughnut_(economic_model)).)

**h. Imported / upstream cost severance specifically.**
This is the framework's strongest analytical territory and the literature most relevant to the interview. Fragments of this critique are present in:
- The Cambridge Journal of Regions, Economy and Society piece (the city's circular strategy could entrench accumulation logics that depend on extraction elsewhere);
- The 2025 [*Update to Doughnut Economics*](https://medium.com/@pedagogyoftheeconomists/doughnut-economics-update-30b7d6572192) framework note on imperial-core countries extracting from imperial-periphery countries;
- [*The many holes in 'Doughnut Economics'*](https://asiatimes.com/2025/10/the-many-holes-in-doughnut-economics/) (Asia Times Oct 2025);
- [HCSS 2025 report on Critical Raw Materials in Noord-Holland and Flevoland](https://hcss.nl/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Critical-Raw-Materials-in-Noord-Holland-and-Flevoland-HCSS-2025.pdf) (concrete data on Amsterdam's critical-raw-materials supply-chain dependence).

But: **public-record search 2026-05-08 surfaced no single piece making the imported-cost-severance argument as cleanly as Commons Bonds frames it**. The Amsterdam Circular Strategy itself acknowledges that "no EU member state has sufficient domestic capabilities to become independent" on critical raw materials — i.e., the city's circularity necessarily relies on imports — but doesn't operationalize this acknowledgment into action-level pricing instruments. **This is the framework's clearest opening with Dagan.**

## 6. Citizen / narrative-translation published work

**The narrative-translation work — primary record.** Coalition's published material on citizen engagement is concentrated in **events, festivals, and Pakhuis de Zwijger programming** rather than in essayistic publications. Key venues: the annual **Donut & Friends Festival** (Nov 2024 was the five-year-anniversary edition); **Amsterdam Donut Dag**; **Donut Night**; **Donut Pioniers** programme; **Donut Deal Dag**. These are deliberate strategic vehicles for vocabulary adoption — what Dagan calls making the narrative "accessible to all Amsterdammers" ([ADC bio](https://amsterdamdonutcoalitie.nl/persoon/66873/dagan-cohen)).

**Citizen-level reception research.** The clearest empirical finding is from **Sophia Manolis's 2023 Stanford fieldwork** (hosted at AUAS by Willem van Winden): *the majority of Amsterdam residents did not know what Doughnut Economics was* ([cross-referenced via KCL Sustainability blog](https://blogs.kcl.ac.uk/sustainability/2021/11/doughnut-economics/) and [Sundial Press 2025](https://www.sundialpress.co/2025/07/09/is-the-doughnut-economics-model-an-effective-blueprint-for-achieving-sdg-11s-vision-of-sustainable-cities-and-communities-case-study-of-amsterdam/)). Manolis: the framework's theoretical language is "inaccessible for many grassroots stakeholders and residents". This is a *direct* live tension for Dagan's role — the narrative-translation specialty exists precisely to close this gap, and the public-record evidence is that the gap is only partially closed (1,400 ADC members against ~900,000 Amsterdam residents = ~0.15% direct membership penetration). Whether Dagan agrees with this framing or contests it is a high-value question.

**CHANGENCY / Dagan-cited methodology.** Public-record sweep 2026-05-08 did not surface a Dagan-authored academic or essayistic piece specifically on **vocabulary-adoption methodology** or **narrative-translation civic communications**. His 2017 WDCD video ([YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V6OkQ-if2qs)) and the 2026 *De Klerenpartij* / **ReFan** projects ([AUAS ReFan project page](https://www.amsterdamuas.com/projects/2025/11/refan-reshaping-the-fashion-narrative)) are his most concrete public-record demonstrations of the method. **ReFan (2025)** is particularly interesting as a methodological exemplar: explicit prompt is "How do we create a new cultural narrative about the value of clothing and textiles – a story that encourages sustainable behaviour?" — same structural problem as the donut-narrative work, applied to textiles. CHANGENCY co-led with AMFI / AUAS (lead: José Teunissen). Workshop on 19 June 2025 with 45 fashion-textile-chain participants; 98% wanted continued involvement. **This is methodologically the freshest thing on his CHANGENCY desk** and would be a productive interview hook.

**Design-fiction lineage.** Dagan's design-fiction practice (term coined by Julian Bleecker, Near Future Laboratory) is the methodological substrate. The 2026 *De Klerenpartij* project (a fictional political party, manifesto: *"From fashion-conscious to conscious fashion"*) is a contemporary instance ([DesignDigger Feb 2026](https://www.designdigger.nl/2026/02/26/de-klerenpartij-design-fiction-als-politieke-daad/)).

## 9. Where the framework intersects Dagan's work

**Foreclosure Bond (forward-looking) — strongest intersection.**
The water-deal hypothetical (`research/outreach/dagan-water-deal-hypothetical_2026-05-07.md`) is the worked example of where Commons Bonds' forward-looking action-level instrument fits inside an Amsterdam-Doughnut implementation. The framework supplies what the four-lens methodology *names but doesn't operationalize* — particularly on the **Global-Social** and **Global-Ecological** lenses, where Amsterdam can stay inside its local foundation/ceiling while still importing cost severance through critical-raw-materials supply chains, food imports, construction-input extraction, and battery-material upstream extraction. The Coalition's published material acknowledges the imported dependency (the Circular Strategy itself names CRM dependence) but doesn't translate it into a binding action-level instrument. **The Foreclosure Bond is structurally the missing tool.**

**Restitution Bond (backward-looking) — weaker intersection.**
The Coalition's work is forward-looking by design (transition, breakthrough, regeneration); the backward-looking accountability instrument has less obvious purchase in Dagan's day-to-day. Possible angles: (a) Amsterdam's colonial past and the wealth basis of contemporary circularity capacity (Dutch East India Company / VOC heritage); (b) historical extractive relationships with present-day supplier regions. Public-record sweep 2026-05-08 did not surface Coalition material directly engaging this angle. **Probably best to leave on the shelf for the live interview unless Dagan opens the door.**

**Complementarity-at-different-scales claim.**
The framework articulates this cleanly: Doughnut sets the goal-state; Commons Bonds supplies the action-level pricing instrument that operates within the goal-state. Dagan is uniquely positioned to test this hypothesis empirically because his job is *literally* the vocabulary-bridging work between framework articulation and operational adoption. The interview's most generative move is to ask him whether the complementarity claim feels right to him *as an implementation translator*, or whether it overstates the gap or misunderstands where the gap actually sits.

**McDowell-vs-Amsterdam contrast.**
Cleanly mappable: McDowell County is direct cost severance (coal extracted, costs stayed); Amsterdam's Doughnut adoption is the textbook case of cost-severance-prevention aspiration. The empirical gap — *imported* cost severance — is precisely where the contrast generates new analytical work. Dagan's narrative-translation specialty positions him to assess whether the Coalition's vocabulary can reach into that gap.

**Water-deal hypothetical positioning.**
The hypothetical is designed to be recognisable in Amsterdam policy vocabulary (water + food security are core Dutch civic vocabulary) but generic enough that any extraction-pattern deal would test the same framework moves. If Dagan's response surfaces a *real* recent Amsterdam deal-evaluation moment, pivot to that case. The hypothetical is the fallback.

**Imported / upstream cost severance — the productive friction.**
This is where the framework expects to find traction. Two outcomes are equally valuable:
1. Dagan says *"yes, this is a gap; we know it; we don't have an instrument for it"* — strong confirmation.
2. Dagan says *"we already address this through X, Y, Z vocabulary"* — equally valuable; we re-position the framework against existing language and avoid redundant work.

Either outcome is a good outcome. The framework's intellectual honesty depends on the question being asked rather than the answer being predicted.

## 10. Things to NOT walk in unaware of

- **Dagan has been ADC core since 2019; "Programme Director" hardened as a public title around late 2024 / early 2025.** Asking "when did you join?" or "are you the founder?" will signal a public-record screen wasn't done. Acknowledge the **2019 founding presence + 2025 Programme Director framing**.
- **The "five years on" frame (*droom naar doorbraak*) is currently his live rhetorical pivot.** If the interview can engage that frame substantively — what does *doorbraak* (breakthrough) look like in practice for the next chapter — it will land where his attention currently sits.
- **CHANGENCY launched March 2024** — recent. He's still building the agency. Asking about CHANGENCY's client roster too sharply may probe a not-yet-stable entity.
- **The Coalition's narrative voice is aspirational + inclusive + experiential, not polemical.** Don't expect Dagan to throw rocks at the framework's competitors or at critics. He is more likely to absorb critique constructively than to counter-argue. This is consistent with his role: a narrative-translator must keep the tent open.
- **Vocabulary policing.** Dutch language convention is **donut** (rather than English "doughnut") in Amsterdam contexts — *Amsterdamse Donut Coalitie*, *donutstad*, *donutpioniers*. The English-DEAL register uses **doughnut**. Both are correct; the Dutch usage is local-civic, the English is academic-international. Dagan's bio in English consistently uses *donut* (matching the Coalition's English-language self-styling, *Amsterdam Donut Coalition*). **Use *donut* when referring to the Coalition; *doughnut* is fine for the framework / DEAL.**
- **The "donut" vocabulary's elite-residue critique** (Manolis / van Winden) is a real live tension in Dagan's work, not a strawman. He has likely thought about it more than anyone. Do not present it to him as a fresh observation; engage as if he's aware (he is).
- **The Coalition is not the City.** Dagan represents the coalition-network layer; he is *not* a municipal policy actor. The City has its own circular-economy team (René Koop). Conflating Coalition vocabulary with City policy is a signature outsider mistake. **The Coalition does narrative + network-building + civic mobilization; the City does policy + budget + regulation; they overlap, but are separable.** Be precise.
- **Funding history is delicate.** The original AUAS funding ran through end of 2021. Subsequent funding sources are not as cleanly visible in the public record — possibly municipal, foundation, project-by-project. Don't ask aggressively about funding mechanics; if Dagan opens the door, follow.
- **The framework's strongest territory is imported / upstream cost severance.** The Coalition's published vocabulary names this (Global-Social, Global-Ecological lenses) but doesn't operationalize it. **This is where the framework lands**, not where it competes.
- **Public-record search 2026-05-08 surfaced no Dagan-authored long-form essay or op-ed on his methodology** — the framework's vocabulary is stronger than his on this point right now. Be careful not to lean too hard on framework-jargon; let him name his own vocabulary first, then map.

## Key takeaways for the live interview

- **Open by acknowledging his arc**: 2019 ADC core + 2024 CHANGENCY launch + 2025 Programme Director framing. Don't ask when he joined; he was there from the start.
- **Engage the *droom naar doorbraak* (dream-to-breakthrough) frame directly.** It's where his attention sits and it's a productive substrate for the McDowell-vs-Amsterdam contrast (Amsterdam is moving from dreaming-up cost-severance-prevention to breaking-through into binding it).
- **Position the framework as filling an action-level gap that the Doughnut's Global-Social / Global-Ecological lenses *name* but don't *operationalize*.** This is the cleanest intellectual honesty move and the one most likely to land with someone whose job is vocabulary-translation work.
- **Lead with the water-deal hypothetical**, but be ready to pivot to a real Amsterdam case if Dagan surfaces one. Real beats hypothetical every time.
- **Don't ask him to validate Commons Bonds.** Ask him whether the action-level gap is real in his implementation experience. Either answer is a good answer.
- **He is not a polemicist.** He will likely engage critique generously rather than counter-argue. Plan for a generative, exploratory register — closer to a Mondragon / Vienna implementer interview than to a Darity / Sandel philosophical contest.
- **Read ReFan as the methodological tell.** It's the cleanest available demonstration of how he runs vocabulary-translation in a domain (fashion/textiles) parallel to the donut work. Citing ReFan by name signals you've done the work.
