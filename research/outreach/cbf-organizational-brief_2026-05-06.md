## 1. Organizational structure

CBF is headquartered in Annapolis, Maryland (Philip Merrill Environmental Center) with field offices in **Salisbury, MD; Harrisburg, PA; Richmond, VA; Norfolk, VA (Hampton Roads/Brock Environmental Center); and Washington, D.C.**, plus residential education centers including **Port Isobel** (Tangier archipelago, VA) and **Smith Island/Tylerton** (MD).

**President & CEO:** Hilary Harp Falk (since January 2022; the third leader in CBF's history since 1967 chartering).

## 3. Watermen / working-waterfront programs

- **Port Isobel Environmental Education Center** (Tangier archipelago, 250 acres): hires Tangier captains as **Captain/Educators**. The late **Charles Parks** was a longtime Port Isobel Captain, a Tangier waterman who taught CBF students; the current captain/program manager team is the most natural CBF route to active and retired Tangier watermen. **This is the highest-yield single CBF program for the chapter's load-bearing watermen-voice need.**
- **Smith Island / Tylerton Center** (MD): parallel waterman-culture program on the MD side of the Tangier-Smith cultural axis.
- **Virginia Oyster Gardening Program**: 627 volunteers in 2024 raising/returning 110,000+ oysters, run by Jackie Shannon's team out of Brock Environmental Center.
- **Save Oyster Shells**: Restaurant-and-volunteer shell recycling, ~4,000 bushels/yr in VA.
- **Maryland Shellfish Growers Network**: CBF + UMD Extension joint program for new and veteran shellfish growers (mentor pairings).
- **Tangier Island Shoreline Adaptation Plan**: NFWF-funded ($356,500) effort CBF is administering with Bayland engineering; **Mayor James "Ooker" Eskridge** is the named municipal partner. Eskridge is a working VA waterman, Tangier's elected mayor, and the highest-leverage single individual VA waterman voice.

## 5. Recent advocacy positions (2024–2026)

- **Menhaden (VA-centric)**: CBF in Sept 2025 formally called for a pause on industrial reduction fishing inside the Bay until science is complete. In the 2025 and 2026 VA General Assembly sessions, CBF backed Sen. Marsden's SB474 menhaden science fund and Del. Carr's HB1048 (pause) and HB1049 (observer/seasonal limits) — all blocked. Moore quoted: "We don't have base science to make good decisions."
- **Striped bass**: CBF urged ASMFC to adopt a 14% coastwide reduction for the 2025 season; CBF disappointed by Oct 2025 ASMFC deferral.
- **Oysters**: Supported Maryland's federal disaster declaration request (Feb 2026). Baltimore Oyster Partnership expanded to a 5M oyster goal in 2025.
- **Climate/EPA**: Joint litigation challenging EPA climate rollback (2025–26).
- **MD legislature**: CBF backed Gov. Moore's Chesapeake Bay Legacy Act (signed 2025).
