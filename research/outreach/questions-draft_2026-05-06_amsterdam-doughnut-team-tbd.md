## Context + epistemic discipline

These questions are designed for an Amsterdam Doughnut Coalition team member implementing **Raworth's Doughnut framework** in city policy. The interviewee will not have read or be familiar with **Commons Bonds**. The questions are about their work, not about the book's framework.

**The discipline:**
- Interviewees comment on their work (Doughnut implementation in Amsterdam)
- Author makes the linking inference to Commons Bonds in the prose, clearly marked as the author's move
- The interview's value is texture, primary-source register, and specific human voices — NOT framework validation
- Probe failure modes; do not seek endorsement

**Pre-interview pitch / pre-brief — minimal:**
- Brief context that Chris is writing a forthcoming book on a related-but-distinct framework (cost severance / commons accountability)
- A two-sentence framing of the chapter the conversation feeds into (city-level structural-reform existence proofs / Pattern 2 register)
- Permission to record (with their explicit consent)
- **Do not** ask them to read or evaluate Commons Bonds before the conversation. They have no basis to comment on it; that's the author's territory, not theirs.

**Question count target:** 8 substantive + Q0 attribution. Open-ended; expect organic follow-ups during the conversation. Have all 8 in the chamber but be willing to follow the conversation where it goes — texture lives in the unscripted moments.

## Question set

**Q0. Attribution preference (ground rules).**
> *"Before we dig into the work, I want to ask about your preferences for how I handle our conversation in print. The book and a derivative essay (currently in development for Noema or comparable publication) would draw on this conversation. People handle attribution differently — some are freely quotable, some prefer paraphrase + attribution unless quotes are reviewed first, others ask for full transcript review before anything goes to print. Whatever works for you, I'll abide by it across the book and essays. What would you prefer?"*

**Q1. Role and tenure.**
> *"Before we get into the framework, can you walk me through your role on the Amsterdam Doughnut work? When did you join, what's your current focus, and what's been the arc of your involvement?"*

**Q2. Published framework vs. lived implementation — the gap.**
> *"What did the published Doughnut framework give you that you used directly, and what did you have to invent yourself to make city-policy work happen? I'm interested in the things you've learned that aren't in the published materials — the gap between what the framework provides and what implementation actually required."*

**Q3. A specific moment.**
> *"Walk me through a specific moment — a decision, a meeting, a budget cycle, a political pressure — where the framework helped you do something you couldn't have done otherwise. And, on the other side, a moment where the framework wasn't enough and you had to navigate without it."*

**Q4. The longest-running unresolved tension.**
> *"Looking back across the work since 2020, what's the longest-running tension you haven't fully resolved? Not necessarily a failure — sometimes it's just the thing you keep coming back to that doesn't have a clean answer."*

**Q5. Where it hit walls.**
> *"Where has the implementation hit structural walls — political, budgetary, institutional, technical, coalitional? What kinds of walls are inherent to this kind of work, and what kinds were Amsterdam-specific?"*

**Q6. What you'd tell another city.**
> *"If a mayor or a city council from another city called you tomorrow asking for advice on starting this kind of work, what's the one or two things you'd tell them that aren't in the published materials? What do you wish someone had told you in 2019 that no one did?"*

**Q7. Where the framework's edges are.**
> *"What kinds of problems does the Doughnut framework clearly help with, and what kinds doesn't it handle well? Are there policy questions you've encountered that sit outside the framework's reach — places where you've had to use a different lens or invent something else?"*

**Q8. Referrals + permission for follow-up.**
> *"Two things to close — first, are there other people on the Amsterdam team or in the broader DEAL network you'd recommend I also reach out to, especially anyone whose perspective would push back on the picture you've painted today? And second — would you be willing to take a clarifying question or two by email if I run into something during the writing where I want to check my understanding?"*
