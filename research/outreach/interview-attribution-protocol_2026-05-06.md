# Interview attribution-handling protocol

**Created:** 2026-05-06 by Claude per author direction.
**Purpose:** Reusable discipline for handling attribution preferences across all interview-based outreach for *Commons Bonds*. Ensures every interview leads with an explicit ground-rules conversation about how the subject's words will appear in print, before any substantive questions get asked.

---

## When to handle attribution

**Pre-interview (in advance comms):** Mention in the pre-read brief or scheduling email that attribution preferences will be discussed at the start of the conversation. Phrasing example (from Darity pre-read brief, 2026-05-05):

> *On attribution: I'd like to quote you in the book and in any derivative essays. Scholars / institutional voices / [adapt per recipient] handle attribution differently — some prefer freely-quotable interview answers, others prefer that interview material be paraphrased + attributed unless reviewed in advance, others ask for transcript review before any quoting. Whatever works for you, please let me know at the start of our conversation; I'll abide by it across the book and essays.*

Function: primes the subject so they've thought about it before the call. Lets them reply in writing if they have a clear preference. Saves interview minutes.

**Live interview (Q0 — first thing asked, before any substantive question):**

> *Before we get into the framework questions, I want to ask about your preferences for how I handle our conversation in print. The book and any derivative essays — there's a Noema-track essay in development, possibly an Aeon-track piece on a separate angle — would draw on this conversation. People handle attribution differently — some prefer freely-quotable answers, some prefer paraphrase + attribution unless reviewed first, others ask for full transcript review before any quoting. Whatever works for you, I'll abide by it across the book and essays. What would you prefer?*

Function: ensures it gets done; signals seriousness/preparation; creates clear ground rules for the rest of the conversation.

## The four preference categories

| # | Preference | What it requires from us | Setup implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Freely quotable** | Note in interview log; proceed normally. | Easiest. Recording + transcription optional but recommended for accuracy. |
| 2 | **Paraphrase + attribution; review before direct quotes** | Send any direct quotes to subject before publication for approval. Paraphrased material doesn't need pre-review. | Recording recommended for accurate paraphrase. Pre-publication review pass adds 1-2 weeks to manuscript timeline. |
| 3 | **Full transcript review before any use** | Send transcript to subject for review + approval; subject can redact / clarify / strike anything before we use it. | Recording + transcription required. Transcript-review pass adds 2-4 weeks to manuscript timeline. Subject's edits are binding. |
| 4 | **Off-record entirely** | Conversation informs the framework but is never directly cited. Subject's name doesn't appear. | No setup required, but lower book-leverage for the interview. |

## What to do if the subject hasn't decided

Offer the four categories as a menu. Most subjects will pick one quickly. If they're uncertain, default to **Category 2 (paraphrase + attribution; review before direct quotes)** — it's the most common preference for academic/institutional figures and gives the subject a review touchpoint without burdening the manuscript timeline as much as Category 3.

## What to do if preferences shift mid-interview

If the subject says something they want to retract or wants to put off-record, honor it immediately. Re-confirm the modified ground rules verbally. Note the redaction in the interview log.

## What to do for follow-up correspondence

If the subject sends a follow-up email, the contents of that email follow the ground rules established at Q0 unless they specify otherwise. Treat any explicit "off the record" annotation as binding.

## Documentation discipline

For each interview, record in the interview log:

- Subject's chosen preference category (1, 2, 3, or 4)
- Any modifications agreed during the conversation
- Any post-interview adjustments

This protects both the subject and the manuscript: if any quote is challenged later, we have the consent record on file.
