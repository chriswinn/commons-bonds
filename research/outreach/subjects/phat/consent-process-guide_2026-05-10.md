## What this guide does

You asked: *what do I show Phat? The whole book? Specific chapters? Just the sentences mentioning him?*

Short answer: **specific sentences with enough surrounding context that he understands the framing — not the whole book.** Concrete details, a script for the conversation, and the post-conversation steps are below.

## 2. Cover letter / conversation script

This is the conversation, not the consent form. The author can adapt for in-person, phone, or written delivery. Read it once, then put it down — Phat will hear the difference between a script and a real conversation.

### If the conversation is in person (recommended)

Go to where Phat is — his shop, the dock, his home, wherever he prefers. Bring the packet. Bring two copies of the consent document. Bring a pen.

Open with the relationship. *I'm not here on business. I'm here because I'm writing about Fox Hill and you're part of what I'm writing about, and I wouldn't want to do that without asking you first.*

Explain the project at one paragraph. Use the framing from §1 item 3 above. Do not lecture. Two or three sentences should be enough; if Phat asks, expand. If he doesn't, move on.

Explain the ask. *There's a piece coming out in a magazine in a few weeks, and I'd like to use your actual name in it instead of writing around it. I wrote what I wanted to write — here it is — and I'd like you to read it and tell me whether you're comfortable with it. If you are, I have a sheet of paper for you to sign that gives me permission. If you aren't, that's fine. I won't use your name. The piece will go out the way it reads now, with you described but not named.*

Hand him the packet. Watch him read. Don't talk while he reads. If he asks questions while reading, answer them.

After he reads, ask: *what do you think?* Then listen.

If he wants changes — write them down. *Tell me exactly what to change.* Then offer to bring back a revised version for him to read before signing. The signed version should be the version he actually saw.

If he wants to think about it — fine. *Take whatever time you need. I'm not in a hurry. I'd like to know by [DATE — pick something realistic, e.g., 10 days before the Noema submission window].* Leave the packet with him.

If he says yes — go through the consent document with him page by page. Make sure he sees the rights paragraph (numbered list of what he can do, including withdrawing future-use consent). Sign both copies. Hand him his copy. Take a photo of the signed document on your phone.

If he says no — thank him. *I appreciate you reading it.* Take the packet back if he wants to give it back; let him keep it if he wants. Either way, the *Noema* essay goes out anonymized.

### If the conversation has to be by phone

Mail the packet first. Call after you know it has arrived. Allow him a couple of days to read it before the call. Same script as above, adapted for the phone. If he says yes, the consent document can be signed and mailed back.

### If the conversation has to be by email

Least preferred. Send the packet as PDF attachments. Send a short cover note (the conversation script, in writing, kept brief). Wait for a reply. If he agrees, ask whether he'd prefer to print, sign, scan, and email back, or whether he'd prefer to receive a paper copy by mail and return it that way.

In-person is best. Phone is acceptable. Email is the fallback.

## 3. Logistics

Practical things to have figured out before the conversation:

- **Two copies of the signed consent document.** One for Phat. One for the author's file. Both signed.
- **A pen.** Yours, brought by you. You will be surprised how often this is the friction.
- **The author's contact information** filled in on the consent document before printing (address, phone, email). Without this, the document is not actionable on Phat's end.
- **The full legal name field** intentionally left blank for Phat to fill in himself. Do not guess. Do not fill it in from memory. Phat fills it in.
- **The "materials shown to you on [DATE]" field** filled in by the author at the time of the conversation, not in advance. Bring a pen for this too.
- **A way to take a photograph of the signed document** before you leave (phone camera is fine). If the original signed document gets lost in the mail or in a desk drawer, the photograph is the backup record.

## 4. Verify Phat's status before initiating

Before printing the packet and going to see Phat, the author should confirm Phat is alive and reachable. The named-subject-consent discipline distinguishes living = named-pending-consent from deceased = courtesy-notify-family-if-reachable. The two paths are different — different document, different conversation, different addressee.

If Phat has passed since the source material was captured (the author's most recent confirmation is the 2026-05-09 oral-history dump that describes him as alive and active), the consent process described here is not the right path; the family-courtesy-notify path is. That is a separate workstream — out of scope for this consent package.

If Phat is alive but has had a major change of circumstances (illness, loss of capacity, family changes), the author should consider whether the consent conversation is still appropriate or whether it should be deferred. Use judgment.

1. **File the signed consent.** Take a photo or scan the signed document. Save the file at `research/outreach/subjects/phat/signed-consent_<YYYY-MM-DD>.md` (a markdown file with the photo embedded, or a PDF or image file at the same path). Filing the consent is the trigger for everything else.
2. **Capture his print-form preference** for his name (full given name / short form *Phat* / with or without Vietnamese diacritic / full legal name as on ID). Note it on the signed-consent file. This is the form that gets applied to all edits in step 3 and step 4.
3. **Apply the three edits to the *Noema* essay** per `phat-mentions-inventory_2026-05-10.md` "If consent signed — exact edit list" Edits 1-3. (Body line 137 + frontmatter line 9 + frontmatter line 4.)
4. **Apply the two edits to Chapter 3** per the same inventory file, Edits 4-5. (HTML flag at line 131 + paragraph swaps at lines 132 and 140. Paragraphs 2-4 of the Phat portrait do not require change.)
5. **Update PM dashboard §9** (`tools/workstream-handoffs/pm-session-handoff_2026-05-10.md`) — mark USER ACTION 1 + 2 complete with the signing date and a one-line evidence note pointing at the signed-consent file.
6. **Update the named-subject-consent memory** (`/Users/c17n/.claude/projects/-Users-c17n-commons-bonds/memory/feedback_named_subject_consent.md`) — add a status note in the Why or How-to-apply section recording that Phat's consent landed on [DATE], so future sessions don't re-anonymize him.
7. **Submit the *Noema* essay** to `edit@noemamag.com` per the submission window (late May / early June 2026).

### If Phat declines

1. **Note the declination.** A short markdown note at `research/outreach/subjects/phat/declination-note_<YYYY-MM-DD>.md`. One paragraph: when, where, what was said, what the author committed to going forward (most likely: continue anonymizing in all publisher-facing prose; respect the decision in any future work).
2. **No edits required to the *Noema* essay.** It is already submission-ready in its anonymized form.
3. **Anonymize the Chapter 3 portrait** per `phat-mentions-inventory_2026-05-10.md` "If consent declined / unreachable — Ch 3 needs anonymization." The portrait at lines 132-140 is currently named-pending-consent; it needs to come off the name and the identifying business details to match the *Noema* essay's anonymization pattern. Recipe is in the inventory.
4. **Update PM dashboard §9** — mark USER ACTION 1 + 2 resolved-as-anonymized with the date.
5. **Submit the *Noema* essay.** Same window.

### If Phat asks for changes before signing

1. **Capture his asks in writing the same day** at `research/outreach/subjects/phat/requested-changes_<YYYY-MM-DD>.md`. Verbatim if possible. (Do not paraphrase; the author's later memory of the asks may drift.)
2. **Revise the essay accordingly.** This may be a small surgical edit (a word or detail) or a larger rewrite of line 137. The companion mentions-inventory file is the place to log what changed and where.
3. **Bring the revised version back to Phat for a second read.** Do not skip this step. The signed version should be the version Phat actually saw.
4. **If Phat is satisfied with the revised version, proceed with signing** per "If Phat signs" above.

### If Phat wants to think about it and the author has not heard back by the deadline

1. **Submit the *Noema* essay anonymized.** The submission window is the binding constraint; do not delay submission past it. If consent lands later, the named version can be restored in a future printing.
2. **Leave the Chapter 3 portrait in its current named-pending-consent state.** The book is not on the same submission window as the essay; the chapter can wait for Phat's decision. The consent-pending HTML flag at line 131 and the disclaimer prose in the portrait are the chapter's interim posture — clean to leave in place until Phat decides one way or the other.
3. **Keep the line of communication open.** Phat may decide later. Do not treat the deadline-pass as a no.

## 6. Honorarium considerations

Some publishers and authors include a small honorarium for named sources, particularly for working-class subjects who are giving real time and trust. This is a question worth raising with the eventual trade publisher when the book contracts. It is not the author's call alone, and it does not need to be resolved before this consent conversation — but flagging it here so it does not get forgotten when the publisher comes on board.

A common pattern in trade publishing: a modest one-time payment (a few hundred dollars; sometimes a copy of the book and a meaningful inscribed dedication serves the same relational purpose). If the publisher has a standard policy, follow it. If they don't, raise the question and make a deliberate choice.

This is out of scope for the consent conversation itself. Do not introduce money into the consent ask. The consent is consent; the relationship is the relationship; if a small honorarium becomes appropriate later, it comes from the publisher.
