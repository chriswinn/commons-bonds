## What this document is

I am writing a book called *Commons Bonds* and a set of essays that go with it. I want to use your actual name in the published version, not a stand-in. That is what this document is for. Your signature here gives me permission to do that.

The terms below are written so we both know what we are agreeing to. Read them carefully. Ask me anything you want to ask. Take as much time as you need.

## Your rights

These are yours, written down so neither of us has to remember them later:

1. **You have the right to read the relevant text before you sign.** I will hand you a small packet — the actual paragraph from the *Noema* essay, with enough surrounding context that you understand the framing. Read it. Sit with it.
2. **You can ask for changes.** If something is wrong, if I have misremembered a detail, if you would prefer different wording — say so. I will consider any change you ask for in good faith. If we reach a version you are comfortable with, that becomes the version we both sign on.
3. **You can ask for parts to be left out.** If there is a sentence or a detail you would rather not see in print, tell me. I will work it out with you.
4. **You keep a signed copy.** You get one. I get one. Same document, both signed.
5. **Your signature here is consent for the materials as I showed them to you today.** If I substantially expand or revise the material later — for example, if a future book chapter goes deeper into your story than what is in the *Noema* essay — I will come back to you with the new material and ask again before that material is published.
6. **You can withdraw consent for future uses by giving me written notice.** If at some point you decide you no longer want me to use your name in any *new* work — a new essay, a new edition, a paperback reissue with new material — write me a note saying so, and I will stop using your name in anything new. (I cannot un-publish what is already in print, and I will not pretend I can. Once a book or magazine has been printed and distributed, what is in it is in it.)
7. **You can ask me questions at any time.** Now, before signing, after signing, a year from now. My contact information is at the bottom of this document.

## A plain note on legal review

This document is not a legal contract drafted by a lawyer. It is a written record of an agreement between two people who know each other and who want their understanding of that agreement to be clear and on paper. Either of us is free to consult a lawyer before signing, and either of us probably should if we feel uncertain about anything in here.

If a publishing house contracts the book, that publisher may require a more formal release form from you down the road — that is standard practice in publishing. This consent is intended to serve as the author's good-faith documentation of your permission, and to be transferable to the publisher when the time comes. If the publisher requests a more formal release, the author will bring that release back to you for review and signature at that point; this document does not substitute for that future paperwork.

The author has chosen plain language over legal language because the relationship is what matters and the paperwork should reflect that. If anything in here is unclear, ask.
