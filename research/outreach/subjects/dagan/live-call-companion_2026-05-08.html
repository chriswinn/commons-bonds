Q2 — Four-lens Global-Social asymmetry: naming vs. acting on imported cost severance

"The Amsterdam City Doughnut report's signature methodological contribution is the four-lens framework — Local-Social, Local-Ecological, Global-Social, Global-Ecological. The Global-Social lens explicitly names the impact of Amsterdam's consumption patterns on people elsewhere: labour conditions in textile factories, mining for metals in electronics, the imported costs that aren't visible in Amsterdam's local foundation/ceiling. But reading the published Coalition material, the Global-Social lens's what-to-do vocabulary feels thinner than the work on the local lenses. Which is exactly the framework I'm developing's strongest opening: McDowell County, West Virginia is the textbook failure of direct cost severance — coal extracted, costs stayed locally. Amsterdam's work is the opposite at the local level. But the cost severance Amsterdam imports — rare earths from Niger Delta, battery materials from extraction zones, cobalt for the energy transition — is the same structural mechanism, displaced in space. Where in the Coalition's actual work is that imported / upstream cost severance most visible to you? And what's the gap between naming it through the Global-Social lens and acting on it at the city scale?"

Push if shallow: "The Amsterdam Circular Strategy itself acknowledges that no EU member state has sufficient domestic capabilities to become independent on critical raw materials. The acknowledgment is on the page; the action-level instrument to bind that imported dependency isn't. Is that gap the way you'd describe it from inside?"

Raworth / Doughnut Economics (most likely lineage pivot — pre-load)

"Doughnut Economics operates at the economy-system level — articulating where economies should aim (the safe and just space between social foundation and ecological ceiling) and design principles for getting there. The framework I'm developing operates at the actor-transaction level — naming the structural mechanism (cost severance) by which accountability fails, and pricing what's been foreclosed (RCV). "How does this add to Hotelling?"

"Hotelling 1931 modeled scarcity rents for exhaustible resources assuming efficient pricing — the math is right. The framework's claim is that the inputs have been wrong: the scarcity rent in Hotelling's sense excludes the cost of permanent option-value foreclosure on future generations, which is what RCV names. The framework's contribution is an accounting instrument that puts intergenerational option-value-loss inside the scarcity rent rather than outside it — making Hotelling's math operate on the right inputs."

"How does this extend Ostrom?"

"Ostrom 1990 showed that commons can be governed without state or market through institutional design — but her design principles work specifically for commons that can be managed in sustained-yield perpetuity. The framework extends to commons that can't be managed that way: non-renewable resources from the start, and renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity. The two-instrument bond architecture is what operates where sustained-yield discipline can no longer apply — accountability-by-instrument for cases where accountability-by-management isn't possible."

"Where does Polanyi sit?"

"Polanyi's fictitious-commodities frame in The Great Transformation is the deep substrate the framework builds on. The framework's observation — that certain costs should not be transactable externalities but are treated as such — is a descendant of Polanyi's 'land, labor, money are not produced for market exchange' move. Cost severance is the contemporary mechanism that produces the crises Polanyi identified."

"How does this relate to Mazzucato?"

"Mazzucato's distinction between value-creation and value-extraction in The Value of Everything is the clearest articulation of what economics has been refusing to name precisely. Cost severance is positioned as a precision-add on her value-extraction vocabulary — value-extraction is what the actor does; cost severance is what the actor does to the costs of doing it."

"How does this relate to Boyce?"

"Boyce at PERI works the intersection of ecological economics and reparations the framework also engages, particularly through Economics for People and the Planet and the carbon-dividend literature. Cost Severance Damages is presented as an analytic instrument; Boyce's work shapes how the framework imagines those instruments in distributional terms."

"How does this relate to Harvey / Klein / primitive accumulation?"

"Harvey's 'accumulation by dispossession' names the contemporary phenomenon — Marx's primitive accumulation operating now, in real time. Klein's shock doctrine names the political conditions under which it accelerates. The framework's contribution is naming the mechanism that produces the phenomenon Harvey describes: cost severance is the structural move by which value is extracted while costs are deliberately transferred. Harvey names what; the framework names how."

"How does this relate to reparations economics?"

"Reparations economics — Darity & Mullen, Hamilton, Boyce — supplies the methodological lineage for the Restitution Bond. Backward-looking accountability for harm already realized: that's what reparations economics has been doing rigorously for decades. The framework extends the methodology to extraction-community contexts beyond the Black-American case, while keeping the original case central to the lineage."

Mask-tradition pivot (low probability with Dagan; capture for completeness)

"The Aeon pitch's working title is 'The Mask of Abundance.' The construction puts the framework structurally inside the Dunbar / Du Bois / Ellison / Fanon lineage of unmasking systemic harm — and that's deliberate. The framework's actual subjects of severance — the McDowell coal miner whose lungs sound like his father's, the communities downstream of every extraction site — often ARE the communities that tradition speaks of. Brief acknowledgment in the Aeon essay; substantive engagement in Ch 8 (McDowell) and Ch 1 (framework's intellectual lineage). I'd welcome guidance on whether the engagement plan is sufficient."

"Why bonds and not funds / accounts / trusts / reserves?"

"The framework chose 'Bond' deliberately for three structural reasons. First, real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, surety bonds are existing legal instruments with a century-plus regulatory track record. Second, legal-adoption-travel: 'Bond' already lives in regulatory contexts — lawyers and policymakers recognize the structural properties immediately. Third, role-encoding precision: 'Bond' specifies an amount posted against an obligation, returned if conditions are met, forfeited if they aren't — that matches Cost Severance = RCV − B cleanly."

Glossary

Alphabetical. Click any term to expand its definition.

Amsterdamse Donut Coalitie (ADC)
Amsterdam Donut Coalition. Founded December 2019; civic-network layer (citizens, businesses, knowledge institutions, neighborhoods) catalyzing implementation of the Doughnut framework adopted by the City of Amsterdam April 2020. ~1,400 members; 41 organisations; 100+ collaborative projects (UIIN July 2025). Dagan is Programme Director (title hardened ~late 2024 / early 2025); ADC core team since 2019 founding.
Binnen de donut
Dutch: "within the donut." Coalition vocabulary for the safe-and-just space inside the Doughnut's two rings.
Change-makers
Coalition vocabulary for active-citizen network participants. Aspirational/inclusive register.
CHANGENCY
Dagan's creative agency for societal change, founded March 2024. Tagline: "Let's Make Shift Happen." Methodologically anchored in design fiction. Mission language explicitly opposes the advisory-report register in favor of collectively designed visions.
Coalition vs. City
Critical distinction. Coalition (ADC) = network layer (civic mobilization, narrative, collaboration). City of Amsterdam = policy + budget + regulation (current circular-economy lead: René Koop; political champion of April 2020 adoption: former deputy mayor Marieke van Doorninck). Dagan represents the network layer, not the City. Conflating them is the signature outsider mistake.
Cost severance
The structural mechanism by which value is extracted while the costs of producing it are deliberately separated and transferred to people, ecosystems, or futures who didn't capture the upside. Framework's primary contribution. Operates across capitalism, feudalism, colonialism, command economies — and across the full extraction spectrum (non-renewable + renewable past regen).
Cost Severance = RCV − B
Framework's central accounting equation. When B (the bonded amount posted) is below RCV (the worth foreclosed), the difference is the cost severed onto others.
Cost Severance Damages (CSD)
Framework's analytic instrument applying matched-comparison methodology (à la Umbrellas Don't Make It Rain) to extraction-community cases (McDowell County, Libby, Niger Delta, opioid-Appalachia).
Design fiction
Speculative-design methodology (term coined by Julian Bleecker, Near Future Laboratory). Uses fictional artifacts, scenarios, or institutions to make alternative futures imaginable. CHANGENCY's methodological substrate. De Klerenpartij (Feb 2026) is a contemporary instance — a fictional political party as design-fiction object.
Donut vs Doughnut
Dutch civic register uses donut (matching Amsterdamse Donut Coalitie and the Coalition's English self-styling); DEAL/academic register uses Doughnut. Both correct. Use donut for the Coalition; Doughnut for the framework qua framework.
Donut & Friends Festival
Annual ADC festival since 2021. November 2024 edition was the five-year anniversary; Dagan delivered the retrospective alongside Kate Raworth, Hans Stegeman (Triodos), Thami Schweichler (United Repair Centre), Nathalie van Loon (AmsterDOEN).
Donut Night: Van droom naar doorbraak
January 27, 2025 event at Pakhuis de Zwijger; ADC's year-five inflection-point conversation. Dagan was the framing voice. ("Dream to Breakthrough.")
Donutpioniers
Dutch: "donut pioneers." Coalition vocabulary for active-citizen change-makers. Recurring Pakhuis de Zwijger programme strand.
Donutstad
Dutch: "donut city." The Coalition's signature public-facing framing for Amsterdam-as-Doughnut-city.
Doughnut Economics
Kate Raworth's framework (2017 book); operates at economy-system level — articulating where economies should aim (the safe and just space between social foundation and ecological ceiling) and design principles for getting there. Adopted by Amsterdam April 2020 (first city in the world). Complementary to Commons Bonds, not competing: Doughnut sets the goal-state; Commons Bonds operates at the actor-transaction level supplying action-level pricing instruments.
Droom naar doorbraak
Dutch: "from dream to breakthrough." Dagan's current live rhetorical pivot for ADC's year-five inflection. Year 5: dreaming was the first chapter; breakthrough is the next. Engaging this frame substantively lands where his attention currently sits.
Foreclosure Bond (B2)
Framework's forward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm yet to be realized. Lineage: environmental-bond and civic-republican. Real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, surety bonds.
Four lenses (Doughnut Unrolled)
Methodological signature of the Amsterdam City Doughnut. Local-Social (people of Amsterdam faring) · Local-Ecological (Amsterdam's environment) · Global-Social (Amsterdam's consumption affecting people elsewhere — labour conditions, mining for metals) · Global-Ecological (Amsterdam contributing to overshooting planetary boundaries). DEAL canonicalized April 2022 as Doughnut Unrolled.
Global-Social asymmetry
Framework's diagnostic shorthand for the gap between the Doughnut's naming of imported impact (via the Global-Social lens) and the Coalition's what-to-do vocabulary on that lens (which reads thinner than the local-side work). The framework's clearest analytical opening with Dagan.
Imported / upstream cost severance
The framework's diagnostic for cost severance displaced in space: value lands in Amsterdam, costs sit upstream in supply chains (rare earths from Niger Delta, battery materials from extraction zones, cobalt for the energy transition, textile labour). Where the framework expects to find traction with Dagan.
Living labs
Coalition vocabulary for in-situ experimentation sites. Aspirational/experiential register.
Manolis (Sophia)
Stanford Urban Studies & Sociology undergrad; 2023 fieldwork hosted by Willem van Winden at HvA. Most-cited empirical finding: the majority of Amsterdam residents did not know what Doughnut Economics was. The framework's theoretical language is "inaccessible for many grassroots stakeholders and residents."
Pakhuis de Zwijger
Programmatic venue partner (de facto ADC stage). Donut Night, Donut Deal Dag, Donut Pioniers, Amsterdam Donut Dag are all hosted here.
Programme Director
Dagan's current ADC title (hardened ~late 2024 / early 2025). Earlier coverage: "leader," "lead," "core team."
Raworth (Kate)
Author, Doughnut Economics (2017); co-founder DEAL; first AUAS Professor of Practice (2020); ADC's intellectual anchor. Published the 8 April 2020 launch post Introducing the Amsterdam City Doughnut.
ReFan: Reshaping the Fashion Narrative
June 2025 workshop, Kohnstammhuis Amsterdam. CHANGENCY co-led with AMFI/AUAS (lead: José Teunissen). 45 fashion-textile-chain participants; 98% wanted continued involvement. Methodologically: same structural problem as the donut-narrative work, applied to textiles. Cross-domain methodological exemplar; cite by name.
Renewable past regenerative capacity
Framework's term for renewable resources extracted faster than they can regenerate. Once across the threshold, effectively converted to non-renewable; same RCV accounting applies.
Residual Commons Value (RCV)
The worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down. Framework's pricing-instrument target.
Restitution Bond (B1)
Framework's backward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm already realized. Lineage: reparations-economics.
Supperclub
Early-1990s Amsterdam "restaurant for all senses" Dagan co-founded as artistic director; staging of dinner-as-performance. First chapter of his bio arc.
Two-instrument decomposition
Framework's structural move: B = B1 + B2. Separates backward-looking restitution (Restitution Bond) from forward-looking foreclosure (Foreclosure Bond).
van Doorninck (Marieke)
Former Amsterdam deputy mayor for sustainability and urban planning; political champion of the April 2020 Doughnut adoption. Successor on the city-side circular work: René Koop.
van Winden (Willem)
HvA / Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences research professor; lead of REGENALYZE. Sharpest published critic of ADC. LinkedIn pulse pieces: The Amsterdam Doughnut Coalition: some critical notes; Is Kate Raworth radical enough?; Degrowth, green growth, or just another recession? Headline arguments: still substantial overshoot of planetary boundaries; "20% pluk" / participation-concentration; "doughnut community is still predominantly white"; doughnut "hardly applied to address urban social and racial inequities." Hosted Manolis.
Water-deal hypothetical
Framework's worked example for the Q3 substrate: a deal that would leverage water-management infrastructure in a way that could affect crop-growing capacity. Full 7-step walkthrough at research/outreach/subjects/dagan/water-deal-hypothetical_2026-05-07.md (internal scaffolding; don't show Dagan; ready to walk through any step if he interrogates the apparatus).
WDCD (What Design Can Do)
Dagan's seven-year (~2016–April 2023) programme-architect role. Led Climate Action Challenge and Make it Circular Challenge (with IKEA Foundation).
