## Email

**To:** william.darity@duke.edu
**Subject:** Thank you — and a quick written confirmation from our conversation

Sandy,

Thank you again for our conversation Wednesday afternoon. I keep thinking about the place where you stopped me to ask whether the common bond was a metaphor or something literal — that question reframed how I think about the framework's center of gravity, and I'm sitting with it.

I want to put a few things from our call into writing — partly so you have a record of what we agreed to, partly because the trade publisher I'll eventually contract with will likely appreciate the documentation when their permissions team takes a look down the road.

**On attribution.** When I asked at the start of the call about how you'd like your words handled in print, your answer had two parts. On your published written work, you said:

> *"Anything that I have in writing is essentially fair game. Even if the quotation used is not in the context that's consistent with my views, that's fair game."*

On material from our conversation, you said:

> *"My only constraint would be — please let me see what you're going to use before you [use it]."*

I'll abide by both across the book *Commons Bonds* and any derivative essays (Noema currently in preparation; Aeon possibly, on a separate angle; one or two more if the publishing arc lands the way I'm aiming). Specifically, that means I'll treat your published written work as freely quotable with attribution, and I'll send you any direct quotes or substantive paraphrases I want to draw from our conversation — book and essays both — for your review before they're published. I'll batch those into per-essay or per-chapter packets to keep the request count low and the context clear; the first batch will likely come when I have a draft of the Noema essay's reparations-economics section, which I'm aiming at in the next few weeks.

If anything about that two-tier framing isn't quite what you meant, please correct me and I'll defer to your refinement.

**On the recording.** I should be straight with you: I had the call on Otter.ai for my own working transcript, but I didn't separately ask your verbal yes to that at the top of the call — I went straight to the attribution question. The recording is legal on my own consent in both Virginia (my end) and North Carolina (your end), and the audio is only used to produce an accurate transcript for the materials I'll be sending you for review per the attribution preference above. The audio file sits on my own working devices, not a cloud service. Once your attribution review and the relevant manuscript work are complete, I'll delete it and let you know it's done. If you'd prefer I delete the recording now and work from notes plus my memory of the call, say the word and I'll do it today.

**On publication scope.** The materials drawing on our conversation will likely include:

- **The book** *Commons Bonds* — trade publisher TBD; target publication 2026–2027.
- **A magazine essay in Noema** — currently in preparation; targeted for late May / early June 2026 submission.
- **A magazine essay in Aeon** (potentially, if the pitch I have under preparation is accepted) — different angle, would likely draw on a different portion of our conversation if at all.
- **Op-eds + talks** drawing on the same material from time to time.

If a future use surfaces that's substantially outside this scope, I'll loop back to you before publishing.

**On what you said you'd take a look at.** You said yes to seeing the framework, and asked specifically about Chapter 6 for the prose engaging the math. I'm aiming to send a clean version of both the Technical Appendix v1.0.0 and the Chapter 6 draft within the next 1–2 weeks. I'll caveat that the Technical Appendix is long (~115 pages); Chapter 6 is shorter and is the right place to start if you only have an hour. No pressure on the timeline of your read — whenever fits.

You also said, near the end, *"there may be an option that you may not have considered."* If you were pointing at the Fogel and Engerman two-volume model — *Time on the Cross* with the narrative volume and the technical-support volume separate — that's already shifted my thinking about how to handle the manuscript-plus-appendix size problem with an agent and publisher. I'd be grateful for whatever else is in your "option you may not have considered." Either as a written reply or in conversation if it surfaces; whatever's easiest.

**On the reading recommendations.** Three things I'm following up on from the call:

- *Black Reconstruction* — I've read pieces of it but not in full; queuing it up.
- Amartya Sen's work on functionings and capabilities and on social-welfare-function construction — your framing for the right-wing pushback against giving value to future generations is exactly the gap I've been struggling to defend in Chapter 6. Engaging with Sen there.
- *Time on the Cross* — as the structural model, per above; I have no illusions about being a fan of the substance.

**On warm-intro indications.** We didn't get to this question on the call — I ran the clock on the priority questions and you had the 3:30 hard stop. I don't want to push it as homework, so I'll just leave the question open: if, when you've had a look at the materials, someone in your network comes to mind as someone the framework would benefit from engaging with — particularly anyone you'd expect to push back hardest on the specific moves — I'd be grateful for the surfacing. No pressure either way; the conversation we had already produces value on its own.

**On staying in touch.** I'll send the Tech Appendix and Chapter 6 within 1–2 weeks per above. The Noema-essay material that draws on our conversation will come to you per the attribution preference, ahead of submission. If anything I send doesn't sit right, please tell me — either by email or by phone if it's easier. I'd rather rework prose than have anything go to print that you'd want different.

Thank you again for the time. The conversation has already shifted two specific things in the manuscript I hadn't expected: your point that "restitution" lives inside the FHTE definition of reparations as one of two types of redress (which means I need to position the framework's backward-looking instrument relative to your typology rather than alongside it), and your observation that Ostrom assumes homogeneity of interest across what she calls the community — which is a second break-point I hadn't yet built into the framework's Ostrom-extension positioning. Both will land in the next round of revisions.

Best,
