## The framework, in one paragraph

*Commons Bonds* names a structural mechanism that operates across capitalism, feudalism, colonialism, and Soviet industrial planning — and across the full extraction spectrum, from non-renewable resources to renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity. In each case, value is extracted while the costs of that extraction are deliberately severed from the entity that profited and transferred to others. Two contributions. **Cost severance** is the act that cuts profit from the cost of producing it. **Residual commons value (RCV)** is the worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down. The framework's central accounting equation is *Cost Severance = RCV − B*, where B is a coordinated **two-instrument bond posting**: the **Restitution Bond (B₁)** discharges harm already realized (backward-looking; reparations-economics lineage), and the **Foreclosure Bond (B₂)** discharges harm yet to be realized (forward-looking; environmental-bond and civic-republican lineage). The thesis of the accountability-gap chapter is empirical: across American extraction regimes — both non-renewable extraction and renewable extraction past regenerative capacity — B is far below RCV by orders of magnitude.

## Where your work sits in the manuscript

- **Chapter 5 (The Accountability Gap), §"Restitution and Foreclosure":** *From Here to Equality* (Darity & Mullen 2020) is named the contemporary landmark of reparations economics; *Umbrellas Don't Make It Rain* (Hamilton, Darity, Price, Sridharan & Tippett 2015) is named the methodological foundation for the wealth-as-structural-variable claim that grounds the Restitution Bond instrument.
- **Chapter 6 (Methodology):** Darity-Mullen wealth-gap calculation is engaged as the contemporary state of the art for pricing harm whose damage is structurally embedded rather than tortious — the methodological lineage the **Cost Severance Damages (CSD)** instrument extends into extraction-community contexts (McDowell County coal; Libby asbestos; Niger Delta; opioid-Appalachia mortality demography).
- **Bibliography §18.5:** *From Here to Equality* + *Umbrellas* both anchored.
- **Existing vocabulary the framework positions alongside:** stratification economics (your *intergroup-relational* framing); the racial wealth gap as structural variable (per *Umbrellas*); lineage-based reparations (per FHTE 2nd ed. + the recent "Stop calling everything reparations" framing). The framework's *cost severance* and *RCV* terms are positioned as precision-adds, not replacements.

5. **Reading recommendations.** What in your work — or others' — would most sharpen the framework's positioning in the literature?
