## Pivot articulations — drop-in if Colden goes there

### "How does this extend Ostrom?"

> *"Ostrom 1990 showed that commons can be governed without state or market through institutional design — but her design principles work specifically for commons that can be managed in sustained-yield perpetuity. The framework extends to commons that can't be managed that way: non-renewable resources from the start, and renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity. The two-instrument bond architecture is what operates where sustained-yield discipline can no longer apply — accountability-by-instrument for cases where accountability-by-management isn't possible."*

If she wants depth: bibliography §1 (Ostrom, North, Coase, Hardin).

### "How does this add to Hotelling?"

> *"Hotelling 1931 modeled scarcity rents for exhaustible resources assuming efficient pricing — the math is right. The framework's claim is that the inputs have been wrong: the scarcity rent in Hotelling's sense excludes the cost of permanent option-value foreclosure on future generations, which is what RCV names. The framework's contribution is an accounting instrument that puts intergenerational option-value-loss inside the scarcity rent rather than outside it — making Hotelling's math operate on the right inputs."*

If she wants depth: bibliography §18.6.

### "Where does Polanyi sit?"

> *"Polanyi's fictitious-commodities frame in The Great Transformation is the deep substrate the framework builds on. The framework's observation — that certain costs should not be transactable externalities but are treated as such — is a descendant of Polanyi's 'land, labor, money are not produced for market exchange' move. Cost severance is the contemporary mechanism that produces the crises Polanyi identified."*

### "How does this relate to Mazzucato?"

> *"Mazzucato's distinction between value-creation and value-extraction in The Value of Everything is the clearest articulation of what economics has been refusing to name precisely. Cost severance is positioned as a precision-add on her value-extraction vocabulary — value-extraction is what the actor does; cost severance is what the actor does to the costs of doing it."*

### "How does this relate to Boyce / ecological economics?"

> *"Boyce at PERI works the intersection of ecological economics and accountability the framework also engages, particularly through Economics for People and the Planet and the carbon-dividend literature. Cost Severance Damages is presented as an analytic instrument; Boyce's work shapes how the framework imagines those instruments in distributional terms — including for renewable-resource cases like the Bay."*

### "Foreclosure Bond — isn't that license to extract?"

> *"The accounting equation does that work: B = RCV is the structural condition. If B is set to RCV, posting the bond IS internalizing the cost — any rational actor stops extracting before posting. So the bond framing isn't 'license to extract'; it's the instrument by which 'license to extract' becomes legible."*

### "Why bonds — and not funds, accounts, trusts, or reserves?"

> *"The framework chose 'Bond' deliberately over alternatives — Account, Reserve, Fund, Trust — for three structural reasons. First, real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, and surety bonds are existing legal instruments with a century-plus regulatory track record. Second, legal-adoption-travel: the framework's success criterion is vocabulary adoption in regulatory contexts, and 'Bond' already lives there. Third, role-encoding precision: 'Bond' specifies an amount posted against an obligation, returned if conditions are met, forfeited if they aren't — matches Cost Severance equals RCV minus B cleanly."*

## Glossary — alphabetical reference

### Framework terms

- **Cost severance** — The structural mechanism by which value is extracted while the costs of producing it are deliberately separated and transferred to people, ecosystems, or futures who didn't capture the upside. Framework's primary contribution.
- **Cost Severance = RCV − B** — Framework's central accounting equation. When B (the bonded amount posted) is below RCV (the worth foreclosed), the difference is the cost severed onto others.
- **Cost Severance Damages (CSD)** — Framework's analytic instrument applying matched-comparison methodology to extraction-community cases. Renewable-past-regen application: the Bay.
- **Foreclosure Bond (B₂)** — Framework's forward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm yet to be realized. Lineage: environmental-bond and civic-republican.
- **Renewable past regenerative capacity** — Framework's term for renewable resources extracted faster than they can regenerate. Once across the threshold, effectively converted to non-renewable; same RCV accounting applies. **The Bay chapter is the framework's central case for this regime.**
- **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** — The worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down. Framework's pricing-instrument target.
- **Restitution Bond (B₁)** — Framework's backward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm already realized. Lineage: reparations-economics + restitution-tradition.
- **Two-instrument decomposition** — Framework's structural move: B = B₁ + B₂. Backward-looking restitution + forward-looking foreclosure.

### CBF / institutional terms

- **ASMFC** — Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission. Multi-state interstate compact body managing coastal fisheries from Maine to Florida. Colden is MD's *legislative proxy* on the Atlantic Menhaden Management Board.
- **CBF** — Chesapeake Bay Foundation. Annapolis-based; founded 1967; three regional EDs (MD/VA/PA) + Federal Director; President Hilary Harp Falk (since Jan 2022).
- **CBWA / Chesapeake Bay Watershed Agreement** — multi-jurisdictional voluntary commitment; 2014 baseline; 2025 revised; 2040 horizon. Distinct from federal *TMDL*.
- **CESR (2023)** — STAC's Comprehensive Evaluation of System Response report on the 40-year Bay nutrient-reduction effort.
- **DNR (MD DNR)** — Maryland Department of Natural Resources. Secretary Josh Kurtz (current).
- **MWA / DFA** — Maryland Watermen's Association (Robert T. Brown Sr., president); Delmarva Fisheries Association (Robert Newberry, chair). Organized political voice for watermen.
- **Oyster Advisory Commission (OAC)** — MD DNR's stakeholder advisory body on oyster management. Colden is appointed member.
- **Omega Protein** — Industrial menhaden reduction company; operates out of Reedville, VA. Structural counterweight on menhaden.
- **OysterFutures** — Stakeholder consensus process on Maryland oyster management. Colden's *"cherry-picks"* critique was directed at DNR's selective implementation of OysterFutures recommendations.
- **PSFA (2020)** — Public Shellfish Fishery Areas proposal (DNR, Nov 2020) to establish criteria for new harvest areas; Colden's *"undermines the spirit of the law"* critique. CBF opposed.
- **SB 922 (2024)** — Maryland Senate bill that would have repealed the prohibition on DNR altering existing oyster sanctuary boundaries. Colden submitted UNF testimony Feb 27, 2024.

- **VMRC** — Virginia Marine Resources Commission. Virginia's analog to MD DNR for marine fisheries management.

### Fisheries-management vocabulary

- **Bay cap (the)** — 51,000 metric ton ASMFC menhaden Chesapeake Bay cap; established Nov 2017 on Colden's original motion. Singular, capitalized in CBF MD usage.
- **Five tributary-scale sanctuaries** — Harris Creek, Little Choptank, Tred Avon, St. Mary's, Manokin. Permanently protected by 2019 MD legislation; completed Aug 2025 (Manokin). Colden defends them.
- **Juvenile recruitment survey / young-of-year index** — Annual survey measuring spawning success. MD 2024: 2.0 fish/sample vs. long-term avg 11.0. Sixth consecutive below-average year.
- **MSY** — Maximum sustainable yield. Conventional fisheries-management target.
- **Rockfish** — Maryland colloquial name for *striped bass*. Both used interchangeably in CBF MD copy and Colden's public statements. **Don't "correct" between them.**
- **Sanctuary (oyster)** — Tributary-scale closure to harvest; the five large-scale ones are Harris Creek, Little Choptank, Tred Avon, St. Mary's, Manokin. Distinct from MPAs; DO NOT call them "marine protected areas."
- **Stock assessment** — Periodic comprehensive scientific evaluation of a fishery's status. Blue crab next due 2026.
- **TAC** — Total Allowable Catch. Quota set at the species/regulatory-unit level.
- **TMDL** — Total Maximum Daily Load (federal EPA mechanism). Legally enforceable. Distinct from the voluntary CBWA.
