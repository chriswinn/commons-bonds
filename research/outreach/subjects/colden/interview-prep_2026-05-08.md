## Talking-point notes per priority question

Brief notes on what to draw out + what to push on if Colden gives a short answer to any priority question. Use only if needed; don't lead with these.

### Q1 (vocabulary precision) — MUST-ASK

- **Draw out:** a specific yes-or-no on whether *cost severance* + *RCV* add work that her existing vocabulary doesn't.
- **Push if shallow:** *"Is the work it does adding precision to 'overharvest,' 'collapse,' 'depletion,' 'recruitment failure' — or to something else? Or does it sit alongside those terms without adding analytical precision?"*

Pay close attention if she names *overharvest*, *recruitment failure*, *ecosystem-based management*, or *adaptive management* as the territory the framework is overlapping.

### Q2 (structural read on institutional-failure pattern) — SHOULD-ASK

- **Draw out:** her structural read on what's load-bearing in the ASMFC/DNR failure pattern *beyond the politics of any particular decision*.
- **Push if shallow:** *"You've used 'cherry-picks' on the OysterFutures implementation. You've used 'undermines the process' on the 2020 PSFA proposal. - **Career-protection care:** she will likely *not* personalize critiques. Don't push her into naming specific officials or industry actors. Architecture-level read only.

### Q3 (renewable-past-regeneration empirical anchors) — SHOULD-ASK

- **Draw out:** specific species + specific moments — which crossed the threshold, which are still reversible.
- **Push if shallow:** *"Your 2017 MEPS paper named a 0.3 m oyster reef threshold for self-sustaining ecosystem function. Does the threshold construct generalize from reef structure to species-level fishery outcomes? Does it describe stripers? Blue crabs?"*
- **Chat-open probe:** *"Is there a moment in Bay management history where, looking back, the regenerative threshold was crossed in real time and it was visible at the time — versus only visible in retrospect?"*
- **Watch for:** the 1980s striper collapse (Goldsborough's institutional-memory territory); the oyster-reef collapse spanning 1880s–early-1900s; the 2017–2025 striper downturn; the 2020s blue crab decline. Whether she draws a sharp line between "crossed and now we manage what's left" vs. "approaching threshold but still reversible" — and where she places each species.

### Q4 (sanctuaries as regulatory-failure-insurance) — SHOULD-ASK

- **Draw out:** whether the regulatory-failure-insurance framing lands or misfires; what the sanctuaries actually do that the framework's read might miss.
- **Push if shallow:** *"The 2019 legislation made the five tributary-scale sanctuaries permanent. SB 922 in 2024 was an attempt to allow DNR to alter their boundaries; you submitted unfavorable testimony. The framework reads that arc as: the harvest regime structurally cannot self-correct, so a portion of the resource is walled off from harvest pressure entirely. Is that fair, or is the sanctuaries-as-architecture story doing something else — ecosystem-recovery seeding, climate-resilience infrastructure, scientific reference sites?"*
- **Chat-open probe:** *"What's the case for sanctuaries that's not 'we don't trust the harvest regime'?"*
- **Vocabulary-failure probe:** *"Does 'regulatory-failure-insurance' read as fair characterization of sanctuary function, or does it overstate the failure register? Is there a different framing that captures the same structural work without locating it in regulatory failure?"*
- **Watch for:** any reframing she offers (sanctuaries as *positive infrastructure* rather than *defensive insurance*); the 0.3 m threshold work as the science-grounded case for sanctuaries; the climate-resilience framing (oyster reefs as living shorelines) which doubles their role beyond species recovery.

Does that ring true, or does it miss how watermen describe their own position?"*
