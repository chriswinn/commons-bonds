## The framework, in one paragraph

*Commons Bonds* names a structural mechanism operating across the full extraction spectrum — from non-renewable resources (coal, oil, minerals) to renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity (Chesapeake fisheries, oyster reefs, forest systems). In each case, value is extracted while the costs are deliberately severed from the entity that profited and transferred to others — communities, ecosystems, future generations. Two contributions. **Cost severance** is the structural act that cuts profit from the cost of producing it. **Residual commons value (RCV)** is the worth permanently foreclosed when a renewable resource is pushed past regenerative capacity (or a non-renewable resource is drawn down). The book's renewable-past-regeneration chapter argues the Chesapeake is a regulatory architecture that has consistently lagged the science — with the menhaden Bay-cap-from-historic-landings methodology and the structurally-distinct contemporary striped-bass crisis as the load-bearing cases.
