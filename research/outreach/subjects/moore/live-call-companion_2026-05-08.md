### "Foreclosure Bond — isn't that license to extract?"

If Moore raises the concern that the Foreclosure Bond reads as "pay the bond, extract anyway":

> *"The accounting equation does that work: B = RCV is the structural condition. If B is set to RCV, posting the bond IS internalizing the cost — any rational actor stops extracting before posting. If B is set below RCV, the framework explicitly names that as cost severance — exactly the misuse pattern we're pricing. So the bond framing isn't 'license to extract'; it's the instrument by which 'license to extract' becomes legible. For the Bay menhaden case: the bond would be posted against the foreclosure of striped bass prey availability + osprey starvation + blue crab habitat — and the bond's value would equal the worth foreclosed. If foreclosed worth exceeds extracted value, the rational move is to stop extracting."*

### "How does this extend Ostrom?"

> *"Ostrom 1990 showed that commons can be governed without state or market through institutional design — but her design principles work specifically for commons that can be managed in sustained-yield perpetuity. The framework extends to commons that can't be managed that way: non-renewable resources from the start, AND renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity (Chesapeake fisheries are a textbook case). The two-instrument bond architecture is what operates where sustained-yield discipline can no longer apply — accountability-by-instrument for cases where accountability-by-management isn't possible."*

### "How does this add to Hotelling?"

> *"Hotelling 1931 modeled scarcity rents for exhaustible resources assuming efficient pricing — the math is right. The framework's claim is that the inputs have been wrong: the scarcity rent in Hotelling's sense excludes the cost of permanent option-value foreclosure on future generations, which is what RCV names. The framework's contribution is an accounting instrument that puts intergenerational option-value-loss inside the scarcity rent rather than outside it."*

### "How does this relate to Boyce?"

> *"Boyce at PERI works the intersection of ecological economics and reparations the framework also engages, particularly through *Economics for People and the Planet* and the carbon-dividend literature. Cost Severance Damages is presented as an analytic instrument; Boyce's work shapes how the framework imagines those instruments in distributional terms."*

### "Mask-of-Abundance / Dunbar-tradition pivot" (LOW likelihood with Moore; reuse Darity-prepared pivot if it surfaces)

If Moore raises the question of the Aeon essay's "Mask of Abundance" title:

> *"Yes — choosing 'mask' for the title puts the framework structurally inside the Dunbar / Du Bois / Ellison / Fanon lineage of writing about systemic harm hidden from dominant gaze. The framework's actual subjects of severance — the McDowell coal miner whose lungs sound like his father's, the watermen whose grandparents' Bay isn't there anymore, the communities downstream of every extraction site — often ARE the communities that tradition speaks of. The plan is explicit acknowledgment: brief in-text aside in the Aeon essay; substantive engagement in the book — Ch 8's McDowell case explicitly threads the Dunbar tradition into the framework's account of who absorbs cost; Ch 1 names the lineage where the framework's central rhetorical move first surfaces. I'd be very grateful for any guidance you have."*

### "Why bonds — and not funds, accounts, trusts, or reserves?"

> *"The framework chose 'Bond' deliberately for three structural reasons. First, real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, and surety bonds are existing legal instruments with a century-plus regulatory track record. Second, legal-adoption-travel: 'Bond' already lives in regulatory contexts; lawyers and policymakers recognize the structural properties immediately. Third, role-encoding precision: 'Bond' specifies an amount posted against an obligation, returned if conditions are met, forfeited if they aren't — that matches Cost Severance equals RCV minus B cleanly. Account is too generic, Reserve implies one-sided holding, Fund implies general pooling, Trust carries fiduciary connotation but not the conditional-return structure."*

## Glossary — alphabetical reference for framework + CBF + VA-fisheries + Moore vocabulary

- **ASMFC (Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission)** — the interstate fisheries-management compact (Maine to Florida) that issues Atlantic menhaden + striped bass management plans; Bay cap (51,000 mt menhaden) is set by ASMFC. Moore is a recurring on-record commenter at the Atlantic Menhaden Management Board and Striped Bass Management Board.
- **Bay cap** — the 51,000-metric-ton (since 2017; previously 87,000 mt) ASMFC-set annual harvest cap on industrial menhaden reduction fishing inside Chesapeake Bay. **Generated from historic landings, not Bay-specific ecology** — this is Moore's central methodology critique (Feb 2026 VA Mercury anchor quote).
- **Cost severance** — Framework's primary contribution. The structural mechanism by which value is extracted while the costs of producing it are deliberately separated and transferred to people, ecosystems, or futures who didn't capture the upside. For the menhaden Bay case: Omega's ~100M-lb/yr harvest leaves the Bay as fish oil + animal feed; costs (osprey starvation, striped bass prey deficit, blue crab habitat impacts, waterman income loss) stay in the Bay.
- **Cost Severance = RCV − B** — Framework's central accounting equation. When B (the bonded amount posted) is below RCV (the worth foreclosed), the difference is the cost severed onto others.
- **Cost Severance Damages (CSD)** — Framework's analytic instrument applying matched-comparison methodology to extraction-community cases. For Ch 3's Chesapeake application: comparing the realized Bay ecosystem-cascade impacts against a counterfactual Bay regulated under Bay-specific ecological reference points.
- **ERPs (Ecological Reference Points)** — ASMFC's 2017-2020 collaborative framework for menhaden management that explicitly accounts for predators (striped bass, bluefish, weakfish, spiny dogfish). Moore was a CBF-side participant. **Coastwide, not Bay-specific** — this is the structural gap Moore's 2026 framing names.
- **Foreclosure Bond (B₂)** — Framework's forward-looking accountability instrument; discharges harm yet to be realized. For the menhaden Bay case: a bond posted against the foreclosure of striped bass prey availability, osprey survival, blue crab habitat — bond value equals worth foreclosed.
- **Forage fish** — Moore's consistent term for menhaden; ecological-role framing. Distinguishes from species framings that obscure the pelagic-prey function in the Bay food web.
- **Foreign-owned** — Moore's deliberate framing for Omega Protein (Cooke Inc., Canadian parent). Populist register Moore deliberately uses; signals the value flowing out of VA / out of US, costs staying.
- **Industrial reduction fishery** — Moore's consistent term for Omega's Bay menhaden operation. Distinguishes from the bait fishery (smaller, commercial-bait, also in VA) and from charter / wild-harvest / aquaculture watermen.
- **Menhaden Fisheries Coalition** — industry counter-coalition; issued the **Oct 2025 rebuttal naming Moore in the headline** ("Refutes False Claims by Chesapeake Bay Foundation's Chris Moore") after his Sept 24 2025 VA Aquarium climate-fisheries talk. Concrete, current, personalized industry pushback.
- **Omega Protein** — Reedville-based industrial menhaden reduction fishery; Cooke Inc. (Canadian) subsidiary; ~260 employees; second-largest employer in Northumberland County after the school system. Politically explosive; the named counter-actor in Moore's most-cited quotes.
- **Pause-Bay-fishery position** — CBF's canonical Sept 2025 articulation: pause industrial menhaden reduction fishing inside Bay until Bay-specific science is complete; allow industrial fishing to continue offshore in coastal Atlantic waters. Moore's "Time to Pause Menhaden Fishing in the Chesapeake Bay" *Save the Bay* blog post is the canonical source.
- **Port Isobel Environmental Education Center** — CBF's 250-acre residential education center on the Tangier archipelago. Hires Tangier captains as Captain/Educators. **Highest-yield single CBF program for the chapter's load-bearing watermen-voice need.**
- **Renewable past regenerative capacity** — Framework's term for renewable resources extracted faster than they can regenerate. Once across the threshold, effectively converted to non-renewable; same RCV accounting applies. Chapter 3's central thesis applied to the Chesapeake.
- **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** — Framework's pricing-instrument target. The worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down.
- **Restitution Bond (B₁)** — Framework's backward-looking accountability instrument; discharges harm already realized. Lineage: reparations-economics. (Less directly applicable to the Chesapeake fishery case than the Foreclosure Bond, but available if Moore goes there.)
- **SCEMFIS** — federally-funded program ($2.5M via Trump admin's Jan 2025 spending package) collaboration with VIMS / UMCES / NOAA on Bay-specific menhaden science. Moore is the CBF-side bridge to the slow-science timeline.
- **Sherfinski (David)** — CBF Virginia Communications & Media Relations Manager; intercepted Moore cold outreach 2026-05-06; offered routing assistance; the gating contact for the Moore interview.
- **Stratified Bass / Striped Bass / "rockfish"** — the Chesapeake's iconic sportfish; recovered from 1970s-1980s collapse via 1985-89 moratorium; **currently in renewed crisis under structurally-distinct contemporary stressor stack** (warmer water + habitat loss + invasive blue catfish + menhaden prey deficit). Moore's 2024 framing: "will not have the same recovery they had in the 1980s."
- **Stressor stack** — Moore's analytical move: multiple compounding stressors (climate + invasive species + pollution + fishing pressure + habitat loss) rather than single-cause attribution. Framework-compatible structural pattern.
- **Sustained-yield perpetuity (Ostrom)** — Ostrom's commons-governance domain — the regime where institutional design can manage commons indefinitely. **Framework extends to non-sustained-yield contexts** (renewable resources past regenerative capacity).
- **Tangier Island** — VA Bay island community with ~400 residents; Trump-supporter, climate-skeptical demographic; CBF partner via 2024-25 NFWF $356,500 Shoreline Adaptation Plan. Mayor "Ooker" Eskridge is the highest-leverage single VA waterman voice; facilitable via Moore through the active partnership.
- **Twin Rivers Watermen's Association** — Northern Neck wild-harvest watermen association; Wayne France (past president), Ed Arnest (current, Hague, VA). Direct chapter target.
- **Two-instrument decomposition** — Framework's structural move: B = B₁ + B₂. Separates backward-looking restitution (Restitution Bond) from forward-looking foreclosure (Foreclosure Bond).
- **VIMS (Virginia Institute of Marine Science)** — College of William & Mary marine-science institute; Bay-specific menhaden science target ($3M three-year study, repeatedly blocked); Lipcius (blue crab), Mann (oyster), Latour (menhaden).
- **VMRC (Virginia Marine Resources Commission)** — VA fisheries-regulatory body; Moore is a recurring public commenter / advisory committee participant.
- **Watermen (cultural)** — wild-harvest, charter, oyster aquaculture operators; multi-generational ties to the Bay. **NOT industrial-reduction-fishery workers.** The chapter's load-bearing voice category.
