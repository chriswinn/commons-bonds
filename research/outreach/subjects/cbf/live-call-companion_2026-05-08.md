## Pivot articulations — drop-in if subject goes there

### Lineage articulations

#### "How does this extend Ostrom?" **(most likely lineage pivot for CBF — fisheries are commons)**

> *"Ostrom 1990 showed that commons can be governed without state or market through institutional design — but her design principles work specifically for commons that can be managed in sustained-yield perpetuity. The framework extends to commons that can't be managed that way: non-renewable resources from the start, and renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity. The two-instrument bond architecture is what operates where sustained-yield discipline can no longer apply — accountability-by-instrument for cases where accountability-by-management isn't possible."*

If they want depth: bibliography §1 (Ostrom, North, Coase, Hardin).

#### "How does this add to Hotelling?" **[C][F][G]**

> *"Hotelling 1931 modeled scarcity rents for exhaustible resources assuming efficient pricing — the math is right. The framework's claim is that the inputs have been wrong: the scarcity rent in Hotelling's sense excludes the cost of permanent option-value foreclosure on future generations, which is what RCV names. The framework's contribution is an accounting instrument that puts intergenerational option-value-loss inside the scarcity rent rather than outside it — making Hotelling's math operate on the right inputs."*

#### "Where does Polanyi sit?" **[F][G]**

> *"Polanyi's fictitious-commodities frame in The Great Transformation is the deep substrate the framework builds on. The framework's observation — that certain costs should not be transactable externalities but are treated as such — is a descendant of Polanyi's 'land, labor, money are not produced for market exchange' move. Cost severance is the contemporary mechanism that produces the crises Polanyi identified."*

#### "Why bonds — and not funds, accounts, trusts, or reserves?" **[C][F][G]**

> *"The framework chose 'Bond' deliberately over alternatives. First, real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, surety bonds are existing legal instruments with a century-plus regulatory track record. Second, legal-adoption-travel: the framework's success criterion is vocabulary adoption in regulatory contexts, and 'Bond' already lives there. Third, role-encoding precision: 'Bond' specifies an amount posted against an obligation, returned if conditions are met, forfeited if they aren't — that matches Cost Severance equals RCV minus B cleanly. Account is too generic, Reserve implies one-sided holding, Fund implies general pooling, Trust carries fiduciary connotation but not the conditional-return structure."*

## Glossary — alphabetical reference

### Framework terms

- **Accountability-instrument architecture** — institutional structures where the binding of cost to actor has gone past values-statement and into operational discipline. CBF programs (sanctuaries, observer requirements, science funds, restoration goals) sit in this category.
- **Cost severance** — The structural mechanism by which value is extracted while the costs of producing it are deliberately separated and transferred to people, ecosystems, or futures who didn't capture the upside. Framework's primary contribution.
- **Cost Severance = RCV − B** — Framework's central accounting equation. When B (the bonded amount posted) is below RCV (the worth foreclosed), the difference is the cost severed onto others.
- **Cost Severance Damages (CSD)** — Framework's analytic instrument applying matched-comparison methodology to extraction-community cases.
- **Foreclosure Bond (B₂)** — Framework's forward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm yet to be realized. Lineage: environmental-bond and civic-republican.
- **Regulatory architecture lagging the science** — Framework's thesis: management units (Bay caps from historic landings; coastwide ERPs; sanctuary boundaries) consistently fail to match the biological unit the science describes. Cost severance is what happens in the gap.
- **Renewable-past-regeneration** — Framework's term for renewable resources extracted faster than they can regenerate. Once across the threshold, effectively converted to non-renewable; same RCV accounting applies. **Bay chapter's flagship case.**
- **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** — The worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down. Framework's pricing-instrument target.
- **Restitution Bond (B₁)** — Framework's backward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm already realized. Lineage: reparations-economics.
- **Two-instrument decomposition** — Framework's structural move: B = B₁ + B₂. Separates backward-looking restitution from forward-looking foreclosure.

### CBF organizational structure

- **Brock Environmental Center** — CBF's Norfolk / Hampton Roads / Lower Bay operations base. Home of Mobile Oyster Restoration Center + oyster gardening program.
- **CBF** — Chesapeake Bay Foundation; chartered 1967; HQ Annapolis (Philip Merrill Environmental Center). Field offices: Salisbury MD; Harrisburg PA; Richmond VA; Norfolk VA; Washington DC. Residential education centers: Port Isobel (VA Tangier archipelago); Smith Island/Tylerton (MD).
- **Hilary Falk** — President & CEO since Jan 2022; only the third leader in CBF history.
- **Philip Merrill Environmental Center** — CBF Annapolis HQ.
- **Port Isobel** — CBF residential education center on the Tangier archipelago (VA); 250 acres; hires Tangier captains as Captain/Educators. Highest-yield single CBF program for the chapter's load-bearing watermen-voice need.
- **Smith Island / Tylerton Center** — CBF MD-side residential education center; parallel to Port Isobel.

### CBF programs

- **Chesapeake Oyster Alliance (COA)** — CBF-founded 2018; 110+ partner coalition; Tanner Council Sr. Manager.
- **Hope on the Half Shell** — CBF Feb 2024 oyster report (Moore-anchored on VA side); recommends 20 additional Bay rivers for restoration.
- **Maryland Shellfish Growers Network** — CBF + UMD Extension joint program for new and veteran shellfish growers.
- **Mobile Oyster Restoration Center** — barge-based VA oyster restoration unit (Shannon-led).
- **Save Oyster Shells** — restaurant-and-volunteer shell recycling, ~4,000 bushels/yr in VA.
- **Tangier Island Shoreline Adaptation Plan** — $356,500 NFWF-funded effort; CBF administering with Bayland engineering. Mayor Eskridge named municipal partner.
- **Virginia Oyster Gardening Program** — 627 volunteers in 2024 raising/returning 110,000+ oysters; out of Brock Environmental Center.

### Fisheries-management vocabulary

- **ASMFC** — Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission. Coastwide management body for migratory fish (stripers, menhaden, etc.).
- **The Bay cap** — singular, capitalized in CBF usage; refers specifically to the 51,000 MT ASMFC menhaden Chesapeake Bay cap (set Nov 2017 on Colden's original motion).
- **Base science** — Moore's term; refers to baseline biological assessment that current Bay-cap methodology lacks. *"It's a struggle because we don't have base science to make good decisions."*
- **CESR** — 2023 STAC report on the 40-year Bay nutrient-reduction effort.
- **Chesapeake Bay Watershed Agreement** — multi-jurisdictional voluntary commitment; 2014 baseline; 2025 revised; 2040 horizon. Distinct from federal *TMDL* (legally enforceable EPA mechanism).
- **CBP** — Chesapeake Bay Program (federal-state partnership coordinating Bay restoration).
- **Cherry-picks** — Colden's distinctive characterization of regulatory selective implementation (DNR's OysterFutures management plan). *"Cherry-picks recommendations most favored by watermen while ignoring or glossing over others."*
- **ERPs (Ecological Reference Points)** — ASMFC's 2017-2020 collaborative framework replacing single-species management with ecosystem-based modeling for menhaden (Moore was a participant).
- **Foreshore-adaptation framing** — CBF's calibrated framing for Tangier work; *not* climate-rhetoric-forward; uses *erosion / shoreline / adaptation* vocabulary politically tractable in the Tangier community.
- **Industrial reduction fishery** — converts menhaden into fish oil + animal feed (Omega Protein / Reedville). Distinct from *bait fishery* (smaller, commercial-bait, also in VA).
- **Forage fish** — for menhaden; ecological-role framing emphasizing menhaden's role as prey base for stripers, ospreys, etc.
- **Foreign-owned** — Moore's deliberate framing on Omega Protein (Cooke Inc., Canadian parent). Pointed populist register; CBF uses it.
- **OAC (Maryland Oyster Advisory Commission)** — appointed advisory body; Colden serves.
- **Omega Protein** — Reedville, VA-based industrial menhaden reduction company; ~260 employees; Cooke Inc. Canadian parent. Counter-coalition to CBF on menhaden.
- **PSFA (Public Shellfish Fishery Areas)** — MD wild-harvest areas (vs. sanctuaries vs. aquaculture leases). 2020 PSFA proposal CBF opposed (*"undermines the spirit of the law"*).
- **Renewable-past-regeneration** — see Framework terms above. **Chapter's flagship construct.**
- **Rockfish / striped bass** — both in active use; do not "correct" between them. CBF uses both interchangeably.
- **Sanctuary (tributary-scale oyster restoration)** — MD's five tributary-scale sanctuaries: Harris Creek, Little Choptank, Tred Avon, St. Mary's, Manokin (all completed 2025). VA equivalents at 10-tributary scale completing 2025: Lafayette, Piankatank, Great Wicomico, Lynnhaven, Lower York, Eastern Branch of Elizabeth + others. Don't refer as "MPAs."
- **STAC** — Scientific and Technical Advisory Committee (CBP).
- **TMDL** — Total Maximum Daily Load; legally enforceable EPA pollution-reduction mechanism.
- **VMRC** — Virginia Marine Resources Commission. VA-state-level fisheries management.

### Per-staff distinctive vocabulary

- **2026 oyster paradox** — Colden articulation; population recovering, watermen suffering. Cleanest cost-severance-readable Bay articulation.
- **80% bait-catch reduction** — Moore's testimony figure; quantitative scarcity-cascade indicator downstream of industrial reduction fishery.
- **Connecting threads** — Moore's framing: menhaden as connecting thread across stripers / ospreys / blue crabs.
- **Eyes on the water** — Colden's framing on watermen's role: *"Without watermen, there's certainly a lot fewer eyes on the water and a lot fewer people caring passionately about the Bay."*
- **Looming disaster** — Colden + Moore parallel framing on stripers; *"surveys have made it clear — we're facing a looming disaster."*
- **Maine-to-Florida modeling** — Moore's critique of coastwide management generality vs. Bay-specific need.
- **Red flags flying** — Colden's blue crab framing.
- **Symmetry-of-deference** — Colden's methodologically precise framing: *"Just as we were confident in increasing TAC when science says we should, we need to be willing to take reductions when science indicates that's warranted."*
