## Live interview opening — Q0 attribution

**Verbal phrasing** (per `research/outreach/_protocols/interview-attribution-protocol_2026-05-06.md` §"Live interview"):

> *"Before we get into the framework questions, I want to ask about your preferences for how I handle our conversation in print. The book and any derivative essays — there's a Noema-track essay in development, possibly an Aeon-track piece on a separate angle — would draw on this conversation. Practitioners and institutional voices handle attribution differently — some prefer freely-quotable answers, some prefer paraphrase + attribution unless reviewed first, others ask for full transcript review before any quoting. Whatever works for you, I'll abide by it across the book and essays. What would you prefer?"*

**Default-if-uncertain:** Category 2 (paraphrase + review-before-quotes) — common preference for institutional voices, low manuscript-timeline burden, especially appropriate for 501c3-staff who are professionally constrained.

### Q0.5 — Vocabulary ground-rules (only for policy-specialist / strategic-institutional registers)

For Poston / Falk / Prost / Goldsborough specifically, after Q0 and before priority questions, consider:

> *"Before we get into the priority questions — the book uses some specific framework vocabulary (cost severance, RCV, regenerative capacity past-threshold) that may or may not map cleanly to how CBF talks about this work. I'll use both — yours and mine — and I'd like to know during the call where they line up and where they don't. Pushback is the most useful thing you can give me."*

For operational / on-water / community / working-waterfront / gateway registers, **skip Q0.5** — the framework vocabulary should land in the conversation organically rather than be flagged up front. Framework jargon-density up front can register as academic-register-alienating in those contexts.

## Priority tiering

Order in the live conversation matches the pre-read brief Q1 → Q5. If running out of time:

| Tier | Questions | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **MUST-ASK** | **Q1** (renewable-past-regeneration on the Bay); **Q2** (regulatory architecture vs. biology) | Q1 is the chapter's central thesis; Q2 is the structural-mechanism articulation. Both load-bearing for Ch 3. |
| **SHOULD-ASK** | **Q3** (cost-bearing today); **Q5** (voices) | Q3 tests whether *cost severance* vocabulary lands; Q5 unlocks routing. Q5 is cheap (~3 min) and always works at close. |
| **STRETCH** | **Q4** (generations) | Q4 is genuinely open-ended; depends on subject's lived/professional perspective. Don't force; if subject opens it organically (likely), follow. |

**Time discipline:** with 30-45 min interviews:

- **First ~5 min:** Q0 (attribution) + Q0.5 (if applicable). For working-waterfront / on-water / community register, skip Q0.5; use the additional time for warming.
- **Next ~25-30 min:** priority Q1-Q5 in pre-read order; let questions run deep when the subject opens them.
- **Final ~5-10 min:** closing block (network mapping; warm-intro asks; tech-appendix offer per Three Tiers).

## Career-protection vocabulary discipline (CRITICAL)

**Apply across ALL staff, ALL registers, no exceptions.**

### The no-Soviet-comparison rule

The framework's broader scope (§"cross-system framing" — capitalism / feudalism / colonialism / command economies) is **internal scaffolding** for the CBF context. **Do not introduce Soviet industrial planning, state-socialism comparisons, Aral Sea analogies, or any cross-system extraction comparison** in the brief, the pre-read, the live interview, or any subsequent CBF-facing correspondence.

**Why:** career-toxic for ANY 501c3 staffer in the politically heterogeneous Bay watershed (PA agriculture, VA Northern Neck rural-economy, Tangier political identity, conservative Maryland Eastern Shore, urban Baltimore/Norfolk/DC, etc.). CBF must maintain working relationships across that full political spectrum. A framework articulation that flags as anti-capitalist / pro-socialist would foreclose that.

The Bay chapter (Ch 3) **stands on its own Bay-specific terms.** The cross-system comparison cases live in earlier chapters (Ch 5 reparations / Ch 6 methodology / Ch 8 McDowell). They remain framework-internal scaffolding; CBF staff don't engage them.

### Political-volatility flags

- **Omega Protein / menhaden:** CBF Forage Campaign opposes; industry has named Moore by name in Oct 2025 attacks. Any CBF staff member is operating in a live-friction landscape. Frame engagement around Bay-specific *science* (gap; observer coverage; Bay-specific assessment) rather than around *industry villainy*. Moore's signature register — "industry blocks science" — is the calibrated CBF posture; mirror it.
- **Tangier political identity:** Mayor Eskridge is a Trump-supporting, climate-skeptic VA waterman; Tangier is a politically homogeneous Trump-supporting community. CBF maintains an active partnership via the $356,500 NFWF Shoreline Adaptation Plan, framed as *erosion-and-shoreline* rather than *climate-rhetoric-forward*. **Do not introduce climate-skepticism critique in any conversation that touches Tangier.** The framework engages cost severance structurally — political identity of cost-bearers does not figure in the analysis.
- **Charter-vs-commercial dynamics:** CCA Virginia (recreational) is CBF-aligned on menhaden; commercial reduction-fishery workers (Omega Protein, ~260 employees in Northumberland County) are the counter-coalition. The chapter's *watermen voice* is wild-harvest / charter / aquaculture — NOT industrial reduction-fishery workers. Don't conflate.
- **Race / Indigenous / treaty-rights framing:** Public-record sweep on Moore + Colden surfaced no positions on these axes. Likely a domain CBF staff would be wary of engaging if framework presses there — career-protection sensitivity. Probe gently if at all; don't lead with these.
- **Federal politics:** Both Colden and Moore have noted federal cuts (Trump-era 2025-2026) elevating state-level leverage. They use generic "federal cuts" rather than partisan framing. Mirror that. Don't introduce partisan framing the subject hasn't surfaced first.

### Vocabulary policing — what CBF staff likely correct

- **Rockfish / striped bass** — both in active use; do not "correct" between them.
- **Sanctuaries** — specifically *tributary-scale oyster restoration sanctuaries*. Don't refer as "MPAs" / "marine protected areas."
- **The Bay cap** — singular, capitalized in CBF usage; refers to the 51,000 MT ASMFC menhaden cap; not generic.
- **Industrial reduction fishery / bait fishery** — keep distinct.

## Contingency plan

Per FPD-11 (`alignment/commons_bonds_framework_positioning_disciplines_v1.0.0.md` §FPD-11). Adapted for CBF context.

**Probability:** lower than at first glance for Colden / Moore (rich public records suggest comfort with framework engagement). Higher for working-waterfront / on-water voices who may not engage framework vocabulary at all and instead engage experientially. The contingency below covers both kinds of pivots.

### Trigger conditions

- Subject explicitly says they haven't read enough of the framework or don't engage policy-economics frames.
- Subject gives short / surface-level answers to framework-specific questions, suggesting they're hedging because of unfamiliarity OR institutional discipline.
- Subject signals (verbally or via tone) discomfort with the analytical register; wants to talk about lived experience instead.
- Multiple framework-specific questions are landing flat or producing deflection.

### Verbal pivot move (Type 1: institutional/policy-specialist subject hedges)

> *"That makes sense — and to be clear, I'm not asking you to commit publicly to specific framework claims. Let me ask about your work directly instead, and I'll use what I learn to position the framework better in the manuscript."*

### Verbal pivot move (Type 2: on-water / community / working-waterfront subject prefers experiential register)

> *"Let me set the analytical questions aside. What I most want to learn from you is what you've watched change on the water — and what feels like it's at risk. Your voice in your own words is what the chapter most needs."*

### Framework-agnostic question bank (pick 3-5 based on subject)

1. **Watermen-voice listening (any working-waterfront subject):** *"What have you watched change on the water that prior generations of watermen wouldn't have watched change?"*
2. **Cycle-comparison (Goldsborough; any long-tenure subject):** *"Compared to the 1980s — or whatever earlier cycle you lived through — what's structurally different about the current crisis?"*
3. **Operational-reality (Shannon; restoration-side subjects):** *"Walk me through what restoration actually takes — week to week, season to season — that the public conversation about it doesn't capture."*
4. **Community-bearing (Everett; community-engagement subjects):** *"What does the Bay's condition cost the communities you work with — beyond what gets measured in stock assessments?"*
5. **Coalition-dynamics (Council; coalition-building subjects):** *"What does the COA partner network see that the centralized advocacy voices miss — and where does that show up in the policy fights?"*
6. **Network mapping (any subject):** *"Whose voice — beyond your own — would most help the chapter land its argument? And how would you suggest I reach them?"*

### What conversation still produces (even if framework calibration doesn't happen)

| Conversation element | Book destination |
|---|---|
| Direct subject quotes on their existing work | Ch 3 cited-evidence material |
| Operational / lived-experience texture | Ch 3 narrative texture |
| Vocabulary mapping (subject's terms vs. framework's) | FPD-9 adjacency mapping |
| Cycle-comparison insights | Ch 3 renewable-past-regeneration substrate |
| Network introductions (warm-intros to other voices) | Pipeline expansion for further fieldwork |
