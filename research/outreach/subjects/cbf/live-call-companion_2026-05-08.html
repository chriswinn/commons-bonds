Lineage articulations

"How does this extend Ostrom?" (most likely lineage pivot for CBF — fisheries are commons)

"Ostrom 1990 showed that commons can be governed without state or market through institutional design — but her design principles work specifically for commons that can be managed in sustained-yield perpetuity. The framework extends to commons that can't be managed that way: non-renewable resources from the start, and renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity. The two-instrument bond architecture is what operates where sustained-yield discipline can no longer apply — accountability-by-instrument for cases where accountability-by-management isn't possible."

If they want depth: bibliography §1 (Ostrom, North, Coase, Hardin).
"How does this add to Hotelling?" CFG

"Hotelling 1931 modeled scarcity rents for exhaustible resources assuming efficient pricing — the math is right. The framework's claim is that the inputs have been wrong: the scarcity rent in Hotelling's sense excludes the cost of permanent option-value foreclosure on future generations, which is what RCV names. The framework's contribution is an accounting instrument that puts intergenerational option-value-loss inside the scarcity rent rather than outside it — making Hotelling's math operate on the right inputs."

"Where does Polanyi sit?" FG

"Polanyi's fictitious-commodities frame in The Great Transformation is the deep substrate the framework builds on. The framework's observation — that certain costs should not be transactable externalities but are treated as such — is a descendant of Polanyi's 'land, labor, money are not produced for market exchange' move. Cost severance is the contemporary mechanism that produces the crises Polanyi identified."

"Why bonds — and not funds, accounts, trusts, or reserves?" CFG

"The framework chose 'Bond' deliberately over alternatives. First, real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, surety bonds are existing legal instruments with a century-plus regulatory track record. Second, legal-adoption-travel: the framework's success criterion is vocabulary adoption in regulatory contexts, and 'Bond' already lives there. Third, role-encoding precision: 'Bond' specifies an amount posted against an obligation, returned if conditions are met, forfeited if they aren't — that matches Cost Severance equals RCV minus B cleanly. Account is too generic, Reserve implies one-sided holding, Fund implies general pooling, Trust carries fiduciary connotation but not the conditional-return structure."

Glossary

Alphabetical within each section. Click any term to expand its definition.

Framework terms
Accountability-instrument architecture
Institutional structures where the binding of cost to actor has gone past values-statement and into operational discipline. CBF programs (sanctuaries, observer requirements, science funds, restoration goals) sit in this category.
Cost severance
The structural mechanism by which value is extracted while the costs of producing it are deliberately separated and transferred to people, ecosystems, or futures who didn't capture the upside. Framework's primary contribution.
Cost Severance = RCV − B
Framework's central accounting equation. When B (the bonded amount posted) is below RCV (the worth foreclosed), the difference is the cost severed onto others.
Cost Severance Damages (CSD)
Framework's analytic instrument applying matched-comparison methodology to extraction-community cases.
Foreclosure Bond (B₂)
Framework's forward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm yet to be realized. Lineage: environmental-bond and civic-republican.
Regulatory architecture lagging the science
Framework's thesis: management units (Bay caps from historic landings; coastwide ERPs; sanctuary boundaries) consistently fail to match the biological unit the science describes. Cost severance is what happens in the gap.
Renewable-past-regeneration
Framework's term for renewable resources extracted faster than they can regenerate. Once across the threshold, effectively converted to non-renewable; same RCV accounting applies. Bay chapter's flagship case.
Residual Commons Value (RCV)
The worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down. Framework's pricing-instrument target.
Restitution Bond (B₁)
Framework's backward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm already realized. Lineage: reparations-economics.
Two-instrument decomposition

CBF

Philip Merrill Environmental Center

Smith Island / Tylerton Center

Fisheries-management vocabulary
ASMFC
Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission. Coastwide management body for migratory fish (stripers, menhaden, etc.).
The Bay cap
Singular, capitalized in CBF usage; refers specifically to the 51,000 MT ASMFC menhaden Chesapeake Bay cap (set Nov 2017 on Colden's original motion).
Base science
Moore's term; refers to baseline biological assessment that current Bay-cap methodology lacks. "It's a struggle because we don't have base science to make good decisions."
CESR
2023 STAC report on the 40-year Bay nutrient-reduction effort.
Chesapeake Bay Watershed Agreement
Multi-jurisdictional voluntary commitment; 2014 baseline; 2025 revised; 2040 horizon. Distinct from federal TMDL (legally enforceable EPA mechanism).
CBP
Chesapeake Bay Program (federal-state partnership coordinating Bay restoration).
Cherry-picks
Colden's distinctive characterization of regulatory selective implementation (DNR's OysterFutures management plan). "Cherry-picks recommendations most favored by watermen while ignoring or glossing over others."
ERPs (Ecological Reference Points)
ASMFC's 2017-2020 collaborative framework replacing single-species management with ecosystem-based modeling for menhaden (Moore was a participant).
Foreshore-adaptation framing
CBF's calibrated framing for Tangier work; not climate-rhetoric-forward; uses erosion / shoreline / adaptation vocabulary politically tractable in the Tangier community.
Industrial reduction fishery
Converts menhaden into fish oil + animal feed (Omega Protein / Reedville). Distinct from bait fishery (smaller, commercial-bait, also in VA).
Forage fish
For menhaden; ecological-role framing emphasizing menhaden's role as prey base for stripers, ospreys, etc.
Foreign-owned
Moore's deliberate framing on Omega Protein (Cooke Inc., Canadian parent). Pointed populist register; CBF uses it.
OAC (MD Oyster Advisory Commission)
Appointed advisory body; Colden serves.
Omega Protein
Reedville, VA-based industrial menhaden reduction company; ~260 employees; Cooke Inc. Canadian parent. Counter-coalition to CBF on menhaden.
PSFA (Public Shellfish Fishery Areas)
MD wild-harvest areas (vs. sanctuaries vs. aquaculture leases). 2020 PSFA proposal CBF opposed ("undermines the spirit of the law").
Rockfish / striped bass
Both in active use; do not "correct" between them. CBF uses both interchangeably.
Sanctuary (tributary-scale oyster restoration)
MD's five tributary-scale sanctuaries: Harris Creek, Little Choptank, Tred Avon, St. Mary's, Manokin (all completed 2025). VA equivalents at 10-tributary scale completing 2025: Lafayette, Piankatank, Great Wicomico, Lynnhaven, Lower York, Eastern Branch of Elizabeth + others. Don't refer as "MPAs."
STAC
Scientific and Technical Advisory Committee (CBP).
TMDL
Total Maximum Daily Load; legally enforceable EPA pollution-reduction mechanism.
VMRC
Virginia Marine Resources Commission. VA-state-level fisheries management.
