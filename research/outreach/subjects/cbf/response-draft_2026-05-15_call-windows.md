## Author review notes (v6)

### Core reframe (v1 → v6)

v1 framed the call as a live framework-translation pass — *"easiest if I do the translation work live; the two terms (cost severance and residual commons value) are short to gloss out loud."* That kept Val + David in the position of evaluating economic vocabulary they had explicitly disclaimed competence on (*"we are not economists"*). v6 reframes the call as **expertise-respecting** — they advise on routing + introductions + updates from a position of competence (CBF Comms managers who handle their EDs daily and write CBF's external messaging), while the framework material gets handled with Allison / Chris later if and when an interview routes there.

### What v6 does differently

- **Drops all framework vocabulary from the email.** No *cost severance*, no *residual commons value*, no *framework*. The chapter's central claim — that the Chesapeake Bay's seafood industries have repeatedly fished species past the point of renewability, effectively turning what was a renewable resource into a non-renewable one, with fishing communities and the Bay's ecosystem absorbing the cost — is said in plain English when it appears.
- **Opens by honestly scoping the public-record vs. live-conversation tradeoff.** The opener — *"the public record already covers most of what the chapter would actually quote"* — signals the homework is done and de-risks the gatekeeper decision (Val + David don't have to vouch for the framework's worthiness to recommend their EDs).
- **De-risks the ED-interview ask.** The Routing bullet offers a lighter exchange ("getting their input on how we speak about [the central claim]") as an explicit alternative if scheduling is tight. Framing the lighter touch as *scheduling accommodation* (not "we don't really need the full interview") makes it warm and easy for Val + David to recommend.
- **Promotes Watermen routes to load-bearing ask.** The chapter's outline names the generational arc (grandfathers / fathers / children) as the temporal spine — so "families have worked the Bay for generations" matches the chapter need rather than gatekeeping. Pairs with "livelihood affected by overfishing" to specify the cost-bearing criterion. Geographies dropped — Val and David know the territory.
- **Replaces "engagement advice" with "Updates."** Original "what to be careful about / what's been mischaracterized in print before" framing positioned Val + David as PR risk-warners; v6 keeps just the chapter-impact piece — where their thinking has shifted since the public record — which is a genuine pre-publication-quality ask.
- **Drops the pre-read offer entirely.** v1 offered the one-page framework summary; v6 doesn't. *"No prep needed"* is sharper now because there really isn't anything they need to read — the call serves the three concrete asks.
- **Time-ask softened to ~20–30 minutes.** v1 said 30; v6 acknowledges the call may run shorter.
