### "Why bonds — and not funds, accounts, trusts, or reserves?" — LOAD-BEARING

**[Most-likely high-stakes pivot. Have at the fingertips. Going sideways here harms framework credibility.]**

> *"The framework chose 'Bond' deliberately over alternatives — Account, Reserve, Fund, Trust — for three structural reasons. First, real-world precedent: reclamation bonds, environmental performance bonds, and surety bonds are existing legal instruments with a century-plus regulatory track record; the framework extends established practice rather than inventing a new vehicle. Second, legal-adoption-travel: the framework's success criterion is vocabulary adoption in regulatory contexts, and 'Bond' already lives there — lawyers and policymakers recognize the structural properties immediately. Third, role-encoding precision: 'Bond' specifies an amount posted against an obligation, returned if conditions are met, forfeited if they aren't — that matches Cost Severance equals RCV minus B cleanly. Account is too generic, Reserve implies one-sided holding, Fund implies general pooling, Trust carries fiduciary connotation but not the conditional-return structure."*

### "How does this add to Hotelling?"

> *"Hotelling 1931 modeled scarcity rents for exhaustible resources assuming efficient pricing — the math is right. The framework's claim is that the inputs have been wrong: the scarcity rent in Hotelling's sense excludes the cost of permanent option-value foreclosure on future generations, which is what RCV names. The framework's contribution is an accounting instrument that puts intergenerational option-value-loss inside the scarcity rent rather than outside it — making Hotelling's math operate on the right inputs."*

If she wants depth: bibliography §18.6 (Hotelling, Hartwick, Solow, Pearce-Atkinson, Ramsey).

### "How does this extend Ostrom?"

> *"Ostrom 1990 showed that commons can be governed without state or market through institutional design — but her design principles work specifically for commons that can be managed in sustained-yield perpetuity. The framework extends to commons that can't be managed that way: non-renewable resources from the start, and renewable resources pushed past regenerative capacity. The two-instrument bond architecture is what operates where sustained-yield discipline can no longer apply — accountability-by-instrument for cases where accountability-by-management isn't possible."*

### "Where does Polanyi sit?"

> *"Polanyi's fictitious-commodities frame in *The Great Transformation* is the deep substrate the framework builds on. The framework's observation — that certain costs should not be transactable externalities but are treated as such — is a descendant of Polanyi's 'land, labor, money are not produced for market exchange' move. Cost severance is the contemporary mechanism that produces the crises Polanyi identified."*

### "How does this relate to Boyce?"

> *"Boyce at PERI works the intersection of ecological economics and reparations the framework also engages, particularly through *Economics for People and the Planet* and the carbon-dividend literature. Cost Severance Damages is presented as an analytic instrument; Boyce's work shapes how the framework imagines those instruments in distributional terms."*

### "How does this relate to Harvey / Klein / primitive accumulation?"

> *"Harvey's 'accumulation by dispossession' names the contemporary phenomenon — Marx's primitive accumulation operating now, in real time. Klein's shock doctrine names the political conditions under which it accelerates. The framework's contribution is naming the *mechanism* that produces the phenomenon Harvey describes: cost severance is the structural move by which value is extracted while costs are deliberately transferred. Harvey names what; the framework names how."*

### "How does this relate to reparations economics (Darity / Mullen / Hamilton-et-al)?"

> *"The *From Here to Equality* program (Darity & Mullen 2020) is the contemporary landmark for reparations economics, with the racial-wealth-gap-as-summary-statistic methodology operationalized via *Umbrellas Don't Make It Rain* (Hamilton, Darity, Price, Sridharan & Tippett 2015). The framework's *Restitution Bond* instrument is positioned in that lineage — a precision-add on backward-looking restitution as a financial-instrument form. The framework's contribution is the two-instrument decomposition: pairing the Restitution Bond (backward-looking) with the Foreclosure Bond (forward-looking) for harm yet to be realized."*

### "How does this relate to Raworth / Doughnut Economics?"

> *"Doughnut Economics (Raworth 2017; Amsterdam adoption 2020 onward) operates at the economy-system level — articulating where economies should aim and design principles for getting there. The framework operates at the actor-transaction level — naming the structural mechanism (cost severance) by which accountability fails, and pricing what's been foreclosed (RCV). The two are complementary, not competing: a Doughnut-organized economy can still have actor-level cost-severance failures within it, particularly imported / upstream cost severance from extraction zones outside the city's borders. Doughnut tells you where to aim; Commons Bonds tells you how to bind specific accountability moments along the way."*

### "Foreclosure Bond — isn't that license to extract?"

> *"The accounting equation does that work: B = RCV is the structural condition. If B is set to RCV, posting the bond IS internalizing the cost — any rational actor stops extracting before posting. If B is set below RCV, the framework explicitly names that as cost severance — exactly the misuse pattern we're pricing. So the bond framing isn't 'license to extract'; it's the instrument by which 'license to extract' becomes legible."*

- **Market-shaping (vs. fixing)** — Mazzucato's *Mission Economy* slogan — active state-led direction-setting, not market-failure correction after the fact.
- **Mission Economy** — Mazzucato 2021 — seven-pillar framework for organising public-private collaboration around time-bound, measurable missions (climate, health, etc.). Apollo program as model. Now official UK Labour framing under Reeves.
- **Mission Economy seven pillars** — (1) Value: collectively created; (2) Markets: shaping not fixing; (3) Organisations: dynamic capabilities; (4) Finance: outcomes-based budgeting; (5) Distribution: sharing risks AND rewards; (6) Partnership: stakeholder-value-driven; (7) Participation: open systems / citizen co-design.
- **Patient capital** — Mazzucato — long-horizon public investment willing to accept early-stage risk and slow returns. Distinct from concessional finance / soft money.
- **Pattern 2 register** — Framework's voice. Threaded demonstration of affordances through case treatment + lineage citation rather than codified-methodology section. Deliberate refusal of formula-economics / consultant-deliverable register. *Mission Economy* + *Doughnut Economics* are the framework's two cleanest contemporary exemplars.
- **Pattern 1 register** — Codified-methodology / how-to register; consultant-shaped; what *The Big Con* attacks. The register the framework refuses.
- **Purpose-washing** — Mazzucato Substack-coined cousin of greenwashing — performative purpose-orientation without conditional-return mechanism.
- **Renewable past regenerative capacity** — Framework's term for renewable resources extracted faster than they can regenerate. Once across the threshold, effectively converted to non-renewable; same RCV accounting applies.
- **Residual Commons Value (RCV)** — The worth permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource — or a renewable resource pushed past regenerative capacity — is drawn down. Framework's pricing-instrument target.
- **Restitution Bond (B₁)** — Framework's backward-looking accountability instrument — discharges harm already realized. Lineage: reparations-economics + restitution-tradition.
- **The Big Con** — Mazzucato + Collington 2023 — sustained empirical attack on the global consulting industry as structural-extractor of public-sector capacity. Three solutions: disclose conflicts; rebuild internal capacity; reform contract-outcome evaluation.
- **The Value of Everything** — Mazzucato 2018 — value-creation vs. value-extraction. **Bibliography §13 anchor cite for the framework — Ring-1 flagship.** Defining quote: *"activities focused on moving around existing resources and outputs, and gaining disproportionately from the ensuing trade."*
- **Two-instrument decomposition** — Framework's structural move: B = B₁ + B₂. Separates backward-looking restitution (Restitution Bond) from forward-looking foreclosure (Foreclosure Bond).
- **Value extraction** — Mazzucato 2018 — rent-seeking masquerading as value-creation; the diagnostic she developed. Distinct from *rent* (category) and from *financialisation* (master mechanism). The framework's *cost severance* is positioned as a precision-add on this term.
- **Why bonds?** — Framework canonical articulation: (1) real-world precedent (reclamation / environmental-performance / surety bonds); (2) legal-adoption-travel; (3) role-encoding precision (posted / returned / forfeited matches *Cost Severance = RCV − B*). Account too generic; Reserve one-sided; Fund pooled; Trust fiduciary-not-conditional. Highest-stakes pivot for this interview.
