## 2. Signature work + intellectual project

Mazzucato's project, across four landmark books and a forthcoming fifth, has been to **rebuild the moral and analytical foundations of how economies value, finance, and direct economic activity** — moving from a market-failure correcting state to a market-shaping, mission-driven public sector. Each book operates at a different layer of the same critique.

### *The Entrepreneurial State* (Anthem 2013; Penguin / PublicAffairs editions)

**Core move:** the canonical innovations of the 21st century — internet, GPS, touchscreen, voice recognition, chip lithography, the basic-research underpinnings of pharmaceuticals — were **publicly funded** at the high-risk early stages, then commercialised by private actors. Apple's iPhone is the headline case study: each of its core components originated from research efforts financed by US government agencies (DARPA, NSF, NIH, the CIA's In-Q-Tel) ([Wikipedia: The Entrepreneurial State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Entrepreneurial_State); [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/the-entrepreneurial-state-appl); [TIME 2015](https://time.com/4089171/mariana-mazzucato/)).

**The argument the framework should know.** Two layers:
1. *Empirical* — public investment is the actual upstream driver of high-risk innovation; the "private sector as risk-taker" story is mythologised.
2. *Normative* — risk-taking-by-public-sector should be paid for: if the state takes on the upside risk via patient capital, it should also be entitled to share in the upside via equity, royalties, conditional procurement, or direct revenue capture. The current arrangement **socialises risk and privatises reward**.

**The contested-cite caveat (load-bearing for the framework's positioning).** The iPhone case is **the most-attacked single empirical claim in Mazzucato's work**. Right-leaning critics (Alberto Mingardi at Cato; Nintil's multi-part series; Adam Smith Institute; Mises Institute) argue (a) her definition of "publicly-funded" is over-broad and treats indirect / unintended consequences of government R&D spending as direct entrepreneurial-state action; (b) the leap from "public funded the upstream science" to "public should own the upside" is unwarranted; (c) the *integration* into the iPhone form-factor — the entrepreneurial achievement — is private sector, not public ([Cato critique by Mingardi](https://www.cato.org/sites/cato.org/files/serials/files/cato-journal/2015/9/cj-v35n3-7.pdf); [Nintil iPhone series](https://nintil.com/mazzucato-and-the-iphone-i/); [UnHerd 2018](https://unherd.com/2018/06/mariana-mazzucato-wrong-entrepreneurial-state/)). **Implication for the author:** if you cite the iPhone case, do so via Mazzucato's *argument structure* (state-funded upstream R&D → privatised downstream capture) rather than via the specific component-by-component attribution; the latter has critique-vulnerability the former does not.

### *The Value of Everything: Making and Taking in the Global Economy* (Allen Lane / PublicAffairs 2018)

**Core move — load-bearing for the framework.** A history of how economics measures *value* (from François Quesnay's Physiocrats through Smith / Ricardo / Marx to neoclassical marginalism), arguing that the modern conflation of *price* with *value* allowed actors to **portray themselves as value-creators while in reality they were extracting** rents from existing value, moving it around, or destroying it ([PublicAffairs page](https://www.publicaffairsbooks.com/titles/mariana-mazzucato/the-value-of-everything/9781610396745/); [LSE Review of Books](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsereviewofbooks/2018/10/09/book-review-the-value-of-everything-making-and-taking-in-the-global-economy-by-mariana-mazzucato/)).

**Defining quote (canonical for the framework's lineage citation):** *"activities focused on moving around existing resources and outputs, and gaining disproportionately from the ensuing trade"* — this is the framework's primary cite-form for "value extraction" (see `tools/back-matter/sources/bibliography.md` §13 entry for *The Value of Everything*).

**Targets.** Asset managers; pharma patent rentiers; hedge funds; Silicon Valley platform monopolies; financial-sector bonus extraction. Mazzucato's diagnosis is **rent-seeking inside financial capitalism**, primarily intangible / financial / IP rents.

**This is the framework's anchor cite.** The bibliography entry rates it **LOAD-BEARING (Ring-1 flagship term "Value Extraction")** and notes the framework extends Mazzucato's diagnosis from rent-seeking-in-finance into the **physical-resource-extraction domain** — adding cost severance as the specific mechanism, the Accountability Bond + RCV as the pricing instrument, and an explicit causal chain (Value Extraction → Cost Severance → Severed Cost). See §7 for the precision-add analysis.

### *Mission Economy: A Moonshot Guide to Changing Capitalism* (Allen Lane / Harper Business 2021)

**Core move.** Building on the entrepreneurial-state argument, Mazzucato proposes that government should organise economic activity around **specific, measurable, time-bound missions** — modelled on the Apollo program (1968–1972) — that mobilise public-private collaboration toward goals like climate neutrality, clean oceans, healthy aging, etc. Markets should be **shaped, not merely fixed** ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/52745048-mission-economy); [LSE Review of Books](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsereviewofbooks/2021/04/01/book-review-mission-economy-a-moonshot-guide-to-changing-capitalism-by-mariana-mazzucato/); [Crooked Timber book chat](https://crookedtimber.org/2021/07/24/book-chat-mariana-mazzucato-mission-economy/)).

**The seven pillars of mission-oriented practice** (load-bearing taxonomy the framework should be able to recall):
1. **Value:** collectively created
2. **Markets:** shaping, not fixing (the most-quoted phrase)
3. **Organisations:** dynamic capabilities (developing real public-sector competence, not outsourcing)
4. **Finance:** outcomes-based budgeting (patient capital with mission alignment)
5. **Distribution:** sharing risks *and* rewards (the entrepreneurial-state move applied at the deal level)
6. **Partnership:** purpose- and stakeholder-value-driven, not shareholder-value-only
7. **Participation:** open systems, citizen co-design

**Critique to be aware of.** *Mission Economy* takes more flak than *Value of Everything* on whether (a) the Apollo analogy generalises (Apollo was a single technical mission with a clear physics target; climate, health, inequality are not analogous); (b) democratic accountability of mission-design is under-specified; (c) the political conditions for mission-setting (who sets the mission; against whose interests) are softened ([LSE Review](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsereviewofbooks/2021/04/01/book-review-mission-economy-a-moonshot-guide-to-changing-capitalism-by-mariana-mazzucato/); [Issues in Science and Technology](https://issues.org/review-mission-economy-mazzucato/); [SER review](https://academic.oup.com/ser/article-abstract/20/3/1501/6693675)). **Mazzucato has heard all of these and has continued to develop the framework rather than retreat from it.** The 2025–2026 IIPP working paper run (see §4) is largely a response to operationalising mission-thinking against these critiques.

### *The Big Con: How the Consulting Industry Weakens Our Businesses, Infantilizes Our Governments, and Warps Our Economies* (with **Rosie Collington**, Allen Lane / Penguin 2023)

**Core move.** A sustained empirical attack on the global consulting industry — McKinsey, BCG, Bain, PwC, Deloitte, KPMG, EY — arguing they (a) overclaim expertise; (b) cost more than they appear to; (c) systematically prevent governments and firms from developing **internal capabilities** (point 3 of the *Mission Economy* pillars); (d) operate with structural conflicts of interest, working simultaneously for governments-on-decarbonisation and fossil-fuel-major polluters (the case Mazzucato cites: McKinsey advising the Australian government on climate transition while holding contracts with 43 of Australia's biggest emitters) ([Wikipedia: Big Con](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Big_Con_(Mazzucato_and_Collington_book)); [LSE Review March 2024](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsereviewofbooks/2024/03/12/book-review-the-big-con-how-the-consulting-industry-weakens-our-businesses-infantilizes-our-governments-and-warps-our-economies-mariana-mazzucato-rosie-collington/); [American Prospect](https://prospect.org/culture/books/2023-03-06-big-consultings-new-con-mazzucato-collington-review/); [New Statesman interview Feb 2023](https://www.newstatesman.com/encounter/2023/02/mariana-mazzucato-consultancies-depend-weak-governments)).

**Three proposed solutions:** mandatory disclosure of consultancies' conflicts of interest; rebuild internal public-sector capabilities; reform contract-outcome evaluation.

**Recognition:** *The Big Con* was named one of *The Times*' five best business and technology books of 2023; FT and *Guardian* "to read" lists.

**Implication for the framework's outreach posture.** *The Big Con* is the freshest book on Mazzucato's desk in trade-book form (until *Common Good Economy* lands June 2026). **The author should not, under any circumstances, position himself as a consulting-industry voice or use consulting-shaped vocabulary** ("deliverables," "engagement," "stakeholder optimisation," "best practice frameworks"). The framework's voice — author-as-independent-thinker, threaded-demonstration register — is structurally aligned with what Mazzucato wants more of, not what she's spent two years critiquing. Lean into that alignment; don't muddy it.

### *The Common Good Economy: A New Compass* (Allen Lane / PublicAffairs **forthcoming, June 2026 UK / September 2026 US**)

**Core move.** A capstone synthesis. The book "issues a corrective to prevailing economic thinking by defining a new theory of economics with the common good at its center"; builds on *Entrepreneurial State* and *Mission Economy* into a comprehensive theory of how collective action, participation, and reciprocity should organise economic life. **Four interdependent principles:** justice, equality, sustainability, global solidarity. Provides a **"common good compass"** with concrete cases on water, procurement, finance ([Hachette page](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/mariana-mazzucato/the-common-good-economy/9781668659007/); [Penguin UK page](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/464576/the-common-good-economy-by-mazzucato-mariana/9780241722244); [Mazzucato's site](https://marianamazzucato.com/books/the-common-good-economy); [Substack: Why We Need An Economics Grounded In The Common Good](https://marianamazzucato.substack.com/p/why-we-need-an-economics-grounded)).

**Companion institutional vehicle.** The **Global Council for a Common Good Economy**, launched April 2026 in Barcelona with Spanish Deputy PM Carlos Cuerpo, is the live political-economy body operationalising the book's framework ([UCL Bartlett](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/news/2026/apr/prof-mariana-mazzucato-and-minister-carlos-cuerpo-launch-global-council-common-good-economy)).

**Critical implication for the framework.** *The Common Good Economy* is **the single freshest item on Mazzucato's desk in May 2026** — she is in active book-promotion mode in the months ahead. Any interview will likely be filtered through "does this connect to my Common Good framework?" before it's filtered through "does this connect to *Value of Everything*?" The framework should engage *both* lineages — *Value of Everything* (cost severance as precision-add on value extraction) and *Common Good Economy* (RCV / Foreclosure Bond as concrete instruments inside common-good market-shaping). Showing awareness of the forthcoming book signals a respectful read of her current attention.

## 3. Stance on key issues

### Public value + value-extraction
**Foundational.** Public value is not what markets price; it is what societies need. Value-extraction (rent-seeking masquerading as value-creation) is the inverse failure mode. The two-by-two — public value created vs. extracted; private value created vs. extracted — is the analytical scaffolding. The framework's *cost severance* lives inside her **value-extraction** quadrant but specifies the mechanism with greater precision (see §7).

### Mission-oriented innovation policy
The state should set ambitious, time-bound, measurable missions, then orchestrate public-private collaboration toward them. Markets are **shaped** (in the direction of the mission) not just **fixed** (correcting market failures after the fact). This is now the official UK Labour government framing under Rachel Reeves ([UCL Bartlett Sept 2024](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/news/2024/sep/professor-mazzucato-welcomes-chancellors-active-government-commitment-and-labours-five-missions); [IPPR essay on Labour missions](https://www.ippr.org/articles/why-we-need-missions-to-drive-economic-renewal)).

### Climate finance + green industrial strategy
Reform of international finance, framed around **market-shaping for the common good**. The SDG financing gap has grown from $2.5T pre-COVID to $3.9–7T today; current "blended finance" approaches (de-risking private capital for development goals) are insufficient and structurally biased. She advocates **patient public capital + conditionalities + mission alignment** instead. Recent work (2025): "Mind the Mission, Not the Gap — Rethinking blended finance for public purpose" (IIPP WP 2025-09); "Reimagining financing for the SDGs" (UN DESA Policy Brief No. 170, Jan 2025); "Mission-Oriented Country Platforms: Engines for a just green transition" (IIPP WP 2025-20, Nov 2025) ([UCL publications page](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/publications/2025/nov/mission-oriented-country-platforms-engines-just-green-transition); [UN DESA Voice Jan 2025](https://desapublications.un.org/un-desa-voice/sdg-blog/january-2025/blended-finance-not-working-it-time-new-approach-mobilizing)).

### Pharmaceutical pricing / public-private partnerships
Where the **risk-reward asymmetry is most visible**. Public funds basic research → private pharma captures patents and prices to extract → public pays again for the drug. Demands: conditionalities at the procurement / IP / pricing layer; equity stakes in upside; price-discrimination prohibition for public-funded drugs. *People's Prescription* report (IIPP 2018) is the canonical statement; **the Oct 2025 IIPP WP 2025-18 with Stratton + Williams-Eliyesil on the Oxford-AstraZeneca COVID vaccine** ([UCL publication](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/publications/2025/oct/mission-oriented-public-private-partnerships-lessons-oxford-astrazeneca-covid-19-vaccine)) is the freshest version of the argument — taxonomising conditionalities that worked (early commitment + non-refundable grants + reasonable global pricing + storable-in-fridge access requirement) versus those that didn't.

### Big tech regulation / AI
Coined **"digital feudalism"** in 2019; has carried it through 2025. Key argument: AI as a general-purpose technology is at an inflection point; without market-shaping public action it will recapitulate the rent-extraction model of the platform economy, with "algorithmic rents" as the new mechanism. Advocates **EuroStack** (independent European public-good digital infrastructure), antitrust on AI foundation-model layer, public-purpose conditionalities on AI procurement ([Project Syndicate Feb 2025: Resisting Digital Feudalism](https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/ai-digital-feudalism-inevitable-unless-policymakers-step-in-to-shape-markets-by-mariana-mazzucato-2025-02); [UCL News March 2025](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/news/2025/mar/comment-resisting-digital-feudalism); [Substack: AI for What?](https://marianamazzucato.substack.com/p/ai-for-what)).

### Consulting-industry capture
*The Big Con* (2023) is the canonical statement. Position: consulting industry is structurally extractive — rents off the **erosion of public-sector capacity** that it then offers to repair, infinitely. Three solutions (disclose conflicts; rebuild internal capacity; reform outcome-evaluation) are her policy ask. **She has continued to advocate publicly through 2024–2025** (FT op-eds; UCL News August 2024 "Ending the Consultancy Cult" ([UCL News](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/news/2024/aug/analysis-ending-consultancy-cult); [New Statesman Aug 2024](https://www.newstatesman.com/comment/2024/08/ending-the-consultancy-cult)).

### Financialization
Treated across all five books as the master mechanism by which **value extraction overwhelms value creation** — share buybacks; financial-sector rent capture; financialised non-financial firms (Apple, GE in its decline phase); private-equity asset-stripping. She is firmly in the *Minsky-Keynes-Schumpeter* synthesis tradition, with debt to **Carlota Perez** on technological-revolution-and-financial-bubble dynamics.

## 7. Where the framework intersects Mazzucato's work

This is the load-bearing section.

### A. *Cost severance* as precision-add on *value extraction* — does it do analytical work or duplicate?

**Framework's positioning.** The framework's bibliography entry for *The Value of Everything* (`tools/back-matter/sources/bibliography.md` §13) and the canonical articulation in `alignment/commons_bonds_framework_positioning_disciplines_v1.0.0.md` §"Mazzucato (value-creation / value-extraction)" both position **cost severance as a precision-add on value extraction**, not a replacement:

> *Mazzucato's distinction between value-creation and value-extraction in The Value of Everything is the clearest articulation of what economics has been refusing to name precisely. Cost severance is positioned as a precision-add on her value-extraction vocabulary — value-extraction is what the actor does; cost severance is what the actor does to the costs of doing it. The framework operationalizes the value-extraction frame at the transaction level by giving it a pricing instrument and a structural-mechanism vocabulary.*

**The analytical work cost severance does that value extraction does not (claim).**
1. **Mazzucato's value-extraction is primarily focused on rent-seeking in financial / IP / platform capitalism** — the asset manager, the patent rentier, the share-buyback CEO. The framework extends to **physical-resource extraction** (McDowell County coal; Libby asbestos; Niger Delta oil; opioid-Appalachia; Norway oil) where the extractive actor and the cost-bearer are spatially / temporally / generationally separated.
2. **Cost severance specifies the mechanism.** Value extraction names *what the actor does to value*; cost severance names *what the actor does to the costs* — a complementary and more specific structural move. The two together permit a **causal chain**: Value Extraction → Cost Severance → Severed Cost → Foreclosed Future.
3. **Cost severance is operationalisable at the transaction level.** Mazzucato's value-extraction works as a moral-political indictment; cost severance is **deal-evaluable** — you can ask of any specific transaction, "is this a cost-severance event? if so, what is severed, to whom, over what time horizon?" The Foreclosure Bond is the instrument that prices the answer.

**The honest risk this section addresses: does cost severance duplicate value extraction?** Plausibly partial overlap. *Mazzucato might reasonably reply*: "rent-seeking and value extraction in my usage already covers what you're naming as cost severance; you're putting new vocabulary on existing analytical territory." The framework's response is the three-part claim above — value extraction names the *what*, cost severance names the *how*, and cost severance generalises the diagnosis from the financial-rent domain to the physical-resource-extraction domain. **The interview is the place to test whether this distinction lands or whether she sees it as duplication.**

### B. *RCV* (Residual Commons Value) as instrument-pricing-target

**Adjacency.** Mazzucato has not developed a vocabulary for the **value permanently foreclosed when a non-renewable resource is liquidated**. Her work prices **rent capture** but not **option-value foreclosure** in the Hotelling / Hartwick / intergenerational-equity sense. RCV is the framework's contribution to that gap — a pricing target that a Foreclosure Bond can be sized against.

**Likely Mazzucato response.** Her 2024 Water Commission report and 2025 Country Platforms paper are her two closest analytical neighbours to RCV — the water work because it explicitly frames water as a **global common good with transboundary cost-severance** (atmospheric-cycle dimension); the country-platforms work because it tries to operationalise mission-aligned green transition at the country-deal level. **The interview should probe whether RCV reads as a useful pricing primitive inside her common-good market-shaping framework, or whether it imports too much neoclassical-pricing apparatus into a framework she's deliberately keeping qualitative.**

### C. *Foreclosure Bond / Restitution Bond* as forward-looking and backward-looking accountability instruments

**Where this maps onto Mission Economy.** *Mission Economy*'s seven pillars include **Distribution (sharing risks and rewards)** and **Finance (outcomes-based budgeting)** — both of which are operationalised at the *deal-shaping* level. The framework's two-instrument decomposition is **deal-evaluation tools** for missions whose downstream cost-severance has not been priced into the deal terms. Specifically:
- **Foreclosure Bond** prices *forward-looking* foreclosed-future cost in any extraction-or-foreclosure deal. Inside a Mission Economy framing, it is the instrument that makes the "share rewards proportionally" pillar bind: if the public is taking on the risk-of-foreclosure, the deal must price what's being foreclosed.
- **Restitution Bond** is the *backward-looking* counterpart — for harm already realised. Maps to the conditionality / recapture work but extended into reparations-economics territory.

**Why this pairing matters for the Mazzucato register.** Her Mission Economy framework articulates the *desideratum* (share risks and rewards; build mission-aligned partnerships) but is thinner on the *binding instrumentation* — what specific contractual / accounting / pricing forms make the desideratum bind. The Commons Bonds two-instrument decomposition is one possible **operationalisation layer**. Whether she sees it as her own framework's natural extension or as a competing apparatus is the interview's question. Most generative outcome: she sees them as **complementary at different scales** (Mission Economy organises the system; Commons Bonds prices specific deals within it), parallel to the Doughnut Economics / Commons Bonds complementarity articulated in §"Raworth / Doughnut Economics" of the framework-positioning doc.

### D. Pattern 2 register exemplar — the framework's use of Mazzucato as positioning lineage

**The framework treats Mazzucato as the #1 Pattern 2 register exemplar** alongside Raworth (Doughnut Economics) and Bollier (*Mine!*). Pattern 2 register, per `commons_bonds_working_principles_v1.0.0.md` WP#10 and the May 2026 v1.49.0 / v1.50.0 session handoffs, is:
- **Threaded demonstration** — framework affordances demonstrated through case treatment + lineage citation rather than codified in a separate methodology section.
- **Not Pattern 1** — Pattern 1 is codified-methodology / how-to register (consultant-shaped; what *The Big Con* attacks).
- **Deliberate refusal of formula-economics** — the analytical work is done at the level of historically-specific cases, narrative threading, and concept-precision.
- *Mission Economy* and *Doughnut Economics* are named as the cleanest contemporary Pattern 2 exemplars in the live literature.

**Implication for outreach voice.** The author's interview voice should **demonstrate** Pattern 2 register rather than describe it. Specific moves:
- Lead with case-specificity (McDowell County, Libby, Niger Delta, Amsterdam) before framework-naming.
- Cite *The Value of Everything* for vocabulary; *Mission Economy* for institutional architecture; *The Big Con* to signal what the project is *not* (consultant-register).
- Avoid "deliverables / frameworks-as-product / methodologies as content" register absolutely. **Mazzucato will read consultancy-shaped vocabulary as the failure mode she's spent years critiquing.**

### E. Specific framework moments where her vocabulary lands or misses

**Lands cleanly:**
- Cost severance as descendant of value extraction (the bibliography's anchor-cite is correct).
- Foreclosure Bond as instrumentalisation of the "share risks AND rewards" Mission Economy pillar.
- The framework's case-set (McDowell, Libby, Niger Delta, Norway, Amsterdam) is exactly the domain where her *value extraction* vocabulary naturally extends — physical-resource extraction with cost-bearer / value-capturer separation.

**Lands with friction:**
- The framework's *Hotelling-rooted* RCV apparatus may read as too neoclassical-pricing-formal for her qualitative register. Watch the framing: present RCV as a **structural pricing primitive**, not as a Hotelling-derived equation.
- The "intergenerational option-value" language is closer to Sen / Anderson capability-and-commitment register than to her market-shaping register. May need translation.

**Could miss:**
- If the framework's instruments are framed as *technocratic deal-engineering tools* (consultant-shaped), they will read against her *Big Con* critique. They should be framed as **public-purpose accountability instruments** with civil-society / democratic-deliberation surface area.
- If the framework over-emphasises **bonds** as the financial form, she may probe whether the bond-form is itself a financialised vehicle that recapitulates the rentier dynamic. The framework's response (per `commons_bonds_framework_positioning_disciplines_v1.0.0.md` §"Why bonds (and not funds, accounts, trusts, or reserves)?") needs to be at the author's fingertips.
