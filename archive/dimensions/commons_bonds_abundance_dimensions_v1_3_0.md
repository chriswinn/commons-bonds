## 1. CIT positioning + commons-as-structural-identity (v1.4.0 reframing of C9 patch §3)

The Commons Inversion Test (CIT; renamed from AIT 2026-04-24) is the framework's epistemic core. **Commons categories are organizational scaffolding around the structural primitive (Commons), not load-bearing structure themselves.** Category additions, mergers, or renames are organizational revisions, not framework-integrity events. The CIT operates on any extraction–commons relationship independently of a containing list of commons categories — its validity is a property of the extraction–commons relationship itself, not of any particular set of commons categories.

**Commons as structural identity (v1.4.0 ratified):** The framework is a commons-governance theory extended to extraction. The structural primitive is *Commons* (something held collectively, with claim-bearers whose claims extraction can deny / capture / consume / extinguish). "Abundance" and "scarcity" are properties of the relationship between an extractor and a commons (commons-relative-abundance enables commons-consumption-without-immediate-cost-pricing; commons-relative-scarcity prices what the relationship costs). They are NOT primitive variable types in their own right — that was the v1.3.0 Path F framing, now superseded.

CIT has two sub-forms (per the AIT→CIT rename rigor pass):
- **Commons-Absence Inversion** — would the extraction price differently if there were no commons claim at all? (Tests whether the cost being severed is a commons-extraction-cost, vs. private-property externality.)
- **Commons-Consumption Inversion** — would the extraction price differently if the commons were inexhaustible / self-restoring at zero marginal cost? (Tests whether the asymmetry is *consumption-of-finite-commons*, vs. coordination of mutually-acceptable use.)

The §2.N "What becomes visible under CIT inversion" subsections per category are commons-consumption-inversion worked-examples specifically.

Commons categories provide four services that CIT alone does not:
- **Systematic coverage** — ensuring no structural commons-extraction asymmetry is invisible for lack of a category to see it through.
- **Vocabulary portability** — giving the framework's key terms labels that travel (Harvey / Klein / Raworth compression test).
- **Pedagogical shape** — letting the book introduce the framework one commons category at a time.
- **Scholarship seed points** — giving future work citeable footholds per category.

When tension arises between category clarity and CIT rigor, CIT wins. Category revisions are cheap; CIT revisions are structural. The current 10-category set is presented as **examples surfaced across the 18 cases examined in this book's research, not a canonical / complete enumeration of commons** (Option C' political-philosophical-accommodation discipline).

### 2.1 Habitability *(locked 2026-04-22)*

**Summary.** Habitability names the shared-atmospheric-medium / habitable-substrate dimension: breathable air, climate-stable substrate, atmospheric-ecosystem preconditions. The dimension prices structural asymmetries by which capture-parties externalize respiratory, climate, and substrate-degradation cost onto fenceline, downwind, future, and substrate-dependent populations. Locked 2026-04-22 (was "Atmospheric") to resolve multi-scale × 2-environments failure — life-support / engineered-habitat off-Earth contexts now read naturally as "habitability cost."

**What becomes visible under CIT inversion.** Under abundance of breathable, stable atmospheric medium (air self-restoring; emissions harmlessly dissipating; climate stable regardless of combustion), the refinery still operates and earns revenue — but the respiratory and climate cost evaporates. Asthma rates level to baseline; property values recover; climate-foreclosure on fenceline children's futures disappears. The decisive observation: *extraction doesn't stop, but cost-pricing reprices immediately* because the capture depended on the air-shield evaporating cost out of view.

**Case cluster.** Occupational respiratory exposure (coal miners' pneumoconiosis, foundry workers, chemical-plant exposures); fenceline-community air quality (Louisiana Cancer Alley, Houston Ship Channel, Los Angeles refinery corridors); emissions-led climate foreclosure (carbon externalities; overlapping with Ecosystem on climate content); indoor-air-quality in aged housing (lead paint, VOC off-gassing, asbestos remediation); smog / particulate concentration in dense urban zones; off-Earth life-support contexts (space-station air quality, Mars-colony atmospheric engineering — pedagogically load-bearing for Ch. 7).

**Scope boundary and other observations.**
- **Nested-under-Habitability framing for Ecosystem** (§2.6) — habitable substrate is the precondition for ecosystem services. Nested relationship explicit in both dimension intros.
- **Overlap with Ecosystem at climate** is real but resolvable: Habitability is air-as-medium (what is breathed), Ecosystem is services-from-biosphere (what the place provides). Case-level assignment depends on which is the primary asymmetry.
- **Off-Earth expansion is intentional** — the name carries the dimension into life-support / engineered-habitat contexts; pedagogically load-bearing for Ch. 7 asteroid-miner material and framework universality claim per protocol §5.4.

**Relevant literature.**
- *Environmental justice / fenceline:* Bullard, *Dumping in Dixie* (1990); Pellow, *Resisting Global Toxics* (2007).
- *Climate science / atmospheric:* IPCC Assessment Reports; Hansen, *Storms of My Grandchildren* (2009); Mann, *The Hockey Stick and the Climate Wars* (2012).
- *Economic externalities:* Pigou, *The Economics of Welfare* (1920); Coase, "The Problem of Social Cost" (1960, counter-framing); Stern, *The Stern Review on the Economics of Climate Change* (2006); Nordhaus, *The Climate Casino* (2013).
- *Occupational health:* Derickson, *Black Lung* (1998); Ashford, *Crisis in the Workplace* (1976).

**What's uniquely priced vs. adjacent dimensions.** Habitability prices shared-medium degradation (air / climate-substrate). Ecosystem (§2.6) prices biosphere-services loss (pollination, hydrology, soil fertility) and is nested under Habitability. Spatial (§2.2) prices place-specificity and immobility — the fenceline community's inability to relocate. Strip Habitability alone: the refinery can still contaminate water tables (Ecosystem active) and residents still can't afford to leave (Spatial active), but respiratory and climate cost specifically vanishes — isolating the other dimensions' contributions to the same case.

**Naming status.** LOCKED 2026-04-22 per Priority-1 cohort (was "Atmospheric, rename pending"). "Habitability" resolves Test 20/23 compression failure of "Atmospheric cost" and strengthens off-Earth multi-scale coverage. "Life-support" was a prior candidate disqualified under abundance-naming pattern review.

### 2.2 Spatial *(locked 2026-04-22)*

**Summary.** Spatial names the location-as-scarcity dimension: place-specificity, distance-as-cost, stranded-asset constraint, immobility-as-bargaining-shield. The dimension prices the structural asymmetry that immobility imposes on cost-bearers vs. mobility-capable capturers. Locked 2026-04-22 (was "Geographic") to resolve the multi-scale × off-Earth universality failure documented in the v1.0.0 layer/tier stress test.

**What becomes visible under CIT inversion.** Under abundance of place-flexibility (frictionless relocation; no stranded-asset cost; all locations equally accessible at zero cost), stranded populations can walk away from extractive terms; housing-cost concentrations collapse; company-town bargaining asymmetries evaporate because workers exit; climate-migration-foreclosed populations move freely before impact accumulates; off-Earth contract-coercion relies on return-trip-cost which evaporates. *The extraction doesn't stop* — firms still extract — but the cost-bearing terms must be re-priced because no cost-bearing population is trapped.

**Case cluster.** Appalachian coal sacrifice zones (McDowell County canonical); housing-affordability concentration metros (SF, NYC, Seattle); infrastructure deserts; climate-migration foreclosure; indigenous displacement (place-specificity component — identity-component routes Kindred); H-2A visa place-employer-binding; off-Earth long-duration contracts (asteroid-mining, lunar base, orbital-habitat); gig-economy employer-region-lock-in; company-town historical cases.

**Scope boundary and other observations.**
- **Off-Earth universality is the decisive rename driver.** "Geographic" presupposes planetary-surface referent; "Spatial" travels as off-Earth becomes less speculative. For a framework meant to last 50+ years, this durability matters.
- **Kindred co-occurrence is common but independence holds.** Appalachian case activates Spatial + Kindred (place-specific identity bonds); bilateral independence preserved through cases like migrant-worker H-2A (low-Kindred-to-place, high Spatial) and fully-relocated diaspora (high Kindred, low Spatial).
- **Optional prose bridge** in Earth-focused chapter openings to ease readers into the abstract register: "what we are about to call the spatial cost, and what Appalachian readers know as the geography of the trap."

**Relevant literature.**
- *Economic geography:* Krugman, *Geography and Trade* (1991) — Nobel Prize groundwork.
- *Spatial justice:* Soja, *Seeking Spatial Justice* (2010); Harvey, "Spatial Fix" (2001).
- *Environmental justice:* Bullard, *Dumping in Dixie* (1990) — also Habitability ancestor.
- *Commons governance:* Ostrom, *Governing the Commons* (1990) — commons are always spatial.
- *Housing / urban:* Jacobs, *Death and Life of Great American Cities* (1961) — crosses into Cohesion.

**What's uniquely priced vs. adjacent dimensions.** Spatial prices the structural bargaining-asymmetry of immobility itself. Habitability (§2.1) prices what-is-breathed-in-place; Ecosystem (§2.6) prices what-the-place-provides. Kindred (§2.5) prices identity-deep bonds to place as non-substitutable attachment; Institutional (§2.4) prices company-town governance. Strip Spatial alone: hierarchy still exists (but workers leave it), attachment still matters (Kindred), medium still degrades (Habitability), services still collapse (Ecosystem) — but *extractive terms collapse* because immobility no longer bargains them in.

**Naming status.** LOCKED 2026-04-22 per Priority-2 cohort (was "Geographic, rename pending"). "Spatial" resolves off-Earth multi-scale failure and aligns with Temporal as parallel abstract-axis primitive.

**Pre-drafting items:** dimension-intro off-Earth resolution passage; optional Earth-register bridge prose in chapter openings.

### 2.3 Temporal *(stable)*

**Summary.** Temporal names the time-separation dimension: intergenerational cost-shifting, deferred-vs-present allocation asymmetry, compounding effects over decades. The dimension prices the structural asymmetry by which present captures and future (future-generation OR future-self) bears cost. The canonical mechanism is consent-normalization applied temporally — "you agreed to this" treated as closing the accounting even as cost accumulates across years or generations.

**What becomes visible under CIT inversion.** Under abundance of temporal-cost-visibility (no separation between present and future allocation; future-self and future-generation bear cost in visible present-terms), the 28-year-old feels the 68-year-old's cost now. The firm that captured present-value by deferring cost to future workers, future retirees, future generations, or future-self-as-aged cannot price the deal as it did. Retirement structures reprice; climate externalities become immediately costly; deferred-maintenance strategies collapse.

**Case cluster.** Pension / retirement cost-shifting (defined-benefit → defined-contribution shift as canonical mid-20th-through-early-21st-century case); climate as temporal cost (carbon externalities borne by future generations); deferred maintenance / infrastructure (US bridge-and-water-main underinvestment); 120+ hour-week consent-normalization case (canonical for the shield type; cross-dimension with Autonomy); early-career trade-off compounding (partnership-track law-firm / consulting / finance / medical residency); generational wealth-transfer asymmetries; inherited environmental debts; Social Security defunding patterns; intergenerational labor / care cost-shifting within families; climate-policy temporal discounting; **healthcare-cost deferral patterns** (Affordable Care Act cost-structure shifting from employer-group to individual-market over time; long-term-care financing gaps absorbed by family caregivers across decades; employer-based-insurance aging-population dynamics where younger-worker premiums subsidize older-worker claims with eventual rebalancing; Medicare Part D "donut hole" temporal cost-shifting patterns).

**Scope boundary and other observations.**
- **Strongest Goal-2 direct-contribution dimension** — individual readers can price their own temporal tier immediately without requiring collective or institutional context. The 120-hour-week framing reads to the individual-reader instantly.
- **Cross-dimension with Autonomy** — the 120-hour-week case activates both (coerced-in-present + costly-in-future). The canonical consent-normalization shield (v1.2.1 §5.2) operates temporally in these cases.
- **Cross-dimension with Institutional** at pension-governance cases — Institutional prices hierarchy governing the pension; Temporal prices the time-separation that makes cost-shifting possible.
- **Stable name** — no naming pressure across any test.

**Relevant literature.**
- *Intergenerational ethics:* Parfit, *Reasons and Persons* (1984); Gosseries, *Theories of Intergenerational Justice* (2009).
- *Discount-rate theory:* Stern, *The Stern Review* (2006) vs. Nordhaus, *Climate Casino* (2013); Weitzman, "Why the Far-Distant Future Should Be Discounted at Its Lowest Possible Rate" (1998).
- *Pension economics:* Ghilarducci, *When I'm Sixty-Four* (2008); Munnell & Sass, *Working Longer* (2008).
- *Climate as temporal cost:* Gardiner, *A Perfect Moral Storm* (2011); Jamieson, *Reason in a Dark Time* (2014).
- *Labor and time:* Schor, *The Overworked American* (1991); Jacobs & Gerson, *The Time Divide* (2004).

**What's uniquely priced vs. adjacent dimensions.** Temporal prices time-separation as shield — future-bearer cannot advocate for itself. Autonomy (§2.10) prices coercion-structure of the choice; Temporal prices the futurity of the cost regardless of whether the choice was coerced. Institutional (§2.4) prices hierarchy — Temporal cost-shifting occurs within AND without hierarchy (pension mismanagement inside firms AND climate externalities outside them). Strip Temporal alone: the 120-hour-week case remains coercive (Autonomy active) but loses its temporal asymmetry — the immediate health cost makes the deal reprice now.

**Naming status.** Stable. No rename. Compresses cleanly on Tests 20/23. Strongest Goal-2 direct-contribution of any dimension.

### 2.4 Institutional *(stable)*

**Summary.** Institutional names the organizational-coordination / hierarchical-position / collective-action-infrastructure dimension: cost-asymmetries generated by organizational structure itself — who has veto, who has routing authority, who has information-gatekeeping position, who bears cost from decisions made in rooms unobservable to them. The dimension prices the shield generated by institutional opacity.

**What becomes visible under CIT inversion.** Under abundance of organizational transparency (no hierarchy-generated selective access; no up-or-out; no position-based information gatekeeping; every party's access to decision inputs and outputs equivalent), the junior associate's contradicting data reaches the client directly. Clients receive uncorrupted analysis. Markets price consulting accurately. Non-profits and universities cannot shield administrative decisions from affected members. Regulatory capture by organizational-insiders-with-routing-authority loses its mechanism.

**Case cluster.** Corporate hierarchy under up-or-out dynamics (consulting / finance / law-firm); company-town historical cases (McDowell County, Pullman, Bisbee); CEO-era organizational decisions with subordinate cost-bearing (canonical territory for this project's author-specific material); NGO / university governance opacity; research-foundation gatekeeping; hospital administrative authority vs. nurse / clinician judgment; consulting-firm partner-associate dynamics; audit-firm client-capture patterns (Arthur Andersen–Enron); law-firm associate-exploitation patterns; **tech-platform governance as distinct hierarchy-type** (platform power exercised over users and independent contractors rather than traditional employees; algorithmic management of gig workers where the "hierarchy" is partially automated; employee-as-product data extraction where the user is the hierarchical subordinate even absent an employment relationship; cross-dimension with Autonomy for the algorithmic-coercion component and with Epistemic for the training-data and model-behavior opacity component).

**Scope boundary and other observations.**
- **Stable name** — no naming pressure. "Institutional cost" reads clean.
- **CEO-era case content is load-bearing and exposes the author via Test 13** (career-risk via professional-community exposure) at the secondary level per v1.2.1 §7 correction. Pre-drafting gates (NDA review, subordinate anonymization) address this at case level.
- **Overlap with Political at state-institutions boundary** — state institutions in organizational mode (NASA operations, agency administration) are Institutional; in distributional mode (regulatory capture, legislative allocation) are Political.
- **Overlap with Autonomy at employment-relationship cases** — Institutional prices the hierarchy, Autonomy prices the coercion. Company-town activates both.

## 5. Methodology notes from the Part 2 pass

### 5.1 Canonical cases define a dimension's pressure profile

A dimension's canonical cases are not illustrations attached to an abstract definition — they are what the dimension means in practice. Career-risk, legal exposure, and professional-retaliation pressures route through specific dimensions via their canonical cases, not through the dimension abstraction.

Empirical confirmation from Part 2:
- Epistemic's canonical cases (tobacco / pharma / opioid) make it the primary Test 13 career-risk load-bearer.
- Political's canonical cases (regulatory capture in healthcare financing) make it the secondary Test 13 carrier.
- Institutional's canonical cases (CEO-era content) make it a tertiary Test 13 case-level concern.
- The other seven dimensions are low on Test 13 at dimension level because their canonical cases do not involve industries with professional-retaliation capacity.

Further confirmation from the Kindred lock pass: the Kindred dimension's chosen-family scope surfaces only because the canonical-cases set was extended to include chosen-family cases; the scarcity axis was always specific-non-substitutable-attachment, but the Demographic name obscured the extension. Name + canonical cases co-define the dimension's reach.

**Book implication:** when introducing a dimension, its canonical cases must be named alongside its scarcity axis. Scope boundary statements at dimension intros should reference canonical cases, not abstract definitions alone.

### 5.2 Character 25 (NASA Langley grandfather) is a system-wide ethical anchor

The grandfather-at-NASA-Langley character functions as an existence-proof counterweight across at least five dimensions (Habitability, Institutional, Kindred, Ecosystem, Epistemic). Without this dimension visible in the book's positioning, the framework reads purely critical — which Test 25 (steelmannability) flags as fragile.

Re-confirmed in the Kindred lock pass: the Hidden-Figures / Langley-community existence-proof reads strongly under the Kindred name, where NASA Langley appears as an institution that built specific-identity-deep professional kindred networks rather than only as a counterpoint to organizational opacity (Institutional).

**Book implication:** the existence-proof framing — that honest institutions and principled allocation do exist and that the framework's rigor is diagnostic rather than universally condemnatory — should be visible in early book positioning, not only in a closing gesture.

### 5.3 Kantian consent-precondition pair (Epistemic ↔ Autonomy)

Epistemic and Autonomy form a Kantian consent-precondition pair: consent requires knowledge (Epistemic axis); consent requires non-coercion (Autonomy axis). Epistemic-structural coercion is itself an Autonomy-dimension case; Autonomy-limited agents cannot exercise epistemic access. The pair reads more philosophically precise under these names than under Informational / Agency, which conflated with information-theory and principal-agent-economics usages.

**Book implication:** each dimension intro should surface the pair relationship. Chapters that use 120+ hour-week case content (Temporal + Autonomy) may cross-reference Epistemic where the consent-normalization shield operates through information asymmetry as well as through social framework.

### 5.4 Single-dimension cost severance is cost severance

The framework is additive and compositional, not conjunctive. A case that activates one dimension is a full instance of cost severance — the framework does not require multi-dimension co-occurrence to identify cost-bearing. Dimensions price their own scarcity axis independently.

Canonical single-dimension examples:
- A retiree discovering that their pension was restructured on terms they never understood — **Temporal** alone.
- A knowledge worker recognizing the consent-normalization pattern in their own employment — **Autonomy** alone (or Autonomy + Temporal).
- An LGBTQ+ individual whose chosen family is legally unrecognized in medical-decision contexts — **Kindred** alone.
- The NYC consulting-years isolation case — **Cohesion** scarcity at Kindred abundance — is canonically a single-dimension case by design. The non-co-occurrence is the pedagogical mechanism for distinguishing Cohesion from Kindred.

Multi-dimension activation (Ch. 7 asteroid-miner activating 9–10 dimensions; Ch. 8 coal activating many) demonstrates framework coverage and universality. Goal-2 direct-contribution work — "accept a job with open eyes and honest accounting" — often succeeds at single-dimension precision: a worker pricing their own tier on one axis, because that is the only axis their specific situation is severing on, is the framework working as designed.

**Book implication:** dimension chapters should not require readers to locate themselves in multiple dimensions before the framework earns its keep. A single-dimension identification is a successful use. Chapter-closing examples may show multi-dimension cases, but the framework's directly-useful floor is one-dimension recognition.
