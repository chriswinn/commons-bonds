Tier 2a naming: 
* "Individual Risk Cost" replaced by "Actuarial Risk Cost" (Decision A ratification, April 19, 2026). The actuarial framing is more precise: the tier prices a population-level statistical distribution of occupational and environmental health outcomes, not an individual harm. This aligns with how insurance, public health, and regulatory systems actually measure and absorb these costs. "Individual" was descriptively inaccurate — the tier is not priced per individual, it is priced per exposed population using actuarial methods.

	New material to test (against current rigor) before adding
Run these against the Success Criteria heuristic before adding to glossary:


1. Does this sharpen the framework or expand scope? (Sharpen = add. Expand = probably skip.)
2. Does it help a reader recognize the mechanism in their own situation?
3. Does it add vocabulary the discourse needs, or duplicate vocabulary already established?
4. Does it require Book One to make claims that would be stronger if held for Book Two?
5. Does it increase legal or political exposure without proportionate utility increase?


When in doubt, trim. Book One's job is to make the invisible visible and hand the vocabulary to the discourse. Anything beyond that job risks weakening the book's ability to do its actual job.

Watch items
* Do not let the glossary grow past ~25 terms. Each term must do work no other term does. Duplicates weaken the asset.
* Do not name the eight tiers in Chapter 1 — that's Workstream 04's rule. The glossary exists; the book doesn't name the structure until Chapter 6.
* Do not paste glossary entries into the Berggruen essay draft. That's competition material.
* After this workstream, the Technical Appendix (Workstream 08) will add formal Definitions A.11–A.14. Glossary entries should be plain-English; Appendix entries are mathematical. Don't conflate.
