Executive Summary

Commons Bonds is a comprehensive framework for pricing what markets exclude: the true intergenerational and community costs of extraction. The framework names the mechanism of cost severance — the systematic separation of value capture from cost-bearing — and provides mathematical tools to measure and address it.

Core Innovation: The Abundance Test methodology reveals hidden cost variables by systematically stripping away abundance layers. What appears free under abundance becomes visible and quantifiable under scarcity. This makes the framework applicable from individual decision-making to civilizational policy.

Validation: Three independent mathematical approaches converge on the same result: current extraction is systematically underpriced by factors of 15-122x across tested cases. The framework passes universality tests across all scenarios (terrestrial to extraterrestrial).

Application: Provides vocabulary for discourse, individual decision-making tools, and policy frameworks for honest accounting of extraction costs.

Core Framework Architecture

1. Cost Severance

The mechanism by which costs are cut loose from profits and transferred to people who had no say.

CS = RCV - B

Where RCV is true cost and B is accountability mechanisms.

2. Residual Commons Value

The price of permanent foreclosure plus ongoing externalities.

RCV = Foreclosure + Externality Tail

Weighted by substitutability across infinite time horizon.

3. Abundance Test

Systematic methodology for revealing invisible cost variables.

Strip Abundance → Costs Visible

Universal method for identifying hidden tiers.

Key Insight: Cost severance requires distance between value-capturer and cost-bearer. This distance can be spatial (extraction community vs. consumers), temporal (present extractors vs. future generations), or social (powerful vs. powerless). Mars colony scenarios prove this: when extractor and community are identical, cost severance approaches zero.

Mathematical Foundation

The RCV Integral

RCV = ∫₀^∞ { [1 - S(t)] · U(R, t, Q(t)) + E(R, t) } · D(t) dt

Component Definitions:

  • [1 - S(t)]: Probability no adequate substitute exists at time t
  • U(R, t, Q(t)): Utility from resource R being available at time t
  • E(R, t): Externality flow from extraction persisting at time t
  • D(t): Social discount factor (declining rate per Weitzman)

Abundance Test Methodology

Abundance Layers and Revealed Costs:

  • Atmospheric Abundance: Strip → Oxygen, climate regulation costs visible
  • Geographic Abundance: Strip → Spatial displacement, transport costs visible
  • Temporal Abundance: Strip → Rate acceleration, timing costs visible
  • Institutional Abundance: Strip → Governance, enforcement costs visible
  • Demographic Abundance: Strip → Knowledge transmission, culture costs visible
  • Ecological Abundance: Strip → Ecosystem service, carrying capacity costs visible
Methodological Principle: Any proposed cost tier must pass the abundance test: Does this cost become visible when specific abundance is stripped? Does it disappear when abundance is restored? This prevents arbitrary tier proliferation while ensuring completeness.

Application Framework

Individual Level: Eight-Tier FGC Decomposition

  1. Immediate Survival Cost: Direct threats to life/functioning
  2. Lifetime Health Cost: Long-term health impacts
  3. Community Stability Cost: Local social infrastructure damage
  4. Relationship/Family Cost: Strain on personal bonds
  5. Dynastic Labor Cost: Impact on children/future generations
  6. Opportunity Cost: What you can't do because you're doing this
  7. Environmental/Ecological Cost: Local environment degradation
  8. Existential/Meaning Cost: Damage to purpose, dignity, integrity

Policy Level: CSG Classification

  • CSG ≈ 0: Extract freely (coal, sand, iron ore)
  • Low CSG: Standard accountability (conventional oil, copper)
  • Moderate CSG: Enhanced reserves (uranium, lithium, cobalt)
  • High CSG: Strategic preservation (rare earths, platinum group)
  • Conditionally High: Insurance preservation (helium-3, speculative resources)

Civilizational Level: Universal Principles

  • Asymmetric Regret: When uncertain, default to reversible actions
  • Community Transition Reserves: No community bears transition costs alone
  • Political Capture Prevention: Transparency requirements for extraction lobbying
  • Intergenerational Equity: Future generations get a voice through pricing
