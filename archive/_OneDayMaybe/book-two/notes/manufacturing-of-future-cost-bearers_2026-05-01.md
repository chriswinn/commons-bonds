## §3. Mechanism statement

The mandate-without-support pattern (when articulated through reproductive-coercion specifics: abortion access removed + contraception restricted + no childcare / healthcare / food / shelter / education infrastructure) operates as **structural manufacture of future cost-bearers** — the system creates the next generation of extractable people while severing every cost of their creation to the cost-bearer who has no agency in the creation.

Connects to intergenerational-pricing-gap argument: future generations are not just denied access to depleted resources — in some configurations they are *produced specifically to serve as the next generation of labor and consumption*. Extraction begins before the child draws its first breath and does not end at death (casket + plot).

## §4. Double-severance pattern (sharpening, 2026-05-01)

The mechanism severs costs **twice** in this configuration:

### §4.1 First severance

The cost of the forced birth is severed from the entity that engineered the coercion (the political coalition that banned abortion + restricted contraception without funding support infrastructure) and transferred to the child mother (e.g., 12-13-year-old) who cannot legally work and lacks family economic support.

### §4.2 Second severance

The resulting human being's lifetime extraction-debt (birth bill → consumer debt → labor extraction → casket-and-plot) is severed from the system that benefits from their consumption + labor and transferred to that human being themselves.

### §4.3 Structural shape

- **Two cost-bearers:** the child mother + the new human being.
- **One mechanism:** mandate-without-support.
- **One beneficiary cluster:** the political coalition + the consumer-debt-extraction industries.

## §5. Why this is load-bearing

- **Completes the cost-severance lifecycle argument** — the system doesn't just extract from people who exist; it engineers the creation of new extractable people through coercive instruments without funding consequences.
- **Connects to intergenerational-pricing-gap (RCV / IPG) argument** at a deeper register — not just resource-depletion across generations but *manufacture* of future generations whose costs are pre-severed before they are born.
- **Double-severance pattern is a generalizable structural mechanism** — the pattern (one mechanism, two cost-bearers, sequential severance) likely operates beyond reproductive-coercion territory. Worth investigating whether the pattern recurs in other coercion-without-support configurations.
- **Author-articulated as load-bearing** — per `draft2.md` line 143: *"It's one of those observations that, once stated, cannot be unfelt."*

## §8. Generalization-pattern investigation (Book 2 scope)

Does the double-severance pattern recur in other coercion-without-support configurations? Worth investigating in Book 2; could anchor a dedicated Book 2 chapter on the pattern's reach.

Candidate domains for generalization-pattern investigation:

- **Immigration policy without integration support** (work authorization restrictions + no language / housing / legal-services / credentialing-recognition support → undocumented labor extraction; cost severance to immigrant + cost severance to descendants of unauthorized parents).
- **Criminal-justice policy without re-entry support** (incarceration + no housing / employment-discrimination protection / mental-health support / family-reunification infrastructure → recidivism + multi-generational poverty + cost severance to former offender + cost severance to children of incarcerated parents).
- **Eviction without housing support** (no-cause-eviction permitted + no first-month / security-deposit / case-worker / legal-representation infrastructure → housing-insecurity cycle + cost severance to evicted family + cost severance to children whose schooling is disrupted).
- **Asylum policy without legal-representation support** (asylum claims permitted + no public-defender equivalent for asylum proceedings → high denial rates for legitimate claims + cost severance to claimants + cost severance to family members deported back to persecution).
- **Foster-care exit without transitional support** (aging-out at 18 + no housing / employment / education / healthcare bridge → high homelessness / incarceration / pregnancy rates among former foster youth → cost severance to former foster youth + cost severance to their children).
- **Long-term-care policy without family-caregiver support** (Medicare doesn't pay for long-term care; family caregivers absorb the labor + lost-wage cost; double severance: first to family caregiver + then to elderly person whose care is rationed by family economics).

The double-severance pattern recurring across these domains would be load-bearing on a Book 2 chapter framing the pattern as structural rather than reproductive-policy-specific.
