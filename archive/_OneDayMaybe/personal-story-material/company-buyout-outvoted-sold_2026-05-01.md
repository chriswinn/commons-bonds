## §1. The scene

The company eventually received a buyout offer from one of its competitors. Chris did not want to take it.

But the rest of the owners decided to sell. It did not matter what Chris wanted to do with his portion of the company; they had absolutely outvoted him. The majority carried the decision.

Looking back on it, selling was the right thing to do.

Chris had gotten so used to being CEO — to going after work, to chasing the next contract, to building the team and clearing obstacles in front of the work — that he did not really know what he was going to do next. But it was the right move.
