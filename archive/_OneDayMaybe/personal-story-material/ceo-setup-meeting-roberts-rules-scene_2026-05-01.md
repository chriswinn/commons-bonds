## §1. Context — how Chris arrived at the setup meeting

Chris was working at NASA daytime + side-consulting nights and weekends. NASA was funding-constrained post-Apollo; the side consulting kept Chris's compensation aligned with the lifestyle his prior career had built and kept him connected to current technology.

A consulting client engaged Chris to look at a problem they could not articulate clearly. The U.S. government had auctioned off the TV bandwidth that analog TV stations had been using; the FCC had set a deadline for analog-to-digital conversion; companies were chasing the conversion work but the company that engaged Chris was not understanding why their conversion-team economics worked sometimes and not others.

Chris's diagnostic approach: there are infinite ways to solve any problem; the real question is not "can we solve it" but "what way is the right way for THIS context" — given the specific parameters that matter (faster / cheaper / easier to support / familiar tools for the team / etc.). Asking those parameter-clarifying questions surfaced the actual mechanism: the core team knew the work backwards and forwards, but the locally-hired help on each market's job site were sometimes still learning on the job, redoing work that had been done wrong, costing days of compounded delay that bled the project's profit. Chris turned that division around.

The company decided to roll that division off into its own company. They invited Chris to a meeting after work. He felt honored — they were even meeting after-hours so he could be there in person, given his day job at NASA.

## §2. The scene proper

The meeting started by Robert's Rules. It was all very formal. Someone made a motion to roll that division off into its own company. Someone seconded it. The motion carried.

Now that the division was its own company, the chair said, the first order of business was naming the CEO.

Someone looked at Chris and said: *I nominate Chris Winn to be CEO.*

Chris was shocked. He did not see that coming. Looking back, this was completely a setup — completely premeditated. Everyone in the room knew it was going to happen except him. He should have connected the dots that it was about to happen.

He looked at the person who had just nominated him and said: *I was going to nominate—* and pointed to another person in the room, someone older than him, someone who was already well on his way through executive positions at different companies.

That person looked at Chris and said: *I second the nomination of Chris as CEO.*

Chris looked at all of them and said: *This is a setup.* He chuckled. Then he objected: *I have a day job. I work at NASA.*

The room went: *Well, you work here at night. So — let's see. Do you want the job?*

Chris said: *I will give it everything I've got.*

And he did.
