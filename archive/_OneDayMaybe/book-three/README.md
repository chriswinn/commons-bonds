# Book Three — *Pricing the Final Frontier* (notes-only; deferred)

**Status:** Notes-only. Not actively being drafted. Per the two-book split decision and the Book Three framing as horizon work for institutional adoption of the framework beyond Earth.

## Scope

Book Three applies the Commons Bonds framework to off-Earth extraction, space law, and the institutional architecture that will govern resource decisions when humanity's extraction operations cross jurisdictional boundaries that no current legal framework adequately addresses:

- Asteroid mining and lunar resource extraction under Outer Space Treaty + recent national space-resource laws
- Mars colony thought experiments extended to institutional design (Chapter 7 "Colony Administrator" is Book One's compact version; Book Three develops the institutional architecture)
- Cross-planetary resource pricing under extreme substitutability uncertainty
- Intergenerational commons at civilizational scale — future generations as the extraterrestrial commons-bond counterparty
- Space law framework adoption pathways (treaties, multilateral bodies, private/state hybrid jurisdictions)

## Activation triggers

Book Three is the longest-horizon work. Activation triggers not firmly defined in current decisions, but presumed to include:
