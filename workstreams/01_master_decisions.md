- **Two Pillars:** (1) Cost Severance — naming the mechanism. (2) Residual Commons Value (RCV) — pricing what was unpriceable.
- **Two Methodologies:** (1) Abundance Inversion Test — discovery. (2) Universality Test — validation.

Book Two is *The Subsidy Economy* — applied/policy/advocacy work, named beneficiaries, requires coauthorship and institutional affiliation and a publisher with legal backing. Book Two is **notes-only** for now; reevaluate in 3–5 years after CRNA / PhD / institutional affiliation.

**Success criterion, stated once:** "If, ten years from now, a labor lawyer uses 'severed cost' in a brief and the judge doesn't need it explained — and the author whose name is on the book never knows this happened — the book succeeded." Not royalties. Not prizes. Vocabulary used by people solving real problems.

## The four decisions

### Decision A — The eight-tier conflict

**The conflict:** Two eight-tier decompositions of RCV exist in the Drive folder, both dated April 2026, both called "Full Generational Cost" / "FGC," both naming eight tiers, but with *different cuts*:

| April 18 glossary (`commons_bonds_updated_glossary.html`) | April 19 new file (`commons-bonds-eight-tier-decomposition 9.html`) |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Immediate Survival | 1. Lifetime Survival |
| 2. Lifetime Health | 2a. Individual Risk / 2b. Mission Failure Reserve |
| 3. Community Stability | 3. Dynastic Labor |
| 4. Relationship / Family | 4. Foreclosure |
| 5. Dynastic Labor | 5. Community Transition Reserve |
| 6. Opportunity | 6. Ecological Carrying |
| 7. Environmental / Ecological | 7. Knowledge and Culture |
| 8. Existential / Meaning | 8. Political Capture |

**Why they're different:** The April 18 list cuts by personal-life categories; it's narratively useful. The April 19 list cuts by transaction structure and maps each tier to a specific *abundance layer* (atmospheric, geographic, temporal, institutional, demographic, ecological, political) — making it falsifiable via the Abundance Inversion Test.

**Recommendation:** Adopt the April 19 version as canonical. Rationale: it passes the methodological test the April 18 version cannot. "Existential / Meaning Cost" does not map to a strippable abundance layer, so it can't be generated by the Abundance Inversion Test, so it fails falsifiability — and the framework's defense against "you're pricing the unpriceable" depends on falsifiability.

**What to do with the April 18 version's useful pieces:**

- "Your son growing up without his father present" as the Dynastic Labor example → move to Chapter 1 Knowledge Worker extension (Workstream 04) as narrative demonstration.
- "Existential / Meaning" thread → returns as prose in Chapter 10, where meaning is the point, without needing formal tier status.
- "Relationship / Family" category → folded into Dynastic Labor at the personal scale.

**If this decision goes the other way** (April 18 canonical): Workstreams 02, 03, and 08 are unworkable as drafted. The Abundance Inversion Test loses the tier-generation function and becomes prose only. This is a project-altering choice.

**Decision needed:** Adopt April 19 version as canonical? ☐ Yes ☐ No

### Decision B — Option C architectural surfacing

**The question:** Does Option C (two pillars + two methodologies) get labeled as such in the book text, or does it stay as invisible book architecture?

**Case for surfacing:** Readers who come to the book expecting a framework will appreciate knowing the scaffolding. It aids citation ("Winn's two-pillar framework…").

**Case for keeping invisible:** Surfacing frameworks as frameworks makes a book feel academic. *Evicted* and *Doughnut Economics* both have rigorous architectures that never announce themselves. The reader experiences the pillars as they're introduced (Cost Severance in Chapter 2, RCV in Chapter 5–6, Universality Test in Chapter 7), without being told "these are the two pillars."

**Recommendation:** Invisible in the main text. Surfaced in the Technical Appendix, Glossary, and any future academic-audience extension pieces. This lets the reader experience the mechanism before meeting the taxonomy.

**Decision needed:** Invisible in main text, surfaced in apparatus? ☐ Yes ☐ No — or specify alternative.

### Decision C — Chapter 10 framing

**The conflict:** Three framings of the book's contributions exist in current files, all incompatible:

1. **Old three-contributions list** (in Chapter 10 scaffold + Ripple Effects): "cost severance / residual commons value / solidarity architecture." Solidarity architecture is retired under Option C, so this list is obsolete.
2. **Option C list** (finalized mid-April): Two pillars + two methodologies.
3. **Villain-analysis landing** (April 19): Two pillars + institutional legibility infrastructure as the closing prescription.

**Recommendation:** Chapter 10 closes on the villain-analysis framing — "Establish the prior claim of future generations on commons resources as a first principle of political economy, expressible as a financial instrument, enforceable wherever institutions exist to enforce it, and nameable as a debt wherever they do not." Option C stays as book architecture (Decision B above). The old three-contributions list is retired everywhere.

**Why villain-analysis over pure Option C for Chapter 10:** Option C describes what the book *is*. Chapter 10 needs to describe what the book *does*. "Name the debt even when no one is enforcing payment" is a prescription, not a taxonomy. Taxonomies close books weakly; prescriptions close them strongly.

**Decision needed:** Chapter 10 lands on villain-analysis / institutional legibility? ☐ Yes ☐ No

### Decision D — Two-book split commitment

**The question:** Commit to the Book One / Book Two split now, and trim Chapter 8 accordingly?

**What the commitment means concretely:**

- Chapter 8 shrinks to one fully-worked example (coal, continuing from Chapter 2) through the eight-tier framework, plus a brief aggregation signal ("this pattern, applied globally, implies a subsidy of approximately $10–15T annually; what happens when we price it honestly is the subject of a separate work"). Probably halves current length.
- Full-economy simulation material, named-industry critique, and the GoFundMe / tax-gap / housing clusters move to `/_One Day Maybe/Book-Two-Subsidy-Economy/`.
- Publishing strategy updates to reflect staged approach: Book One near-term (late 2026 / early 2027 target), Book Two notes-only pending coauthorship + institutional affiliation + legal-backed publisher.

**The uncomfortable truth in the two-book analysis:** "Your intellectual contribution may be most powerful if you write the framework that lets others do the political work safely, rather than trying to do both the framework and the politics yourself." This reshapes the project identity from "I will fix this" to "I will build the instrument others use to fix this." Both are valid. They are different books.

**Recommendation:** Commit. The nursing trajectory, the liveaboard structure, the absence of institutional / legal / PR infrastructure, and the five vulnerabilities catalogued in the two-book analysis all point to the same conclusion. This is not cowardice; it is strategic sequencing. Book One protects Book Two by establishing the vocabulary first. Book Two protects Book One by not preloading it with advocacy.

**Decision needed:** Commit to two-book split? ☐ Yes ☐ No
