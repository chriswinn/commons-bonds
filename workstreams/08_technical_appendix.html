The additions this workstream makes

Definition A.11 — Dynastic Labor Cost

The cost imposed on a worker's lineage by below-Full-Generational-Cost compensation for their productive capacity. Formally:

Given a worker w whose productive capacity is extracted at compensation C(w) < FGC(w), the Dynastic Labor Cost DLC(w) is the present value of the intergenerational wealth trajectory the worker's lineage would have accumulated under C(w) = FGC(w), minus the present value of the trajectory under the actual C(w), compounded at a conservative return rate r over the relevant generational horizon.

Cite the Social Security counterfactual (Chapter 5) as the empirical reference case at population scale.

Cross-reference: capital theory in Marxian surplus-value analysis, but refuses the asymmetry in which capital compounds intergenerationally and labor does not.

Definition A.12 — Community Transition Reserve

A pre-committed, independently held fund, sized at extraction inception and held outside the control of the extracting entity, required to finance the complete economic transition of any community whose existence becomes structurally dependent on the extraction operation.

CTR(c, t) is computed at time t=0 (inception of extraction) as the projected present value of full community transition costs at extraction conclusion, discounted using a risk-adjusted rate that accounts for the probability of premature operation termination.

Distinct from: decommissioning funds (physical cleanup), severance packages (individual workers), reclamation bonds (environmental restoration).

Most policy-actionable concept in the framework — can be added to extraction contracts directly without requiring the rest of the framework to be accepted first.

Definition A.13 — Ecological Carrying Cost

The cost of extraction processes on surrounding ecology, priced across the full recovery timeline. Distinct from Foreclosure Cost (which prices the absence of the extracted resource itself). ECC prices the secondary damage to hydrological function, biodiversity, carbon sequestration capacity, flood mitigation, pollination services, and other ecosystem services across centuries or millennia as appropriate.

ECC(R, t) = ∫[t to ∞] D_eco(R, τ) · e^(-ρτ) dτ, where D_eco is the damage function to ecosystem services and ρ is the Weitzman declining discount rate.

Cite Daly's strong sustainability framework as the theoretical basis; note that Daly's framework did not formalize the recovery-timeline integral.

Definition A.14 — Political Capture Cost

The cost imposed on democratic and institutional systems by extraction industries that use a fraction of their captured value to suppress honest pricing of their own costs. Includes lobbying expenditures, regulatory capture, suppression of safety research, narrative capture through funded media, and broader institutional corrosion.

PCC is both a consequence of cost severance and a cause of its persistence. This dual property makes it structurally different from the other tiers and explains why the framework's predicted CS > 0 persists in equilibrium — the parties benefiting from invisibility invest in maintaining it.

PCC(industry i, period p) = Σ [lobbying + narrative capture + regulatory capture + research suppression] during p. Empirical measurement is partial at best; the definition is operational but precise quantification is an open research question.

Theorem A.3 scope note

The Ripple Effects doc flagged that Theorem A.3 (Structural Cost Severance) explicitly states "for all non-renewable resources," which became inconsistent when Definition A.1 was rewritten to accept ρ ≥ 0 (all Commons Resources).

Two options. Pick one:

  • Option 1 (scope note): Keep the theorem statement as-is. Add a scope note: "Theorem A.3 is stated for non-renewable resources (ρ = 0). A generalized version covering all Commons Resources (ρ ≥ 0) follows as Corollary A.3.1." Add a one-paragraph corollary statement.
  • Option 2 (rewrite): Rewrite the theorem statement to cover the general case with non-renewables as the headline special case.

Recommendation: Option 1. It preserves the original theorem for citation, is mathematically equivalent to Option 2, and requires less Appendix churn. The Session Archive noted that Chris flagged this: "flag me when you get to the appendix pass." This is that pass.
