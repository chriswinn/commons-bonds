## Part A — Chapter 4: Nigeria Contrast Case

### Where Chapter 4 stands now

The current Chapter 4 file (`Commons Bonds - Chapter 4 (THE EXISTENCE PROOF)`) presents Norway as proof that cost severance is a policy choice, not a natural law. The chapter ends on Norway's limitation: even Norway doesn't price the barrel's permanent physical absence.

### What the Nigeria paragraph does

The Norway existence proof, alone, is vulnerable to a reader's response of "well, maybe Norway is just special." The Nigeria mirror forecloses that reading. Same resource (oil). Same physics. Opposite institutional choice. Opposite outcome. The comparison proves that cost severance is institutional, not geological.

### The paragraph (already paste-ready)

Source: `Commons Bonds - Nigeria Contrast Case` (Google Doc). The paragraph is:

> Now consider what happened when countries did *not* make Norway's choice. Nigeria sits atop the same kind of resource wealth. The Niger Delta's communities have absorbed decades of ecological destruction — oil spills, gas flaring, water contamination, displacement — while the revenue flowed to multinational corporations and a narrow political elite. The contrast is not geological. Both countries had oil. The difference is entirely a matter of whether the political economy captured the resource value for the community or allowed its extraction by external capital. What appears as natural economic outcome in the American and Nigerian cases is, as the Norwegian case proves, a political choice embedded in institutional arrangements.

### Placement

After the paragraph on Norway's results, before the paragraph on Norway's limitations. The source doc states this placement explicitly. Do not expand into a Nigeria case study — 150 words is the full Book One dose.

## Part B — Chapter 5: Social Security Case

### Where Chapter 5 stands now

The current Chapter 5 Draft file (`Commons Bonds - Chapter 5 (Draft)`) presents cost severance across industrial accountability failures: Libby, Deepwater Horizon, Exxon Valdez, Baotou. The reclamation bond failure is presented as the system's own confession that it cannot price what it is doing. The social cost of carbon is presented as the closest existing attempt.

### What the Social Security case adds

Social Security demonstrates the same mechanism operating at *national financial scale* rather than industrial scale. It is also the clearest empirical demonstration of Political Capture Cost (Tier 8) available — the unified budget adoption in 1968, the Treasury-IOU mechanism, the 22-year pattern of masked deficits, and the roughly $108 trillion counterfactual gap between actual fund value and what compound equity returns on the surplus would have produced.

### Four modifications to the raw material

The source file (`commons - consider including 12.html`) is strong on data but written at a higher exposure level than Book One can carry. Apply these four modifications before integration.

1. **Frame as political / soft cost severance, not physical.** Adopt the villain analysis's own concession (Hole 1 from the villain file): Treasury bonds are real legal obligations. Social Security benefits, while threatened, are not legally extinguished. The framework handles both physical and political cost severance, but they differ in reversibility. Lead with this framing so the case does not read as overclaim.
2. **Use the mechanism-focused framing, not named actors.** Do not name LBJ, Reagan's OMB, or 2003 Congress as actors. Use: "No single actor experienced themselves as a villain. Each faced a locally rational decision under locally visible constraints. The incentive structure produced the outcome regardless of who sat in the chair." This is the villain analysis's institutional-legibility point, and it lowers exposure dramatically.
3. **Keep the $108T counterfactual number but caveat honestly.** The four caveats in the source file (market distortion at fund scale, gold price-fixing pre-1971, political risk, 2008-style volatility) are the difference between a credible claim and one that gets dismissed as back-of-envelope. Include them. They also demonstrate intellectual honesty, which raises the case's persuasive power.
4. **Do not include the Iraq War financing line.** It is accurate. It is verifiable. And it triggers the advocacy-label reflex (Two-Book Vulnerability 2). Save it for Book Two. The case lands fine without it.

### Placement and the closing pivot

After the industrial cases, before the reclamation bond / social cost of carbon section. The transition should note that cost severance is not limited to physical extraction — it operates at any scale where value is captured in the present and costs are deferred to parties with no voice in the transaction.

The closing pivot for the chapter, from the source file, is strong and should be preserved (phrased in your voice):

> The industrial cases are scenes. The Social Security case is arithmetic. The mechanism does not change; only the scale at which it becomes undeniable.
